I didn’t plan on posting again this soon, but something happened last night and I don’t really know what to do with it. A few people told me to take another look at the photo I mentioned, and I kept avoiding it, but eventually I gave in. I wish I hadn’t.

The first time I looked at it, I only focused on the words scratched into the wall. That’s all my brain really registered. But when I opened it again and zoomed in, I realized there was a lot more there than I noticed before. The marks weren’t just random scratches. There were layers to them, older ones underneath, newer ones on top. Some of them didn’t even look like they were made with anything sharp. They looked uneven, stretched, like something soft had been dragged across the wall again and again.

That alone made me uncomfortable, but then I noticed something in the corner of the photo. At first I thought it was just a shadow near the doorway. The lighting in that room was always bad, so I tried to ignore it. But when I adjusted the brightness, it didn’t behave like a shadow. It didn’t blend into the wall. It held its shape.

It looked like something standing there.

Too tall for the doorway, slightly bent, like it didn’t quite fit in the space. I don’t remember seeing anything there when I was inside that room. I don’t even remember taking the photo properly, which is what makes this worse.

I was about to close the app when I noticed something else.

There were two photos.

I only remember taking one.

The second one was taken from outside the room, from the hallway. It was angled slightly inward, like someone was standing behind me while I was inside. You can see part of my shoulder in it, and my hand holding the phone, taking the first picture.

I didn’t take that second photo.

I checked the timestamps multiple times. They were taken just seconds apart.

I didn’t sleep much after that.

Every time I closed my eyes, I kept replaying that moment at the top of the stairs. The door slightly open, the breathing, the way it said my name. It didn’t sound threatening. That’s what’s been bothering me the most. It sounded… familiar, like it already knew me.

Around 3 in the morning, I woke up to a sound. At first I thought it was coming from outside, maybe someone walking past the building. But after a few seconds, I realized it was inside my apartment.

It was the same sound I heard upstairs.

Slow. Dragging.

I stayed still and listened. It stopped. Then a few seconds later, it started again, a little closer this time.

I forced myself to get up and check. The hallway was empty. Nothing looked out of place. No doors open, no movement, nothing.

But when I came back into my room, my phone was on the floor.

I’m sure I left it on my desk.

The screen was still on, and the gallery was open. The second photo was displayed.

I stared at it for a while before I noticed what had changed.

The doorway wasn’t empty anymore.

Something was closer.

Not fully visible, just part of it. Long, thin, and… wrong. Like it was leaning further into the room than before.

I dropped the phone without thinking. I didn’t want to look at it anymore, but even after turning away, I could still picture it.

I haven’t opened the photo again since.

I don’t want to.

But something else has been happening, and I don’t think it’s a coincidence.

I’ve been feeling hungry.

Not normal hunger. I’ve been eating properly, but it doesn’t go away. It just sits there, constant, like something is missing and I don’t know what.

The worst part is that it feels familiar.

I remember feeling like this before, after the flood, when things got really bad. I don’t want to think about that part yet, but my body remembers it even if I try not to.

I don’t know how to explain this without sounding completely insane, but I don’t think whatever was in that building stayed there. I don’t know if it followed me, or if it was already there and I just… noticed it again.

Earlier today, I checked my reflection. I don’t even know why, I just felt like I needed to.

For a moment, everything looked normal.

Then it didn’t.

It was only for a second, but my reflection wasn’t doing what I was doing.

It was smiling.

I wasn’t.

I’m keeping the lights on tonight, even though I don’t think that’s going to help.

If anyone here has experienced anything like this after going back to places like that, I need to know.

Because I don’t think this is just in my head anymore.

And I don’t think it’s outside either.

I think it’s getting closer.
