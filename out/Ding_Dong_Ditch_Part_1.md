There was a sudden knocking at the door but when I went to open it, it stopped. No bells in this apartment, and the flimsy doors were so thin and light it almost felt like a terminally online, Hikikomori like me could punch a whole through. It had only been a week since I moved in what I would be calling my house for the next 4 months. Don’t get me wrong, there was nothing wrong with the house itself, it was clean, there was water, heat and everything else I would need. That was a bummer because I wish I had something about it to complain about. It reminded me of the song where the singer finds the perfect girl but he still missed his ex. My messy old room at my parent’s house had the comfort not even this modern mono-design minimalist piece of cuboids could replicate.

Wish my internship was remote but no, they had to bring me a thousand miles from home for an accounting internship. If only it was possible to add a bunch of numbers together form my home rather than bringing me all the way here to do…well, the same. Being the only company that offered me an internship, I guess it is what it is.

There it was! The knocking at the door. I went up to the door at a pace a little more than casual with it being the third time someone has knocked on the door this week but when I open it, no one. I peeked my head out staring into cavernous depth of the hallway on the right and then to the left to be greeted by the same view. Could you blame me for not noticing the first sign of trouble? I always found it so stupid how characters in horror movies always fell right into the net of trouble but clarity is a privilege of the observer, a luxury the maze runner is denied. Have you heard of a funnel trap before? The funnel is designed so the exit looks like the entrance; a fish literally cannot logic their way out because their escape instinct is the trap.

There was still a week left for my internship to begin. I had all the furniture set up, all my clothes in the closet, shower curtain attached and bedsheets put on. It was evening by that time; I had started reading a manga on my tablet. A knock on the door again. This time I just stared intently at the door, my eyes scanning the door almost trying to see through the vibrations in the air to see where knocking is. I felt it coming much closer to the ground than I expected, almost as if the person opposite the door was a toddler, or someone in their early teens. I gently placed my feet on the carpet floor, slowly tip toeing while crouching like a cat staring intently at its prey. You probably couldn’t tell because my eyes were black but my pupils were dilated to the max. As I moved in closer the knocking didn’t stop. I leaned closer to the source, a child, no a teen, some…young kid…like with a voice that just hit puberty giggling against the door.

I open the door almost swinging it away and once again, no one. My hand begins to shiver as I slowly close the door again. And now for the first time, my brain was reacting to something it couldn’t solve. Probably the first time ever, my brain was functioning like it had never before, thoughts spiralling in my head so loud and so quickly that I couldn’t grasp a single one to make sense of.

Like a rainfall over a forest fire, the thoughts calmed down just as quickly as they rose. One of the biggest evolutionary flaws being my only salvation. You see, when humans observe something they cannot understand, they tend to gaslight themselves into taking every unknown in their memory and poorly fitting a sense of reason to them. Try squeezing a square shape through a circular hole, and you could say it fits and believe it to the point where you start to believe that every square is a circle and never entertain the thought of a square shaped hole.

I gaslit myself to believe it was a “knocking sound” rather than a knock probably coming from the water heater right next to the entrance. That was the conclusion my mind landed on, conveniently ignoring all the other evidences that would shatter my sense of reality. How cowardly of me to accept a reality I could comfort myself with than to face the truth.

The knocking continued. While it wasn’t continuous, it was regular. It had no pattern, no rhythm; it just happened at a random time of the day. What I did sense was a growing frustration. The knocking lasted a little longer, was more urgent and more frequent. If you drop a frog straight into the boiling water, it will leap out for its life the moment it touches the surface. A frog resting in cold water slowly bought to a boil, by the time it realizes it’s being cooked alive, it’s already too late. At some point, hearing a few seconds of knocking, knowing full well that no one is outside, it was just a minor inconvenience. Headphones on, oblivious to the obvious imminent danger as the knocking only grew.

The final weekend before work begins. While I normally prefer the comfort of not leaving my house, living in this new empty unfamiliar place felt rather uneasy. It wasn’t the bland design, heck, it wasn’t the knocking sound either. It was simpler. I missed my old room, and without it this place felt hollow, stripped of something warm. It almost felt like it was made with the intention of making me feel at home but by someone who’s never felt what a home is like. While everything was perfect, I couldn’t sense a single ghost, a single presence of anyone who may have lived here before. Maybe it was because I have never left my parent’s house other than to live in a dorm. Is this what it feels like to be alone? What was this underlying emotion making me instinctually uncomfortable in this structure? Was this…fear?

Night had begun to fall. I got out of the shower, threw on an oversized t-shirt, and buried myself under the blanket. Watching the flurry, I felt an almost strange sense of calm watching the light drizzle under the street lamp like tiny sparks. Wait a minute… I never heard a single knock today. Did they fix the water heater? I let out a long sigh, staring at the window.

My eyes begin to slowly close, I feel myself being engulfed by the warmth of sleep. Did it always feel this good falling asleep here or was it the lack of knocking? As I drifted further below the ocean of wakefulness, my body froze. Every muscle fiber gripping every micro-inch of my bones, my eyes blown wide. The sound was unmistakable. It was no “knocking sound” from a broken machine, it was the sound of knuckles clanking against wood. It was intentional and it was louder. It wasn’t at the main door. Someone was knocking on my bedroom door.

Every impact of the sound made me flinch. I clutched the blanket like my life depended on it. Like a small animal caught in a trap, my body had switched gears into a mode that almost felt like was eating on itself. Every single thought in my head had vanished, all my conscious self was doing was staring at the bedroom door right next to me. The knocks felt sharp and almost pierced through my stomach, they were vibrating through the air towards me almost like being physically hit. They were intentional and continuous like someone desperate to enter in filled the room. The only other sound being the deafening “lub-dub” of my own heart drumming in my ears.

I wanted to scream and cry on the top of my lungs and ask who was it outside, but it felt like my mouth, just like my fingers and toes, had gone completely numb. I could hear a few voices outside, unlike the child-like voices before, these seemed…older…. They were laughing and giggling among themselves, as if finding a sadistic confidence of being allowed in. What was a 19-year-old girl lying under her blanket supposed to do except stay frozen and accept the worst.

As a few minutes went by which felt like hours, my ears started adjusting despite the panic mode. The knocks felt different each time. I couldn’t make out their language but the tone felt like one voice telling another “Wait, let me try it now” and a faint giggle. The light awareness amidst the panic finally helped me open my tear ducts. Eyebrows squinted and my eyes bloodshot red I began to sob. Almost like something in me gave up on the panic after it realized nothing can be done. Crying really was our body telling us “A bad situation will come that we have no choice but to endure”, the only other choice was hoping it will kill you because that would be the only relief from the pain.

A male voice saying something analogous to “Wait, wait” and then a second later, a huge thud against the door making me scoot away from the door and stick firmly against the wall at the other side of my bed, my blanket being the only shield from the coldness of the space around me. Another huge thud that sounded like a kick and my door broke open. The voices let out a collective uproar. I screamed and put the blanket over my head digging my face into the pillow, my heart beating through my chest. All I heard was muffled rapid padding and giggling sounds fading away along the carpet towards the entrance and vanish. I did not have it in me to take the carpet off my head. I laid there clutching my chest hoping my heart doesn’t give up on me as my tears soaked the mattress.

When my eyes opened, I instinctively took off the blanket. The rays of the sun filling my room, heart still racing. I leaped off my bed, immediately putting a hoodie while on the call with 911.

“911 what’s your emergency”, a woman’s voice.

“There’s been a home intruder! Please just send someone”

“Okay calm down now. What time did this happen?”

Odd, considering they know it isn’t immediate.

“Last night, they tried to break my bedroom door down”

“Well, why didn’t you let them in?”, a muffled laughter.

“What?”, I ask with my eyebrows raised and doublecheck my phone screen.

“I-is someone on the way…”, I ask cautiously.

“Oh, yea, yea they should be here now” and almost immediately…a knock on the door.

I rushed to open the door and to my dismay, no one. My throat closed as I shut the door and collapsed on my knees picnicking. All I could hear from the other end of the call was laughter, like someone was finding sadistic joy out of this.

My purse with a phone charger, wallet, my phone (of course) and with no time to waste I walked out of the apartment. I had to get out of this place, this godforsaken state! I had to go home. Tears wouldn’t stop flowing through my eyes. I missed my father, my brother, I missed venting my bad days with my momma. The elevator was out of order; I walked further to the end of the corridor opening the door towards the stairs.

I climbed down the stairs hoping to reach the door that opened to the streets.  But every floor led to a hallway with rooms. While I haven’t left my house since I’ve moved in, I could’ve sworn I lived on the third floor. Three floors down, the same hallway and at its center, my apartment. This time, I wasn’t even panicking nor was my mind playing tricks on me, I knew what I was seeing. For all its glory and horror, it had no exit. Not because the doors were locked, but because the shape of it made exit impossible, every path curling back into itself like a thing with no outside. I cannot lie, a part of me was in grudging admiration. A bizarre tick of morbid fascination in my brain urging a smile against the incomprehensible.

Did I…ever tell you about a Funnel Trap?
