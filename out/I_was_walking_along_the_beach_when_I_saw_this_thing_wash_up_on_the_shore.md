I recently went on vacation at a beach house in South Carolina. Living up north, I don't get out to walk along beaches much. So I decided to grab a flashlight and head outside to look for shells, sand dollars, and maybe take pictures of any animals roaming around at night. What I was not expecting was to take a picture of something almost... alien, in nature.

So I was shining my flashlight at the shoreline, looking for any shells or rocks washing up on shore, when all of a sudden, I shine my flashlight on this... black.. sludge. I walk past for a moment, not even paying attention, my brain not even registering that I hadn't just seen something so macabre. Then my brain processes, I suppose it took me a second to even realize there was something there, maybe it was because of how... disgusting the thing was. It was a black, sludgy, bubbling goop with claws and legs of multiple different crustaceans sticking out. It was disturbing, but I thought it may have just been some unfortunate meal for a bigger sea creature; but... what irked me the wrong way was... it was also sparsely hairy? and the legs seemed to flinch as the claws, likewise, seemed to pinch; almost like it was alive. This emorphous sludge, which had to have been around 12 feet in diameter, filled with what would aptly be described as the remains of an Orca's all you can eat crustacean buffet, was only around 10 or so feet behind me. 

Obviously, I wasn't gonna get a closer look, but I think some may have gotten on my shoe as I was walking past. The bottom of my left shoe has a deep black stain that I can't scrub out. Anyway, I looked at the hideous black sludge for a couple minutes, just to retain memory of what I was seeing, before I turned around and started walking back, making a big arc around this thing as to make sure I wouldn't get too close. Unfortunately, after I was past it and started walking back to my vacation home, I turned around out of curiosity. And I swear I saw this thing's legs touch the ground and start to scuttle back into the sea, the goo sloshing along the sand. After a couple more steps I made a made a double take, and it was gone.

During my walk back, I started to notice all these dead seagulls lying on the beach, decaying, yet leaking out of any openings seemed to be that very same black sludge; bubbly and hairy all the same. Looking back at it, I'm glad I hadn't gotten any closer. 

I didn't go into the ocean for the rest of that vacation. Instead, I spent time in the pool, where it hopefully couldn't reach. The morning after my encounter, all the dead animals were gone, as was the black sludge. There was a thick gray fog; however, I still wonder if it had something to do with the black sludge. I've tried to tell others about what I saw, including the NOAA, but after filing a report, they responded telling me it was likely pollution, and that they would look into it; I never got another response. Most people I told believed I may have just been tired, seeing algae or sea foam covering crabs, and that the decay and the stain on my shoe were all just coincidence. I don't believe what everyone is telling me, I think that thing was alive, an undiscovered creature; perhaps from deep on the sea floor. 

I'm going back in a week, I can't rest until I know for sure if I saw something. I'm gonna try to get some photos and video of it; I just hope it shows again.
