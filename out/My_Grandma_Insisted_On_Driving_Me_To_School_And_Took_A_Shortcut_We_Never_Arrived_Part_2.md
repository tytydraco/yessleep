So, some of you were curious, so I thought to just get it out there. There’s really no point to keep it bottled in, all to myself, so I’m gonna share my dream, before that though, let me make a few things clear. For those wondering how the police found me, they confirmed I didn’t arrive at school before checking road cameras. 

They saw the car I was in and they managed to identify the road the car took. Since there were no cameras on the dirt trail, they asked people if they saw either me or my Nonna, on the road the car was last seen on. They end up speaking with the gas station clerk, who did manage to recall seeing me and my grandma, he also recalled seeing the direction I went. 

“They went that way, I think.” The clerk told the officers, pointing to the left, where after a kilometre, the road ended, merging into a dirt trail. So, the officers went down the trail and they found me, in a beat up car, with no Nonna. You may be asking, what did they figure happened to my grandma? I have no damn clue, because I don’t think they did anything.

My grandma is old, which is my way of saying she had bad knees, and probably bad everything! The point is, my grandma could not have possibly evaded search parties that the officers claimed to have sent, in a relatively small forest. Sure, I know the darn truth, but I’m annoyed that the police officers think I’m lying, and don’t even have a convincing explanation for themselves. Okay, enough of that, this isn’t a disguised rant post against police officers, I promised.

For those thinking the old lady who drove me to school was not my Nonna, I assure you, she was. And one final thing, this dream I’m about to share is not a meaningless dream, I know it’s crazy, but I know it’s telling me something real. Now, if you think I’m insane, then how dare you! I didn’t even want to post this in risk of being made fun of and I built up the courage to post this anyway.

To accuse me of insanity is to make me doubt myself and that’s a cruel thing to do to me, especially what I’ve been through. Okay, here it is:

The dark and nothingness of my dreamless sleep is wiped away, as a dream fabricates. I am in the car, no, I am the air inside the car or maybe I am the car. I see myself, sitting nervously in the passenger seat, backpack at my feet, and my physical, dream self looks out the window, I do too. I see we are on the dirt trail already.

Then my heart skips a beat as a deafening, sandpaper-rough voice, a voice that also sounds like many people I know whispering the same thing at the same time, speaks:

“You’re starting to remember…” The voice says eerily, filling me with unease, and nostalgia somehow, and I know the voice didn’t belong to my dreaming mind. My dream self does not seem to have heard the voice, but my dream Nonna does. My Nonna looks the same as I remember the last time I saw her, but she went dangerously pale when she heard the voice. I felt a pang of sadness and nostalgia seeing her again, I thought I would never see her again, but then I didn’t consider seeing her in my dream state.

My dream self asks my Nonna a question, but she doesn’t reply and I know why. The booming voice is speaking over my dream self.

“Are you feeling it? The nostalgia? Are you missing it? Do you want to go back? Do you miss the good old days?” The voice asked, and I could hear the malice emitting from the voice. My Nonna, if possible, turned an even lighter shade of white. My dream self kept trying to talk to Nonna, ask her stuff, get her attention, but she ignored me. 

Then I, the real me experiencing the dream, felt I needed to look out the windshield. On the side of the dirt trail was a figure, tall, lanky, and looming over the car. And it’s face, it brought on such yearning and longing because I could see everyone I ever met painted onto this figure's own face. A dozen pairs of lips, yet 1 pair of lips, separated, and out rattled a statement that sounded like more of a threat:

“I can take you back…” My Nonna gasped and said out loud:

“Is that-” But she didn’t finish, because I saw as she intentionally swerved the car into the figure. In true dream-like fashion, the figure warped and turned into a tree right as the car connected. I saw the windshield cave in and my dream self and Nonna being knocked cold instantly. I don’t know what my Nonna saw to drive into the figure/tree on purpose, but it must have been something she missed, something she wanted to go back to.

Then, in quick succession, everything outside the car evaporated into a black mist, the tree, the dirt trail, everything evaporated and shrouded the car in an unpleasant darkness. Don’t just think the car was engulfed in a dark shade of black smoke, think more outer space but without stars, without balls of flame lighting up all of outer space, it would be not just black, but a black nothingness where seeing was impossible. That’s what the car was surrounded by. Slowly, everything started to fade back in, but differently.

It wasn’t the dirt trail that materialised, it was the grassland that I came to know very well, and almost yearn. The grass was so lush and such an amazing healthy green, cut so neatly. There was nothing in sight no matter which way you looked, it was just you and your thoughts. The sky was clear, and a perfect blue, dotted with a bright star that turned everything so colorful and vivid.

I don’t know how long I was stuck admiring this scene before I heard shifting. I peeked over to the driver's seat and saw Nonna unclipping her seatbelt and scrabbling at the door handle. I turned to the passenger seat and saw my dream self, unconscious, covered in bruises and bloody lacerations with shining pieces of glass lodged into my arms. I watched as my dream self woke, eyes opening slowly and observing everything slowly.

Then my dream self jumped as Nonna opened the car door and stumbled out into the lush grass. I hear my dream self groan something out but it is too weak to be audible. Nonna strides quickly away, further and further from the car. I watched as my dream self calls out to Nonna with a clearer and stronger voice, but Nonna is just too far away to hear the call, and even if she did hear it, I doubt she would have stopped, considering her trance-like state.

So my dream self unbuckles the seatbelt and crashes out of the car, and then stops. Actually, froze is a better word. The uncanny voice speaks again:

“I will take you back, you will be able to experience the best moments of your childhood again, you will not regret this…” My dream Nonna in the distance pauses as the same figure from the dirt trail fabricates itself in front of her. The figure is much larger now, the height of a 2 story building. The figure grabs its chest, and tears it open.

As black liquid pools out, I can hear the delightful sounds of kids giggling and playing emanating from its split chest, such a sweet sound. I can feel the heat of a summer day, spent playing with friends, and an ice cream truck sounds in the distance. I hear the sounds of TV films being played, the crickets chirping happily outside, and the smell of pizza. Those were such simple, fun times.

 I see Nonna, willingly pulling herself through the figure’s chest cavity, and then she is gone. And then, the figure is also gone. That’s it, that’s my dream, that’s what I wanted to share. I hope you guys understand all of that, and understand all I’ve been through. 

I think I’m going to do some things to trigger nostalgia. Look back at some old photos, listen to old music, visit places I haven’t been to since my childhood, track down old forgotten friends, ECT. Because honestly, I yearn to go back, I had everything perfect! I had a lot of things that people would dream of, some that people won’t even understand, and naming all of it would be like writing a novel.

I hope by doing all this, I can trigger some nostalgia which might attract something that can offer me what I want, and I know it might not be safe, but I’m willing to take that risk. You guys have to understand, I just really want things to be like they used to, and there’s no changing my mind.
