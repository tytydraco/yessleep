I awkwardly tied my apron on and headed to the counter. Despite working a mid-day shift, I felt exhausted. My stomach tightened with dread as I saw a shop full of customers lining up to get their lunchtime coffee.

I tapped my code into the register, every press sending a wave of pain thrumming through my skull.

Sam, my coworker, tapped my shoulder. “Hey, rough night, you look tired?”

It was, and I was, but I tried to hide it in my voice, “Oh yeah, don’t worry about me though, I’ve got enough energy to make it through my shift.”

She seemed a bit concerned but didn’t press further. I knew she was looking at the dark circles under my eyes that had formed after 6 nights of insomnia.

An impatient customer spoke up from the other side of the counter. “Hey are you ever going to serve the customers, or are you just going to talk all day?”

My eyes went wide and I snapped into customer service mode. “Oh, sorry sir, how can I help you today?”

He rattled off his order and I mechanically punched it in.

The natural lighting of the store was killing me as my eyes swam with bright lines that threatened to overtake my vision, but I made it through another two hours of the shift.

A man walked up to the counter, he looked well put together with a clean white shirt, clear skin, and his sandy brown hair delicately styled into what resembled a natural windswept look. He smiled as he stopped in front of me. “Hi, how are you doing today?”

I smiled back at him, feeling a little energized for a second. “I’m doing great, what can I get for you?”

He went into a faux thinking pose for a second before replying, “How about a latte, lets keep it simple.”

I tried to return his energy. “Oh yeah, I can definitely make one of those, I like them a ‘latte’” I made air quotes with my hands as I said it.

His face dropped. “Was that supposed to be a pun?”

I could feel my face flushing a bit. “Oh … yeah, sorry.”

He looked at me blankly for a second, then launched himself across the counter. 

We were slammed down to the floor with a crash, scattering merchandise across the floor. The force of the impact sent him rolling off of me, but in the seconds that I could see his face, I saw wild eyes and a vicious smile.

I could barely move, my body was wracked with a combination of fear and pain. From the periphery of my eyes I could see him standing up.

He looked down at me and screamed, “WHY WOULD YOU SAY THAT!”

His words sent a metallic wave of sound echoing in my ears, mingling with the screams and yells from around the store.

I groaned from the floor, and he kicked me in the side. My ribs flared with agony, and I tried to speak but could only manage another groan.

Then suddenly, he was gone.

Sam stood where he had just a moment ago, holding a metal stool with a slight red sheen on the seat. She yelled something to one of my coworkers, then crouched down beside me.

“Are you okay, hey are you awake, please look at me.”

I tried to look back at her but my eyelids were forcing themselves downwards.

Finally a chance to sleep.
