Hoping that Hailey hadn’t gotten ahold of my phone again and ordered more “stuffies”.

Maybe someone had sent it to the wrong house? But it had my name on it. The hair on my arms stood on end as I continued to stare at it. This just didn’t make any sense.

Ms. Lendel was standing outside chatting with parents as they came to pick their kids up. She wore a knee length green dress that matched her eyes. Her dark auburn hair hung loose around her shoulders. I wouldn’t have described her as hot but she was pretty for sure. She had just started at the school last year as Hailey's first grade teacher.

I could see all of the kids playing on the orange and blue playground as I approached. Hailey was doing her best to climb back up the slide she had just come down. I immediately envisioned another kid sliding down and hitting her but stopped myself from telling her to get down. The book I had been reading said it was very important to let them explore and try new things. I loved climbing slides as a kid so who was I to stop her?

“Hey, How was she today?” I said crossing my arms doing my best to have a conversation without taking my eyes off Hailey.

“Good, like she always is. She never causes any problems.” Brenda leaned in closer, talking a little quieter.

“Between you and me? She's the most well behaved student in my class, hands down. I can only assume it’s because of her grandparents or something.” She said jokingly. The compliment filled me with equal parts pride and embarrassment. I turned to Brenda to see her looking up at me with a small smile.

“Yeah I don’t know where she gets it from but it’s certainly not me.” I laughed. I could see Mr. and Mrs. Gorski walking toward us and I really didn’t want to get stuck talking with them. Mrs. Gorski was as Karen as you could get and Mr. Gorski loved making off-color jokes. Hailey also noticed me and waved, with both hands, from the top of the playground. She started making her way over to the slide which I knew meant she was heading this way.

“Well I better go scoop her up.” I said starting toward the playground.

“Have a nice day. And please let me know if you need anything!” Brenda said, as I walked away.

Hailey met me halfway between the car and the playground. She launched herself into my arms. I caught her, lifting her up and hugging her as we made our way toward the car.

“Hey little girly. How was school today?”

“It was really good today Dad. I made a new friend!”

“A new friend that’s great! What’s their name?” She looked up to the sky like she did every time she was thinking, after a moment she shrugged.

“That’s okay, we can always ask them tomorrow. That’s so cool though! What did you guys do today?”

“Nothing, they asked me if I wanted to be friends and I said yes!”

“That’s fair, that would make you friends.”

She nodded very seriously which made me smile. I finished buckling her into the carseat and kissed her on the forehead again.

“Ready to go home and help me make dinner?”

She nodded again excitedly.

I was expecting more questions on the way home but she was asleep before I even started the car. I didn’t want to turn the radio on and wake her up so I drove in silence. My thoughts kept drifting back to the tape. I spent the 20 minute drive back home trying to come up with any explanation but couldn’t.

I unbuckled her as gently and quietly as I could, placing her against my shoulder. She stirred for a second then nuzzled against me falling back to sleep in the warm afternoon sun. I easily held her with one arm as I closed the car.

I could see Sarah’s back as I rounded the corner toward my apartment. Her long black hair was tied up in a bun. It looked like she was fumbling with her keys trying to lock her door. She went running everyday around this time. Based on the yoga pants and T-shirt today was no different. She looked over her shoulder at me. She went to talk before seeing Hailey sleeping and paused. She finished locking the door and moved closer, speaking quietly.

“How’s it going?”

“Not too bad. Just getting ready to start dinner.” Sarah reached up and stroked Hailey’s hair.

“Ohh, what's for dinner?”

“Nothing too crazy, just some spaghetti.”

“That sounds good. You’re going to have to teach me how to cook one of these days. I don’t know how much longer Rachel and I can survive on frozen meals.” She smiled. “Speaking of which, are you still okay to drop Hailey off on Friday? It’s all Rachel has been talking about all week.”

“Yeah, Hailey’s been super excited too. I really appreciate you watching Hailey. I owe you one for sure.” I smiled.

“Oh don’t you worry about it at all. It’s the least I could do after you dropped me off at the airport. And if this works out well can we swap off.” She said as she stopped stroking Hailey’s hair placing her hand on my arm.

Hailey opened her eyes looking at Sarah.

“Oh, I’m sorry did I wake you up?”

Hailey grumbled and turned toward my chest.

“I better get out of here before she wakes up. Don’t say I didn’t warn you Saturday morning. Because I’ll be sleeping in.” We both shared a laugh as she gripped my arm a little tighter.

“Okay, I’ll see you Friday then” She said smiling and walking away disappearing beyond the corner of the hall.

I carried Hailey through the living room, down the hallway, past my room and into hers. The room was still painted pink and white stripes from when this was her nursery. Stuffed animals covered every open surface. The dresser, the window sill, her bed. They seemed to congregate in plies leaving paths too small for my feet. I pushed my way through the hordes of stuffed animals and laid her down on her purple unicorn sheets and pillow. I tucked her in with her favorite fleece blanket. The one with the tabby kitten on the front. I wanted to be angry at how messy the room was but how could I? Looking down at my amazing daughter I could feel nothing but love.

As I walked back into the kitchen my eyes were drawn to the closed box on the table. I stopped and stared at it for a second. DId I leave it open or closed? I didn’t remember. I walked over to the box slowly and opened it again. The smell of old musty cardboard filled my nose as the VHS stared back at me from the middle of the box. A black monolith surrounded by decayed and destroyed cardboard. Looking at it seemed to fill the room with static again. Like air before a thunderstorm.

I closed the box again and put it under the table before starting dinner. I was stirring the sauce when I felt a tug on my shirt. I looked down to see Hailey reaching both hands up at me. I picked her up and kept stirring.

“Hey girly, did you have a nice nap?” She shook her head. “Aww I'm sorry. Do you want to stir?” I said, trying to distract her. She did her cute over exaggerated nod.

I put her down and pulled a chair over. I placed her on the chair and handed her the spoon holding her hand in mine. She helped me stir for a minute before looking up and smiling. I couldn’t help but lean down and kiss her head.

We spent the rest of the night hanging out on the couch. She watched Bluey and played with her dolls, while I scrolled. I put her to bed around 8 and managed to get into mine by 9. I still woke up exhausted the next day. Hailey had tried to crawl into my bed 3 times last night and I had to fight with her each time. Hearing her crying in the other room broke my heart but she had to learn to sleep alone.

It wasn’t until we were standing at the front door post tantrum that I noticed Hailey’s hands.

“Hey Girly, where’s Mr. Bear? Not taking him with you today?”

Hailey just stared up at me for a moment with a totally blank expression. She then scrunched her face up and reached her arms out to me. I picked her up.

“Hey girly is everything okay?” She nodded back but didn’t say anything. I didn’t want to push it. Truth be told I was actually glad. Brenda had asked me if I could try to have Hailey come to class without it. Maybe she had finally grown out of it. I carried her the whole way to the car and buckled her in. The ride to school was quiet. No questions this time. I checked a few times to make sure she hadn’t fallen back to sleep but Hailey just stared out the window. She was fine by the time I dropped her off. Back to waving to everyone with as much gusto as she could muster. I chalked it up to kids being weird and went to work.

The rest of the week flew by and it was Friday before I knew it. Anxiety screamed at me as I stood outside Sarah’s door holding Hailey’s hand. Hailey didn’t seem to notice as she jumped up and down excitedly in her pink princess pajamas. I still wanted to find any excuse to pull out but I couldn’t. It wouldn’t be fair to Hailey. How could I let my worry and fear stop her from being so happy?

I motioned to Hailey to knock. She let go of my hand and knocked way too many times. Sarah opened the door to find an excited Hailey waving hard, her smile beaming. Rachel peaked out at me from behind Sarah’s leg. Between her jet black hair and matching pink pajamas she looked like a spitting image of her mother. Sarah crouched down and Hailey ran up and hugged her. Sarah pulled away.

“Are you ready to spend the night?” She asked.

Hailey nodded.

“Do you want to play Mario?” Rachel whispered, her voice high and sweet, still hiding behind her mother. Hailey nodded, walking past Sarah and into the house with Rachel.

“Hey, thanks again for watching her. I honestly don’t know how long it’s been since I had the house to myself.”

“It’s really no problem.” She said rising up and leaning against the doorway. Her hair hung loosely past her shoulders.

“Well I really do appreciate it. Just let me know if you need anything or if she’s too much to handle in the morning let me know and I can come get her.”

“Jack,” She said, reaching out and putting a hand on my arm. “Stop worrying about it. Just have a nice break and try to relax.” She smiled. “I remember the first time I let Rachel stay at another house I was a wreck. If you start to worry too much you can just come see her. Or call” She pulled her hand back.

“So, any plans tonight?”

I scratched my stubble as I thought. “Honestly I’m not really sure. Part of me wants to go out. I haven’t been over to O'Malley's since Hailey was born. But part of me wanted to stay home and just sleep until you bang down my door tomorrow. Begging me to take her back.” I gestured inside with my head.

Sarah cracked a grin and looked me up and down.

“If it was me I would probably just sleep all night. And hey if they get along then you’ll have plenty of chances to go out” She smiled.

“That’s true.” I agreed. “Okay, well I’m going to head out then and get started on sleeping then.”

Sarah opened her arms for a hug. I hadn’t been expecting this and was completely caught off guard. A thousand thoughts and a new flood of different anxiety filled me as I stepped forward and hugged her tentatively. She did not share the same hesitation as she squeezed me hard. The scent of flowers filled my mind as her soft pajamas shifted under my hands. I couldn’t remember the last time I had hugged another adult.

A weight settled on my shoulders as I closed the front door. A mountain of worry for Hailey. Would she have a good time? Would she behave for Sarah? A truckload of what-ifs plowed into me and I did my best to ignore them as I got ready for the evening.

I finally felt a shift about halfway through my long shower. The worry had slowed from a flood to a trickle and I was starting to feel myself relax. I took a walk through the apartment after I got dressed. Breathing in the silence and isolation. This was the first time in years I had been alone. Truly alone.

After flopping myself on the couch I had already decided to do my best to stop worrying and make the best of the time I had. Who knows when I’ll be alone again? So I ordered a supreme pizza, broke out a 6 pack, and put on some old horror movie. I couldn’t watch those with Hailey around yet but I hoped to share them with her someday.

I was already through my 4th beer when the movie ended around 10 o'clock. It was about time for bed. I went into the kitchen to put the last two beers in the fridge when I saw it. The dilapidated box was sitting on the table. I didn’t remember putting it there. Had Hailey been messing around with it? I put the beer away before walking over to the box.

The closed box sat staring up at me. With how beat up the box was I didn’t know how the flaps stayed closed. My head swam as I stood there. I hadn’t drunk that much had I? I decided I was just going to deal with it in the morning. I stood there for another transfixed by the box. What if the tape wasn’t in there? What if Hailey had taken it? I would just check to be sure.

I opened it to see the tape hadn’t moved an inch. It was still dead square in the center. My head swam and my teeth seemed to vibrate as I looked at the tape. Tinnitus filled my ears but I didn’t notice. The only thought that was going through my head was: What could be on it? I reached out and picked it up. A light static shock zapped my finger but it didn’t even register. The only idea I could focus on was figuring out what was on this tape.

The tape looked like a standard VHS. Black exterior, white reels on the back, however, there was no sticker on the front. It was completely blank. I held it at arm length inspecting it. Something didn’t feel right and I didn’t want to bring it closer than I had to. But I didn’t want to take my eyes off of it. A thought percolated up from the corner of my mind snapping me back to reality. I did have a tape player!

I sat the tape back down in the box and started toward my bedroom. I stopped as the overhead light flashed quickly for a moment. I was filled with an overwhelming sense of being watched. I looked around my apartment trying to find a source but there was nothing. I walked over to the window and moved the curtains back. The window showed a dimly lit street with a vacant lot across from the apartment building. I didn’t see anyone out there. After a minute of watching I chalked it up to one too many drinks and moved toward the bedroom. The voice in my head was yelling at me that I just had to know what was on the tape.

I found it under a stack of Marlo’s clothes. An old combination DVD and VHS player her parents had given us for Hailey. Along with some copies of a few animated movies. Her Dad was always very insistent that “My grandchildren were going to grow up with the classics.” That was when her parents were still talking to me.

I hooked the tape player up in the living room and sat it next to the TV. I switched the TV over, a bright blue screen stared back. I walked across the room back over to the tape. “Hi hungry I’m dad.”
