Captain Crunch Teeth Girl Knows Where I Live. Please Help.

Sup. I’m not really a Reddit guy, but someone told me this is where you post when weird stuff happens and you don’t know who else to tell, so here I am.

So I’m just gonna explain what happened and maybe someone can tell me what I’m supposed to do. Please help me out here.

I deliver pizzas for this crappy little place in rural Kansas. The kind of town where the only real businesses are a gas station, a liquor store, and a depressing little casino full of geriatrics.

Everything else is just cows and corn.

Miles and miles of cows and corn.

It’s like a wasteland made out of fucking corn and cows.

Most deliveries are easy. Farmhouses, trailers, bars, stuff like that.

But there’s this one delivery nobody else will take. And I’ve been stuck with it since I started.

Deer Creek Road.

My boss always gives it to me because I’m the youngest guy there and I’m usually a little baked anyway, so I guess he figures I won’t freak out as easy.

They order once a week.

Always the same thing.

Three all-meat pizzas.

They never use the app. They always call the shop.

And the way they talk is… strange.

Not like English is their second language.

More like English is their third attempt at language after grunting and banjo noises.

Imagine Kid Rock trying to sound like John Wayne after chewing a mouthful of ass.

Stuff like:

“Bring the meat breads. Three rounds. We got coin.”

Then a bunch of laughing in the background like ten people are standing around the phone listening.

The place itself is about fifteen minutes outside town.

No houses nearby.

No lights.

Just a dirt road cutting through fields and trees like someone dragged a shovel through the dark.

At the end there’s this sagging old shack surrounded by a ring of campers. Maybe ten of them.

All rusted and dented like crushed beer cans.

Some have lights on inside though. Yellow lights that flicker like an arcade machine that’s about to die.

The first time I pulled up there I thought it was some weird campground.

Then the family came out.

I’ve seen maybe six or seven of them.

Big people. Pale and hairy. Real hairy.

Their hair looks like it’s never been washed. Thick brown bowl cuts with rat tails hanging off the back.

Honestly… kinda bitchin’.

They smell strong too.

Not rotten exactly.

More like campfire smoke mixed with bacon grease and wet dog.

Like someone slow-roasted a pile of wet ass.

They’re also really friendly in a way that makes your skin crawl.

Stand too close.

Laugh too much.

And there’s this girl about my age who always comes up and sniffs the pizza box before they pay me.

Not like someone smelling food.

More like a dog sniffing something weird on the sidewalk it isn’t sure if it should eat or bury.

Short little sniffs.

Head tilted.

Eyes squinted.

Then she smiles.

Her teeth are… messed up.

Jagged yellow triangles.

They look exactly like Captain Crunch cereal pieces jammed into someone’s gums.

Anyway.

A few weeks ago something happened that made me quit the job.

I had just dropped off their pizzas and was heading back down Deer Creek Road. I’d lit a roach because the drive back to town is boring as hell.

Then a sheriff’s cruiser lit me up behind me.

The cop that got out was a real piece of work.

The kind of small-town cop who acts like he’s the final boss of the county.

He immediately started talking to me like I was trash.

Called me “stoner boy.” Said he could smell weed from a mile away. Started asking where I’d been and what I was doing down Deer Creek like I’d just robbed a bank.

Then he got weird about it.

Kept asking what the family up there was like.

If they said anything strange.

If I’d seen anyone else around.

Then he grinned in this way that made my skin crawl and said:

“People disappear out here sometimes, boy. Yep, sure do. One day they’re here, next day—puff—gone forever. Just like that smoke you been shoving into your brain. Now where’s the stash?”

Then he dragged me out of the car, cuffed me, punched me in the gut, and shoved me into the back of his cruiser while he started tearing through my car looking for weed.

From the back window I could still see the road leading to the family’s place.

That’s when I saw shapes moving in the trees.

At first I thought the weed was messing with me.

Then more of them stepped out.

Six… seven… maybe more.

Just standing along the tree line watching.

The cop noticed them too and started yelling for them to get back.

They didn’t move.

They just laughed.

Weird barking laughs like coyotes trying stand-up comedy.

Then one of them stepped closer to the road.

He had a machete.

The cop panicked.

Pulled his gun and fired.

Shot the guy right in the head.

Everything went quiet for about half a second.

Then the rest of them rushed him.

I couldn’t see much from the cruiser but I heard it.

Metal hitting bone.

Screaming.

Wet noises like someone stomping on a watermelon.

Eventually one of them walked over to the cruiser.

He had the cop’s keys.

They were dripping with blood like a popsicle melting in July.

He unlocked my cuffs and opened the door.

Looked at me and laughed.

Then said:

“Meat man go.”

So I went.

I drove straight home.

Next morning I quit the pizza job and decided I was never going back to Deer Creek Road again.

A few weeks later that sheriff’s cruiser got found abandoned in a ditch outside the county.

There were multiple bodies in the trunk.

People that had been missing around here.

I never told anyone I was there that night.

I figured that was the end of it.

Then last night I looked out my apartment window and saw someone standing next to my car.

Just leaning over looking through the windows.

It was her.

Captain Crunch Teeth Girl.

She stayed there for a minute… sniffing around the car like she was curious about it.

Then she slipped something under my windshield wiper and walked away down the street.

Didn’t even look back.

The note just said:

“Don’t forget our pizza.”

This morning my boss called me.

Somehow convinced me to come back.

Offered me a raise and back pay for the week I quit.

Which means Friday…

I’m delivering to Deer Creek Road again.

And now Captain Crunch Teeth Girl knows where I live.

So seriously.

What the hell am I supposed to do?
