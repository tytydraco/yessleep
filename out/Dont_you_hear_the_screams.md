It started with something mundane.

Around 9:30 I arrive home from work. I extract a beer from the fridge, kick back on the couch, and throw on the Netflix original I'd arbitrarily selected the night before. My wife greets the amateur actors with a grimace and myself with a smile. She sits down on the adjacent couch. Sweat beads on my chest and underarms. It's even hotter in here than it is outside. 80 something degrees. I crack a window. Something builds. Chatter, then high-pitched laughter. The faint but unmistakable drone of children. I hadn't heard them when I came in. A perfectly timed interruption, and a foil to my misophonia.

"Whose kids are up this late?" I assert. Lin furrows her brow and tilts her head, as if trying to make out the sound. I pause my show.

"Nah, still don't hear 'em," she says.

"I'm near deaf in my right ear, you're tellin' me you don't hear that?"

We sit for a moment.

"Nope," she shrugs.

I grumble. The noise builds. The sweat builds. I shut the window and make my way to the basement, where I know it's cold. I crack my beer and take two healthy swigs. Begrudgingly, I watch Netflix on my phone. 

--

The same thing happens the following day. This time, I hear them the moment I step out of my car. Louder now. Almost like the number has doubled, gone from a gaggle of kids to a crowd. It's even hotter today. I pause and listen, persisting through the early signs of a migrane. They're somewhere down the road. Somewhere between the fields, the dilapidated shed, and my neighbor's house, half a mile away. I walk inside my house. Lin's already on the couch, watching her own show. 

"These kids are killing me," I tell her. "Come outside, why don't you?"

She sighs, hesitates, then pauses her show, gets up, and opens the door. I wait. She turns to me, purses her lips, and shrugs.

"Sure it ain't your ears actin' up?"

I clutch my head and shut the door behind us. "It's kids, Lin. How in God's name aren't you picking 'em up? This ain't just my ears." I try to calm myself. "Maybe it's yours. You stopped hearing high pitches or somethin'?"

Lin shrugs again and sits back down. "I don't know. Can you still hear 'em with the windows shut? I'd like to watch some tv."

"Can't now. I can feel a migrane comin'."

"Alright. Well, hope you feel better. Get some rest, maybe. Sure that new job of yours ain't helpin' much."

I groan, and massage my temples. I crack two beers and drink them both in the shower. I lay down for a few hours, then sleep.

--

The next day's my day off. I decide to get a second opinion. Maybe Lin's ears aren't so good after all. I take the trek to my neighbor's house, walk up to his steps, make sure the lights are on, and knock. Joe greets me at the door.

"Hey man, how's it going?" He looks at me inquisitively.

"Hey sorry man, I got a question. You been hearing those kids playing down the road recently? It's been driving me up a wall."

All the blood leaves Joe's face. He gives me a small shake of the head, then shuts the door. I linger on his step. I watch him draw the curtains and shut off the lights. It's the first time I've felt truly unsettled since I encountered a grizzly a few months ago. I walk home in silence.

--

The silence continues through the night. Lin and I have a peaceful next few days. Three whole evenings without a migrane.

--

The fourth day rolls around. I open my car door, and it's as if I've walked  into a wall of flames. Needles to my skull. I double over, nearly crying out in pain. The kids are back. This time, they're screaming. Not in cheer. In agony.

I fight my way to my step. All the windows are up. Lin's not on the couch. The tv is off. I slam the door open, and catch a glimpse of her hair in the kitchen. Hearing my heavy breathing, she turns around with immediate concern.

"What? What is it?" 

My face goes cold. My tounge's in a knot. I finally speak, in a hushed, shaky voice.

"Don't you hear the screams?"

She purses her lips and turns to the ground. I don't give her a chance to reply. I go back outside, still clutching my head, amidst the flames. I hop in my car. It's quiet, spare the audible throbbing in my ears. I allow myself to rest for only a moment, before cracking the window, allowing the sound to fill the car. I speed towards the source.

I arrive at my destination. A bubble of uncertainty seeps up through the pain, then horror, at a shape I can just barely make out. The screams are coming from the dilapidated shed. 

Now come gunshots. Hundreds. Before I form a single thought. 

I duck down. White hot lasers bound through my skull. A pain I've only felt once before. I scream. The kids scream. The gun screams. 

Now I am the only one screaming. 

I am angry. So angry I can feel  adrenaline travel from my toes to the top of my head and swallow every ounce of pain. So angry I could kill. I will kill. I take out my own gun from my glovebox.

I exit the car. An armed figure exits the shed. 

I shoot. I miss. Static fills my head. I fall to the ground.

The figure disappears into the night.
 
An agonizing ten minutes pass before I come to. I rise to my feet, slowly. I approach the shed. I open the door. The smell of blood fills my nostrils.

I take a single breath before turning on my flashlight. I drop my phone and my gun and I vomit.

A pile of children's bodies fill the shed. Draped over one another in awkward positions. At least twenty of them, unclothed and covered in bullet wounds. I reach for my phone, hearing an audible squelch as I remove it from the pool of blood and bile beneath my feet. I wipe it on my pants. I vomit again, then call the police. I know it'll be at least twenty minutes before they arrive. The urge to kill dissipates, and the desire for death prevails. The only thing stopping me from ending my life is having to reach once more into the pool of blood to retrieve my gun.

"911, what is the location of your emergency?"

I'm nearly unable to speak. 

"I heard screaming, then gunshots, then - sorry. I'm sorry. I'm on the road. I'm between-"

I give them my address and my neighbor's.

"Thank you, sir. Police will be there any minute now. We just got a similar call. Are you safe? Do you see the gunman?"

"Yeah, I- well, I did, he ran-"

I hear sirens down the road, then see lights.

"The police are here. Thank you."

I hang up. Two cars approach. Eight cops exit bearing guns. 

They're pointing them at me. 

An electric shock rips through my body. I look down. My hands are covered in blood.

Oh. Oh God.

"KEEP YOUR HANDS WHERE I CAN SEE THEM!"

My gun is sitting at the foot of the shed.

"PUT YOUR HANDS BEHIND YOUR BACK!"

No. No. No. No. No.

Handcuffs. 

"You have the right to remain silent."

I am the man with the gun.

"YOU FUCKING BASTARD! YOU BASTARD!"

I yell into the silent night.

"Anything you say can and will be used against you in a court of law."

Back of the police car.

"IT WAS YOU! IT WAS YOU! NOT ME! YOU FUCKING BASTARD!"

Window open. Door slammed shut. 

"If you don't have an attorney, one will be provided for you."

He was supposed to die.

"If you decide to answer questions now, you may stop answering at any time without a lawyer present."

I wish I could've seen his face.

"Do you wish to speak to me?"

Window shut.

--

I am fucked. 

--

Absolutely, miserably, fucked.

--

My trial is in three days. Charges I do not wish to write.

--

I can't afford an attourney. I just started my job. 

--

Lin won't speak to me. She won't speak to the police. She won't speak.

--

I can still hear the screams.

--

I am begging.

--

Before my trial.

--

Please.

--

Someone.

--

Kill me.
