I was in my second year of college at the time. I lived with my dog in a small, cheap house that wasn't too far away from my school's campus. I had planned to live with a few other friends to help cut down on the rent, but shortly before the school year started, those plans fell through. The isolation was manageable—it's just the amount of rent I had to pay that was slowly killing me. Anyway, back then online chat room websites were really in their prime. Audio and even video sites were slowly becoming a normal thing. Ever since I got my laptop for school, I would often use it to visit these sites. I met a lot of different people, some who I still call good friends to this day.

One night I got home from one of my night classes and, like usual, got on my laptop to join voice chats and servers with people that had similar interests as me. This went on for a while, until after a couple hours when I met a guy called Chris. We talked for around 45 minutes, just general college stuff, nothing too specific. It turns out he went to one of my school's rival schools, which gave us a lot to talk about. At one point I casually mentioned how my computer was acting up. Chris told me he was studying I.T. and offered to help me fix the issue. I agreed, and he gave me a link telling me to install the application it led me to. I was unfamiliar with the site, but nothing about it yelled sketchy, so I installed it. Chris walked me through a few extra steps. It was a weird experience. The application allowed him to literally control my mouse and do whatever he needed to on my computer. I sat back and watched as he got to work trying to fix the issue.

After a while, I had to step out to let my dog back inside the house for the night. I was gone for only a few minutes. Just as I stepped back in my room, I noticed something on the laptop quickly being closed out. I dismissed it as Chris just doing what he needed to do. I asked him if the issue was fixed, and he said yeah, that it was. I thanked him and told him I was gonna go to bed because it was now starting to get really late. I thought he was actually pretty cool, so before I left, we scheduled another time to talk.

After that, I closed out of everything and put my laptop to sleep and went to bed. I slept for a few hours. When I opened my eyes, it was still dark outside. I checked the time—3:30 a.m. My whole room was lit up by my laptop screen, which I believe is what woke me up.

I sat up, trying to understand how it could have turned on by itself. I got out of bed and sat down in front of the screen. It was just sitting there on the desktop, showing my background. I tried to move the mouse to put it back to sleep, but it wasn't responding. The mouse was frozen.

Frustrated, I began continually clicking the mouse, trying to unfreeze it. Nothing was working. An image then popped up and covered the whole screen. It was dark. My eyes had to take a second to adjust to it. Soon I realized that it was a picture of my empty room, like as if the laptop's camera had taken it. I pressed the escape key. I even tried Ctrl + Alt + Delete, but none of it was doing anything. Then another image appeared. This time it was a picture of me. It looked like it had just been taken. My heartbeat was picking up speed and was getting louder in my ears. I finally looked up at the laptop's camera. The small light next to it was now on, indicating that it was being used.

Then a video appeared on the screen with a play button in the middle of it. It was me again, now with a horrifying expression on my face…

The mouse started moving by itself towards the play button. It clicked it, and the video started. As I desperately put one hand over the camera and the other holding the power button, I held the power button for about 15 seconds. One final image appeared on the screen. It only showed for a brief second before the laptop finally powered off and the screen went black.

But I could still tell exactly what the image was of. It was a low-quality picture of the outside of my house in the dark. I sat there frozen, taking in what I had just seen.

Breaking the silence was the sound of my dog aggressively barking from downstairs. Knowing my dog rarely ever does this, I panicked at the thought of someone trying to break into my house.

This would never end up happening.

However, my dog's barking slowly stopped, and the rest of the night was just silence.

The next morning, I moved all of my stuff into a hotel and stayed there until I found a new place to live, purely out of fear that someone would come back to that house. I never talked to Chris again, and I still don't know what was done to that laptop. I had school files on that thing that weren't backed up, but I couldn't bring myself to turn it back on. I accepted that they were gone.

I ended up setting that laptop on fire and burning it completely.
