I don’t usually work evenings on the farm.

I help my dad out here, but most of the time it’s during the day. Nights feel different out here. Quieter. Too quiet.

A few days ago I was feeding the cows just before it got dark.

Everything was normal until it wasn’t.

They just… lost it.

Running in circles, crashing into each other, making this low noise I’ve never heard before. Then they all suddenly grouped together and faced the same direction.

The tree line.

I just stood there for a second trying to figure out what was going on.

Then I heard a branch snap.

Close. Like really close.

I grabbed a tool from the fence and started walking toward it. That’s when I noticed blood on the ground. Small droplets. Fresh.

Leading into the forest.

I followed it.

It wasn’t far before I saw him.

Guy on the ground, holding his side like he was hurt. There was blood on his shirt, but something about it didn’t look right. I don’t know how to explain it. Just… off.

I still went over.

“Hey, are you alright?”

He looked up at me and said, “Yeah… I think so.”

And I don’t know how else to say this, but it didn’t match. His voice. It was too calm. Like nothing was wrong.

I asked again if he was okay.

He just looked at me and said, “Do you trust your instincts?”

That threw me off.

I kind of just stood there for a second before answering.

“Yeah… I think so. I’ve had to. My dad’s not really around like that. I just learned to rely on myself.”

He watched me the whole time.

Then he said, “You’re more self-aware than most.”

I didn’t know what to say to that.

I just told him I’d go get help.

He didn’t try to stop me. Just watched me leave.

I went back to the house and told my dad there was someone hurt out by the trees.

He didn’t even look at me.

Just said, “Then help him.”

I told him I couldn’t carry him alone.

He got annoyed. Said I always make things bigger than they are.

I tried to explain there was blood, but he cut me off and said he wasn’t dealing with it.

So I went back by myself.

The guy was still there.

Same position. Same expression.

Like he hadn’t moved at all.

I helped him up and walked him back to the house.

He didn’t struggle. Didn’t lean on me. Just… walked.

I sat him down at the table.

For a second it was just the two of us.

Then he looked at me again.

“Why did you help me?” he asked. “I’m a complete stranger.”

I didn’t answer right away.

Then I said, “I don’t know… probably because no one really helped me growing up. My dad just expects you to figure things out. I don’t want to be like that.”

He didn’t react. Just listened.

Then he nodded.

“I understand.”

And I don’t know why, but that made me feel worse.

That’s when my dad came in.

He saw him and suddenly cared.

Told me to go get supplies.

I was about to leave when my dad started speaking to the man .

So I stopped just outside the room.

And listened.

My dad asked if he was alright.

Same answer. “Yeah… I think so.”

Then the guy asked him something.

“Do you regret how you raised him?”

I just froze.

My dad got defensive. Said his dad raised him the same way and he turned out fine.

There was a long pause.

Then the guy said, “That explains a lot.”

Something about how he said it made my chest tighten.

I left after that.

I drove into town to get proper supplies.

It took longer than I expected.

When I got back, it was fully dark.

The house was quiet.

Too quiet.

I walked in and called out.

No answer.

I found my dad on the floor.

He was alive, but barely. Breathing shallow. Eyes unfocused. Like something had been taken out of him.

The guy was gone.

No blood. No signs he was ever there.

Nothing.

I called the police.

They asked me what he looked like.

I told them. Tall. Clean. Almost… perfect.

Then they asked my dad.

He described someone completely different.

Short. Rough. Messy.

Nothing like what I saw.

They think one of us is lying.

I know what I saw.

I just don’t think he’s lying either.
