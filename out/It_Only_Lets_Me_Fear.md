It came not at night, when every shadow gets a tangible shape.   
When every drop of water in the pipes becomes blood  
or creak a sound of nails scratching against the floor.  
  
It came through my mother. Through a smile that shone my way  
just a tad too wide for what a human should be able to do.   
A smile that coldly contrasted against the scorching noon outside.  
  
‘You taste delicious’ It said, and I turned away.   
I was seeing things. I’m always seeing things.   
Fear is my natural state of being.

Time passes. Visions and sounds sometimes get worse, sometimes better. Work hit me hard. I’m too tired for fear. 

‘You shouldn’t be’ It whispers.   
  
I decide to be deaf again. I have already argued with the voices in my head plenty of times. Not giving them attention makes them go away. Makes me go away. I know each voice is me.

My keyboard clicks by itself in response.

Lack of sleep makes my head stronger.   
  
It makes me see figures again. Just pieces of limbs hiding in the corner of my eye, a feeling of presence in the room. 

I know that it’s my brain, but the knowledge only helps when I’m too tired to care. I try to stay tired.  
  
Dreams are the worst. Sleep strips away every defense. No work, no noise, no reality to hide behind. Just an open door.

I often open my eyes to a dark circular room. I’m surrounded by tight rooms with steel bars. Each of them holds a person taking their own life in a different way. They don’t speak. I don’t speak. We both watch each other as life leaves their bodies, only for their flesh to squirm back into shape again each time. 

Sometimes it’s my loved ones in those cells. It laughs as I listen to their screams. They never die out and their begging doesn’t stop.  
  
The only relief are the dreams where I die. Death marks my freedom.

‘Feed me’ I heard it whisper, before my mother slipped from a chair she was standing on and twisted her ankle. That was the first time fear made me physically ill.   
  
I moved out soon after. Just me, my cats and whatever followed me that day. 

Without others around, It became much more direct. Doors opening by themselves, items falling, knocks coming from the walls inside. Physical evidence that it’s not my imagination.  
  
After a few months, I’ve grown accustomed enough to become stupid. I thought It had no real power, so I stopped being afraid. That same week I opened my apartment doors to the sight of one of my cats on the kitchen table, his teeth and eyes missing. 

‘Trophies’ It said. This time, the voice wasn’t coming from my head. I instinctively turned my gaze towards the source and saw it for the first time. There were drops of blood leading to the air vent, where a pair of yellow, bloodshot eyes was looking straight at me through the gaps. 

I cried for weeks. There’s a smell of bleach coming from the vents now. The same one I used to scrub the table clean.

It started coming to me at night. There were no lips on its face, just rows of rotten teeth. Its skin was wrinkled and darkened, as if smeared with mud. Its hair grew in patches, mixed with bald spots and pieces of rotten skin.   
  
What always struck me the most were the eyes. Yellow, happy and intense. Like it couldn’t contain the excitement to see me. The same eyes that laughed at me from the vent. It would sit on my bed or hang above me and run its sharp fingers against my skin.   
  
My whole body would freeze in those moments.  
‘Fear me’ It always demanded. And I did. I really fucking did.

But it wasn’t enough. Sometimes it would deem my fear unsatisfying and that’s when others suffer. It started with a car accident where my parents barely made it alive, then a cancer diagnosis and finally my friend’s lethal overdose.  
  
So I have to fear it, to feed it. I have to constantly live in a mockery of life where any misstep can make me lose people that matter to me. And I can’t do it. I can’t.   
  
Fear attracts it and keeps it close. A parasite that has learned my schedule, my people, the exact pitch of my screaming. It doesn't want me dead. It doesn't want me defeated. It just wants me here, in this apartment, waiting for morning that never quite fixes anything. 

It only lets me fear.
