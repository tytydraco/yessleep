I remember the time I felt it. Like a hand closing around my throat just for a second while I was scrolling through some old forum threads late at night. It was years ago back when I was obsessed with Mandela Effects. I would read about how people swore that Berenstain Bears was spelled differently or that the Monopoly guy had a monocle that he never actually wore. My stomach dropped as I dug deeper questioning if my own memories were reliable. Were they mine or just glitches in whatever this reality's

The Mandela Effects really got to me. I mean, what if my memories were not even mine? What if they were mistakes in the way things are? This feeling came back stronger week. I was browsing subreddits killing time, when I stumbled on a post about glitches in the matrix. Nothing new right? People claiming the world stuttered for a heartbeat. Colors inverting, sounds warping, everything feeling wrong before snapping. I laughed it off at first like those chain emails from school that promised bad luck if you did not forward them. They were stupid. There was always that tiny what if spark before I hit delete.

This time though it was not a joke. The post mentioned something called the Weaver. Not a person, not a monster. A pattern. The Weaver forms when enough people become aware of the hidden truth at once. Each new mind that grasps the Weaver weaves another thread into the whole. The more who know. And share the Weaver. The stronger it gets until it is complete.. Once the Weaver is done... I do not know. The post did not say. It just warned that ignoring the Weaver deciding not to pass it on by posting about it yourself kills you in this universe. Snaps your thread clean.. You do not stay dead. Your awareness jumps to another branch another version of reality where you did post about the Weaver spreading the word to keep the pattern growing. Except you know what happened. Because for a split second you see yourself lying there gone.

The pain is not physical. It is all psychological starting as this weird tightening sensation in your neck when you even consider closing the tab without sharing the Weaver. Like fingers pressing in building pressure the longer you resist. It makes your breath catch your thoughts doubt, fear, that insistent urge to just hit post and make it stop. I felt it the moment I tried to walk. That grip in my mind squeezing, turning hesitation into something until I gave in and shared the Weaver here.

I did not believe it at first. I closed the tab went to bed.. The feeling did not fade. The feeling grew, that phantom pressure in my neck turning into on panic like my own thoughts were strangling me from the inside. Everything went black. No warning. Just the vise clamping down my heart seizing in terror. I collapsed onto the floor, vision blurring, the room spinning under the strain.. Then... I was back. Standing over my body sprawled out cold on the carpet eyes vacant skin paling. It was me. The version who had refused to post about the Weaver. Dead from what looked like an aneurysm triggered by the unrelenting psychological torment of fighting the Weaver.. I was not in that body anymore. I was in this one, the one from the branch where I had chosen to share the Weaver all. The air felt thicker the colors off like a skewed copy of my apartment.

I panicked, checked my pulse. Racing. Alive. Grabbed my phone; time had jumped a minutes like a blackout.. I knew it was not. I had died. Switched. That body lingered in my vision for a second before the universe smoothed it away. No mess, no trace, an empty floor. The neck pressure vanished the instant I accepted this reality the one where the story was already there.

The morning I still felt echoes of it. Weird glitches: texts vanishing, browser history blank where the original post about the Weaver should be. Searching the Weaver turned up nothing.. I remembered.. I knew refusing again would bring back that tightening in the neck that psychological prod forcing the choice: share the Weaver or snap.

That afternoon I told a friend over coffee. He listened, went quiet said it felt familiar. Later he texted a link to another buried thread. Idea, different words. Someone else who had felt the pull of the Weaver posted to ease it. Described the snap: the neck grip building until you either spread the Weaver or glimpse your own corpse as you jump threads.

Now I am unable to stop. Every time I think about not mentioning the Weaver the sensation returns faintly a reminder. The Weaver is patient waiting to see if I will keep adding threads.

If you are reading this and that weird feeling is starting in your neck. The press when you consider scrolling past without posting about the Weaver. Do not ignore it. The Weaver is already noticing. I am terrified of what happens when the Weaver finishes.... When someone, like you becomes the loose end staring at their own dead body in another life.

I posted this to keep the Weaver going. Maybe it will help. Maybe it will just pull you in too.
