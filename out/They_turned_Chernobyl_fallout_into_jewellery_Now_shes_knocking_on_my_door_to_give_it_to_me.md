I wasn't even born back then, but people in our village still repeat this story to this day, and it keeps coming back to me, but more on that at the end. We had this old drunkard called Vanya. Everyone drinks around here, but they used to say Vanya’s liver had evaporated in alcohol fumes ages ago, yet he lived a long life. Sometime after Chernobyl blew up, Vanya vanished for a few weeks. People thought he’d drunk himself to death and wasn't coming back, they were even bidding on his shack, whoever offered the most moonshine would get it.

Then one day, and it was scorching hot, the kind of heat where dust just rises from the ground, Vanya walks into the village. He wasn't even staggering, meaning he was sober. He had some sort of pouch in his hands and wouldn't show anyone what was inside. He went into his shack, since they hadn't managed to clear anything out of it yet, hid the pouch, and didn't come out for a few days. So the local blokes gathered and went over to his place with some moonshine because they figured if Vanya wasn't drinking, he must be ill. They stayed at his place the whole evening.

When they left him, he couldn't remember a thing, and they had taken the pouch. He spent a week looking for it while it passed from house to house, and nobody knew how to sell it to make a profit. My nan said it was even in our cellar for three days next to the jars of pickled gherkins. And that's how it went for about a year, while Vanya stopped looking and lost his mind, though he kept saying it was the fortune of his life.

Eventually, some scientists from America arrived. They stayed with us the whole summer. One chemist took a liking to our Zoya, and nobody else wanted her because she wasn't keen on hard work. Everyone was shaking their heads in disbelief, but the girl’s dad was thrilled and sold the pouch to the chemist, and built a brand new house within a year. Our Zoya was supposed to go to America with the chemist, so he took the pouch to a jeweller, and within a week, Zoya had an engagement ring, a wedding band, earrings, and a necklace made out of whatever was inside, and there was still enough left for more rings. She walked around dressed up like a Christmas tree, and that's how she left with him as his wife, with the rest of the treasures packed in her suitcase.

But they only made it to where the Polish-Lithuanian border is today. They took her suitcase and left her only with what she was wearing. They took Zoya to some convent in Lithuania first, and then the chemist built her a house nearby and gave her servants, while he himself vanished. Every day she would ask "When will the master return?", but nobody would tell her anything. Her face and hands glowed, so people in the village said she was not of this world.

She lived for a few more years, and then she came back to our village. I was already born by then, and Vanya passed away sometime later. Zoya still walks around our village to this day, asking who will take her treasures and take over the mission, but we steer clear of her. She’s the only person who knocks before entering my shack. But I don't open the door.
