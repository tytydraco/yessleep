I grew up in the town of Detroit Lakes Minnesota, a peculiar little town, nearing the border that leads to North Dakota, where not much happens down there, near the outskirts of town where I lived, there was a large chunk of wood, that's where the oak trees reside.

my grandparents lived within walking distance, my father would frequently have me stay overthrew until I was old enough to be by myself, he worked at a local logging company, so for most days he was at work.

My grandmother told me that I was allowed to play all i wanted in the backyard, but I was to never go into the woods, if I accidentally knocked a ball even 3 feet Into the woods, I was to grab her or my grandfather to get it for me, at first I believed she was just being overprotective, a tendency of hers that would annoy me a bit.

When I was 5, I was playing with a toy plane my father bought for me, it was later in the day, not yet dark, but nearing sunset.

I briefly looked into the woods where I saw a lone flame, it appeared to be a candlelight, it was my grandfather, he was roaming the woods, I called out to him, only for him to yell at me to go inside, normally he was a calm and patient man, this was the first time I've ever heard him talk in an aggressive tone.

When my father picked me up that night, my grandparents looked visibly distraught, I was in the car the whole time, when my father came in he didn't say a word, but I saw a single tear going down his face.

He never told me what my grandparents told him.

I wish that's where I could end this tale, but sadly it gets worse.

When I was 7 I befriended another child my age, we rode the same schoolbus, we'd frequently go to his home after school, his parents were kind people, they clearly loved their son, but one day he stopped showing up to school, my father and I went to their home, where his mother was a wreck, apparently he walked into the woods, but never returned, strangely there was never a proper search party, and years later when I looked up his name, the official report said he died in a car accident. 

for the next 5 years I wasn't allowed out of anyone's sight, I was no longer allowed to play in the backyard of my grandparents, but when I looked out a window into the backyard, I saw what looked like 2 yellow eyes, not starting ay me, but more at the house, I thought it was just a cat, there were a lot of strays in the area, so I didn't think much of it.

When I was 12 years old, my father and I moved into town, we were no longer living on the outskirts, so I was allowed to be more independent, I'd frequently bike to a local park, which was more out of the way, closer to where my grandparents lived, I'd always go with a couple of friends, it became our hangout, the rusted up merry-go-round to the slide that was broken in half, it seemed like no one has visited the playground in over 3 decades.

later that same year I went to visit my grandparents, I was helping them move things in their basement, when I accidentally dropped a box full of pictures, I looked at some of the pictures and they were of my dad, I've seen pictures of my dad as a kid before, so I know what he looked like, but in all the other photos there was another kid, I was taken aback, because I never knew I had an uncle, I put all the pictures back in the box, and later that night I asked my dad about it.

He just told me that he'd tell me when I was older, that I wouldn't understand if he told me then.

the next time I went to the abandoned playground, I went on my own, I was just there to be by myself for a while, I was reading a book, the light coming from my phones flashlight, when suddenly my phone turned off, I charged my phone to full battery before going to the park, so I knew it wasn't just me, I decided to make my way back home when suddenly I heard a voice, one I never heard before calling out to me, i turned around and it was those same yellow eyes I saw all those years ago.

it was shrouded in darkness, it's eyes being 2 light orbs, the only visible details of The Thing, i was frozen in fear, then it spoke to me.

"I have been watching you" it said, in a deep voice.

it was at that moment that I got on my bike. and went back to town, pedaling like I've never pedaled before, when I got back home my father was there waiting, he had a knowing look, he told me that he didn't want to tell me this soon, but The Thing I encountered, is what took his brother and my friend.

he told me that back in the 1980's that thing took my brother, when my grandfather went looking for him, he found the creature, a dark lit candle on the ground where my brothers clothes were, my grandfather grabbed the candle, when suddenly he heard a voice telling him to put it down, The Thing tried to grab the candle from my grandfather, but my grandfather was able to get to the house, where The Thing called out to my grandfather, but my grandfather didn't listen to it.

the candle is still lit to this day, all those years ago when I saw him in the woods, he was keeping watch, making sure The Thing wouldn't get me too.

I never went to that playground again, is what I wish I could say.

1 year later, after me and my friend group stopped visiting that playground, one of my friends, Audrey went missing, no one bothered searching  except for me, and my other 2 friends, Ronald and Mike, we decided to look for her the night she vanished, being pissed off that nobody was looking for her.

each of our parents tried to stop us, whether it was under threats of being grounded or being sent to military school or whatever else, but the 3 of us felt like we were the only ones who gave a crap about her, not even her own parents were out there.

suddenly I got the idea of visiting the abandoned playground, where the 4 of us always went to hang out, I told my friends that I was going alone, somehow something inside me knew she was there.

when I went, I saw Audrey, unconscious on the ground, The Thing, was standing over here, it saw me and started approaching me.

"We meet once more..." it said to me.

"What are you doing to her?" I asked, fear In my voice

"It's nothing personal, it's just what I need to do."

"what does that mean? what are you?"

I then noticed something, Audrey was sinking into the earth.

"it seems like it's already starting" The Thing told me.

I didn't want to lose another friend, so I offered a Deal to The Thing.

"If you let me save her, I will take her place, whatever you plan to do to her. you can do to me instead, please just let her go!!!" I pleaded, desperation in my voice, pleading with The Thing.

"i do suppose... you do have more meat on you, if you can grab her body before it sinks, I will accept your offer"

I rushed to her, I grabbed her. and started pulling her out, I got her out, I was ready to accept my fate, when I heard a familiar voice coming from the woods... I saw the candle.

My Grandfather heard the commotion, he came and saw me holding Audreys Body in my arms, with The Thing standing in front of me.

"You will not claim my Grandson" he said to The Thing

"Perhaps then... you should give me the candle..." it replied

The Candle, clearly the candle meant something.

"This thing? This is where you put my son is it not? this was on his remains when I found it, you put your victims into this candle."

"T-That is... correct, every new soul i claim, keeps that candle lit, it's where they reside, if the candle goes out, so do they, this is why I must feed."

One thing didn't make sense to me The Thing stuttered for a creature that sound so sure of itself, it seemed like it was trying to come up with a proper response.

"It's Lying." I told my Grandfather. "The candle doesnt contain their souls, it contains The Thing's soul, it's trying to trick you" 

Suddenly The Thing let out a blood-curdling scream, where it attacked my Grandfather, the candle dropped to the ground, I placed Audrey's body on the ground, I made my way to the candle.

I picked it up, and The Thing looked at me, i could see the fear in it's eyes.

"Child, give me the candle." it said to me.

I didn't say anything, I was about to blow out the candle when I felt a sharp pierce in my stomach, The Thing stabbed me in the belly, with a single sharp claw, I was drifting in and out of consciousness, but before I drifted out, I saw my grandfather pick up the candle, then I fell asleep.

I woke up, in the hospital the next week, I was comatose for that time, when I woke up, Audrey was in the same room, waiting for me to wake, she apparently woke up after I fell unconscious, she called 9/11 who rushed and took us both to the hospital, I was told the stab wound luckily wasn't too severe and that Audrey stopped the bleeding, but one thing she told me has always stuck out to me.

I'm 22 now. writing this story, and I will always remember what she said to me "when I woke up, there was nothing around but Me, You, and a blown out candle."
