“DO NOT ENTER”, warned the faintly-lit red neon sign on the dorm door across the hall. Its electric glow painted the old tile floor and white concrete walls. The sign was there when I moved in but I didn’t really notice it until my first night in my new dorm room, when the flickering red light crept under the door in the darkness. I tried to sleep until a loud but distant noise jolted me awake. I sat up in a sudden rush of fear, feeling panic set in.

“Caroline,” I whispered shakily, hoping my roommate was awake.   
No response.  
 My throat clenched as Caroline’s silence swallowed the whole room. I sat for a moment before calling again, this time a little louder.  
“Caroline?”  
Nothing.  
I peeled back the covers and cautiously made my way to the bed across the room. It was empty. A chill crept up my spine as my face went cold. I darted back to my bed, jumping under the covers. 

My clammy hands trembled as I attempted to find the knob on my bedside lamp.  
***click***  
A dingy yellow light partially lit the room, casting shadows across every wall. I peered over at Caroline’s bed again, confirming its emptiness. The dim red light continued to flicker under the door as I sat silently. *She’s just using the restroom,* I assert to myself. There was a shared bathroom at the end of the dormitory hallway. Caroline must be there. 

I decided that I would feel safer if I waited until Caroline returned before trying to sleep again. I curled up on my side, my eyes locked on the empty bed across the room. I grabbed my phone in an attempt to distract myself from the darkness slowly asphyxiating me. The minutes slipped by as my anxiety grew.  
***1:11 AM***  
*What could she possibly be doing in there for so long?*   
The question hung in my head as a familiar icy chill slithered through my spine. 

I typed out a text to Caroline.  
“are you ok?”  
*Send*.

*buzz*

I nearly snapped my neck to look over at the door in front of me. I could have sworn the buzz of Caroline’s phone came from there. I crawled to the end of my bed, peaking over the edge. There was a dim white light accompanying the ever present red one in the hallway. My heart dropped to my stomach as the white light went away, leaving the sickening red flicker alone again. 

I slowly slipped out of my bed, feeling the cold tile on my feet again. My body trembled as the dread set it. My shaky hand met the handle of the door as the sound of my ragged breathing filled my ears. I turned the knob, hesitantly pulling the door open.

*creeeaakk*

I pulled the door back completely before looking up and making eye contact with the sign.   
“DO NOT ENTER”, I read as my heart pounded before looking down. Caroline’s phone sat face up at my feet. I leaned out, peering down the dark hallway and saw the pale glow from the bathroom, barely trickling into the darkness.

Caroline’s phone went off again, creating the same loud buzz against the solid floors. I looked at the cracked screen, displaying a message from an unknown number.  
“Hello?”   
I picked up the phone and hesitantly scrolled up. More messages flooded the screen from the same unsaved number.  
“Helloooo”  
“Answer.”  
“Are you okay?”  
*buzz*  
A new text from the same number.  
“Go back to bed.”  
My heart sank as I turned my head, scanning the length of the hallway. There was no movement but I could feel that I wasn’t alone, like there was something out of sight waiting for me to read the text. I gripped Caroline’s phone and swiftly made my way back into my dorm, trembling before slamming the door and triple checking the locks. I silenced the phone before throwing it on Caroline’s bed and returning to mine once again. I sat up in bed trying to rationalize this situation in my head until I eventually drifted off.

Shortly after though, something pulled me from my sleep. A faint shuffling, almost like someone was dragging their feet, walking down the hallway. I blinked awake, already feeling my heart drop to my stomach. The sound continued, each step sounding heavier, louder, closer. I slowly slid out of the bed, trying not to make a sound. The shuffling stopped suddenly as I exited my bed. I held my uneasy breath as I approached the door- not to open it, just to listen. I cupped my hand around my ear and listened into the hallway through the door.

*knock knock knock*

3 faint knocks, not on the door, but on the wall. I flew back, slamming my body against the bed, making a loud banging sound.  
   
I dropped to the floor, facing the door, my knees pressed against my chest as my hands clamped over my ears. The knocks didn’t sound like they came from the other side of the door. It was as if someone was standing in the room and knocked on the wall right beside my head. 

Suddenly, the sound of faint breathing replaced the sickening silence of the room. It was coming from Caroline’s bed. I uncovered my face, my eyes were met with the familiar flickering red light. My body refused to allow me to investigate the now heavy breathing. 

*buzz*

Caroline’s phone went off again, despite having been silenced. The breathing ceased. I murmured a quick prayer under my breath before gathering the courage to finally glance at Caroline’s bed. It was untouched, there was nobody and nothing, except the newly lit up phone. I stood up and made my way to the phone, barely able to walk from terror and exhaustion. My legs felt like they were treading through deep water. My eyes met the brightly lit, mangled screen again, there was a new text from the same number.  
“You’re not alone.”

It was getting harder to breathe as my heart slammed against my chest. This had to be a dream, a choking nightmare i’d wake up from any minute.

*buzz*

Another text, same number.   
“Why’d you have to open the door?”  
I took a shaky step back, feeling the heavy pressure of wading water again, slow and helpless. 

*buzz…*

I flinched, the phone lit up again. I hesitantly peaked over at the screen.  
“You let it in.”  
I felt an intense shiver as my body started shaking, my skin grew colder. I loved scary stories from a safe distance but hated the suffocating feeling of being in one. My body wasn’t in fight or flight, just paralyzing fear. The silence came with an unbearable feeling of dread, almost like it was also listening.

Beneath it, something moved. Breathing, not heavy this time, just loud enough to barely hear it over my own. My eyes snapped to the corner of the room where Carolines desk was. The chair was slightly turned toward me. A jagged shadow was casted against the wall as the chair swayed slightly, creaking with every movement. 

I trembled as I listened to the synchronized creaks and slow breaths before they both suddenly stopped at once. I forced my legs to move, cautiously making my way towards the chair. I scooted it aside, revealing nothing but empty space under the desk. 

I saw a red light out of the corner of her eye, not beneath the door this time but reflecting in the mirror. I turned slowly to look at the mirror. In backwards letters, the same red sign projected a new message in the mirror.   
“DON’T TRUST HER.”  
The light from my lamp dimmed slightly for a second. It didn’t flicker, it dimmed as if something had moved between me and the jaundiced bulb. The sign in the mirror disappeared.

*buzz*

I flinched once again at the sound of a new message. I attempted to stand up. It felt like my legs were broken, the blood in my veins felt ice cold. I eventually was able to get up and on my feet with the help of the desk chair, making the same creaking noise as before. I approached the phone again, tapping the screen. It was empty, no new messages and all the old ones were gone. My eyes widened, I turned slightly, seeing my phone screen brightly lit on my bed. My heart dropped.

I quietly shuffled over to my bed as fast as my body could go. I picked up my phone, revealing a new message. It was from Caroline.  
“Heyyyy it’s Caroline. I lost my key. Can you come let me in the room?”  
I looked over at Caroline’s phone confused before noticing a new sound in that moment, something in the still silence. A heavy door opening and closing in the distance, as if someone just entered their dorm floor from the stairwell. The sound of slow footsteps flooded my ears. They were getting closer every second until they stopped. I glanced over, now seeing a shadow blocking the red glow from under the door.

*knock knock knock*

I jumped with every tap then stood still, hoping whatever it was would go away. 

“Helena, let me in.”  
It was Caroline’s voice.   
*knock knock knock*  
I stood like a statue, trying hard to hold my breath and keep my trembling body still.   
The shadow under the door moved. There was an obvious shuffling noise before the red light was almost completely covered. The figure crouched down and put its head to the floor as if it was peaking under the door. It moved again, returning to its original position.

*buzz*

I received a new text from “Caroline”.  
“I see you”

I, still frozen in place, locked my eyes on the shadow beneath the door. My breath was shallow, nearly silent while I waited for the figure to move again. Whoever was on the other side knocked again, starting quietly, but slowly speeding up and getting louder, more urgent. Then, it stopped as suddenly as it started. The silence had returned, even more dreadful than before.

Then the sound of footsteps. Dragging slowly across the cold tiles, drawing closer every second. 

*knock knock*

“Helena… please let me in, I’m scared.” It was still Caroline’s voice, trembling, she sounded desperate. My mind raced once again, thinking back to the warning I received. Was Caroline the person the mirror was referring to? The footsteps grew uncomfortably close. “Please don’t do this Helena.”  
Caroline’s voice said, louder but still shaky. I made the reluctant decision to let her in. Still shaking in fear, I walked over to the door, unlocking it and turning the knob slowly. 

I stood in fear while the door creaked open. Before I could even look out I heard the door across the hall slam, accompanied by a muffled scream.

The hallway was empty. The red sign shook from the movement of the door. My heart was in my stomach and my legs trembled. I reluctantly looked up and down the hallway once again. There was nothing out of the ordinary. I stepped back in the room and slammed and locked the door behind me, then slowly sat on the floor again with my legs to my chest, trying to catch my breath. I closed my eyes trying to calm my nerves before dozing off once again. 

*buzz*

I blinked awake, remembering what I was scared of again. This time in complete darkness, my lamp had somehow gotten turned off, but there was a dim light coming from my bed. It was my phone again. I pulled myself up on my bed frame and made my way to the phone again. I felt as if I was moving in slow motion. When I finally got to the bed there was no notification on my phone but I did notice the time.   
***1:11 AM***

HOW? How has no time gone by? How is this possible? What exactly is it that I am dealing with here? Where is Caroline? 

*buzz*

There was a text this time. It was from a Caroline.   
“Why Helena”

*creakkkk*

I turned my head quickly, looking at the door, but it wasn’t my door, it was coming from the hall. As I was looking over I saw something terrifying. The sign had turned off. There was no longer a red glow creeping from under the door.

*creakkkk*

*buzz*

“enter”  
It was an unknown number. This felt like a trick. I thought I might be losing my mind but I figured that if I didn’t listen to whatever this thing was then something bad might happen to Caroline. Just as I was trying to gather my thoughts and decide the best course of action I heard another whisper, this time, from right behind me, in my ear.  
“go”

I felt a shiver down my spine. I couldn’t do this alone. I thought there was a real chance that Caroline might be in danger so i did what i thought would be best, calling the police. I gripped my phone and dialed 911. The phone rang twice before I heard a woman’s soft voice on the other side of the line,   
“911, what’s the location of your emergency?” 

“He-hello my name is Helena Stein I need to report a missing person. I am at Staton University, building C, room 341.” I responded with my shaky voice.

“Alright Helena, are you in danger?” she said, sounding concerned.

“I am not sure. I don’t feel safe” I said in between voice cracks.

“You shouldn’t.” she responded before the line disconnected.

I pulled the phone from my ear in shock. My shaking hands only got worse as a million thoughts raced in my mind. What was I supposed to do other than go across the hall? 

Up until this point I hadn’t even thought about trying to leave. I’ve been too scared to think straight. I put on my sneakers and grabbed my phone, keys and switchblade before opening the door once again. A little quicker and quieter this time. I shut and locked the door behind me, making my way down the hall to the big double doors that lead to the stairs. Once I got there I pushed on the bar, trying to open the door but it was locked. I shook the door and banged my body against but nothing helped. I stood still for a second trying to think of any way i could get out until I heard a familiar sound, dragging foot steps coming up the stairs. 

My chest burned with anxiety. I turned and raced back to my room. I got to the door and jiggled the handle before remembering it was locked. I quickly pulled my key ring out of my pocket, searching through the keys with my trembling hands only to realize my room key was gone. Every other key was in place but the one I needed was just gone. I buried my head in my hands, feeling so hopeless, starting to cry softly.

*creakkkk*

I turned quickly, my vision was met with the door across the hall, now open, presenting me with a room full of darkness. This was my only option. I had to go in and try to save Caroline myself. I walked over to the door slowly. The fear of both the known and unknown overwhelmed me. My heart started racing, then palpitating. Until my vision went blurry and I fainted.

I woke up to a new startling sound but this time I was in my bed, the hallway light shining in, painted onto the concrete walls. The police were busting down my door.   
“DROP THE WEAPON AND GET ON THE GROUND NOW! HANDS BEHIND YOUR BACK!”

I looked down at my body, which was now covered in blood. Blood soaked hoodie and sneakers, my switchblade in my right hand, also drenched. I dropped my knife before sliding off my bed onto the floor that had a new red hue. I laid on my stomach and put my hands behind my back as ordered. The officers aggressively pulled me up by my wrists and led me into the hallway. 

I found Caroline. There she was, lying on the cold white tile in the door way of the room across the hall. She was covered in blood and ghostly pale, not moving. The neon red sign was gone. She was dead. 

“CAROLINE!” I exclaimed while trying to get to her. The officers pulled me back, gripping my arms and shoulders tighter.  
“WHAT DID THEY DO TO HER?!” I yelled with my raspy voice.

They try to say i’m crazy but I know what happened that night. During sentencing the judge didn’t care to hear what I had to say. I honestly don’t remember much from the trial besides the prosecutor saying Caroline’s estimated time of death, 1:11 AM. I was apparently quote “not guilty by reason of insanity.” So here I am in this ward still trying to prove my innocence. It’s okay though, I’ve spoken to Caroline and she believes me.
