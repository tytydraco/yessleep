I was closing. It was 5pm and I had just finished my shift at the gift shop for our town’s dam. By no means is it a particularly unique dam, it’s a tall grey curved blockade with a flat concrete base where some kind of pumping system is set up. 

To be honest I don’t really know why it exists, I wasn’t paying full attention during my training shift and my best guess is it helps keep certain species of salmon separate. Although that guess is based entirely on the fact that we stock a lot of salmon themed birthday cards. 

I was unlocking my bike I had chained around a lamppost, when I noticed a figure standing at our viewing platform. At that point, the entire place had closed so it couldn’t have been a visitor. Assuming it was one of the dam’s technicians enjoying the view, I called out.

“HEY!” My voice echoed across the dam, reverberating off the concrete mass.

Due to the distance I couldn’t quite make out his face, but his head turned. Quickly, looking at me.

“Enjoying the view?!” I asked.

*He didn’t respond.*

I shrugged, got on my bike and headed home. 

Past the fork in the path that led to the next town over, down the hill that continued into the town centre and through the fields that stretched before our house.

The journey home was 30 minutes long, spanning the entire width of my town with the dam being at its most western point and my house being at its most eastern. In fact, myself and my grandparents lived just before the sign reading;

**“NOW ENTERING NIMEL, POPULATION 800!”** 

The sign was quite old with the population being about 15 years out of date and its left pole’s polish being worn away by the string my grandmother would drape our wet clothes over. 

From the sign it’s a short walk to the garage where I keep my bike locked and where my grandfather spends his evenings when he isn’t working as a volunteer fire fighter. 

I entered, there he sat. On the worn sofa chair he had placed in front of a CRT my grandmother had bought him 20 odd years ago.

“How’s the game?” I said, wheeling my bike in. He was watching college football.

“Could be worse, how was work?” He responded, not taking his eyes off the screen.

“The dam broke and the entire town’s gonna flood.” I replied, leaning my bike in an empty corner of the room.

“Heh. Oh no! The salmon!” He sarcastically cried, before taking a sip of his beer.

“Night.” 

“Night” I left and headed to the house's kitchen. 

Inside was my grandmother, sewing the last of a series of buttons onto a lattice shirt. Around three years ago she started a clothes mending business, it wasn’t particularly profitable, in fact she often did work for free. But I think she enjoyed the busy work. 

“Leftovers are in the fridge sweetie, I got a pint of Neapolitan in the freezer.” She said as I entered, folding the shirt then looking up to give me her full attention 

“Thanks Grammie” 

“How was work, hun?” she responded.

“It was alright, lunch was good thank yo-“

Grammie gasped.

“Oh. My. God. Sandra- the one you worked with” she cut me off then began rifling around in the adjacent laundry basket.

“Yes. Sandra.” I said, my grandmother had a sweet spot for gossip. 

“Well, her brothers engaged to that samson girl, the one you had a little thing for in 8th grade.”

“Uh… Becky?” I asked taking the leftover lasagna and placing it in the microwave. 

“Yes! Rebecca. I offered to do any repairs for them, y’know with the youngen on the way.” She continued. “And look!” 

She pulled out a blue infant’s onesie. 

“Isn’t it darling?” She said grinning.

“Yep” I responded. The microwave dinged and I took my dinner out of it.

“When do you think you’ll-”

“Not till after college grammie” I cut her off.

“Hmm, Just haven't met the right girl yet. Plenty of fish in the sea, hun” She smiled.

I chuckled lightly, took my food and headed to my room, not long after my grandparents went to bed, myself following soon after.

I awoke at about 3am. I still had my eyes shut but there was noise faintly echoing through the house. 

*Thump*.

*Thump.*

*Thump.*

I tried to ignore it at first but it persisted. I assumed someone had left the backdoor open and the wind was blowing it to and thro.

I looked outside, flashing blue and red lights lit up the road outside our house. We’d often get people going well past the speed limit before they entered nimel as a chunk of it technically isn’t either town. (My room is kinda in that chunk)

I put some shorts on and left my room. The noise immediately became clearer as I entered the upstairs hallway. 

*Thump* 

*Thump*

*Thump*

To the right was the staircase.

To the left was my grandparents bedroom, its door shut.

It shook with each thump, like a giant hand was knocking against it. 

“Grammie?” I called.

*Thump*

*Thump*

*Thump*

“Grandpa?” 

*Thump*

*Thump*

*Thump*   
.  
“You guys… okay in there?”

*Nothing*.

I walked towards the door, I’d rather know everything was okay and intrude on them than one of them having fallen and needing help.

I pulled down on the handle and pushed the door forward. But was met with resistance. The door didn't have a lock, so something had to be blocking it.

“Guys? You okay?”

*Still nothing.*

I pushed the door harder, yet I was met with even more resistance. I had managed to open a crack in the door, through which I saw an empty bed.

“Hello?” I called out. 

Through the crack I heard a more audible sound. The shuffling of feet. Not pacing or walking around a room but repeated steps between each thump against the door.

I pushed against the door harder, millimetre by millimetre then the thumping stopped. 

The pressure relented ever so slightly, but I still couldn’t move the door. Something emerged in the gap, from the dark.

The now slack jawed glossy eyed face of my grandmother.

Her warm smile was gone and she looked at me like I was a stranger. No, like an animal. An alien. A strange thing that didn’t belong.

“Grandma? Are you guys okay? Is grandpa okay?” I asked, still pushing against the door.

*“goodnight dear.”* she said without moving her mouth. It felt like they weren’t words. 

“Are you… okay? What’s going on? You gonna be alright to go back to bed? Do you want me to call Dr Connell?”

She looked to her left and spoke once more without moving her mouth *“goodnight dear”*

The pressure against the door relented and swung open, if I hadn’t steadied myself I would’ve fell with it.

On the other side was my grandfather, his face identical to my grandmother's, his eyes which were typically squinted from years of working in the sun were now wide open revealing two glossy eyes. His mouth agape as if it had no jawbones to hold it closed.

He groaned with no movement of his mouth *“sleep well”*

“What?” I responded. Before I could get an answer, if he could even give one, he pushed passed me my grandmother following suit. 

I stood there shocked? Or confused?

They just walked down the stairs. 

I watched from the landing as they met the front door. Without hesitation my grandfather threw his body at the locked door. Next to him my grandmother pressed her brittle hands, it wasn’t so much an attempt to push open a door but to move a large object.

*Thump* 

*Thump*

*Thump* 

I ran to get my phone, if they were sleep walking this repeated slamming surely was not good for the body. If they weren’t and this was some kind of episode then they needed help. 

*Thump*

*Thump* 

*Thump* 

I had no signal, no WiFi. Nothing.

*Thump*

*Thump* 

*Thump* 

I rushed downstairs to try the landline, past my grandparents and into the kitchen. I pulled it off the receiver.

*Thump* 

*Thump* 

*Thump*

Not a buzz? Or a tone to indicate it was being used else where. Just noise. I assumed the power was out.

*Thump*

*Thump* 

*Thump*

I looked back to my grandparents, my grandmother’s frail hands put pressure against the door as if trying to flatten the solid oak. Each time she placed them her wedding ring made a hollow knock against the surface. She wasn’t looking at the door, she wasn’t examining the wood to see if she should reapproach her efforts. She just did it. 

Like she was made to do it. Like a robot whose sole purpose was to apply weak, elderly pressure to the front door. 

My grandfather’s efforts had no more life in them then hers, each bash wasn’t fueled by rage or fear or anything that would motivate the action. He did it. He let his weighty albeit aged frame slam against the door, over and over.

*Thump* 

*Thump*

*Thump*

In this transfixed shock I neglected to notice a minor but awful detail. Their bedroom door swung inwards, meaning no matter how many near zombified slams or presses against the door they tried, they’d have to take the whole door down to get through.

*Thump*

*Thump*

*Thump*

The front door, when unlocked, swung outwards.

*Thump*

*Thump* 

**CRACK**.

Wood splintered out, my grandparents stop for barely a second. The door had broken off its lock and swung open.

My grandparents left the house, bare foot. My grandmother in her nighty, My grandfather in his pajamas.
