Markus tried to comfort her, but there was no use, soon enough she started screaming about a terrible headache, explaining it like she felt something crawling around inside her head. Having had enough, she grabbed a knife and slit her throat. Markus tried to stop her, but he just ended up cutting his hand while trying to pull the knife out of her hand. It was all so intense I could barely take in everything that happened in the time it happened.

After that, Markus cried his eyes dry, ”Why!?  Why, Mira!?”

He then went and grabbed Ravi by the collar of his shirt, ”What the fuck are you!? What do you want!? I know you’re in there!”

Ravi just begged him to stop, another sign that he was obviously not himself. If we weren’t in danger, Ravi would’ve insulted Markus and told him to let go, I can’t even imagine how he would’ve truly acted in this situation. That’s why neither me nor Petro stopped Markus when he grabbed the knife from the floor to kill whatever was pretending to be Ravi.

Ravi finally fought back, and aggressively too, bit and screamed in anger. But it didn’t stop Markus from repeatedly stabbing him in the chest. When he was done, I carefully approached them, Markus was shaken up and might’ve still somewhat blamed me for all of this, but my curiosity got the better of me. 

I asked him, ”Could you cut open his chest?”

He looked at me with tired eyes, ”Do it yourself.”

He fell to the floor, leaning against the counter, and laid the knife down in front of my feet. Before I picked up the knife, I grabbed two rubber dish gloves and improvised a face mask out of cloth and paper towels, I was not gonna go close to Ravi’s body without protection, I wouldn’t have to worry too much about my eyes as I had glasses. I cut him open and, as I expected, found growths physically similar to the ones we had taken samples from back at the whole. It looked like crimson, glowing fungus all over his lungs and abdominal organs, they were still moving, but faintly and slowed down progressively.

”Herregud.” Markus said.

It looked like the substance, although clearly dying within him, was still trying to spread. Considering Ravi had only been infected for at most a few hours, this thing must have an incredibly rapid growth speed.

”I’m an idiot.” Markus said, now significantly calmer.

”I have his blood all over me.”

”That doesn’t have to mean anything, certain liquid diseases require open wounds or orifices to actually transmit.”

”You wanna bet your life on it?”

I didn’t, and we also didn’t know if this was some sort of illness, it might just require the slightest of physical contact to enter your body. Obviously Jiro had been infected just by not using face protection.

”How many hours have passed since you called for help?” Markus asked.

”I don’t know, maybe 6 hours.” Petro said.

”You should leave, get to the moon pool and take the HOV to the surface, you can wait it out up there.” Markus said.

”Jiro is still somewhere around here.” I said.

”I know, that’s why this place is not safe.”

We argued for a while about not doing anything hasty, taking Markus with us or returning for him, but he protested against all of it. He just told us to go, be careful on our way to the moon pool, and leave him behind. Eventually we agreed to go without him, we suspected he killed himself as soon as we left, but we didn’t want to see that. On our way back to the moon pool, we saw no sign of anyone else while walking through the hallway.

On the way, Petro said something I did not expect, ”I’m sorry I took your pasta.”

”What?”

”I saw you wrote your name on it, but I ate it anyway.”

”Why are you saying this now?”

”I just though, if we’re gonna die, might as well get everything off my chest.”

I was compelled to say that we weren’t gonna die, but I don’t know if I really believed that anymore. ”It’s okay, I forgive you.”

”If we get back, I’ll pay for a restaurant dinner, the best pasta you ever taste. I promise.”

”That’s nice, but if you’re asking me out on a date, I’m not really interested.”

”Just want to repay. You’re really not my type.”

”You know, if I didn’t know you, I would’ve taken serious offence to that.”

”Sorry.”

”It’s okay.”

We had reached the moon pool, the HOV was parked where we left it, and when we were about to enter, Jiro came out from behind a box and attacked us. He had changed a lot in the time since I last saw him, his eyes were blood shot and his skin was pale, the veins in his face were glowing and it was covered in protruding spikes and sharp growths, I could only imagine what the rest of his body looked like underneath the clothes. He went for me and I grabbed his arms in an attempt to hold him off as he started rambling complete nonsense.

”Filthy pikaia, you left us, you all left us! How could a frail vertebrate overcome us, our perfection!? You all envied us, the crustaceans, cephalopods, the ctenophores, and you all left us!” 

His speech capabilities had certainly improved, but I had no idea what he was talking about. Petro grabbed Jiro and threw him to the side, kicked his face in until it was completely crushed. Under his skin, attached to his brain, were similar growths to the ones on the whole and the ones in Ravi’s body. We checked our bodies to see if he had left any marks or scratches on any of us, we couldn’t see anything, but Petro sure as hell could feel it. When he took a step closer to the HOV, his foot hurt, and looking under his shoe, one of the crimson spikes from Jiro’s face had penetrated Petro’s sole.

He nervously laughed while letting out a single, ”Shit.”

I fell to the floor and held my head in my hands, he also sat down in front of me, breathing heavily after finishing laughing. I was broken, I couldn’t handle it anymore, but Petro tried to convince me to enter the submersible craft.

”It’s probably best you go.”

I didn’t respond.

Without knowing why at the time, he just said, ”Yes, I’m afraid.”

”What?”

”You asked that question while in the HOV earlier. I know you weren’t talking to me, but I just wanted to give you an answer since I heard yours.”

I looked up, tears in my eyes, ”Why?”

”When I was young, I lived in Crimea with my family. We would often go to this beautiful beach at the Black Sea, one day my brother swam out into the water, further than he had ever swam before. A storm came in and he wasn’t back, the waves were to strong to go out looking for him, even with boat. When the storm had settled, we searched for days, but found nothing, not even a body. Since that day, I’ve been terrified of the ocean.”

”Then why are you here, there are mechanic jobs on land you know?”

”I know, but they don’t scare me.”

”You want to be scared, why?”

”I don’t know… maybe because we feel most alive when we’re scared, or maybe because I think I deserve it.”

”Why would you think you deserve it?”

”I don’t think I’m ready to share that story. How come you are here?”

”What do you mean? I’m a marine biologist, this is my job.”

”Yeah, why are you a marine biologist? Where did that interest come from?”

”My folks liked fishing, and I would often go with them to the coast. I liked looking for sea shells, shiny rocks, starfish, crabs, and various trinkets washed up by the waves. I developed an interest for the ocean, saw it as an endless source of treasures and mysteries, I wanted to solve some of those mysteries myself. I decided I wanted to become a marine biologist at an early age, not knowing how much of the business was just paperwork.”

He nodded slightly, ”That’s a nice story, good reasoning, but I think you should go now, for your own good.”

I insisted no, but he wouldn’t take that for an answer. He practically yet carefully forced me into the craft, eventually I didn’t resist. I regretfully left him there and took the HOV back to the surface, where I waited for another hour or so until I saw the DPEA ship in the horizon.

I’m now back in Kinsale, I still work for the DPEA, but nowadays I mainly stick to analysing species and specimens closer to land, as well as doing office paperwork. I still love the ocean, but after my experience at the Pontus Void, I decided some mysteries are better left as mysteries.
