Halloween...

Growing up in the city of Birmingham, England. Halloween for myself wasn't exactly something common, if ever in our fair city. Sure there were things like Samhain but speaking to most, they would refuse to celebrate the holiday, stating that originally it was a pagan holiday... or at least that's what I would be told by my parents and other peoples parents growing up.

As the years went by however, I began to notice the holiday becoming more frequent in peoples lives. With things like pumpkins being placed at the front of homes, decorations slowly lining the walls of local supermarkets and parents ushering their children along in cheap plastic costumes of the latest popular trend. I realised that times were starting to change since I was their age. Throughout my childhood I watched a lot of tv and like most children my age I was exposed to plenty of American television. Both cartoons and live shows that would always revel and enjoy the Halloween spirit with trick or treating and haunted houses and parties. 

The Simpsons of course were always a big influence with their constant show of their tree house of horror episodes... the one that parodied the Stephen King movie The Shining with Homer going mad? Always a personal fave. I must have seen that air on channel four as a kid many many times and I would watch it almost religiously.

Now an adult, I still lived at home being the ripe old age of twenty and with very few friends since leaving my school days behind, I spent a lot of time on the internet or walking the streets. Always looking for something to do or to just pass the time. It was on one of these walks, a few miles from my home that I came across a handmade sign. Battered but still new, hanging from a nearby phone pole and stapled into the wood. It wasn't anything special. Just a sign made from cardboard, with big words written in black marker.

“Come to our haunted house and celebrate with us your Halloween spirit” The sign stated, matter of fact.

Now I wouldn't deny that this did interest me. Although in recent years the shops and the city had slightly embraced the idea of Halloween, most of the older generation still refused to acknowledge it outside of it being “just another day” on the calendar. Most people would keep their lights off during the evening of Halloween just to avoid having children walking up to their door and knocking on it for sweets which nobody ever bought for the holiday. A haunted house however? That was right up my alley. I could finally celebrate the event I'd spent years seeing through the medium of television and see what Halloween could really be.

Looking over the sign, it had an address further in the city, with a time set for midnight. “One night only. Tonight.” Now I definitely couldn't pass this up. Maybe I turn up and I've already missed the house? Maybe it was tonight? If anything I would at least get a nice walk out of it. From my daily activities of walking the streets, I knew the general area the address stated but not the exact spot. Other than it was near the canals and water ways that ran throughout the inner workings of my home city, I couldn't think as to where the address was listed. Thank god for GPS.

Several hours later I'd changed clothes, grabbed my phone and wallet and headed out from the warmth of my home to the brisk cool autumn air of October. It wasn't exactly Halloween yet but close enough and the haunted house appeared to be a one night event. I had to go and experience this. Whether I get another chance isn't something I'll think about for now but I'm not risking the opportunity to finally experience the holiday.

I didn't have much money to my name, just a few pounds in my pocket and I wasn't planning on using that for bus fair so I decided to walk. It wasn't too far from my home, maybe an hour or so but I made the travel regardless. Looking at my phone I glanced the time. It was almost midnight, right when I assume the haunted house would start. The board did state it was tonight at midnight but whether that was another night or midnight just past I didn't know. But I would still head there and find out and if I'm lucky... I would have a real experience of haunting and frights.

A short time later I turned up near the stated address according to my phones GPS. I walked along the brickwork on the ground, laid out as a pathway alongside the canal near the outskirts of the Birmingham. Looking around it seemed strange. There were no houses here. Just several old buildings and run down stores. With a few alcoves dotted along the way, filled with beer cans and food wrappers. I looked at my phone again, the bright light illuminating my face. The app indicated I was one hundred feet from my destination just ahead of where I stood.

“This seems a little...” I muttered to myself before a figure stepped out from the shadows ahead of me. They waved their hand invitingly, almost friendly. I looked around nervously before stepped forwards towards the figure. I could make out a costume of sorts, maybe a zombie? It was hard to tell but it had become a common sight I'd noticed with Halloween. Considering how easy it was to put together the costume and with the amount of shows and films depicting the undead it was getting pretty popular.

I walked closer to the figure who stepped further out into the street lights with a tight rictus grin on their face and I responded with a stiff smile on my own.

“Hey I'm... here for the haunted house?” I asked nervously, looking around for other people. The figure refrained to respond, merely looking at my with the same smile and waved their arm towards a door built into the side of the wall alongside the canal walkway. A cardboard sign was stuck to the front, with that same black marker stating “Haunted House! Tonight Only at Midnight!”. I guessed this was the place but with nobody else here it was a little unnerving to say the least.

I wanted to ask my new would be zombie friend if anybody else was here but they merely shuffled me along. They moaned briefly and gestured to the door, opening it for me to enter. I guessed this was part of the experience so cautiously I obliged with the undead doorman and walked through the old narrow door frame.

As I left the outside behind me, the door was closed with a soft thud and a loud audible click behind me. I turned to glance at the door and then back. I was locked in. I shook my head with a small nervous chuckle. Typical spooky cliches. I bet when I walk forward a ghost is going to fall in front of my face as well.

Now I was inside I could see the area was lit with torches placed into carved gaps in the brickwork. It gave the corridor ahead of me a macabre atmosphere that heightened my expectations for the haunted house. As I stepped forward, my footfalls echoed off the stonework around me. I called out, wondering if anybody else would be here to guide me through.

“Hello?” I called out, quieter than I thought I would be. I guess the setting was slowly getting to my nerves. After waiting a moment there was no response, just more silence following after my initial greeting. Through the dim light of the torches however I noticed a small plain table beside the wall with paper laid flat on it. I stepped over and glanced down, reading the written word on the yellowing note.

“Happy Halloween and enjoy your frights, just walk through the dark and towards the light! Neither banshee nor beast nor monster will speak but scare and ensnare and deliver a screech! Make your way through and do your best to survive. This haunted house is one hell of a ride!”

I read over the note a second time. Does this mean I just.... walk through until I reach the end of the house? I guess they had people in hiding for this house but I could handle that. I think anyway. I've religiously watched horror movies since I was a kid. I've got this!

After psyching myself up I made my way down the empty hallway. The table behind me and the path forward, I slowly crept along, watching for anything that might jump out to try and scare me. It wasn't easy though. The torches made the shadows shift and once or twice I jumped slightly in fear when I thought something moved before realising the stone corridor was too narrow for anybody to actually hide. I was being silly.

A loud throat wrenching scream echoed ahead of me. I stopped in my tracks. Was that in front of me or....

A sound of hurried footsteps came from somewhere behind me. I turned, panicked as I saw a figure running towards me, mouth open wide. I stopped in place, frozen with fear. The figure kept running towards me and getting closer, the scream getting louder. 

I could see their ragged clothes, wet with something viscous as their feet slapped the ground underneath. Turning to finally run from it, the figure caught up and.. went through me, the scream deafening in my ears for a moment before it went silent again. I clutched my chest, my heart hammering against my ribs.

“T-that was pretty good!” I called out, breathless. I don't know what kind of budget these people had but that was a pretty good scare. I took a solid second to collect myself and carried onward. Met with silence again, save for the sound of my own footfalls on the ground.

Nothing happened for a time, I kept walking, the shadows danced against the flames of the torches that flickered occasionally as I passed on by. However it wasn't long until I came to a rather large room. There was little light in it, save for a lone candle on a table ahead of me. I went towards it, carefully as I glanced around. The room was large, feeling like a chamber of some unknown ominous intent. They really hit the nail on the head with this place. On the table was another note, written in the same hand from the one at the entrance.

“Through the stones and bricks of dark you walked with courage, verily. Surviving the ghastly wight upon which you felt through fear alone. Partake upon this brief reprieve as you decide upon your door, for all will lead to the end but which you choose you pick one... and no more.”

These notes were fairly poetic it seemed. Not exactly Poe but still unsettling to read. As I looked up I saw four doors ahead of me. One to my left, two ahead of me and one to my right. Very little distinguished them from each other, aside from their placement and I took a moment to decide. I felt as though this had been done before, many times but still took a moment to pick a door. If they all reached the end it wouldn't really matter what one I chose except their contents would be unknown.

I took a moment, watching the wax melt down the side of the candle as I made my choice to pick.

A sound of chains rattled behind me. A clattering metal on the ground. I looked back nervously, glancing over my shoulder but nothing showed. A sound effect surely, to make me hurry my choice. I looked at the doors, one through four. I wouldn't be able to experience each one in turn so I had to make a decision.

An aggressive banging from the door to my left and a shiver ran up my spine. Louder and harder the door banged. The sound of grunting from behind it. I looked at the door as it visibly shook, dust flying from the wood as whatever behind it hit it again and again. I walked towards it. This was door number one and it didn't seem like a good idea to choose it. The door shook again, faster. A screaming angry howl as chains rattled more and more. I stepped back, looking to door number three. A loud crack, and the wood splintered and split. Revealing whatever was on the other side that was desperate to get inside. Something.... bloated and grotesque squinted back through the cracked opening. It sneered, sharp teeth dripping as it slammed against the door again. I turned quickly and ran to the third door opening it quickly and rushing through. I wasn't about to find out whatever that thing was.

Quickly I made my way through the third exit and pushed forwards away from whatever that thing was breaking through the door. I heard its baleful howl of the creature behind me. The sounds of chains slamming to the ground in anger as it bellowed in rage.

As I kept walking it again became silent. I calmed myself. This haunted house was great. Scary as hell but it was really really great.

Torches would slowly become more spread out as the hallway grew wider. It was getting harder to see and I pulled out my phone to use as a flashlight. I didn't have much battery left. Or really any reception for that matter. I shouldn't have been surprised really. I had no idea how far I'd gone in this place and chances are that the layout may had been interfering with the phone signal.

“No...” I heard a voice whisper in my ear. I stopped, looked around. What was that? It was sort of... ethereal and unpleasant. Like someone had breathed right into my ear.

“H-hello?” I asked, nervously. “Is anyone there?”

Silence. Again.

“You're never leaving here alive” That same voice whispered into my ear. I turned and looked, panicked. That was some next level scary shit.

“Run.” The voice whispered, harsher and deeper. Out of the corner of my eye I saw something... glowing and I started running. My shoes pounded on the floor as I sprinted ahead. I turned to glance back to see something glowing and chasing after me. 

A floating eyeless husk was right behind me, its mouth opened wide in a silent scream, arms stretched out towards me as it grew closer and closer. I kept running, my breath sharp in my lungs as I willed my legs to keep carrying me forwards. I glanced back one more time to see the things face inches from my own and I screamed. Its arms almost wrapped around my neck and I screamed louder. I felt the cold dampness of its form near my skin, my hair standing on end, my spine stiff and tight as I ran with everything I had. The path ahead split and I ran to the left without hesitation. The thing behind me disappeared as I turned the corner. A loud shrieking followed, an unearthly horrifying screech that pierced through me and what felt like down into my soul. I kept moving however, to another split and another. A right and then another left and then to another door. I stopped, panting heavily as I tried to catch my breath. There was no sound save for my own breathing as I stood before the door. 

I opened it, careful to not make a sound as I slipped inside and closed it behind me. As the door closed, the room brightened slightly as my eyes adjusted to the gloom. I looked around, horrified by the sight before me.

Several old torture devices were laid out among the walls and on the floor. A rotted body lay on a torture rack, its limbs stretched past the point of breaking. One arm torn from the body, another stretched past anything it should have naturally been capable of. I looked to see another, a large wheel with a body strapped to it. Their limbs contorted at odd angles, the face sunken and sallow. Chains dotted the walls and a pile of hot coals glowed red between the two. I didn't know what I was looking at. But I knew I shouldn't stay here. I looked for another door. Only one other aside from the one I came through  lay ahead of me. I quickly walked towards it, glancing at the walls and floor as I walked past several bodies. Some looking fresher than others hung loosely from manacled chains connected to the walls.

My eyes lingered on one particular body. What appeared to be a more recent victim, their clothes were quite modern compared to the others. More of a recent fashion that mirrored my own. Their eyes stared blankly forwards  before the pupils slowly shifted to gaze at me. A moment passed as I made eye contact with this individual and then they began to thrash their body against the wall and their restraints. Muffled screams came from their mouth that I could see had been stitched closed. Their eyes desperate and pleading for help.

The door that lead to my exit slowly began to creak open and I froze, the eyes of the hanging body looked at me in panic and fear, shaking their head back and forth in an attempt to refuse whatever they knew would be coming. I quickly moved towards the door as it opened, standing to one side and held my breath to stay as quiet and still as possible. As the door opened inwards to the room I stood on the other side, hidden from view. A lumbering figure stepped past, dragging the body of a bloodied man behind them. They were clothed in a blood coated butchers apron, a cloth mask across their face, their hands large and quick as they worked to chain up the body they'd dragged along with them into the room. I quickly stepped around the door and out of the room, running quickly as I heard the sound of muffled screaming fall behind me into the dark.

The pathway was shorter this time, It wasn't long until I came to another door in my path. I placed my hand against it, nervous and scared. My hands trembling as I opened it slowly. I peered in and saw one more candle, adorned atop of a skull and one more yellowed note. 

I looked around, seeing several more doors and the sound of water. I glanced to see running water alongside this room. Possibly the canals again that circulated throughout the city. Slowly I crept up to the note and began to read.

“Through death and dismemberment you have survived, this night of Halloween you chose to thrive. Though creatures of night chased you unknown you outwitted, avoided and got through alone. This is the end, the last room of the place, we thank you for coming to our Halloween haunted house.”

I read over the note again. This was the last room? I went back to the door I stepped through. It was locked. How? I looked back around the chamber at the doors ahead of me and watched, in horror as each opened in turn. The repulsive thing covered in chains stepped through, howling and yipping. Its face a pulsing mass of flesh. It lumbered through the doorway and into the room, a sharp toothed smile on its face. From the second door the bulky beast with the butchers apron stepped out, fresh blood coating its clothing. Red smeared across its cloth mask. It held a hook and a knife, slick with fresh viscera. The last door was smashed open as a body of decaying flesh hunched over. 

Its limbs broken and disfigured, its eyes sunken. It was the one from the entrance who guided me through, looking more decomposed and corpse-like than before. A terrifying visage of three brutal monsters. At this point I was pretty sure this wasn't a haunted house. I looked back, the door behind me still locked and pressed my back to the wall. Was this it? Was I going to die here because I just wanted to finally experience some Halloween frights?

The chained creature laughed, its body heavy and sloven as it lumbered forwards, its chains dragging on the floor as its undead cohort shambled their body along the wall, hissing through rotted vocal chords as it looked on hungrily. The butcher ran a finger along its hook as it watched, its dark eyes shining with joy as it cleaned the knife across its blood stained clothes.

The living corpse lunged, its arms raking at my shoulder as I tried to shift away, its body slamming into the wall beside me. I screamed in pain, its nails had scratched through my clothes and alongside my skin, the fresh gouge burning. 

The other monster whipped out its chains, the metal sparking off the floor before me. They were trapping me like a rat in a corner and I was about to die. I looked around. My heart hammering, my eyes watering. The pain in my shoulder burned and the body beside me pushed up as it slowly turned its head, eyes sunken in hollow pits that stared at me. It lunged again, jaw unhinged, mouth unnaturally wide to bite down. I moved away quickly, my foot slipping as I stumbled, landing on the ground. The butcher continued to watch, biding its time as it slowly moved forward. I didn't want to end up in one of those torture devices or chained to the wall like the others. As the zombie struggled to get back up, the skin on its arm began to slough off as it collapsed again under its own weight, struggling to push itself up. The bulbous beast with its chains staggered forward, a thick slimy hand reaching out to snatch me up off the ground. Its fingers stretched out, nails sharp as I pushed away from off the ground to increase the distance. I felt nothing behind me as I crawled back. Nothing pressed against my back, just a cold wet sensation on the floor and the sound of rushing water. I glanced behind me quickly and back at the monsters that began to surround me.

“Fuck. You.” I cried out and pushed myself into the water below. The cold rapid darkness of the water enveloped my body as I was pulled along by the canals flow. Howling, roaring anger followed behind my fall, the sound diluted out by the rushing water in my ears as my body was carried to wherever the water would flow too. I gasped as the air escaped my lungs, water flooding into my nose and throat.

Better this... than them... I thought weakly as my head began to pulse and throb painfully from the lack of oxygen. 

My body moved faster with the flow of the canal. The darkness around my body slowly shifting as the water began to brighten. I kicked my legs and stretched out my arms, the pain burning and itching, my body screaming in agony as I pushed myself upwards to the surface for air. I kicked and kicked, dragging my limbs through the liquid that held me down. My hands clawing through the water until my head shot up above the surface and I took a deep lungful of air. For a brief moment I saw the sky overhead, stars glinting in the night before the water pulled me back down into its murky shadows. I scrambled through the water for the safety of the canal walkway, reaching out to pull myself forwards til finally I breached the surface again and was able to grasp onto the solid brickwork. I breathed slow, coughing water out as I pulled myself painfully up onto the bricks, my body laid out flat from fear and exhaustion. I looked up at the sky above and coughed out some more water. Taking my phone from my flooded pocket I looked at the wet electronic in my hand. Wet and most likely broken, I laughed.

I was bloodied, sore, battered and bruised. But I was alive and I had survived.

“Halloween? Never again.”
