I'm going to begin with some disclaimers, as the title of this post is a little misleading. First, I'm not 100% certain of the count. I was for a long while, but I have to concede that there's now a margin of error of at least ±5. This is despite my best efforts to remember, and I'm unnerved by this evidence of my mental slippage. More on that later. Despite this lack of certainty, I've been denoting the last few posts of mine as day five thousand. They seem to do better when the title includes a round number. I'm not entirely sure why I care about something like that; maybe it just feels good to engage in something as human as attention-seeking. It helps as well to imagine that I have some agency in determining the size of the audience that reads my testimony. It helps even more to indulge in the fantasy that one day someone with some esoteric/occult expertise will reply with a method of escape. Of course, this is all just my way of coping. I've long since come to terms with my fate.

Second, I'm only making my best guess with the word "demon". I suppose I could have said "I've been trapped in a time loop by an otherworldly entity", but that doesn't have the same ring to it. It also didn't get the post as much traction as when I've described it as a  "demon" or "devil". I have a lot of these little games. Another one involves tracking which phrasings/details attract which commenters. There's one guy who will only reply if I mention the city I live in; another will only say anything if I lie and write that I've been trapped for 1000 years. Novelty has become a rare resource necessary for my survival, so please excuse any random detail in this post as part of my regimented sanity protocol. 

As I was saying, I have no idea if my captor is actually a demon or anything of that sort. That being said, I don't think the term is entirely off base. I'm not religious (especially not anymore), but this thing definitely fits the description of what I'd imagine a demon would look and act like. I've thought long and hard about it, and demonic parasite is the best explanation I can provide for the identity of the creature standing in the corner of my room right now. But I'll never know for sure. It could be an alien, or a ghost, or some escaped laboratory experiment for all I'm aware. It's not like it would tell me. It's only spoken a handful of times, none of which have made me believe that it has any intention of explaining itself to me. 

I know even less about how it found me and trapped me in the loop to begin with. I didn't explore some ancient abandoned temple and get cursed, this isn't the tragic result of some Faustian bargain with a cryptic stranger or the ironic backfiring of a monkey's paw wish. In fact, I've always held it as a vague rule not to mess with things that I don't understand. I'm a cautious person by nature, and had gone through life taking any opportunity I could to reduce risk or avoid getting caught up in anything dangerous. But it didn't mean a thing in the end. The only explanation I can provide as to how I've ended up here isn't an explanation at all, it's a brute fact. I am profoundly unlucky. I've spent years thinking about that day, about this day, and haven't been able to pinpoint anything at all that, if I had changed, would have saved me from this fate. My getting sealed away in time was just a freak accident, no different than a plane crash or a tsunami. If creatures like this exist, and they do, it means that some unlucky bastard is going to end up in their line of sight by sheer happenstance. I can only imagine that I was standing on some extradimensional psychic fault line in the wrong place at the wrong time, and have now been swallowed whole by something awful. A child sticking out their tongue during a snowstorm is bound to catch at least one snowflake, even if millions of others will find the ground safely. I suppose the snowflake melts quickly, sparing it from a fate like mine. 

A lot of the beginning is getting hazy, but I still remember the first loop very clearly. I actually didn't notice anything was wrong for the first few hours of the day. I woke up pretty early, so I guess my head was still foggy enough that I didn't recognize that the date on my phone was the day prior. On my morning commute to class I began to squint while admiring the sunrise through the window across from me on the train. The way the sun reflected off the water looked oddly familiar, like I had seen it in a dream or a movie. This vague déjà vu only hardened into quiet terror once I arrived at the library. As a grad student, I was there to get some work done on my thesis prior to my first class of the day. Upon opening the document, however, I was met with none of the additions I had made the night prior. Terrified that I had lost all of that progress, I checked the edit history only to find that the most recent edit on the document was the night before last. Before I could react properly to this, I was interrupted by my friend Jon walking towards me.

"Back here so soon? You really can't keep burning the candle at both ends like this," he said with a smirk, pulling up a chair next to mine. "I suppose I can keep you company."

My eyes widened in shock as I turned my head slowly to face him. He had said this yesterday. This had happened yesterday. This moment, this exchange, those words in that order. My heartbeat slowly started to quicken as I mumbled something barely coherent and motioned to get out of my seat. After making a beeline for the door to get some fresh air and calm down, I took some deep breaths before checking my phone. 8:37 AM, February 24th. Those deep breaths were of little use as I began to hyperventilate. Today was supposed to be the 25th. I knew that with more certainty than I might have for other days, because the 25th is my father's birthday. I had spent the night prior editing a video of all my family members wishing him a happy birthday. I was supposed to send it sometime today. But today was yesterday. I collapsed to the ground, my body revolting against this disgusting truth. Through ragged breaths only one thought kept racing through my mind. *This cannot be happening.* 

But it was happening. I spent the next few hours fighting off a psychotic break as I made my way through the crowded city, asking people periodically for the date. It was February 24th each time without exception. Every instance of confirmation worsened the stinging, restrictive feeling deep in my chest. I eventually resolved that this might just be a solitary glitch in the matrix type of occurrence. This was a lone hiccup, and I'd get to rejoin the flow of time upon waking up tomorrow morning. Those dreams were crushed when I woke up on the following February 24th, holding my head in my hands for hours after checking my phone. 

This is when things start to get blurry. I remember my first few months in broad strokes, but the details are starting to fade. I know that my first stage of grief in the face of eternity, denial, consisted of determining what lesson I needed to learn in order to free myself from what I still believed was some cosmic punishment. I meditated, went back to church, called old girlfriends, anything I could think of that might lead to some form of reconciliation. None of it did a thing. After a while, believing that I could somehow free myself through my own efforts became an overbearing curse instead of a beacon of hope.   
  
I instead began to give myself over to the loop, exploring the new avenues for entertainment that had opened for me. I'd find out exactly which strings of words would make Jon, my professors, or eventually even the girl sitting next to me on the train laugh, cry, or fall in love with me. I found out how to convince them that I was stuck in a loop, and got a sick sense of catharsis watching their faces twist into warped expressions of horror and grief. It felt a little gross at first to know more about these people than their closest loved ones, but I got over that moral hangup. I got over a lot of moral hangups. Swimming in deep time will do that to you. My entertainment in those early months and years extended far beyond exhausting every dialogue option with the people in my path. My body resetting every day meant that I could try whatever drugs I wanted without building up any sort of tolerance or chemical addiction. The fear of overdosing wasn't a limiting factor either, as anytime I did, I would just wake up in my bed at the start of another February 24th. I had to have spent at least six months trying heroin for the "first" time. I look back fondly, if not foggily, at those days. 

Instead of running up against addiction, it was boredom that curtailed my drug use. It was a surreal experience to look at a heroin needle with nothing but apathy in my heart. I have to be the only human alive to have ever gotten tired of hard narcotics. Hedonism is just escapism at its core, and escapism is only worth anything when there's a reasonable belief that you could actually escape whatever you're trying to avoid. With no such possibility available to me, even the most seemingly adrenaline-filled activities would eventually become dull and even annoying. Maybe some of you could shoot up for eternity, but I got sick of it. I got sick of violence, too. You can only throw a haymaker at your professor during the middle of class or assassinate the mayor so many times before you become desensitized and over the whole thing. Humans are inherently drawn towards meaning, and the more time washed over me, the more I realized that this overcast day in February had absolutely none to offer me. 

My memory is the most spotty for the next stage of my imprisonment. As thrill-seeking gave way to a melancholic haze, I stopped caring about my situation. No longer trying to escape or enjoy my new life, I just refused to engage with it altogether. I spent what must have been decades just lying in my bed, barely thinking about anything. Do you know what it feels like to go that long without any desire to eat food? Without the faintest urge to lift your head off your pillow? I was already dead to the world the moment I became trapped, but I was now thoroughly dead to myself. I had tried suicide before I became voluntarily bed-bound, but every 500-foot free-fall towards the concrete ended the same way: with my alarm jolting me awake as I let out a guttural scream. This was my next best alternative. I planned to lie there indefinitely, hoping that the more nothing I experienced, the less conscious I would become. Near the end, it was starting to work. I began to feel less like a sentient creature and more like someone's memory of one. It was almost as if my awareness itself was being siphoned off, like I was losing what made me aware that I could lose things. 

At some point during this self-induced cognitive decline, however, something primal within me latched on to the clumping of shadows in the corner of my room. It felt instinctual, like my amygdala had successfully identified a threat for the first time in an eternity. It had been impossibly long since I had consciously focused my eyesight on anything, but this sudden burst of brain activity returned me to a state where I desired to unblur my vision and focus it on those shadows. My eyes widened in terror when I was finally able to see what was actually standing mere feet away from me. 

It was a tall, muscular, humanoid figure with coarse, charcoal grey skin. With its head mere inches from the ceiling, it stood facing me, blocking the door to my room. It wore nothing except for a thick black cloth tied around its waist. Even when I had focused my eyes, the outline of the creature still appeared fuzzy, like it was perpetually in my peripheral vision. It had no eyes. The top of its head instead featured a large patch of wrinkled skin, like that of an elbow, except the lines were far deeper. Despite the lack of eyes, I was certain it was intently focused on me. It had no nose either, only an enormous ear-to-ear smile revealing rows of ragged black teeth. It drooled out of its grinning mouth, thick strands of saliva hanging from its lips. It must have realized I was looking at it, as it let out four words in a quiet, breathy voice.

"Go back to sleep."

Although its words were barely whispered, it was like each one penetrated my soul with unadulterated malicious intent. I knew why it was drooling. I understood right then and there that this was a predator, and I was its prey. I didn't listen to its command; I don't think I could have even if I wanted to at this point. The influx of shock and sensation coursing through my body after ages of nothingness felt like a bucket of freezing cold water on my entire nervous system. I sat there, frozen, eyes widened in horror and fixed on the thing. But as I stayed laser focused on the creature, it started to become more and more blurry. It was like it was dissolving the longer I stared at it. This continued until it became nothing more than the odd clump of shadows I had originally identified. After remaining on my bed for what felt like hours, I finally gathered the courage to walk through them. Nothing happened. 

I started to live again after that first encounter. I wasn't completely sure why, maybe because escapism came back into fashion once I had an experience I would do anything to forget. I tried to imagine that it was just a consequence of my mind melting, that it was merely the death rattle of my subconscious. But deep down, I knew the truth. It was real, and my intuition was telling me that I owed my decades of torment to the creature in the corner of my room. But why had I never seen it before? Why wouldn't it just harvest my soul or drag me to hell or its mothership or wherever the hell and just get it over with?

Here is what I know. This creature, this demon, doesn't eat in a traditional sense. It has seemingly no interest in its victim's body; it only cares about the mind. Furthermore, it's only interested in minds that are desolate and inactive. This is the only way I can make sense of that first encounter and subsequent appearances. Now, you would then imagine its ideal meal would be a brain-dead hospital patient, right? But this can't be the case. I was a budding intellectual pursuing a graduate degree in evolutionary anthropology, and it still chose to wear me down instead of feasting on an already broken mind. I have to conclude then that this demon is not a scavenger. It hunts, and the loop is how.  
  
In my old life, I spent a lot of time researching humanity's origin. Most people attribute our evolutionary success and dominion over the natural world to our enhanced intelligence, but this is only part of the story. Early humans were endurance hunters. We had the highest stamina of any land mammal in the world. We would stalk our prey relentlessly, giving them no respite to catch their breath or recover from any sustained injuries. We were an all-encompassing oppressive force of death and despair for any poor bastard that crossed our path. It didn't matter what you did; we would catch you eventually. I was previously under the assumption that this was a unique evolutionary niche, that we were the only creatures that hunted this way. I know better now.

Even psychopaths can be tortured using solitary confinement. A space devoid of any novelty or purpose will eventually lead a mind to insanity. The loop is my cell. It is the mechanism by which the demon means to hollow out my skull, to lead me to functional brain death. I have no way of knowing whether this is something necessary for its digestive process, like cooking raw meat, or something optional meant to add flavor, like adding seasoning. Either way, this is how it chooses to go about its hunt, and it almost succeeded. 

This, of course, is only my working theory as to what is occurring. But it's substantiated by what has occurred since our first interaction. Upon my return to the world outside my bedroom, I began to notice changes in the normal sequence of events I was accustomed to. It was subtle at first, but when you've lived the same day thousands of times, you pick up on every imperceptible detail. Jon would walk a little slower towards me in the library, or the sun would be a little lower and dimmer in the sky than it should have been on my train ride.   
This worsened as time went on, with things becoming increasingly empty and dull. I'd reach for a new book at the library only to find that all of the pages were blank. I'd try to shout obscenities at a stranger for a reaction, only for them to walk right past. The sky, the buildings, and the trees all started to fade into a dark grey.  
  
All the while, I would continue to see clumpings of shadows on the edges of my peripheral vision, taking a more solid shape if I continued to ignore them. It's my understanding that the demon is tightening its grip on the loop upon failing to secure the kill in my bedroom. It's lowering the bandwidth on the whole thing, systematically removing any source of stimulation I can use to keep myself sane. It hates when I'm distracted, when I'm engrossed in anything that allows me to forget my situation for even a moment. I was reading one of the non-empty books one day, particularly gripped by the main character's internal monologue. I didn't reach the end of the page before I heard a single word whispered from directly behind me. It was still in a hushed tone, but still far more forceful than when I had first heard it speak.

"Stop."

This is how I've come to surmise that it wants me docile and brainless. That same day, I wrote my first post on here. It was short and took the form of a cry for help more than a testimony, but I did it again the next day. It felt good to give a voice to my struggle. This brings us to now. I've spent the last 5000 (ish) days doing everything I can to stave off the inevitable mental decline, with my daily posts being a key component in my sanity protocol. The protocol is simple, but I follow it to the letter. It really just amounts to spending each day engaging in as much novel stimulus as possible. Books, movies, creative writing, and even reading all of your experiences on here. I've walked into each building in my city and explored it fully. I know how to speak 10 languages (I'm currently working on Icelandic). I have a graduate-level grasp of quantum physics. If I were to return to the normal flow of time, I'd be a bona fide polymath. But I'm not. I'm a death row inmate.   
  
The protocol has had to undergo some alterations as fewer and fewer sources of novelty become available. One day, I woke up to find that entering the URL for any streaming service returned nothing but a black screen. So did Wikipedia, which was a particularly brutal loss. I've had to resort to analogue media for most of my stimulus. Vinyl records, VHS tapes, the works. I've always liked old stuff, though, and there are plenty of stores in my city that sell vintage gear (these days, the cashier won't even say or do anything if I blatantly shoplift). I'm grateful that this website still works and I'm able to continue to share my testimony. Writing my story takes up several hours of each day, but it is essential. It's how I retain my memories regarding what has happened and who I am. Without daily repetition of my situation, I would have long since gone insane.

But I can't keep this up forever. I know that. Eventually, the demon will catch on, and my records won't make any sound. Eventually, every book I open will be blank. I might even lose my mind before that happens. Cracks have been forming in the last year. I didn't want to admit it for a while, but my recent loss of count regarding these posts finally made me confront it. Some days it'll take me longer than it should to remember Jon's name, or to recall that "tomorrow" is my dad's birthday. Oh my god. Oh my god. His name isn't spelled like that; it's John. God fucking damn it, have I really been writing it like that this entire time? Case in point. I'm slipping. It's going to win eventually, and it knows it. Even now, I can see the faint outline of a smile in my peripheral vision. That gross, smug fucking smile. 

There came a point in this cycle where I wanted to die, to fade away. But the more I came to understand what was actually happening to me, the greater the urge to fight became. This didn't just happen to me out of nowhere; I owe what I estimate to be almost a century of suffering to this demon. It's his fault. I have no idea how many people it has damned to this sick fate in the past, and I don't know how long it took for all of them to break. But I won't go quietly. I'll spit on the carpet as I'm dragged through hell. I'll elongate this process as long as I possibly can. If all I can do in the face of this otherworldly evil is inconvenience it, then that's what I'll do. I am Sisyphus and the rock hates me. But I hate the rock even more. 

What is the fly thinking about as it dissolves in the mouth of the Venus flytrap? Does it earnestly believe that it has a chance to fight back? I suppose we'll never know. I'll see you all tomorrow.
