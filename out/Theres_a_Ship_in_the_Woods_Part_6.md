[Day ](https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/comments/1r3ckuc/theres_a_ship_in_the_woods_part_5/)9 at the Cabin

So, not much to report from today. At least as far anything supernatural goes. Nope just my own stupidity and the consequences which come with that stupidity. I'll get right into it. It was a little past noon when my boredom officially peaked. I wasn't in the mood to read, and I really didn't want to go for a walk again. I found myself wandering back and forth in the living room, I tried to knock on the walls to look for secrets or something but I don't know what that would sound like, and on the deck where I could see more overcast clouds swirling. I was keeping an eye out for birds when I noticed something else. I saw during my first day here that there wasn't a ladder that went up to the crow's nest, felt it was because the folks who build this either didn't add one or took it away for safety reasons or what have you, but when I examined the mast again I saw it. The "rungs" were carved into the tree. Now just looking up at the lookout gives me awful vertigo, but with nothing else to do I figured why not and decided to climb it.

With the mast being a redwood, branches and leaves removed, it's a tall piece of work. I was careful, going slow and not looking down that much. My pace felt like I was going ten feet a minute. I swear it took me, like, half an hour to get to the platform. Enough time for the shadows to move around me and fog to roll in, or maybe I was just at cloud level. Because the circumference of the mast is so big the platform gave me a lot of room to walk around on, thankfully. Of course just the idea of looking over the barrier wall made me want to vomit, so I stuck close to the trunk while I did my looking around.

That's when I saw it. A lockbox tucked into a carved out alcove. I pulled it out and gave it a onceover. Looked pretty normal, didn't seem old or weathered. No key though, I would've had to break it to open it and that seemed like a lot of work for something that was most likely empty. It felt empty at least and shaking it brought up nothing so I put it back and resumed my meandering around.

I caught more glimpses of birds through the fog up there, some squirrels too if I looked into it long enough, they didn't want anything to do with me though. One bird however, a crow or raven I don't really know the difference, came and perched itself on the barrier wall. It just watched me, tilting its head back and forth and hopping along the edge. I went towards it, its behavior made me want to test its tolerance of me, but now I know I should not do that. It darted at me out of nowhere and in my frenzy to keep its talons out of my face I almost fell over the wall.

Luckily, I didn't, I was able to catch myself, but I did end up looking down and had the very unpleasant view of watching the peaches I had for lunch make their way to the deck below. Climbing back down took longer than going up, and cleaning the upchucked peaches took even longer cause I had no idea where the bleach was. It never occurred to me to look for the cleaning supplies these past few days, I guess that's on me, but who the hell puts cleaning supplies in a towel closet?

I don't know how to get stains out of wood and it feels too late to look it up now. I scrubbed at it for a while, wood's just discolored now. I swear if I get fined for damages, I'll sue them for unsafe architecture or something. I caved and checked out the books again while boiling water for the ramen Otis brought up, the saint of a man. My eyes went back to Moby Dick and I begrudgingly grabbed it up. It's not a bad read so far, a bit long winded, but not completely boring. I got through a few chapters while I ate and then while waiting for it to be late enough to justify going to sleep.

There's not enough room on the bedside table to put it there tonight, and the drawers are filled with my clothes, so I put the book on the floor to be read some more in the morning. And that's it for now. I'm gonna try and get some shut eye with the sounds of this nearby owl. Its hooting sounds accusatory, but I'm surely just imagining it. Goodnight.
