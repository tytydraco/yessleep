A few days ago I posted [My coworker asked me why I ignored him this morning. I was still asleep.](https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/comments/1ubegb7/my_coworker_asked_me_why_i_ignored_him_this/) Long story short, I've been hearing footsteps around my house at night, my coworker saw me somewhere I don't remember being and my favorite hat was inexplicably out of place. 

Since then things have only gotten worse. And I've spent so much time trying to find some kind of explanation, but all my efforts only seem to bring up more questions. 

That night, after I talked to my coworker and made that post, I went to the gas station on 35th to ask around. I wanted to see if anyone there recognized me... Anyways I went in about 10 PM and walked up to the counter. I was about to ask if I was there that morning when the clerk beat me to the punch. 

"Hey, you here to stock up on some more reds?"

"What?" I asked.

"You know, Marlboro reds, the cigarettes." he explained.

"I think you have me confused with someone else, I don't smoke"

He looked at me like I was speaking gibberish. "Could've fooled me" he said, chuckling.

"What do you mean?" I said, a sinking feeling building in my gut.

"I mean... you come in here almost every morning around 4 AM. You always buy a pack of Marlboro reds." 

"I work at 8, I'm still asleep at 4" 

"No offense, man, but you always look like you're half asleep when you're in here." 

Before I could say anything else he said, "Wait you said you don't smoke? What are you giving all those cigarettes to someone else?" He chuckled again.

"I don't... I don't... know..." I stuttered.

He squinted at me, "Hey man are you alright?" 

I started backing away, my stomach felt like it was full of lead. The clerk said something else to me but by then I couldn't process a thing he was saying. I turned around and sprinted back to my car. 

I sat there for a good 20 minutes trying to digest what the clerk had said to me. Whole time I felt like I was being watched. I kept looking over my shoulder and glancing out the windows. But there was nothing there. No eyes staring at me from the shadows, nobody in the backseat, nothing. Could I really have been sleep walking to the gas station every morning for god knows how long? If so why the hell would I be buying cigarettes? I've never smoked in my life, hell I try not to even touch those things. None of it made sense.

Eventually I worked up the courage to drive home. The whole way there I couldn't help but constantly check my mirrors, even when I knew there was nothing behind me.  As I pulled into my driveway a sense of dread washed over me. I was terrified to even open my door. No matter how much I tried I just couldn't shake the feeling that there was something waiting for me inside my house. 

When I finally went inside I grabbed a kitchen knife and went through every room in the house. 

Nothing.

Everything was just as I'd left it. 

After taking a while to calm down as well as double checking that every door and window in my house was locked I decided to lay down and try to get some sleep. I decided to bring the knife with me. I had only been laying there for a few minutes when they came back.

The sounds.

A creaking floor board here. A door hinge there. All separated by minutes of agonizing silence. 

With what happened at the gas station I decided it would be a bad idea to ignore them. I was so paranoid I got up 3 separate times to sweep the whole goddamn house. 

Brandishing a butcher knife as I called out to what I hoped were empty rooms.

Nothing called back. 

I felt like I was going crazy, I couldn't tell if the noises were real or in my head or a mix of both. I was completely lost.

After literal hours of this I got fed up and found some noise canceling headphones to drown out the noise. I finally managed to drift off to sleep at about 3 AM. When the morning finally came I quickly showered and ate breakfast before deciding to keep searching for answers. I was convinced that there was something that would make this whole thing click, something that could prove that this is all in my head. 

I spent the next few hours calling various relatives of mine asking if they remember me sleepwalking at any point. That search came up dry. They all basically told me I had never slept walked in my life. The only remotely interesting thing I found out came from my dad. He told me I had never been a sleepwalker but then he went on a tangent about how my grandfather had "A sleepwalking phase" a long time ago. I asked for details but he told me he couldn't remember much. I don't know if sleepwalking is genetic, but maybe I could've inherited it from him. That would explain a lot.

I spent the rest of the day on my computer researching possible causes for my symptoms but I couldn't find anything that checked all the boxes. I also checked my purchase history with my bank and there were no withdraws or purchases I didn't remember. After hours of fruitless searching I decided to quit for the day. 

As I was about to put my headphones on and try to get some sleep a thought hit me.

If I had those headphones on I wouldn't be able to hear *anything*. Someone could be waltzing around my house or worse my room and I'd be fast asleep, none the wiser. What if there was some sound that could put all the pieces together and I just didn't hear it? Then again I couldn't keep losing this much sleep. I sat there, panicking, for minutes trying to come up with a solution. 

Suddenly, I realized I could just leave my phone on a sound recording while I slept with the headphones on. That way I could get some sleep and gather info at the same time. So I decided to do just that. 

I woke up the next morning feeling better than I had in weeks. That was the first full night of sleep I had gotten in a long time. Before I even got out of bed I listened to the whole recording on 2x speed, slowing down when I heard anything of interest. 

What follows is my best attempt at a transcript. 

10 PM: Silence accompanied by light snoring.

11 PM: Rain begin to hit the windows.

11:32 PM: Slow footsteps can be heard downstairs.

11:56 PM: A single foot step heard at the bottom of the stairs

12:11 AM: Another footstep.

13 more footsteps can be heard over the course of the next 2 hours each one getting closer.

2:13 AM: One, slow footstep is heard at the end of the hallway.

2:22 AM: Another footstep, this one closer than the last.

2:37 AM: Another footstep.

2:45 AM: A footstep is heard outside the bedroom.

2:51 AM: The bedroom door slowly creaks open.

2:53 AM: The bedroom door closes.

2:58 AM: extremely quiet footsteps are heard throughout the room.

3:07 AM: *click*

3:08 AM: The bedroom window slowly slides open, the sound of rain intensifies. 

3:09 AM: The window slides shut.

Rainfall and snoring can be heard for the rest of the recording.

I have never been more scared in my life than when I heard that recording. I sat there for what felt like hours, shaking, too terrified to move. I felt dizzy like I was about puke. I wanted so badly to believe that I just heard myself sleep walking but I didn't. 

When I finally managed to crawl out of bed I crept over to my window.

It was unlocked.

I peered outside. 

Outside my window there was a trail of foot prints leading away from my house and into the tree line.

There was something off about the trail. The spacing was... wrong. Like whoever made them was taking deliberate, oversized steps.

And that's not even the worst part.

When I finally forced myself to go outside and investigate the trial, I found that my shoe fit right into foot prints.

Like a fucking puzzle piece.
