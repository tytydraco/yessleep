A couple of weeks ago, I was watching the “Benny’s Crowd” marathon, but about three episodes into Season One, when Benny’s hosting that big party and he goes to his neighbor’s apartment to borrow a wine opener? The door to Mrs. Darby’s opens, but there’s no Mrs. Darby. The apartment’s totally dark and nothing happens for, like, a minute. It’s just dead air.

Mrs. Darby should have opened the door, right? She’d then spend the next five minutes interrupting Benny as he’s trying to borrow the wine opener for the party and hearing stuff break in his apartment, but… it didn’t happen. Apartment 8C was dark and after this awkward pause of dead air, Benny finally goes in.

He goes, “Hello? Mrs. Darby?” and the audience starts laughing but it felt like my blood just froze. The laugh track and bubblegum piano music sound like they’re playing through busted speakers, and I’m thinking it’s a gag or an outtake. That’s when the door closes behind him and I spot this seven-foot guy standing behind the door, wearing a real severed pig’s head with buttons for eyes, and a butcher’s smock all bloodied up. Oh, and he’s got the corkscrew.

I actually screamed “look out,” but it was too late. Benny turns and slips in Mrs. Darby’s blood, hitting his head on the counter. And then he just lays there as this butcher lays into him, stabbing him over and over again. I was frozen, watching Benny scream and get torn up, but the party at his place is too loud. Nobody hears him except me. Then the Butcher stops… and I swear he looks at me. Or the camera. Whatever, it still felt like he could see me.

I grabbed the remote and turned my television off. I really don’t know how long I sat there, too shocked to budge, but when I worked up the courage to turn the T.V. back on, Benny was on the floor, dead, and the killer was gone.

I thought about calling 911, but what the fuck was I going to tell them? I looked online, on the “Benny’s Crowd” forums, but everyone was talking about the marathon and their favorite episodes. Nobody saw what I saw. I thought for sure I’d gone crazy.

The next day, the channel was frozen, Benny dead in the dark apartment, the flies buzzing around him. It hadn’t been a bad dream like I’d hoped and I flipped away from it. No destination in mind, just mindlessly hopping from station to station.

Then I caught a glimpse of the pig head again and I scrambled to flip back, trying to find him. That’s when I landed on “Joey Conquers the World,” and Joey is talking to the camera as he heads to school. I’m literally too numb to remember which episode or season I was watching, except that maybe Joey looked a bit older. As I’m watching him cross the street, Crossing Guard Annie asked him how it felt to tell his parents the truth about… whatever. Doesn’t matter because this school bus comes out of nowhere and fucking creams them. Crossing Guard Annie gets sucked under the wheel and mulched, and Joey turns into a block-long streak of red crayon on the asphalt. The Butcher’s driving.

I ran to the bathroom and threw up. My legs were shaking, I was sweating… I must have stayed there for a half-hour, tucked between the toilet and the wall, listening to the screams coming from the television, hearing the school bus plow through more kids.

I didn’t turn on the TV for a week. I actually took three sick days, hiding in my apartment. I couldn’t sleep, couldn’t eat. I think my friends called, but I was a zombie. Finally, my buddy Alex shows up at my door. I must have looked like shit because he straight up took one step back. “What’s wrong?” he starts asking. “You look like shit, what happened?” Over and over again. I couldn’t bring myself to tell him, I didn’t know how, so I just turned on the TV and I told him “Watch.”

I found this show I always hated, the sports comedy “Buddy Buddy” and we started watching it, and I know I’m making Alex nervous, constantly looking over at him like “wait, just wait.”

One episode goes by. Then another. Nothing happens and now Alex looks really worried. Just as he gets up, Buddy 1 and Buddy 2 are giving each other a bro hug and telling each other, “I love you, man,” when the Butcher drives a javelin through them both.

I’m screaming and pointing at the screen, yelling “See, see, see,” and Alex is screaming back, “What, see what?” He doesn’t see it, and now I’m sure I’m going insane. Meanwhile, the Butcher lights one Buddy on fire, and they’re both screaming and trying to pull themselves off each other.

I haven’t talked to Alex since. He won’t return my calls, and almost everyone’s ghosted me except for Kylie. I tried recording the episodes where the Butcher shows up, and sending it to her, but she messaged me back saying that all she sees are normal episodes but that she “believes ‘I see it.’” That’s what you say to people you think are crazy, right? Still, I had to watch someone get their skin peeled off on “Life’s a Potato,” and Ally Sweet get sandwiched under a burning mattress on “Home Sweet Home” to record the Butcher. But when I rewatched them, everyone is already dead.

After a while, I couldn’t stop watching. I guess I was shell-shocked or something, but “Hot Temper,” “Justin Case,” “Four Naked Men,” all of them… butchered.

You know that K-Drama “Will They Won’t They”? They won’t, I can tell you that.

I don’t dare flip past Mr. Rogers or Sesame Street. I can’t watch them die. Even channel hopping kills off characters, and I see flashes of blood and guts in the thumbnails or images as they go by. Or I see his face with those black button eyes, just staring at me. I tried to stare back but… I don’t last more than a couple of seconds.

“Then just stop watching television!” That’s what you’re going to tell me, right?

I tried but the Butcher is everywhere. On my internet, on every screen I happen to catch, at the office. If there’s a television on at the bar, he’s driving the Zamboni over the hockey players on the red ice. If there’s a guy looking at cat videos on the phone… let’s just say no more cat videos. Anyone watching anything is looking at corpses as far as I can tell. It’s all dead air.

And now, over the past couple days, the screens I’m looking at are showing me at work or wherever I am at that moment. I tried it at home, and it’s me from different sides, all camera angles, centering me perfectly. I know what comes next. He’s going to kill me on screen, I just know it… but will it really be me, or just a reflection? I mean all those actors who played those characters are still running around right? But will I die for real? Or just me on the screen?! I’m panicking and I’m struggling to breathe.

I couldn’t take it anymore. I just called Kylie and her and Alex are coming to pick me up to take me to the hospital. Maybe they’re right. Maybe I just need help. Wish me luck.
