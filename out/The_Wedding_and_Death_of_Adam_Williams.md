"Hey, check out this weird-ass post I found", Elaine leaned over and showed me her phone.

I frowned. She had looked up Mr. Williams on Insta. *While* we were in his class. Our teacher was currently busy at the blackboard, but my experience told me that wouldn’t last long.

Nevertheless, I leaned forward to see better. Elaine smelled like rain and vanilla. Our shoulders were touching. I squinted.

The post she had pulled was in black and white. An announcement. There was a picture of Mr. Williams with some creepy AI-generated angel on top. The text was even worse.

*We are saddened and happy by the passing of our Brother Adam. He is with the Lord now.*

I blinked and looked at Mr. Williams in front of us. Definitely alive, and currently ranting about the plight of Jay Gatsby. I looked at the announcement again. Definitely him. I rested my left hand on Elaine's knee while the right clicked on the account. Some church. Just normal Christian stuff. The most recent post was interesting, though. It was in the same font as the death announcement, but with brighter colors.

*The Lord rejoices! Our Brother Adam will get married this Sunday!*

"Getting married“, I frowned, "isn’t he... you know...“

"Liliane, Elaine!,“, Mr. Williams called, "please keep your hands to yourselves. Also, what are you looking at under the table?“

"I... uhm... sorry“, I stammered.

"Yeah“, Elaine nodded over-eagerly, "we won’t do it again.“

"Alright“, Mr. Williams smiled, "let us move on. So, the green light shows..."

Elaine and I exchanged a glance and kept to ourselves for the rest of the lesson. But I just couldn’t get that post out of my head. *He is with the Lord now...*

  


As soon as the bell rang, my friend bolted. I think she was scared Mr. Williams was gonna give us detention if we stayed. I wanted to leave, too. I do not like confrontation. Still, I found myself unable to get out of the room, so I just stood there awkwardly for a while.

"Mr. Williams“, I finally managed to whisper. My voice made the room feel creepy all of the sudden. "Do you plan on dying this Sunday?“

He chuckled dryly. "Who gave you that idea?“

"Your Church’s Instagram.“

"Ah“, he raised his eyebrows, "well, you might have misunderstood. I’m not dying. I’m getting married! In a proper church and all.“

'To... a *woman?*', I wanted to ask. But I couldn’t. Of course I couldn’t. Mr. Williams seemed to sense there was something I wanted to say.

"Liliane“, he smiled warmly, "you and I... we aren’t like the rest of them, are we?“

I nodded slowly. "Yes. *I* know that.“

"But you are a good kid, Liliane. And it can still get better. I... why don’t you come to my wedding this Sunday? Bring Elaine if you want. It might teach you something.“

I forced myself to smile. "Okay. Yeah. I’d like that.“

He gave me a little dorky wave as I left.



I did not, in fact, like it. The church was terrible. The ceiling was too low, and there was a disgusting pink neon-glow everywhere. It also smelled like incense. My head hurt. And there were voices, too many voices. Chatter from all sides. It felt like I was going crazy.

"Oh, hello!“, exclaimed a loud, shrill one next to me, "you must be one of Adam’s students, right? I’m so glad you made it.“

I forced myself to focus on her. I even managed a smile. The woman had Mr. Williams’s eyes. "And you are his mother?“

"I am“, she sighted, "I wish more young people would come and see my boy. Finally becoming what God wanted him to be.“

I smiled nervously. I wished I had stayed home. Elaine had been smart enough to "avoid this shitshow.“ I knew I should have done the same. But... there was something inside me, some sort of pull. I had felt it ever since I’d seen those two posts. I kept checking the Church’s Insta. The memorial was down by now, but the wedding invite was still up.

"It’s almost time“, Mrs. Williams whispered and slipped in the front row. I sat myself somewhat in the back, but close enough to the aisle to get a good look. The man next to me hummed a wedding march. As the actual song started to play, Mr. Williams walked down the isle. He wore a nice suit. All white. Elaine would have joked about that. Mrs. Williams was crying now, crying and laughing at the same time. I wondered if my parents would cry tears of joy at my wedding. If they would like the suit on me.

The music swelled, and I turned to look at the bride’s entrance, only to find myself staring at a sea of smiling faces. Everyone was looking forward.

I turned around again quickly, my face burning red from embarassement. Mr. Williams was standing in front of a priest. The man in black took both my teacher’s hands.

"Adam“, he said, „you are here now to join in the holy bond of marriage.“

"Join whom?“, I asked the man next to me.

He looked at me like I was an idiot. "God."

"God is graceful“, the priest said, „even to those who cannot be with Women. They will serve Him. They will do it as good as any of us. Even better, for it takes so, oh so much *courage* to be you." He gave Mr. Williams a box. The rings. "Oh Adam. Thank you for being honest with us. You are so brave. " 

Mr Williams turned around, facing his Church. The gold shone beautifully in his hands. It wasn’t a ring though. It was a knife. A beautiful knife, but still a kife. I swallowed hard.

"I, Adam Williams, take the Church to be my lawfully wedded wife“, Mr. Williams said. His voice sounded... off. He was slurring a little, but he continued. "to have and to hold from this day forward. For better, for worse, for rich, for poorer. For.. uhm... what was the last part?“

I wanted to get up, but I couldn’t move.

The priest smiled patiently. "In sickness and health“, he made a vague gesture towards his own wrists, "until death do us part.“

"Right“, Mr. Williams nodded. "In sickness and health“, he sliced his left wrist, "until death do us part.“ He slashed his other wrists.

"You may kiss the bride“, the priest said as Adam Williams fell to the ground.

I winced at the thud. Then, I winced harder at the sounds my teacher made. He was definitely still alive. I knew I should have screamed, or ran forward. It would have been the right thing to do. But my head hurt. A thousand faces smiled at me. I didn’t want them to turn into frowns.

So, the only thing I managed to do was turn to my neighbor.

"I’m sorry“, I whispered, "but... uhm... is he killing himself?“

"He’s taking the Church as his bride“, the man answered, "the only way he can love us and God properly is in death.“ He had tears in his eyes. "Sorry, I know I shouldn’t cry, but... oh, his mother must be so proud."

There was awkward silence as my favorite teacher bleed out on the ground. It took a while. I think is first cut wasn’t deep enough. He had to make more. I just... sat there. I wanted to look, I desperately wanted to look. But at the same time, I was ashamed. And that shame got worse with every passing second.

After a while, I finally mustered up enough courage to speak to my neighbor again.

"Uhm, I’m sorry, Sir, I have a question. Don’t the Catholics oppose suicide?“, I asked, "like... really strongly?“

He shrugged. "This isn’t suicide, this is love. Besides, we aren’t Catholics.“

"So...“, I made another awkward pause, "a cult, then?“

He raised his eyebrows. "We prefer the term 'free church.‘“

Mr. Williams made a gargling sound, and I couldn’t help myself anymore. I looked at him.

My teacher’s gaze met mine, but I don’t think he saw me. His eyes were already dead.

And he was still stabbing.

  


They covered him in a white sheet. His mother wanted me to stay for the reception, but I claimed my parents didn't allow me at places with alcohol. That seemed plausible to her. She was still crying tears of joy.

They have re-uploaded the death announcement. The correct time, now.

Coroner said it was a suicide, which I suppose it was technically true. But the coroner also put the time of death half a day after Mr. Williams’s supposed marriage. And claim there were no drugs in his system. Maybe that's right. Maybe Mr. Williams was actually sober during the whole thing. I don’t know. I don’t know what they did to him, or if they really did anything at all. That’s how everyone makes it seem: Like nothing ever happened. Even though we are such a small town, his death barely made any waves.

"He’s with the Lord now“, my politic's teacher said. Live goes on.

In class, I sit next to Elaine and keep my mouth shut, like I always do. My best friend asked many questions after Mr. Williams’s wedding and death, but eventually, she stopped. Still, she doesn’t leave me. Elaine is lovely. She smells like rain and vanilla.

But whenever I try to breath her in now, the smell of blood and incense seems to get all mixed up in it. Mr. Williams has taught me his final lesson.

Now, I keep my hand to myself.
