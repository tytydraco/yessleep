This is hell, literally **HELL**. I'm just running away right now, from my house because I don't want that thing to catch me. Well let me tell you all what was happening for the past few days.

**Monday, 27th July**

That night, I was lying on my bed. I was literally bored and couldn't sleep because the AC was not working. So yeah, I turned on my radio and started tuning it. Well yeah, I didn't have a T.V. in my room but to be honest, I liked my radio more than the T.V. in my dad's room. Well I was tuning it and literally had zero hopes of getting a valid channel at 3:00 a.m. But after 10-15 minutes of tuning, it really broadcasted a channel but listening to that channel was my worst mistake.

That signal didn’t sound like any radio channel I’d ever heard before.

At first, I thought it was just static… but it wasn’t normal static. It had a rhythm. Like breathing.

Slow… uneven… almost… *alive*.

I was about to switch it off when a voice cut through.

Not clearly. Not cleanly.

It sounded like multiple voices speaking at once, overlapping, like they were fighting to be heard.

>

I froze.

My hand was still on the dial, but I couldn’t move it.

Then the signal stabilised for a second.

Just one voice this time.

Calm. Mechanical. Almost like those emergency broadcasts.

>

My heart started pounding.

Restricted? Like military or something? I actually thought I’d accidentally tuned into something illegal, like some secret government communication.

I should’ve turned it off right there.

But I didn’t.

And that was my first mistake.

The voice continued, but something was wrong.

It wasn’t talking *to everyone*.

It was talking to **me**.

>

I laughed nervously. I actually laughed, bro. I thought it was some kind of creepy prank broadcast.

Then it said my name.

Exactly. Perfectly.

No distortion. No static.

Just my name.

That’s when the temperature in my room dropped.

I’m not even exaggerating.

The fan wasn’t working, the AC was dead, but suddenly it felt… cold.

Like someone had opened a door to winter.

The radio started making this low humming noise, and the voice came back—this time… closer.

>

The signal cut for a second.

Silence.

Then—

Knocking.

Three knocks.

From inside my room.

Not the door.

Not the window.

Behind me.

From the wall.

I turned slowly, my whole body shaking.

There was nothing there.

Just the wall.

But the knocking came again.

Three times.

Slower this time.

Like… it knew I was listening.

I grabbed the radio and switched it off immediately.

The room went completely silent.

No voice. No humming.

No knocking.

I just stood there, staring at the wall, trying to convince myself I imagined everything.

After a few minutes, I laughed it off.

Yeah… I actually convinced myself it was nothing.

I went to sleep.

Tuesday.

Everything was normal.

Almost.

The radio turned on by itself at 3:00 a.m.

I woke up to that same static.

That same… breathing sound.

My heart dropped instantly.

I didn’t even touch it.

I just stared at it from my bed.

And then the voice came back.

Clearer than before.

Closer than before.

>

I jumped out of bed and unplugged the radio completely.

The sound didn’t stop.

It kept playing.

Even without power.

That’s when I heard it again.

The knocking.

Not from the wall this time.

From the door.

Three knocks.

My parents were asleep. I checked. I literally ran to their room—they were both there, completely unaware.

When I came back…

My door was slightly open.

I’m 100% sure I closed it.

And the radio was back on.

That’s when I saw it.

For just one second.

Standing in the corner of my room.

Too tall.

Too still.

Not facing me.

The voice whispered:

>

I ran.

I didn’t even think.

I just ran out of my house.

Barefoot. No phone charger. Nothing.

I’ve been outside for hours now, typing this from my phone.

And I swear… I can still hear it.

Not from the radio anymore.

Not from my house.

From behind me.

If I stop typing…

If I stop moving…

I think it gets closer.

I shouldn’t have tuned into that frequency.

I don’t think it was a broadcast.

I think it was a **door**.

And I opened it.

If anyone knows anything about restricted radio frequencies… or signals that shouldn’t exist…

Please tell me.

Because I don’t think it’s just in my house anymore.
