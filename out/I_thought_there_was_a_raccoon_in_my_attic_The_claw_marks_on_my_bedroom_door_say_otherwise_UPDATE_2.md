Thanks everyone for your concern. Turns out there's more to the story after I got home. Here's a recount.

\----

When I saw the thing closing the attic panel, taking my wife's dress with it, I froze. Then I heard the shower shut off in the master bathroom.

Sarah.

I nearly collapsed from relief. I thought the thing had her too.

I threw open the bedroom door just as she came out of the bathroom, drying her hair with a towel.

"Why is the refrigerator open?" she asked.

I stared at her. "That's your first question?"

She frowned. "What should it be?"

I pointed toward the kitchen and just stood there. Pointing. 

When I got home earlier I expected the house to be destroyed. But it wasn’t. The refrigerator door stood open. Food containers were scattered across the kitchen counter. But aside from that, everything looked normal. For a second I wondered if I'd imagined the whole thing.

"Half the food is gone." I finally said.

"More than half." She looked at me. "You know, if you're stress eating again, there are healthier ways to deal with things."

"It wasn't me." I shot back.

"Was it the ghost?" She quipped.

The fact that she said it so casually bothered me more than it should have.

"Something is still up there." I pointed toward the attic panel. "The thing got past the boards."

That finally got her attention. She walked over and examined the scratches around the frame. The deep gouges in the wood. The splintered edges.

"What do you think it is?" She asked.

"I don't know." I hesitated. "But, I think we should stay somewhere else tonight."

She laughed softly. "Michael, I've been sleeping in hotel rooms all week."

"I'm serious." My voice almost shrill.

"So am I." She took my hand. "I just want to sleep in my own bed. With my husband."

I wanted to tell her about the trapper. About the noises. About the missing clothes. About how the thing had been standing outside our bedroom door. Trying to get in.

Instead I said nothing.

“Tomorrow we’ll call an exterminator.” She offered.

“Yes. An exterminator.” I said without thinking

"But, tomorrow.” She continued. “Tonight, let’s go out to dinner. And then sleep in our house.”

I agreed. It would just be good to be out of the house. Even if just for the evening.

When we got home she was asleep within minutes.

I wasn't.

Every creak of the house made me sit upright. Every gust of wind sounded like claws moving across wood.

But nothing happened.

No scratching. No thumping. No attic panel sliding open. Just silence.

The next morning Sarah called the exterminator company.

"The same guy?" I asked.

"Why not?" She shot back.

"Because the last time he was here he spent twenty minutes talking about Atari." I whined.

"He already knows the house." She countered.

I couldn't exactly explain that the Atari conversation was the least concerning thing about his visit.

An hour later the exterminator arrived.

Sarah suggested we run to the grocery store while he inspected the attic. I thought it was a terrible idea. Then I remembered how badly I wanted to be somewhere else. Anywhere else.

So we left.

We were gone for maybe thirty minutes. When we returned, his van was still parked outside. The driver's door stood open. The exterminator was crouched inside.

At first I thought he was looking for something. Then I realized he was shaking. Blood trickled down one side of his face. His eyes looked unfocused. Like he wasn't seeing us. He was clutching something in both hands. Sarah's missing dress. The one that had vanished into the attic.

"Hey," I said carefully.

He flinched. His entire body jerked. For a moment I thought he might scream. Instead he whispered something. I couldn't make it out.

"What?" I asked gently.

His eyes finally found mine.

"It doesn't want you here anymore." Then he started crying.

Not loud, or dramatic. Just quiet, exhausted crying. The kind that comes after something breaks inside a person. We ended up calling his company to come get him.

That made two people. Two people broken by the thing in the attic.  The trapper and now the exterminator. Both seemingly tough men. Both absolutely terrified. Both saying the same thing.

Whatever was in the attic wanted us gone.

Oddly enough, Sarah seemed completely unfazed. And somehow her calmness made me feel calmer too.

That night was the first decent night of sleep I'd had in days.

The next morning every bit of food in the refrigerator was gone. Again.

My missing clothes had been piled neatly in the living room. And now some of Sarah's clothes had disappeared. Not just the dress. Several items. I expected her to finally panic. Instead she announced she was spending the day with a friend.

"A friend?" I asked.

"She's having some health issues." She responded.

"I'll come with you." I offered.

"No." She smiled, "she needs girl time."

I didn't like it. And liked being alone in the house even less. But eventually she convinced me.

After she left, the silence settled over everything. I sat in the kitchen for a while trying to decide where to start cleaning.

Then I heard it.

Thump. Scratch. Scrape. The sound came from directly above me. The attic. My stomach dropped. The noises grew louder.

Then came the sound I hated most. Wood sliding against wood. The attic panel opening.

I froze.

A few seconds later I heard another sound. My bedroom. The handle turning and the door slowly creaking open.

Whatever was up there had gone into the bedroom.

I didn’t know what to do. Should I run? Should I trap it in the bedroom? 

I looked around for something. Anything to give me an idea of what to do. 

For some reason I grabbed the largest kitchen knife I could find and moved toward the hallway.

I peered around the corner of the hallway, toward the bedroom. The door was almost closed again. Just before it shut, I caught a glimpse of something. Only a glimpse. Even now I can't swear it was real. My first thought was that it had to be over seven feet tall.

My second thought was that maybe it wasn't standing at all.

The shape seemed to bend where no body should bend. One elongated arm, or maybe a leg, disappeared into the darkness above the doorway. For a moment it looked less like something entering the room and more like something lowering itself into it.

Also, the thing was black. Not dark. Black. Like light simply didn't touch it.

The door clicked shut.

I stood there listening. Something shuffled around inside. Drawers opening. Objects moving.

Then I had an idea.

At the far end of the hallway sat a small table. It just held a vase with flowers. I placed my phone on it. If the thing came back out, maybe I'd finally have proof.

I started recording and then ducked around the corner.

Not a second later the bedroom door handle turned. The door creaked open. Movement. It was slow and deliberate. It wasn’t footsteps or crawling. Just movement.

Then I heard breathing. Heavy. Wet.

It grew closer. Closer. And beneath it...

Click.

Click.

Click.

It was a mix of bird chirps and knuckles cracking.

The breathing stopped. The clicking didn't. I realized it was listening. Looking for me.

There was more movement. The clicking and breathing were close. I swear I could feel it vibrating against my skin. The thing was just on the other side of the wall from me. Just around the corner.

I reached down for the knife. My arm brushed slightly against the wall. It made enough of a noise that the clicking stopped.

I held my breath.

The breathing on the other side of the wall changed slightly. It was more deliberate. Short, quick breaths. It was smelling the air. It was smelling me.

I reached my hand out toward the knife. My fingers touched the handle. 

As I lifted it, the blade scraped lightly across the hardwood floor. The sound seemed deafening.

Everything went silent.

Then sound and movement exploded. The table crashed over. 

I stumbled backward. Raised the knife. Ready to defend.

I waited. Nothing came around the corner. Instead I heard frantic movement racing away from me. Toward the attic. Toward the ceiling.

Then, BANG.

The attic panel slammed shut.

Silence.

For several seconds I couldn't move. Then I remembered the phone. I ran to the overturned table. The phone wasn't there. I searched the hallway. Nothing. It had taken it.

The only footage I might ever have captured was now sitting somewhere in the attic.

Just then the front door burst open. I spun around with the knife raised.

Sarah screamed.

Then she saw the hallway. The overturned table. The broken vase. My face.

Her look of terror turned to empathy. I almost burst into tears. 

Instead of crying I told her everything. Every detail. The noises. The sighting. The missing phone. The thing hunting me in the hallway. When I finished, I expected disbelief. Fear. Anger. All of the things I had felt.

Instead she looked tired. Like someone that has been holding a secret far too long.

"There is always something in the attic, Michael." She finally said.

I stared at her. "What?"

She looked toward the ceiling. "There has always been something in the attic." Her voice dropped almost to a whisper. "For as long as I can remember."

I stared at her before finally asking "Why didn’t you tell me?"

She looked confused. “Because it never bothered anyone before.”

I was dumbfounded. “It tried to get into the bedroom. It knocked me off the ladder. It wanted to attack me.”

All she could say was, “I know. I know.”

Sarah was quiet for a long time. Then she looked at me and whispered, "I thought it liked you."
