I am going to try to write this as clearly as I can because I am very tired and I am scared that if I do not write it down tonight I will not be able to write it down tomorrow.

My grandmother died in February. She left me a ring. I had been the only granddaughter who visited her regularly in the last few years and the ring had been in her family for a long time, so it made sense. The ring came with no note and no instructions. My mother handed it to me in a velvet box at the funeral and gave me a long look I did not understand at the time.

It is a thin gold band with a small dark stone. The stone is not a diamond. I do not know what it is. It is older than I am. My mother thinks it is older than this country. I do not know. I never asked the right questions while my grandmother was alive.

I started wearing it the week after the funeral. Just to remember her. I am not married. The ring fit my middle finger snugly, and I liked the feel of it.

The first morning I woke up without it.

I was confused for about two minutes. I am not a person who takes off jewelry in my sleep. I sleep heavily and I do not move much. I checked the bed. I checked the floor around the bed. I found the ring on the bathroom counter, sitting in the soap dish. I assumed I had taken it off when I had gotten up to use the bathroom in the night and forgotten. I put it back on and I did not think about it until the next morning, when I woke up without it again.

That morning the ring was on the kitchen counter, next to the kettle.

I do not get up to make tea in the night. I am a heavy sleeper. I have not gotten up in the night in years. But I am also a rational person, so I assumed I had sleepwalked, and I bought a small camera and pointed it at my bed.

The footage from the first night shows me lying in bed from approximately 11:18 PM until 6:42 AM. I do not get up. I do not move my hand. The ring is on my finger when I fall asleep. The ring is on my finger at 11:47:23 PM in one frame, and at 11:47:24 PM in the next frame, the ring is gone.

There is no moment of removal. The ring is on my hand. Then it is not.

I bought more cameras. I put one in the bathroom, one in the kitchen, one in the hallway, and one pointed at the front door. I covered every room I could think of. I synchronized their timestamps. I went to bed.

The next morning the ring was on the doormat by the front door. Inside, not outside. The deadbolt was still locked.

The footage from all the cameras showed the same thing. Ring on my hand at 11:51:09 PM. Ring on the doormat at 11:51:09 PM. No movement of the ring through space in any frame of any camera. No flicker. No glitch. Just the ring, in one place, then in another place, in the same instant, in two different rooms.

I called my mother.

I did not tell her about the cameras. I told her the ring kept slipping off in the night and asked if she knew anything about it. She was quiet for a long time. Then she said, "Did your grandmother tell you how she got it?"

She had not.

My grandmother had a sister. The sister died young, in another state, in an apartment fire. The ring had belonged to the sister. My grandmother had taken it from the body before it was buried. She had worn it for sixty years. My mother said my grandmother had told her once, when she was drunk on a Christmas Eve in the late nineties, that she had stopped wearing the ring at night because she did not like where she found it in the morning.

My mother said this very quietly. Then she said, "I told her not to leave it to you."

I have been tracking the ring's location every morning for two weeks. The locations are not random. The ring is moving. Six feet on a slow night. Twenty feet on a fast one, and always in the same direction.

I plotted the locations on a map of my apartment. The trajectory exits my apartment through the front door. I checked the next morning and the ring was in the building hallway. I checked the morning after that and the ring was in the parking lot. I have been finding the ring further and further from where I left it, and the direction is consistent.

I am not a religious person and I do not believe in a lot of things, but I drew a line on a wider map this evening, extending the trajectory in the direction the ring is moving, and the line passes within a mile of the address where my grandmother's sister died. The address is more than a thousand miles away.

At the rate the ring is moving, it will get there in about three years.

That is not the part that scared me into writing this.

I went back through the camera footage tonight, all of it, every night. I had been watching the ring. I had not been watching the room. In the footage from last Thursday, in the bottom right corner of the bedroom camera, just behind the bedroom door, there is a hand.

I do not mean a person. I do not mean an arm. I mean a hand. Reaching around the door frame at the level of the floor. Fingers spread on the carpet. It is in the frame for less than half a second.

The next night it is in the frame for a full second. The hand has moved further into the room.

The hand has been getting closer every night. I went back and counted. Nine nights. Nine inches.

The bedroom door is open right now. I have not slept yet. I am sitting up in bed with the lamp on and the camera pointed at the door and the ring on the bedside table.

Whatever has been pulling the ring back to my grandmother's sister has been coming for me at the same time.

I do not know if the hand is coming for the ring or for me. I do not know whether it stops when the ring leaves the building.

Tonight is night ten.

I am going to stay awake.
