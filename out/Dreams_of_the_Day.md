actual post:

I'll make something very clear right now. I don’t work, I live with my parents, have very few friends and as you might have guessed – No love partner.  
The only thing preventing me from being a total neet is that I at least go to college, though the classes are at night, leaving my day completely unnocupied.  
Since I go to class at night I often stay up very late, either studying or just finding myself unable to shut my brain off after leaving campus.

The point is that if i don’t have any projects that i have to work on for class and there are no exams i often just sleep during the day. 

And every time I do, I dream. I’m not sure if people realize but dreams you have during the day are far different than the ones you have at night. It's all lucid, you always know that you’re dreaming and you don’t lose your rationale.   
That sounds incredible at first, but it’s not.   
People assume lucid necessarily means that you can control anything in your dreams, defy the laws of physics. But that's not true for Day Dreams. You’re aware of all of your surroundings but you are subject to the rules of this world.

I have been “day-dreaming” for a long time now. 

The furthest I can remember is when I was still a baby. I have this vivid memory of a dream I had where everything was black and white, black smoke filled the skies of my neighborhood and on the gate of my house there were these giant rectangles stamped. I remember even back then feeling unsettled by all of this.

When I was around two years old, I remember having this dream where my parents were trapped in a ferris wheel by… a My litte pony’s character. I was too young to question this and it terrified me. So much so that at the end of the dream I remember going up to the attic of my house to meet this character and agreeing to something so that my parents could be released. I don’t remember what I agreed to, all that I know is that I woke up shortly after that.

Now I'm telling these dreams in sequence because in my head they’re connected somehow. I can’t think of one without thinking of the other, even though they were probably at least a few months apart.

After that most dreams I had were pretty tame, so much so that I only remember a few, mostly because they were pleasant or interesting.

As I grew up and started going to school I couldn't sleep during the day anymore, so these dreams ceased entirely.

That is until 2020. Now i don’t have to explain this to you, the world completely shut down due to Covid and every single school soon started switching to online classes. At that point I was already twelve, so old enough to have some rationale but also young enough to not give one single crap about school. You can figure where this goes. I would join class via Zoom or Teams meetings but immediately after either play minecraft, roblox or just sleep.  
This is when those weird Day dreams started to become part of my life again. But honestly they were good. I had this dream constantly where I was in a place where the floor was made of fluffy clouds, the skies were blue, no one could get hurt and everything that I wanted I could create only thinking of it. I could even hear what was being lectured while I was dreaming, so at least I was learning something after all.

This lasted a litte over a year until the middle of 2021. I was day dreaming again in my personal paradise when I heard the teacher randomly choosing students to answer a few questions she had prepared.   
I started panicking, realizing that I would be caught as absent if she chose me and I didn't answer.  
I tried everything to wake up, I stabbed a knife into my chest, nothing, I didn't feel anything. I jumped off a tall ledge. Nothing, not a scratch. I ran through this endless layer of clouds until I found an exit. There was a hole in the sky, a doorframe positioned right into the skybox, almost as if it was a portal. On the other side of this frame there were stairs that led downwards. Their style was completely different to anything I had seen in this paradise, the walls and floor looked like bare, dirty and weathered concrete. It was all dimly lit in either yellow or red lighting – I can't remember exactly. I ran downstairs and on the second bend I found a rusty gateway that led to this dark hallway, so dark that I couldn't actually make out anything on the other side. I shook the gate, screaming to be let out, saying I would do anything to wake up.   
The gate opened– and this is where my memory of the dream starts getting extremely sparse. All I know is that there was someone on the other side and I signed or agreed to something.

I woke up immediately after that, and I guess it served me right that in the end the teacher didn’t even choose me to answer anything.

But what's strange is that after that I never had that “paradise” dream ever again. Mind you I would have this dream every single day. But after that it just stopped. In fact i even stopped dreaming at all, it was all just shut eye, wake up a few hours later.

Soon enough Covid restrictions started getting lifted and I guess I became more responsible so I wouldn't sleep during class anymore.

Everything was normal for a while.

Until this year. Last year I graduated from high school and enrolled into college.

You already know the deal, my whole day is unoccupied.

So I started sleeping during the day again. And again these days dreams started popping up. This time though they were just… places. Places that resemble locations I've been to before with just one thing wrong. Or places I've never been to, created by my mind.

I’m fully lucid when I'm on them, but again I have no control. All I can do is walk.

They make litte sense when thinking about the rules of our universe but at least are architecturally sound. I’ve dreamt of an empty museum with jarring exhibits and even an elevator that doubled as a time machine. 

A Gas station, perpetually stuck at sun down only lit up by that wonderful pink and orange sky. 

The weird thing about them though is that it feels like I can only leave when they let me.

I remember I was on this mountain-side pathway taking in the view, only to hear my alarm going off. I tried waking up but I couldn't. I finally gave in and tried the extreme method of throwing myself off over the guard rails, hoping that would wake me up. What followed was me hitting these jagged rocks and just feeling an extremely sharp pain. Pain that coursed through my whole body. I felt like that for at least ten seconds until I actually woke up.

I know I sound insane but it's almost as if this place was holding me, and let me go begrudgingly only after I managed to actually die there. 

This trend continued, not only of places holding me but also this weirdly realistic feeling everything had. I could feel everything I touched, every single pain, everything I drank and ate. 

The problem with that is that it doesn’t matter the place that I dream of, I can only be let go once it lets me.

Sometimes the places are unsettling, sometimes they are nice, I don't control what I get.

Recently what has been getting me is that these places feel more and more like they don’t wanna let me go. No matter how much I try to wake up for any reason they won’t let me until a certain time has passed. 

It almost feels like they have adapted. They know I'll try to jump off a ledge, they know I'll try to stab my chest. They removed all of these things so i can’t do anything. 

The worst happened yesterday. I had this dream where I was in a bathroom, a tiny cramped bathroom. I could smell the shit, I could feel my feet cold and damp on this floor covered in piss.   
I didn’t wanna be there, so I tried waking up. But it wouldn’t let me. I pounded my fist on the wall, I screamed. But nothing happened. As you could guess there was nothing in this bathroom I could use to “free myself” without having to hurt myself much more beforehand. I waited for what felt like ages inside of that stinking bathroom until I finally woke up.

This is the latest experience I've had. 

I know you might think the solution is simple, “don’t sleep during the day”. And it might be that simple but changing habits is hard.   
I fear that if I slip up and do it one more time that it won’t let me go anymore.

While i’m writing this it's currently 4 AM. I pray that they don’t consider that day. If I ever enter a coma while sleeping during the day– Just know that I'm there. Talk to me. I will hear you. And if I really don't wake up. Kill me.
