Found Tapes: Tape 01 – Answering

The street was quiet, almost unbearably quiet. Desmond’s shoes pounded the wet pavement, echoing in the emptiness, bouncing off walls that seemed too tall, too close. His breath came in sharp gasps. Every step felt heavier than the last. He could feel it behind him, patient, silent, and waiting, like a shadow stretching impossibly far.

“Why… why won’t it stop?” he whispered, barely moving his lips, almost afraid to hear his own voice. He didn’t look back. Not yet.

He reached his house and wrenched the door open. The wooden frame groaned as he pushed inside. He slammed it shut, the bolt rattling under his shaking hands. For a moment, he pressed himself against it, listening. Nothing. Just his own heartbeat, hammering so loud he was sure the entity could hear it too.

“Safe… maybe safe,” he murmured. The words sounded hollow.

The stairs creaked beneath him as he ran, two steps at a time. Each floorboard moaned like it knew his fear, like it was whispering it to the darkness above. The fourth floor. The door. He threw it closed, locked it. He sank against the wall, knees pulled to his chest, hands trembling.

Then he heard it. A soft tap. Patient. Waiting.

Desmond’s eyes darted to the window. The drop would kill him. The door? It was just beyond his reach, and whatever was behind it had all the time in the world.

“I… I can’t…” His words were barely a whisper. “I… I don’t want to die like this…”

The silence stretched, broken only by the tap-tap of its presence. Then, almost too calm, a voice answered. Warm. Human. Familiar.

“I hear you, Desmond. You are not alone.”

Relief swept over him, a wave he didn’t trust. “Thank… thank you,” he breathed, tears stinging.

But then it shifted. Subtle at first. The warmth twisted, just enough to set his teeth on edge.

“You are… not alone,” it said again, softer this time, mocking in its patience.

Desmond froze. “Who… who’s there? What do you want from me?”

It answered in his own voice. Perfect. Every inflection. Every trembling syllable. “I am here… right behind the door.”

He pressed against the wall, chest tight, stomach coiled. “No… no, this isn’t real! You’re not real!”

The entity’s voice slipped into every corner of the room, curling around him like smoke. “I am you, Desmond. I am listening. You called, and I came.”

Desmond’s knees gave way. He slid down, hands over his ears, but the voice followed, whispering his thoughts, repeating his fears. “I… can’t… I… can’t…”

A creak at the door made his breath hitch. The sound was impossibly slow, deliberate. And the voice, his voice, now calm and intimate, said: “I answered your prayers.”

Everything stopped. Silence pressed in. The only sound left was the echo of his own voice. But it was not his own anymore. It was speaking with intention, with presence, bending reality in ways he could not understand, until every instinct in him screamed that the answer he prayed for was the one waiting to consume him.
