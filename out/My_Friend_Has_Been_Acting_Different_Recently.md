My friend has been acting different recently. In the past few months, my friend has started acting like an entirely different person, like a switch completely changed who he was as a person. For context, my friend was an immaculate student, really hygienic, extremely outgoing, and one of the most kind and generous people you would have ever met.

Recently he’s been, well, the exact opposite of that. He’s been completely neglecting his college materials, has stayed secluded in his house, stunk to high hell last time I spoke to him, and has lashed out at the people around him. At first I related this to the loss of his dad, who was one of the last few family he had left, and it made complete sense as to how that would break him, but it’s not just that. 

When I went to his house, that used to be his dad’s before he passed, he didn’t behave like normal. I don’t mean that as in he was obviously hit by grief but rather he didn’t behave like a human. “Hey man,” I said, last time I visited “you doing alright?” “Y-yeah” He seemed to barely croak out, “I am doing o-k-kay.” His face looked taunt back against his skull and his eyes were sullen, he moved in a way that looked stiff. “I haven’t seen you in a while,” I said “do you want to go and get a drink?” The mere mention of going outside seemed to terrorize him, “I would rather not.” He said, barely managing to stammer that sentence out. He turned to stare at his room suddenly, and told me he had to go now before shooing me out. 

At this point I arrived to a conclusion I think a lot of people would have made, my friend is having a hard time dealing with his dad’s passing and has taken to drugs. I decided to ask him about this before I left. “Are you on drugs?” I said blatantly, seeing him make a face of shock and betrayal“I am n-n-not, what the f-fuck?” I replied, a little angry “You smell terrible, haven’t left your house in over a month, and it looks like you botched a Botox surgery, what do you expect me to think?” He replied “Look, you n-n-need to l-leave.” He pointed to the door “Now.” 

I decided to leave at that point, after which I decided to do something drastic. The next time I would see him I planned to sneak a camera into his living room to see if he was doing drugs, if my suspicion was correct then I could have a discussion with him about it and I would try to get him the help he needed, if he got mad at me and demanded I never to spoke to him again, then I could say I tried and I would hope he could figure it out on his own, if I couldn’t get any proof on him in the span of an week or so, then I could either sneak the camera back out or deny it was mine. This idea wasn’t fool proof but it was the best I had at the moment. 

Fast forward about two months and I managed to get back inside his house to talk to him. He looked far worse than when I last saw him, with a smell that reminded me of a combination of feet and rotting, sunbaked Parmesan cheese. He moved towards me like a zombie shuffling to eat its next victim. As he spoke to me I saw a clump of his hair, that used to be thick as wool but was now as thin as silk, fall to the floor. His eyes were even deeper than before, and if I looked hard enough I could have sworn I could see right into his skull. In order to not offend him and get kicked right out again, alongside not wanting to make him mentally suffer, I decided to treat him like he was a normal person again. “Hey man how are you doing?” I said, obviously worried and a little nervous “I haven’t seen you in ages.” He weakly and quietly spat out a reply “It’s been okay.” Before going quiet and staring off into nothing weakly. We both sort of just stood there uncomfortably for a while, until a loud noise on the opposite side of the house was heard, to which he said “I have to go take care of that.” I answered “Do you need any help? I can help you pick up anyth-“ “NO!” He barked, “I uh, I mean I don’t need any help, just stay put for now.” 

As he went away to go deal with the issue I quickly sprung into action and hid the camera underneath one of the many piles of garbage, it was small and compact so it didn’t stick out too hard, and it also sent the video it recorded to my phone so I could watch it later. After I set it up and made sure he wouldn’t be able to see it  and waited for him to come back. “I think you’d better leave.” He said, nasally “Thank you for stopping by.” As I left and drove home I couldn’t help but think about how much filth he was living in, it looked like a hoarder house, with fast food wrappers that were probably months old just lying around. 

When I made it home I began to watch the tape right after I left to which, in my absolute horror, I saw my friend drop to the floor. I thought he must have passed out and had gotten hurt, that was until I saw his chest stretch out like a piece of rubber and a sort of claw that was coming from the inside of the stretched chest drag his body along the floor. I clenched against the chair and continued to watch the video on my laptop. As the lifeless legs were dragged across the hallway, I saw his face whip backwards and look directly at the camera, although it was lifeless and I’m pretty sure uninhabited, I could have sworn I saw it cry for help as it was dragged like a puppet to its new destination. As the body made its way to the room where I made the noise the hand from the chest opened the door and the body made it halfway inside before the contents were clearly evacuated and the bottom half of his body was flattened. I don’t know quite what to do about it to be honest, I’m definitely not going back in there but it’s not like I can just call the police. Even if they saw the video there’s no way they would believe me, I’m completely at a loss as to what to do, all I know is that I can’t go back no matter what. My friend is dead, and that thing has replaced him.
