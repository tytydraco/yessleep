&#x200B;

I was losing my mind from work stress. 

&#x200B;

The pressure was unreal—constant emails, non-stop Zoom calls, and a mountain of daily BS were suffocating me.

&#x200B;

I just needed to get away, somewhere totally quiet where I could disconnect. 

&#x200B;

I ended up booking a "Finca"—which is basically an old rustic house—through an agency called Eco Ibiza. 

&#x200B;

It was located all the way in Sant Joan de Labritja, in the northern part of the island. 

&#x200B;

It’s a super isolated area surrounded by thick, empty woods, completely away from the loud tourists and the regular party scene.

&#x200B;

On my second day there, I was walking around the quiet alleys of the old town when I met this girl named Lilia. 

&#x200B;

She had this weird, quiet vibe to her that completely set her apart from everyone else on the island. 

&#x200B;

She took one look at my exhausted face, smiled, and said, "You look like you're carrying the weight of the world. 

&#x200B;

Just sitting alone in a room isn't going to fix you. Tonight, there's a real party deep in the woods".

&#x200B;

Before I could even ask where, she pulled out this old, folded paper map, drew a big red mark right in the middle of nowhere, and said, "Just follow the map. 

When you get to the end, you'll know where to go."

&#x200B;

I drove for almost an hour on these sketchy, narrow dirt roads that twisted through the pitch-black hills. 

&#x200B;

When I finally reached the spot on the map, I shut off the engine. 

&#x200B;

It was dead silent and pitch black.

&#x200B;

I got out of the car, and right then, I felt this low, heavy thumping vibrating through the trees. 

&#x200B;

It was the sound of primitive drums. 

&#x200B;

I followed the noise on foot, walking deeper into the thick woods, totally hooked by curiosity.

&#x200B;

The path finally opened up into a huge clearing. 

&#x200B;

The scene was bizarre, but my burnt-out brain didn't register it as a threat at first. 

&#x200B;

Dozens of people were dancing in frantic circles. 

&#x200B;

They weren't wearing normal clothes. 

&#x200B;

They wore lightweight burlap robes adorned with scattered strands of dry straw, the loose fibers swaying gently as they danced around the fire.

&#x200B;

In the very center, there was a long platform made from a massive tree trunk. 

&#x200B;

On top of it, four men were tied up tightly with thick ropes.

&#x200B;

What's crazy is that I didn't think much of it because these tied-up guys weren't screaming or begging. 

&#x200B;

They were laughing hysterically and singing these weird, old songs, like they were totally high on something or maybe it was just some avant-garde interactive show. 

&#x200B;

I was so desperate to escape my own head that I just threw myself into the crowd. 

&#x200B;

That's when Lilia appeared out of nowhere, spinning around like crazy. 

&#x200B;

She looked absolutely breathtaking under the firelight, her hair flying wild, sweat glistening on her skin, and this magnetic, raw energy radiating from her. 

&#x200B;

As we started dancing together, the heat from the massive fire and the thick summer humidity wrapped around us, becoming intense and suffocating. 

&#x200B;

But I didn't care. 

&#x200B;

For the first time in months, the heavy weight in my chest vanished. 

&#x200B;

The overwhelming warmth, the pounding rhythm, and the sheer, hypnotic beauty of this girl made me feel incredibly, fiercely alive. 

&#x200B;

I was laughing, completely losing myself in the moment, feeling a rush of pure euphoria I hadn't felt in years.

&#x200B;

Then, without any warning, the drums stopped instantly.

&#x200B;

Before I could even process what was happening, a few men wearing masks made of dried mud stepped forward. 

&#x200B;

With quick, and terrifyingly cold movements, they slit the throats of all four tied-up men at the exact same time right across that wooden trunk.

&#x200B;

Time literally froze. Blood sprayed everywhere, raining down like a thick, hot red mist over the crowd. 

&#x200B;

I stood there, completely paralyzed, feeling the warm, wet blood splatter across my face and clothes.

&#x200B;

I expected people to scream and run, but the exact opposite happened. 

&#x200B;

The crowd went absolutely feral. 

&#x200B;

They started dancing even harder under the bloody rain, with these huge, creepy grins on their faces, smearing the fresh blood all over their straw suits.

&#x200B;

That's when it hit me. 

&#x200B;

I hadn't stumbled into a party.

&#x200B;

This was a hellish ritual for something I never believed existed.

&#x200B;

I grabbed Lilia hard, shaking from head to toe, trying to drag her away from this slaughterhouse. 

&#x200B;

But she wasn't scared at all. 

&#x200B;

She was laughing and dancing in pure ecstasy, her eyes wide and completely vacant. 

&#x200B;

She screamed over the roaring crowd: "Don't be afraid! They want this! They chose this honor! All this blood, all this madness... it's all for him. 

&#x200B;

For our beloved, our leader, our king of kings!"

&#x200B;

Right then, the blood-soaked people locked hands, forming a massive circle around the clearing. 

&#x200B;

They started chanting together in a deep, heavy voice that made the ground shake under my feet. 

&#x200B;

Lilia led them, dancing like a maniac as they chanted:

&#x200B;

"O Cernunnos, guardian of the ancient woods,

You who cross the shadows between roots and mist,

We hear the thud of your steps in the silence of the night,

And we see your antlers loom among the oak branches.

&#x200B;

O lord of beasts and forgotten paths,

O spirit of the wilderness that never sleeps,

The seasons turn in your name,

And the rivers whisper your ancient secrets."

&#x200B;

With the very last line of the chant, every single torch went out at the exact same time. 

&#x200B;

Total darkness. 

&#x200B;

From behind the bloody platform, a massive shadow began to rise, towering way over the trees. 

&#x200B;

I couldn’t distinguish its features at all, it was just a strange, blurry, and distorted shape, but it was way larger and taller than any human.  

&#x200B;

Then, something stepped out from the deep shadows behind the platform. 

&#x200B;

I couldn’t distinguish its features at all—it was just a strange, blurry, and distorted shape, but it was way larger and bulkier than any human. 

&#x200B;

Coming straight out of its head were these massive, terrifying deer antlers. 

&#x200B;

It didn't pay any attention to the crowd or to me. 

&#x200B;

It just reached over the platform, gathered the four dead bodies from the wooden trunk, and dragged them back into the pitch-black woods. 

&#x200B;

The choking smell of copper and the primordial wilderness filled the air.

&#x200B;

And then, just like that, the torches flickered back to life.

&#x200B;

Everyone stood up, and even though their bodies were completely smeared with blood, they just casually started leaving like absolutely nothing had happened. 

&#x200B;

People were laughing, chatting about random things, and walking back down the trail as if they were just leaving a regular festival. 

&#x200B;

Lilia didn't say a word about the blood, the bodies, or the creature. 

&#x200B;

She didn't try to explain anything. 

&#x200B;

She just turned to me, smiled the most peaceful, beautiful smile I'd ever seen, and said, "Now we will be blessed until next year."

&#x200B;

She reached out and took my shaking hand, gently guiding me back toward the path. 

&#x200B;

And the most terrifying part of it all? As her warm fingers wrapped around mine, a part of me actually felt a deep, overwhelming sense of relief. 

&#x200B;

The crushing anxiety, the burnout, the endless work pressure... it was all completely gone. 

&#x200B;

It has been a very, very long time since I felt this kind of peace.
