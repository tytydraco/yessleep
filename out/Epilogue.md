Arizona became my new life in January 2010. I found myself working at Owen's, a sports bar in Sun City. I still work there today, all these years later. 

My main manager, Jim is the only coworker I have had that has remained for the past 16 years. I was given the title of the bar manager, not that it really meant much. This place was just like the gas station. We were friends, and we would get drunk together many times after work, but I could never come to tell him what happened to me.

Truth be told, we were never about sharing personal details or burdens. Just two dudes who enjoyed beer, football, and the occasional long hikes in the mountains in the Valley of the Sun. He was better than my other coworkers, who came and went in all these past years more times than I could ever begin to count. Yet, after I left California, I stopped being interested in meeting people and building a big network in my social life. I lived for bartending, and photography when I was not at Owen's.

In 2018, I thought I really had left everything from my encounter with Tyler behind. I could never forget that night, but I felt that enough time had passed where I could truly feel that I was back into my own self again. I took up going outside and hiking for the first time in years then. 

I had not been back to my small town since I had left. My family knew I could not feel like myself by staying there, so they would always make the exhausting effort to come see me here. Eventually, my parents themselves got tired of all the unwanted attention, so they retired and moved to Sarasota, Florida. Bradley, my brother, went to college at San Diego State University, and he never looked back. He is married and has a 2 year old son.

So again, by the Monday in September 2018 that I again encountered the demon that came into this world, it seemed that things were supposed to be "normal" again. I no longer cried with my parents on the phone as much about that night. I was beginning to live life outside of simply working, my parents were finally enjoying their later years, and time had buried so many wounds.

It was a sunny day, as it often is in Phoenix. We opened the bar at 4. I found myself staring at the clock by 4:15. Everything was cleaned, no maintenance needed to be done. All that was desired was customers, and the door chime sounded loud about 5 minutes into staring at that clock while hearing random ESPN anchors talking in the background. The only work related thing I had even heard up to this point was Jim telling me he was going outside to smoke.

After I heard the chime, I look back and I saw an average height, 50 something year old man. He had thinning, slicked back hair and wore sunglasses so dark you could not see his eyes. This was a bar with tinted windows, so it felt like a bit much.

He sat down in the middle of the long row of stools. I had never seen this man before.

I approached him as I would with any other customer.

*"Welcome to Owen's, what can I get you today."*

*"I will get a Sam Adams beer please."*

And so I went to the draft faucet, held the glass diagonally, and poured him a nice cold and foamy beer. I could sure use this after work.

I turned around with the beer in my right hand, which was the more scarred of my two hands from when I was pulled out the window frame.

And it was when I turned around that he stared at me without the sunglasses. 

And I saw the piercing blue eyes again. I froze. And he was frozen too. Just staring at me.

All I could hear was the TV in the background.

*"Its ok, Jack."* He whispered

I dropped the glass of beer. I felt my shoes and socks get soaked and the aroma rising to my face. I was scared before, but now I was pissed.

*"Who the fuck are you?"* I yelled to him.

He stared at me for a moment.

*"I bore witness to you on the night you battled my savior and won."*

I wanted to cry, but I could not. All I could do was freeze and shut down. 

*"But you know you lost a piece of you. For the angel only truly came because it heard the screeching of the demon and the cries of all it crossed.*



*Do you know who I am, Jack?"*

I felt my jaw dropped and the tears steadily rolling down my cheeks. My hands were shaking. My stomach felt like a million stones were piling up inside, just like that night.

I knew who this was.

It was Tyler's dad*.* I vaguely remembered him when he would pick him up from school. He aged so much but he still looked like his son. The last time I had seen his face was from my hospital bed. He spoke to the press on the local news thanking the community for their support, and apologizing for what his son did. I remember when I was nearing my recovery and watching that. 

Somehow, that night I didn't care enough. I had no ill will towards him because his son was not a man of flesh and bones, but instead the darkest thing I thought I could come across in this universe. All I saw was a father who was lost in his own way just like I was. 

Until I left California, I had hoped he talk to me so we could find a resolution. But I never saw him and eventually, I gave up this hope. Giving up this hope was then forgotten within itself as time inevitably carried on.

*"Why are you here?"*

I could not cry anymore. I had cried enough. I wanted to know so badly what that night was.

*"Because our war with the higher being did not start the evening you sunk through the waters of Earth. I sent my son out because my Savior commanded me long ago in those woods of Trinity. He said that my son should be sent to hold the crown of all those who may roam in the dark woods as far as primitive man could explore. He, my savior teller to the Earthly demons told me it must be done at all costs or else the angelic forces would win. I hoped not to deceive but instead lay ruin without any challenge of man or saint."*

*"And yet you did deceive",* I thought to myself. We all thought you lost your son to the fate of other men or monsters, but instead it was the man who was supposed to protect him.

He spoke again.

*"You. You stopped us then. But we struck again. Again. and Again. We are not here to cross your path or to thwart you. Not today, or any day."*

He stood there in silence.

That was not a valid answer.

*"WHY THE FUCK ARE YOU HERE"* I don't think I have ever been this loud in my life. My scarred chest feels so numb doing this.

*"You won the game that the beings lay upon man. And so did I. You chose to fight the evil. I embraced it the night I fell into the pond*."

*"Embraced him how?"* I asked him. I never wanted to know anything more in my life before.

I held on to the nearest glass bottle of whiskey I could grab, it was under the bartop. I was ready to kill him, and I didn't care even if Jim would walk in to see it.

*"One day I saw a man just like you did. He told me that the Worshipped wanted a boy to be the man of the lands that man could reach on his own accord. Our town was where those lands started, who knows where it ends? He, really, really, really is nicer with boys than man. With the beings of youth he tricks, yet with the grown he does not ask or wait. He took me near the pond and in the void we went. He commanded me to bear him a son, so I lived everyday learning how a true demon of man deceives.*

*He is my teacher, and I must not fail him. But you, even if you are just a man, will never shine bright through your sight. So we fare on to the men who won't fight. The men who are truly mere"*

*"So why did you come back to me?"* I just wish he could tell me like any person would. But he was not a person.

He then yelled louder than I ever could. My ears just hurt even thinking about it now.

***"BECAUSE WE WILL NOT BE LEFT DEFINED BY WHAT YOU THWARTED AND YOU WILL KNOW THAT WE ROAM ACROSS ALL LANDS OF MAN AND MATTER. YOU WILL NOT RUN FROM US ONLY STAND BY US AS THOSE AWAY FROM YOU WILL SHOW THIS WORLD WHO WE ARE AND WHAT WE BRING ON BEHALF OF HIM. HE WANTS YOU TO KNOW THAT WE STILL WORSHIP AND LIVE FOR HIS BLESSING IN RETURN TO BRING ALL MORTALS TO THEIR MOST DEVASTATED. AND IF, IT WERE NOT OF THE ANGELS THAT THE DIVINE CREATED TO COUNTER US WE WOULD RULE ALL OF FATE"***

His eyes were bright blue again. He was slowly tearing up, and I just stood there frozen. All we could hear in the background was the ice machine working and the TV commentary.

He was right, is all I could realize as other demons sent to this world by the divine will strike, and I am of the side that survived. That was all I could think to myself.

He spoke in a broken voice. It was so faint, as if even the demon could not look at me and how I stopped his destiny.

*"Why did you stop us"* He asked softly, as if I had been the one to have sacrificed Tyler.

*"Because I will not live for your love of the wrath of man, but instead the love that the wrath seeks to destroy"* 

I was grasping the bottle so tightly and my hand was so warm and strained.

He spoke again.

  
*"Then let go of the bottle."* He spoke through his devastated tears. I could see how my survival of him all those years ago hurt him. He was vulnerable. I don't know why I felt bad.

I let go of the bottle.

*"You will not stop us as beings. You and I quarreled and the divine justly deemed us as equals. We roam together, with no more battles to give, and only the dual destinies of man that can be served by both of us."* His tears were gone. He was calm.

*"So be it."* I told him. 

*"So be it. Our souls live on, but only mine remains in the battle."* He told me.

*"And when does yours end?"* I wondered if such a being ever ended.

*"I gave Him my son and so he wanted him as king and then many more men and women for the kings to have served upon Him. He promised me that when I perish, I will not be absorbed into his demonic desires of man, but rather that I live out on this Earth for an eternity across as many men until this world is no more. I owe him no more sons, but instead I stay to this world so he knows how mortals evolve and fight, and ever since the day I drowned underground I have served his worship by being the eyes of Earth to him. All the other eyes know of you and your triumph as they see you while looking for mankinds easiest and most desirable for loss.",* He told this to me in the calmest way possible.

And then I saw it. I knew what Tyler meant when I told him I thwarted the bad in this world. That the light and dark of this world will never stop fighting. I can see my light winning in my life and existence, but his dad will always be out there.

He put his sunglasses on and got up, and began to walk towards the distant exit.

"Sam.", I yelled out to him.

That was his name on the news. I remembered it because it was everywhere when all the town could do was talk about me and what happened.

He turned around and looked at me.

I had so much I wanted to say but so little words.

*"Others will try to triumph over your destiny."* I think it was the first time I spoke to him like he did to me.

He just looked at me for a minute. He said nothing. His eyes were just as bright, but I was not scared anymore.

He turned around and left through the door. I looked through the window but did not move. I watched him walk straight across the street. He worried not about incoming cars, but instead just slowly faded away. And then he was gone.

I wiped my face after being still for a minute. My tears were gone, and I was fine. I knew I could not die of the demons now, but I still felt like I knew nothing of anything.

Jim walked back inside not long after.

Why did I live that night? Why did that car save me? Will I ever know the angels that saved me?

Eventually, people came in that night in large numbers. The Cardinals were on Monday Night Football, which perhaps turned out to be the true horror story of the day. 

When the bar was closed at 2am, and I was done cleaning everything and handling the cash, I stopped and looked at the bottle I grasped. I had not served the whiskey in it the whole night. I could think of serving every other possible drink but that brand of whiskey. 

I did not sleep for the next several days. Sometimes I relived the night of the incident, but other times I simply looked over my shoulder for when I would see those eyes again. 

I got tired of looking eventually, though this took years.

  
I messaged Charlotte from the gas station all those years ago on Facebook about a week after all this happened. I told her I just wanted to chat and see how she was. She was one of the only people outside my family who supported me after what happened, without judging.

We talked about life and the town, but the truth was I just needed to know if more devastation was brought upon what was once my home.

There was none like that night, but she told me how Sam had died the previous year, with the word in town being that he had hung himself.

I did not care to be shocked anymore when I realized I saw his ghost. He has now been born onto a new body and will strike a new boy or man to serve his unjust king.

Today marks 8 years since my reunion with death and destruction. I wonder when the next time will be, if there will even be one. All I knew after that night was that yes, I won. But Tyler and Sam both lost, and I spend every day imagining all the other souls ripped apart by the demons sent to this world. 

All these stories I will never know their moments and how they transpired. But I do know what these stories are capable of.

Light and dark will both result from his eternity. I am still on the side of the light, but I will always remember when I was on the other side and I barely left.
