Stupid right? Not even my idiotic brother, Jason, would fall for that. What’s a girl to do when you email some random middle aged man about their CD listing of ‘Confessions of a Broken Heart (Daughter to Father)’ by Lindsay Lohan and they hit you with “Do you ever wonder what your future looks like? I can tell you”.

I know the smart thing to do would’ve been to just ignore whatever this was and get off the internet for the night. But between being pissed that my favorite CD was out of stock, Jason snoring like a damn train engine in the other bed, and my parents having their fifth screaming match of the day in their bedroom, I really needed a distraction. And what better entertainment than messing with some nutjob that wants to write make-believe stories of my life.

“Ok? Have at I guess”

They took a few minutes to reply. I was smug, thinking they had either been scared off by my little jab or were struggling to actually conjure something up.

“*Your life will be defined by a constant cycle of ups and downs. You will get some of the things you always wished for and other dreams will never come true.*”

Such a lackluster response didn’t deter me from my fun. Psychics are scam artists who always say just what people want to hear. (If they really were useful, I suspect my dad would’ve been a millionaire and never married my mom). I didn’t respond, trying to goad them into saying more.

“*People will only remember you for what you were like in your youth*”

That one managed to hit a nerve. My mother is a serial complainer about my academics, especially compared to when I was a kid. Always a lecture on how I used to be such an academic prodigy till I became chummy with the wrong crowd and that I should be more like my saint of a brother. 

“*And for your lack of faith*”

Way to somehow bring my dad into the sore spot conversation to. I don’t personally care about his investment in religion, as long as he would stop trying to force his beliefs of what I should or should not do with my body on me. Who I get around with is none of his business.

“*Your family will go through a split* *and you will be at the center of it. However, the split will be your saving grace and give you the peace and freedom you always desired*”

A person buying a CD of a song on familial angst having problems with familial angst themselves? I’m truly shocked.

“*Your mother and father will separate due to this incident.*”

I couldn’t resist butting in then. “Yeah. Not like 50% of marriages end in divorce anyways”.

I think that might have really ticked them off.

“*Your mother will become reclusive and will almost never leave her house or use the road.*”

My mother is a grade-A socialite who loves poker nights and dinner parties even more than her own children. Asking her to stay inside for the rest of her life can practically be considered a death sentence. 

*“Your father will become a raging alcoholic who drinks himself to death”*

I might not see eye to eye with my father, but that doesn’t mean I wanted to hear a stranger telling me how they think my dad will pass from what’s likely liver failure. My father who always smells of incense smoke, sandalwood, and dusty pages of holy texts becomes an alcoholic? And because of me? Please. “Wow looks like someone’s mad”

“*There is no anger. Only looking for the point of no return*”.

I didn’t really take well to the cryptic response considering the bombshells Mr. Fortune Teller was trying to shock me with, so I simply sent back a series of question marks. 

This time I didn’t get a text response and three audio files are attached instead. The first two were too incoherent and muffled like the audio was coming from underwater. The third one made my heart skip a beat. I thought I could hear a man sobbing in the back- he sounded like Jason. I don’t think it actually is him though; I’ve never heard Jason cry in my entire life. Doing this is a low blow.

“Ok so tell me then but what could I have done to start this! Start sleeping with a drug addict again and run away?!"

This time I got almost an instantaneous reply.

“*I don’t think you should know that.*”

That was the last straw for me. I didn’t want anymore of this crazy toying with me even if it was all just some prank. I needed to find a way to get out of the house for a few days and just clear my head before I blew a gasket at the wrong people. I could hear Jason being startled awake by the sound of me shoving whatever clothes and essentials I could into one large duffel bag. It didn’t really matter though. I was outside the front door before he could even put on his shoes to try to chase after me.

I’m now sitting in my car, exhausted, and writing all this down on my phone with text to speech. All I want to do is slam my face into the nearest hotel bed, which is approximately 2 miles away. I just have one more bridge over the lake before I get there. I just want to know what you all think. What the hell was up with that guy? 

Jason’s blowing up my phone. It’ll be fine. I'll just call him back tomorrow.
