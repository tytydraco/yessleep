I always wondered how far our generation would go for a like or a viral video on the internet.


My friend and I decided to join a challenge that was going viral at our school. What could possibly go wrong? Honestly, I'm very skeptical about supernatural things, or at least I was until that moment. The truth is, my friend and I didn't want to be left out. We were tired of being seen as the losers in the class. We thought that by doing this challenge, people would start seeing us as cooler and hang out with us, but it didn't work out that way.


That day we decided to skip class and go to that place to do the challenge, of course. The place was a few streets away from our school. We acted like nothing was happening so our parents wouldn't find out. That day I didn't put notebooks or pencils in my backpack; instead, I packed my camera and stabilizer to record the moment. We had to prove that we could actually accomplish this feat, something that no one had dared to do up until then; they only talked about it online and in the school hallways.


I met up with Fernando, my friend. We went down a street further up from the school so no one would see us, and we arrived at the place.


It was an abandoned, two-story house. From the outside, it looked very neglected; nature was already starting to reclaim it, and the facade looked really damp, as if it had never stopped raining on it.

The truth is, I was only going to record, and my friend was going to do the challenge. He's more interested in people's approval, or rather, in Ana seeing him as a bad boy. Ana is the girl he likes, and as you know, she doesn't even know Fernando exists.


We went into the house...

The challenge was simple...

We had to go into the room where the owner of the house had committed suicide. We had to say a kind of invocation to her ghost three times, and she would appear. When she did, we had to stare at her for three minutes... Without blinking, without looking away or looking off out of fear. Every time you did, she would get closer to you, and the timer would reset. And if she got as close as possible, she would strangle you.


Upon entering the room, there was a chair in the middle of the room, a dusty bed, and a baby crib in the corner.


I set up my camera, and Fernando was nervous. He kept saying no, but his hands were trembling, and he was slurring his words. I told him to relax, that most likely nothing was going to happen. Honestly, up until that moment, I didn't believe it would all work, but I didn't want to take away my friend's hope—my only friend.


"Fernando, the recording has begun," were my last words before he started the invocation.


WOMAN OF THE ROPE, HANGED WOMAN, COME AND LOOK AT ME, SHOW ME YOUR SOUL.


WOMAN WITH THE ROPE, HANGED WOMAN, COME AND LOOK AT ME, SHOW ME YOUR SOUL.


WOMAN WITH THE ROPE, HANGED WOMAN, COME AND LOOK AT ME, SHOW ME YOUR SOUL.


Fernando finished saying it, and for the first few minutes nothing happened. I kept recording, and we both looked at each other, somewhat uncomfortably.


Suddenly, the chair creaked as if someone had climbed onto it. It was made of wood and began to groan. Fernando now had a look of fear on his face. He looked at me, but I was on the lookout for any movement so I could record everything. At that moment, I started to feel chills, and the atmosphere in the room became tense.


Suddenly, the chair flipped violently, landing upside down. At that same moment, two black feet emerged from the crib in the corner of the room. They were black, as if all the blood of a person had been trapped in their feet for years. The legs appeared, and we could see that woman, that thing, arched in an inhuman way. Her feet and hands were on the floor, and she was looking at us with her head turned away. Fernando didn't flinch. The challenge had begun....
