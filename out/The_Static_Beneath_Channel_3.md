In hindsight, I wasn’t originally planning on posting this here.

I was hoping someone from my local tech shop would reach out to me by now, but I’m starting to get impatient. And, rather than talking to weirdos on Craigslist or the Facebook Marketplace, I thought I’d try my luck here on Reddit.

You see, my name is Evan Mercer, and I have a bit of a TV problem that started with my grandfather passing away.

Sorry if that got dark real fast, but I promise the context is important to what I’m experiencing. 

And while sad, don’t feel too bad; we weren’t incredibly close.

I saw him maybe a couple of times growing up, typically around holidays, when my parents felt guilty enough to pay him a visit. It probably didn’t help that he lived in the Ozarks and his house was an old split-level bungalow with no signal and shoddy power, even in good weather.

Most of what I know about him I learned second-hand from my father, his brothers, and my cousins. From what they said, I gleaned that he was quite eccentric, which led to him being paranoid and then senile towards the end of his life.

By the time I was in middle school, my parents stopped taking me to see him entirely. When I asked why we wouldn't be seeing him at the next Thanksgiving, my mother said he didn’t want any company. However, my cousin told me he was acting crazy, covering up mirrors and smashing appliances. Apparently, he also started to hear voices.

Anyways, when he died, not too many people wanted anything to do with his estate, especially since he had little to no money or assets. But my dad felt a responsibility as the oldest son to take care of things as best he could, and, being a dutiful son myself, I agreed to help out too.

Originally, I was just going to help him move out some stuff and get the house ready for the open market, or get demolished, whichever came first. Unfortunately, in the first week we were there, my dad got in an accident and was sent home to rest for at least a month.

So, the house and junk fell into my lap until he got back.

Luckily for me, I work remotely, and my lease had just expired, so I figured a month or so with free housing at one of America’s premier natural landmarks while sorting my affairs didn’t sound too bad.

\---

The first few days on my own were mostly just dusting and stuffing garbage bags.

The old man had a bunch of junk! Rusty tools and warped magazines, coffee tins filled with screws, moldy blankets, and a bunch of VHS tapes with peeling labels, just to name a few. For God’s sake, half of the rooms smelled of rainwater and cheap cigarette smoke over the years.

All in all, while kind of gross, everything about the house was about what you’d expect from a recluse like my grandpa.

Well, everything except for the Television in the basement.

Now, if you aren’t aware, basements in Arkansas, especially in the Ozarks, are incredibly rare. The topography and necessities just make it insanely difficult to construct them. But it seems dear old grandad somehow procured the funds and skills to build one under his home.

The basement itself wasn’t finished. It was one of those low-ceilinged concrete ones with a bunch of exposed pipes and a singular dangling pull-chain light. How rustic.

The stairs leading down to it also happened to be where my dad got hurt. He was trying to pull something heavy up, but he tripped and fell. He asked me to try to finish up the  
job down there, but I was putting it off for as long as I could. For starters, I hated basements. That, and I didn’t wanna fall either.

However, the sooner I finished, the sooner I could focus on enjoying the nature surrounding me.

It was the first thing I really noticed. A large mass sitting in the center of the room, shoved under a tarp with stacks of yellow newspapers folded around it.

And when I mean a large mass, I mean it’s pretty huge. The TV is one of those old CRT monsters with fake wood paneling and thick convex glass. The screen itself was cloudy with grime, and there were dark marks, probably from heat, formed near the vent slats on the back.

The weirdest thing about it was that it had no branding on it.

No RCA. No Zenith. Nothing.

Maybe it was a custom job; my grandpa was apparently pretty handy if the stories about his younger years were true.

Thankfully, I didn’t fall like my dad, but I will say, it was a struggle to get that beast upstairs. I had no idea old TVs would be so heavy.

I was just gonna throw it out, but I got to thinking how much one of these things could be worth if it was still in working condition. So, not having much else to do other than keep cleaning, I decided to take a break and see if it would turn on.

From the get-go, it seemed like a waste of time. I mean, the rotary dial on the front would just click uselessly in place except for one:

**Channel 3.**

I shrugged, assuming the knob was worn down from overuse and even jokingly yelled, “Looks like someone enjoyed their VCR!”

With there obviously being no response to my lighthearted comment, I got to plugging the TV cable into the nearest outlet, and messing with the buttons.

Nothing happened.

No static crackling. No glow. No indication that the device was on.

It was just dead.

A bit dejected, I decided to leave it there for the time being and enjoy the outdoors like I wanted.

That should've been the end of it, but something odd happened that night.

\---

Numerous sounds spread around the house in the stillness of the night.

Pipes moaning, wood creaking, and wind scratching along the trees. Despite the eerie soundscape, I drifted off on the couch in the living room. However, my rest didn’t last long, as I was woken up by a sudden, sharp hissing sound.

I jerked up and rubbed my eyes, proceeding to check my phone for the time.

3:13 AM.

The noise got louder the longer my body sat up on the couch, droning incessantly.

Begrudgingly, I lifted myself and turned the corner towards the sound, where I was met with the TV glowing in the dark.

Not brightly, just enough to illuminate the floor around it with a greyish tone.

The screen was filled with static—no image, just the constant blare of white noise and fuzzy lines.

I was more surprised than frightened at this point. A bit relieved, actually. Maybe the TV just needed some time to power back on after it was plugged in. I mean, who knows how long it was sitting under that tarp?

Still, something about it was odd. I can’t quite explain it, but the static exuding from the machine seemed deliberate, I guess? Like, the waves folded into a pattern the longer I stared and listened.

Stepping closer and crouching beside it, I put my hand over the knob. The air around the TV felt warm, almost like a space heater. It was when I reached around the back that I noticed something. The power cord was lying limp on the floor.

For a moment, I just stared blankly at it hanging in my hand. I convinced myself that I must have loosened it accidentally somehow, maybe while walking around the set.

But even if that was the case, how the hell was the TV on?

Not seeing any other course of action, I plugged it in again, and nothing changed. The static continued uninterrupted.

I remember laughing nervously with one short breath through my nose.

“Okay”, I said out loud. “Very fucking funny.”

As if someone was hiding nearby, ready to jump out and scare me.

Again, seeing little else to do, my hand went for the cord and this time, pulled it free from the wall.

Only after another few minutes did the light finally dim and collapse inward into a tiny white speck at the center of the screen.

It lingered for a moment, then disappeared.

The room was now cloaked completely in darkness.

I stood there for a good while longer, dumbfounded by the events that had just transpired, and continued to listen.

Because after the TV was shut off…

I could’ve sworn I still heard static.

\---

Anyway, that was about three weeks ago.

I’ve since finished cleaning up around the house, which, according to my dad, should be up for sale in the next month.

Almost everything got trashed or donated, but I ended up taking a few things with me, namely the TV and some of the old VHS tapes; well, the tapes that I think can be restored.

That probably sounds stupid given everything I just wrote about, but there really is something about that old thing that’s got me fascinated. Like, what if I really could get it fixed up and running?

Plus, I figure there has to be a technological explanation for whatever happened that night and what’s been happening since I moved into my new place. This is what I need your help with!

Since transporting it with me to Houston and trying my best with the tools I have, it still doesn’t work properly. Honestly, I think I made it worse, because once in a while, mostly late at night, I swear it turns on and blares that grating static sound.

I think I might have fucked up the audio IC (Integrated Circuit) or power supply, cause it definitely shouldn’t be doing that consistently.

So, if anyone here has any information on what could be wrong, or knows someone I can talk to about fixing up CRT TV’s and systems, it would be a huge help! In any case, thanks a bunch for reading, and I hope to talk to y’all again real soon with new updates, for better or worse.

Oh, and before anyone asks, it still only turns to Channel 3.
