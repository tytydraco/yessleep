I have never been a religious man, a man who follows laws that exceed our own understanding and control how we eat, sleep, and breathe. I prefer to keep my own two feet planted onto the ground, and my mind stuck to this world and this world alone. Why must a man look up to the sky for an answer to a question that can be found by looking around the soil he stands on?  I have to keep myself to this mindset, or else I will lose myself to the grasp of other worldly things. In my line of work, I must hold true to these ideas that I have just typed, or the wrongdoings of man will stay hidden and never be brought to light. I am a homicide detective for the local sheriff's department in a small town in the south, the name of which I will keep separate from this post, and have been in this profession for about 7 years now. I will keep my name redacted as well for the sake of my family and to avoid any conflict with the confidentiality of the case I am going to discuss. I am not a man posting this for any sympathy, but a man looking for a chance to let some of my inner demons out that have been built up for 7 years. 

People often think of a homicide detective and think of the stereotypical life of crime procedural shows, where a crime is committed, and the case is solved in a loop that happens each week. While the shows are indeed entertaining, they only capture a mere 30 minutes of what goes on during these cases. The shows don’t encapsulate the smell of decaying meat that rests on your clothes for days and refuses to leave your mind, or the sight of how truly horrifying Mother Nature recycles her waste with maggots, flesh flies, and rats gnawing on fingers, or the fresh taste of decaying pupils. Some cases aren’t as severe as others, where one case may be a body hanging from a fan while the next case may be a body displayed on the wall, skin stretched out like a taxidermy, just to demonstrate the following of a new God that was formed from the suspect taking some new strand of psychedelics.  Others have done my job for way longer than I have and have experienced worse horrors than what I have experienced, but I feel that, unlike others, I can use this resource to help deal with my struggles and continue to do what I do best.  

These examples of cases that I previously mentioned are just some of the many cases that I have been a part of, and while they do take some mental toll on me for some time, I have always been able to pull myself out of that dark pit they try to throw me into. Over time, I feel that I have become somewhat desensitized by years of doing this job, and I am thankful for that, but last week, when I got the call that my sister had been found deceased in her home, it all suddenly became real again. Just as with my name, I will keep her name private and address her as Jane. Jane was 3 years older than I was, and always made sure to reach out to me each week to “keep me in check”. 

“Just calling to make sure you’re still kicking and don't have any screws loose, well, I guess with you, that's never been the case since I was always blessed with those genes,” she said with a laugh, “ seriously please call me back more and you know you always have a place at church with me, I would love to see you out. I miss seeing you more, but I love you, and I'll talk to you later.” 

That voicemail was sent to me on April 18, 2025, which was just 2 days prior to when it is suspected that she ended her life. Jane was found 4 days after this suspected date of her suicide when her friend who came over to help plan Jane’s baby shower found her along with the corpse of her unborn baby lying across the floor with the umbilical cord still attached. Being a small town, word got out fast about the suicide of Jane, and newspapers published stories about the findings of both corpses, with reaffirmation that the sheriff’s department would release as many details as possible. Churches gathered together to preach the importance of confessing your sins and how ending your own life isn't the answer to worldly problems. Down at the office, they tried to call me off, tell me that I would have an “unfocused” approach to this case, regardless if I was the only homicide detective within about 10 counties, they tried to push for me to take the time to grieve with my family rather than do my job. I loved my sister. She was hard to deal with at times, sure, but I never questioned that she was one of the great things about my childhood and the reason why I chose to take up a line of work that helps others. We differed in the religious aspect of life, where she always tried to bring me to church; of course, I declined, but nonetheless, I made sure to show I loved her.  All this said, I had a job to do, and regardless of how much it hurt to see the scene in a home that I had been in, and a bloodstained carpet in which we had once played boardgames on, I could not let others left questioning this case when I could at least do Jane some justice in solving this for good. 

While I would rather not go into great detail on the scene itself, considering my association with Jane, I will rather attach this quick summary from the report of the crime scene to give a better idea of what the scene contained.

Though as the years passed my sister and I did not get to actually meet in person as much as we both wished to, I still have a decent understanding of what was going on in her life and how she was doing for the most part. I know that as of 7 months ago she had come back home to her house to find a note from her husband on the kitchen counter. Instinctively she came to me with the note and gave me all the facts hoping I would have an answer to what was going on. 

“Calm down Jane,” I said as she paced the room,” run all the info you can through me one more time so I can make sure everything agrees.” 

“Ok,” she sighed with her eyes noticeably becoming more red as she fought off tears,” I had come home from work, the door had been locked when I got there, nothing seemed out of place, the only thing different was that note.” 

“So as far as you saw there wasn’t anything off? No signs that maybe he was taken?” 

“Not that I could see no.” 

“Had y’all been in any fights recently? Anything that could have made him leave and need a break? Has he done this before?” I pushed the tissue box closer to her side of the desk as tears began to slowly roll down her face. 

“No. We’ve gotten in some arguments over the past couple months but nothing out of the ordinary. I had noticed that he had seemed a little more frustrated than usual but he had told me that work was getting busy and so I just assumed that was it.”  

“Jane this happens more than you think,” I said, “people handle things differently and with this baby coming he might just be trying to take all of it in and is doing it in an unhealthy way. I’m not saying what he is doing is right, but maybe he trying to find a break from everything. I’ll talk with some of the guys around here and see if we can get any hits on his license plate or any type of identification. Tell me about this note.” 

“I-” she paused with a blank stare and slowly reached into her purse and handed me a folded piece of paper, “ I don't want to read it again.” 

Part of the reason I am keeping my name private is that I have the note and have held onto it ever since that day in the office. You might ask why I have hidden it and why I haven’t done the right thing and simply turn it in with the rest of the evidence to give us the best chance of getting to the bottom of this. The answer... the note feels too personal to me. The note has confused me since I laid my eyes on it, and after what has transpired in these past couple of days, it feels as if it is taunting me, trying to throw me off track. If you want the truth, this paper makes me question more than I usually like to, and adds layers to this case that I would like to avoid but cannot. At the beginning of this post, I said that though this was personal, I had a job to do, but the more I sit in this damn office typing this, I can feel the note pulling me more into the humanity of this case and reminding me that it’s impossible not to let human emotions in. I am going to type the contents of the note so that maybe whoever is reading this might be able to help me understand what it means, or maybe I am posting this to help it feel less like a target at me. Here is what was on the note. 

 

*Have you heard the sounding of the trumpets? Do you still pray to be taken into the sky? I remember when you would fall onto your knees in tears as those in white robes and masked in glory would speak of mercy and promise you safety above the clouds. Do you feel our little girl pushing harder against your insides as the light from the heavens shines onto you, hoping one day to take her away from you? My sweet Jane, you will be taken away from this Earth and led away from the sins that hold you down. I dream of it, I see it, I hope of it. I however fear to hear the trumpets for I know that while you are lifted into the sky I will be crushed by the weight of man’s wrongdoings. I fear the days that come and my sight is blinded by blood and lust. The light will only blind me and I now have been too far soaked by the blood of this world. Forgive me. Deliver me from this evil. I am yours and now I am theirs.* 

  

I will post again soon to update on my findings and anything that I have found. So many questions remain and I must find what the hell is going on, because I must know what the hell happened in that house.
