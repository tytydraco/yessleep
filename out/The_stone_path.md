6 stones wide and an endless sea of them in front of me, I am unsure how long this path is but I must Walk. I do not remember how I found this path, maybe I was mindlessly going home from work, taking my dog on a walk? yet I have no leash, have I been walking that long? I want to turn around but how long would the walk back be? let me recollect my thoughts, when I first stepped onto the stone path.

What was around the stone path was buildings touching the stars, lights flickering in multiple rooms, all the lively energy away from the path. There were more than stones at first, there were black bags in round, odd shapes with some large containers with green and yellows. I wasn't on the path yet, I was a smooth path with cracks and needles being its bags and containers; I was scared? no confused, intrigued, lulled. even at the start I didn't know why I started walking the path but it called to me like a siren, and I had nothing stopping me from ignoring its call and so my journey began.

The path did not follow the rules of the world, it cut into those other smooth paths, buildings were shaped around it instead of beside it, I could glimpse into some rooms that had life, families consuming as they sat in a circle, water flowing from pipes separated because of the path, remembering now I walked through a building which had loud shaking boxes with people ignorant of me.

Now that I recall no one saw the path, or I for that matter. some people walked right across it, was I the only one to fall to the siren call? oh! no there was someone once. by this time I walked out of the area with the buildings next to stars and it has stretched to a place where something else touched stars. I was amongst trees, a dense thicket residing next to the path, still unaffected by the world around it.

"Hey, what are you doing?" I blinked, have I been blinking? I did not starve or ache from the walking I felt the same as I first stepped on. I looked at the person that spoke to me, it was a woman in brown colored pants with multiple pockets and black long sleeve shirt.

"Are you talking to me?" my voice clawed and pulled itself out of my throat, I hadn't spoken for a long time, longer now as I try and no sound comes out.

"Do you see anyone else around here?" she spoke annoyance of my ignorance of the world, the more I recall she had a tent set up beside her and a small fire providing warmth, for which I did not need for there was no temperature on the path. I tried to speak again but before the words began the climb, she handed me a plastic bottle filled with water which is the last thing I remember tasting.

"Well I'm walking on this path and..." what was I doing, a moment of clarity but something much fiercer struck me. I didn't want to get off, no I couldn't get off. death was certain if I step off. The color must have drained from my face, dropping the bottle which broke, pieces scattering amongst the dirt.

That is what I saw but whose reality is true? I wondered this in fear as I saw her pick up the bottle, gripping nothing while wiping dust off thin air. her aggressive front turned tendered and worried seeing my expression; my blood was rising, that twisting feeling in your stomach, you have to do something now this doesn't feel right.

So I ran, I ran fast hearing shouting behind me, seeing the inner rings of trees that were split in half to make space for the stone path that continued on in front of me. that was the last person I spoke to, or at least chose to spoke to; I cannot leave the path so I might as well not question my conceptual reality.

My mind is hurting, not just a headache but like a muscle that I let atrophied and began working again. I look around me now and it's simply nothing, a white space that only exists for the path to continue. is this the end? but the path continues and so I must push forward, for I do not tire, I do not hunger, I do not overheat or freeze, I seem to merely exist to walk the path.

I realize thinking about the woman, her clothes were so significant because I have none. I remember the soles of my feet touching the stone path in an arctic area, animals on tall glaciers and aquatic animals below the path. I was wearing a suit and tie, formal wear... I must of been at a job, oh how curious to know nothing about something you've known all your life. I saw the deep oceanic blue of the inner glacier as a path was perfectly crafted through. I realize that I was seeing things that no other human has seen yet at that moment I only wanted to know what my home looked like.

There was a time that I accepted death as an option. the path does something to you, your brain feels disconnected like your mind is always on a highway it has traveled for centuries only coming back to life when something pops out.

The tall buildings now touching clouds, the garbage now removed, the streets were bustling; I was and still unsure if people could see me or not, as they walked they went to the left or right to avoid me. in that moment I looked at the sidewalk seeing my clothes tattered from the different climates that I traveled through. but I only wanted to see my home that I did not know if I had still or die trying.

My foot was past the 6th stone about to touch cement when I did not feel fear, I felt my hair stick up, my pulse slowed, eyes wide as my survival instinct kicked in. I thought it was just my emotions trying to stop me until I saw it the distant, it had no shape no mass, height, affiliation with a species that I knew, though I couldn't visualize it I knew what it was, it was finality, and ending of all and nothing more and I could feel it getting closer.

Home was a forgotten dream now as I ran once more but I did not stop, I dare not look behind and even now as the memory comes back I run once more faster and faster as I continue the loop once more. I saw the city, the woods, the glaciers, even my withered shoes as I continued to run.

On the third lap my hair stopped standing and my mind went back into its lulled state. I came back consciously on I believe the 18th lap as thudded into an object, my shoes. I am 5'8 but now my shoes were larger than myself. I had to push it to continue on the path... I wanted to investigate as I knew this was odd but once I could walk forward that was all I knew.

There was a moment where I could not see the path, I knew I was still on it as I didn't feel in danger but the world around did not exist, the last colors I remember seeing were red chaotic orbs that would not sit still and then nothing. a guillotine of the senses happened as the blackness consumed me. that's what I thought at least but I remember having a keen sense of time again. I do not know how long I have traveled but it was equal to decades, no generations at this point and with knowing this came regret and boredom, the thought of death came but even trying made me remember finality, which still gripped the primal nature of my body.

So I put it to chance, in the darkness where I couldn't even see the path I spun until I fell, I was not in control so let fate decide where I end.

I was stuck in the darkness for eons.

And then like a child coming to the world light struck my eyes, I could see and feel my body once more, even my bitter end that has stayed with me till now the stone path.

Do not think the path has ended, as I look ahead there are more stones, but coming to light I realize that I may not tire but I must rest, for if I were to continue I might not have another chance

When finality comes, do not run, when the darkness enshrouds you and time seems to be a wasted resource do not make rash decisions like I, and do not step onto the stone path.
