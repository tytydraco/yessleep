Curiosity has a way of possessing you unlike any other emotion. It allows shame and sometimes fear to seep into your skull and picks away at the nerves in your prefrontal cortex that tell you what you are doing is wrong. It can puppet you, mouth agape and eyes wide open as you peer into whatever curiosity your misguided mind desires. And when you find out that your horrible, harrowing hook is more haunting than you could have dreamed, where do you go from there?

When I last posted, I planned to just leave it at what it was. Maybe I was still drunk and what I saw and felt was just a mishap. Perhaps I dreamt the whole thing. The latter conclusion feels a bit silly to draw from, considering how hard it was to forget how the cold water from the hose outside felt on my warm, filth-covered feet. I had to walk outside, just a towel wrapped around my body in the chill cool of the night just to purify my feet from the putrid puke-inducing liquid blemishing them. I vividly remember sitting outside in the cold until I could see the slight hint of indigo breaking midnight blue-colored sky. I stayed out nearly all night just to gather my thoughts.

So why am I posting now? That would be because last night, I caught my boyfriend in the bathroom, way past 10 of course. Let me bring us back to where I left off last time to give you some better context.

Since that night, I had been using the bathroom at the gas station. I've settled for showering every other day because now I'm paying out of pocket for my showers. It's only been a few days, but I seriously need to find some other way of bathing before I run out of money.

My first shower at the gas station was rough. Typically, I wouldn't even consider going to a place like that but I had no choice. With shame and lingering feelings of fear and resentment, I'd shuffle my way under the shower head, letting it's clear droplets cleanse my broad shoulders. I couldn't help but stare at my feet as I showered, checking them over again and again.

After my second shower, some courage had built up in me. Sure, I had shared my experience online with y'all, but I needed an outside opinion; someone from the North side of Deaten Town. With my dirty clothes gathered in a bag, I scrambled to find the cheapest candy bar I came across as I walked up to the counter.

"Just this for ya', today?" The cashier barely spared me a glance before taping away on the screen in front of her.

"Yup." The 'p' popped off my lip before I could catch it. I could feel embarrassment already muddying my thoughts but my deep-rooted desire for something, anything, that could help me gave me the push to prevail. "Say, do you happen to live around here?"

The cashier paused and finally looked me over. Before she could jump to any rotten, but fair, conclusions as to why I would ask such a question, I followed it up with "I've been having some strange plumbing issues in my house just a few blocks down and I was wondering if you or anyone else you know was having some of the same troubles?"

After seemingly easing her mind, she shrugged her shoulders and scanned the item. "Not that I know of. I'm not really at the house much anymore but we haven't had any issues like that." She checked the screen before continuing. "Oh, and it'll be $1.84."

After sorting through certainly not enough cash to shower here for the next week, I pulled out two dollars from my wallet and set them on the counter in front of her. "Ah, okay thank you."

I left with some spare change, a candy bar I barely wanted, and no more information coming out than I had going in. Perhaps I really was somehow still drunk that night. Maybe the hand was just a big bug that I saw or something else floating in the odd substance. I couldn't bring myself to ask Liam about it, since he wouldn't tell me anything anyways. My only hope was to get a job or face my fears head on.

Turns out, when you have no job, no connections, limited experience, and a lack of a college degree it's a bit hard to get a paycheck in under a week. That meant that yesterday, I had to shower at home. I debated resorting to using the hose outside but I'm sure the neighbors wouldn't be too happy about that.

There were a few things that I kept in mind that eased my tension before entering that reeking restroom again. First off, the water from the hose outside and the kitchen sink have been running clear. This shows that there's not an issue throughout the whole house. Secondly, up until I used the bathroom that fateful night, the water had run clean. Finally, Liam has taken showers every morning since Cinco de Mayo and hasn't mentioned anything to me.

From the looks of it, as long as I follow the rules, nothing out of the ordinary will happen. I took a deep breath, for more reasons than one, before entering the bathroom. My eyes scattered across the room and attempted to grab onto anything that was out of place. I could feel my shoulders relax.

It took a while for me to feel comfortable enough to put my full, naked, and vulnerable body under the translucent water. I waited and waited for the color to change, but it didn't. To be completely honest, I couldn't help but let a little bit of the frustration and fear that I had built up in me over the past few days slip out in the form of tears that dripped down the drain with the rest of water. Maybe it was the smell, but despite how relieving the shower should have been, my stomach never settled.

I'm not one to spill all of my private life online, but I'm a bit worried about my boyfriend. He's been different in more ways than one. Maybe it's because he has more access to me, but he's been more intimate with me than usual, or at least he has attempted to be. We haven't done anything since that night. I feel bad turning him down, but I keep getting nauseous. I think it's the smell.

Despite avoiding that kind of intimacy, I've craved another. As if a child attached to a teddy bear, I would bury myself in his arms. It made me feel more nauseous in the moment, but it was worth the mental relief that it gave me. He provided safety and promises of a better future.

That's why, upon waking in the middle of the night, I was surprised to feel that Liam was no longer in my arms. A short investigation of the bed further asserted his absence. Maybe he went to pee out back? I wasn't too worried and assumed he would be back soon.

That was until I heard talking down the hallway. The door was left cracked and the talking was barely even audible. I could feel my heart sink.

Through the crack, a faint light illuminated my arm. As my eyes adjusted, I could tell that it was coming from the room at the end of the hall—the bathroom. Much like your average zombie in a movie, I rose from the bed and mindlessly sauntered to the door, cracking it open just a creak more, and listened.

"No Momma, he wouldn't understand..." His figure cast a tall shadow down the hallway that nearly reached the door I was standing at. "I'll talk to him."

He spoke on and on for a while. It almost seemed like he was interrupted by someone at points, but I couldn't hear any one else's voice. At some point, I brought myself back to bed, but not to sleep.

The sun's been up for only a few hours now as I'm writing this, and he's laying peacefully beside me. I'm scared to leave the room. What if the bathroom door is open? I'm gonna talk to him but I need some advice first. Please, God, what do I do?
