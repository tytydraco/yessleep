Someone keeps putting something in my coffee.

In the past three weeks I’ve lost consciousness five times. I don’t know what happens or how long I’m out for. All I know is that my memory fades after I finish my morning coffee, and I wake up the next day in my bed with remnants from the night.

After the first time I thought that maybe I had just dreamt going into the office, but when I looked at my phone, I had in fact missed the entire previous day. When I went into work, I asked my colleagues what had happened, if I’d passed out or gone home early, but they all said the same thing. At around 3 PM I’d gotten up hastily and left the building. My computer opened to a blank screen. I didn’t say anything, I didn’t stop when asked where I was off to, I simply went to my car and drove off. I don’t know what I did that day. There’s no evidence of me doing anything other than coming home. No charges to my card, no scratches or bruising, nothing at all. 

I decided to just go about my day as normal after talking to my coworkers. I went into the kitchen area to fix my coffee like normal… and that’s the last thing I remember. I woke up the next morning in my bed. Once again there was nothing out of the ordinary, besides the fact that it was like I’d erased an entire day from my memory. Of course, this is where I made the decision to go see a doctor. They ran some tests, did a couple of different scans, but nothing came back out of the ordinary. “Stress,” they said, prescribing a couple of days off work. Isn’t the healthcare system just incredible? Well, after being told that I was perfectly fine, I decided that it must’ve been some fluke brain fog or something, I don’t know, I just wanted to rationalize it. I took the doctor’s advice and stayed home the rest of the week. 

Nothing out of the ordinary happened during that time, and I figured that maybe the doctor wasn’t so off base after all. Monday morning I decided I would be going back to work, and right after I finished my coffee… yeah. Back in bed the next morning. But this time something was off. I was covered in dirt. There were no prints throughout the house, and even my sheets were clean, but I was dirty from head to toe. This was alarming to say the least. I checked my card, I checked my car, I checked with coworkers, but apparently yesterday I’d stayed ‘til five, there were no charges to my card, and my car didn’t have dirt anywhere in, or on, it. I was really starting to get freaked out now, and it was actually this time that I thought it may have been the coffee. After all, that was the last thing I’d remembered from the three times this had happened. “That’s it. No more coffee,” I thought to myself, and after cutting that out, the next few days went off without a hitch.

Finally it looked like I was getting back to normal, I thought I must just be hyper-sensitive to caffeine or something. I tried not to give it too much thought, and just got back to my usual routine. But then I found myself craving it. I needed to have coffee. 

One night, I decided to make a cup at home. I never really drank it at home, but I just couldn’t stop the feeling of need that was boiling up inside of me. So I made it, fixed it how I like it, drank it, and nothing happened. I was perfectly fine. I might be the stupidest guy in the world, but I felt like it was all over. Just a crazy couple of days that were now behind me. So, of course, I decided to make a cup the next day at work, and this time I didn’t lose consciousness right away. As the final sip left the cup, I felt better than I ever had in my life. There was just something about this cup of coffee that really hit me. I could feel my body becoming more and more energetic, I could hear everything happening around me at twice the usual volume, my eyes were becoming tense, my breathing rapid, everything felt incredible! And then I woke up again. In my bed. Surrounded by fur. 

It was all over my bed, my floor, and me. I thought that I must have gotten attacked by an animal or something last night, but I didn’t hurt anywhere and showed no signs that I’d been bitten or even scratched. I noticed that all of the hair was the same color as mine. Just as I began to think about it, the craving for more began. There was an unspeakable amount of pain that I could feel building up in my chest, a fire that I needed to extinguish. I knew exactly what I needed to put it out. It was seven in the morning, and I knew no one would be there, but I had to get my coffee. I went outside to look for my car, and it was nowhere to be found. I assumed I’d left it at the office or something, and decided I’d just take an Uber. Fifteen minutes until the nearest one would get to me, I knew I could get there faster. So I ran. I’ve never been much of a runner, but I hadn’t even tried to run since high school, and I guess something had changed since then. I ran faster than I thought was possible and didn’t even feel out of breath. There was no pain in my legs either, just the burning in my chest still.

I burst into the office doors and ran up stairs to my department floor. The doors were unlocked, probably a Janitor or something, so I got right in. I went straight to the kitchen and found a cup already sitting there with a note next to it, “The final cup. Enjoy!” I didn’t even think about who made it, or what could have possibly been in it. I just downed it as fast as I could. Immediately the burning sensation stopped. I started to feel like I had the day before. There was nothing that could stop me. As I was about to walk out the doors, I noticed my arms were beginning to grow darker, the same color as my hair…

I woke up the next day. This time was different, there was something wrong. I ran to the bathroom and looked in the mirror, and saw that I was covered in blood. I searched all over for a cut or a scrape, but there was nothing. This wasn’t my blood, and as I brushed my teeth that morning, I noticed that my toothbrush had been stained red after I used it.

That was the last time I woke up from passing out the previous day. Now I remember everything, and there hasn’t been a day in the past week or so that I’ve even gone to sleep. I don’t know who did this to me, but now I turn into something different at night. I’m still in my body, with my own thoughts and senses, but I lose control. I wish I knew more, but I’m still getting used to this new lifestyle of mine.

All I know is that someone kept putting something in my coffee.
