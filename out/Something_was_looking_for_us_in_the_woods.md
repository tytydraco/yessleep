Every year me and my buddies plan a camping trip. A while back, we wanted to do a hard-core Appalacian trip. West Virginia was the place. 5 of us isolated from civilization for 3 days. At least thats what we thought.

This story centers around me and my buddy Brandon. A hell of a guy. Also a little, out there? I talked to one of the guys in the group after he took a joke way too seriously and we believe he's on the spectrum somewhere, its a common thing where he'll miss jokes. That's neither here nor there. Either way, we considered our gear, I had just big enough if a tent for us both to lighten his load, he wanted to pack his rifle. We were going to be incredibly far back in the woods where we probably wouldn't come across anyone, and we all felt a little safer with at least one of us packing something serious. For any gun nuts here, he had a really nice short barrel 300blk with a suppressor. I'm just glad I didn't have to carry it.

By the end of the first day, with the dirt road we drove down, and the hiking, we must have been 20 if not more miles from even a main road. We had planned on being as isolated as possible and we were. Set up camp and sat talking and joking around.

Thats when things got weird. After walking most the day, setting up camp, sitting around a fire, that first complete moment of silence hits you and reality sets in. I spend a lot of time outdoors and it hits every time but this felt so much more intense. Looking around the group, it looked like it had hit us all. A few moments passed before we all started just saying we were going to sleep. Brandon and I climbed in my tiny tent, almost cuddling.

I remember every detail from that night clearly. I've laid awake at night and relived it. I woke up to Brandon shuffling around, obviously not trying to wake me up.

"I have to use the bathroom" his exact words.

"There's probably not a bathroom for quite a few miles, you mind going outside?" I said back, referencing to a joke we made on the walk in. "Dammit I gotta piss too", all in whispers, as if the desolate woods would hear us, or that everyone else wasnt out cold.

Unzipping my sleeping bag I felt the cold air hit me. I still remember the sound my boots made stepping out onto the damp underbrush. Being hit with cold mid autumn wind. The moonlit night, just enough for us not to turn on our flashlights and disturb everyone else. His gun slung on his back, mine on my hip. I remember finding a tree not far from the tent, and watching him wander a little further off towards where we came in from. I figured it was one of those odd things that he did, or just extra privacy.

Now, I am being 100% truthful about what we saw next. The other guys didn't believe us. I wouldn't believe me. Even with how serious he is, I wouldn't have believed him.

I turned around and saw him kneeling down, pointing his rifle on the other side of this hill we were on. A shiver went down my spine as I got my pistol out and snuck over. As I approached him, he was white as a ghost and I could see from the movement of his rifle that he was shaking uncontrollably.

Thats when I got there and looked out past the embankment. Chills immediately shot down my spine. Thinking about what I saw takes me back.

There stood a man about what I reckon to have been about 20 yards. In the dead of night I could see him clear as day. I still see him sometimes when I close my eyes. A man so white he almost glowed in the dark. No hair, no shirt, black shorts, and no shoes. He was looking around, like he knew someone had been here. Not on a trail, but just in the middle of the woods. Then he proceeded to walk, almost jog to our right, occasionally stopping to look around, then back to a speed walk.

We sat there frozen. Not a word between us. Just watching. Him maintaining an aim in the general direction, me with my gun to my side frozen.

I find myself asking the same questions I can't answer every day. Could someone even be that paper white? I dont think so personally. Why was whatever we saw in shorts and nothing else? I don't think someone could have survived wearing that with how cold it was, especially no shoes. How could he see? Where he came from was pitch black and so was where he went. Had he followed us? Was he looking for us? The questions go on and on. We were so far out, there were no roads nor trails.

We sat there for what felt like an hour before we said anything, him overlooking where this thing walked, and me looking back at the other 2 tents. We were frozen in fear until we began to see the sunrise. We woke everyone up and said we had to leave. We had no explanation. We told them the same exact story, and that we weren't going to stay in those woods one more minute longer than we had to.

I appreciate the platform to get it off my chest. I texted Brandon about it once, a few months ago. He said he thinks about it every night too. I recently moved into a house surrounded by woods and every time the wind blows through the trees it takes me back. Its begun to affect my sleep. I recheck my door locks 3 times a night. I expect to look out my window and see it. Every creak in the house is him.

I thought about seeing a therapist but how so I not sound bat shit crazy? "Oh yea me and my buddies went into bumfuck nowhere West Virginia to larp as survivalist in the woods and almost shot a skinwalker" they'd put me on meds.
