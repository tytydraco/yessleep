There's something in my house. It has only been here for four months, but in that time it has unraveled my life and ruined my existence. I can't tell anyone it's there,the last time I did I was given a look that suggested I should keep this suffering to myself. No, this isn't ideal, this isn't what I want, but opening my mouth has done nothing good. So now here I sit, in a darkened room with the dull glow of a computer screen illuminating my haggard face. I'm afraid to turn it off. I know it's behind me, sitting where it always does. Somewhere near the far end of the space I'm in, watching me expectantly. Like it knows I'll have to face eventually, and when I do I know what it's going to do.

Six months ago my wife and I moved into this apartment. The asking rent was less than half of other asking prices in the area. We though what we had found was paradise, but the hell that waited behind that door had different ideas. The first week was great, we celebrated our luck and embraced this opportunity at a glorious and beautiful life. The second week was less celebration and more getting things on track, we were still swimming in a sea of joy at this point, the apartment was great. Roomy, spacious, on the first floor and disconnected with its own little back patio. It was like living in a townhome for far less. 

The third week, things started to go south. 

One night, after a long day at work and a night spent enjoying eachother's company, something woke me up. Not a sound or anything of that sort, but a feeling like something wasn't right in my home. This weight of dread, this sinking wrongness drew my eyes to the far corner of the darkened room where I saw it, illuminated by the light of the moon. It was a small figure, no larger than a child, sitting in a chair I didn't know we owned wrapped in what looked like a gauze hood. Its skin was oily black, shimmering in the pale light of the moon. It had no nose, and its eyes were pure white save for a pair of black pinprick pupils. I stared at the strange thing for several minutes before clicking the light on in my room, taking my eyes away from the odd little creature for only a moment. That seemed to be all it took, though. As I turned to face the thing again to get a better look in the light, it was gone.

The sudden change woke my wife, "What's going on?" I heard her ask groggily as I clicked the light out only to see the little thing sitting there again.

"Do you see that?" I asked, pointing at the creature.

My wife, Jenna, looked to the little thing and tilted her head. "See what? There's nothing there?"

I blinked, staring at the strange creature as it raised a long, slender finger to its face, almost as if telling me to quiet myself.

I stared at it, then looked back to my wife, "You really don't see anything there at all?" I asked curiously.

"Just the closet door, that you left open." she murmured as she tucked herself back in to bed.

I didn't take my eyes off the little creature, and it stared at me in kind, as if acknowledging that I was the only one who could see it.

I don't know when I fell asleep, but I woke up feeling like I had been hit by a train. 

When I finally dragged myself out of bed it was around three in the afternoon. Jenna had been hard at work cleaning the house and when I finally rose she teased "Thought you died there for a bit, had to keep checking in on you to make sure you were breathing. Everything ok?"

I sighed, looking to where I had seen the little thing the night before. There was no sign of it, and talking about it only made me feel crazier. "Just a weird dream." I lied "Had a hard time sleeping because of it." 

She peered at me, as if trying to look into my thoughts before offering a smile. "Alright, well, since you got to sleep in all day, you get to clean the bathroom."

That was it. That was the last I'd see of the little creature for nearly a week.

Then, during the evening while I was responding to some work e-mails, I felt that sinking dread in my gut again and as I turned around in the dimly lit office I saw it again. It seemed to be sitting as it stare at me with those wild, beady eyes. As I acknowledged it, it began to smile. It was a horrible and uncomfortable thing, the creature had too many teeth and I couldn't make out any lips, so it was all white teeth against and ink-black backdrop for a face. It held out its hand, palm up as if asking for something.

I stared at it, dumbfounded. "What do you want?" I asked quietly, trying to avoid grabbing any attention from Jenna in the other room. The creature said nothing, instead continuing to hold out its hand, going so far as to point at it with the other. "I can't give you anything if I don't know what you're asking for." I continued. The creature's many-toothed smile turned to a frown as it held its hand out again, thrusting it toward me expectantly.

I shrugged, turning to get back to my work, the little thing still just within my periphery. 

I felt it move before I saw it.

The creature drew closer, as if demanding my attention, and when I turned to face it again it was so close to my face I could see the veins in its strange little eyes. It wasn't looking at me, though. It was looking at the computer screen, at the work email I had opened. Slowly, its gaze turned to me before it reached forward and tapped at the screen. It held its hand out again, incessantly tapping the monitor. The thing had this...sterile smell to it, like gauze and chemicals. I had to pull my face away to keep my nostrils from burning as it kept its hand forward, demanding something I couldn't offer. Eventually, I reached for something on my desk, a coffee mug, and put it in the creature's hand. It paused its tapping and looked at what I'd placed in its hand, then it looked at me and nodded, as if satisfied with the payment I had given it. Slowly, the thing pushed itself away from me, cup in hand as it crawled backward into a shadowed part of the room I couldn't see.

I rushed toward the light in my office and clicked it on. The thing, along with its offering were gone. I blinked in shock, trying to figure out what the hell just happened, or what that monster was that I gave something to.

I don't know if denying it then and there would have prevented what came next, I just know that I wished there was more I could have done.

I got a promotion at work the next day. "Exemplary Performance" they called it. I had done nothing different, and had been working for the same company nearly six years without so much as an acknowledgement of anything I had done. So, imagine my surprise. It wasn't a small promotion either, I was given an executive position, with an office and everything. The one thing I noticed when I moved in was the coffee mug. It looked exactly like the one I had given that weird little imp the night before, right down to the chip in the ceramic.

Jenna, of course, was thrilled to hear that they were finally acknowledging all of the hard work I did, and I'll admit, it was nice to feel like I was appreciated. Something inside me, though, made me feel like this wasn't something I should be happy about.

The next night, I woke feeling that same wrongness I had felt before, and when I looked to that dark corner of the room where I had first seen the little thing, I saw it sitting there again. It smiled eerily at me as it held out its hand again, a debtor demanding payment for a service rendered. I looked at it, horrified by its presence, and moreso knowing that it was likely the simple gesture of giving this thing my coffee mug that convinced it we were somehow in an accord.

"I don't know what you are..." I murmured, trying to keep my voice low. "But I don't want what you have to offer." the thing tilted its head, hand still held out, palm still upturned expectantly. It extended its arm outward, expectant of payment. I looked at it and shook my head. "I didn't want anything you offered in the first place..." I whispered, "Please, just leave me alone." it looked at me curiously, before pointing directly to me. It shrugged and slunk back into the dark.

I was mugged the next day. On my way to work, in broad daylight, a man came up to me holding a boxcutter. He demanded everything I hand, and threatened to cut me if he didn't get it. Something about the look in the guy's eyes told me he was serious, so I gave it to him. He wasn't done, though. He took everything including my clothes, all the while no one seemed to pause or care. It was like we were in our own little theater, and I was the joke act of the night. When the man left me naked, standing on the sidewalk, the world seemed to take notice again.

I was quickly approached by a police officer who put me in the back of their squad car while I explained what happened. Much to my surprise, they believed my story. They took a statement and took me home. I made all of the appropriate calls regarding my bank cards and just stared at the wall in disbelief. Then, I felt it again. As I spun to face the thing with it's stupid smile it held out its hand like a greedy child waiting for their allowance. I glared at the little monster and flicked on the light in my room, giving it no dark place to slink off to, but to my surprise...it didn't vanish. It just held out its hand, demanding some form of payment for ruining my day.

"Fuck off you little freak. I'm not-" I paused, the thing wasn't point at me this time, it was pointing at a picture. A picture of Jenna. I looked at it, fury in my face. Was this thing threatening my wife? All over a fucking coffee cup? I still paused, I still hesitated. I was mugged today, after all, and this little thing was the cause, I was sure. I couldn't take the risk of it hurting Jenna. A defeated sigh escaped my lips as I walked over to the desk and picked up a pen. I slapped it into the creature's hand and it looked at it a moment, grin slowly turning into a frown as it dropped the object and held out its hand again.

"What the fuck do you want, then!?" I screamed, glad Jenna wasn't home. "I was mugged this morning." I growled. "I'm sure you know that. I've got no money, and I had to take the day off work! What the fuck can I even give you!?"

The creature said nothing, infuriatingly, it just held out its hand expectantly. In a rage, I cocked my hand back and slapped the thing. Its head violently jerked to one side and the frown on its face turned to a glower. It pointed more fervently to its hand as it stared at me, beady little eyes demanding something it refused to ask for. I wanted to hit it again, but my hand tingled like I had struck tv static.

I blinked at it, confusion painting my features as I continued to offer it things. Clothes, plates, money. Nothing seemed to be enough, it just kept dropping them and pointing to it hand. I was frustrated, desperate, I didn't know what to give it. Finally, I decided to offer something else. I began to reach for it's hand.

I was stopped by the sound of the door opening, Jenna had come home early. Not really surprise, I did tell her I had been mugged. I turned to look at the creature, its glowering frown slowly turning back to a grin as it slunk away from me, no payment taken. 

Jenna ran into my office and hugged me, concerned over what had happened. She checked me over, double checked, and triple checked just to ensure I was alright. When she was certain thigs were fine, she calmed down. I explained exactly what had happened and how weird the whole thing felt. She agreed with me, especially when I told her it was in street view and no one stopped it. We both hoped the police would be able to handle things, but for the night we were just going to spend it together. No work. No distractions. Just us.

I woke to Jenna's coughing.

She was in the bathroom, hunched over the toilet, her skin was ghost-white and a thin sheen of sweat coated her features. "Something's wrong..." she groaned, shortly before falling unconscious. 

I rushed her to the hospital as fast as I could, and spent hours in terror as I waited to hear what happened. 

When the doctor finally came, his expression was grim. "I've never seen anything like it..." he whispered as he spoke to me. "Her body's just...shutting down." he looked to me, holding my hands tightly. "You'd...best go say your goodbyes. I don't think she has long."

My world spun. Everything around me was just a dull buzzing noise, I felt sick, like I was falling and flying all at the same time. Staggering into the room, I saw her, attached to machines that were barely keeping her alive. I fell to my knees. The world suddenly felt so heavy, and I felt so lost. I held her hands and wept, blaming myself. Blaming that thing. 

Jenna died early that morning. Everything about her passing was medical mystery, no one could explain it. I knew though. I knew what had happened.

I chose to look into the thing after that. To try and figure out what it was. I contacted priests, mediums, paranormal experts, occult researchers, anyone I could think of to help me figure out how to deal with this thing. Not one could explain it. Some 

I didn't go back to the apartment for a few weeks. Spent my nights locked away in a hotel. I couldn't go back. Not to the memories of us. Not to where I knew it was waiting.

It wasn't waiting there, though.

On the last night of my hotel stay, I was awakened to that feeling of wrongess I had felt so many times before, and when I opened my eyes it was there. Sitting in the chair near my bed, grinning wildly and holding out its hand greedily. My eyes welled up with tears as I looked at this thing, unable to even pull myself from my bed. "Why...?" I asked it as it held its hand out. "Why her? She didn't do anything to you...she was innocent. She couldn't even see you! WHY DID YOU TAKE HER FROM ME!?" the thing tilted its head, it said nothing, it simply held out its hand as if still waiting for payment.

"I tried..." I whispered. "I tried to give you something. To give you what you were asking me for..." shaking my head I pulled myself out of bed and stared at the little monster. "You wouldn't take anything..." I staggered over to the desk, reached into my pants and grabbed whatever cash I had in there. Fifty-three dollars. I shoved the money into the monster's hand and it looked it over, considering what was offered before shaking it onto the ground and holding out its hand again.

In frustration, in rage, in grief, I broke down. I sobbed in front of this thing for at least an hour and it just watched, waiting for me to finish before it held out its hand again in silent demand. "I have nothing...nothing you want." the creature nodded, tapping its palm with its free hand before slinking into the darkness.

I lost my job two weeks ago.

The whole company went under. Something about an embezzling scheme. I was just a junior executive and hired after the investigation had begun, so I was safe...but my livelyhood was gone. My wife was gone, and every day I was growing more isolated from the world. Every day that thing would show up and make its demands.

So now I'm here. Writing this all down in hopes that someone, anyone will believe me. This thing has haunted me, reaching out as though it wants something, but it would never take what was offered. Not after the first gift was given. I felt it grow excited when I began to reach for it. To take its hand.

I think I know what it wants from me, and God help me...I'm tempted to finally give in.
