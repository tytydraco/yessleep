(Part two)  





I'm still trapped in the facility, but I'm a lot better off than I was. I managed to adjust the temperature from inside the storage room. A safety precaution in case of accidental lock ins. But escaping with those things still out there just wasn't an option. To be honest, I'm alive only because the security guard that was missing from the front gate came to check on the situation.

As I sat there trying to think of a way out of that nightmarish predicament, the sounds of growling and scratching abruptly came to a sudden halt. The silence was deafening considering it was all I'd heard for the last hour or so. 

I sprung up to peek out the storage door window. I could see the back of the four men in a violent dash towards the entrance of the lab. I lost sight of them as they rounded the corner and exited the lab.

I knew this was the only chance I would get to escape the tomb I was trapped in. I slowly opened the door and crept out into the aisle. Staying low, I slowly made my way to the doorway, grabbing a surgical knife left out on the counter as I went.

I looked left. I looked right. Both ends of the hall were clear. I began to make my way towards the control room. It felt like an eternity. Having to round the corners at a snails pace made the journey back excruciating. I hadn't seen or heard anything until I reached the corridor that led to the control room. I stopped just before turning down the final hallway to peek around the corner.

About 30 feet past the entryway, I saw Devon and two of the lab workers completely dismembering Jeff, the facilities security guards who was missing from the front gate when I had first arrived. They weren't just thrashing, ripping and pulling him apart, they were gnawing on his flesh. Even swallowing what they bit off. 

I could feel my stomach turn as I looked away. Now I understood why they suddenly lost interest in me. The thought of this guys family having to go on with life knowing he was devoured by these monsters, made it even worse.

I composed myself enough to slowly make my way down to the open doorway. The closer I got, the louder the crunching and slurping noise got. It took everything in me to keep from losing my breakfast. 

Slowly I crept towards my destination. Just as I made it inside, I looked down to see the other labtech devouring Cynthia, one of the night shift house keepers. She was emptying trash when the entire scene unfolded outside the control room where she was working. 

I took a big breath before I positioned the knife in my hand. With a heavy swing, I drove the blade directly through the side of the maniacs neck. He let out a loud screech, turned around and staggered towards me. I wasted no time and begun striking him in the throat over and over again until he laid there lifeless. 

The noise undoubtedly drew the other three away from the mutilated officer. I slammed the door shut and turned the dead lock to seal myself in. The pounding, scratching and screaming all started over as if the soundtrack to this nightmare started right where it left off

In the control room, there were monitors set up all along the walls to my right. I started turning on the security feed giving me a clear picture of what was going on around the entire facility. 

Every camera mounted on the exterior walls showed a thick haze looming around the entire campus. As employees arrived, they all drove and walked straight through it. 

The cameras also revealed that every entrance into the building was blocked by people turning into rabid animals. It was instantaneous. As soon as they made contact with the cloud, they began screaming and pounding on the doors. Almost like they know I'm in here. 

Lucky for me, the control room has a  kitchen with a vending machine, soda machine, coffee maker and even some leftover lunches in the fridge. It also connects with the locker rooms so I should be OK for the moment.

I've tried calling 911 over and over again. All I get is a busy signal. I'll keep trying, but I'm starting to think that maybe opening that ventilation was a bad idea. 

I need to take care of the two bodies in here with me. I'll drag them into the locker room for now. After that, I'm going to eat and try to get some sleep if it's possible. Thank you all for reading. I feel better telling someone. It makes me feel not so alone.
