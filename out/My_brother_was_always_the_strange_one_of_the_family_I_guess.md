I remember teasing him mercilessly when we were children.

About his hair, that was just a shade darker than mine or our parents’; about the way he always slouched, his sickly complexion, and his far too thin and lanky body...

Let’s be real here, I was a shitty sibling.

He, being born one year after me, looked up to me, but to be honest, I wanted nothing to do with him.

I told him that he had been adopted, but Mom and Dad just didn’t have the heart to admit it, only so I could see his face scrunched up with this pained expression.

Mom was furious, and I, of course, told her that it had just been a joke and that he was too sensitive...

Yeah, like all bullies do.

Thinking back now, I must have looked like a monster to him.

Until I was ten, maybe twelve years old, he followed me around everywhere, but by the time I turned fourteen, my brother started to pull away.

Even at that age he realized that I wasn’t worth admiring.

He avoided me at home and in school, and although back then I thought it was great, I can now see how much I put him through.

When not in school, he stuck to his room, only coming out for dinner or at our parents’ request...

It must have been hell for him, right?

When I was sixteen, and he was fifteen, my brother got into this emo or goth stuff.

I didn’t get it back then... I still don’t even now, to be honest, but I mocked him mercilessly for wearing black and, as I put it, trying to seem cool by listening to shitty music and pretending to read old-looking books.

More and more kids at school joined in, and I remember that pained expression on his face when I cornered him as he tried to hide from my friends and me.

Man... I hate myself for this, believe me...

I would like to say it got better when I left for college, but all we did was just stop interacting with each other.

He was living his life, and I mine.

We saw each other maybe twice a year, at our parents’ house, and I never even bothered to get his phone number or e-mail address...

That was our relationship by then, and I know that all the blame for that falls on me.

I never even apologized.

Two years ago, at Christmas, my mom told me that my brother had moved to the same city I was in and would be all too happy if we could bury the hatchet.

I didn’t even give her an answer, nor did I reach out to him.

Back then, I felt like the world was at my feet, and I think part of me already realized that interacting with my brother might hasten the karma that was coming my way...

I visited his apartment twice in the two years, though.

Both times while he wasn’t there.

It was nothing more than just the typical watering the plants and checking for leaks while he was gone on a conference across the country...

I did it reluctantly, after Dad almost begged me to, and both times my brother left something for me, a bottle of good wine, but I ignored the offerings.

Was that arrogance or shame?

I’m not so sure anymore.

What I did do was check out his apartment, and I have to admit, I felt almost jealous.

His place seemed great.

You could really get a feel for the owner living a happy life in there.

Yeah, he still collected weird stuff, things that made me roll my eyes, like those dumb little figurines and some strange, old books, but it didn’t seem like he was trying to prove anything, just following his interests...

I guess those old feelings flared up once more back then, at my second visit.

About a week before we saw each other again last Christmas, my partner left me for someone else; I got into a bit of a heated argument with my boss at work, and that stress led me to drink a bit too much at our family’s party.

My memory is kinda fuzzy, but I still remember feeling this anger inside as I watched my brother talk to my parents, telling them that his life was going great.

I told him that I was happy for him... sarcastically, of course, and that he had done well for someone adopted.

No one laughed, and my brother just shot me that tired glance...

I was steaming inside.

After what had to be a bottle of wine, I got into a screaming match with him...

It wasn’t his fault; it wasn’t even the alcohol’s fault... that was just me, again, trying to spread the hurt I was feeling, attacking someone else just to get my mind off how shitty I felt...

I should have apologized the next day; turned up at his place and told him everything I’m trying to say right now.

That I was a shitty sibling and always tried to tear him down...

But I didn’t.

I couldn’t even send him an e-mail or shoot him a text...

When did I get so self-absorbed?

This time though, I know it was shame that made me act like that, even if I didn’t want to admit it.

Our parents finally gave up making me see reason after a month, which brings me to three days ago.

I lost my job a few weeks back, fired for cause, and getting a new job, even a few levels below my old one, seems impossible right now. My nice car broke down, and I have to drive this shitty replacement. Rent in my building suddenly shot up, and I think I can smell mold coming from one of the walls... Another fling fizzled out and ended as badly as one can imagine, and I swear, everything in my life has started turning to shit those past few months...

I was lying on the bed, doom-scrolling, as my phone suddenly rang.

The number wasn’t one I recognized, but as soon as I took the call, I knew who was on the other end.

My brother.

He was breathing heavily; I think I heard him sobbing as well...

I was too shocked to even speak, so I just waited and listened until finally, his voice came through.

“I am sorry,” he said. “I am so sorry.”

He cut the call short before I could reply, and suddenly, I got this... premonition...

I could feel it so clearly.

Something bad had happened to my brother.

I tried calling back, but there was no answer.

For the first time in my life, I truly felt this connection with him as I jumped up and raced out of the door, got into my car, and broke pretty much every speed limit on my way to his apartment.

He didn’t answer his door, but I got in anyway.

The flat I found myself standing in looked exactly like the one I had visited the last time, but it felt different.

Strangely dark, cold, and uninviting.

I looked around and found a few old books strewn about, but no sign of my brother.

His keys were still there, as was his phone, wallet, shoes, and jacket.

There was no sign of him leaving; he was neither caught by the doorbell camera nor seen by the security guard downstairs...

I know he didn’t walk out, but apparently, that’s the theory the police are going with right now.

The sad thing is, not even our parents believe me.

I spent twenty hours driving up and down the streets, calling his contacts, looking through the video of his camera, snooping around his phone...

Nothing.

It was as if he had simply vanished into thin air...

That would be bad enough on its own, but after I finally gave in, went back to my place, and crashed on my bed, I saw him again.

It wasn’t a dream... at least it didn’t feel like one.

I blinked and opened my eyes, looked down the bed, and saw this figure, sitting there on the corner of the mattress, its face turned away from me.

“I am sorry,” he said.

I screamed his name in response and jumped up, but as I reached out for him, his figure suddenly vanished like a shadow hit by light.

It couldn’t have been a dream, right?

At least, that was what I told myself, then called my parents to see if they had heard anything from him by now.

My mom said no, and when I told her about that strange dream, she seemed even more exhausted than normal and begged me to go to bed as well.

Stress... that was her explanation.

Reluctantly, I followed her advice, but this time, I didn’t make it to my bedroom but just laid down on my couch.

I don’t know how much time passed, but suddenly I woke up as someone called my name.

It was night, the lights were out, and I could hardly see anything, but as my brother’s voice called my name for a second time, my eyes immediately focused on the deep, dark shadow in the corner.

Someone was standing there.

I could feel it, even if I couldn’t see it.

“I’m so sorry,” the voice said, his tone grim and sad.

This time, I tried to get up more carefully but slipped and hit the small couch table with my head, and the moment the thud echoed around my living room, the feeling of the presence vanished as well...

As I lay there, on the floor, holding my ringing head, even I had to admit that something was wrong with this whole mess.

Maybe it was the guilt, maybe the stress... whatever it was, I needed to see a doctor as well.

Sleep was hard to come by the rest of the night, but first thing in the morning, I made an emergency appointment with my therapist.

On my way there, two of my tires blew out, and I had to Uber the rest of the way...

The doctor came to the same diagnosis as I did, and as you have in all likelihood as well...

A lack of sleep leads to stress and probably guilt-induced hallucinations.

He gave me some meds and sent me back home.

I definitely shouldn’t have taken them.

They made me drowsy and tired.

I fell asleep in my bed, but as soon as my body stopped tossing and turning, I felt it again.

This presence, so close by.

I looked up and could see the figure of my brother, sitting at the foot of my bed.

“I’m so sorry,” he said again, but I couldn’t even open my mouth to answer.

He started swaying from side to side. “I’m sorry I put that on you.”

I could feel my tongue lolling around my mouth, too tired, too numb to form sounds.

My brother stopped shifting and froze, then lowered his head and turned his body.

I could feel this cold chill in the air as I could do nothing but watch helplessly as he started to crawl up my bed toward me, his face turned down and his fingers ghostly pale.

“I did it to you... I’m sorry,” he said again.

Even in this hazy state, I felt the danger I was in.

My heart was beating like crazy; I was sweating and trembling but couldn’t move a muscle.

He was still crawling toward me, mumbling, then sobbing, as his hand touched my foot.

I wanted to scream but couldn’t even open my mouth fully.

Something was terribly wrong with him, I understood then.

He was coming closer and closer.

His hand touched my hip, then chest.

I could smell him.

The sweet scent of his shampoo mixed with the sour stench of sweat and fear.

“I think we’re both bound for hell now,” my brother whispered, raised his head, and I could see his face for the first time.

His eyes were gone, but tears were streaming down his cheeks.

His mouth was formed into a silent scream.

Blood was running from his nose, dripping down onto my bedsheet.

I blacked out, and as I came to, I could see the red spots on my bedding...

The doctor said I must have developed night terrors and has given me ‘something stronger ‘; my mom told me she thinks I just had a nosebleed while sleeping...

Then why is there this bloody trail running from the foot of my bed up to the pillow?

I can feel my brother now, every time I close my eyes.

He’s here.

I know it.

Waiting in the shadows, together with something that isn’t human at all...

He put it on me; he admitted as much himself.

I want to apologize to you, Sam.

I never was the sibling you deserved, and whatever happened to you, I am sorry for that as well.

Please, please... just leave me be.

I admit to everything and more.

The people here are my witnesses.

Please let me live.

I’m sorry.
