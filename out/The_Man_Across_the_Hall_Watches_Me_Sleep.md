&#x200B;

I wasn’t going to post this.

I know how it sounds. I’ve read enough stuff on r/nosleep to know exactly what people say when something feels even slightly off.

Carbon monoxide. Sleep paralysis. Fake.

I checked all of that.

This is different.

\-----

The first thing I found was the note.

It was pushed halfway under my door when I got back from taking the trash out. I almost stepped on it.

No envelope. Just folded once.

\> “You leave your blinds open at night.

He’s been watching you for months.”

I actually laughed.

Not because it was funny, but because it felt… corny. Like someone in the building trying to mess with me.

I checked the hallway. Empty. Same ugly carpet, same buzzing light that flickers every few seconds.

I crumpled the note and tossed it.

But that night, I closed my blinds anyway.

\--

Nothing happened for a few days.

That’s what made it worse.

If something did happen right away, I could’ve written it off as coincidence. But instead, everything stayed normal—just long enough for me to feel stupid.

Then little things started.

Small enough that you question yourself.

My TV volume would be lower than I left it.

My phone would be unlocked on apps I didn’t remember opening.

Once, my front door was closed, but not latched.

I started double-checking everything.

Locks. Windows. Blinds.

Especially the blinds.

\--- --

A week later, I found the second note.

This time it was inside.

On my kitchen counter.

I know I locked the door that night. I remember checking it twice because of the first note.

But there it was.

\> “You forgot to close them last night.”

I checked the blinds immediately.

They were open.

I don’t remember opening them.

\--

That was the first time I looked across the hall.

There’s an apartment directly opposite mine. Same layout. Same door.

I’d never really paid attention to it before.

The door was closed.

But something about it felt… wrong.

Like when you can tell someone’s looking at you, even if you can’t see them.

I stepped closer to my peephole.

And I swear to you—

There was something on the other side.

Not a full face. Not clearly.

Just… pressure. Like an eye was already there, pressed up against it.

Watching back.

\- -

After that, I stopped sleeping right.

Every sound in the hallway felt intentional.

Footsteps that would stop right outside my door.

The faintest brushing noise, like fabric against wood.

Once, I heard breathing.

Not loud.

Just enough.

\-

I told myself I was being paranoid.

Until the photo.

It was on my phone.

Taken at 3:12 AM.

I’m in my bed, on my side, facing the wall. You can see my back, the blanket pulled up to my shoulder.

And in the corner of the frame—

My door is open.

Just slightly.

\---

I don’t remember waking up.

I don’t remember anyone coming in.

I don’t remember taking that picture.

But it’s there.

Still in my camera roll.

\- --

I stopped ignoring the notes after that.

Because they didn’t stop.

They changed.

They got… personal.

\> “You talk in your sleep.”

“You check your phone 4 times before you actually go to bed.”

“You think about him more than you admit.”

That last one messed me up.

Because it was true.

And I’ve never said that out loud.

\-

A few nights later, I finally saw him.

Not clearly.

Never clearly.

I opened my door after hearing something outside—just a soft shuffle—and he was already there.

Across the hall.

Standing in his doorway.

The light behind him made him look like a silhouette, but I could tell he was looking straight at me.

Not surprised.

Not caught.

Waiting.

Like he’d been standing there, knowing I would open the door at that exact moment.

We just stared at each other.

And then—

He smiled.

Not wide. Not exaggerated.

Just enough.

And closed his door.

\--- -

That’s when everything changed.

I should’ve left.

I know that.

But I didn’t.

Because at some point, it stopped feeling like I was being watched…

…and started feeling like I was being seen.

\---

The next note said:

\> “You don’t have to be scared anymore.”

And I wasn’t.

Not really.

\---

I started leaving my blinds open again.

At first, it was by accident.

Then it wasn’t.

There’s something about knowing someone is watching you that changes how you move.

How you exist.

You become… sharper.

More aware.

More intentional.

Like everything matters.

\-- --

Then he started teaching me.

Not directly.

But I understood.

How to be... a creeper.

Where to look.

How to notice patterns.

How people think they’re alone when they’re not.

Ya know... There’s a building across from mine.

Fourth floor.

Corner unit.

There’s a kid who lives there.

He stays up late. Falls asleep with his TV on. Never closes his blinds.

At first, I just looked.

The same way I imagined HE looked at me.

From a distance.

Through glass.

\-

The first time I realized the kid could see me…

I didn’t move.

I just stood there.

Let him notice.

Let it sit.

That exact moment—

That recognition—

It does something to you.

\--- -

I got the last note a week ago.

No slipping under the door.

No sneaking inside.

It was just… there.

On my bed.

\> “You don’t need me anymore.”

I haven’t seen him since.

I checked the apartment across the hall.

Management says it’s been vacant for years.

\-----

I don’t really think about that part anymore.

It doesn’t feel important.

\-

What does feel important is this:

The kid across from me?

He started closing his blinds a few nights ago.

Then last night—

He left them open again.

Just a little.

Like he wasn’t sure.

Like he was testing something.

\---

I stood there for a while.

Watching him.

Waiting.

\--

I think he knows now.

And I think he’s going to leave them open again tonight.
