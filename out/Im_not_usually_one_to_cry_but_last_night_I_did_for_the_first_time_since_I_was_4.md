I would consider myself a night owl; I like to go for 2am jogs, shower and then stay up scrolling on TikTok in the dark. That was exactly what I did last night. Well, what I intended to do. It was particularly humid, and I excitedly hopped into the shower after my run. I turned it on, letting the warm water trickle down my bare skin.   
  
And then I heard it; a faint knock on the bathroom door.  
  
“Nigel?” It was my mom's voice. That was strange. I knew my Dad would sometimes stay up for work, but my Mom would usually be asleep before the clock even struck midnight. “Yeah mom, what is it?” I asked, startled but curious about what she was doing up at this ‘ungodly’ hour. *Silence*. “Nigel?” she said, a little louder this time. A little annoyed, I turned off the shower and repeated myself. “What is it??”. *Silence again.* A whole minute went by and… nothing. I shivered a little as the air got noticeably colder.   
  
I was a little concerned, but considering I was still covered in soap, I told myself I would check on her in a few minutes and turned the warm water back on.   
  
Suddenly, a heavy pounding began on the bathroom door, violent and irregular, and the bathroom lights turned off. “MOM WHAT THE HELL? WHAT DO YOU WANT?” I half-yelled, partially angry, partially flustered. My mom was NOT the kind of person who played pranks.   
  
And that’s when I noticed it. The faint glow of the lamp in the hallway… I had turned it on earlier. It shone through the crack on the door… but something wasn’t right. There was no shadow. There was no one there, there couldn’t be. And yet the knocking grew louder.   
  
I panicked and glanced at the mirror reflecting the door. and there she was. My mother was in the bathroom with me, facing away towards the door and slamming her head into it repeatedly, Her bed hair flying in the air. Her nightgown hung precariously on her shoulders as her body swung back and forth. “mum...?” I whispered. Then the knocking stopped.   
  
I stared in horror as the woman in the mirror turned to face me in an odd slow motion. But something wasn't right. She didn't have HER face. It was my own, with strands of long dyed hair draped over my dark eyes. An eerie grin carved into his face. I closed my eyes as the tears came rushing. I sunk to the floor and rocked myself, back and forth. Back and forth. My hands found my ears. “This isn't real, this isn't real.” I told myself… and suddenly I heard nothing.   
  
I opened my eyes, but there I- HE was, staring at me. His eyes were lifeless and yet that disgusting smile remained. I couldn’t breathe. And then the light came on, and along with it, hope, in the form of an unfriendly voice. My sister's voice. “Dude, why are you in the bathroom with the lights off? I need to pee. Hurry up.” 

I don't know what happened. Maybe it was some kind of demon, or for some fucked up reason God decided to prank me. All I know is that it was real. At least to me. My family doesn’t have a history of mental illness and that was the first and only time I’ve ever experienced something like this. It’s 7AM in the morning while I’m writing this and I have not slept. I’m gonna get some shut-eye.   
  
I hope I get to see my actual mom when I wake up.
