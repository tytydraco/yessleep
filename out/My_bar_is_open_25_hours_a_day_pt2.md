So, I accidentally forgot to mention that we have a certain list for the 25th hour regulars. Grace came up with the idea as she's been here the longest and seen "a lot of tomfoolery and skulduggery", as she puts it. If you're ever working here you can actually find the poster she made for us in the break room so we don't forget. She's really nice like that.

Said list reads as follows:

1. Three piece: Bloody Mary
2. Ed, Ted, and Sped: Yzyb'Qvool'Yzwp'IslG with Vodka
3. Co'chetti: Rum and Coke, served with cough syrup
4. Dylan: deny request, give non-alcoholic or water only
5. Bossman: Ylwb Martini (stirred not shaken, teeth for extra tips, ONLY if available)
6. Keith: Pickle Brine and Whiskey Chaser, MUST come with peanuts

From time-to-time we have these men dressed in black three piece suits turn up with one of them always carrying a pristine white briefcase. They're usually harmless, having only threatened violence once in the time I have been here, but the boss can always calm them down with a drink and conversation out back, or the cellar behind the iron door. I've seen faint movement beneath the floorboards and heard them talking in hushed voices. I can never make out what they were saying, only that the tone of the conversation is serious. More serious than the boss ever talks to us when we mess up. Whatever relationship they have with the boss, my best guess is that it's transactional. The agents walk away with a much heavier and smudged briefcase.

Ed, Ted, and Sped are three triplets that I physically cannot describe. Every time I try to recall what they look like, nothing comes to mind. I don't know their height, weight, eye colours, hair colours, body type, nothing. The only thing I do know for certain is that the more whipped cream they have, the happier they all are. I might make a sketch next time and show it to you.

Co'chetti, on the other hand, will never be erased from my mind. Appearance wise, Co'chetti's head grazes the ceiling when his gaunt, gangly frame stands upright, and he constantly complains whenever the fan slices chunks out of his greying flesh. The wounds ooze a reddish black which gets flicked across the room like a festive sprinkler. Aside from the gory clean up, the bridge between his head and shoulders takes the form of a snaking piece of wrinkled wet meat that causes his head to continuously move at odd angles. An undulating quiver rattles through him whenever he talks, then turns into a violent shiver upon wheezing out his intensely hearty laugh. Even after hearing that laugh so many times, I still have to battle the queasiness bubbling in my gut as the sound bounces against the walls of my skull. Always good to have some medicine on standby when he's near.

Dylan is probably the one I'm the most terrified of. Every time I see that middle aged man drag his feet across the floorboards with his hands in his pockets and head hung low, I will do just about anything to get away from him. Don't get the wrong idea though. He is not a lovecraftian horror nor is he wearing a skin suit. He is just boring. Really, *really* boring. Whenever you talk to him for more than 3 minutes, it's like being stuck in a ziplock bag squeezed of oxygen. He just goes on about everything wrong in his life, and alcohol only makes it worse because he is a sad drunk. I don't know how many of you have dealt with a sad, light weight drunkard telling you his entire life story every time he walks in for a drink, but I can tell you it has made me think on many occasions why I haven't quit. I have more fun rehydrating the people who were unable to break conversation with him. Their fingers can snap off so easily it's not funny.

Bossman is the boss' cousin who likes to mess with us as they're almost identical. He'll bark random orders at us which a lot of newbies will instantly follow without question. He has this weird authority in his voice that can force you to betray your own survival instincts if it means fulfilling his commands. Of course, the way you can spot the difference between them is by their drink of choice. Boss and Bossman will always take you up on a Ylwb Martini with the key difference being that the boss is a bit of a 007 fan. The boss even does his best Bond impression whenever he orders it. Although, his cousin has started to get even better at copying him, so fingers crossed he doesn't get a taste for his martinis shaken.

Last, but not least, is Keith. Where do I even begin? Keith has made me want to put a fist through his head on more than one occasion. He's demanding, whiny, deliberately makes a mess, leaves slime (that's what I tell myself) wherever he sits, and likes to get a rise out of employees and fellow patrons alike. He always gets away with it because he pays an egregious amount of money every time he's in here. Like, he literally paid over 10K so he wouldn't get in trouble for starting a bar fight which broke our AC. It's been 5 weeks since the incident and we *still* haven't found someone to come out here and fix it! The dayshift is allegedly on the brink of heatstroke because of it. All because of Fat Fuck Keith! I'm sorry, I genuinely despise that man with every cell in my body.

That is everyone from the 25th hour regulars list. It's definitely saved me and others both literally and dramatically, and according to Grace it really lessened the turnover rate of the Brew 'n Bones. Or slowed it depending if you're a glass half full or half empty person.

On a side tangent, Evan's been acting worse lately. He came in muttering to himself, and his teeth now look like cheddar blocks stuffed awkwardly into his gums. The smell he emits is as indescribable as Ed, Ted, and Sped. There wasn't even a twitch in his line of sight, nor fidget of his eyelids when I waved my hand in front of his face, and he didn't respond to me calling his name.

I'm kind of tempted to have him stay over at my place for a day or two to keep an eye on him. I really hope he doesn't become another Old Man Henry. If I can stomach the memory, I might be able to tell you guys what happened to Henry.
