"And how might I help you?" The deep voice of Brother James sounded like the bellows of some ancient organ behind me.

I spun around "I left my pen here yesterday"

His brow knitted closer, obviously not believing me "your one and only pen?"

His question caused me to notice how he stood purposefully in the doorway. "Yes, Sir" I answered nervously.

At this he leaned down and in a gutteral but barely audible tone whispered "Get out of my classroom, you dirty sinner"

My face became cold, as if my very blood had been stalled by his words. All I could think about was "he knows".

The lean man stood tall once more, and standing to side cast his arm out, indicating to where I should direct my trembling legs. "Be gone, boy"

My feet operated on their own, they marched to an instinctual tune of self- preservation. "Thank" I stuttered "Thank you, sir"

The door slammed behind me. 

The proceeding lessons - maths and English - were mere background noises as the thought of Brother James, Brother David, and Brother Maxwell knowing about myself and Danny. My fear intensified when I thought about exactly how much they might know. 

Lunch arrived, and with it I was surprised to see how my peers no longer looked at me. They had received no gossip or information, and as such simply stopped looking for it, Danny's disappearance, which occured only a handful of days ago, no longer seemed interesting. However, the same could not be said about the brothers. Along the checkered floor they moved like glaring chess pieces, as if each were trying their best to corner me. It was  here that I noticed a developing bruise perched on the jaw of Brother James.

There was something predatory in his eyes, the way they searched the room for any thing he alone deemed as unfavourable behaviour. When his eyes landed on me, he glided along the floor toward me. Checkmate.

"Brother Maxwell wishes to see you in his office." His sagging jaws like disused mandibles "I would go now boy" 

Maxwell and I sat in silence for a while. The ticking of the small blue clock on his desk, rhythmically conversing with the void. 

"Conrad" he suddenly spoke "it is disappointing that you are sitting here once more" 

My leg began to bounce slightly "I am sorry si-" 

His raised hand ceased my talking "I do not deal with apologies" his hand returned to the desktop "I deal with promises. Do you understand?"

"Yes, sir"

"And I promise you, Conrad." I held my breathe as he articulated every word "Enter another classroom without permission again, and I'll ensure that you clean every square inch of that room with a toothbrush"

I sighed with relief.

"Do you understand?"

"I do, sir"

Brother Maxwell stared at me for a moment, his eyes were searching for something. As he stood up, as if to dismiss me, I blurted out the question that bubbled away in my head.

"How is Danny?"

The old man smiled. He lowered himself back into his seat, his elbows bending in such a way that he vaguely resembled a spider, ready to pounce.

"Danny" he grunted as he hit the seat, his eyes alight with a sort of excitement. "Danny is still making a recovery, I suspect we will not being seeing him for sometime."

My eyes met his, and after a moment, I had felt like I revealed something I shouldn't have.

"Can you tell him that I was asking about him?" I spoke quickly, wishing to leave.

"That I cannot do, Conrad." His smile seemed to deepen "I've spoken to you already about the dangerous of such a relationship. I will not offer fuel toward it"

"I understand, Sir"

His glare was fixed on me now. "Conrad" he paused, looking at the blue clock. "I want you to return to your room for the day. I will inform Brother James of your apology, and I will inform Brother Adrian that you will not be attending his French lesson."

I nodded. 

"You are dismissed"

That night I went to the pond.

 The water was as still as ever. I thought of the pebble that Danny threw that night. What did it think about when it looked back up from at the surface. Did it think about the excitement its momentary flight gave it? Or did it think about its life before it ever met Danny? 

I can't believe I let that boy think that I loved him. My selfish need to not be alone did this. I did this.

A gnat landed on the water's surface, allowing a small ring to grow from the point of impact. This tiny wave brushed gently against the reeds that sprung from the shore.

I stood up, and as I pushed the mesh of the fence out of the way, I watched the pond become still once more. 

I felt tears form, and it wasn't until I had climbed the fire escape, and clicked the window shut that I let them fall. 

Eventually I slept.

I repeated my nocturnal visit the next night. The hawthorn had buds of new life was what I first noticed. Then I noticed the upturned mesh. Had I forgotten to turn it downward last night? I scrambled through it.

The mud was badly disturbed. As if some injured beast struggled its way beyond the mesh. My heart beat loudly as ideas ran across the plains of my panicked mind.

A mass of yellow lay by the water's edge. Around him the mud had been kicked and flattened by his frantic convulsions.  He fought against them to get here. 

He came for me.

His lifeless face lay downward, little gnat- ripples toyed with his curly hair. 

The mesh behind me was ripped away, and I heard the panting, exhausted breathing of Brother David approach. I wanted to run, but I couldn't. I only tore my eyes from him when Brother David turned me to face him. He rose his pink fist, and I felt my world fade as he let it fall.

I awoke in a tiled hallway. Religious mosaics adorned the dimly lit ceiling. Somewhere above me I heard what sounded like a large fridge door shutting. 

I was wearing a yellow habit.
