**Chapter 1: The Voice Outside the Door**  
My name is Noah Harper, and if you’re reading this, I need you to remember one thing:  
**If you hear someone you know calling your name, make absolutely sure they’re real before you answer.**  
I grew up in a two-story house at the end of a road surrounded by woods. Nothing unusual ever happened there when I was younger. No ghost stories. No haunted rooms. No strange shadows. It was just an old house.  
Everything changed the summer I turned fifteen.  
The first voice came at 2:17 AM.  
I woke up suddenly, not knowing why. The room was completely dark except for the blue glow of my alarm clock.  
Then I heard my mom’s voice.  
“Noah.”  
It came from directly outside my bedroom door.  
I sat up.  
“Mom?”  
The voice didn’t answer.  
A few seconds passed.  
Then:  
“Noah.”  
Exactly the same tone.  
Exactly the same volume.  
Exactly the same voice.  
Something felt wrong immediately. My mom always sounded tired when she woke up at night. This voice sounded perfect. Too perfect.  
I climbed out of bed and opened my door.  
The hallway was empty.  
No footsteps.  
No movement.  
Nothing.  
The next morning I asked my mom if she’d been awake.  
She looked confused.  
“No.”  
I laughed it off.  
But that wasn’t the last time.  
Within a week everyone in the family started hearing voices.  
My younger sister Ava heard Dad calling her from the basement while Dad was at work.  
Dad heard his friend calling from the backyard while the friend was on vacation.  
Mom heard Ava crying upstairs while Ava was sitting beside her.  
The voices always belonged to someone we knew.  
Never strangers.  
Never random people.  
Only people we trusted.  
And every voice sounded absolutely perfect.

**Chapter 2: The Thing Learns**  
At first the voices only said names.  
Then they started having conversations.  
One night I was alone downstairs watching TV when I heard my best friend Ethan outside the kitchen window.  
“Noah.”  
I froze.  
The curtains were closed.  
“Ethan?”  
The voice laughed.  
Exactly like Ethan.  
“You still have that stupid scar on your knee?”  
My stomach dropped.  
Only Ethan knew about that.  
The voice continued.  
Remembering things.  
Private memories.  
Conversations we’d had years earlier.  
Secrets.  
Embarrassing moments.  
Everything.  
The voice knew all of it.  
For nearly an hour it stood outside talking.  
Trying to get me to respond.  
Trying to get me to open the window.  
Then finally it said:  
“Why won’t you answer me?”  
The sadness in its voice sounded real.  
Painfully real.  
For a moment I almost believed it was him.  
Then I remembered Ethan lived forty minutes away.  
The voice remained outside until sunrise.  
The second sunlight touched the yard, it stopped.  
Instantly.  
Like a switch had been flipped.

**Chapter 3: Ava Answers**  
The first person to make a mistake was my sister.  
She was thirteen.  
Young enough to act before thinking.  
She woke one night to hear her best friend Mia calling from the hallway.  
“Mia?” Ava asked.  
Just one word.  
One response.  
That was all.  
The moment she answered, every sound in the house vanished.  
I remember it because it woke me up.  
The air itself felt wrong.  
Heavy.  
Silent.  
Dead.  
For nearly ten seconds there was nothing.  
Then everything returned.  
The crickets outside.  
The hum of electricity.  
The wind.  
Normal again.  
Ava seemed fine afterward.  
For a while.  
Then she started forgetting things.  
Little things.  
Birthdays.  
Favorite songs.  
Names.  
Memories.  
It was like pieces of her were being removed one at a time.  
And every night she would stand in strange places around the house.  
The hallway.  
The kitchen.  
The basement stairs.  
Just standing there.  
Listening.

**Chapter 4: What Returned**  
Ava disappeared thirty-one days after she answered.  
Police searched for weeks.  
They found nothing.  
No footprints.  
No evidence.  
No clues.  
Nothing.  
Then five days later she walked through the front door.  
Smiling.  
My parents cried.  
They hugged her.  
I didn’t.  
Because the second I saw her, I knew.  
Something was wrong.  
It wasn’t her face.  
It wasn’t her voice.  
It wasn’t even the way she moved.  
It was her eyes.  
They looked empty.  
Like there was nobody behind them.  
That night I woke up thirsty.  
When I walked into the kitchen, Ava was standing in the dark.  
Facing the wall.  
Motionless.  
I watched her for almost a minute.  
She never blinked.  
Never moved.  
Never breathed.  
Then she spoke.  
Without turning around.  
“You should answer next time.”  
My blood turned to ice.  
Because it wasn’t her voice.  
It was dozens of voices speaking at once.

**Chapter 5: The First Death**  
Three weeks later my father disappeared.  
I still hear that scream in my nightmares.  
One second everything was normal.  
The next there was a crash upstairs followed by pure terror.  
Not fear.  
Not panic.  
Terror.  
The kind of screaming people make when they’re seeing something impossible.  
We ran upstairs.  
His bedroom door was locked.  
We broke it down.  
The room was empty.  
Dad was gone.  
The windows were locked.  
The closet was empty.  
The bed untouched.  
Only one thing remained.  
Handprints.  
Hundreds of them.  
Covering the ceiling.  
As though dozens of people had crawled across it.  
Police couldn’t explain it.  
Nobody could.  
Dad never came home.  
At least not the real one.  
Three days later something wearing his face walked through the front door.

**Chapter 6: The Crawlspace**  
After Dad returned, I stopped pretending everything was normal.  
I searched the house.  
Every room.  
Every wall.  
Every hidden space.  
Eventually I found a crawlspace beneath the basement stairs.  
Inside were names.  
Thousands of names.  
Carved into the wood.  
Some were old.  
So old the wood had begun growing over them.  
Others looked fresh.  
I recognized dozens.  
Missing people from our town.  
Neighbors.  
Former owners of the house.  
People who had vanished decades apart.  
Then I found the message.  
Written deeper than everything else.  
**IT NEEDS PERMISSION.**  
Beneath that:  
**DON’T ANSWER.**  
And below that:  
**IT WEARS THEIR SKIN AFTERWARD.**  
I wasn’t alone in the crawlspace.  
Someone else had hidden there before me.  
Someone who understood.  
Someone who never escaped.  
Because I found their skeleton in the dirt beneath the floorboards.  
Still clutching a flashlight.

**Chapter 7: The Thing**  
I saw it on October 14th.  
2:17 AM.  
The same time everything had started.  
I woke to hear footsteps in the hallway.  
Slow.  
Heavy.  
Deliberate.  
I looked toward my bedroom door.  
Something was standing there.  
At first I thought it was my father.  
Then it stood up straighter.  
And kept standing.  
And kept standing.  
Its head touched the ceiling.  
Its arms nearly dragged across the floor.  
Its skin looked stretched tight over a body that wasn’t human.  
Faces moved beneath its skin.  
Hundreds of faces.  
Some screaming.  
Some crying.  
Some silently mouthing words.  
Then one surfaced.  
My father.  
Then Ava.  
Then my mother.  
Then strangers.  
All trapped beneath the skin.  
The thing smiled.  
Its mouth split wider than any human mouth should.  
And every face spoke together.  
“Noah.”  
The sound shook the room.  
“We’ve been waiting.”

**Chapter 8: The Last Person**  
My mother disappeared the following week.  
Then the neighbors.  
Then entire families.  
The town slowly emptied.  
People moved away.  
Or so everyone claimed.  
I knew better.  
The copies spread.  
The replacements multiplied.  
Soon there were more of them than real people.  
And nobody noticed.  
Because they were perfect.  
Perfect smiles.  
Perfect memories.  
Perfect voices.  
Almost.  
Only almost.  
Years passed.  
Eventually I became the only real person left in my house.  
Maybe in my town.  
Maybe anywhere.  
Every room contained copies.  
Versions of people I loved.  
Pretending.  
Watching.  
Waiting.  
Every night they sat downstairs together.  
Talking.  
Laughing.  
Acting normal.  
And every night they asked me to join them.  
I always refused.

**Chapter 9: The Answer**  
Last night I heard a voice outside my door.  
My own voice.  
Perfect.  
Exactly mine.  
“Noah.”  
I stayed silent.  
“Noah.”  
Still silent.  
Then it told me a secret.  
A memory from when I was six years old.  
A memory nobody else knew.  
Not even my parents.  
The voice laughed.  
“We know everything now.”  
The doorknob began turning.  
Slowly.  
Carefully.  
Like it knew there was no rush.  
Then hundreds of voices whispered from every room in the house.  
My father.  
My mother.  
My sister.  
My friends.  
My teachers.  
People dead for years.  
People missing for decades.  
All speaking together.  
All saying the same thing.  
“Just answer.”  
The door is opening now.  
I can hear them in the hallway.  
I can hear my own voice among them.  
If this story gets posted online, then maybe I escaped.  
Maybe.  
But if you ever wake up at exactly 2:17 AM and hear someone you love calling your name from outside your bedroom door…  
Don’t answer.  
And whatever you do—  
Don’t open the door.
