Living in a large residential University campus is peaceful especially at night, but it gets too peaceful sometimes.

During the exams season, my uncle, an associate professor, asked me to go to his office in his department to pick up the question paper so he could make the copies by the morning.

Too tired from playing badminton and too guilty for not doing anything productive all day, I sheepishly said okay.

The physics department was far enough, but as tired as I was, I didn't think twice, I put my phone on charging, switched the lights and fan off of the room ,before stepping out of the house.

Late at night, walking by the cold road the sudden chilly winds were making me want to hurry up. As I walked past the maths department I saw the lights were on and the fans of the lecture rooms were left on, which was odd to me as the department is usually locked by the end of day, but I shrugged it off. I quickly switched off the fans, but not the lights. 

It must've been around 2 am, yes 2 am, when I reached the physics department. It was colder and there was pitch darkness, I couldn't muster the courage to find the light switch in that damning darkness and I had no flashlights. So I quickly went upstairs, unlocked my uncle's office, turned on the lights and started looking for the question paper under the huge mess of unchecked answer sheets.

As I found it, something outside the door made a sound. It kind of terrified me. I then remember telling myself it must've been a cat, which eased my anxiety. Then suddenly I started hearing a murmuring sound, increasing with the seconds. I listened closely, my heart pounding like I'm going to vomit it out, I very silently and slowly locked the door from the inside. Then I listened closely. There were people!. I wasn't imagining it.

I could almost make out what they were saying, but fear wouldn't let me. A few minutes passed, when there was dead silence outside, I unlocked the door and peeked outside. What I saw absolutely shocked me to my core.

Over 2 am in the morning, I saw in the physics department there were entire classes going on. The people, the lectures, the professors. I have been to physics department countless times yet I couldn't recognize any single one person I saw, conducting classes in the dim light. Now I was more curious than I was scared yet my legs refused to move but I somehow I limped towards one of the group of people standing in the passage talking. All of them, looked at me at once, then took a few step back, and then again, and again. I felt as if the wind was chillier at that end of the passage.

I had no idea what to make of this, at this point I wasn't scared anymore for some reason,so I entered one of the lecture rooms. Nothing was written on the board, all of them just like the group in the passage, with almost like an orchestrated manner gave me this weird look, and without saying a word left the room, one by one, completely ignoring me.  So I grabbed one, gently by the hand. I had never felt colder than I felt at that moment.

It was by this time my curiosity and their avoidance was pissing me off, so the person I had grabbed, or I had thought I had grabbed was a few inches away from me walking away, slightly looking to the side, giving off this eerie look.

Too tired I wasn't thinking, I told myself I don't know the culture of this University, maybe this is just a late night school. Although I wondered why they are acting so weird.

As I went downstairs, I realized I had left the question paper in the office before I heard the murmuring. So I rushed upstairs and to my surprise the entire place was deserted, no sign of anyone, I didn't see anyone coming downstairs from these stairs anyway, I had no clue what was going on. 

No clue what to make of all this, I picked up the question paper and  left the physics department to go home. 

On my way back as I was nearing the maths department, I saw from afar someone standing by the switch board, switching the lights and fans on or off, in one of the lecture rooms.

As I walked closer, toward him, I asked him "what are you doing", he said "you shouldn't have grabbed my hand". In that moment I realized, he was the same guy. I stood there for what felt like 10 years as my face went white, as he quietly went out of the room.
