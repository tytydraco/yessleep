I don’t usually write online, but, I feel like the online community is the only way i can truly tell me experience first hand. I don’t care if nobody sees this. Personally, for my own mental sanity, i need this online, in the world, ready for someone to see. Even if nobody believes me.



My name is Victor. Some call me Vic, and I prefer Vic. Felt like Victor was was a little the archaic for the 20th century. But, who cares. I’m just a guy online writing this to a community that might have a similar experience as i. I live in the bustling streets of Chicago. And i live, ( or did live) in the Becovic Chicago North Side Apartments on street N Clarendon Avenue. I grew up in Chicago but lived more on the south side. My childhood was filled with embarrassing moments, father son time, mother son time, and plenty of frozen Marie calendar meals from the freezer burnt freezer. But, i guess that’s pretty much like a lot of childhoods. If not a lot better then some. I’m just glad my life problems didn’t start until i turned 24 and got my own apartment.



Life was great. Had a great job at a lumber factory, (Calumet Harbor Lumber Co) had plenty of income for my own interests, and the bills. I also purchased the dog of my dreams, A great dane named Titan. Started dating this girl named Ana who was a year younger than me. Like i said, Life was pretty solid. Neighbors were ok, but what can i expect from a dusty Chicago, run down apartment. The man who was above me was around 72 years old and coughed hour after hour because his apartment was full of the soaked, stained cloud of cigar smoke. The neighbor to the right of me was a drunkard, blaring the cheers of the soccer crowd on his TV. And to my left, was a older woman in her 60s, who gave me a poorly knit scarf when i moved in. All of them were nice, some hard to deal with but none of them were hostile, unlike the apartment building a 2 blocks away from my apartment window.



My apartment layout was nice, you walk in, kitchen to the right, roseborro wood cabinets, gas oven that rarely worked, and a platinum white handled fridge. If you walked on, my bedroom and bathroom was to the left, with my small living room /couch room to the right of the apartment. To the right of the couch was a comically large window. I actually really liked the window, but enjoyed the view more. Through my window, i could see roads filled with cars parked and double parked , critters in the trees and can almost see the glimpse of lake Michigan. But that wasn’t the soul of the worries and terrors i had for months.



Across from Windsor avenue, lay another apartment building. The Windsor apartments. It was similar to mine. Similar looking, run down Chicago vibe, just owned by a different management company. I didn’t think much of it. There wasn’t anything to think about. Until now.



It was a cloudy evening in Chicago. I drove home from my workplace. Traffic wasn’t bad. Just a calm, boring, normal evening in north Chicago, Illinois. Eventually, i parked in my shared garage. My 2008 Ford Taurus hummed down as the engine turned off. I walked calmly to my building. It was a bit chilly, as it was February. I took the elevator i always took up to level 5. I walked down my hallway to room 28. I pulled my key out of my pocket, the brass key shimmering in the warm light of the hallway. I walked in, my door creaking. I set my bag down on the counter, taking a Bud light out of the cold fridge. Kicking off my shoes as i plumped on the couch, turning on the TV. As the popular TV show spun on screen i finally took my first sips of the bud light . Lumber work isn’t the most easy work. Muscle pain, splinters, and constant bursting of noise from machines was in the contract. But, it was a good job, so i was grateful for it.



Everything was fine until i heard a faint knocking. I wasn’t expecting anyone, but out of curiosity, i stood up, and walked to my front door in my small apartment. I peeked through the small hole. But to my surprise, i didn’t see anything, or anyone. i thought, maybe it was one of the middle school pranks were you knock and then dash. But, there was no teens in my apartment building. But, hesitantly, i opened the door. I saw no one. But after i opened the door, something made my core shiver like a small child in the cold.



The knocking continued, even with the door open. I tried making excuses like, maybe someone was messing with me. Maybe next door. Maybe im just super tired.



So every 20 seconds, 5 knocks were made. knock knock knock,knock knock. Not a hostile or annoyed knock, not even threatening. Just, a calm knock sequence. After 1 hour i coudnt take it anymore, earbuds helped but, the paranoia of someone outside your door was too much to handle. Even if there wasn’t anyone. So, as i stood up from my couch to open the door, the knocking stopped. i counted, 20, 21,22,23,24. It had been over time. It stopped, almost like it knew i coudnt take it anymore. So, to tired to talk to my landlord, i went to bed, at around 11:29 at night.



I didn’t sleep so well that night. My neck and back warm with sweat. My mind spinning of possible explanations. I thought of looking online but, i wouldn’t know what to look up. Like: why is someone knocking on my door with the door open? Or, How can i tell if my schizophrenic? I didn’t know. I didn’t know what to do. So, the only the I could do, is try to rest my body.



The next day was the same. Went to work, thinking about the knocking, but i didn’t let it effect me on a secular scale. But then, I had a idea. Ill call my friend. I know! Bad idea. In every horror movie, bringing your friend to a haunted house always gets them killed too. But, this was my apartment. I don’t know, I need a second opinion.



His name is hank. Hes around my age, just a year or two younger then me. And we are childhood friends. Still hang out, still play video games online, and still have similar interests. But, im not sure that will matter if he thinks im crazy. So, at my lunch break, I called him. Gripping my phone as I called Hank. Knowing him, he’ll believe anything. Back in high school he believed this “Tiffany” was head over heals for him. Consequently, she didn’t. She already loved this guy a grade ahead of her. Hes stubborn. Not shy. Doesn’t get shocked over crazy things. He even once rode his cheap eBay dirtbike in the high school parking lot at 2 am. I know. Truly a thrilling guy, and hes the perfect person to call when you think reality is glitching. I guess.



He picks up, and i hear his voice. He speaks.



“Vic? Whats up? "



“Hey hank. I’m doing, well, im doing ok. How are you?”



" Still alive! Surprisingly. Just got home from a work trip down south. I’m pretty smoked……. Vic. I know that tone. Your not calling to have some fun.”



“Not exactly. I can explain. I just…. come to my apartment. ill explain there.”



“This better not be a prank Vic. Remember when we pranked that new guy on the high school football team? Hilarious. One of your best.”



“No hank. This isn’t a joke. I need your help. Just come over man. I get home at 7. Will you be there?”



“yea yea. Il be there. But we have to order some pizza. i haven’t ate since yesterday.”



“Yea fine. Order whatever… Ok ill see you then Hank.”



“See you there Vic.”



The call ends. The high tone of the phone beeps when I hung up. I finish my lunch, stressed about the knocking the rest of the day. As well as stressing about hank not believing me.



As the daylight ran out, i left work at around 6:00. I drove slow. My fingers slightly shaking on the leather steering wheel at the thought of another restless night. Snow flakes fell like leaves onto my windshield. The wipers swishing them away.



I got home, parking in my garage and taking out titan for a short walk. I now am happy of the the dog i chose, A dog that could dust a human in a race but also didn’t care if it sat on the couch all day. After walking him, i fed him some leftover grilled chicken from a week back and then hitting the couch.



Around an hour and a half passes as i blankly scroll through endless TV channels, none of them sparking my interest. and Hank walks in at around 9. He walks in, looking a bit hungover from his apparent “business trip”as He says my name.



“Vic? Whats going on? You better have something work that 30 minute drive. "



He grabs a drink out of the fridge, popping the cap off with my cap opener and plopping on the couch beside me.



“So, what’s up?”



“Can we just… chill for an hour? "



“uh.. Yea sure. Just..Tell me whats this all about.”

I exhale deeply. Trying to grapple with the fact that my best friend could think im crazy in the next few sentences



“Yesterday, I came home from work, and there was a knock at my door. "



“And?” hank said without any sense of fear.



" But the knocking wasn’t…. it didn’t… there was nobody at the door. But the knocking wouldn’t stop. I thought it was my neighbors but, it wasn’t. I don’t know whats going on.”



Hank laughs like a child, his belief in my fading like my belief in my own satiny. Hank kept laughing, and then he stopped and said:



" Your crazy man. I thought this wasn’t a prank! You got me there you got me ther-
