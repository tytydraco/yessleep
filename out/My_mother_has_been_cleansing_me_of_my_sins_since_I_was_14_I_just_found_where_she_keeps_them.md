I used to think every Filipino household had a secret they didn’t talk about. You know the vibe. The smell of floor wax, the glow of the altar in the hallway, and the quiet devotion of a mother who just wants her son to be "pure."

My mom, Carmela, is a regular woman. She wears dusters, cooks the best sinangag in the neighborhood, and goes to Mass every Sunday. But she’s always had this thing about "heat."

When I hit 14, she told me the changes in my body weren’t puberty. She called it "stagnant heat." She told me that if I didn’t let her "extract" the impurity, it would rot my soul and turn me into a monster.

So, for ten years, I let her do it.

It became a routine. I’d come home from my office job, and we’d have a "session" in the living room. It was wordless. Mechanical. I’d feel the weight lift, she’d use a fresh white handkerchief, and I’d feel "clean" enough to exist for another day. I thought she was disposing of it. I thought she was my protector.

Last night, we had a blackout.

I was fumbling in the pantry for a spare fuse when I felt a latch behind the sacks of rice. I pulled it, thinking it was a hidden toolbox.

It wasn't.

It was a temperature-controlled cabinet. Inside were hundreds of glass jars. Rows and rows of them, glowing faintly in my flashlight's beam. They were labeled in her neat, motherly cursive.

*“October 2016. First Lesson. Pure.”* *“May 2019. Slightly Tainted.”* *“February 2026. Overflowing.”*

Ten years of my life. Ten years of my "essence," bottled and graded like vintage wine. I felt like I was standing in a morgue for my own ghost.

I heard her slippers before I saw her. That smell of baby powder and oil hit me first. She didn't look shocked. She didn't even look guilty. She just looked at me with that terrifyingly warm smile and said, *"Joven, why are you looking at my vitamins?"*

She told me I was her "garden." She told me that because she carried me for nine months, she has a right to the harvest. She told me that the only reason she looks ten years younger than the other moms on our block is because of my "sacrifice."

I tried to run. I tried to smash the jars. But she just looked at me with so much pity. She told me that without her to drain the "filth," I’d eventually just leak it everywhere. She told me no other woman would ever touch a "spoiled" man like me.

And the worst part? I believed her.

I’m writing this from my bedroom. I can hear her in her room next door. She’s humming a hymn. I can hear the clink of a glass jar opening. I can hear her rubbing my stolen years into her skin.

My skin feels tight. It feels heavy. I can feel the "heat" building up again, and for the first time in my life, I’m not just scared of the impurity. I’m scared of how much I want her to come in and take it away.

She just called my name. She says we need an "extra session" tonight because she found a new wrinkle.

I think I'm going to open the door. I don't think there's anything left of me to save.
