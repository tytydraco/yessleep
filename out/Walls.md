 I am writing this knowing no one will hear me. This story will just be forgotten, maybe even never read  But this is my life, no matter how cursed. My name is Eddie, and this is my story.

It all started on one of the warmest days we have had in a while. Being March, it has been cold for quite some time. Nature was starting to finally show through the thin white blanket of snow. A day I always get happy about. I finally get to wear hoodies, etcetera etcetera. So, I decided to take a walk during the evening. I usually do that anytime I can, loving to walk with my earbuds in. During the evening my mom works, my little brother goes with our dad, so I'm alone most of the day. So, no one to see it...

I got ready for my walk like I usually do, putting on baggy pants with either a drug rug (I know what it's actually called, but c'mon) or a baggy shirt. With my earbuds in and my messenger bag draped over my shoulders, I went out. I took that first deep breath of what I thought would be nice fresh air. But something was... Off. The air smelt stagnant, like I was in an entraped space. It was thick, making my breathing deep. I didn't think much of it, with where I live and all. It could be a dead animal, someone's b.o. (yes, people can smell that bad), whatever. So with something heavy on my chest, shallow breathing, I started my walk. I walked to Linwood Park, somewhere only a few minutes away. It was silent... Too silent. The silence that doesn't calm you, but feels so empty, painful. No birds, no wind. The no wind thing struck me as weird. It was the middle of March, a very windy month in Indiana. But with me being too optimistic, I thought "At least my bangs won't go back". I wish that was the only thing I had to worry about.

I went directly to the big round swing, made for two but still enjoyable for one. I put my bag down, and started to swing with my eyes closed, head to the swing. I stayed there for a couple of hours as the sky turned dark. Watching the stars, I heard something shuffle in the bushes to my left. I sprung up as quick as I could, and pulled out my pepper spray. I have to admit, I'm not strong. I'm only 109 lbs at 5'3", so I need anything I can grab. I heard it again, but in the opposite direction. But this time... A figure. A women. An unnaturaly tall women, about 7'5". Her face seemed so long, but I couldn't tell since I'm legally blind. I ran, running faster than I ever have before. I could hear fast paced walking coming towards me. Not running... Walking. I started to cry, not knowing if I would be allowed to hold my little brother again. All I wanted to do was to get home to wait for him to arrive back from dads. My heart beating, I sniffled. That smell.

It was stronger.

Getting closer.

It started smelling more like rotting flesh anytime I slowed down, even the slightest.

As soon as I saw my porch, I reached my arms out, never looking back. I hit the door with my body, not knowing how fast I was truly running (faster than I'm used to, I basically ran into the door out of fear), rushing to grab into my bag for my keys. My eyesight even more blurred with tears, I finally got ahold of my keys. I scraped the doorknob in a cheesy horror movie way. Slamming the door behind me, I locked both the knob and dead bolt.

I was shaken. Holding on to my own body, I tried doing chores to get my mind off of the unholy thing that just made itself known to me.

"Why me? Why me...? Did they know me?" I thought, not being able to shake it. 

"Maybe I was hallucinating, I do have them. Never ever this bad, but maybe"

Each thought I had, my brain said no. Like it was warning me, saving me.

My brother and dad came home with big cheerful smiles, my brother immediately going to his VR just like normal. I talked to my dad but he said it was probably just my paranoia, me being small and alone in the dark might have made my brain see something.

But he didn't know, as he was talking...

That smell.

So light, but lingering. My dad has a deviated septum... So he couldn't smell it. I wasn't about to bring my little brother into it either... What if she started messing with him instead?

So I stayed silent.

So silent, I almost started to forget it as dad and I talked. 

That night, I tucked my brother in like normal, and hung out with my mom (she gets off late, so I stay up late to be with her), and went to bed. Everything was going normally, like the day I just had was a fever dream.

But about 4:35 in the morning, I woke up to that stench. It gagged me.

She was here.

In my room.

But I couldn't see. Only smell. 

Feel.

Hear.

Her breathing sounded spaced apart, like she was trying to catch it. She got so close to me. So close I could feel her ice cold hand on my face. Her breath was empty, nothing but sound came from it. 

Then.

She started to scream and squeeze my head in her hands.

I couldn't scream. Couldn't move. 

Just tears.

She then stopped, cocked her head, and slid into my walls.

There was no crack. No hole.

She just...did. The walls waved around her.

Just then my mom went to get me up for the day.

I ate like normal, spoke like normal.

But I knew.

I know she's in my walls.

Breathing.

Watching.

The scariest thing... I still don't know why, nor when she will attack me again. I'm not a murderer, a thief. And knowing she's still there.

I know because I can still smell it.
