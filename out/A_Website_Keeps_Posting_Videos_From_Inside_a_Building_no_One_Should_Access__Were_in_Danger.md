I‘m writing and moderating this post in behalf of our institution. For the duration of this inquiry, refer to me as „Felix“.

Before I present you all this website that presents a urgent problem to our institution, I have to give you some quick informations about myself and our division, that are necessary for you to fully understand the complicated situation we‘re in due to this threat:

My department is the „*HCSch*“ (Home Cyber Protection), I’m working as a Cyber Intelligence Analyst for the german military and my main task is to collect information about threats in the digital world and eliminate them.

My specific area of work is the World Wide Web. We’re a huge department of around 50 Analysts. Since eight years, our institution uses a complex System called „*Lilly*“ *(the leading developer gave it the name of his ex-wife).*

Lilly automatically scans websites, detects potential threats, and categorizes them for us, so that we can divide the workload and act more effiicient:

**1: Political extremism**

**2: Religious extremism**

**3: Organized Fraud**

**4: Illegal Content** (esp. in form of explicit content)

**5: Document/data forgery**

**6: Illegal Arms Trade**

Each of this categories uses a different standardized procedure to deal with the threat.

*For example:* In Case of „**4: Illegal Content**“ we often just have to contact the hosting provider, so that we can file a criminal complaint and then just let them delete the content.

In prospect, cases of „**1: Political Extremism“** or „**2: Religious Extremism“** often are more complicated and often take months to be solved. Only experienced Analysts work on such cases. If we get knowledge of a new extremist group, we have to collect as much information as we can without alerting the group.

We had a really complex case two months ago, where a cult kept constantly recruiting new members and seemed to predict most of our actions. They seemed pretty dangerous because they heavily persecuted people who dropped out of their sect and threatened everyone they seemed as a non-believer with violence and the „wrath“ of their god.

The investigation dragged on, because they deleted their internet presence multiple times, before we were able to collect enough information.

Lastly, with a bit of luck, we were able to infiltrate the cult. That led to a heavy battle between our armed forces and their cult members and ended in the arrest of the leaders.

We still couldn‘t figure out how they we‘re able to anticipate us for so long.

The last year we had a huge turnover in our department. Due to the increasing importance to monitor social media apps and the declining usage of smaller websites, large parts of our workforce were transferred to another department. With over 15 years of experience I‘m one of the few „Veterans“ left. Right now I‘m the person in our team who handles the most complicated cases, those who need to be handled with much caution.

You also really need to know about the 7th category we have. I haven‘t mentioned it before, because most of the websites the system places in there are insignificant for us or simply the result of an error. It happens every few weeks that our system mistakenly places a website in there, it’s more like a trash bin for everything the system flags wrongly and helps us keep the important categories free of such mistakes.

**7: Uncategorizable Threats**

Four weeks ago our system flagged a new website called: „**Mevidesne**“.

It happened on a late saturday evening so our department was already emptied out.

I was one of the few people left this evening, my two daughter‘s Madleen and Lily are already old enough to take care of themselves, so I’m often working until late in the evening to collect paid overtime.

The mentioned Menu button led me to a empty subpage with the exception of one video.

Curios, I move my cursor over the start button.
