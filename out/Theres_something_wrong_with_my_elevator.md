I don’t even know how I should start this. I guess it started with her and the elevator. With Claire stepping out of it one Tuesday and never coming back. The official report called it a missing persons case. The police said she probably just skipped town and that I was overreacting because we’d been fighting. But I know exactly what I saw. Or I guess what I *didn’t* see. 

The elevator in our building has always been shit. One of those old, creaky ones that smell like mildew and sounds like it’s about to die on every floor. But then it started doing something else. Something that made absolutely no sense. Sometimes when the doors opened, It wasn’t just our lobby. Sometimes it was just plain wrong. Not physically wrong at least. Everything would look the same, but the vibe was off. Like everything was photoshopped. The air would smell like ozone and old paper, and everything seemed too bright and too quiet.

Then people started disappearing.

The first one was Mr. Hendricks from 4B. He was a weird guy, quiet, retired, never talked. He went into the elevator one morning just to go down to the lobby and when the doors opened, he was gone. Not a scream or a crash or any sign of him. The police shrugged it off. Said he probably just took the stairs. But I just didn’t believe that was it.

Then, it was Linda from 3A. She was heading out to meet her sister for lunch when she just up and vanished. Her phone was found on the elevator floor, screen cracked, last open app still on her sisters contact. The timestamp? 11:47am. Her sister confirmed she never showed up.

I know I probably should’ve said something about it the first time. But honestly, who would believe me? A building full of people, and no one else had seemed to notice the way the elevator lights would flicker that sickly yellow color sometimes. Or how the buttons would light up in an order that made no sense. Or how, if you listened hard enough, you could hear something breathing behind the walls when the doors were closed.

But then it was Claire. 

She stepped into the elevator after our fight. We’d been screaming at each other about something that seems so arbitrary now. Ten seconds later the doors opened. But it wasn’t our floor. It was her office. Only not her office. The exact same layout, the same furniture, but the photos on her desk were different. The client list on the whiteboard had names I couldn’t recognize. And when i called out, no one moved.

Then i saw the mirror. In the reflection I could see both doors. The one behind me in the hallway, and the one in front of me. And in the gap between them, something moved. It wasn’t a person or a shadow; it was something else. It watched me for a second and then the doors closed. Claire never came back. And I think the elevator’s waiting for me.

I don’t sleep anymore. Not since I started hearing the voices inside the walls of the elevator. Or since the last time i was in the elevator and the buttons lit up in a weird sequence I didn’t recognize. They were symbols not numbers. I pressed the one that looked like a door.

The elevator lurched. Not up. Not down. Sideways, maybe? The lights flickered and for a second, I thought I saw standing at the edge of the hallway. She was smiling. And then she wasn’t there anymore.

The doors opened and I was somewhere I didn’t recognize. The hallway looked like ours, but the wallpaper is peeling in in patterns that make my eyes hurt. The air smells like burnt sugar and something metallic. There’s a door at the end, slightly ajar. Behind it, I can hear music. Not from a radio or anything, but inside the walls. 

I should turn back and call for help. But the elevator’s gone. The buttons on the wall are gone. There’s only the door.
