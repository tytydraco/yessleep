I’m trying to stay calm, but I can’t deny the evidence any longer. I’ve never posted before, but I’m writing this in the hope that, somewhere, someone will at least know what is happening. And in case I’m the next to go missing…

The first things that I noticed:

On the first day of my French Lit class, my professor walked into the room and immediately made the students close the blinds. He claimed that looking out the window was a distraction. He joked that students “wished they could get away,” but he made us promise that whoever arrived first would make sure the blinds were closed. He doesn’t even like fluorescent lights, and so each class has been held mostly in darkness. 

He routinely drinks from a gray water bottle that is stained red at the bottom. 

He has stated on multiple occasions that he is unable to watch television because he “hasn’t been able to get used to it,” as if he grew up in a time before television existed.

He’s pale.

Although the class is supposed to be a survey of French literature, he chooses predominantly monster stories, many of which are about vampires. 

These things can all be chalked up to coincidence, however, and it wasn’t until about halfway through the semester, when I made the unfortunate mistake of going to his office hours, that I really started to think that something was wrong.

It was a cold day in late winter, and snow had covered the campus in a thick white frost. I made my way to the basement of the French building, where my professor was supposed to be holding office hours. The hallway was dark and seemingly deserted, and something just felt *off*. 

As I approached the room at the end of the hall, I became aware of a strange noise. It is hard to describe, but it was this mix between a growl and a slurping noise. It sounded like it was coming from some kind of animal. Like a starved predator feasting on a hard-earned kill.

I moved slowly, as quietly as I could, towards the slightly cracked door of my professor’s office.

He was hunched over something on the floor, his back to me. Through the crack in the door, I saw that there was a large, thick blood stain pooling around his feet. He was…feeding.

Something squealed, and I bolted out of there as fast as I could.  

The next day, I was running late, and when I got to French class, my professor was already mid-rant, speaking energetically about how monsters are misrepresented in film and literature. He spent the entire class discussing a strange and disturbing vampire film that wasn’t even on the syllabus. The film was about a man who got a job delivering babies so he could eat them. 

And the worst part was, he was *defending* the man:

“There’s nothing inherently evil about hunting prey,” he said. “The real fear is simply that it could be anyone. Anywhere. At any time.” 

I kept my eyes trained on my notebook, but I could feel his gaze on my face. 

“Blending into society,” he continued. “Just like Freud discusses in his essay, The Uncanny. Something familiar, comfortable, a person who appears ordinary, but there’s something slightly off about them…”  

It was like he was challenging me, taunting. Like he knew what I’d seen the day before.

I looked up for the first time since class had started and spotted the small specks of red, staining the collar of his blue shirt.

Since that day, multiple members of the French department have vanished without a trace. The first time, it was clear that the college didn’t know where he, Professor Gilbert went. According to the school newspaper, they were “looking into it.” But a week later, they simply said that he “went on sabbatical.” 

I don’t know why the college would cover this up. Maybe they just don’t know how to explain why they keep losing professors under mysterious circumstances. 

Rumors started to spread around campus that the college wasn’t paying professors well, but then students started to go missing, too. 

My French class in particular has gotten smaller, but no one seems to even notice. I tried to bring it up to the guy who sits next to me, but he just said it was “probably because the final’s gonna be sooo ass.” 

I’m scared that the police won’t believe me, and I’m scared my friends will think I’m crazy. I really don’t know what to do.
