He first called to me when I was 9. It was in June. I was worn out from a long day at my baseball tournament. Our team had won, and I remember it was all I could think about as I showered, brushed my teeth, and got into bed. It was like I was still on the field with my teammates. The cheering from the crowd, the hugs from my parents, the pizza we got afterwards. The memories were still fresh in my mind, and I remember smiling to myself as I thought more about it.   
That summer night, I would lay in my bed, tossing and turning for what seemed like hours. The distant lullaby of cicadas floated into my room. The excitement of the day fighting my need to sleep. I would close my eyes and see the baseball field once again. My heart was racing at the thoughts and i quickly rose out of bed. I looked out of my window to the forest. I remember every year it seemed the trees were creeping slowly into the backyard, a silent plot to soon envelop my house. The moon was lighting the trees In a blueish coat.   
I was sitting up in my bed now, listening to the distant call of the cicadas. Their clicks and chirps filled the night, The full moon tricking them into thinking the sun was still out. I looked at my nightstand where my alarm clock was. 11:05 PM. Way past bedtime, my parents would freak out if they caught me still  awake. The sounds of the night had settled down by this point. There was an eerie silence, a far team of crickets now being the only sound to reach my room. I lied down and shut my eyes tight. I thought that if I faked sleep long enough, my brain would be tricked into falling asleep finally. The thoughts of my baseball game had faded, but were quickly replaced the by sound of the cicadas.   
The sounds had returned once again suddenly. Their screams were so loud, and incessant, it was all my mind could fixate on.  
 They seemed much louder than before. I couldn’t tell if it was because it was now the only thing I was thinking about, or they had actually gotten louder. The noises slowly faded, and I was granted silence once again. About 3 minutes later they started up again. Louder than before. I wasn’t overthinking it, they really were louder this time. Through the now third occurrence, a strange realization came over me. I got out of bed and went up to my window. I placed my ears against it and listened. They weren’t just louder, somehow they had gotten closer. Somehow, I could tell that the clicks and hisses from the invisible source was right on the edge of the trees now. Why were they getting closer? What was bringing them to me? 

 I think that’s when I heard the voice. With my ear pressed against the window, I could hear more than just the cicadas. The details of the night were much more pronounced now. I could hear crickets, katydids, and especially the cicadas clicking and humming rhythmically. A choir of noise would slowly rise in volume, then slowly go quiet, one by one. It reminded me of ocean waves. Waves of screaming and humming from the insects would rise and fall in volume. In the silence in between the droning buzz however, I would hear a strange mumbling. I couldnt believe it at first. It was masked under the sound of the insects. It was like i was hearing a conversation on the other side of a wall. The low vibrations of a man’s voice rung in my head. I couldn’t make out what he was saying. Was I just imagining it? No, it had seemed so real. I thought it was all in my head at first. I was so fixated on the rhythmic wave of the insects songs that I couldn’t pull away from the window. The more i listened, the more I could make out the inflections in the voice. Somehow, I could hear passion, a warmth, and deep care in the words, even if I couldn’t understand them.   
Was i dreaming? It didn’t feel like it at all. The voice was so real yet so imaginary. In another wave of silence, I realized I wasn’t even hearing it physically. I stepped back from the window and realized I could still hear the voice. However, a few seconds after I moved from the window, the voice faded into a quiet hum, and blended back into the night. The silence that followed was even more eerie than the voice. I went back to the window, and placed my ear on the glass. The cicadas call rose slowly once again, and the voice hid within it. Even from the impossible distance the cicadas were humming from, I felt the intention within every word. I could feel the words swirling in my mind. They pooled together and I could almost see them form into strange shapes.   
It was almost like, when you close your eyes and focus really hard on all of the strange floaty things swimming in your eyelids. Colors and shapes formed from the strange words. I couldn’t understand them. What was happening to me? The cicadas and crickets chirping crept up. Silently at first, but slowly increasing in volume. The fall of the wave never came. The noises washed over the edge of the forest and crashed over my house. The sounds rose into a deafening crescendo. I backed away from the window and fell to the floor. Silence would not come. The sudden wall of noise hit me all at once. It wasn’t just coming from outside now. It was above me, in the walls, in my head. The voice spoke louder and louder as the noises surrounded me.   
Tears began to form in the corners of my eyes. I covered my ears but the ringing and chirping of thousands and thousands of cicadas flooded my head. A constant endless deafening ringing crowded my mind. The voice spoke faster now. Colors, so many colors flooding my eyes. The shapes crashing in on themselves, swirling and mixing with the deafening sounds. I was crying at this point. Curled up in the fetal  position on the floor. I think that’s when I finally heard it. At the peak of the cicadas screaming, everything stopped. All at once, the room went silent. The only noise now was the ringing In my ears. In the silence, I could hear his voice. A deep, raspy old man’s voice spoke to me in my mind. Cold, stern, and commanding. A simple four words: “Come to me, child.” A father’s demand. 

I screamed, and screamed, and screamed and screamed.
