\[Part One\]

This whole ordeal started in 2001. Every summer my family went to the town's county fair. We have gone for as long as I can remember. It was July I was 10, I looked forward to going every year, the rides, the food, everything. It was a pretty small town so my parents just gave us 10 dollars and set us loose, since I was so young I always had to stay with my older sister. This was a chance for mom and dad to have some free time away from the four of us. My other two siblings would always wonder off doing their own thing, but I had strict orders from my father to not leave Sam’s side. Annoyed she had to babysit me, she dragged me to all the different kiddie rides I wanted to go on. She is a good sister.

The day flew by, we had managed to go on every single kiddie ride I could manage. Taking a break getting some food, we sat down and the plastic table being held down with a cinder block covered with a cheap plaid table cloth, blown in the summer wind. As we were eating, I felt that someone was watching me; looking around I didn't see anyone. I went back to my foot long corndog and funnel cake. The food was gone, it was back to rides, but with no kiddie rides, we just wandered around; that was until we came across the trick mirror maze. That was something I could do, so we hopped in line, handing the pimply-faced ticket collector the last of our  tickets. 

Sam  was sent in first, she told me she would wait for me at the exit. After a short wait it was my turn; slowly feeling my way through the maze, hitting my head against the reflective mirrors once or twice. I finally found my way to the exit, looking around; I couldn't find Sam, my heart started to beat a little faster. Not because I couldn't find her, but because I broke the one rule my father had given me. He was never an angry man, just very strict, he has served in the military as a staff sergeant training new recruits for many, many years. There would be hell to pay, I would probably lose toy privileges.

Frantically looking for her, scanning the limited area in between the countless legs of people passing through the area. I couldn't find her. Resigning my fate to no toys for a week, I would walk around the fair, to see if I could see any of my siblings. What felt like hours of walking; I accidentally bumped into someone,

“Sorry mister” I said in a shaky voice.

My eyes looked down at his big red shoes, my eyes slowly looked up at his tall body, he was wearing a white and blue one piece, with large red balls going down the middle, a small pink and yellow flower pinned to his chest. His face was that of a stereotypical clown, painted white with blue diamonds painted over his eyes, and a big red painted on smile, he had a big red nose on too. His hair was pointed, like horns, colored like the rainbow.  He didn't say a word to me, just bent down to eye level with me and gave me a big smile, honking his big red nose. He handed me a balloon, it was blue, that's my favorite color. Eagerly grabbing the balloon, he reached down a hand, inviting me to grab it. Being stupid and scared, I grabbed his hand. We lived in a very safe town where I never was taught about stranger danger. We walked around for a bit, almost just wandering the grounds. Through all the chatter of the crowd and the noise from the rides I could hear the clown breathing heavily. After a bit of walking I finally saw mom and dad and there was Sam trying to explain how she had lost me. She was getting an earful from  dad. That made me chuckle a bit.

I hesitated for a second, but I let go of the clown's hand and ran up to my family. Seeing me run up, their expressions relaxed, going from angry to relieved, 

“Where were you!” my mom screamed out grabbing me tight. My father was giving me the stare down waiting for my answer. 

“I waited for Sam”

“ I couldn't find her, but the nice clown helped me find you.” I pointed over to where the clown had been standing. He was gone.  

I didn't think anything of it, I was glad to see everyone again and relieved because dad didn't take away my toys. My sister, on the other hand, lost phone privileges for a month. We left the fair after collecting the other two who had handfuls of new plushies. My sister didn't talk to me for a week after the incident. When she did, it was clear that she was still angry at me.

“I was standing right next to the exit! Thanks to you dad took away my phone!” 

We got into a big arguing match about it, I called her a poopy head ( I was really proud of my comeback at the time). Eventually, life went on. My sister got her phone back and peace was returned to the house. Mom and dad were still clearly upset about the incident. I didn't know the reason until recently; the fair never hired clowns.

 After that summer we never went back to the fair. A few months later the new school year was about to start, Carlos was my best friend at the time. I hadn’t seen him since the last school year. His family would leave town for the summer. I was excited to see him again, when we reunited it was like no time had passed, he told me all the cool things that he did during the summer. We traded stories of our summer ventures, Carlos wanted to hang out and play at his house after school. I agreed, I just had to call my house when we got there. When I arrived at Carlos’s house, I called home, the phone range, and voice mail. Strange someone was always home, mom stayed home most of the time taking care of us kids. I called one more time, They picked up on the first ring. 

“Hello? Mom?”

 Before even waiting for a response, I dove right in telling her I was at Carlos’s house and would be back before dinner. I got no response, just a click of the phone, ending the call. I didn't flinch, usually mom was ok with me going over to Carlos’s house. We had grown up together so our families knew each other very well. We played with his action figures, taking turns being the bad guy. Soon enough, the sun was starting to set. I said goodbye to my friend, and ran home; Carlos lived one neighborhood over, in the same neighborhood that the school was in. The only thing that was dividing our neighborhoods was a gas station; you had to go past it to get to my neighborhood. As I was running past the gas station I heard a familiar sound. It was a honk. Turning my head toward the sound, I didn't see anyone, but in the same direction I saw a cluster of blue balloons tied to the telephone line. Shrugging it off I continued home. Getting to my house, I was greeted by my father; home from work, sitting on the porch seemingly waiting for my arrival.

“Your mother was worried sick about you.” He said in a stern voice. 

“I called sir, I promise.” I said in a shaky voice.

“DONT LIE…” his voice momentarily rising before going back to his normal voice.

“ You just lost toy privileges for a week.”

I was looking forward to playing more when I got home, I was broken. Tears welling up in my eyes, and anger starting to rise. 

Raising my own voice.

“I DID CALL, SOMEONE ANSWERED!" I said with all the fury of a 10 year old could muster.

I was in big trouble now, father took my toys away for another week. I stormed off to my room; closing the door behind me. When the door shut, hidden behind was a blue balloon with a painted smile. Rage still burning in me; My thoughts were clouded, in that moment I thought this was all revenge from my sister. She had to have answered the call and didn't tell mom to get me in trouble. 

I stormed out of my room and banged on Sam’s closed door. Tears and snot rolling down my face. She swung the door open, a swoosh of wind pulled me in. My sister was 7 years older than me, so I pounded on her legs trying to do any damage to her as I could. As you can imagine, it didn't do much.

“WHY DIDNT YOU TELL MOM THAT I CALLED.” I cried out.

She pushed me off of her, the commotion caused my mom to storm upstairs, trying to figure out what was going on. I was confined in my room for the rest of the night. 

Just before it was my bed time my mom came in. She sat down on the side of my bed, I was facing away from her, still angry. She rubbed my shoulder and said.

“I called the Lopez house…” she said in a soft tone.

“You told the truth, Carlos’s mom told me you called.” 

She reaches over towards my face, holding my favorite action figure. The feeling of anger dissipated, even though it was bed time mom let me have ice cream before I went to bed. That was one of my fondest memories of mom. I felt like I was vindicated. The next morning, everyone gathered for breakfast, we had all my favorites, pancakes, bacon, moms famous scrambled eggs with cheese. My father apologized to me, the look of shame on his face was evident. Mom did explain to me that no one was home at the time of the call.  I was confused,

“someone picked up the phone, I heard breathing through the phone.”  

The room went silent. Shocked looks darted across mom and dads faces.

“Sam answered the phone, but didn't tell you. She is trying to get me in trouble. She also put a balloon in my room.”

She jumps up scooting the wooden chair across the linoleum floor making a loud screeching noise.

“THAT’S A LIE!” she yells out.

 dad interjects, placating the angry teen.

“You said there was a balloon in your room?” he asked

I explained to him that it was hidden behind the door and had a big red smile drawn in paint on it. 

“Danny…” 

“Sam was at school still, when you called.”

I didn't really understand, my young brain struggling to understand the true weight of the situation that was unfolding. Someone was in the house, they answered the phone and left the balloon.

Things were tense the rest of the morning, I was told that I was not allowed to go to Carlos’s house for a bit, and I was to come home right after school. I was still being punished for attacking Sam. Dad stayed home from work for a couple of weeks after that. For all of his faults, all the times that he got angry at me, I still loved him. He was undoubtedly this family's protector. He stayed awake every night, sitting in the living room with his shotgun at his side.

For the next few months nothing strange happened, I went to school, Carlos’s house every day. It was the daily routine. One day, during a school assembly we had a bunch of different people perform at our school, we had a couple of BMX riders do some tricks on a halfpipe, had a guy come in with a bunch of different reptiles and spiders, but the one that stood out the most for me, was the clown. It was the same clown that was at the fair, he was wearing the same costume, but for some reason it looked dirty. Like he had rubbed dirt and dust all over. His performance was a flop, all the other kids in the class including me and Carlos, were not entertained by what he was doing. It seemed like he could tell we were not entertained by him. So in a last ditch effort to appease us he goes around the corner and grabs a large potato sack; he pulls out a big bundle of balloons. 

They were all different colors, red, white, yellow, green, and blue. He handed out balloons to everyone. When he got to me, he gave me an impossibly large smile and handed me a blue balloon. Everyone was happy about getting balloons, but I couldn't help but feel something was wrong. The clown promptly left, giving us a cartoon wave and running around the corner. I asked if Carlos wanted to switch balloons with me. He had a green one, he reluctantly agreed and we swapped. The classroom was buzzing with energy, everyone talking about the different things we got to see. I couldn't help but notice that the teachers were huddled off into the corner of the room, whispering amongst themselves. Later that night, well after my bedtime, in the distance I could hear the sound of police sirens. 

The next morning, I came down to the kitchen expecting breakfast was ready. The only thing I saw was mom and dad sitting at the table. They told me to sit down. Dad, not one to mince his words, came out and said it.

“Carlos, has gone missing…” Mom scolded and punched his arm.

“What do you mean missing?”

“ His mom went to check up on him, in the middle of the night, but he was gone.” 

The realization hit like a truck, it must have been the clown. I told mom and dad about our assembly the other day, it seemed to have hit them just as hard as it did to me. I told them that he had given me a blue balloon. I could feel the guilt, welling up in me, tears started to form. I didn't tell them about the balloon swapping. I didn't bring home the balloon nevertheless. That the clown must have been looking for the blue balloon and instead of finding me he found Carlos. That was my thinking back then, looking back now with an adult perspective there was no way that was the reason why. The school shut down for the next week. The whole town volunteered to look for Carlos, they spent weeks looking for him, looking in every bush, every body of water, anywhere they could think of. Weeks turned into months, the search had died down, the only people really searching anymore were the Lopez family, and a handful of volunteers. 

I missed my best friend, there used to be a time where we wouldn't go a single day without seeing each other. After that day I didn't see the clown again. Though guilt lingered for many years after his disappearance, always at the back of my mind eating away at my psyche. I was now in high school, I never once stopped thinking about Carlos, although the town had mostly forgotten and moved on, I never did. Sure I lived a somewhat normal life after that, my father had died, when I was 16 leaving just my mom and my four siblings. It was tough losing him, mom didn't stop crying for a week after it happened. He had a military funeral, all of his buddies showed up paying their respects. Right after the wake, pulling into our driveway, I saw something sitting on the porch. I walked up my porch and saw a bouquet of flowers sitting in front of the door. Next to it, hidden from view, was a cluster of balloons. They were blue.
