I don’t think I would have noticed anything was wrong if it hadn’t started messing with my sleep. At first, it felt normal—just one of those phases where you wake up randomly at night for no reason. I’d check my phone, maybe drink some water, and go back to sleep. Nothing scary. Nothing dramatic. Just… off.

But then it started happening every night.And slowly, the nights stopped feeling the same.

Some nights I’d wake up feeling like someone had called my name. Not loudly—just enough to pull me out of sleep. Other nights, it was the feeling that someone was in the room with me. You know that instinct? The one that makes you look around even when you don’t want to? That I kept telling myself it was nothing. Stress. Overthinking. Maybe I was scrolling too much before bed.

Then one night, I woke up and actually saw him.He was standing near the door.At first, my brain tried to make sense of it—maybe it was just a jacket hanging, or a shadow from outside. But the longer I looked, the more it felt wrong. He wasn’t shaped like anything familiar. Too still. Too upright. Like a person trying very hard not to move.I didn’t scream. I didn’t even sit up.I just stared.And after a few seconds—seconds that felt way longer—my eyes adjusted, and suddenly… there was nothing there.

The door. The wall. Empty.

That should have been enough to convince me it wasn’t real.But the next night, I woke up again.And he was there again.This time, he wasn’t near the door.He was closer.Not close enough to touch me. Just… closer than before.That’s when I stopped brushing it off.I checked everything the next day—locks, windows, even asked around casually if anyone had been near my place. Nothing. No explanation.But nights kept coming.And every night was slightly different.Sometimes he’d be near the corner of the room. Sometimes near the foot of the bed. Once, I woke up and he was just… standing sideways, like he had turned mid-step and stopped when I woke up.That one really got to me.Because it felt like he had been moving.Like I interrupted something.I tried staying awake a few nights after that. Thought maybe if I didn’t fall asleep, whatever this was wouldn’t happen.

But nothing ever happened while I was awake.The room stayed normal.

Quiet.Safe.And then, the moment I slipped into sleep

He was back again.Closer.Always closer.I started noticing small things too.The door sometimes wouldn’t be exactly how I left it. Not open—just… not fully shut. Like someone had pushed it slightly and stopped.Once, I woke up and my blanket was pulled a little lower than where I remember leaving it.

Not enough to panic.Just enough to notice.

Last night felt different, though.I woke up, and for a second, everything felt normal.

Then I realized,He wasn’t standing anymore.He was sitting on the edge of my bed.Facing away from me.

And that’s what made it worse.

Because it didn’t feel like he was there to scare me.

It felt like he was waiting.

I could see his shoulders, the shape of his back. More solid than before. Less like a shadow, more like an actual person.

I didn’t move.Didn’t breathe.And then slowly, very slowly.He turned his head.Not all at once. Just enough that I knew he was trying to look at me.

And I swear I could feel it.

That moment when someone’s eyes meet yours, even in the dark.There was something almost… curious about it.Not angry.Not aggressive.Just aware.Like he finally knew I could see him too.

I shut my eyes immediately.

I don’t even remember deciding to.

I just did.

When I opened them again, the room was empty.

Everything back to normal.

But I haven’t been able to relax since.

Because tonight, something’s already different.

It’s not even that late yet.

And I’m not asleep.

But I keep hearing small sounds around the room.

Nothing loud.

Just enough to make me pause.

Like someone shifting their weight.

Or taking a slow step.

And I think that’s what scares me the most now.It’s not waiting for me to fall asleep anymore.
