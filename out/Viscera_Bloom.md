I am a little short on time. I must get this story to you so that you have a chance of surviving the magnum hubris of our species.

I was never really a religious or superstitious person, but I always lived with the belief that the world existed as it does now not because we haven’t innovated or explored enough, but because something prevented the human race from reaching new horizons. I never thought in my lifetime I would meet the barrier that prevented mankind from eternal life. 

I remember that it began like every other semester of college, with the main difference being it was the beginning of my senior year. A chapter of my life populated with late night partying, stressing out over projects that became irrelevant within a couple weeks, and creating amazing memories with people whom I considered lifelong friends. 

“Leo!” Victoria yelled across the student union foyer. She came running up to me while her bag and various accessories jingled as she trudged toward me. When she got close enough, she lunged at me and gave me a hug comparable in force to a hydraulic press. It briefly knocked the wind out of my lungs. “This is going to be the best school year ever!” she said, barely able to contain her excitement. 

“It is nice to see that you survived your internship. Did you eat many organs while you were there?” I jokingly said and pretended to walk like a shambling zombie straight from the *Night of the Living Dead*. She jabbed me in the arm for that comment. 

 Victoria was a very eccentric and earth-friendly person. She declared her major in biology her freshman year and made it most of her personality ever since. Over the summer she interned at a small pharmaceutical company that specialized in medicine for transplant patients. Which ended up being right up her alley, as her research project for the university was something that, if successful, would change the way organ transplants were handled forever. 

I can be considered a reliable source on all information regarding the project since Victoria has made it her top priority to tell me every possible detail, whether I want the information or not. 

Her project, which also involved our other close friend Braden, was an innovative way to create and easily grow functional organs that would immediately be accepted by recipients’ bodies. They were doing tests combining the so-called “beefsteak” fungus with human DNA and human stem cells to create a fast growing replacement organ. I must admit, while I understand very little of the scientific part of the project, I personally think it is pretty cool. The university has already featured them on dozens of social media posts and newsletters for it. 

“I think my phone must have taken me on a different route, this doesn’t look like my normal insane asylum.” Braden said with a chuckle as he walked up to meet me and Victoria. Victoria greeted him with the same friendly attack as she had given me. 

Braden was like a human rendition of a scratch-off lottery ticket. On the outside, he dressed and carried himself around as a total frat guy. Parties, drinking, and crazy after-hour plans were what he wanted people to see. But if you scratched the concealing facade away, he was an incredibly smart individual with strange obsessions. He could be talking about how many beers he consumed in less than an hour but if someone mentioned one of the several niche topics he regularly delved into he would switch his tone and attitude like a sleeper agent. 

I met these two during our freshmen orientation week. Orientation week was a way to treat college freshmen not like adults like they expected us to be, but as kids who were beginning a very long and stressful summer camp. Attendance was not required, but they “strongly encouraged showing up for all the fun possibilities.” We happened to be the only 3 people who couldn’t find our assigned group, and thus spent our time that week avoiding the eye-rolling orientation sessions and the crappy catering. 

Since then, we met every evening at the student union to eat dinner and talk about whatever news piqued our interest. Our favorite recurring topic was the conspiracy involving our university. 

We all made the sickening decision, whether for financial convenience or belief in astonishing false advertising of the university marketing teams, to attend Arcadia University of Science and Technology (AST for short). You likely have not heard of it, since it exists somewhat deep in the Cherokee National Forest. Our mascot is Moony the Owl, which can be seen at every sporting event and university gathering accompanied by his two security guards. Unlike most colleges in the United States, AST has a secondary unofficial mascot called The Red Menace who’s entire purpose is to tackle Moony. 

The Red Menace is a common discussion topic in our group, as it originates from legends told about the region around AST. Rumors stated that sometime in the 90s a large, amorphous humanoid creature would be spotted in the national forest, and would terrorize the city of Four Rivers outside of AST’s campus. What interested us was when the rumors said the sightings ended. There was a historic fire in the old experimental biology building that had been set by a professor who went insane. Some say the Red Menace sightings drastically decreased after that fire. I know a couple of people who went and explored the abandoned old biology lab, but they all said it was uneventful and boring. 

As the semester progressed, we continued on with our regular topics. There were a couple of theories we would discuss in light of the urban legends. Such as the professor being the Red Menace and eating people, or the professor lighting the fire to run off with the Red Menace to Mexico and live happily ever after. It was a nice break from my engineering courses, and their biology labwork. 

That was until a couple days before Halloween, when Braden didn’t show up to our nightly ritual. This wasn’t an uncommon occurrence, we were busy people. Usually, if we were busy though, we would communicate about our absence.  

“Usually he doesn’t stay at your lab past 7. Are you sure he was there? He usually beats us here” I spoke with a curious but mostly unserious tone.

“I could’ve sworn he said he was leaving right after me.” Victoria responded, now pulling up her phone and clicking through several apps. “His location says he is still at the lab.” She showed the screen to me. 

“Maybe he fell asleep mid-test.” I chuckled at the thought of Braden sleeping with all of the lab attire. 

“I left my favorite coffee mug there this morning, let’s go check on him.” Victoria said after ignoring my joke. She seemed relaxed, but it was easy to tell there was a slight bit of uneasiness to her speech. 

We made our way across campus on the cool fall night to the biology research experimental learning building. Victoria badged us in, and we made our way up the stairs to the third floor where their lab resided. 

Victoria went first out of the stairwell and I followed right behind her. She stopped very quickly after walking into the hallway. So abruptly that I ran right into her. 

“What gives Vic? You can’t just stop in the walkway.” I protested as I got my bearings. She stayed perfectly still looking down the hallway. I slowly followed her sightline to the end of the hall. Their lab door, a heavy door with a big white ‘8’ painted on the glass, stood perfectly aligned with the middle of the hallway. The anomaly that petrified my friend drastically contrasted the typical academic architecture. 

A thick dark liquid at the base of the door reflected the harsh fluorescent light bulbs above. We both stood silently processing the scene, and the hallway remained quiet, with only the hum of the fluorescent bulbs carving the silence like a perfectly sharpened blade. 

“I…I’m scared.” Victoria barely managed to softly whisper. I could see her starting to tremble where she stood. I have been in the lab several times to visit, and I know none of the liquids they had on hand could appear like that. Victoria’s breathing was starting to hitch. “That looks like blood Leo, that looks like blood.” 

“You said Braden’s location was in the lab?” I asked nervously. Victoria’s anxiety was rubbing off on me a little bit. She nodded hesitantly in response. 

I moved close to the wall and Victoria clung to my back at an arm’s length away. Each step brought us closer and closer to the Lab 8 door. We were only about 25 feet from the door. I was trying not to think of the hundreds of possibilities that could have transpired in that lab. I stopped for a second and looked back at Victoria. Tears had started to well in her eyes as we approached the door. I was glad to know we were both in the same state.   
Just as I turned around, increasingly loud footsteps came from inside Lab 8. I put my arm back to brace Victoria for whatever came through the door. It sounded like someone was sprinting on the other side. 

BAM.

The door flew open with enough speed for the handle to hit the wall. Braden ran and slipped in the puddle and fell on his knees from out of Lab 8. Me and Victoria jumped back a few steps at this sight. Braden looked like he had fallen off a cliff and climbed back up. Chunks of his hair were missing, and it looked as though he was having spasms all over his body. 

He struggled to stand, and he reached out a hand toward us. 

“Le…Vi…” he struggled to articulate as various gurgling and unnatural popping sounds came from within him. Then suddenly he stared wide-eyed at us as though we had just told him he had won a million dollars. 

What happened to Braden in front of us would permanently be etched in memory as a burning reminder for what my biggest fear now was. He stared at us wide-eyed as we watched in horror. The skin on his face looked like it was flexing, as if he was using every muscle on his face at once. His eyes looked like they were being pulled by some unknown force back into their sockets. Muscles under his face didn’t flex, they shifted. As his skin stayed stationary, the muscles and bones inside of his head rearranged themselves like a Rubik's cube inside of a wet balloon at a gradual pace so slow it felt eternal. His jaw cocked violently to the side as ear-shattering crunching could be heard echoing down the hallway. Vocalizations emanated from his mouth cavity, mostly clicks, sharp inhales, and fragmented words spilled towards us.  

“Vic…We need to leave.” I whispered and started taking steps backward. I pushed her backwards with my arm. Neither of us could take our eyes off of the scene unfolding before us. 

Just then, a large spike erupted from the right pec of Braden's body, spewing blood in various stages of coagulation across the wall next to him. A second large spike exploded out of his left ribcage. 

We continued to back up towards the stairwell, but Braden’s metamorphosis was seemingly coming to an end. The muscles and bones stopped shifting and settled into the abomination that stood at the end of the hall. It thrashed what was left of its head toward our direction, twitching violently. It continued making loud clicking from its mouth cavity. Until it gave us the cue we needed to run as fast as we could away. 

“ALIVE.” It shrieked down the hall. In that instance I grabbed Victoria and shoved her through the stairwell door. I slammed the door shut and we began flying down the stairs as fast as we could force our legs to move. As we hit the last flight moving towards the ground floor, I heard a loud crash come from the top of the stairs. I heard it struggle to move it’s new form toward the stairs with loud gurgling and incessant clicking. Me and Victoria had to get out of there as soon as possible. 

I am sorry to cut this off, but I will have to leave you all for now. I will continue with more details of my account when I can find some refuge.
