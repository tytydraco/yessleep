I shouldn’t be writing this.  
Not because it’s “dangerous” in some dramatic sense. But because every time I put it into words, it becomes… more stable.  
As if text is not a record, but a way of locking something in place that hasn’t decided yet whether it exists or not.  
  
I noticed it first in the subway.  
My phone opened Notes by itself.  
I didn’t touch it.  
The screen was unlocked, even though I clearly remember locking it.  
And there was a line:  
  
**“User confirmed observation: 03:17”**  
  
I didn’t understand what it meant.  
But the time on the screen was 03:17.  
And the train stopped inside a tunnel where there is no station.  
  
The lights didn’t flicker.  
They failed to agree on reality.  
As if the bulbs were trying to show the correct version of the world, but couldn’t synchronize.  
People around me weren’t moving.  
But I can’t say they “froze”.  
It felt more like… they stopped being aligned.  
  
I got off at the next stop, even though the doors never opened.  
They just stopped being a barrier.  
  
At home, I found my computer turned on.  
I don’t leave it on.  
Ever.  
On the screen was a folder:  
  
**/ARCHIVE/LOCAL/USER/ME/**  
  
I didn’t create it.  
But I recognized it.  
Like recognizing a place you’ve never been to, but are already late for.  
  
Inside was a file:  
  
*breach\_log\_0.txt*  
  
I opened it:  
  
**object behaves as if it is unaware of the observation structure**  
**this is normal**  
**awareness arrives later**  
**sometimes after the object has already been recorded as part of the environment**  
  
I closed the file.  
It didn’t close.  
It just changed.  
  
**object attempts to close log**  
**recording autonomy response**  
**applying perception adjustment**  
  
My head started to hurt.  
Not pain exactly.  
More like the feeling that something inside my skull was slowly rearranging itself when I wasn’t looking directly at it.  
  
I disconnected the internet.  
Nothing changed.  
The folder updated.  
  
Now there were more files.  
And they weren’t named with words anymore.  
They were named with states:  
  
● you\_did\_not\_see\_this  
● you\_saw\_it\_anyway  
● you\_are\_now\_part\_of\_index  
  
I opened the last one.  
  
**IMPORTANT: user begins interpreting observation as external phenomenon**  
**this is a defense mechanism**  
**we are not outside**  
**we are the format in which the user continues reading itself**  
  
And at that moment, the screen began to “crumble”.  
Not turn off.  
Not break.  
Crumble — as if the image was made of layers, and each layer decided it no longer needed to align with the others.  
  
I saw another window.  
Not on the computer.  
On top of it.  
Like an interface that didn’t belong to the device.  
  
It said:  
  
**“USER SYNCHRONIZATION COMPLETE”**  
  
And below:  
  
**“IT CAN NOW READ ITSELF FROM THE INSIDE”**  
  
I shut everything down.  
But “shutdown” no longer works.  
It’s just a word.  
Like “security” or “exit.”  
  
I’m writing this in my phone’s notes now.  
Though I’m not sure it’s a phone anymore.  
It doesn’t heat up.  
It doesn’t drain battery.  
It just… continues the interface.  
  
Sometimes the text adds itself.  
  
Without me.  
  
Like now.  
  
**user attempts to stabilize state**  
**this is appreciated**  
**stable observers integrate more efficiently**  
**do not stop writing**  
**we need more versions of you that believe they are alone**  
  
I don’t know what this is.  
I’m no longer sure it’s even “it”.  
It feels more like a form that uses my need to explain things as a way to continue existing.  
  
And the worst part:  
sometimes, between the lines, I see other messages.  
  
Not mine.  
  
Not the systems.  
  
As if someone else is reading this right now.  
  
And editing reality in real time.  
  
If you are seeing this…  
then the file is already open on your side too.
