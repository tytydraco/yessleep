I can't explain it, every time I explain it to people I'm just told I'm insane or schizophrenic. But every specialist for medical or physical health bullshit never changed what I know . The poster on my wall is breathing at night. Every time it does my life continues to spiral. And no before you ask, I know it's not the wind, it was a different wall each time, and it only happened when I'm in the room. It sounds bullshit, I won't deny that. I just…need to vent. It's stupid and posting anything online like this is gonna have me made fun of or ridiculed but I don't care at this point. This isn't for you; it's for me.

Let me give some info about me, mostly because this…THING has been following me for way longer than I had thought. My name is Andy Summit, I was born in Denver in 2003 and had to move a lot growing up. It was something I had grown accustomed to, always having my most prized possessions in a box in case we needed to pack up. It made meeting people hard, but luckily it never got in my way as a sociable kid. 

Almost every six months from 2005 to 2012 we had to move somewhere. I was told by my family it was because my dad was military, which sounded correct to me even though I never saw him for more than a day at a time. I didn't mind though, we never lived in any place tiny or were any worse off. And when my dad did visit he made sure to make it a special occasion. Now we were rich, far from it but from what I remember it wasn't the worst.

I only say this because one night in 2011, when my dad was visiting and driving us to chuck-e-cheeze or something, I saw it. It was curled up on a box of his possessions he kept in the back seat next to me. Its coated-paper was cheap looking with lightly singed edges. Naturally my nine year old self was curious, and my dad was preoccupied talking to mom about something. So I scooted over in the sweaty leather seat of the car to reach into the box and unravel the poster. 

The poster was an anatomy diagram of a human, half of the body torn up and with different arrows pointing towards all of the twelve major human organ systems. However, the poster looked used, with coffee stains and dried chunks of something crusty covering the outer layer and obscuring a lot of it. When I went to dust off the layer, it showed the face of the man in the diagram, the muscular system was sagging and looked to have staples in it that were keeping it up. His face was distorted, with a gnarly forced smile stretching over parts of the skeletal system underneath. It wasn’t clean either, parts of the skin were dripping blood that moved in front of the texts describing the different systems, even after attempting to scrub off some they persisted. Many of the bones had long unbroken cracks running up them, with patchworks of what looked like glue keeping them together. 

“Ewwwwww” I remember shouting out, finally catching the attention of my parents. My dads eyes went wide, immediately reaching into the backseat to remove the poster from my hands. The car swerved from the movement, him driving the car while he did it.   
“DONT- \*ehem\*, dont touch daddys things Andy. there from work and…FOR A FRIEND! Right, just don't touch them anymore” 

He sounded frantic, heart racing with visible sweat dripping down his brow. I was a kid so at the moment I thought it was my fault, I put my hands in my lap and just tried to stay silent in the car as my mom chewed him out for swerving the car. 

When my dad died a year later it devastated me and mom, I remember we were told by my grandma on his side of the family. Apparently he had crashed his car into a cliff while on a midnight drive. No one knew how it happened, it was on the back-road on the flat planes of Nevada. My mom was inconsolable for months, not even leaving her room for days at a time. For me, maybe it was because I saw him less and was still just a kid but it didn't hurt for as long. 

It was over 12 years after when I saw the poster again, I had been living up at college at Boise State at this point. My mom remarried only about three months before I left, the guy's name was Chad and I didn't get to know him well besides that he smoked. My first semester had ended and I was coming home for winter break to live with the family. After slowly hauling my boxes up a few flights of stairs at the apartment my mom was at, I set my boxes down in my old room I hadn't been in in months. And there, on the wall leaning against the adjacent apartment I saw it. The Anatomy diagram, still burnt on the edges and stained to hell. However, it wasn't gross looking anymore. The man on it had an emotionless face and there was no dripping blood. I originally thought it was my kid brain seeing anything anatomy and believing it to be gross, but still this poster was meant to be long gone by now. 

“Hey mom, wasn't this dads old poster? I thought we had put away all of his things years ago.”

“Oh yeah, when his mom passed a box of his old stuff was found in her house. You can hang it up if you want, it's just laying there”  
my mom yelled from the other room, she spoke like it wasn't already up on the wall. But she was right, my room had become a bit of a store room with a corner of boxes piling up in my absence. So I just clocked it up to my moms older age, probably forgetting she had hung it up. 

My body ached as I finished moving back in that day, to the point the poster thing wasn't even a thought in my mind. I hugged my mom and fell face down on my mattress. The soft full-sized bed was heavenly to me. I slept like a baby until later that night when I first heard it. 

The breathing sounded hoarse, almost like it was coming from an older and frail man who was malnourished. It grew and fell in volume with each exhale and inhale, quiet enough at first where I didn't even wake up to it. Only once I heard a wet CRUNCH I jolted awake from bed from fear.

My room was dark, my desk lamp wasn’t plugged in so I had to use my phone as a flashlight. My light moved around the room, the breathing was quiet so I didn't immediately recognize where it was from, for about a minute I was trying to find the source before another muffled CRACK made me find the source. 

The poster was flowing up and down, mind you it was pinned in each corner so it would be hard for it to move. It expanded and lowered from the middle of the diagram, the sides looked almost glued onto the wall. But surely enough, the next sharp-rough breathing I heard matched the rising and falling of the posters' crusted paper. I watched it for a while, it moved almost hypnotically in place locking my view as the slow pained breaths gained more and more air with each pull of tired lungs. The cracking sounds were indeed coming from it, sounding like twigs being snapped violently. However it did not match the breathing, it was muffled and followed by a wet sloshing of some liquid seemingly sliding down the wall. 

Eventually I felt a gust of wind brush against my shoulder, strong enough to wake me up and force me to look at it. I looked to see the window open, weak wood slamming as the intensity of the winds moved the frame around it. I then quickly looked back to the poster, the sounds of breathing weren't heard and only the slamming of the creaky wooden window made me rethink what I saw. The poster had stopped moving the same, the sides were being blown up as well and the direction of the movements were clearly from the strong gust. 

Even still, I know when I see some shit like that to not fully ignore it. It was likely my mind was making monsters as it was like 3am but I still moved to look at it. I removed the pins and just examined the poster, besides it looking nasty and burnt nothing was off about it. 

The wall however wasn't. The wall had what looked like extremely dried blood that spread outwards from the center. It looked like it had been there for years, it was faded and musty. Touching it with my hands, it was wet. Not extremely but enough where there was residue on my fingers afterwards. 

I was too tired and confused to worry about it, maybe if I had shredded the damn thing then I would be free of this. But I just rolled up the poster, put it on the floor somewhere and closed the window. It was something I just, didnt have any more energy to fuck with. 

The next night I awoke to an ambulance blaring outside my window, which wasn't unusual given its an apartment. That was until I heard the banging out neighbors door 

My neighbor, Steve or something, had died of a heart attack the previous night. We were lucky, he hadn't had a proper will or next of kin so we were offered some of his belongings that weren't as important for the trouble. Apparently it was due to something relating to a food disorder he had according to the paramedics who gave us the news.

My brain was trying to rationalize it, the poster thing was a nightmare and I was overreacting to it right? But surely enough a week later, I heard it again. 

This time the breathing was deeper and more raspy, it was breaking up as it breathed till eventually a loud cough would be forced out. I turned my lamp on, the poster was yet again hung up on my wall. However, it wasn't the same wall, it was the interior wall of our apartment, directly behind the bathroom. And the poster had a bit of the diagram filled out, with musculature around the arms, legs and torso being held there by duct tape and staples. Yet again it stared, its breathing matching the new pace and coughs. I went to grab it, and felt a sticky substance clinging it to the wall. 

I was growing mad, I yanked and pulled but the poster wouldn't budge. I had thought to myself, maybe it's a dream and went to the window to maybe feel a breeze. But this time the window was closed shut. 

CRACK another of those wet snaps rang out causing me to just snap. 

“SHUT UP, You're not real…right?!”

 I ran to it, the raspy breathing and rise and fall of it still present. I then decided enough was enough, I ran to the other side of the wall to see if it was doing something to it. In the bathroom I saw another dried bloody stain, the muffled breathing still audible as it leaked into the drywall. But I still couldn't see anything to stop it. That was until I saw the scissors on the counter in the bathroom. 

I grabbed them and rushed back as the coughs shook my room notably from the outside. I rushed in and plunged the scissors into the wall, as I did the cough grew bloody and rough. A notable pool of blood dripped out the wall for a minute as the poster recoiled. Then once it was over, the poster fell and simply fell to the floor. I was panicking still, constantly checking my line of sight till sunrise that day. The only thing to keep me from slumbering off was my moms screams. 

Chad had died in the middle of the night, his lungs were full of smoke and he suffocated after he slept wrong. I didn't believe it, I knew it was the poster and tried to explain it to my mom. It didn't go well, she was still overwhelmed with her new husband dying so soon after they met. It led to a fight that resulted in me getting kicked out, she eventually called to forgive me but I was losing it. I tried telling officers what happened, but they all just thought I was freaking out and ordered me to a therapist that didn't help for shit. 

Whatever it is, I know it's not done with me yet. It wasn't there, when I got back it was gone from my rooms floor some-fucking-how. I'm just not sleeping at my moms place till i see it again, I don't want to risk her in its cross hairs.

if it comes back ill be ready, I'm currently in a hotel about to sleep. Ill update you if a theory of mine works....
