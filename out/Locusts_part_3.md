I killed a man today. It all happened so fast, he was standing right there, right next to the hatch. I told him to hurry but he just froze when he heard them. I had to close it or we’d all be dead now. The guy wasn’t even wearing one of those beekeeping suits we use for expeditions, I guess his bunker just didn’t have any. I didn’t even get the guys name. To everyone else he was just some sort of sign, a sign that we weren’t the only vestige of civilization left. They don’t even care that he’s gone now, that he went the same way as Libby except I made the choice for him.

He told us he was from what he thought was the next town over, that there was another bunker there and they sent him to find more people. I tried to get him to come inside right away but he was like a scared cat, flinching away whenever I reached out. I had almost coaxed him in by the time I heard the buzzing.

”shit. Get inside, now. I can hear them”

He just stopped In his tracks and stared over his shoulder at the approaching storm.

”Are you deaf? I said get inside!”

He stayed still, tears falling down his cheeks.

”Oh god. I knew they would get me. I knew we should’ve waited for Michael to heal, he would know how to handle this”

I could tell he wouldn’t be moving anytime soon. I grabbed his arm and tried to pull him in but he was heavy.

”Sam? What’s going on?”

I heard old farmer Owens approaching from behind me.

”quick there’s a swarm, and he won’t move. Help me pull him in”

Owens came up next to me and grabbed his other arm. We pulled as hard as we could, but John Owens was coming up on 70 and I hadn’t done manual labor a day in my life.

”Sam”

”Don’t talk just pull god damn it! I won’t watch this happen again!”

”Sam they’re to close. We need to look out for our own”

I could hear that horrible sound coming closer, the volume rising as they closed in on their prey. I didn’t say anything, I just kept pulling in vain. They were close enough to see by the time Owens spoke again.

”Close the hatch, Sam”

He dropped the mans arm. I just stared at him with a mixture of shock and disgust. How could he say such a thing? How could he hold so little value in this strangers life?

”Just help me pull John”

The buzzing got closer.

”Sam”

”I said help me pull!”

Tears were streaming down my face as I glared back at him, but he simply looked at me with that kindness that only an elder can, and I knew I didn’t have a choice. I closed the hatch.

”No! Please let me in, I’ll come in this time! Please!”

I couldn’t tune out his screams no matter how hard I tried, and I couldn’t bare to walk away either. It wasn’t like when I saw the locusts take Libby, she went peacefully. He didn’t. I just stood there and listened as that horrible noise slowly drowned out the strangers screaming. He was begging me for mercy, but I couldn’t let him in and both of us knew it. When that awful buzzing subsided so did his screams, and for the first time since Libby died all I heard was silence.

”Sam, let’s have a drink. It’s time we finish off that whiskey anyway“

”I don’t drink John”

”Well you do know“

I gave in and followed him back to the quarters. He told me about his life before the locusts, but I won’t share that publicly. It was almost enough to make me forget about him, about that stranger I left for dead. Hell, maybe it would’ve been enough if the noise hadn’t come back. It always does. Its almost mechanical, just a constant drone with no end in sight.

It’s getting late, so I’ll rap this up. All I have left to say is one thing: don’t leave the bunker. Whatever you do.
