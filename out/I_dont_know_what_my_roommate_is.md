I was given a week to move out. I wasn’t surprised. My parents always hated me, I knew that they were eager to get me out of the house, and I was fine with that. I hated them as much as they hated me. I got paid minimum wage, so I didn’t know where to go, and I didn’t have any friends close enough to let me stay at their place.

There was just one day left for me to find a place. Everything was either too expensive or, well, honestly just that. I wasn’t picky. As long as there was a bed to sleep in and some air conditioning, I wouldn’t complain.

I did most of my searching on craigslist, and then, one night, I saw it.

“Looking for one roommate”

It wasn’t the title that caught my eye. It was the price.

**$0.**

I thought it might have been a listing error. I clicked on it. However, they seemed to be very aware of what they were offering.

**“Spacious one bedroom apartment. All rent and utilities provided for. Must answer questionnaire after replying to email”**

That wasn’t the only thing that made me raise an eyebrow. The photos looked… off. They were nice, but too nice. They looked like stock photos, in sterile white, studio lighting.. Completely different than the amateur pictures that I saw in plenty of other listings.

But I was desperate. I wanted a place to stay. 

So, I sent the email.

“Hey, is this still available? I’d like to answer the questionare.”

I got a reply instantly. Before I even put down my phone. I’m pretty sure that the response I got was automated. The email contained six questions.

1. What is your blood type?
2. Do you have any chronic health issues?
3. When do you usually sleep?
4. Do you own any guns or possess any combat training? (i.e. martial arts)
5. At 2-5 PM every single Friday, you will be required to vacate the premises. This is non-negotiable. You are recommended to leave atleast ten minutes early, and cannot arrive even a second earlier than 5 PM. Do you agree to this requirement?

Any sane person would have closed their gmail and searched up their nearest homeless shelters, but I didn’t. I don’t know why. I didn’t want to be homeless. But frankly, maybe there was a small part of me that was curious.

1. O negative.
2. I have some minor eczema.
3. Around 11 PM.
4. I own no guns and possess no martial arts training.
5. I agree.

The reply wasn’t instantaneous, even though I was expecting it to be. I stared at the phone screen for another ten minutes, and then went to bed. The next morning, I opened up my gmail and saw the response:

“You have been approved. Move in the premises within the current day.”

I didn’t need any encouragement. I already had my bags packed, or, more accurately, bag, since I barely had any possessions. When I arrived, I knocked on the door, expecting my roommate to appear. I wondered what they looked like. They hadn't mentioned their gender.

I kept waiting, and waiting, waiting to hear the sound of the door opening and the face of my new roommate.

Nothing.

I knocked again.

Nothing.

Ten minutes later, I was frustrated. I heard some shuffling noises inside. Were they just ignoring me? I had no idea.

Then, I got a text.

Which was odd, since I never gave the person behind the listing my phone number.

“Under the carpet.”

I looked underneath, and found a key. But I was a bit annoyed. Why wasn’t this guy answering the door? He really made me use a spare key underneath the carpet to get inside? More importantly, how did he even have my number? Was he even a he? I knew nothing about who this person was.

“Sorry, but why couldn’t you just open the door?”

I waited. I got a reply a few seconds later.

“Because I’m not there.”

What?

Okay, if he’s not there… then where is he? How did he even know I was at the front door? Is he watching me or something? 

I looked around. Nothing. Just the warm breeze and the chirping of birds.

Wait, more importantly, I heard some noises inside, that can’t mean…

“Are there rats inside of here?!”

I hated rats. I am almost sure that I have a phobia. There was a rat infestation in my parents house that they never cared to fix. I thought I recognized those shuffling noises earlier. Did this asshole really list an apartment without making it clear that there were rodents?

And of course, he didn’t reply.

But I guess I didn’t really care. I wasn’t in a hurry to meet this guy anyway. So, I opened the door and went inside.

The inside of the apartment looked brand new. I mean, the photos were completely different, but I was surprised at how clean everything looked. It was like the first time walking in a hotel room.

I looked around. Silence. Maybe he was at work or something? I dunno. All I knew is that I was starving. So I walked up to the fridge and opened it.

Nothing, again. 

I was starting to get a bit creeped out again. At first, I thought that he might have cleaned up the place for me, or he was just obsessed with keeping everything spotless. But now it seemed like nobody was even living in this place.

I texted him again.

“Are you coming back soon? Am I even speaking with my roommate?”

No response. I walked over and took a seat on the couch. Again, it seemed… off.

It felt new. It even smelled new. How could I tell the difference between a new and used couch? I dunno, but this thing was exactly like a factory new couch, the type you sat on at the mall. Then, I got a text.

“No, I’m not your roommate.”

I felt chills run down my spine. I don't know why. I began to look around. There was nothing different. I’m glad that I showed up early in the evening and not late at night.

Then, another text. The notification startled me.

“Your roommate is shy. But she is in her room.”

I felt a bit relieved for some reason. My brain was trying to rationalize everything. Okay, maybe this was her father? She might just have anxiety or something. I felt the need to apologize, partially because I might have referred to his daughter as a rat.

“Sorry, who is this?”

Just a few seconds passed. I heard some more scurrying, but didn’t pay any mind to it.

“Her caretaker.”

I let out a sigh of relief that I didn’t know I had been holding. Okay, I guess this was making a bit more sense. There were still some things that didn’t add up, like why the listing was free, or what the weird questionnaire was about, but I was willing to put that behind me.

I got another text. This one was longer, a refreshing change to the blunt, simple messages they had been sending me, yet it seemed familiar.

“I would like to set a few rules before you make yourself at home.

1. Do not ever enter the room at the end of the hallway. That is her room. She is extremely afraid of people, so you will give her a panic attack if she even looks at you.
2. You are recommended to sleep before 9 PM, and you are forbidden from leaving your room after 9 PM. She will often get up and roam the apartment at night. If she sees you, she will again have a panic attack.
3. No matter what, do not acknowledge her existence or disturb it at all. Do not try to speak to her through the door. Do not listen to loud music that may startle her. Live peacefully and quietly, and there will be no problems.”

Okay… I could deal with that. I wasn’t the type of guy to host parties every other week or anything, and I made sure to always use earbuds. However, I wondered if her anxiety was that bad, why wasn’t she in a specialized place? Or living with her caretaker? But the only part that really irked me was sleeping before 9 PM. But I didn’t even care. I mean, it was worth the free apartment. By now, I just wanted to relax.

A month had passed since then. I followed the rules well. I’d like to think of myself a considerate guy, and I didn’t want to give that poor girl any problems. I had maintained the same routine every single day. I woke up, went to work, got back to the apartment and kept to myself until I did it all over again.

I always wondered what was behind that door. I mean, of course I did. Sometimes I would find myself in front of it, just staring. Even doing that felt like I was trespassing. It looked like a normal, clean door. Sometimes, I was almost tempted to lay down flat and peak underneath, but I never did.

What concerned me most was the smell.

It was foul. Absolutely foul. Like a mixture of metal, feces, and garbage. I mean, I was imagining mountains of trash behind that door. I thought that she must have been a total slob, but also felt bad for her.

The Friday's didn't affect me at all. I mean, I worked a 9-5 anyway, so how could it? When I got back to the apartment, literally nothing was different, and I had no idea what the rule was even for.

But the worst part wasn’t the mornings.

It wasn’t the evenings.

It was at night.

I usually went to sleep at about 8. I became used to waking up early in the morning, at about 4 AM. Surprisingly, I enjoyed the early mornings. It was a habit for me now. 

However, one night I stayed up.

It was about a week after I had moved in. I was in my room, on my PC, and my phone alarm rang. 

8 PM. I was supposed to go to bed.

However, I decided to stay up a bit longer. I mean, I still had an hour to spare, and I wanted to finish a movie I was watching.

One movie turned to the whole trilogy, and before I knew it, I turned my PC off and saw that it was 12:01 AM. 

I was in the complete dark. I'm not usually scared of the dark, but for some reason I felt really on edge. The only thing I could hear was the wind blowing outside.

However, without my earbuds plugged in and the gentle whirring of the PC fans, I heard something else.

I heard steps from outside my door. It made my heart drop. It didn’t sound like a person. I heard her taking a few short steps every few seconds. It sounded like something was scratching against the floorboards. It sounded like she was walking on all fours.

Then, I heard something else.

It was unlike anything I ever heard before. I don’t know how to describe it. The closest thing I could say is that it resembled a growl, or a wail. It was a sound of misery, it was low, quiet, and deeply guttural and hurt, like a wounded animal. No human could ever make a noise like that.

I also heard heavy, labored breathing. She sounded like she was in pain. The noises were not feminine at all, they sounded monstrous, and also quiet. By now, my heart was racing. I had no idea what to do. I tried to make as little noise as possible and got in bed.

The worst part was that I knew she heard me.

I took the smallest step I could. She stopped. The breathing stopped. Everything stopped. I got in bed as fast and quietly as I could, and shut my eyes.

The next morning I woke up, and nothing seemed out of the ordinary.

Was it a bad dream? I hoped so.

I walked out of my room cautiously. I didn’t notice anything. Was it really just a bad dream?

Then, I got a text.

“I told you to sleep before 9 PM.”

How did he know that I stayed up? There had to be cameras somewhere, but I didn’t know what to say. I felt trapped.

“I’m sorry.”

I wanted to ask about what I had heard, but I didn’t need to.

“She has a breathing condition. She is also partially disabled. She finds it easier to crawl on all fours.”

A breathing condition? Really? No breathing condition made you sound like that. But I wanted to believe it. I had to, if I wanted to keep pretending that everything was normal.

From then on, I grew more paranoid. I felt like that I was being watched in my own apartment, and I probably was. However, again, the rationalization in my brain was still there.

He probably have cameras for her. He is probably are watching in case she gets in an accident, or maybe he is worried I might do something.

I mean, my mother kept cameras in my grandmother’s room in her retirement home. That seemed normal. Was I just being too paranoid? What if she got in a horrible accident that made her that way? I shouldn’t try to judge.

I woke up more often in the dead of night, covered in sweat. Again, I heard the breathing. I heard the wails, and I heard the crawling. I just forced myself to go back to bed.

Then, one day, I came back home after I got off work early. Nothing seemed off.

But as I walked inside the apartment, I was mortified. I realized that it was friday.

The door at the end of the hallway was open, and I saw everything that was inside.
