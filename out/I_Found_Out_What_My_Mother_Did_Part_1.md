The early years of my childhood were rough.

I never had much luck making friends, and for a long time, I didn’t understand why. I tried everything I could think of. I changed the way I dressed, how I did my makeup, even the music I listened to, I tried anything that might help me fit in.

But nothing worked.

At school, kids either ignored me or barely spoke to me at all. Outside of school, it wasn’t any different. I felt invisible or like I was someone people actively avoided.

There were nights I cried myself to sleep, not understanding what I was doing wrong. The only person I ever really talked to about it was my dad but even then, I never got answers.

It wasn’t until I got older that things finally started to make sense and when they did… I almost wished they hadn’t. My mom died when I was just a few months old, that’s all my dad ever told me growing up.

Whenever I tried to ask about her, he’d give short, careful answers. Just enough to satisfy the question but never enough to really explain anything and every time her name came up, his mood would shift.

Like he was reliving something he didn’t want to remember.

Something painful.

Still, I knew one thing for sure.

People in Shadow Falls knew who she was. 

Teachers, neighbors even random strangers at grocery stores would stop me just to say the same thing:

“You look just like your mother.”

At first, I didn’t think much of it. They would smile when they said it sometimes even laugh a little, like they were remembering something fond.

From what I could gather, my mom had been popular. A cheerleader. The kind of person everyone liked. The kind of person who didn’t have to try to fit in because she already did.

The complete opposite of me.

But something happened when she went to college, that’s where everything changed and I didn’t learn how it changed until I went looking for answers myself.

I remember sitting on my bed late one night, laptop glowing in the dark, typing her name into a search bar, I don’t know what I expected to find.

Old photos or something that would finally make me feel closer to her.

Instead… I found an article dated back in 2010 and the moment I read the first few lines.

My stomach dropped. 

**“Chloe Gaskin, 19, has been charged with first-degree murder but was subsequently declared legally insane and remanded to a psychiatric facility pending further evaluation.”**

I just stared at the screen, reading it over and over again, like maybe I had misunderstood.

Like maybe it wasn’t real.

But it was.

That was my mom.

Then suddenly everything made sense. 

Why people looked at me the way they did.

Why no one wanted to get too close.

Why my dad never talked about her.

I kept digging even though part of me didn’t want to. I found out her victim was her best friend Makayla. The attack happened in Midtown during the middle of the day in front of witnesses, the reports described it as unrelenting.

Makayla suffered multiple stab wounds with no attempt to stop and then I read the part that made my hands start to shake.

One witness claimed my mother was laughing while she did it.

Repeating the same phrase over and over again:

**“The Grinning Man loves his girls… The Grinning Man loves his girls…”**

I didn’t realize I had stopped breathing until my chest started to ache, so I slowly closed my laptop then I just sat there in silence staring at the ceiling.

My thoughts wouldn’t stop.

They kept coming, one after another, each one worse than the last.

*How could I come from someone like that?*

*Why didn’t my dad tell me the truth?*

*Was she always like that… or did something happen to her?*

But there was one question…

One that wouldn’t leave me alone.

*Who…or what…was the Grinning Man?*
