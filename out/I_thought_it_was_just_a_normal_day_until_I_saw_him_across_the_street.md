The day started too perfectly, too evenly, as if someone had carefully smoothed everything, removing all rough edges. The sun shone unnaturally calm, the asphalt was dry, the air warm, and even the usually dark, dusty stairwell seemed almost cozy. I walked inside, and the door closed behind me by itself—no creak, no slam, just a soft click of the lock. For a moment I froze, not from fear, but from a strange feeling that something didn’t match, as if reality had shifted slightly. But I was tired. It was evening, the time when thoughts stick to your head, and your eyes search for peace.

The apartment greeted me with its usual silence. I took off my shoes, walked into the room, dropped my bag, and almost automatically pulled out my phone and opened the dating app. The screen took a little longer to load than usual. Without sound, without breath, with a cry tearing everything inside me that was no longer mine. I cried, unable to stop. People below shouted, ran, someone called an ambulance.
