I want to start by saying I'm not really someone who believes in this stuff. I grew up in a house where anything weird got chalked up to coincidence and forgotten about by dinner. So I've been sitting on this for two weeks trying to find a normal explanation before posting it, and I genuinely can't.



It was a Tuesday. Nothing about it stood out. I had class in the morning, skipped the gym, and drove to a gas station around 7pm because I'd been putting off filling my tank for like three days. Pulled in, turned the car off, reached for my wallet.



My phone rang. My own name on the screen. My own number calling me.



I figured it was a spoofing thing. Telemarketers do that sometimes, make calls show up as someone in your contacts so you pick up. I hit ignore and went inside while the pump ran.



When I got back to my car I had a voicemail.



I stood there next to the pump in the cold and listened to it. Then I listened to it again because I didn't really process it the first time.



It was my voice. Not kind of like me. Actually me, including this specific thing I do when I'm tired where I trail off at the end of sentences. The message was 52 seconds long and it described the rest of my evening.



Not vague stuff either. It said I was going to go home and heat up the leftover pasta in the back of my fridge, the one I'd forgotten about for four days. It said I was going to get a call from a number I didn't recognize around 9pm and that I shouldn't answer it.



I stood there long enough that the pump clicked off and the guy at the next pump started looking at me.



I drove home. I still don't know why I didn't call someone or go somewhere else. I just drove home like an idiot.



The pasta was in the fridge. I had completely forgotten it was there. I heated it up and ate it standing over the sink because I do that sometimes when I'm freaked out, I don't know why.



At 9:04 my phone rang. Number I didn't recognize, local area code, nothing in my contacts. I watched it ring. They didn't leave a voicemail.



I haven't slept right since. Not because I'm scared exactly, more because I keep going in circles trying to explain it and I can't get there. The spoofing thing doesn't work because whoever did it would have needed to know my exact plans for the night, plans I hadn't even made yet when I was sitting in that parking lot. The "I called myself somehow and forgot" thing doesn't work for the same reason.



The voicemail is still on my phone. I've listened to it probably forty times at this point. It still sounds like me.



Nothing dramatic happened after. The number never called back. I just have 52 seconds of my own voice knowing things it shouldn't have known yet and no way to make sense of it.



I'll update if anything else happens.
