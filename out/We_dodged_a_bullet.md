It was the year 2000. My best friend and I were 11, and finally deemed old enough to trick or treat alone. We followed throngs of people around the neighborhood, our pillowcases of candy becoming heavier with each house. 

Some older ladies or couples would ask us to step inside for a photo, to capture a memory for their golden years. It was all innocent, and we'd stepped into most of these homes with our parents in prior years anyway.

Around 8pm, as the crowds started to thin, we decided on one more house. Then we'd go home, dump our haul on the carpet, and swap what we didnt want. I always took her Smarties, she always took my Now and Laters.

We approach the last house. Its in the back of the neighborhood; we'd never stopped here before. Still, my friend urges me to walk up to the door. She's always been the braver one. 

I follow closely behind, not a fan of the two massive boxwoods partially blocking the front steps. The house is an A Frame, and there is one light on upstairs in the single window. The brick and siding is dark, and the boxwoods, now behind us, make me feel trapped.

My friend rings the bell, unfazed. A man in his 30s or 40s opens the front door, and unnaturally large smile on his face. He urges us inside to where his candy bowl is. And even though this was typical, I felt as if we were crossing into a danger zone. 

The man steps back and looks at our costumes. She was a devil, and I was a hippie. She cocks her hip and swings her tail around, unaware that this is no longer an innocent gesture with the way we are being gawked at. 

I get my 6th sense of danger from two things... my mother letting me fall asleep to Forensic Files. And the fact that my aunt had been murdered the year before. My friend does not get this sense. Not yet.

The man gestures to the staircase. "Candy is upstairs, come on." 

Now my friend freezes. We dont budge.

He chuckles as if hes realizing that hes being suspicious, then assures us. "My wife is upstairs in the shower, and she'd love to see your costumes too!"

Finally, we bolt. We run all the way home, even cutting through the woods to her house. We don't tell any adults, though I dont remember why. We should have.

Roughly 20 years later, an acquaintance is convicted of possessing CSAM. My husband and I go to the family watchdog website to see if we live near any of these monsters. We'd never suspected his high school acquaintance, so we felt the need to know about others we may not suspect.

A few small blips are in the neighborhood, but all minor offenses (as if any should be considered minor...). 

Then I see it, a red dot. Its in my dads area of the neighborhood, where we grew up.

I zoom in.

Its that house with the boxwoods.

I click.

"Sexual assault of a minor. Multiple counts."

I shut the laptop, and finally tell someone else the story of that night.

It could have been us if we'd gone upstairs that night.
