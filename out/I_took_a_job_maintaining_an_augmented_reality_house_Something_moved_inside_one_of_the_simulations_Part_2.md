A few people asked me questions about what was in that simulation or what exactly Peter was doing there.

At the time I assumed he just didn’t want to explain the system in detail. Looking back on it now, I’m not so sure.

\-

We stood there for a moment in the hallway with the door still open. Wind moved slowly through the grass beyond the frame. Peter was staring out into the field.

Not at me.

Not at the doorway.

At the horizon.

Eventually he stepped forward and pulled the door closed. The field vanished. Just a normal bedroom again. Plain carpet, bare walls, and a single window with the blinds halfway down.

Peter stood there for a moment longer, like he was listening to something.

Then he turned back toward the stairs.

“Let’s check the logs,” he said.

We went back down to the control room.

The monitors were still cycling through the house. Empty rooms. Hallways. The staircase we had just used.

Peter sat down at the console and started pulling up system diagnostics.

“Projection stability looks good,” he muttered through laced fingers. “Latency normal.”

I was still thinking about the footprints.

“...does the system ever simulate people?” I asked after a moment.

Peter didn’t look up.

“No.”

“Animals?”

“No.”

“So nothing else could have been in that environment.”

Peter paused for a second. Then he said quietly, almost to himself,

“Not intentionally.”

That wasn’t exactly reassuring. He rewound the camera feed from the field room.

“Let’s take a look.”

The monitor showed the bedroom door opening. Then the environment loaded. The field appeared on screen exactly the way I remembered it. Grass moving in the wind. Blue sky overhead.

The camera angle showed me stepping through the doorway. I watched myself walk deeper into the grass. Everything looked normal. Peter slowed the footage as I moved farther away from the door.

“Here,” he said.

He tapped the keyboard and zoomed slightly into the horizon. For a moment I didn’t see anything. Just grass. Then I noticed it.

Something darker than the grass standing near the center of the frame. Tall enough that the top of it rose above the blades. It wasn’t moving.

Just standing there.

I leaned closer to the screen.

“What is that?” I asked.

Peter didn’t answer. He advanced the video a few seconds. The grass shifted slightly in the wind.

The dark shape didn’t move.

The video continued. Eventually I reached the point where I had turned around and started walking back toward the door. Peter paused the footage again.

The shape was gone.

“Was that there when you were inside the room?” he asked.

“No,” I said, immediately. “I didn’t see anything.”

Peter rewound the video again and played the segment a second time. The shape appeared at exactly the same moment. Standing in the grass. Watching.

“You’re sure?” Peter asked.

“Yes.”

He checked the system logs.

“Only one biometric signal detected,” he said. “Just you.”

“No second user.”

“No additional headset.”

I felt strange, my chest tightening.

“Could the system have generated it?” I asked.

Peter stared at the screen.

“I didn’t code anything like that.”

He advanced the footage a little further. The wind rolled through the grass. My footprints stretched behind me. And then the dark shape appeared again.

Closer this time.

Peter froze the video.

Neither of us spoke for several seconds.

Finally I said, “That’s about where I saw the grass move.”

Peter nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

He rewound the video again. The shape appeared. Then the grass shifted. Then it disappeared. Peter leaned back in his chair.

“That’s not possible,” he said quietly, almost to himself.

I glanced toward the hallway. The door to the field room was still closed upstairs. I sighed and turned toward him.

“Can we check the room again?” I asked.

Peter hesitated. Then he nodded. We walked back upstairs. The house felt quieter than before. When we reached the bedroom door, Peter stopped.

“You didn’t see anything out there?” he asked again.

“No, just the footprints.”

He studied my face for a moment. Then he opened the door. The bedroom looked completely normal.

No field.

No grass.

Just carpet and blank walls.

Peter stepped inside and looked around the room carefully.

“Environment inactive,” he said.

He walked back into the hallway. We stood there for a moment. Then we went back downstairs. Peter pulled the camera feed up again. The field environment was still visible in the recorded footage.

Grass moving.

Sky drifting slowly overhead.

The dark shape was gone.

For a moment I thought that was the end of it.

Then the video advanced a few more seconds. And the grass near the door started to move again. Peter leaned forward slowly.

The blades parted.

Like something stepping through them.

Toward the doorway.

Toward the place where I had been standing.
