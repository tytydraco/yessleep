I don’t really know where else to post this.

For the past few weeks, I haven’t been able to sleep more than two hours at a time. I fall asleep fine. I eat well, I work out 4–5 times a week, I don’t drink much caffeine, I have hobbies I enjoy. I’m not particularly stressed......But I wake up every two hours. Like clockwork. At first I thought it was just random insomnia. I didn’t even check the time the first few nights. Eventually I noticed a pattern. I’d wake up, roll over, check my phone. 2:18 a.m. 4:18 a.m. 6:18 a.m. It’s not always 18, but it’s always exactly two hours after I fell asleep.

What’s weird is I don’t wake up grooooggy. I wake up instantly alert. Like someone just nudged me. No nightmares. No loud noises. Just… awake. (??????)

The first week I blamed stress. The second week I cleaned up my routine even more. No screens before bed. Magnesium. Breathing exercises. You name it.

Didn’t change anything. Then I started noticing something else.....

Every time I wake up, I have this really strong feeling that I wasn’t alone a second ago. Of course, not in a dramatic “there’s a shadow figure at the foot of my bed” way. There’s nothing there when I open my eyes. It’s more like that feeling when someone stops talking the second you walk into a room. Like I interrupted something.

Last night I decided to record myself sleeping. I don’t even know what I expected to see. Sleepwalking maybe? Some obvious explanation. I propped my phone up on my dresser so it could see my bed and most of the room.I fell asleep around 12:11 a.m. then woke up at 2:11 a.m.

Wide awake. Same as always.

I didn’t check the video right away. I just turned it off and went back to sleep. This morning I watched it. For the first two hours, nothing happens. I’m just asleep.

At 2:10 a.m., I sit up. Not slowly. Not stretching. I just sit straight up. My eyes are open. I turn my head toward the corner of my room. The one by the closet door. Then I stay like that completely still for almost a full minute. Then at 2:11, I lie back down. At 2:12, I wake up again. On camera.

I sit up, look around like I’m confused, grab my phone. That’s the part I remember though. That’s when I was “awake.” I have absolutely no memory of sitting up the first time and I thought maybe I was sleepwalking with my eyes open or something, but here’s the part that’s bothering me.

When I was sitting there the first time, staring at the corner?

My head tilts slightly....like I’m listening. I'm kind of scared but I’m recording again tonight, but I moved the camera so it’s pointed at the corner instead of me.

I don’t know what I’m expecting to see but I’m starting to get the feeling that whatever’s waking me up every two hours isn’t trying to scare me.

It’s making sure I don’t see what happens after.
