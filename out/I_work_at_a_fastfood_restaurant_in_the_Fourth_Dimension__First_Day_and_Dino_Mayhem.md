It was the first day of me working in the Fourth Dimension. I overslept most of my alarms and woke up at 7 PM in complete chaos, shouting “fuckfuckfuckfuckfuckfuckityfuck!" while hurriedly trying to get dressed, slipping and falling multiple times, and tumbling around on my bedroom floor like in a slapstick comedy from the early 2000s. When one of my roommates, shirtless with his vile pot belly bouncing and a twenty-sixer in one hand, asked me, somewhat slurred, where I was rushing to. I replied acidly:

“Work … you should try going one of these days!”

He let out a hearty laugh that came straight from his comically bouncy stomach; “That will be the fuckin’ day, eh?”

*Yeah, never mind; if he got a job, it would probably create a paradox that could jeopardize the human race as we know it,* I remember thinking to myself. And with that, I slipped on my dilapidated six-year-old runners without tying what was left of the strings and dashed out the front door, into my car, and down the road to my little gravel lot. A sudden onslaught of late-night traffic had heavily delayed me because people only seem to want to drive when you're running late, but I managed to arrive by 7:50. All I had to do was wait and brace myself for... ugh, Bessy. I swallowed hard. The pit opened, and the humiliation ritual began once more. It was no more pleasant than the first time, and I thanked the Lord above when Bessy had finally retracted from my insides.

Heading inside, I was greeted by Xnua, who uncomfortably skulked his tail around my hips. “Mr. Pierre!” he had said in that oh-so-eerie exuberant yet monotone cadence. “Excited to see that you survived the trip over—not everyone survives good ol’ Bessy!”

“Wait, hold on… what?”

He made a low pfffft noise and said, “Something bad only happens every ... one in a million? Eh, that’s more an issue with the two-dimensional types. Anyway, let’s get you hooked up.” We headed into the back, where he handed me my only uniform that he made me SWEAR on my life I would protect and maintain because it's really expensive, and if I lose it, the cost of a new one will come out of my paycheck. The uniform in question, in my oh-so humble opinion, was rather obnoxiously loud and gaudily kitsch, the top being a bright carmine that could blind a man if he stared at it too long, and navy blue slacks with little sewn-on iconographs of that elephant-thing in the window.

With Xnua grabbing me by the shoulders, I was shoved onto the kitchen floor, where I promptly said in a perhaps not-so-decorous manner, “What the fuck is that thing?” It was a creature about three feet tall that I can best describe as being flatter than paper or the waist of a female celebrity from the early 2000s, with an oval body that had wonky and asymmetrical eyes near a long, twisty, and turvy mouth. Tentacles sprouted from beneath the oval. It made a few angry beeps and burs.

“It’s okay,” Xnua said. “Our new pal here probably hasn’t met a person from the second dimension before. Pierre, this is Orbral; he will be your main coworker on Mondays and Tuesdays. Most of our staff are only part-time, you see! You and I are the only full-time people right now… well, Tantra does Monday through Friday as well. So, for Mondays and Tuesdays, it’ll be you and him on the floor, with Tantra on the grill, and it’ll be you, her, and Oxy from Wednesday to Friday. Oh, right, that reminds me.” He called for Tatra. From the far back, a rather foxy woman emerged. Quite literally. She was an anthropomorphic fox akin to Krystal from Star Fox, but with a slightly more robust build, wearing a grease-stained apron.

“What do you want?” she said with the quite caricatured growl of a cafeteria lunch lady.

“Uh, okay,” I said softly.

“Great! Orbal, show him how the cash register and the coffee are done!”

And with that, he sped off to who knows where, leaving me with my new best buddy, Orbral. He couldn't speak English, and I couldn't speak Two-Dimension (to this day, even my understanding of the Fifth-Dimension language isn't where I want it to be), so we had to rely heavily on hand signals. Surprisingly, most of the rest of the day went pretty normally—almost so normal that it was a bit absurd. helped me memorize how many packets of coffee were needed per brew, how to get the antediluvian cream and sugar machines actually to dispense, plus his, I guess, “hidden” secret about how to calibrate them to dispense the right amount rather than so much cream and sugar it would give those who order a Wayne Gretzky coffee a heart attack—which is a surprisingly daunting task not for the faint of heart.

There were not too many unusual customers on this particular shift. A couple of people who looked like Roswell-type grey aliens, a couple of incomprehensible conglomerates of shapes and colours. You had a couple of the types I was hired to deal with. For instance, all the hippies on a spiritual retreat in Nepal or whatever that have transcended into enlightenment. One guy I got to break out my French for, which I had been meaning to practice more with. (Even though it is my native tongue, I am rather rusty at it, having lived in anglophone Canada for so long).

For enlightened people, they sure as hell do NOT like waiting for their late-night coffee, good god. Or was it late at night? There wasn’t really *time* in the Fourth Dimension in the way that we typically think of it. But in terms of the menu, we apparently do have a way of knowing when to switch it over from the breakfast menu to the dinner menu. And speaking of that…

After experiencing such a tremendous mouth-gasm, I was reluctant to go back to work. But I did. And for the most part, the rest of the shift went completely normal! Nothing could—

*Rumblerumblerumblerumblerumble;* the building shook; *weewooweewooweewoooweeewoo*; sirens blared in every direction. The customers surged to exit the restaurant, each using their own mode of transportation to try to escape the fourth dimension. Xnua came tumbling out of his office and shouted calmly:

“All hands on deck, we are experiencing a time surge!”

“A what?” I asked.

“Just grab onto something, the next twenty minutes are going to be rough!” I did so. The purple that had surrounded the windows was now dense forest, with a rocky beach to the right of the building.

“Xnua,” I said through clenched teeth. “What the fuck is going on?”

“Oh, it’s just a time surge,” he shrugged. “They're rough, but they happen. You know, temporal anomalies.” I was about to reply when I heard a low growl. I slowly turned my head. Peeking in from the door was a bloody dinosaur! But not like that Jurassic Park shit—it was close to fourteen feet tall, covered in white feathers except for the peacock-like plumes on its long tail, and it had fangs for days.

“Xnua,” I whispered, “that doesn’t look too friendly.”

“It does not, no,” he admitted.

“Is there anything you can do?” I asked.

“Yeah, this.” He ran and locked himself in the freezer.

“Hey, you fucking mother—”

The dinosaur let out a thunderous roar before charging at me. I managed to dodge, just narrowly avoiding being eaten. Over and over, the enormous beast wrecked things in its search for its next meal. My breathing was heavy, and my steps were slowing down. I wasn’t exactly in athletic shape. Orbral slithered out from beneath a crack in the counter. He was frantically saying something when, in what felt like an instant, the dinosaur swooped down and caught Orbral in its mouth. I heard a crunch, then swallowing, then hacking, and suddenly my face was covered in a mucilaginous, cold, 2D goop. I put one trembling hand to my face and slowly wiped some off, then stared at it. W-what the fucking hell… what the actually fucking fuck fuck hell? I was flung into a cupboard or two before I could grab something solid. The building rumbled again, then it stopped.

“Ugh, finally!” I heard Xnua say evenly. “I hate being in the third dimension so much, it makes me feel so powerless.” He held out one hand. The dinosaur roared at him, but it shut up, and not by its own free will. Xnua pushed his fingers inwards, and the dinosaur caterwauled and crunched until crimson blood and little chunks of flesh and softened bone were splattered on the kitchen as it turned into a cube and fell into Xnua’s hands. He threw it in the air a few times. “Heh, well, sorry about that. You think you could clean all this up?” I didn’t say anything. “Good!”

I spent the rest of my shift cleaning up dinosaur bits; they reeked of eggs and pork, and the blood was almost impossible to mop. I felt… a bit empty. As I drove home that day, I couldn’t help but think of that little 2D guy. I mean, sure, we didn’t know each other, nor did we speak the same language. He wasn’t even human. Hell, he wasn’t even from the same dimension! But did he have to die for what? For a fucking fast food job!? It dawned on me why this job paid so much; I had just entered hell, and I was supposed to serve the devil a cup of coffee and an egg muffin with a smile. I lay in bed that night, and the events of the last day played over and over in my mind. Was the money really worth it?
