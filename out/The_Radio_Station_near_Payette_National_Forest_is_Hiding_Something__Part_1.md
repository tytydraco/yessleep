It was around 15 years ago when my Mother disappeared. She disappeared in a national park while on the way home from a weekend visiting my grandparents. For years I thought she had died in an accident but now I know the truth. She’s very much alive, and I know exactly where she is.

The only thing left to me by her was a journal. Covered with dirt and dried dark red splotches under the seams of the canvas binding. I’ll transcribe whatever I glean from the journal. A good chunk of the pages are torn or scratched out so it’s hard to read.

  
\~



“Goooooood evening listeners, this is your host Mike Traut with 99.6 FM. It is the witching hour and we have a creepy new special event. Any listeners can now call in at 666-6666 and provide us with the spookiest *real* story!” A grizzly gruff man called out over the radio, the audio crackling. It hadn't seen any sort of repair in at least a decade.

My hands gripped the wheel. The rubber creaking as I jolted from the loud voice. I was in the middle of highway hypnosis. At least it woke me up.

I tuned back in to the radio broadcast.

“The winner, chosen by me, will receive a small cash prize of *100 dollars*!” It was about 15 minutes of silence with occasional small talk from the host before any calls came in.

The first caller was an older woman named Annabel. “We were hiking in Joshua tree.. maybe.. 20 or 30 years ago..” the old woman’s breathing was rough like she was exerting herself just by remembering.

“My son.. he had just graduated college and we wanted to celebrate with a camping trip. Anyway 3 days of normal wilderness camping later we ended up getting a bit lost. The trails started leading to dead ends and spots where landmarks used to be, started to feel wrong. Like they had been changed right behind our backs.” The old woman gulped at something. Probably trying to ease what sounded like a scratchy throat with water.

“My my ma’am! That’s pretty creepy-“ The host was cut off by the old woman speaking over him.

“I’m not done!” She shouted uncharacteristically loudly. She was breathing heavier and sounded fatigued.

“My son. Despite being a sane grown man. He started acting like a scared little boy again. It all started because of a low hum he said was echoing around the forest.” She gulped down more water and coughed roughly.

“I already had hearing aids by that point so I told him I didn’t hear anything, I even tried changing the frequency to make him feel better. They picked up nothing for me.” Her raspiness and coughing calmed down as she assumedly was reminiscing.

“He was a good man. I know it. Even after I woke up that night to him outside the tent. Seizing like a dying animal. The worst part was I could finally hear the ringing. It stopped after a few minutes that felt like hours and my son stood up suddenly in a flash and took off into the treeline. I could occasionally make out his form in the distant trees when the fire crackled but other than that I never saw him again..” Right as Annabelle had concluded her story the host chipped in again. His chipper tune is a bit of a jolt compared to the tale she told.

“Really spooky story there ma’am! Thank you for calling in! I’ll call back if you end up being tonight’s winner!” He seemed to be too chipper for the mood that story set. Almost like he wanted to move on from whatever the hell she was talking about. I didn’t blame him.

“Alright, after a few messages from our sponsors we will be right back with the next caller!” He continued. I was then exposed to 5 minutes of the most asinine advertisements. Hand cream, used tires, anything else you could imagine would be boring.

The sound of the host answering the phone made me grip my steering wheel again. This time tighter.  
  
“Hey Mike I have an interesting story to tell! One about Payette and the legendary monster creeping in those woods!” This voice sounded more like a middle aged man. Though he felt like an excited child trying to seem as scary as possible. That wasn’t what got to me though.  
  
I was driving near the Payette forest right then, and I was just about to have to stop for the night. I haven’t seen a road sign in at least an hour..
