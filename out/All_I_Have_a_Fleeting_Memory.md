My dad used to tell me journals were windows into the soul, the true image of a mind being flayed open. When I was five I remember breaking into my sister's journal, one of those cheap girl locked diaries they sold on Nick channel commercials. I was so fascinated with her hand writing she brushed a pen on paper like Michelangelo painting on canvas. When she found out she told dad and I had been scolded pretty bad for that one but from that day on I just loved how each person would not only write their own blissful stories, but show their identity on the paper almost glimpses into what they could be or tell.

This led me to beginning my transfixing into what most would call mundane; graphology. The study practice and execution of hand writing itself. Not just the story or what they were writing about but the literal style structure and method people would write in. In light of that a good if not great story would also ignite my desire. Through my whole childhood and even into the time at university I would be surrounded by the letters and numbers of famous authors or just one of my classmates with chicken scratch. From the most elegant cursive of the girls in class to the rough if not innocent nature of the boys as well. I after some time could tell you just by the writing how the person felt, if they were telling the truth or even helped the teachers in not one of my proudest moments, distinguishing the difference from my teenage classmates 10,000th time perfecting their parents signature to that of said parent who clearly had no time to sign another failed homework assignment.

I graduated early from high school skipping over the boredom and mundane brushing fast and gaining the principal's honor roll in continuation school. All though it was an unknown school that I had achieved in I had begged and pleaded through message and call to gain access to the Oxfords degree program in Graphology. With the financial help of my father who I trusted was more grateful in the idea to get rid of my constant pestering of this famous writer's style of hand writing from 1607 comparing it to the way a news article in 1935 had almost identical I could tell he would miss me dearly. I went off to a new country for the first time in my life exchanging the warm California sun and beach for dreary England weather, but the chance to achieve my greatest dream of seeing the world's writing outweighed whatever inclination I had of getting wet and ragged.

It wasn’t like that at all though, studies got in the way for the other subjects, people in London loved to party and I managed to squeeze my unappealing self into the transduction of the old city lights and life. After four years I had graduated from the university but not with nearly as bright accolades as I had in America.
