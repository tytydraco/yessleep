If you have cats, great! Keep them! They are not only cute, but also lifesavers. I hope this story will remind you, if not teach you that. I will try my best to retell what happened to me around 7 years ago. But all I can say, I've had at least one cat ever since. 

“This is the last game today guys, okay? I’m for real this time.” I spoke into the mic while chuckling. I glanced down at the monitor, 02:23. I had to work the tomorrow- actually in the next couple of hours. But who cares I lived alone at the time and I could do whatever I wanted. Or as I soon would be reminded of, I didn’t live alone. The screen in front of me turned black and I inspected the monitor in shock. 

“What the-” I glanced down at my computer and saw my cat had stepped on the power button. I rolled my eyes while smiling and picked him up. A black and white tuxedo cat, my only friend here in my lonely apartment, Loki. He licked his mouth and whispered a gentle meow.

“You think it's time for me to sleep huh? Well maybe you are right.” I put Loki down on the floor. I began undressing. But once I took my shirt off I saw Loki staring intensely towards the hallway, with my bathroom at the end. I didn’t mind it at first because that's what cats do sometimes, they hear things we don’t. I kept undressing and I could not stop shooting glances at Loki as I proceeded to remove my socks. He had not moved, he had not shifted his gaze. I was unsure if he had even taken a single breath. 

“Loki?” I said his name and tried to lure him with the classic cat calling sounds you do. It usually gets his attention right away since it's a sign that it's time for food. But at the time, no. Nothing. I anxiously made my way towards him, and as soon as I reached for him he turned around and meowed cutely again like he had done previously. I let out a sigh of relief. Maybe it was nothing. I picked him up again and felt his fur towards my bare chest. I stared down the dark and empty hallway, just seeing the bathroom at the end slightly. With the door standing ajar since Loki sometimes likes to go inside and explore. It was… eerie. I’d never been scared of the dark before, definitely not scared of my own bathroom door. But something about that moment just made me gulp and turn on the ceiling lights in my bedroom. The nightstand lamp wasn’t enough. The new ceiling lamp lit up the hallway more, and I could see there was nothing to worry about. I let out a sigh of relief, and prepared myself for bed. 

The next day I came home from work, tired like always. But I was extra tired because I had a hard time falling asleep the night before. I didn't know why, but now when I think about it. I think it was because of that moment with Loki, and the feeling of fear had still lingered on after I turned off the lights. 

When I came home and sat down on my sofa and let out a sigh of relief. It was day, the birds' song peered through the window ajar as a fresh breeze filled the living room. Today I would not spend time playing games with my friends so late (I said that to myself many times but usually did not end up that way), and I would watch a movie, game on my own and go to sleep around 23:00 latest. 

My day went on as planned until I got a call from a friend at 22:30, as I was playing games on my own. I ignored it. He called again. I ignored it. He called again. But the third time I answered with a sigh. His camera appeared on my 2nd monitor and mine as well. 

“What is it, I need to go to bed early today…” 

“Come onnnn! Just one game then. You dipped on us yesterday!”

“That was actually Loki turning off my computer.”

“Wow, is your cat actually smart?”

“I don't know if it was intentional though, anyway sure ONE game.” I saw my friend smile and nod through his camera. His room was messy. I could see clothes on the floor, empty paper mugs from fast food chains. I could even see dust bunnies in the back if I wanted too. In my camera, to my own dreadful realization, you could see the hallway. The door behind me was open so I could faintly see the bathroom door at the end, once again standing ajar. Now noticing that I could not keep my eyes off it, still getting reminded of yesterday. But I tried to brush it off in my mind and tried to focus on the game.

Minutes passed and the thought of the scary hallway left my mind. My friend and I were laughing and everything was good. But the game was over, and I was actually going to bed this time.

“Its time to dip for today.” 

“Fine, but tomorrow it's Friday, so you can stay up right?”

“Yea we’ll figure something out.” I was just closing down the tabs on my computer and reaching for the turn off button on my screen. But then my friend said:

“Why is your cat just staring in the back, he’s done it for a few minutes now?” I froze. Not again. Instead of turning around I see my cat in my own camera window on my 2nd monitor. He was not sitting in the bedroom like yesterday, he was sitting in front of the bathroom door. 

“I-its just something he does.” 

“What, did you stutter just now?” My friend chuckled as he spun his chair. 

“No?” I rolled my eyes and made a face towards my webcam, trying to act brave. I blinked slowly and took a deep breath. I spun my chair to face my little feline friend. But I turned to stone. For a split second I saw- I don't know what I saw. If it even was real. I saw my cat jumping in place dashing towards me. Hissing towards the hallway from the bedroom. But other than that. I also saw… I hand. A giant hand. Claws, like knives. A hand black like charcoal. Only for a split second. It reached for Loki but retracted back into the bathroom and smacked the door only when I noticed it. My soul entered my body once again and I ran to turn on the lights in my bedroom and closed the door to the hallway. 

“Whats all that sound?” My friend asked.

“Did… did you see that?” 

“See what? I’m in the bathroom?” I laughed. From the situation, out of fear. I probably didn't know how to react, thinking I'd gone crazy. He owns a Bluetooth headset. He didn't see. How could I know if what I saw was real? But Loki… hissed. He reacted. It must be real.

“I have to go.” I just turned off my computer and removed my clothes and jumped into bed. My second door in my bedroom leading to the kitchen was still open, and I was on the way to close that too, but I couldn’t, since I couldn't keep the litter box away from Loki. It wasn't facing the hallway or the bathroom, and it was slightly ajar so from the angle of the bed, it looked closed anyway. I didn’t turn off the lights that night. I don’t even know if I got any sleep. I just laid in my bed, spiraling my thoughts into oblivion until I presume my brain got tired and I fell asleep.

The next morning I called in sick to work, I had never felt so tired like I did that day. I didn’t leave my room, unless I was going to the kitchen, but I avoided even catching a glance towards the hallway. I only prepared food for Loki and myself. Even if it was daylight I couldn't erase the memory of that grizzly monstrous hand reaching for Loki. I just watched videos all day long, this time actually ignoring my friends messages and calls. Maybe I shouldn't have been alone in my thoughts, but that is what I wanted at the time. Loki was purring in his cat tree next to me while I sat in front of the computer. I glanced at him and smiled. I looked at the time, 00:43. Maybe it's time for bed. I didn't even know what time of day it was since the window blinds were closed and the ceiling lights had been on the entire time all around the house, even in the hallway. I had picked up courage to wander there, but I had closed my eyes and felt the light switch, turned it off and ran back to the bedroom. But I had not entered the bathroom. I just closed it with my hand, hard. I didn’t have the need for a number two that day anyway, but I had resorted to piss in a bottle. I did not have anything long term planned out; it was just my rational thoughts making spontaneous decisions. 

I turned off my monitor, undressed and went to bed. I felt worn out mentally, but other than that I didn't feel scared to go to bed this time. I knew the lights were on everywhere (almost everywhere), so whatever was there, if it was real, would not come in here. I hoped. I didn’t know the capabilities of whatever that was. You just assume monsters don't come into the light. 

As I laid down, Loki, as a rare occurrence, decided to sleep near my feet. He usually slept in his own place in the living room. I was happy that someone else was here, maybe to watch over me. I fell asleep rather quickly. At least I think I did, can't remember. But a sound woke me up sometime during the night. It was just a tiny sound, almost like a click. But there were no signs of anything. I laid still in my bed, not moving an inch. Could be the pipes, maybe? I tried to close my eyes again. \*Click\*. I kept my eyes closed, but I could feel my body shivering and my heart racing. What was it? \*Click\*. I opened one eye slowly. The lights were still on. Loki was still sleeping. What was that sound? I glanced at the door leading towards the kitchen, still ajar. I could see a tiny crack into the kitchen from my position. It was black. The lights were not on. How was this possible? I began hyperventilating and grabbed my phone while covering my face with my duvet. I turned on my flashlight on my phone to feel safe. \*Click\*. The orange hue coming from the light of my ceiling lamp that surrounded me through my duvet was gone. It was now dark. I forcibly closed my eyes and tried to focus on my breathing. It could be a nightmare. I was sleeping wasn’t I? I pinched myself hard but it hurt, and I was still here. Fuck. 

I waited for a few minutes, I think. It felt like it. Until I, with the slight amount of courage I had left, lifted up my duvet. First facing the window, which was on the opposite side of the door leading into the hallway. I aim my flashlight, anticipating the worst. I flinched, nothing. Just the window blinds. I slowly panned my phone around the room. I stop as I see Loki standing up, with his back arched up and hair raised. He did not make a sound though. But he was ready to fight. I pan my phone even more to the source of his aggression.   

Skinny, ghoulish and just black bones of a body. Monstrous, inhumane, almost elongated. White shiny marbles with large black pupils staring right at me. Dozens of them. Two black claws had reached for me, frozen midair and I met its many eyes which resemble a clutch of eggs. Bloodthirsty, like each eye was hungry for a different piece of my body. I didn’t see a mouth, I didn’t see much. Just a contrast of its skinny body and the glowing eyes. It blinked and I could hear the wrinkly skin hugging its eyes. It growled quietly, attempting to not make a sound. Like as if it thought that I couldn't see it. After a few seconds Loki hissed and the monster began to shout dreadfully. I grabbed Loki and rolled both of us down to the floor on the other side of the thing. I didn't know what else to do. I closed my eyes and hugged the panicking Loki. But I did not let go even if he scratched my body. Glass shattered onto me and cut my body.

Everything turned quiet and Loki wriggled himself out of my grip and jumped on top of the bed. The whistling of the wind made its way into the apartment. I opened my eyes and a tiny bit of light had made its way in. The moonlight from outside. The thing had torn down my blinds and jumped the window. I sat up and saw Loki licking his paw, there was a tiny shard of glass stuck in it. I stood up and was so in shock I ignored the glass underneath my feet and shivered frantically. 

After that day I knew that thing had left my apartment. Loki was back to acting like himself again. But I took him to the vet and got myself checked out as well. I made up a story to the landlord and he bought it, but was skeptical. But the window and the blinds were fixed. My fear after that day kind of disappeared. I guess since I know it left I felt safe again. But maybe it was also thanks to knowing that my cat was smart, and that I should not underestimate his senses again. 

Now I am old, Loki passed away a few years ago. But I never stopped owning a cat. It was one of my most important criteria when finding a partner. I actually named my first son after my cat. He saved my life. If you have a cat, even a dog or any other kind of animal. Don't underestimate them. They know, we don't.
