I work as a mortician, and strange things are happening. A week ago, a rather disturbing one happened.  I had just clocked in for the night shift when I felt a warm breeze drifting through the room—strange. It was strange since mortuaries are kept freezing due to the bodies,  so I instantly went looking for the source of the warmth. Someone had left a window open, which was strange since I was the only one supposed to clock in the whole day. “Maybe it was the janitor,” I reasoned to myself.

I closed the window and went to check the board for what I was supposed to do. I won’t go into the nitty-gritty of what I had to do; that’s not what this is about. I was fixing up a young man who had been shot to death; all the bullets and other things had been removed. I just had to embalm his skin and clean him up for the funeral. I was taking a break to drink some coffee when suddenly I heard a banging noise from the morgue.

I walked in there slowly; I assumed it was probably just the muscles of a dead body reacting to the cold. I opened up a door to one of the cold containers, the body where the sound was coming from, and I jumped a little, seeing the corpse. It was a young woman, a rigor mortis young woman.  Her muscles had locked into a face of pure terror, and she had been bumping against the container.

I sighed and tried to put her into a lying position, but the stiff muscles didn’t make it easy whatsoever. I eventually fit her in, and then I went to continue my coffee break. I caught a look at myself in the reflection of the coffee and chuckled; my pale skin made me look more like the corpses than a regular person.  The heavy bags under my eyes looked like lumps of coal, and my long, dirty blonde hair made me look like a less cool Kurt Cobain. I finished my coffee and did some exercises to keep my muscles loose. 

I walked back to the table where the body was, and I gasped loudly. The corpse was gone. I rubbed my eyes and checked that I was in the right room, and I was. I paced around the room anxiously when suddenly I heard the sound of bare feet smacking against the concrete floor, and they were running past the room. I jumped in fear and ran out of the room. 

“Who’s there?” I did my best to sound confident and strong, while I was the farthest thing from it inside. I was praying that it would just be the janitor or another one of the morticians. I heard a strange dripping sound and then the sound of someone sprinting barefoot through the mortuary again. I put my hands into fists as I slowly walked out of the room and looked around.

Gasping in horror, my eyes tried to comprehend what I was seeing. It was the corpse, standing at the end of the hall with blood dripping from his eye sockets. He was still completely naked, and I felt frozen as he suddenly charged at me. The putrid smell of the dead body made my nose wrinkle as I stood up.

As the body got right in front of me, I punched him across the face as hard as I could. I crouched down and punched him a few more times before getting up to run, but he grabbed me by my ankle, and I fell and ate shit on the hard concrete as I tried to run. I reached into my belt and pulled out the embalmbing tool and slashed it at him wildly as he started crawling towards me like a centipede.

His mouth hangs open, the skin sagging like a towel on a stick. I watched in horror as he didn't feel a thing as I stabbed it into his face over and over. I started kicking him in balls since thats the closest part of him my legs were. Embalmbing liquid oozed off of him and onto the ground as he got closer, completely unaffected by my attempts to get him off.

He slowly brought himself closer to me, and I felt liquid courage flooding through me as I brought my fist against him. I caught him in the jaw and knocked him to the ground and straight up booked it the fuck out of there. Strange things have happened at this Mortuary for the whole three motnhs I have worked here, but I have gotten somewhat used to it. There is a reason we have locks on the containers that we keep the corpses in, and it's a damn good reason. Sometimes, the dead aren't fully dead.
