Today is my graduation day. In a small town like ours it is one of the important things that can happen in your life. So like any other graduate, I woke up as early as I can, even though I checked the time it's just 2:46 in the morning.



I tried to knock on my parents' door, wondering if any of them are awake. Looks like I am the only one so I returned to my room to take a bath. Mom promised me that the owner of the only salon in town would come to our house to put make up on me, but she won't be here until six. Out of boredom I looked for things I can do to be ready for today's big event. 



As I checked for who knows how many times for my things. My Mary Janes look shiny and new, my favorite necklace set aside and ready to be worn, however, it looks like we forgot to iron my toga. I may not be as good at ironing clothes as my mom, but I can still do it, I'll try. So I tried looking for the iron, but after 15 minutes of looking, I still couldn't find it. I know I can wait until the sun shines or when my parents wake up and ask them where it is, but I can't help myself.



I turned to the kitchen and looked for the flashlight in the drawers and decided to go to my grandmother's house, I'm sure the backdoor is open. It has always been open and I can't remember when it's not. I can sneak in and get their own iron, it won't be much of a problem. 



So I got out of the house as quietly as possible. My grandma's house is just 10 minutes away, not much of a distance. As I got out, I looked up at the sky and there was no moon, it was dark, and cold. I walked faster and in just a few minutes I was already at the back of their house. As I reached for the back door, just as I expected it would be open. With my flashlight in hand I made my way through the kitchen. 



I flashed light at the top of the table, and I can see the ingredients of the feast she promised to cook for me today. There were fresh fruits, ingredients for spaghetti, and a box of cake. It looks like she forgot to put the cake in the freezer. So I picked up the box and opened the freezer, then I noticed something.



Huh?



I couldn't help but question myself as I saw the frost covered shelf. No beef? My stomach churned, nervous. Did she forget? Grandma is always so detailed. She wouldn't forget what her dear grand daughter requested, it was the main course of the feast.



Unless of course, she wanted the meat fresh.



Maybe she really forgot or will buy it later for it to still be fresh. So I continued to the living room, and finally to my grandma's room.



I knocked, and knocked, and knocked, but nobody answered me. It looks like my grandmother is not awake as usual with her bout of insomnia. Well it's not a problem, she doesn't lock her room either. She's not in her room, how odd. Was she in the rest room? So I spoke out loud if I could borrow the iron. Silence means yes, so I took it. and gone back through the living room, the kitchen, and out through the back door. 



The road was still dark, filled with the hoot of owls and chirping of cicadas. To keep the nerves at bay, I hummed a tune. As I walked down the road and passed by a neighbour's house, my eyes caught something on the roof. Or someone.



I stood, frozen.



A woman on all fours crawling across the roof of one of the houses. Her limbs are too long, moving, with spider-like agility. It was my grandmother. Still wearing her old nightgown, crawled, a dark red, wet tongue darting out and wiggling from her mouth. Accompanied by a sharp scratching noise on metal, she turned.



She saw me, and she smiled.



Gone were the dentures I know; the ones she had now were sharp, white, and numerous. She recognized her favorite granddaughter.



And I realized, she has fresher meat ready for the taking. I didn't wait for her to move or say a word. 



My fingers white, clutching hard on the iron, I ran.
