I’ve recently gotten into writing as a hobby. I love horror, so I naturally gravitated towards it. I’ve had lots of outlandish ideas so far: Deepsea monsters, alien invasions, pretty much any theme you can find if you search for “horror” on streaming services. Unfortunately, I’ve hit a writer’s block about a month ago. I felt like I had done everything I could think of, so I didn’t know how to continue with my hobby.

I ended up joining a couple online forums for writers; I thought maybe swapping some ideas would help me think of something. That’s when a user going by “Asmodeus” suggested writing about something from my daily life. I didn’t understand at first, since I didn’t feel like my personal life was particularly scary, but once I thought about it, I started understanding the appeal. Writing about something most people could relate to, but adding an uncanny twist to it might resonate with a larger audience.

With my newfound motivation I started another story. “Locusts” I called it. I live in a secluded part of town with lots of greenery around. There are days when the crackling sound of grasshopper wings is so overwhelming, I have to put on noise cancelling headphones, just to be able to hear my own thoughts.

I decided to put that feeling into the story, and ended up writing for the whole afternoon when I first started. Here’s a small excerpt, I think is relevant for context:

“The biblical plague befell onto Jackson, a single person affected by an overwhelming and all-consuming force. They stalked him. They surrounded him. They started eating him.”

That was the last part I wrote before I went to bed. My goal was to write about someone in my position: someone living on the countryside, someone who’s used to hearing grasshoppers all around. But they would be getting louder and louder. Until Jackson would start losing his mind and finally succumb to the plague.

I’m glad I didn’t finish the story that day.

I didn’t have time to continue writing when I got up. My alarm rang, ripping me out of my dreams into the monotony of my nine to five. I was so motivated, I couldn’t focus on work. I kept fantasizing about how to continue my story, how I could get to the ending I wanted and bring about that beautiful suffering for Jackson.

When I got home, I didn’t even bother to put my work clothes away. I threw them on my couch, grabbed my laptop and turned it on to continue my story.

I was very focused the day before, so I remember clear as day, I was close to finishing my fourth page, but looking at it now, the story was continued a bit into the fifth page. I was wondering if I was misremembering something.

I decided to re-read it from the beginning.

“Ashton woke up confused and dazed.” I closed the laptop reflexively. That was my name. I thought about it for a second looking for an explanation. I might have forgotten to differentiate between myself and my main character in my manic motivation yesterday. It wouldn’t be the first time. If I had an idea I would often go on long-winded writing sprees, trying to express all of my thoughts, only for me to then have to rewrite everything and delete most of it, since it didn’t make any sense. I decided to keep reading.

The rest of the story read pretty much the same as I remembered it from the day before, with one exception: the name “Jackson” didn’t appear in the story at all. Every occurrence of his name, even those I explicitly remember, would read as my name instead.

When I got to the part about Jackson being stalked and gnawed on by the locusts, reading “Ashton” instead made me feel sick. I guess putting fictional characters in horrible situations and imagining them happening to oneself are completely different.

I distinctly remember that being the last part I wrote. I saw a grasshopper through the corner of my eye at work and couldn’t stop smiling, remembering that last scene. But it wasn’t the last scene anymore. Somehow, more had been added.

“When Ashton realized what was happening, he wanted to stop the plague. he wanted to stop writing, he needed to. But he couldn’t. For his curiosity needed the story to end.”

I sat there for a while. I definitely didn’t write this. The reference to writing? I didn’t include anything like that in the story I was sure of that.

I felt odd, like I was uncomfortable in my own skin. I could feel goosebumps all over my body. My motivation was gone. I didn’t want to write this anymore. I closed my laptop as I heard the grasshoppers get louder.

 I looked out of my window and saw it. A grasshopper was standing right there, seemingly looking for something by my window. I stared at it for a second. Then, I walked over to it and took a closer look. It was the first time I actually saw what they looked like from up close. Their elongated face, the black eyes staring in who knows what direction, and the small fangs sitting at the bottom of it’s face. The thin, almost translucent wings. Shaking, making that awful sound. I closed my blinds and started walking away.

The sound was getting louder. Way louder. Too loud for the grasshoppers to only be outside my home. But they couldn’t be inside, right? I got out my noise cancelling headphones. I put them on. It was even louder now. Of course, since a grasshopper was sitting in the ear muffs and was now pressed against my ear.

I threw my headphones across the room and hastily tried to swat it away from my face. I saw it land on the ground. It slowly jumped away towards the window. As I raised my hand towards my face and pressed it against my ear I noticed something. It was still attached, but less so. Blood was streaming down the side of my face. It bit me.

I was about to run out of my apartment, when I considered the amount of these things that might be outside, directly in front of my door. I grabbed my phone and my laptop, and ran upstairs to the bedroom. I locked the door, the windows, and took one of my blankets to fill in the gap between the door and the floor. Only then, did I allow myself to take a breather.

My best friend was my coworker, Manuel. We didn’t really share a passion, considering he hated horror, and I wasn’t particularly interested in his climbing adventures either. But we did somehow always end up in the same projects. He invited me over to his place in the city a few times, which is why I immediately thought of asking him if I could stay there for the next couple of days.

But how would I leave. Maybe the swarm would calm down if I waited a day? What even made them this aggressive? I kept thinking back to Jacksons- my story and really hated the idea that the two might be connected somehow.

I opened the laptop back up and deleted the story. I was over it.

I stayed up the whole night, waiting for any unusual sounds. A distant humming kept me up. I didn’t dare ignore it and go to bed. When morning came, I called in sick to work and kept sitting in the bedroom. I still heard a distant noise coming from somewhere around me. However, I couldn’t make out the exact location. It bothered me. I’d wait one more day, then I’d ask Manuel if I could come over, regardless if the sound was gone or not.

I tried to stay up but eventually fell asleep. I don’t remember if it was a dream, or if it was the reason I woke up, but I remember my blanket moving. Vibrating in a way. I heard the noise, felt their wings flutter against the blanket cover from the inside, and felt the panic start to rise. I threw my blanket to the side and ran.

Now full of adrenaline, I ripped open the door. I was about to run out full speed when I noticed it: nothing. I turned around confused, but still, nothing. No rattling under the blanket, no swarm, no sound, nothing. The only thing I could make out was some blood on my pillow, that must’ve come from my ear injury while I slept. I held my ear, still terrified of the whole ordeal.

I grabbed some clothes, electronics, pretty much everything I would need to get away for a couple of days. I didn’t feel like I had a choice anymore. I needed to get out of there.

I am sitting on the stairs waiting for Manuel right now. He said he’s coming to get me. Do any of you have some sort of idea what might be happening? I’m glad I’ll get out of here soon, but I can’t just ignore this. I don’t know if it’s over now, or when I’ll be able to return. Please tell me if any of you know something. I need to know what’s going on.
