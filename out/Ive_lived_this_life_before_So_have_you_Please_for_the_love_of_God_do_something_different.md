I don't know what year it is when you're reading this. But if it's before the year 2089 and if you recognize the world outside your window as roughly normal, then stop whatever you're doing right now.

You can break a routine. Quit your job. It does not matter how small or big it is, it just has to be different and something you do not regularly do. The butterfly effect is real. I know how this is going to sound... because I have written it before. I have written it countless times.

My name does not matter. What matters is that I am a physicist, or I was, before the institution I worked for was dissolved. I worked on a project that I cannot name, funded by people I never knew. We were taken to work in cars and were blindfolded whenever we had to walk outdoors.

The project's premise was: what happens to a person after they die if you bring them back fast enough? It wasn't a medical procedure, you wouldn't need a physicist for that. We were doing something precise. Stopping the heart, maintaining oxygen to the brain and inducing clinical death for intervals of thirty seconds to four minutes and then restoring function.

We had a subject whom I'll call R. He was compensated. He volunteered. He signed everything. And for the first few sessions, nothing strange happened. Then one of our junior researchers noticed something in the footage.  
She pulled me aside on a Thursday. She'd been reviewing recordings from three separate sessions, weeks apart and aligned them on a timeline starting from the exact second R's heart resumed.

In all three videos, at the seven-second mark, R took a sharp breath through his nose.

At fifteen seconds: he raised his right hand and pressed the back of it to his eyes.

At thirty seconds: his left hand came up and scratched, very lightly, behind his left ear.

The same actions took place in the same sequence over every session, conducted weeks apart. We ran it again. And again. Seven seconds: the breath. Fifteen seconds: the eyes. Thirty seconds: the left ear. Every single time. We never told R thinking that if he knew, he'd perform it consciously, and we'd lose the data. But I don't think it would have mattered.

What our team came to believe, after three years of work that I suspect has been buried, classified, or simply erased from the record, is that you have lived your life before. And not in a spiritual or metaphorical sense. I mean this literally, mechanically, the way a song plays again when you hit repeat. Time is not a river moving forward. It's a number line. Infinite in both directions. And your consciousness... whatever it is, occupies a finite segment of it.

Let's say you were born in 1991 and will die at eighty. Your segment is 1991 to 2071. That's it. That's your piece of the number line. When you die, you don't go forward. There is no forward for you. You go back to the beginning of your segment. 1991. Infant. No memories. No awareness of the loop. You live every moment again, make every choice again, feel every feeling again, and you do it the same way, because you are the same person with the same brain in the same conditions, and the same conditions produce the same results.

Killing and reviving subjects seemed to have created a kind of loop... or glitch. R scratched his ear because R always scratches his ear. He has scratched it ten thousand times in that room, in the seconds after returning from death. He will scratch it ten thousand more.

This is your life. You have lived it before. You will live it again. Your children... if you have them, or will have them, their segment extends beyond yours. They move a little further down the number line. Their children, further still. This is why humanity has a history, why knowledge compounds, why we build on what came before: we are not one consciousness looping, but an expanding edge of loops, each generation pushing slightly further into time. Their infinite rebirths cover ground yours never will. But you're fixed to just your tiny segment in time.

We published nothing. But some of us kept working informally, and eventually, through a combination of methods I won't get into here, we arrived at the following conclusion: The loop is not airtight. There are what we call branch points. Moments of genuine randomness. They're actual, physically chaotic events whose outcomes are not determined by the prior state of the universe.  
These are rare. Most of your choices are not free in any meaningful sense. You are a complex machine running a program. But occasionally, given the sample space is large enough, random noise is capable of entering. If you act on that noise, if you introduce genuine randomness into your behavior. If you do something that the prior state of the universe, strictly speaking, did not require of you, you create a new branch.  
And on that branch, your infinite future rebirths run differently.  
You're still looping. That doesn't stop. But you're looping through a different life.

I need to tell you about the world I'm writing from. I want you to understand what you're looping toward.  
The world is controlled by three entities which are not the government. Governments were useful (might sound like a joke to you) for a while and then they weren't. These entities are not companies in any form you'd recognize. They are more like climates. You can't interact with them, just exist within them. Dissent is not illegal. It's simply not possible in any way that matters.  
The mechanisms for collective action were eroded so incrementally, over so many decades, that most people who are alive now have no functional memory of what those mechanisms looked like when they worked. The people who do remember are old and tired and have been tired for a long time.  
There are children being born right now who will never know an unmonitored thought. Because the architecture of everything they use to think, communicate, and understand the world was built to make certain thoughts do not occur to them. It's more elegant than surveillance. Surveillance implies they have something to hide. But this way, they simply have nothing to hide, because the hiding place was never built.

The thing that could have stopped this was not a revolution. Only randomness, pure noise can get in its way. The resistance of people who kept doing inconvenient things. I cannot tell you the one thing to resist or the one thing to do. I can only tell you that the direction was set very early, and that it was set by ordinary life. By what people chose when they thought no one was choosing anything.

Here is what I am asking you to do, and it is not to save my world. You cannot save my world. The branch I'm on, the main branch, is what it is. Every version of me, in every loop, writes this post in a building hiding from the "climate". And every version of you in every loop on my branch reads it too late or doesn't read it at all or reads it and nods and does nothing. That version of you will keep looping in the main branch, in all its infinite iterations. I'm sorry. I can't fix that.

But the version of you reading this right now, tonight, can branch. You don't have to change the world. You have to change your own infinite sequence of worlds. You have to get off the main branch. So do random shit. I know how this sounds. But I've spent years watching a man scratch his left ear at exactly thirty seconds after being brought back from death. I've seen what it looks like when a life is running a program.

We are all R. We are all scratching our ears. The question is whether you are willing to lift a different hand. I won't see it if you do... I will never know. The branch you move to is not a place I can follow. The whole point is that it becomes a different story, and I'm not in it, and the version of you who lives that story will never know it's different because they won't remember this version.

This is the last thing I want to say, and it's the thing that has kept me awake across more sleepless nights than I can count: You won't remember making the choice. The version of you reborn into the new branch won't know they owe it to a blog or a post on a website. They'll just live a different life. You don't directly benefit from it. The you who benefits is not the you who acts. And yet, something about it feels like the closest thing to grace I have ever encountered. The idea that a version of you might be free because of a choice you'll never remember making. That's worth something. I have to believe that's worth something. Please. Do something you can't predict.

*I don't expect this post to stay up. I don't expect most of you to believe it. But I've written it before and I'll write it again, and on one of those infinite occasions, perhaps for one of you, it will land differently.*
