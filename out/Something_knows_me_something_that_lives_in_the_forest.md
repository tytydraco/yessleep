I usually don't drive to work, and I have this path I follow between work and home. Honestly, it is quite the scenery. I usually pass the park and glance at the same few people there almost every week, along with some forest with its own path leading down near a river, it rarely gets tiring every time. 



I take night shifts as a security guard at an art museum filled with all sorts of sensitive things, which makes my job very important as to make sure people don't get away with destroying expensive art. It doesn't pay a whole lot, but I got the job because of the atmosphere, the feeling of relaxing and listening to the sounds of fans from my computer or the rain from outside just gave me positive vibes. 



I mainly sit around all night at a camera screen, and I rarely get up to do much, unless of course some dumb teenagers walked in to vandalize the property. Usually I just try to stop them from vandalizing and use the flashlight to get a description of them before they try and flee.



Now look, I am not the one who enjoys a change in their schedule, I enjoy a consistency with my days really. This was until I noticed something one night as I was taking a walk from my home to work. As I was walking by the forest I see every night, I heard some bushes blaring out of nowhere. It made me jump and point my flashlight at it, but there was nothing there. 



I stood there for a while confused, waiting to see if something would pop out of the bush, but it was silent. I thought "I would rather go to work than sit there and wait for something to happen." And so I did. When I first arrived at work, I was searching for my keys to get in, which usually didn't take much time. That was when I heard what sounded like a large stick snapping. It was so loud I jolted my head behind me, trying to see what may have been there. The path was as clear as I left it, though with a large and broken stick laying there. Without thinking much of it, I headed inside. 



The door for the museum wasn't fancy, just a simple glass door to create a welcoming and open atmosphere for the museum. I sat down, and started monitoring the cameras once more. It was silent for many hours, no vandalizers, no problems, no nothing. That was until around 4:00 to 5:00. 



I was thirsty and got a drink from a mini cooler within the room I was in. Usually, I save coffee for until I start getting drowsy, and I was still wide awake from a good nap. So, I ended up getting myself some cold water. I then heard an immediate, almost ironic, glass shattering noise coming from the camera system. I turned around and saw that it was once again, a bunch of dumb teenagers that broke in and were preparing some spray paint. Already being annoyed from the teenagers breaking in, I walked on down from my office towards the entrance. Then, out of nowhere, I heard something happen to those teenagers. I can still perfectly describe it piece by piece. It was so disturbing, I would remember it through severe dementia. 



First, it sounded like one of the teenagers yelled, then, a loud screeching coming from the general area, which sounded nearly non-human. I heard many screams that seemed to silence one by one as I approached. I ran near the entrance and peeked out from a corner to see what was left of the aftermath that happened. It took me around 10 to 15 seconds to get there, but it sounded like it had already been over for about 5 seconds. There was the entrance, that had been shattered by a large rock, and nothing else.



I was left stunned in place for what felt like a minute, I felt my hands tremble and my mind began to race. "What happened..?" I thought to myself, as I wiped some sweat from my head. I knew the alarm from the glass breaking already contacted the police. Though, I was meant to stand near the entrance, I'd rather go back than go towards whatever the hell I just heard. I went on back to my office, locked the door, and waited for police officers to arrive. I explained as much as I knew and handed over the recording for the officers. They left and went to figure out what happened. When I was walking from work back home, I heard the same exact bush make the exact loud ass noise.



That time I was suspicious, curious even, but I knew better than to approach it. I turned back to get back on track... The moment I turned around to start walking, I heard something whisper. Quietly at first, and I didn't hear what it said. I turned back to the bushes in curiousity, and what I saw made my skin crawl.



I saw what appeared to be a face, but either the lighting was making it appear so, or it had hollowed eye sockets... When I saw what I just described, I didn't know what to do. That was until I heard it repeat what it said. It said it louder and appearingly aggressive. This time, I heard what it said, and my heart sank more than my eyes opened. It whispered my name. I ran as much as I could, I never turned back. It was like all my body senses went away and the only thing I felt was fear of letting whatever the hell that thing is catch up. When I arrived at home, I locked my door and every other door and window in my house, and stood there. My mind was in chaos. "Was I hallucinating?! Did I really need that coffee..?!" I didn't know what to do or say, so I just sat there in fear.



That morning, I was woken up in cold sweat by a sudden knock of the door, and heard the police call out. I knew it was for what happened that night, as they always come over to get more details. Usually they have a disappointed look on their face with the typical "We need to know what happened with those dumb kids" or "What did you hear them say here?". I opened the door and the officers seemed petrified, their hands were trembling, and they were struggling to speak until I asked what was going on.



The officers sat there and told me this.



"Look, I want to start off now with saying, we don't know who all of the teenagers were, one was identified with the fingerprints left on that rock but that was it... I-I don't know what to say... Something grabbed them, i-it wasn't an animal..." They then informed me I didn't need to do anything else, no review, no anything, they just turned around, and left.



I haven't had much happen since, but never had I ever been more afraid of walking. I have driven from home to work since, and whenever I do hear that glass break, it instead scares me. I don't know what took those kids, but what I didn't like most of all was how it knew who I was. It knew my god damn name, and to think this job was going to be peaceful? It became the most horrifying job to be at, knowing that at any time, that thing could come back for me. What haunts me more than anything, is the fact it could have been stalking me at the beginning. I just happened to notice it then. What if it doesn't want to hurt me? Or is waiting for the perfect time to strike? What if the only thing keeping me alive is the fact I sleep during the day, rather than sleeping during the night, where it can strike unknowingly..?



To this day, the thought of what that... THING... Could've been? It is haunting me. I have started hearing scratching on my window before I go out, just to see the window isn't even scratched when I look, or even hear something whisper silently from the forest, just for it to go silent by the time I look for it. I started closing the blinds on my windows and keeping my car windows up, I even made my car doors locked just in case it tried to open it when I was stopped. I wish that this had never happened, I wish I could still walk to the park peacefully without the constant feeling of something staring at me. Sometimes I would see animals and wonder if it is really an animal, or whatever the hell that thing was acting like an animal. I know it sounds crazy, but I'd rather have an image of what it really was than to look for it and see "Nothing there to begin with"
