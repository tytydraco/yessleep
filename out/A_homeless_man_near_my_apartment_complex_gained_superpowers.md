I live in Detroit, so obviously my area isn’t the safest. My family was relatively poor and my dad was abusive to me and my mom, so I needed to move out as soon as possible. Luckily, one of my friends’ parents owns an apartment complex and was nice enough to let me have a room for cheaper than usual.

The complex isn’t the safest, but it’s better than living with my asshole of a father. And luckily, my mom is planning on divorcing him. But, that’s not what I wanted to discuss today.

There was this meth head homeless guy that slept across the street from the building. I think his name was Jim. He was very creepy with women, especially me. I was a 19 year old girl who is relatively small in stature compared to the average woman my age, so I have to be careful on the streets. I actually was able to concealed carry firearms just recently to help protect myself from people like him.

Whenever I’d walk or drive by him, he’d cat call me and make creepy comments. I usually walked to my job as it was only a few blocks away. I worked at a bakery. So, I’d walk past him most weekdays. He’d always have a new pickup line. The day before this story happens he said: “Makes sense you work at a bakery with all that cake you have back there!” I told him to fuck off and he made a joke about how “feisty” I was. I absolutely hated him.

Then, one night, I hear a strange commotion outside. It sounded like two men yelling at each other. I was playing games on my PC with my headphones in, so they had to be pretty loud for me to hear it. I looked at the clock, and it was 11:30 at night. I had a habit of staying up late playing Marvel Rivals and other similar games. I was mid-match, so I couldn’t really check what was going on. I just thought that a drug deal had gone wrong and the guys were arguing.

Then I heard one of the guys scream, like really scream. As if he was in pure agony. I told my teammates in chat that I was sorry, but I had to go. I threw off my headphones and walked over to my window to see if I could catch a glimpse as to what’s happening. There could be some sort of physical fight going on.

When I pulled the curtains back, I saw that same homeless guy, Jim, kneeling on the ground next to a man lying on the ground. There was blood around him. It looked like he was holding a sharp object.

I quickly pulled my phone from my pocket and dialed 911. While the line was ringing, I saw Jim grab something from the hand of the guy he’d stabbed, a syringe. There was some kind of yellow liquid in it, it almost looked like it was glowing. He jammed it into his arm and pushed the liquid into his body. He let out a steam whilst tilting his head back.

Just as he did this, the dispatcher on the line said the classic: “911, what’s your emergency?” I explained to the dispatcher what had happened and what is currently happening. She told me to stay calm and that she’ll be sending officers to my location. She asked if I wanted her to stay on the line, but I said she can go. It’s best that she is able to answer every call she gets, with this being a large, and somewhat crime ridden city.

Outside, Jim was still just kneeling there, but he wasn’t screaming anymore. He was twitching and throbbing. I could only see some of his side profile and the back of his head, but it looked like his mouth was moving. Maybe he was mumbling or just mouthing the words. I just kept watching him curiously.

Suddenly, he stopped moving. He started to stand up from what I could see, but I soon realized that he wasn’t standing, he was starting to levitate off the ground. I gasped.

And as I did so, his whole body quickly turned in my direction. Almost in an instant. His eyes were emanating this yellow light, like the liquid that was in the syringe. He was levitating, but not like that of Superman, his body almost slumped. His legs and arms were just loosely dangling and his back was slouched. I could see faint crackling, yellow light moving across his body. Almost like lightning. The little lightning bolts would burn his clothing slightly. The old, grey hoodie and his tattered,  jeans had many lightning shaped burn marks across them. This lightning probably burned him, but he either didn’t notice, or it didn’t hurt him. His mouth was open and it looked like he was panting. He twisted his open mouth into a bit of a smile, still panting.

After seeing this, I slowly started to dial 911 again.  The dial tone started. I also started slowly backing up away from the window. I was terrified. I ended up backing up so much that he was no longer in view.

The dispatcher picked up and before she could even say anything I interrupted her and said: “It’s me again, get them here quick, there is something wrong and I feel like I’m in danger.”

Before the dispatcher could ask what exactly was wrong, Jim appeared in a flash right in front of the window. He was laughing now. I could hear him. I screamed, but before I could even move, he burst through the window and grabbed me by the neck. He pressed me against the wall. My feet couldn’t touch the floor and I couldn’t move. I’d dropped the phone when he did that and I couldn’t see where it had went.

“Not so feisty now?” He said as I tried to pry his grip off me.

Then, he started tearing off my clothes with his free hand. I tried to scream, but I physically couldn’t with him choking me like that. I was terrified. He took off my top and grabbed my breasts before continuing. And when he’d stripped me fully naked, he started taking off his pants. I tried to yell again but I still couldn’t. Then, I could hear the police sirens approaching. His eyes widened when he heard them and he dropped me to the floor before flying back outside.

I took in a deep breath before trying to cover myself up. I grabbed a blanket and wrapped it around me before rushing to the window to see what was happening.

Jim was floating above three cop cars. I could faintly hear one of the officers calling in for backup. Then one yelled: “Get to the ground and surrender or we will open fire!”

Jim just stayed there. He put his hands on his hips and didn’t budge. Then, the officers shot at him. The bullets seemed to just bounce off him. Then, light shot out from his eyes and one of the cars exploded. The officers were launched in multiple directions while yelling and screaming.

I watched in terror as he turned back to me. I was about to run when he was hit with something that exploded. A missile maybe. And when the smoke cleared, I could still see his glowing eyes, looking right at me.

Nothing could kill this man.

Then, suddenly, a ginormous net was thrown over him. It must’ve been heavy since he fell to the ground. I heard a loud clang, like the net was made of metal.

Then, I heard someone break my door down. It was an officer. He helped me out of the building. They drove me to the station and gave me new clothes.

I had to stay in the station for a few days. They asked me many questions about what happened and what he did to me. I also asked them many questions about how he was able to do any of that.

They explained to me that the government had been trying to make a drug that could give people super human abilities. They’d also found out that this drug gives you a very good high. It was very addictive. All of their subjects had been one hooked. And without it, they suffered such bad withdrawals that they died only days later.

One of the scientists stole some of the drug and decided to sell it for a huge profit. He’d attempted to sell it to Jim. After explaining what the drug does to him, Jim desperately wanted it. The scientist wanted a hefty price and Jim didn’t want to pay it. So, Jim killed him and took the drug.

They explained to me that Jim was now dead and he’d never hurt me again. They asked me to sign an NDA and I did. They paid me a large sum, and apparently, they did the same for other residents and my friends’ dad. My NDA expired this year. And I decided to share this story. Be careful out here folks, and don’t take random, glowing drugs.
