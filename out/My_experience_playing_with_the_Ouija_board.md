Since we met, we’ve always enjoyed telling ghost stories. Matter of fact, that was the thing we bonded over. As children, we’d stay up late into the night at sleepovers and share scary stories. I could’ve written a book with all the tales I would tell her and her brother and sister. The satisfaction I’d get watching as they hung onto my every word, the fear in their eyes.

As we got older, it evolved into watching YouTube videos of paranormal activity and bizarre unexplained phenomena. Then we’d go off and do our own ghost hunting. I remember they lived on the second floor in the rear apartment of an old two-flat building in Chicago’s Back of the Yards neighborhood. Now it was her turn to tell me ghost stories. Only this time, hers were for real.

They always involved the room at the very back of the house. A room too small to fit a bed, so they housed the family computer in it. That’s where we’d spend most of our time scouring YouTube for the scariest videos we could find. She always talked about how she’d hear voices. The hiss of whispers in her ears when she was alone. Even her mom claimed to have heard strange voices coming from the rear room.

So whenever I’d come over to hang out, the stories they’d tell would linger in my mind, causing me to keep an ear open and an eye looking over my shoulder at all times.

For the most part, I never experienced anything. Even after all the scary videos we’d watch together, I was yet to witness anything for myself. That was until one night in the summer of 2005. She invited me over after school. We attended the same high school together. Usually her brother and sister were around to join the spooky fun, but I think this was a Friday so they must’ve been off with their friends this time around.

It was only the two of us. That’s when my friend showed me she had purchased a Ouija board. It was one of those glow in the dark boards you could buy at Toys R Us. We always talked about how cool it would be to play with one, so to actually have it to experiment with had me so excited. This was before cellphones were in everyone’s pocket, so during our ghost hunting we’d usually film using her Canon digital camera or her voice memo recorder. This night we decided to go with the voice recorder.

It was a warm day in Chicago, highs in the 80s. When we got into the room, I remember it was stuffy and humid, so my friend cracked open a window to let in a nonexistent breeze. We cleared a small table and set up the board with the voice recorder right next to it. The nerves started to creep in. We’d spent the evening watching our usual binge of amateur ghost encounters and bizarre unexplained phenomena, so the atmosphere was already tense and uneasy. She placed her hands on the planchette and I did the same. She explained the rules. “We trace a figure 8 three times and then ask it a question. Don’t let go until we tell the board goodbye, no matter what.”

Already my hands started to get sweaty. We made the figure 8 three times like she said and settled the planchette in the middle of the board with both our hands resting on it lightly. “Go ahead, ask it something.” She stared at me with a slight smirk; I could tell she was nervous. “Ok. Is anyone here in this room with us?” The silence was deafening. I remember being able to hear my heart thudding in my chest. Nothing. “Is anybody here? Talk to us.” We sat for a minute, but nothing happened. My hands were super wet from my nerves and holding onto the plastic planchette that slightly glowed in the dim light didn’t help. That’s when I removed my hands to wipe them on my pants and it happened. I’ll never forget.

My friend sat there, still as ever, hands never leaving the triangle planchette. Her hair hung over her face, casting a shadow so you barely could see her. She spoke. Even with her right in front of me, I couldn’t make out what she said. “What?” I asked. She spoke again, but now I could make out just gibberish in this throaty gurgle of a voice. I started to get spooked. I called her name, “I don’t understand what you’re saying, stop playing.” All of a sudden I felt the coldest breeze, like ice, fill the room. Then these loud cracks from all the corners of the room, similar to when a house settles in the quiet of the night. Except I’d never heard it so loud and all around the entire room at once before.

I freaked out and jumped out of the chair. “What the fuck is going on!?” I called her name again. “Stop fucking playing. I’m scared.” She finally came to. “Say goodbye. You have to say goodbye,” she told me. I grabbed the planchette and we pulled it down towards where it had Goodbye written at the bottom of the board. I remember panicking, my eyes filling with tears. “I’m done, dude, I’m not playing with that shit,” I professed. “What happened!? Are you good?” I couldn’t even steady my hands; they were shaking so bad. “What were you saying? You sounded weird and I couldn’t understand anything you were saying.” She just stared at me, puzzled. “I was just asking the question, is anyone here with us.”

We talked about what had just happened. She corroborated my experience and said the room did get cold all of a sudden and she did hear the walls start to snap and creak. She didn’t have much to say about her sudden ability to talk In tongues. All I know is that she was serious when she said she wasn’t playing around. The only thing she asked was the question. I had enough. It was late. I headed home. It was the most frightening walk back to my house I’ve ever experienced.

Later the next week I remember catching her after lunch. She invited me to come by her place. That’s when she reminded me of the voice memo recorder we had set up during the Ouija board session. I forgot all about it. She handed it to me, “you have to listen to this.”  An unsettling feeling started to grow in the pit of my stomach. Memories of that night hit me like a ton of bricks.

Reluctantly, I found a spot to sit down and connected a pair of headphones. Everything sounded normal at first. I even cracked a smile at the part where you could hear us giggling, all nervous, setting up the board. And then the part where she started asking questions came. And that’s when you heard it.

She wasn’t lying. She really did keep asking the same question over and over. Never altering her voice once. But something was responding each time she asked. I could only describe the voice as sounding like Optimus Prime from Transformers. Less robotic, more disembodied. I played it back again and again, trying to decipher what it was saying. And then it clicked. My heart started beating fast. It said, “I’m here with you….”
