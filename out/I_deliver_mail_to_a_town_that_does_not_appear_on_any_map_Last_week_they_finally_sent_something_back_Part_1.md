I work for a courier company. It is not exciting. Most days are highways, convenience stores and little towns that look the same after a while. I usually do not even remember where I have been by the time I get home. Eight months ago a new address started appearing on my route sheets. There was no city listed and no ZIP code. Just "Valewick". Route 17 North."

I thought it was some computer error until my supervisor glanced at the paper and told me to follow Route 17 until I see the sign. When I asked him what sign he meant he just said, "You will know."

forty minutes later after my GPS lost signal I saw a green sign beside the road. It read VALEWICK, Population 214. I almost drove past it before something seemed odd. There were no posts holding it up. No support beams. Nothing. The sign just stood upright in the grass.

Past the sign was a town. It looked ordinary. There was a diner, a grocery store and neat rows of houses. People waved as I drove by. They smiled when I handed them their packages.

Nobody said a word.

At first I thought they just did not like strangers.. After several deliveries I realized nobody spoke at all. They smiled. They nodded. They waved. Not one of them ever made a sound.

On my visit I asked the woman working at the diner if everyone in town had taken some kind of vow of silence. She. Pointed toward the clock hanging above the register. I looked up.

It read 4:18.

When I looked down she was gone.

The kitchen was empty. The booths were empty. The coffee she had poured seconds earlier still steamed on the counter. Outside every person I had seen had stopped moving. A man reading a newspaper stood frozen. A child on a bicycle remained perfectly balanced. Even a dog in the street had stopped.

Every clock I could see showed 4:18.

My radio erupted in static. I got into my van. Drove. I did not stop to think. I just had to leave. I had stayed somewhere I was not supposed to be.

Back at the depot I tried explaining everything to my supervisor. He did not laugh. He did not call me crazy. He just asked what time it had happened.

"Four eighteen " I told him.

He nodded slowly.

"Good " he said. "Never stay past four eighteen."

That should have been enough for me to quit. Instead I kept taking the route. Curiosity gets stronger every time you feed it. Months went by without incident. The people smiled. The town stayed silent. I finished my deliveries. Left before 4:18 every time.

Until Thursday.

There was a package waiting for me.

No label.

No postage.

Just my name written across the top.

Everyone in town was staring at me. Not smiling. Not moving. Just watching.

The woman from the diner stood outside. Pointed toward the clock. It read 4:12.

Inside the package was a photograph of me standing beside my van. The timestamp in the corner said June 18 2027. Four eighteen in the afternoon.

Another photograph showed me entering the diner.

Another showed me running.

Every picture had the timestamp.

In every image something stood behind me.

Not the townspeople.

Something taller.

Something blurred.

The final photograph had been taken inside my van. I was driving. Something enormous occupied the passenger seat. Its head nearly touched the roof. Its face was hidden.

Written neatly on the back were three words.

You left early.

I looked up from the photograph. Realized every person in town was still staring at me. The clock above the diner read 4:17.

Then every clock in town changed together.

4:18.

At the moment the streetlights shut off.

Something below the ground knocked.

Once.

The windows rattled.

Twice.

The silverware inside the diner shook.

Three times.

Everyone smiled again.

They were not smiling at me.

They were smiling at something behind me.

I do not remember reaching the highway. I do not remember the drive home. I only remember hearing breathing from the passenger seat beside me.

When I told my supervisor what had happened he looked frightened.

"Did you open the package before four eighteen?" he asked.

I told him I had.

He sat down without saying anything for a minute.

Then he whispered, "They have never sent anyone back before."

Yesterday morning I found another package waiting on my porch. I have not opened it yet. Something shifts inside every few hours. This morning I noticed my microwave clock had stopped displaying the time.

It still works.

Every time I look at it it says 4:18.

Ten minutes ago while writing this I heard three slow knocks against the window, beside my desk.

Not the front door.

The window.

I swear there was breathing on the other side.

Patient.

Waiting.

Like it already knows my schedule.
