I'm an avid hiker. Me and the group of friends do these hikes where we just disappear in forests for multiple days at a time. The mountains can be dangerous in my country; you need to be aware of environmental danger, animals, even humans. There is a big population of Roma people living in the hills that can be, how to say that, less than friendly.  
  
Lots of times you can sleep in these small cabins for free that are often lockable from the inside for extra safety, but when you don't find one, we found out that the safest way is sleeping on the overlook towers. Mountains are full of such places. Made with really steep stairs at the entrance, they are no problem for a healthy person to ascend, but animals don't even try.  
  
That night we camped in such a place. A clearing at the top of a mountain with a gorgeous view into a valley below. There is a city in the valley, the lights of which create a stunning backdrop.  
  
It was a simple camping spot sitting just on the trail, with one path splitting and going down from the mountain. A fireplace, a small shelter, just a roof without walls, and a three-storey overlook tower, nothing special. For the reasons mentioned earlier, we chose the tower as our base.  
  
After a dinner, we set up sleeping mats, doused the fire and, one by one, started to drift off. It was only 10 p.m., but you tend to be drained after a whole day of walking, carrying a heavy bag. I wasn't really tired yet, so I just stood there for another hour looking at the stars and enjoying the scenery that this remote vantage point provided. Around 11:15 p.m., I finally crawled into a sleeping bag strategically placed at the side so I would have a good view of the sunrise.  
  
The forest has this familiar ambience at night. Mostly you hear crickets, owls, wind tickling the trees, a dog barking in the distance sometimes, and this loud white noise caused by the movement of large amounts of air or maybe a cars in distance.  
  
Sleep was settling in when I noticed the ominous absence of a usual forest cacophony. The only sound still present was that of the wind. I could have dismissed it as normal, but curiosity got the better of me. Peeking through the railing, I noticed a shadow at the very edge of the clearing, where the path we came from lead. It was moving. Growing in size as it was moving towards us. I wanted to warn the others about the potential danger, until it approached the shelter close to the tower. Then I realized the silhouette is tall. Like, really tall, almost as tall as the shelter, which could have been over 3 meters at its tallest point. It moved on two legs, but with real effort, it seemed. Like its knee joints weren't mobile, so instead of smooth movement, it just jerked them aggressively as it was advancing down the path.  
  
I froze. The fear of being noticed forced me to hide. Slowly, I increased the distance between me and the edge of the platform we were sleeping on. Unfortunately, you can't shut your ears, so I just listened. Adrenaline pumping through my veins made my hearing uncomfortably sharp. Each step it made was like an earthquake in my head. Every compression of dirt, each broken stick, every single displaced rock finding its new place under its soles, I registered as if it was happening right next to my head.  
  
I was ready to jump up and scream in a second, when the sound of steps stopped. Or even worse, if I heard the screech of the wooden stairs leading to the first floor.  
Nothing... it didn't happen. The steps grew more and more quiet as the regular forest ambience settled in. I collected some courage and peeked through the railing one more time. It took the path down the mountain and slowly disappeared into the shadow of the conifer woods.  
  
Don't need to mention that I didn't sleep at all that night, expecting its return. In the morning, I would almost think it was a dream or some kind of weird hallucination, if it wasn't for the stench. The whole campsite reeked with a sickly sweet smell of rotting meat that wasn't there the day before. We skipped breakfast; nobody had an appetite with such a delightful aroma surrounding us. As quickly as I could, I left the spot and didn't look back.  
  
I never told any of my friends. They would just think I am crazy. 

Research of local folklore and search for similar creatures in woods of central Europe yielded no results.  
Until this day, I have no idea what kind of creature I encountered that night.
