It's 1PM on a Wednesday. I didn't go to work. I'm sitting in my parent's living room. I was just evicted from my house a few hours ago, after it dug itself into the depths of the earth. The police just left after asking us a few questions, and mentioned something about a different investigation team coming by soon for more questions.

A few months ago, I moved into a new house. I found two roommates to share the place with, but no one I knew very well. We all did our own thing, mind our own business--I mostly kept things to myself.

It was a medium sized house: three bedrooms, two bathrooms, a fairly big living space and a nice kitchen. It was placed in the middle of suburban life, among other same-sized houses. It was surprisingly cheap for a newly furnished house, but we were going to take what we could get. It seemed like a good deal at the time. After years of moving between apartments and college dorms, I'd thought I had finally found a place to stay for a while.

Until small things started to morph.

First, it was a bar of hand soap. I bought my favorite scent, milk and honey. Corny, I know, but it's a nice, warm smell. Slowly, it shifted to a different smell... sulfur-ish, still warm but foul and sour. I felt like I wasn't washing my hands with soap anymore. It was more akin to a bar of bacteria. Does soap expire? I checked the package all over and couldn't find a date. I chalked it up to my roommates sticking the bar somewhere they shouldn't have. 

Then it was the cranberry juice. No longer did it taste tart and fruity, but rather metallic and coppery. And the color changed, too. It was more opaque and orange-red. Did this expire too? I just bought it... but it was practically inedible. Another grocery wasted.

I should have moved out as soon as I saw a blue, bumpy line on the bathtub faucet.

At first, I thought it was some hair dye a roommate spilled, or maybe a painting mishap. Like I said, I don't know what these people do for a living. I grabbed the nearest sponge and started scrubbing, but the blue line didn't fade or wash away. It bent inward with a squish. *What the fuck?* I gave it a firm stroke and flinched back immediately. It was fleshy... and I swear I could feel it pulsate. My stomach churned a little. No, it must be some kind of weird mold. I messaged the landlord in hopes to figure this out.

A day passes. No response from the landlord yet. I didn't want to step foot into that bathroom for a while, so the downstairs bathroom will have to do. 

One morning I was preparing a meal before work. Just my usual--turkey and cheese sandwich with spicy mustard, and I use a plate to prep it on. These are plates that I bought and I know that they're completely ceramic, just your standard market store supplies, but today they felt dry and brittle. A little bit of mustard fell off the sandwich and stuck right to the dish. Not just stuck -- it sank into its pours. I scrubbed the plate as much as I could but the stain wouldn't budge. I googled if the glaze on ceramic plates wash off after a few cycles, but there was no evidence of that being the case. I decided to survive on plastic plates from now on. It was less dish washing, anyway.

A week has passed since I contacted my landlord. Still no response. The blue mark on the bathtub grew longer, one end reaching the bottom of the tub and the other touching the faucet handles. I asked my roommates if they heard anything from the landlord, but they had the same answers. They also sent in maintenance orders for the apartment, all different things happening in their rooms -- a strange, white substance leaking from her radiator; the paint chipping off someone's room walls, revealing a grey, skin-like texture underneath; and the weirdest one of all, someone found an eyeball growing out the corner of a window. At first, they told me, they thought it was something left on the window sill from a kid, some kind of prank in the fall season, but when they went to touch it, it was wet and slimy, and picking it up proved itself. A red veiny cord attached the body part to the window's seam.

I grew sicker the more I had to listen to these discoveries. I decided to take matters into my own hands.

I dug around my room for my tool box, and wielded a bench scraper. I went to the first bathroom and got to work trying to scrape away the horrid blue line on the bathtub, jabbing it first with the edge of the blade.

I was hit in the face by a red spray. A stream of dark fluid shot out from the bumpy marking, pooling to the bath tub's base. What the actual fuck? I wiped the stuff from my eyes, hoping my blurry vision was tricking me. But the smell and the color... I touched the liquid and it was warm. My bath tub was bleeding.

In my shock, the stream of blood started to sputter and spray, making a huge mess on the tiled walls. I grabbed some duct tape from my tool box, but hesitated. Is this thing organic? Does it need a first-aid kit instead? *Can bathrooms get infected?* Ditching the duct tape, I fetched a large bandage and gauze from the first-aid kit and patched up the injured bath tub as best as I could. I even held my hand down on the wound for 10 minutes to stop the bleeding, if that's what all this really is.

One of my roommates walked in confused. "Dude, what did you do? Are you hurt?"

I looked at him over my shoulder, still keeping pressure on the tub. "The landlord hasn't been answering us... I took initiative, I guess. Something's really wrong with the bath tub." I lifted my hand to show him the mess.

He leaned in and took a good look, "Weird... Do you think it's rusty water? Maybe you cut a pipe or whatever. We should call a plumber."

I shot him a funny look, desperate for him to see what I'm seeing, "This is clearly blood. There's blood coming out of our bath tub's *walls*."

"You're tripping," He said, "That's not possible. It’s gotta be some neighboring pipes. Didn’t the landlord mention there was some red clay under the house’s foundation?” 

He’s joking, right? The landlord didn’t say shit about clay. But the benefit of the doubt felt a lot better than some crushing reality of a living bathroom. “Ok whatever, say it’s red clay. This could seriously mess up the rest of our pipes either way. Can you just get a plumber on the line?”

A plumber came by about half an hour later. He patched the hole and claimed it was a broken pipe with a blue lining, and the water was a sewage line. All my years of living in apartments, this still didn’t make any sense to me. When I tried to press him further, he looked at his phone and said he had to go to his next client. Whatever. None of this sat right with me… but fuck me if I had to keep dealing with it on top of working full time. I pushed it for a weekend problem.

The next few days were uneventful, aside from the smells and weird memories. I got so buried at work I barely noticed how wet my bedroom walls became. One night I woke up to a poster falling on my face. The edges were limp and dissolving, and there were tears from where it lost strength between the pins and itself. 

At this point, it might be important to mention I work at a clinic. I run hundreds of lab tests and take samples from patients all day. I know the smell of blood, the familiar texture of flesh and skin. The way this wetness stained my posters and corroded them sent warning signals to my brain. That day at the clinic, I “borrowed” a few petri dishes and swap sets, took a few samples, and brought it back to work the following day for testing. 

Stomach acid. My walls were emitting human stomach acid. Or, close to human.

Surely there must have been an error, I told myself. I ran the test again -- same results. I did the test two more times. Hydrochloric acid, potassium chloride, digestive enzymes. 

I wracked my brain for hours that night, scrubbed through countless articles of anything close to my case. Acid from my walls? Melting my posters and shelves? No one else had this problem. 

The following weeks were filled with desperate attempts to reach my landlord. My roommates became more and more dismissive of the situation. “Someone’s pranking us”, “It’s just the summer heat”, “You’re seeing things”. Did they forget their rooms were falling apart, too? They were practically delusional. It was concerning.

One day I found a roomie face down on the living room carpet. Arms to the side, nose in the fur. I ran over and checked her pulse, and she turned her head to me, an unsettling yet calm smile on her face. Her mouth was covered in a yellowish fluid.

“This is the best house I’ve ever lived in. So much better than those rickety old houses. This place has good bones.”

I stared with concern. “Dude, what is on your mouth?”

She ignored me, and repeated, “This place has good bones. Good bones. My home. I’ll never leave.”

Calling the police was tempting. But the strange shape in the carpet made me snap. It was a carpet-fur covered tit, with lips in place of the nipple. The corners of the lips oozed that yellowy milky fluid that reflected on my roommate’s mouth. 

I ran. I left for my parents’ house that night. They comforted me, told me to call the police, but a pit in my stomach told me that would be useless. Dreadfully I forgot to pack my night medication, and suffered the withdrawal symptoms. I had vivid nightmares that night, of my roommates falling apart like candles in an oven, their skin sloughing off their bodies, but they had these eerily content smiles as they disintegrated to sludge. I saw myself having the same fate.

Reluctantly I had to go back. My roommate seemed to act normal. I tried asking her about the carpet but she denied all of it. “You were probably seeing shit,” she said, “Anti-psychotics can do that to you. Maybe you should just stop taking that poison.”

I never discussed my medications with my roommates.

Despite everything, I still had work in the morning. My room was still wet, but I used a few towels to dry the place out. I took my meds, tuned into a “night sounds” playlist, and went to sleep.

I woke up at 3:00AM to the feeling of my bed melting. The sheets were coating me like icing on a cake. I sunk into the meat of my bed halfway, making it feel like quicksand. I flailed and thrusted myself out of the mess, stepping onto my now squishy, uneven floor. It bent beneath my feet, feeling tender and muscular. 

In a half-awake state I was panicking and running to the bathroom, trying to get the bedsheet sludge off my body. This is the same bathroom with the blue bulge in the tub -- now the room looked like something out of a David Cronenberg movie. The room stunk with a pungent smell, the walls were pink and glistening, and everything breathed in and out like lungs. There was a visible vapor in the air, greenish in color. The fact that I felt everything told me I was not dreaming.

I toured the entire house. Everything was breathing, pulsating, convulsing, twitching. Branches of veins and arteries traveled from ceiling to wall to floor. Bile-like fluid flowed like tiny rivers between creases, pooling in corners. It was coming from the kitchen sink, the basin overflowing. There was an umbilical cord streaming from the couch to the TV. An eyeball stared at me from the ceiling lamp.

It took a burning sensation from my feet to snap me out of it.

I was standing in the belly of a beast, and I was being digested.

I salvaged what I could from my room -- anything that wasn’t already a pile of slop. My computer barely survived, along with some clothes, my meds, and some other essentials, but they were all damaged, all wet with whatever fluids this house secreted. I knocked loudly on the doors of my roommates, urging them to wake up, move, *say something please* \-- no responses, and their doors were locked. Fuck, what could I have even done? I should have done more. I should have. I only thought of myself. The guilt will eat me alive every day.

Getting out was no joke. The door was welded shut with what looked like blood clots. I found the nearest sharp object, a bone shard sticking out of the wall, and cut my way out. I was numb to the blood that bathed me in my panic.

The moment I step out of that house, the moment I turn around to look at it, it morphs and rotates. Have you ever seen an alligator catch prey in the water, and it spins violently in place? This entire house turned into a violent creature. A big, yellow eye stares at me as the beast burrows its way down the foundation, into the ground, leaving a massive sinkhole.

I sat on the sidewalk for hours. I watched the sun rise over the trees in the distance, the sunlight washing over the hole where my home once was. I held my legs tight to my body. I held my eyes shut, hoping I would wake up in bed the next time I opened them.

Around 6AM, I woke up. The sun cooked the blood and viscera on me, leaving a brown crust on my skin. My phone was functional enough to call the police, then my folks. First it was police cars, then it was unlabeled black vans that surrounded the scene. My parents held me tight, beaming that parental fear in their eyes that they were close to losing their child that day. 

There was no trace of my roommates, or anything really. Investigators told me they’ll give me any important details, but I doubt I’ll be given too much. If anyone has open spaces for rent in the \_\_\_\_ area, DM me as soon as possible.

.
