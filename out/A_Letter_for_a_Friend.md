Dear Friend,

I don’t know why I’m writing this but all I have is my phone. Why would you do this to me?

You said it was a game. You said people online had done it a hundred times and the rules were always the same: draw the circle, light the candles, say the name only once, one person must be in the mirror at all times during the summoning, and when the sun comes up it goes back. You promised me, promised, that if anything went wrong, we’d face it together. That was the whole point, right? Two reflections. Two anchors. Two friends making sure neither of us was alone in the glass. You gave me a whole SPEECH!

The first night, it only watched us.

I could feel it before we saw it. That pressure behind the eyes, like standing too close to a ledge or something. When I finally looked straight at the mirror at first, there was nothing there except us, tired, excited, stupid. But every time I glanced away, something moved. Not in the mirror. On the edge of it. The place the mind forgets to pay attention to or something. You laughed when I told you. Said that was just adrenaline. Said “Demons don’t do jump scares”. Then it came out and sat down in front of us. You didn't say a word.

The sun came up, remember? It didn’t leave, and it still hasn't left.

You remember the rule, as long as one of us stayed reflected, it couldn’t cross over. It couldn’t be anywhere except the glass. So we took turns. Hours at a time. Staring. You told me we would be fine, that it wasn't a full day. By day it's still quiet. Almost polite. Just standing there behind my shoulder, pretending to be my posture, my outline, my shadow stitched on wrong.

At night it learned things, it knows I'm scared.

It learned my voice first. Then yours. Then it started using neither. It spoke in memories. In thoughts I didn’t finish thinking. It hurt me without touching me—teeth behind my eyes, fingers under my skin that only existed when I wasn’t looking straight at the mirror.
You said you were going out for supplies before night hit. Water, food.,maybe sage, like that would help now. You didn’t even hesitate when you said it. I believed you. That was two days ago.

The second night alone is when it stopped pretending to be subtle. It figured out I'm alone. It figured out how to drag time out when I was tired. It showed me things happening behind me that weren’t there when I turned around and punished me for turning.

I now understand the story.
That it lives in the flicker you see in the mirror.
That quick movement you catch when you look away.
That’s not imagination. 
That’s it repositioning.
Tonight, I’m going to break the rule, I have to.

I can’t do this alone anymore. I was never supposed to. You knew that. You said you knew that. Friends don’t leave each other staring into hell with burning eyes and shaking hands. Friends don’t say “I’ll be right back” and really mean "Never". If you get this, if you ever come back, even though I hate you, don’t come back to the apartment. Don’t look for me. Don’t look in the mirror. Because once there’s no reflection holding it, it won’t need the glass anymore. If you see something move in the corner of your eye tonight, don’t turn your head.

It knows you. Remember when it comes for you, you wanted to play this game. 

Sincerely, 
your former friend, 
the man you left in the mirror.
