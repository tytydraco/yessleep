Hello, I’m writing this in nothing but my unwashed underwear. Because it’s true, my washing machine is eating my clothes. You know when you lose a sock or a shirt that you could have sworn you washed, but end up shrugging it off because you figure, “It will turn up eventually!” Then it doesn’t?

Because that's happened to me, more times than I’d like to shamefully admit. This started about two weeks ago when my roommate, Ben, moved in across the hall of my apartment complex. I get pretty nervous when meeting people, but I like being in people’s good graces. The day he arrived, I bought a box of those sugar cookies that turn into a dense sand while you chew on them, and brought them to his door. I wasn’t sure if he was home or not, so I left a note and placed it on the hallway shelf.

“Hiya, I’m your neighbor Harry across the way. Hope we can get along! Let me know if you need any help moving in, or have any questions. Nice to meet you!”

Then, I went back to my room and went right to sleep. I must’ve missed him when I went to work in the morning, because I noticed the cookies were gone. I thought it was a little rude that he didn’t put a thank you note, but I figured he got distracted or was shy or something, and just put it past me. It’s not easy coming to a new place, new people, so I’d give him the proper time to adjust.

After a casual day of taking blood donations, packing up bags, and arranging shipments out to some hospitals, I needed to blow off some steam. So I changed into a tanktop and shorts, and got off to the gym. I hit a sick pump in my chest and arms, got my muscles all sore, and went back home. I started my weekly load of laundry in the washing machine, and hopped in the shower.

I could hear the clanging sound that I was all too familiar with until it kicked into its drying cycle. I drowned it out with the soft white noise of the shower. Cleaned up my body until all the sweat was gone. Then, I stepped out of the shower, and began my nightly routine before bed. Brushed my teeth after eating some reheated leftovers, and went to get my laundry. 

I collected my laundry and I immediately knew something was off. The basket was much lighter than it was supposed to be. If I didn’t know better, I would have guessed I was missing a third of the clothes I put in there. Decidedly, I chose to believe I was just hallucinating as a result of tiredness and an overactive imagination. I should have realized it was actually the evil ass possessed water machine, but we all have regrets.

  


The next week went as usual, except for the occasional glitch in the oven, where it would randomly set the temperature to an undesired number or turn it off altogether. Even though I was certain I was doing it properly. Just as well, the microwave started spinning the other direction, and pressing the ‘add 30 seconds’ button would cause the ceiling fan to turn on. I thought if I told my landlord about it, he would blame me and give out a fine. I figured I could deal with what is at most, minor inconveniences.

Laundry day came earlier, because I was already missing some clothes. I was still hopeful they would turn up somewhere, despite the unlikelihood of any of this. So I popped in a full load of laundry, dealing with my oven’s new quirks while it ran. Eventually, I gave up on getting a frozen pizza to cook and called in a pizza from Capriotti’s. It was expensive, but worth it. 

I headed down to grab my dinner when it arrived, and when I got back to my apartment, the laundry was done. This time I knew something terrible had happened. Over half of my clothes were just gone. “What the hell?” I realized, this all started when I tried greeting Ben. He must have been responsible for all of my problems.   
  
“Hey! Open the damn door! I know what you’re doing! How did you even curse my washing machine!?”  
Despite my desperate attempts to get their attention, I was met with only silence.

I haven’t been able to buy more clothes. Too many subscriptions charged at the worst possible time. All of the clothes I had left, I wore twice before I finally threw in the towel. That went in the washer too. I washed the entire wash cycle, the whole 50 minutes. It was boring as hell. When it hit it’s little jingle, switching into it’s dry cycle, there was an unusual whirring. Then the clothes were gone. All of them. I don’t know where or how or even why, but I blame Ben.

I called the cops, putting in a false report that he was trying to commit unaliving. I watched through the peephole when they finally came. I saw them knock on the door. No response. They called out, “It’s the police, Come on Ben. Don’t do this”. No response again. They got the landlord to open it for him.

The first thing they noticed was the stench. The distinct smell of death and rot. There were dozens of flies buzzing around, eating Ben’s eyes and laying their eggs in the upturned box of stale cookies. He choked on one of those stupid fudging cookies that I gave him. There was still leftover icing on his lips.

There was scratch marks on the door, and his neck. The only signs of his desperate struggle. It wasn’t my fault, at least I don’t think I should be held responsible. I was only trying to be nice. But now, I’m being haunted by someone with a weak throat who thought eating 4 cookies at a single time was a good idea. I’m sitting here at my desk in dirty underwear, with a ghost that’s probably laughing his ass off.  
Ill never eat another sugar cookie again. (Unless I really want one)
