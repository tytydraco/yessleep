*I hesitated to post this; it’s just… it’s too much for me to not try and tell somebody! You don’t have to believe me, but I just gotta tell someone.*

Alright… so a couple of weekends ago, my dad insisted I go with him to his hunting camp. I am NOT a hunter, in fact—my religion dictates harm to any creature is bad and reflects back on you… Anyhow, as much as I resisted, it’s my dad; and I always seem to go with whatever plans he has. 

Someone could write a whole essay on that… maybe I will, but no, not right now… too much… in my head…

Back to my story—we left in the dark PM and didn’t arrive till a bit before midnight, even this late the camp was in full swing. Men and women, kids and dogs, sitting around fires, or outside there campers.  

The cool time in the Deep South had just started, which meant there were days were fog came at right before dusk. In the distance if you got lucky and held a powerful MAGlite, you could see the creepy reflective eyes of a couple bucks and does. 

**Digressing, I know.**

When we got close to my dad’s camper, I noticed a weird veer off the dirt road, going to the right. I asked my dad what it was… he simply replied, “oh, that’s the gut pile.” To which I grunted with affirmation and we drove for what felt like a few more miles… maybe he was just driving slower than it seem… until we arrived at my dad’s “tract”. Pretty sure that’s what they called it. Pulled in and through the white, I could see, idk, a ten-point buck? 

I don’t like to hunt, but you stay around the people who do for long enough, the language becomes third nature.

We hopped out the car and he floated to the back, started to pull out his gun cases and told me to go open the camper, “key’s under a rock somewhere.” I rummaged around for a minute or so and finally found said rock, slowly opened the door. Then from behind the car, a demand.

“Hey… go to the other side.” He said. “There should be a long black plug on the ground. Make sure it’s still plugged into the wooden pole…” Still plugged in, I thought to myself, the wildlife come that close? I shook my phone light on and headed around back. A halo of sorts, enveloping my immediate vicinity. 

The thick black wire of the plug was covered in layers of fallen leaves, and upon retrieval a soft green moss. I crawled after the wire until it lead me over to the wooden utility pole. The pole was gone though, more like it was completely ripped off. 

I shone my phone light around for another minute and saw the metal box, about 5 yards away, sitting on the root of a massive pine tree. Next to it, lay the head of the plug.

“Dad,” I yelled out, which was met with a, “WHAT?” And then a long period of silence, until finally there was a shaking of the camper and a few thuds of footsteps. 

He walked around back to where I was and looked at me, “What?!” He said, in a more frustrated tone this time, much closer than he meant to come. My phone light temporarily blinded him, making him take a step back, blink some, then look to the side.

“The plug…” I shined my light down towards the pole and then out towards the pine knot, half looking at them both.

“That’s fucking weird… Lucky daddy’s an electrician and brought his quiet generator…”

He runs to the other side, while I stand vigilant, for no apparent reason. A distant foghorn voice, one of my native/indigenous spouse saying, “You know… the more you look around, the more something will start to look around for you.” I tried to brush off my nervousness by acting like a macho man, coz that’s what’s important right? I heard a crunch, aimed my flashlight upward and then back downward, “Don’t fucking acknowledge it...”

“Hey…” I heard my dad say, “Hey, grab the broken end of that wire—bring it out towards the tongue.”

I sat my phone down on the broken utility pole and I fumbled around for a moment, tripping up on a mess of covered pine knots until I got right about where I thought the end was. I reached my hand down into the leaves and grabbed the wire, something grabbed my arm and ripped it hard, downwards, until there was nothing but a spurting mess of blood and tendons, frayed and splayed out like the tassels on a dancing tube man… I screamed out, loud, and my dad rushed back around to my side. I was holding my stump of an arm, but then, it was back… simply there. Nothing gone. No harm. No foul. Nothing wrong at all… my dad scoffed, and then walked back around the front. 

Seeing that my missing arm was now holding the wire—I took a deep breath and my phone, and went back towards the tongue of the camper.

“Bad news...” My dad says. “The fridge and freezer and everything went out… I had some back strap saved for us tonight, but that won’t work now—doy!!”

He paused for a moment. “There is a little market around the corner, ‘Quik-E-Stop’…” He stops to chuckle, “yeah, I know… but they’re a fairly decent store, and he’s got some good groceries. D’ya mind taking the truck and running up there for us?”

“Uh. Yeah sure… I guess not… Where is it at though? My phone doesn’t have much service up here.”

“You ain’t gonna get any either… best part of this whole trip, not having to hear our bitches whining...”

“For real, dude?”

He guffawed and said, “What?” Shaking his head and holding his hands up in the there. “Here, take the keys. Gonna take the dirt road back into the main camp ’til you get to the ’T’. Then you gotta turn right and then it’ll be a mile, or so, on the left… Honestly, hard as hell to miss.” He hands me the keys and mumbles something like, “Good luck…” or “Oh, fuck…” 

Either way, the tone felt off, and now I was even more uneasy. I didn’t think I was gonna have to fucking drive in the middle of butt-fucked-Egypt at the witching hour and in the mist… but whelp, here I am…
