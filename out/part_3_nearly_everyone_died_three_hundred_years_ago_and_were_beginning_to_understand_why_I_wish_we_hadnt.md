My name is Chris, and I’m a researcher at the Global Coalition for Flash Research. Our job is to investigate, in every way we can, what happened on this planet three hundred years ago. Well, we know what actually happened, but not how or why it happened. And if the thing that happened is something that almost led to the extinction of the human race, that’s kind of something you want to know.

It’s a miracle that we are able to investigate it. Only 8000 people survived the Flash on that terrible day, and the first decades after the Flash were characterized by chaos, hunger and desperation. It was slowly, very slowly, that basic infrastructure was able to be restored. I think most people would never suspect we could bring up civilization again and actually research what happened in 2026. It helped that there was some genetic component to who survived, meaning that often whole families survived together. We still don’t know how that is possible, but we’re grateful for it. If it were just 8000 individuals scattered throughout the earth we’d probably all have died.  

Not that we’re back to the levels we were then. Not by a long shot. Even today we are only spread on very specific clusters of the planet. Most of earth exists of decaying cities with far too little people to populate them. But we are here, and we are finally able to attempt understanding what happened that day. We found the people who knew when the apocalypse was coming. We found the Watchers.

I remember what my first case was, five years ago. That was Case 33, a likely male and likely Dutch or Belgian person in his early thirties. Most of the cases we have are like his, people who didn’t really do anything spectacular but needed a place to express their fear. They mostly did it on anonymous places, which is unfortunate if you want to get to know more about them.  

It is very understandable though why they kept a low profile. Case 39 was just sad. Middle-aged woman, some remote small village in northern Canada. Tried to tell everyone what she saw, got ostracized by the whole town who thought she had gone insane.

Case 52 was also incredibly sad, but very useful for research purposes. I know, that sounds a bit macabre. Older lady in Singapore, jumped right from Marina Bay Sands four months before the Flash. That event got pretty widely publicized, and newspapers got to speak with family members who said that the woman ‘’was convinced she and everyone else would die in four months’’. But she didn’t die in four months, she died that very day. That means she was able to alter her own clock.

We wondered whether that could also work the other way. Imagine you’re a Watcher and you see a man next to you with 10 seconds on his clock about to cross a busy street. Can you stop him? Try to convince him it was essential he didn’t cross the street so that he would survive? We think that should be possible, if you can shorten your clock you must be able to lengthen it too. 

And so we’re getting better and better at our research. We found interesting things, such as a case from Argentina where a Watcher found a Survivor. But where it got *really* interesting was when we found reports of people who were able to manipulate their own clock. Who saw, years before the Flash, that they got only a year to live and took that as a warning to visit their doctor, only for their clocks to disappear after they did. I imagine what these people must have felt when the clocks re-appeared again one year before the Flash.

We wondered, although no one really dared to say that out loud: if you can manipulate your own clock, can you prevent death? And if so, under what circumstances would that be possible? Turns out, we weren’t the first to ask that question.

Reports from secret government services are hard to find. Don’t even ask me how our hackers were able to find the documents. Important documents, from what used to be world’s most powerful countries back then. The USA. Russia. China. I was the one to first analyze them and make a case report.

They knew. They had discovered the existence of Watchers long ago. And they’ve been experimenting with them in ways that were, well, rather unethical. But they discovered many ways to extend someone’s life. One of the final reports was extremely jubilant, noting that ‘’we are certain that death can be avoided if we wish’’. I looked at the publication date of that report. And then my world turned upside down.

2025. A year before the Flash, even to the exact day.

*Some forces are not to be tampered with.*
