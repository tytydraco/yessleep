Hey Reddit. My name is Wilhelmina Harker, Mina for short. I’ve been a graphic designer for a company working with a couple of large firms in New York, and it’s given me the ability to live comfortably. So comfortably, in fact, that when my company transitioned to a more hybrid/work from home style, I decided, screw it, and bought a house in northern New Jersey.

I’m from New Jersey originally; my parents, my grandparents, heck, I think everyone to my great-great grandparents basically grew up 50 miles away from Elizabeth, so when I had the opportunity to strike out on my own and be the New York City girl, I took it. Coming back feels like a bit of a cop out, but I’m kind of done with sirens and smelly Elmos.

When I arrived with my U-Haul, I started to unload all my stuff into neat box stacks on the front porch when one of my neighbors came to my door.

“Hi, Mina, was it?” He was somewhat tall, a bit lanky, and wore a kind but salesman like smile.

“Hi! Yes, how are you?” I shook his hand.

“So, my name is Bram, I live in that house right across the way.” He pointed to a pastel pink house with a couple of lawn gnomes. “I’m the president of the HOA, and I figured I stopped by, tell you a couple things.”

“Oh, yeah, sure.” I put down one of my boxes.

“Alright. My name is Bram Stoker, I’ve lived in this neighborhood for… oh, going on ten years now. Me and the missus came here after I retired. Now all I do is write, and all she does is get on my nerves!” He chuckled. “But yeah, lovely group of people, there’s a bunch of really great restaurants downtown, definitely try the Italian place, there’s also a church to the left of us.”

“Alright.”

“Perfect! So, to ‘lay down the law’, I’m sure you see, everyone has neat and tidy lawns, no garbage anywhere, yadda yadda, typical ‘keep the value up’ crap, haha.” He folded his arms. “Now, one of the big things is no loud noises past 9:00, all the way up to 9:00 in the morning. People wanna sleep, you know?”

“Yeah, no, I totally get it.”

“Perfect! Happy living, friendo!”

He walked away, and I resumed carrying boxes inside my house. I assembled an Ikea couch and table, helped myself to a delicious microwaveable meal, and watched videos on my phone until the sun set. I was starting to nod off, when I heard a knock on my front door.

I wiped the crust from my eyes and walked over to the door. I assumed it would be Bram. It was not. He was tall. Maybe 7 feet tall, but maybe that was just his presence. He wore a black cape with a red velvet lining, and his skin was pale. He spoke with a deep, eastern European accent. 

“Hey there, neighbor!” the monster said. “My name is Dracula, I’m sure you’ve heard of me. I’m that cute little cottage up on the hill.”

“Hey… yeah.”

“I was out for a walk, and I noticed the moving van. Welcome to the neighborhood!” He stuck a hand out for me to shake. I glanced at it, and then returned to his gaze. He looked down at his unmet palm, looked back at me, then back at the palm. “You know, it’s like, totally kind of rude to not formally greet people.”

“Uh… yeah, sure.” I reached my hand past the door, and when my fingers went past the frame, Dracula hissed, his eyes filling with a deep red bloodlust, and quickly moved his hand to grab mine. I recoiled, pulling my hand out and back into my house. “The fuck, man?!”

“Sorry, sorry, I’m very jumpy. I was out on a brisk jog.” He ran in place for a moment. “That said, you know what would be very helpful? I should have done it before I left, but I really must pee. Can I come in?”

“...no…?” I said.

“No, but like, you don’t understand, I’m like, going to totally piss myself. It’s going to be really bad.”

“Fuck no, you just tried to grab me or something, you’re not coming in my house.”

“...oh. Are you like, sure?”

“Yes. Get the fuck out of here before I call the cops.”

“Alright, alright, sheesh.” He threw up his hands. “So annoying, bleh!” He started to walk away.

I watched him as his silhouette disappeared into the night, shook my head confusedly, and then closed my door. I walked up the steps, undressed, and wrapped the blankets around myself.

When I awoke, I showered, dressed myself, and grabbed my things for my commute into the city. When I stepped outside into the bright morning sun, I saw Bram.

“Hey, so, like, this weird guy came up to my house last night.” I said.

“Weird guy… oh! Was his name Dracula?”

“Yeah.”

“Oh! Yeah, oh jeez, he totally slipped my mind; he’s a vampire.”

“...come again?”

“Yeah, in the house on the right of us.” He pointed up a large, steep hill. It seemed like, despite the cheery day, the house was cloaked with foggy weather and misery. The house itself was a manor nearly the size of a castle, with large windows, and a dreary black exterior.

“...huh.”

“Yeah, that’s Mr. Dracula’s castle. Nice fella, all things considering. Just… don’t let him in your house.”

“What… why?”

“Oh, you know, standard vampire stuff; sucks your blood, eats your flesh, makes you an undead servant, only to be freed from immortal torment when your master passes.” Bram chuckled. “Yeah, that’s why this house opened up.”

“The person that lived here got their blood sucked by Dracula?”

“Yeah, lady named Gladys. Liked her too, she had a great recipe for meringue.” He sighed.

“Oh… kay…?”

“Yeah, in the same vein, ha! Pun. Just make sure you don’t leave your house at night and go for a walk. Makes it easy for him then. Other than that, total gentleman.”

I looked warily at the manor. “Haven’t you guys… you know, tried to hire someone?”

“Oh yeah, tons. ‘My great grandpapi was a Helsing’, ‘I’ve read all about it online’, ‘the world must get rid of this evil spawn’, but like,” Bram sighed, “there’s a reason their great-great-great grandparents were vampire hunters and not… them. I saw one guy go up there with a katana and a Demon Slayer shirt. He made me chuckle. But like I said, Dracula’s very pleasant, just don’t let him in.”

“Got it. Thanks.”

After our chat, I got in my car and drove to work. I did the nine to five grind, went to a Whole Foods on the way home, and once I got there, started to unpack more of my things. This house was really starting to come together.

At around nine o’clock, there was a knock on the door again. I walked over and opened it.

“Hey neighbor, I think we got off on the wrong foot.”

I stared at him without speaking. 

“Like, I’ve been living here a long time, and I’m just super used to, you know, popping in to the boi’s homes to like, chillax and, vibe! I figured you were the same.”

I stared more.

“Anyway, so, UPS came at some point today, and they gave me a package that was addressed to you, but it’s really heavy, like, super heavy, can I come in to…”

“Listen, Bram told me about you, I know you’re a vampire.”

We stared at each other in silence. Finally, he opened his fanged mouth to speak.

“W-what? No, that’s crazy, ah ah ah…” He wiped sweat away from his forehead. “He’s like, really old. Like… 120 human years. He doesn’t know what he’s talking about, dude.”

“Yeah okay. You’re not coming inside.”

Dracula stared at me. His eyes gave a stone-like, piercing gaze. “Bro, like, not even for like, a little bit?”

I slammed the door in his face, sighed, and walked up to bed.

The next day was more of the same. Dracula at least gave me some water cooler banter while I was in the office, but other than that, the day kind of flowed into the night. And then, once again, this time at 11:00, he knocked on my door again. Except… when I got to my door, he wasn’t there. Instead, there was a basket with a note that said, “Love me!”

Inside, was a small kitten, his head tucked under the blanket, his small feline tail shaking in the air. It was cute, and after living in apartments for so long, the thought of a pet sounded nice. However….

“I’m not falling for it, Dracula.” I said. No one responded. I looked around the corners of my porch, and looked back at the cat. And then, in a deep Transylvanian accent…

“Meow.”

I stared incredulously. “Dude, did you transform into a cat?”

Silence. Then, a shaky “meow” echoed out of the basket.

“Dude, c’mon, this is like, getting weird now.”

“I am just… normal cat. Meow.”

“Get the fuck off my porch Dracula.”

“Fine, you win… I guess….” Kitten Dracula sighed. “I’m so sorry I’m being like this, your blood just smells, like, really good. Are you type O?”

“Yeah?”

“Oh, thought so! Love that shit!” He cackled. “Anyway, I’ll be back tomorrow, have a good one!”

I slammed the door again.

So, anyway Reddit, I just wanted to put this on here, first, if I go missing, and second, like, what do I do in this situation? He’s clearly not going to leave me alone, so any advice is good advice. Thanks.
