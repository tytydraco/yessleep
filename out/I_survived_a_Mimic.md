I am a 27-year-old male named Hunter, known for exploring a lot of places. Of course, if someone owns the property and is still alive, I always ask for permission before exploring.

This story takes place in July 2022, shortly after I graduated from college in May. My friends and I, who often explore abandoned buildings together, decided to explore one last time before we all got jobs and became busy adults. My friend, let’s call her Mary, started searching online for abandoned buildings. Most places we found weren’t in our state Illinois at the time but eventually, we found one near us. We had no exact address; just a picture taken by some passerby. It was located somewhere within the Chicago area, between Kankakee, the Chicago suburbs, and the city itself probably in an open field near the road and the cornfields. We drove there and finally found the house, which was hard to spot since it was nighttime.

We parked beside the house and gathered our ghost-tracking supplies, which at that time were mostly homemade because we were broke, just out of college. We used old equipment from before college or crafted new tools the night before. We entered the house the front door nearly came off its hinges, given its age. The house was likely built in the 1950s or earlier. As we looked around, it seemed to have been owned by older people. There was a low-quality TV, two rocking chairs instead of a couch, some coats, and tiny jackets. While these clues didn’t prove that older folks had lived there, they were our best guesses.

Around 11 PM., activity began to intensify. Our EMF reader started hitting high levels sometimes reaching threes or fours. We tried to pinpoint the source, sensing a presence moving around us. Tyler, my friend, began researching how such spirits behave. The EMF fluctuated in four main areas: the kitchen, the stairs, the upstairs bedroom, and the living room particularly on the left rocking chair.

As the night went on, we saw a dark silhouette moving among those areas, disappearing whenever we shined our lights on it. To our amazement, the refrigerator started opening by itself. It seemed that the longer we stayed, the more active the spirit became, moving and performing strange actions. Tyler thought it might be a "mimic," though I was too focused on watching the silhouette to be sure. Eventually, the silhouette stopped abruptly. From what we’d read online, once a mimic is observed for too long, it can realize it’s being watched and may attempt to lure or toy with intruders. Although it can’t kill, it might cause harm if provoked. The figure then moved erratically, no longer following its previous pattern, and eventually, we couldn’t see it at all.

We decided to split up, staying close. Mary went to the kitchen, I checked the hallway beneath the stairs, Tyler studied the living room, and Jerry, our third friend, took the upstairs.

In the hallway under the stairs, I discovered a basement. After examining it, I descended the stairs, brought out my phone’s flashlight, and saw several mannequins dressed in old women’s clothes outdated garments. Some mannequins stood on stands, one even had legs on a circular platform. There were also two china cabinets filled with souvenirs and other items. I noticed a slightly creepy, unlocked safe with money inside, but I left it alone—it seemed better not to anger whatever spirit might be there.

Soon, I started hearing voices one like my mom’s, and another like Mary calling me upstairs. I foolishly followed the sound. When I reached the top, Mary was still in the kitchen, but I heard her voice right next to me. I screamed, and it felt like footsteps were chasing me. I broke down the door and ran out, not knowing what might have been following me. Jerry tripped and hit his head on the porch, but he quickly got up and we all escaped.

Outside, we caught our breath. We realized we’d left our ghost tools behind, but since we wouldn’t be using them again, we decided to leave them. It wasn’t worth risking our safety. Mary started the van, and we drove home through the night, stopping at a Circle K for gas and snacks, then heading back to Bloomington.
