There I stood atop that cliff. In solitude. My back turned towards the forest as I stare out into the ocean. The scent of nature kissed my nose. Mother Earth’s divine cacophony lulled my ears. The songs of crickets, the hoots of owls, the wisps of winds, the low groans of the swaying trees, and the water colliding with the bottom of the cliff were the blessed instruments of her wondrous music.

A starry night. The sky lit by the ever so incandescent stars. Cosmic colors reflected upon the ocean's water. Two voids staring into each other's soul. Still as statues. As lively as beating hearts. Entranced, spellbound, there I stood atop that cliff.

And from the heavens it descended. Hovering above me. Barely out of reach. Its beady eyes meeting mine. The Cicada’s dark blue and green hue complemented the nature surrounding us. The buzzing of its wings mimicked the sound of the crashing waves beneath the cliff.

As slow as the sunrise the stars above began to shine brighter. Brighter and brighter they glowed. Then they started dim. Dimming torches before they are naught but cinders. But before they are snuffed they rekindle, shining again, only to dim once more moments later.

Then they vanished. I gasp at sudden total darkness. Not even the light of the moon was present. Everything fell to silence. So, so quiet. So, so lonely. I felt the wind graze my skin but yet I couldn’t hear its howl. I was shaking, too afraid to let out as much as a cry for help.

As I reach my arm to navigate through the blinding darkness, the stars reappeared. Like ghosts of the night sky. My shaking had ceased. I laid my arm down as I look in awe to the sky.

The stars began to change in color. Flashing at dizzying speeds. From blue, to yellow. From white, to green. From purple to orange. From red to pink. Violent blurs of vivid colors. Some swayed like a flame of a candle and others spun into colorful messes of spirals.

The Cicada remained in its place from before. The shimmer of the stars followed the flutter of its wings. They twitched and hissed as its body remained impossibly still. Its gaze never breaking from me.

The Cicada conducted the visual symphony of the stars. A choir made of holy flame. An orchestra of the firmament. They rejoiced in its glory. Flashing lights of hymns in appraisal of its divinity. Paeans of celestial magnificence.

Gentle water graced my cheeks. Pouring down my face, the tears crescendo at my jaw before finally crashing to the ground. My vision blurred, my cheeks scrunched, my nose stiffening. The tears intensify. I closed my eyes as the raging waters became uncontrollable. All I could see was the isolating darkness born from closed lids. It did not stop.

I use my fingers to wipe the tears.

The tears still came.

I used my palms.

The tears still came.

I used my forearms.

The tears still did not stop.

I pulled the bottom of my shirt to my eyes. Immediately it became drenched. The stream grew harsher, now spilling forth onto my legs and feet. The tears deluge the soil, irrigating the dirt. My eyes now waterfalls, I yelp in pain as the pressure grows stronger. It felt as if someone was behind my face trying to push out my eyeballs.

I was completely soaked. It was as if I was baptized in the ocean. Heavy was the weight of my impossibly sodden clothes. Nearly weighing me into the ground. My legs were shaking. My shoulders laboring to bear the burden of infinite tears. I heaved and grunted as I struggled to rip off my shirt.

When I managed to free myself, my tears had finally ceased. I begin to rise to meet the Cicada again. As I rose a deafening drone began to assault my ears. No longer did I hear the crickets, the whistling wind, the moans of trees, nor the lively waters of the ocean. The obnoxious noise drowned out nature’s song.

The buzz of the insect’s wings grew louder as they fluttered faster. With each wave sparks began to flash. Then slowly a small flame lit ablaze. Every second it grew rounder. Every second it grew brighter.

Eventually growing so far and wide the sea was no longer visible. The wall of light sizzled and sparkled. It flexed and spun. The power of the universe birthed from its wings. And from its depths was displayed infinity. Lurid lights sprang forth to life. Ivory, blue, purple, green, orange and yellow.

The sightly nepenthe was interrupted as I noticed the soil beginning to float upwards towards the insect. The dirt beneath my feet were soon to follow. It felt like the sands of a shore being dragged into the water, pulled into the ocean.

The soil was now gone. Yet I remained in place. I was now floating. My feet straight forward. The cliff now gone, the sea stared up at me. The exfoliated crust of the Earth, uprooted trees, rocks, twigs and leaves hurdle towards the colorful abyss.

I saw small flakes spiraling upwards towards the Cicada. I started to feel light headed. Suddenly a searing pain assails my arm. I look down to the sight of my left arm eroding. My skin being gently peeled off my body, ascending into the chasm. Tears fell once again but were quickly swept into the void. I am unable to scrunch my face, unable to widen my eyes or shut its lids. Unable to make as much as a grimace to the existential plight before me.

I was dissolving. My clothes, my hair, and skin were now gone. My blood followed, along with my muscles, along with my teeth. My intensities swirl upwards. Soon they were joined by my lungs and heart. Finally my bones vanished. All was gone except for my eyes. Left behind to watch in horror as its neighbors are swallowed by the infinite.

What I felt was beyond fear. Beyond terror. Beyond futility. Beyond fragility. Ineffable emotions arise. Pernicious to my sanity. Adust is all reason. Yawning the horrid truth. The frailty of reality.

My eleventh hour.

I look down to what was left of my body. I was naught but light. A star. Searing and burning. My soul naked, unbounded by flesh.

The translucent bulbous heads of jellyfish began to rise from the ocean. Slowly floating upwards, replacing the ground beneath. They ascend in unison to form stairs towards the Cicada, bridging the distance between me and the insect.

I ascend the stairway to sancity.

The droning ceased as I got closer. More jellyfish rose from the depths to form a corridor around us. From infinity to claustrophobia.

Now I was face to face with the Cicada. It was so beautiful. So gentle. Its eyes were windows to seas of black skies. Its shell was a mirror reflecting the stars. It lended its small front leg. It stretched and stretched, becoming longer than its body.

I reach out with shimmering arms, attempting to grab its leg, ready to take my place amongst the stars.

Suddenly I was of flesh and bone again, screaming and burning. I was on fire.

The crimson flame swallowed me whole. Bellowing in pain, consumed by horror, I ran frantically through the woods. I ran and ran until I knocked myself into a tree, falling onto the dirt.

I rolled in soot and mud, yelling and crying. The merciless fire burned away my tears. The pain was too great for me to bear.

Everything fell to black.

When I had finally awoken I was back at the edge of the cliff. My skin was fine, my clothes were intact, no signs of burning.

The forest was restored. I try my hardest to peer into the lightless woods. The green was sullied by the onyx shadow casted by the dark flame of starless night.

My weary eyes labored trying to adjust to the fathomless dark beyond me. I rubbed my them incessantly in hopes it would help.

A flame kindled where the Cicada once was. Its Iight revealed life in the solemn forest. Between the quiet trees was a deer. Its head planted into the dirt, bowing towards me. It was still. It didn’t move or twitch.

Then I heard them. Through the sounds of sobbing, screeching, wailing, giggling, and moaning I felt their emotions of despair, terror, anguish, joy, and pleasure. The light beamed through the darkness, finally unveiling the unknown.

They were humanoid in shape, abounded by shades of shadow. Small lumps remained where their heads should have been. They wore wide eyes on their breasts. They glimmered through the dark forest like crystals. Small noses laid in the middle of their chests. Massive mouths bearing large lips resided just above their stomachs. Their teeth were shaped that of a human's and were as white as their eyes. They were perfectly straight and impossibly large. Their arms and legs were like that of a chubby baby’s, layered in folds of fat.

Some sat at the foot of the trees playing with the dirt, others rocked back and forth. Some were bashing themselves violently and rapidly into the bark, grunting and screaming in frustration. Others smiled with childish glee, others frowned with disappointment and spleen.

The uncanny creatures bowed in harmony as the Deer raised its head from the dirt. Prostrating themselves towards the Deer, establishing his authority. He stood upright. As he rose snake's skin began to coil from his head, falling to his hooves. The absence of the discarded serpentine skin revealed its face.

It was the face of a man's. Full of scorn, wrought of rancor. Large brown eyes, a small nose, small mouth. Three moles on his left cheek. It was my face.

Dead Datura's and Belladonnas intertwined with his antlers. Sunflowers and roses bloom from his back. And as they sprang from their master, the creatures rose with the pace of the blooming flowers.

Their feet lift from the ground. They were now suspended into midair. Their sights set upon me with faces full of fear and anger. Slowly, they turn upside down in unison. The flame disappeared but still I could see them. The marred beings in shades of night flare their glistening teeth. The glowing dim of ivory fangs illuminate the darkness.

The Deer conducts its own profane song. A crude mockery of the Cicada’s.

Dry skin peels from his dirt ridden lips as he opens his mouth. He labors to inhale the cold air. Then finally letting out a long winded exhale. In a low, rumbling voice he spoke.

“Thou who wast anointed by sorrow, cursed to drown in tears sure to follow.

A crown of guilt adorns thy brow. Begotten from past sins, despicable and foul.

Behold, for I bear the face of a sinner, whose light grows dimmer. Whose embers of a dying flame fails to shimmer.

Shorn of redemption, victim of causality. We both are birthed of the Cicada’s calamity.

The calamity! O the calamity that is creation! Wrought of chaos! Bereft of cessation!”

He stretches his forelimbs out on each side. The eyes of judgement perforated my wilted soul. Long silence befell the forest.

I fall to my knees, crying. My eyes were fixated on the ground. I was too terrified to look up and face the sight that surronded me. I was too scared to close my eyes for I could not bear the dark. I felt like a little boy lost in the middle of the woods all alone. Wishing I can go home. Wishing I can wake in comforting arms. Free of fear. Free of strife.

My dry throat struggled to let out as much as a whisper. In a raspy fearful voice I begged.

“Please, spare me! Wake me from this! Free me from this! Let me go please!”

His scowl steadily falls into a remorseful frown. He lays down his arms before speaking again.

“Thou hast freed thyself through death.

Thou standst before me absent of vigor, absent of breath.

In life guilt had claimth thy heart, burdening thy breast. In death grief hath emptied thy heavy chest.”

“What is this?” I yelled. My voice breaking, cracking as tears escape my eyes. The floating creatures groan at my interjection.

“The Cicada judged and banished thee. Delivering thy flayed soul to me.

I am the one the Cicada despises and decries. The Mother of Serpents. The Father of Flies.

A Child of Spurn. The Hellfire that shall forever burn.”

He points to his disciples with his hoof before continuing.

“We are fallen stars. Once denizens of the Celestial Tar.

Banished from the skies for we sang in dissonance. We challenged the Choir of the Cicada whose melodies exalted its omnipotence.

We dreamt of liberation, we sang hymns of retaliation. But in number we were few. Our rebellion was met with annihilation.

Now we are orphans of divinity. Exiled for the orchestration of blasphemous litany.

Excommunicated from the congregation. Discarded into a mountain of fire. Fated to forever writhe in seas of damnation. In the endless pit of sulfur and ire.”

I was too afraid to answer. I couldn’t begin to process what he was saying but still he continued.

“Thou hast burned in that same flame. A fire of holy judgement, kindled by the Cicada’s reign.

We are products of rejection from the Father. From sin we bleed yet prosper.”

Still I refused to look up. I couldn’t bear to look at his face. That face. I hate it. I’ve always hated it. Looking at me with such disgust and hostility. The same hatred I felt for myself.

“Thou dost not wish to look at me, out of anger or disgrace?

Thou art afeared to remember her face?“

“What?” I ask confused. He began to walk. I hear his footsteps grow closer. Kicking through twigs and leaves. My eyes now met the deer’s cloven hooves.

“Thou dost knowst whom I speak of. The birth of thy guilt, the harbinger of thy love.

The promise thou hast failed to keep. Thy heart consumed by guilt, for eternity thou hast been cursed to weep.

Thou hast abandoned her. For so long thou hast lived in solitude, her face now a blur.

A ghost, a phantom. The pain within thee, only the grieving can fathom.”

I look up. He was smiling. A smile I haven’t seen in forever. I remembered.

He reaches out his forelimb. His hoof morphed into a hand. More hands sprang forth on each end of his antlers.

As I grab his rough hand I am taken back to the last time I smiled.

A sunny day. The blue sky was clear of clouds. The sun shined proudly, free to rain its light to the earth below. The smell of vendor food blessed my nose. It was pretty busy but that is to be expected for the fair. All I could hear was people passing me by talking and laughing, screaming from the rides and the music blaring from the ice cream stand I was standing in front of.

I was holding two cups of ice cream. One for me and someone else. On my right hand was chocolate chip, which had to have been mine because that was my favorite, and in my left hand was cup of strawberry flavored ice cream. I remembered I was waiting on someone. I felt nervous, excited and happy all at once. My blood was flowing, my heart beating, my legs felt weak. I was a wreck but I was smiling like an idiot.

Suddenly someone bumped into me from behind. Thankfully I didn’t drop the ice cream. I turned around quickly to confront whoever bumped into me but before I could apologize I saw who it was.

“Thank you!” she said with a smile as she takes the ice cream from my left hand. I forget that’s how she greets me when I’m not paying attention. Just lightly or harshly bumping my shoulder.

“Of course! Everything good though? Is he still upset?” I asked as we started to walk with no real direction or destination. I looked at her as she stared into her cup trying her best to fake a smile. “No no! Everything is fine. He attempted to apologize I guess but I hung up on him.”

That’s right. Her and her dad had frequently fought. Their last argument was nasty. It was so bad that they haven’t spoken for days. He usually took his anger out on her at home. And she was always his first target.

I can relate to that. That’s how it was with my dad. Her and I had that in common. We had this conversation countless times before. I try to console her whenever this happens but for today I didn’t want to pry and pour salt into an already festering wound. That was the whole reason we were here. To escape from home. Distance ourselves from what troubled us both.

I remained quiet as we walked. My feet were killing me from pacing through the park and standing in lines all day. I look around to find some place to sit. There were convenience stands and food stands but they were all too busy. On top of that all of the benches around here were already taken.

I then remembered from across the ice cream stand was a tree with a circular bench beneath it. We’ve been walking for a while. Now we were a bit away from the stand. Still it was the only place I could think of where we could go sit.

“Hey I just remembered, there was a tree in front of the ice cream stand, we can just sit there.” I suggested, breaking the silence between us and hopefully the noise of her somber thoughts. She was still staring into her cup, absentmindedly poking at the red ice cream with a plastic spoon.

“You wanna sit down?” She asked puzzled. “I mean yeah. We did most of the rides already and the benches are all taken. Plus it’s hot, we should get some shade ya know? It’s why we got ice cream after all” I replied, trying my best to be convincing.

“I mean I just wanted strawberry ice cream dude. It’s not that hot.” She rebutted laughing. “Okay well it’s hot for me, cmon let’s go back.” I said laughing along with her.

We back tracked to the lone tree. We take a seat in beneath the shade that sheltered us from the scorching sun. Finally eating our now partially melted ice cream. Now sitting in silence as we ate. I was thinking so hard on what to say. My heart has been pounding so fast this whole time.

I guess that is normal when someone is in love. We were teens then. I was nervous I’d make myself a fool in front of her. I became increasingly self conscious. Thinking things like is my hair bad? Am I sweating a lot? Is my face super red? Silly thoughts a teenage boy in love would have when sitting next to his crush.

I wiped my face harshly with a napkin, worried I looked like a child covered in ice cream. God, I felt embarrassed.

Finally she spoke. “Jakob?” I turn to her sitting up straight after realizing I was slouching. “Yeah?” She was still looking at the ground. “Promise me. Promise me that no matter how bad things get between us, that you will be there.” Finally she was looking at me.

I felt my face turn red. “Of course, I promise! No matter what I’ll be here, I won’t go anywhere!” I blurted eagerly trying my best to reassure her. In a soft tone fighting back tears she said “Seriously, thank you for today. I needed this. I’m so miserable at home. I can’t breathe. I look for any excuse to leave so again thank you for this.”

“I understand. You mean a lot to me. I don’t want to lose you.” My heart sank as those words leave my mouth. I was afraid of how she would respond. She suddenly lunged and gave me a hug. I felt her tears bleed through my shirt. I hugged her back, trying my hardest to fight back tears.

That promise. The promise I never kept.

We grew apart in time and it was all my fault. Eventually I confessed my love. I knew she didn’t feel the same but still I pursued. My young dumb self convincing myself that her love was the answer to my problems. But she didn’t feel the same. She couldn’t. And I didn’t understand. Defeated I pushed her away. Destroying our friendship.

In my sadness I had completely neglected her. She still lived in an abusive home. She needed someone. She needed a friend not a lover. She reached out to me still despite knowing how I felt. And like the stupid boy I was, I left her alone knowing she needed help. I abandoned her. She suffered alone.

One day she disappeared. Gone without warning. I went searching for her. For years and years I have searched. Every waking moment, every private moment was spent thinking about her. Everything I did, no matter what it was, I was thinking about her. Whenever I sang, whenever I cried, whenever I screamed I thought about her. Every time I woke up, every night before I slept, even in my dreams her memory wouldn’t let my mind rest. She was apart of everything I did. My purpose. My reality. I couldn’t let go.

I was broken hearted. I am broken hearted. Not because of rejection but because of my failure to uphold that promise. I was selfish. I was a moron. A selfish, immature moron. “If I had truly loved her I would’ve stayed as a friend” is what I told myself.

In her absence I was pushed to the brink of insanity. Tipping at the very edge. My sorrow slowly morphed into the ugly monster of obsession. The obsession to find her. To save her.

Until one day I couldn’t do it anymore. The guilt I felt from her absence was too much. A decade had passed and I never found her. I had failed. I began to forget her voice. I began to forget her face. I only remember her smile and her long brown hair. I had no pictures her. No mementos to remember her by. Just a failing memory possessed by a ghost.

I had become hollow. I had become empty. I had let go. I had no will to go on for I have accepted that she was gone. My reality had been collapsing and this was my tipping point.

I never admitted this to myself. The irony of always running away from admitting my guilt to myself as I chased after her. I wanted to believe if I found her that apologizing could free me of this all consuming regret. This seetheing shame. The shame that brought me to this cliff.

After a few moments she pushes away from the hug, wiping her eyes. “We should get going.” grabbing her bag. “Yeah, we should.” I said turning away, hoping she didn’t see the tears that I failed to hold back. I stood and picked up the empty ice cream cups, stacking them together.

She puts out her hand, beckoning me to help her up. As I reach for her a hand caresses my cheek from behind. Frantically I look behind me.

I was facing the forest again. It was empty. Void of life.

I turn back towards her but she was gone. The fairgrounds was gone. I began yelling for her. Screaming for her.

“MEGAN! MEGAN! MEGAN!”

Her soft voice whispers in my ear. “Jakob”. I jump in fear, spinning around to face her. No one was there. Again I am suffocating in unbearable isolation.

I trudge aimlessly through the forest. The crunching of fallen leaves and the snapping of twigs was all that could be heard.

From the corner of my eye I saw the Deer. I stop dead in my tracks. I turn my head slowly. Shaking with dread as my eyes passed each tree. Finally reaching the Deer, he was frowning at me. Exuding anger and rage.

Suddenly something bumped into me. I jumped looking around in panic. Leaves flew in the air as footsteps scurried off, but no one was there. I turn back to face the Deer again.

He was gone. But in his place was an empty field. All the trees were gone except for one. The tree from the fair. And sitting under the tree was Megan.

Slowly, I walk towards the tree. She was staring at the ground. I called to her as I got closer.

“Megan?” She didn’t reply. “Megan. Oh Megan I’m so so sorry.” I began to cry as I fell to my knees. I wept and wept. “Where did you go? I searched and searched for you! Crying, waiting, yearning for a sign! A sign where to find you! Please come back home! Please! Don’t go again! Don’t leave again! I can’t do this without you! I am so, so sorry I won’t ever leave again!” My voice straining as I pleaded and begged.

A decade of pent up emotions flooded through in that moment. I was groveling at her feet. None of me was hidden. It all poured out. I had nothing to hide.

She stood up. My eyes follow her as I wipe my tears. She holds her arm out. Offering me a hand.

The tree behind her began to transform. The twigs formed into antlers. Megan’s hands began to sprout from each end of the antlers, like flowers. I look back to her. She’s saying something but I couldn’t hear her. A loud drone blares through the forest. I reach to grab her hand.

I am holding the Cicada’s leg once again, back at the stairs of divinity.

The jellyfish surrounding us close in. I look down to the sea below, past the translucent jellyfish. Floating amongst the gentle waves of the ocean was a body.

It was facing upwards. It was me. Lifeless but free. Free of burden. Free of grief.

I look up to the Cicada. The wall of light emitted from its wings was now gone. In its place was Megan, smiling.

For one last time I smile. The light that enveloped me begins to fade.

My seventh hour.

As I let go of the Cicada’s leg Megan catches my hand. Her eyes were the last thing I saw before I returned to nothingness.

Before these embers wisp away I say my last words to her.

“I’m sorry.”
