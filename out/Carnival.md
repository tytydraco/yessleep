They say fate is a very fickle and fragile thing. 

I do believe in the Butterfly Effect. 

Every little choice can have drastic consequences.

I wonder, if I hadn’t accepted that wretched invitation, could I have been… happier? Could I have finally lived the peaceful life I so yearned for?

…It’s no use thinking like that. I chose this. Now I have to keep pushing forward.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

It was currently 8:30 PM.

My shift was about to end. I could hear people chattering about how long and difficult today’s work had been. I paid them no heed and continued typing on the keyboard.

As I began losing focus, I caught a glimpse of my reflection. A thick beard, long hair tied into a bun. An impassive face, with eyes that were more akin to black holes, stared back at me. I shook my head and continued working. As I finished everything, I packed my belongings.

“Laptop, there. Mobile, there. Charger, there. Tiffin box, there. Wallet, there,” I muttered, checking if I had everything.

“Ah, Mr. Phoenix, there you are,” a voice called from behind me.

I turned around to see HR smiling at me.

“I see you’ve completed your work early.”

“Yes, sir. Everything is done.”

“Good, good… Listen, I read your request for a few days off due to personal reasons. While we can’t usually grant leave like that, I think you need it after working so hard. Three days is all I can give,” he said quietly.

“I understand, sir.”

He smiled, patted my shoulder, and left. I stood there for a few seconds before strapping my bag over my shoulders, picking up my car keys, and heading out of the office.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

I reached home at 9:45 PM.

Immediately, I sat on the sofa, staring at the ceiling fan, replaying every conversation in my head. I always do this—to see if I said anything wrong.

This ritual lasts only a few minutes before I pick up my phone and read the message that has been lingering in my mind.

“Dear Adam Phoenix,

You are one of the lucky individuals who have been invited to a Carnival to be held on 19th November, 2025. The address of the Carnival is —

We hope to see you there!”

That’s it. No sender’s name. Just an address and an invitation.

I received this three days ago, and I’ve been staring at it ever since. My dull eyes fill with a strange mix of uneasiness and curiosity every time I read it. The date is today.

Yesterday, I made up my mind.

I will go to this so-called Carnival.

I sighed, got up, and dragged my tired body toward the bathroom for a quick shower. As I passed the large mirror in the hall, I deliberately avoided looking at myself.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

Within a few minutes, I arrived at the location. It was a huge mansion, illuminated by glaring lights, with soft music drifting from inside.

At the entrance stood the manager. He wore an olive-green suit, his face hidden behind a golden mask.

The mask had two eye holes—the right outlined with a red diamond shape, the left with blue. Near the mouth was a wide gap, shaped like a grin. I remember feeling… something. A faint uneasiness.

But I ignored it and handed him the invitation. He glanced at it.

“Welcome to the party. We hope you have a great time here.”

I nodded, and he handed me a mask. I couldn’t see his face, but I could feel him smiling beneath it—a strange sense of comfort settling in.

“Here, wear this mask. You’ll feel better with it on,” he said.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

I nodded and put it on.

Inside, the atmosphere was completely different.

While the outside had felt bleak and eerie, the interior was calming. Almost peaceful.

Every corner of the mansion was bathed in warm yellow light. The music was loud, yet pleasant. There were only a few people—around thirty, perhaps—and all of them wore the same mask.

I looked around, observing everything. Some couples danced beneath a chandelier. Others stood near the buffet, eating and watching the scene unfold.

It felt… easier here, somehow. My thoughts grew quieter. I felt safe. Comfortable.

As I walked toward a counter and picked up a glass of champagne, I stood beside a sofa, watching the dancers.

That was when someone approached me.

He was tall—around 6’2. Dressed in a white, regal suit with matching trousers, with jewelry adorning him, he carried an air of both elegance and seriousness. And yet, something about him felt strangely comfortable.

I turned slightly towards him, studying him as I took a sip from my glass.

“Yeah…”

He nodded, as if he understood exactly what I meant.

“It’s always a little strange the first time.”

I raised an eyebrow at that.

“Strange? What do you mean?”

He… said nothing. Just looked at me for a moment before speaking.

“You’ll get used to it.”

I looked back at the dance floor.

For a moment, everything felt normal again.

“Do you come here often?”

“Sometimes.”

“Sometimes?”

“When it feels… necessary.”

I glanced at him again, trying to read his posture.

“Necessary for what?”

“For things to quiet down.”

“Quiet down?”

He didn’t respond.

Instead, he turned his head slightly and looked at me. Just stared.

Longer than needed.

“You think a lot. Don’t worry… you’ll understand.”

Something about the way he said it lingered.

Then he stopped talking.

I waited for him to say something else.

He didn’t.

When I glanced back—

he wasn’t there.

I wasn’t sure when he had left.

Or if I had simply stopped noticing.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

After that strange encounter, I moved around, trying to talk to a few others.

Everyone spoke nicely. There were no issues. Everyone was polite.

Almost too polite.

But then… something shifted.

At first, it was just me.

I felt it in my face—like my lips were slowly forming a grin.

Subtle.

Unintentional.

I could feel my emotions again. After a long time.

Euphoria. Happiness. Pleasure.

It felt… good.

Too good.

And with it… something else.

My thoughts slowed.

Not abruptly.

Gradually.

Like something inside me was being turned down.

It became harder to think clearly.

Harder to question anything.

At some point—

They stopped talking to me.

I didn’t notice when it began.

Only that it had.

I could see others still engaged in conversation, still laughing, still moving naturally.

But whenever I tried to speak—

nothing.

No response.

No reaction.

Not even a glance.

Some did reply.

But it was always the same conversation.

The same tone. The same pauses.

The same words, rearranged.

Like a broken record, repeating itself.

My body felt warm. Too warm.

The music felt louder.

Or maybe I was just hearing it more.

That’s when I noticed him again.

The same man.

The very first one.

He stood exactly where I had left him, watching the dancers—

as if he had never moved.

As if I was the one who had.

“…You again.”

“I was always here.”

He didn’t even look at me.

“You didn’t leave?”

He said nothing for a few seconds.

“There is no need to.”

“Something is wrong here. I feel… different.”

…

“You’re noticing it more.”

Noticing it more?

I didn’t understand.

For the first time in years, I felt panic rising inside me.

Sharp.

Unfamiliar.

“Just answer me properly.”

He kept looking ahead.

“Don’t resist. It will get a lot better.”

“It will all be over soon.”

As he said that, he turned his head slightly.

And for the first time—

The light caught the inside of his mask.

I could see it.

A wide grin.

Unnatural.

Too still.

As if it didn’t belong to a face at all.

His eyes moved—but there was nothing behind them.

No emotion.

No recognition.

Just… empty.

For a moment, I thought—

I saw it move.

Not like a smile should.

But I couldn’t be sure.

I knew I had to leave.

“You’re leaving?”

The voice came from behind me.

Then another.

And another.

I didn’t turn around.

I could feel multiple gazes pressing into the back of my head.

“You should have stayed.”

“It gets better.”

“It will all get better soon.”

Their voices overlapped.

Too calm.

Too certain.

I said nothing.

I tore the mask off my face.

For a split second—

it resisted.

Just slightly.

Then it came free.

I threw it to the ground and rushed out—

not stopping—

not looking back—

until I was home.

I stood there for a long time.

In silence.

I didn’t look at the mirror.

From that day, I kept having nightmares.

Those people. Their strange grins. That mask.

At the office, my performance improved even further. But there were moments when I could feel that same grin forming on my face.

I was more approachable.

But it felt as if they were judging me.

I can’t trust their words anymore.

I can’t trust their smiles.

At night, I couldn’t sleep.

Fear overtook every sense of my body.

It always felt like someone was watching me.

Staring.

I knew that much.

After that fateful day—

I had changed.

Against my will.

And there was no way to go back.

I hadn’t tried smiling since.

Yet somehow… I was always wearing that grin.
