Hello, my name is Victor, I’m 38 years old. Recently, I started dating a woman my age who has a two-year-old daughter, whom I treated as if she were my own.



We lived in her house. Our bedroom was at the beginning of the hallway, right after the stairs leading to the second floor, and our daughter’s bedroom was at the end of the corridor, a little over six meters away (19 feet).



We lived our lives normally, but there was something that bothered me from the very first day I moved in. Every night, my girlfriend would say something—at the very least—strange. She would tell me that when the fifth step was completed, he would come to get them. I never really understood what she meant by that. She would make a bizarre expression as she said it, like a look of fear stretched into a wide smile, as if two different faces were trapped in one. I still feel disturbed whenever I remember that expression. Whenever I asked her what it meant, she would simply tell me not to be curious, because curiosity killed the cat.

I thought it might be some kind of trauma she had, so I tried to ignore it as much as I could. But a few nights ago, I started hearing footsteps coming from the hallway. The steps were long and crossed the corridor all the way to our bedroom door. There were only four steps. I thought it might be our daughter, but no two-year-old child can cross a six-meter (19 feet) hallway in just four steps and that quickly.

I woke my girlfriend and asked if she had heard it. She told me not to be curious, because curiosity kills, as she ran her hand across my face.

In the following nights, the footsteps grew louder and more spaced apart.

I was terrified. I knew there was something on the other side of the door, and our daughter was vulnerable. That’s when I decided I would take action. I would wait for the last step and then run to the door.

I waited anxiously until I heard the first step at the end of the hallway. Then the second. The third. And finally, the fourth. The fourth was practically right in front of my door, and as soon as I heard it, I leapt out of bed.

The last thing I remember is falling to the floor after taking my first step away from the bed.

I woke up lying on the floor. It was already morning. I felt helpless, as if I had lost consciousness. When I looked at the bed, my girlfriend wasn’t there. The door was wide open, pushed all the way back.

I ran to my daughter’s room. No one was there.

The search has been going on for three days since that night, and there is still no sign of them.

I keep asking myself why I got out of bed. Why I chose to be curious.

Because in the end, my curiosity led my family to whatever took the two people I loved most.
