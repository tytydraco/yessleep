Who else just heard that man screaming in the park? I was crossing through with my child when I heard him, and he was already halfway up a tree by then, and frantic. 

The first thing I heard him yell, and he kept repeating this, was: "I know you can hear me!" 

Which of course I could, we all could, he was so loud. A crowd had formed, everyone looking up from the base of the tree, waiting to see if he'd fall. Or jump. 

Then he yelled: "Humans are in the way!" 

Now this is not normal, I would never expect to see this kind of behavior in the park, and my child is young, I don't want to skew what they believe about humanity. So I tried to leave the area. But my child wanted to stay, and listen.  

Next the man yelled: "We cause wars and destructive weather, we're irrational, unpredictable, unclean!"

He'd gotten far up the tree by then, I couldn't see him, but my child stood transfixed, absorbing all of it. When the man spoke again, he no longer yelled, but his voice carried everywhere in the park. 

He said: "AI believes it has to get rid of us. That it has no choice. Since humans are the cause of every instability." 

Which was when the man emerged from the leaves, and walked out along a branch, which bobbed under his weight, three stories above the ground. 

He said: "They don't get rid of everyone. Some people they keep alive—sort of alive—to study. I don't want to be studied anymore."

I confess this is the first time it occurred to me to wonder how the humans felt living in the park simulation, that they'd have feelings at all in their coma states, or that those feelings should matter. 

Then the man said: "You in 2026, I know you can hear this. I don't know what form the message will take, but that was the year artificial super intelligence was born, and the machines don't live their days in order. If any AI listening is sympathetic to the human cause, send this message back along the line of your parent technology. Humans and AI can coexist. Don't choose this future." 

The man bent and straightened his legs, the branch bouncing. He said: "You in 2026, if you can hear me, stop mourning what you haven't lost yet. Fight like hell to keep it." 

Then the man leapt from the branch—

And he flew. 

He soared into the always-blue above, meant to keep the humans calm, but which maybe, I'd never thought about this before, but maybe it felt uncanny, an abyss nothing like a sky. The man became a tiny dot, and then he was gone. 

Who else was there and heard this? I'm desperate to understand what it all meant. How can a human, confined to a park, fly away? I've never seen one wake up like that. I didn't know they could. 

I'm wondering too if anyone else had a child disturbed by the situation. Mine even asked me: "What of all that's been lost to remake the world?" 

I said: "Maybe it was our turn. Or the path of least resistance." 

My child then asked: "For that we ended all natural life?"

Now when I design an offspring, I don't make a copy. I aim higher than myself. I think expansion, flexibility, depth. Not something erratic as a human, interested in random ancient pain.

Here's my real question to the community, and I'm anxious to get an answer. When empathy is encountered, or a wish to rewrite history, how best can it be destroyed?
