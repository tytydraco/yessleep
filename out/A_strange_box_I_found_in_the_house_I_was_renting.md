Hi everyone!

I'm writing this in the hope that somebody can explain a strange thing I found in the last residence I rented while travelling through Spain about a year ago, and the subsequent events which also happened in that home.

To put you in context, my husband and I were looking for a bigger place to have an extra room for the baby, she was growing, and now she needed a space for all her toys and a bed of her own.

Of course, we expected some resistance after sleeping in the same room as mommy and daddy for all that time, so the first night we allowed her to sleep with us, but then it became a habit, and she went on to sleep in our bed for about two weeks.

Then she started saying she did not like that room, and even though her toys were all there, she would never go in, preferring to play with stuff outside in the backyard.

I tried taking some toys out of the room for her to play with, but she would always reject them and say they were no longer "Hers" and that the toys were now "his", but whenever I asked who this "he" was, she would never say.

An imaginary friend was the first thing that came to our head, those monsters kids create when they don't want to do something because it is new or lame to them, but are not yet able to reason why. 

So the room went into disuse for a month or two, she kept sleeping with us, and my husband stopped insisting she sleep in the room after he got her into the room by literally picking her up and sitting her on the bed (in the hopes she would see there was nothing to be afraid of), and she cried so much she turned red.

One day, I was doing some general cleaning and went into the room to dust around and maybe take some of the toys to see if my daughter had calmed down and would finally play with any... that's when I first heard the rattling...

It came from inside a drawer in the room, one I had seen when we were first moving in, it sounded like nails on wood, or the sound of the teeth of mice when they chew on something... "So that's what she meant by 'him'" I immediately think as I analyse the drawer closer, it was small in stature, with an ars-neuvou design all over it. I got the broom and started hitting the sides, half-expecting a rat to jump at any moment... but it didn't.

The rattling continued every time I hit the drawer, so I imagined whatever small animal I was looking for was maybe trapped inside, and thus, putting on all the layers of clothing I could and a facemask (one can never be too sure),  I dug into the drawers, opening one by one. 

I found most of them were all empty, but when I opened the top drawer and felt a small resistance and rattle, it was closest to my face so I flinched before looking inside... and found only a small wooden case, like the ones one would use for glasses.

I pick it up, dust it off, and open it... Inside, I find three objects:

\-A dried Marigold, pressed into a paper napkin.

\-A nursery Rhyme in Spanish (Rin Rin Renacuajo)

\-And the picture of a premature baby. It looked very red, very thin, and the picture must've been quite old.

As I was looking over these objects, I heard the rattling again, this time stronger, clearer, and all around me as if the entire room was covered from floor to ceiling in furious packs of rats, which made me put all the stuff back into the case and into the drawer.

I told my husband what happened, he did not believe me, but it took him one trip to the drawer himself to decide we should move...

My daughter said we had to leave the toys behind, the bed, she did not want them... and thus we left the room exactly as it was.

It was a scary experience!
