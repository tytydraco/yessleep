It’s time to walk.

Your vision snaps into focus to reveal a strange, otherworldly image. Light reflects off of the dull cobblestone walls, and you look up to see the empty, brownish-yellow sky staring down upon you. The hallway you are in is thin, only about four feet wide, with no ceiling. The stones are slick with various liquids, ranging from dark red to a sickly yellow. Suddenly, you are jostled by a moving object. A person. A man with one leg drags himself across the ground with an arm attached to the body by a bare bone and scraps of dried muscle. A few silent people push by. You try to call out, but the only noise that escapes your mouth is the rasp of dry air. How long have you been standing here?

It’s been a week. You have caught up to the crowd of people that lines the halls. Your progression has slowed considerably as you find yourself integrating with the crowd, and you can’t walk without being crushed between two shoulders or smothered by a chest or stomach. Most of the people are old and frail, but there are some younger bodies, even children. The hall reeks of blood and sweat, and as your nose begins to adjust to the smell, you feel a crunch under your foot and a new, wet stench violates your nostrils. You lift your foot to see the crushed head of an infant. The rest of the child crawls away, losing a few limbs as other walkers blindly step on its frail body. You wipe the skin and soft bone off of your foot and keep moving.

You saw your first rat king after a month of walking. During an almost empty stretch of hallway, you are able to lose yourself in the short lived tranquility that fills the empty space. As you push past a group of people, you feel a resistance as the cluster of bodies does not budge. As you look closer, you see their arms and legs are tangled together, with some limbs fused by shards of broken bone and blood. Two arms, horribly broken and almost braided together, reach out as you briskly rush past. 

Where do you find the will to keep going? What do you believe waits for you at the end of the tunnel? You have felt the presence of a sacred being, wholly alien to any aspect of your mind or reality. Do you believe in God?

Your journey has ended. The hallways are more crowded than you have ever seen in your years of walking. A man with no legs crawls past, knocking you over. A foot punches through your weak skin, puncturing your abdomen as you try to stand up, sending a spurt of pink and foaming vomit gushing out of your mouth. Another person steps on your brittle leg, snapping it. Still, you crawl on. Another person falls on top of you. More follow, eventually forming a pile large enough to block the hallway and crush your bones. People climb over the mass of writhing flesh, further pressing down upon your struggling body. Someone vomits, and you are coated in a hot, reeking liquid that mixes with the coagulated blood, forming a putrid cement over your face. Lost in the mound of flesh, all you can do is writhe.

Your babbling, screaming body will be part of the foundation for a tower of flesh that will continue to form as more people try to ascend the growing mountain. Your desecrated body will be crushed into an unrecognisable paste. The blood and sweat of the thousands of other damned souls will dilute what is left of your physical form until nobody will feel your writhing flesh. You will learn what it means to scream with no mouth and sob with no eyes. You will spend decades under the mountain of wailing flesh until you can no longer remember who you were before the hallway. An eternity of that alien pull driving your stagnant, destroyed flesh forwards is all that awaits you.
