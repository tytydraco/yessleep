I opened a little wooden box that I purchased online three days ago.

It didn't seem like a huge deal at the time. Coins, antique tools, and odd little curiosities that people sell when they clean out their attics are all items that I collect. Most of the time, it's innocuous items that simply wind up on a shelf.

The description of the box was "old wine cabinet." The seller only mentioned that it had belonged to his grandmother and that he had been told his entire life not to open it. He claimed that she kept it locked and sealed in a cabinet because she thought it contained something evil.

That kind of story usually means someone is trying to make the item sound mysterious so it sells faster.

So I bought it.

I noticed the smell as soon as the package arrived. There was a slight smell, initially sweet but also sour, even before I opened the box it came in. similar to rotting fruit.

I assumed something had leaked during shipping.

However, I discovered that the wooden box itself was the source of the odor when I opened it.

It was smaller than I expected, maybe a foot tall, made of dark wood with blackened edges like it had been burned at some point. The lid had strange carved letters on it that I didn’t recognize, and it was sealed shut with four rusted screws.

Whoever owned it before me clearly didn’t want it opened.

I picked up a screwdriver and placed it on my kitchen table that evening.

The first screw came out easily.

I turned the second one, and it made a loud squeal.

By the time I removed the third screw, the smell had gotten stronger. It filled the room now –sweet, rotten, and sharp, almost like ammonia.

I remember actually pausing for a second.

Something about it made my stomach turn.

Nevertheless, my curiosity overcame me. I removed the last screw and lifted the lid.

There were only a few tiny items inside.

An old coin.

A lock of hair tied together with red thread.

A small stone.

And a dried rose petal.

That was it.

I laughed.

After all the buildup, it looked like the contents of someone’s junk drawer.

I closed the box and went to bed.

I had one of the most vivid dreams of my life that night.

In the dream I was standing in my bedroom, looking at myself asleep in my bed.

Everything appeared to be real. The same shadows, the same lamp on my nightstand, even the same crease in the blanket.

Everything looked exactly like real life. 

Then the version of me lying in the bed slowly opened his eyes.

And smiled.

I woke up immediately.

My clock read **3:12 AM**.

I didn't give it much thought. People have weird dreams all the time.

But the next night the same thing happened.

Same dream.

Same smile.

Same exact time.

3:12 AM.

That’s when the other things started happening.

The apartment would start to smell at random. Sometimes I’d walk into the kitchen and the air would be thick with that rotten fruit odor.

At other times, it would totally vanish.

Additionally, my lights began to flicker from time to time, particularly in the room where the box was located.

I initially thought there was a problem with the wiring.

My phone then began acting strangely. Once I walked into the kitchen and saw the screen light up on its own. The camera app was open even though I hadn’t touched it.

The third day was the most bizarre.

I came home from work and found the box open on the table.

I’m certain I had closed it.

The objects inside were gone.

All of them.

I searched everywhere - the floor, under the table, even the trash.

Nothing.

That night the dream changed.

Instead of just watching myself sleep, I noticed something else in the room.

Someone standing in the corner.

It was difficult for me to see. Just a dark shape, almost blending into the wall.

The version of me lying in the bed slowly sat up and turned his head toward it.

Like he knew it was there.

I woke up again at **3:12 AM**.

But this time I couldn’t move.

My chest felt heavy, like something was pressing down on it.

Then I thought it was sleep paralysis.

I’ve had it once before, years ago, so I tried to stay calm.

Then I heard breathing.

Slow, steady breathing somewhere in the room.

I could hear it clearly, but I was unable to turn my head.

It sounded like someone was standing next to my bed.

After what felt like forever, I finally managed to move again. I turned on the light.

The room was empty.

I decided the following morning that I was tired of the box.

I took it outside and threw it in the dumpster behind my apartment building.

I recall feeling foolish for being anxious about it at all.

When I got home that evening, the box was sitting on my kitchen table again.

The lid was closed.

And the four screws were back in place.

I eventually began investigating the odd letters engraved on the lid at that point.

I eventually discovered that they were Hebrew.

Eventually, I came across a number of articles on the internet that said the box is known as a Dybbuk Box, which is purportedly used to capture a wandering spirit or demon known as a Dybbuk.

People who open these boxes frequently report the same things, according to the stories.

The smell of rotting fruit.

Recurring nightmares.

Shadowy figures during sleep paralysis.

Sudden illness.

I didn’t want to believe any of it.

But last night something happened that I can’t explain.

I woke up again at **3:12 AM**.

I couldn’t move.

The door to my bedroom creaked open slowly.

Something walked inside.

I couldn’t see it clearly, just a darker shape in the darkness standing at the foot of my bed.

It stayed there for several seconds.

Just watching me.

Then it leaned closer to my face.

I heard it whispering in a language I didn’t understand.

Right now it’s **3:11 AM**.

I have no idea what else to do, so I'm writing this.

The box is open again.

The coin, the hair, the stone, and the rose petal are all back inside.

But there’s something new in there now.

A photograph and it’s a picture of me sleeping in my bed.

It was taken tonight from the corner of my room.
