Linak: The Unexplained Fires Pt. 1

Fire is the most beautiful and destructive thing on the planet. It cleans water through boiling, it cooks food raw to done, it also scars the world with its intensity, breaking trees and pillaging home. It removes all evidence of things before leaving a charred spot for nature to claim or man to ruin. I was seven and my mom had taken me to the state fair. I didn’t care for the animals parading themselves to torment nor the unanswered calls to the people running the booths failing to time and wear. No, I wanted to see the fire dancers, the vehicles going at near blinding speed through the ramp and out a blazing hot inferno ring. It scared me but in the best possible way; the fear a child gets of something that is unknown but the drive to understand.

I would sneak the matches dad would use to light the grill unit into my room at night after my parents retired to their rooms. I would watch the fire creep down the stem almost burning the tip of my finger and thumb. In my joys though, one night I had made a tragic mistake and looked away a split second too long, letting the match burn me. I of course dropped it engulfing my blankets in a sea of red and yellow. My smoke alarm blared without hesitation and thankfully my parents were home to catch my idiocy quickly. Dad was able to fight the fire with an extinguisher for a bit while mom called 911. The  rescuers came and all though I would hate sleeping in a motel while repairs went on, my childhood home was spared. 

From that day on, fire still fascinated me, but in a much more restrained way. Something about seeing where you sleep catch a blaze didn’t sit well with me. When I got out of high school I decided to try myself out for the fire academy. Even with me being not the strongest of female stature, I had enough stamina and spirit to push myself through the grueling training course and schooling. Climbing ladders, dredging hoses across rough terrain and carrying what I can assume was my dads dead weight with force dragging me down. I passed everything with such flying colors that I was actually asked to work in Richmond, Virginia. I grew up in a small Kansas town and just spent the last year in Topeka so I didn’t mind going to another large city for the time being. It wouldn’t be too far from home so I figured it was a great start to my career. 

I had been in Richmond for some time working my way around the fire house making the best chili the crew had ever had. I was also able to fix a lot of the crew's uniforms thanks to my mom being one of the best seamstresses for the girls around town. Mom would have me practice fixing their Sunday dresses or emergency stitching to local high school prom attire. On the field I showed myself to be an even better first responder. I successfully extracted many elderly and children alike from burning buildings. I had extinguished and guided an entire team to save half of an office building from a guy's misguided use of his break that led to him dropping his cigarette in the trash. The best part though was when I was asked to be part of the state's special wilderness response team for the south end of the Appalachians; the Beaver unit. The unit was stationed in the much smaller city of Damascus and our job was simple, responding to fire codes as normal but with some added caveats. Building trenches to divert fire, take down and out foliage that could set a blaze and even sandbag areas that would prevent mud slides. Dozens if not at least a hundred of us working on multiple teams with priority teams being Alpha and Beta where the back up  would be Charlie and Delta, with Echo and Foxtrot being reserves. I had impressed somebody because I was immediately put on the lead team of Alpha.

I loved working with the new team, getting to know them all like Freddy Summers, a seasoned veteran with way too much time making model cars always having his latest finished one in his locker. There was also Mortimier Felt, a guy who could not get cheesy movie lines out of his head for the life of him. We bonded, we worked, we laughed and we even cried together, especially after Winnie fell in the line of duty. Getting cut off from the group happens, but Winnie got the worst part of it. Stuck on a cliff face with a steep drop on one end and surrounded by a quick rush of flames breed out of wind and careless actions. Winnie was just trying to save a doe that had got itself stuck in an illegal game trap up on that cliff face. Winnie couldn't handle an innocent creature dying by two things man had made, the fire killed him instead. A product of his own inner morality to save an innocent. 

Three months after Winnie had died we had gotten a call of a flash fire that happened along a deep hiking trail pretty far beyond Damascus. Quickly hauling our packs and gear into the trucks and fully loaded engine, we stormed out sirens aloud. It didn’t take long for us to find out we needed to abandon the engine behind and try to contain the fire the best we could until air drops were able to be made. A forty five minute walk from the widest part of the trail later and we could see the forest finally being illuminated more from just our head lights and flashlights. We sanctioned the area and quickly began our mission to secure the perimeter around what we thought would be the fire's destruction radius. Freddy came up behind me and began making sure my trench work was as good as it always was, “you could make a really nice war trencher back in the day if they took girls.” I gave a sympathetic chuckle to the old man not caring for what he said, just that he wasn’t doing his part. “Look Freddy I know you're old but I didn't think you were old enough to remember how it was against the central powers.” He shrugged and pressed on to his trenching. I looked at our line of work towards the fire, Alton being the last man closest to it and I noticed something approaching from the energetic chaos, something that seemed unfazed by the flames.

I placed my shovel on the ground and began to approach where it was coming from, a knot growing in my stomach tight and unyielding. Mortimer or Morti as we all called him, breaking down some dead brush, noticed I had stopped my work, “Amelia, hey Amelia!” His voice rang to me with no answer or motion. I just kept walking to the edge of where Alton was working. Alton looked perplexed at me about to snap me back in place when he heard it, a moan. A gruff muddled moan that echoed in the night even over the cracking of branches and the howl of the growing inferno. It slithered its way into my drums like nails on chalkboard. It sounded human, so human I was sure the figure approaching would be that as well. Through the illumination of the fire though I saw a figure of a deer, a normal every day deer but its fur was gone singed by the flames and its skin was charred to non-existence. Its skin so black from the fires that it couldn’t even be lit by the fire itself now. It didn’t open its mouth to cry though, it opened its chest. I saw it split open right under the gape of its muzzle, it stretched deep beyond what I could see. It kept making the noise through its bursted open cavity, no organs or blood fell out, just sound. We all gazed into the abyss of this thing yelling in the lost night of the forest, I wanted to run over to my shovel and take it out for good, but my captain stopped me. Captain Halloway was a burly man of a firefighter and he knew the job better than even Freddy. So when he shook his head no, I listened silently without hesitation.

It screamed and screamed until the fire finally took it again, fully consuming it in the unbiased flame. We all continued in silence blocking the fire's path to more forest around it. We stayed until a single water transport helicopter dropped load after load of water. I watched as the area around the fire was smothered with gushing water and after the last of the fires flames began to die out the sun began to cut through the mountains and forest. At this point it was safe to get closer to where the fires had annihilated this land, moving on to phase 2. After a fire rolls through, embers and burning charcoal are left behind. We  take the time to extinguish all we can for one ember could cause this mess to pick right back up. I for once was not focused on the job but where that thing fell over and died finally. You would think that the body would leave behind a charred skeleton maybe answering what it actually was. It was gone, so gone from existence that all that was left on the soaked terrain was an imprint into the ground like it had been in the radius of a nuclear explosion. An image of its last form burned onto the earthy canvas. 

Halloway barked at me in his calm but imposing voice, “Amelia come on we can’t worry about that now. Let the fire marshall figure something out we’re not paid for.” I shrugged off the sight of something that I just didn’t have the time to understand at that moment. Halloway calling me over had me continue closer to the light stream that had broken off from the mountains. Down there by the stream I contemplated once more what I had seen, something I think all of us have been doing since the silence of the fire had fallen over us. I threw over what seemed like a soda can at first that had some stringy vegetation over when it went off. A pop so loud I was sure someone had heard at the work zone. Silence fell and I collected myself from where I threw my body towards, away from the eruption of spark and flame. Over where I had flipped the metal looking material laid a small fire that I quickly extinguished with my shovel and I looked down where the object laid still some of it safe from whatever had made it go off. I was careful to just look at it for a bit making sure nothing else would happen. 

I slowly reached out to it and grabbed the object in my hand still draped in what looked like roots of some kind, red and sticky. It was shiny like metal on the inside but incredibly brittle, something I had never seen in person but reminded me of school. It was like an alkaline metal, palatable to the hand but it felt heavier than I would think. When I moved it on the ground I must have hit a moisture pocket to ignite some of the metal that I must have separated from this larger piece. Between the dripping vegetation and metal I could see a layer of black material; something possible to protect the volatile material from the wet substance between. I grabbed from my back pack my lunch that I had prepared for down time earlier the previous night. I threw the dry sandwich out of its foil covered protection into the forest and placed the material inside of it making sure not to capture any more moisture or to turn the wet material onto the metal inside. If anything I could get this to the Marshall and hopefully help with this case, maybe even help my own career in the process. A breakthrough like this could only come a few times to even fewer people.

Right before the sun reached its apex in the sky another team came to relieve us from the duties that we had practically taken care of. Always a B team to our A team this time more specifically Delta to our Alpha. I could tell all of us were silently grateful  to let our lessers take over a job well done. We got back to the trucks and engine at the beginning of the trail, the path now deemed safe already filling up with a few travelers and hikers ready to see the wonders of the wilderness beyond. The heat of the sun tore at my uniform and I managed to take off the thick outer layer before piling into the crowded engine along with Freddy, Morti and the captain Halloway. Morti spoke first as the engine began to pull away , “so anybody else hear that bang from the east during clean up. I think it came from your way, Amelia.” 

I sat silent for a moment, I didn’t know why but I had an urge of uneasiness washing over, I gazed up at the three of them each waiting in their own patient way. I shook my head slightly to catch my breath and jotted my mind to think of something quick, “yeah it was me, a burnt up tree snapped and fell over.” Halloway in his gruff voice barked first, “why didn't we hear the snap and the drop then? We weren't that far away. We most likely would have heard a tree making that much noise twice.” Freddy scooted a little closer and tried to comfort me, “look, we all saw what happened out there. Strange occurrences happen all the time in these parts. Mothman, stairs in the woods, radioactive hillbillies in the caves, so you can tell us.” We all chuckled minus Halloway who at least gave a comforting small grin. “Okay. I was working by the stream to the East when I found this, I must have taken a chip or something off it and landed in water. I think it’s alkaline metal.” 

I pulled the foil covered object out of my pack and showed them all, Morti spoke first grabbing the object with a dry towel in the engine compartments, “well shit it looks like metal. It’s mailable too, what do you think cap?” Halloway stepped towards Morti and had him place the wrapped object in his gloved hands, a shadow of them almost cascaded the whole space we were in. “Yeah I think you nailed the alkaline part but what in the name of Jesus is this stuff?” Halloway grabbed the root like objects with his gloved hand first then after removing one grabbed it with his bare hand. A wet sticky sound produced from it, the captain pulled his hand away and recognized the red thick fluid that was trailed behind, “blood. It’s fleshy, like tissue. What the hell?” Freddy stood up balancing himself as the engine roared its motor taking us down another road to the fire house, “I can see why I’d be wary about this Amelia.” Morti, feeling like he had no choice except through habit, referenced a movie, “Be afraid, be very afraid.” We all shot him a look of disgust and stupidity. Freddy chimed up the only one knowing the movie other than Morti himself “really? The Fly?”

A week had passed since the captain presented the evidence to the fire Marshall including the wrapped piece of alkaline from the recent flash fire. I expected to at least hear something from Freddy being kind of a second in command to Halloway but not a word since that day. It was a bleak night in June, the sky was covered in a thick haze of smoke cascading from another fire, one that had begun to get close to even the outside of Damascus. Our job that night was to create a barrier for the city and to guarantee that it would stay out of reach from the city. Halloway and the chief took it very seriously as they had every team on alert taking care of the situation. Charlie and Delta were tasked with protecting the entrance to the trails, Bravo and Echo were tasked with deep forest control on the west of the trail, while Alpha and Foxtrot were placed with the task to tackle the fire's edge to the city of Damascus to the East. We were turning on a rough embankment clearing dead or dry brush when we saw them. Alton was the first to point it out, “hey is that a- oh my god.” We took a step out on the illuminated ridge petrified, a fish flew by. A literal tropical fish floated and then darted away into the forest close to the edge of the fires. It glowed an electric solo blue.

Freddy spoke up next, breaking the tension of our small dispatch from the main group of us, “that. That was a fucking fish man. Does anybody think they lit an illegal back wood weed farm ablaze or something? Cause that I can say only drugs could make me see.” Morti began to go down the hill trailing the path the fish had taken, “maybe whatever started this fire had something to do with whatever the hell it is we just saw. Real or not, Cap will probably want us to check it out, but we should probably call it in before following.” Morti made a call on his radio informing command we were following some wild life to safety, not disclosing it was flying fish. I tried to chime up but the three others decided on their own silent accord to press into the forest away from our designated area. I felt even though we called it in we should have just stuck it out with the job at hand remembering the last strange wild life we dealt with. It only took going over another thick tree lined hill that we saw. In the air, floating and dancing schools of fish trailed around, jelly fish popping up and down their tendrils barely scrapping the height of a taller man. Octopus stuck to trees mimicking their color but changing to a strange aura blue that seemed to be all the floating and dangling sea life's natural color of a dim lit almost turquoise like.

The fire was approaching this area, as if directed by the wind or outside force. Seeing it I realized it would soon be on top of this hill only maybe half a mile away. We had at best fifteen minutes to clear this area but I knew nothing like that would be achievable. Turning to the others I spoke up over the awe of silence, “guys we need to go, this whole area is going to be covered soon and the Chief and Captain will have our heads if the city gets in trouble. I don't care if these fish start sprouting tits, we need to move.” The others did not listen, Freddy was looking at the moray eels from the ground, their bodies hidden in the large bore holes of the trees at least a story above him. Morti was looking through the grass finding little hidden crustaceans that were dancing across his work boots and gloves. Alton was in a trance watching the jellyfish close by, one had begun to get a little low for its own comfort and tried to race away from Alton. It was like the blue they were giving off the guys stared at too long, an angler fish does the same to its prey. 

I grabbed Morti off the ground and turned his gaze to the growing fire coming our way slapping across his face to get him out of whatever trance these things had on him. He was about to yell at me with a harsh red hand print on his face when he saw the bright orange hell coming our way, “oh it’s 106 in Chicago. Got it.” Morti’s stupid way of saying he would haul ass to help. He grabbed Freddy and showed him the danger coming, he had no response to Morti almost wanting to jump up to the long eels making their approach to the old man. Morti put himself between Freddy and the tree and he decided what was best was to give Freddy a good stomp on his foot. Even through his thick boots he was wearing, Freddy winced in pain and shook his head shoving Morti off of him into the tree. Freddy sensing maybe the heat or the glow of the rage consuming the landscape he grabbed Morti by the shirt rushing the two of them away from the tree den of long predators. They put some distance between the tree and them, stopping at the top of the hill before the haunting sea of trees and supernatural life before them. 

I ran over to Alton, he had no idea what was happening. He just smiled with bliss and awe towards the haunting beings growing ever so closer to him. “Amelia it’s singing to me, it’s like I can hear their colors.” I was about to grab Alton but his reach to the blob of life out extended me from grabbing him. Raising his hands Alton grabbed the jellyfish by the tendrils, but he seemed unphased as if they didn’t shock or poison him like normal sea jelly fish would. As Alton brought it down he gazed on the slimy like creature’s top watching the material inside dance, pulling Alton even deeper into its intoxicating trance of color. I kept trying my best to get to Alton without really looking at all the creatures, too afraid I would be pulled in their enticing glow. I was maybe three feet away from Alton when it sounded like the trance was broken through his voice, Alton tilted his head to the side and muttered “huh it’s got teeth.” The jellyfish opened its head up revealing a horrible twisting of sharp shark-like teeth ripping Alton’s face right off consuming it within seconds. I screamed and watched as his body twitched, releasing the predator, his lifeless body falling to the earth with an astonishingly heavy dead thud. I wanted to get him but dozens of the jellyfish descended from above me and I took off instead. Heart broken to leave a man behind but survival was all I could think about. Morti and Freddy, both watching in agony on top of the hill, tried to rush to help our fallen fighter but I grabbed both their arms and dragged them down the opposite side of the hill, “No! Fuck no! Run!” My heart raced so intensely I could have sworn my blood was jet fuel at this point, I didn’t dare look back. Let the fire consume that section at this point, to hell with those perversions of nature.

We ran past the rest of Alpha, their hollering at us barely coherent to my ears, my blood flowing harshly making them and my legs doing all the work throb with burning flow. We barely made it to the fire crews tent encampment of Field Control, my chest heavy with the rest of my body. Out of the pop up tent came Halloway with Chief Nixon close by their faces stone sour as they looked us up and down, “what the fuck is the meaning of this why aren’t-“ I cut him off his eyes glaring furiously at me but I needed the captain to hear “Alton’s dead!” Halloway looked at me with fear and worry, now not understanding what I had meant, “What? How- why didn’t you report this on the radio? We could have come and gotten him help!” I caught some resemblance of a breath and continued not knowing whether he would believe me or throw me in an insane asylum. I hoped that Halloway being present for the deer incident would at least help him to see through what I was telling him as truth. Then again I couldn’t really understand what happened either, “the wildlife we were tracking wasn’t natural to our world. They were sea creatures, so many of them swimming in the air and sheltering in the trees. They glowed this hypnotic blue and Alton couldn’t help it. He grabbed one and it ate his fucking face Captain. I’m so sorry,” I began to break down a bit, a single tear flowing from my face.  Halloway threw his hands up and shook his head, the frustration of not knowing the unknowing was settling into him. “The fuck Amelia? Are you on drugs! Freddy, Morti, come on! Try to give me something that makes sense here?”

Freddy collected himself trying to breath through his heavy smoke induced lungs saving too many without his rebreather on in old days, “Cap she’s not lying. Sir there are hundreds of tropical sea life or whatever else might be from the sea just out there living like they own the damn forest!” Morti threw his gear off “I am not going back out there it’s game over man game-“ Halloway bursted out purple with pent up anger, “Shut up Morti this is not the fucking time for your stupid movie quotes and phrases! I am sick of the unprofessional nature that brings here! Now the three of you get out of my face, we have a man to find before that fire takes away the only thing his family has left of him!” Halloway was a rough man but never an outright angry man, we had seen him scold some of us before and even I had to take the blunt action when I accidentally served him something at the fire house kitchen that had almonds in it. This though, this was pure anger and we got it deep within us. Especially Morti who never even heard from the captain or chief before about his little quirk. Halloway spun slowly around turning his back to us collecting himself, speaking through his gritted teeth Halloway spoke trying to compose himself. “I’m sorry. It's been years since we’ve had a fire this close to the city and I don’t want to be the crew to lose it. So please team, just do your job. I will go personally find Alton with a rescue unit, whatever may be left if anything at all.” Halloway went into the tent, Chief Nixon following behind trying to comfort his captain but we could see and even hear him now, he began to cry, another man had ended their duty too soon. 

Morti didn’t come with us. He refused, so we told him to just head back to the engine and help out at the command area with any civilians that needed some help getting to safety or provide some supplies for other teams by being a runner. We headed back to our zone, the fire we could see now engulfed the area where the sea of air and trees were haunted by these awful entities. We hunkered down sheltered by barren land and natural barricades. Silence hung over us not wanting to speak of the things we had seen tonight, some of the others from Alpha bugged and prodded wanting to know what horror they soon would find out. That Alton was dead and the world we knew was coming untied at the seams of understanding.
