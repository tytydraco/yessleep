[https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/comments/1u4udel/theres\_something\_moving\_in\_the\_egyptian\_exhibit/](https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/comments/1u4udel/theres_something_moving_in_the_egyptian_exhibit/) 

It has been less than a week since I returned to the museum. For the most part, the medieval, Greek, and Egyptian sections have been fixed; a few items were broken beyond repair, but the museum was able to replace them with artifacts from storage. The only thing that hasn’t been found or replaced was one of the mummies from the Egypt exhibit. Don’t ask me how, it’s not like the glass around the body was even broken, and I feel like I would have noticed if one of the vandals ran out of the room with an entire body over their shoulder.

Speaking of the vandals, they were never caught, found or even identified. The last bit of camera footage shows them running into the east wing, which remains sectioned off and closed to the public to this day. I’m not even allowed to go in there. The museum hasn’t given a date yet for the east wing’s reopening, which I find strange because the police stopped looking there a while ago.

The museum has been really paranoid about another break-in happening, but couldn’t afford another security guard, so instead they decided to give me the responsibility of patrolling the museum a couple of times each night. It’s not that I mind this necessarily, I suppose that I am lucky that I wasn’t fired, but it can be nerve-racking wandering around the halls, flashlight in hand, knowing that that thing from the Egyptian exhibit is out there with you.

 It was on my third night of being there that I had another run-in with the figure. I had just entered the atrium where the museum keeps all its dinosaurs when I heard a sound coming from the far side of the room.  
*clack clack clack*  
My eyes darted over to the far corner, where I saw the silhouette of the figure perched on the back of a mammoth skeleton. The figure's long fingers grasped the mammoth’s ribs like a child on the monkey bars. I charged towards that corner of the room and began yelling at the thing to get down. As soon as the figure saw me, it leapt off the mammoth and scurried out of the atrium, leaving behind only the rich scent of pine. I eventually lost the figure among the taxidermy in the zoology exhibit, but that was just the beginning of things.

Two nights later, I was watching the monitor in my office when I noticed movement in the dinosaur atrium. From the cameras, I could see the figure, clinging to the spindly neck of one of the museum's prized sauropods. I watched as it shimmied up the neck towards the base of the skull and stayed there, watching the ground like some sort of drop bear. However, this time, I wasn’t playing around. What the figure didn’t know was that the night before, I had installed flood lights, the small kind that you can control remotely, all over the atrium, and I was ready to light the slippery bastard up. I watched as the monitor lit up with an explosion of light, and as the flash died down, I could see the figure already on the other side of the room. I followed him on the cameras as he ran up the stairs to the third level. As I saw him duck into the European history exhibit, I grabbed my flashlight and a metal bat I had gotten recently from Walmart, and got ready to track down and confront whoever the hell was stowing away in the museum. 

When I got down to the third level, I followed the smell of resin through the History of Europe exhibit, which led me to the Victorian section. In the Victorian section of the museum, they have all these rooms sectioned off with glass. Each room is supposed to be a recreation of what your average upper-class home would have looked like way back when. The further I went into this section, the stronger the smell of pine became. Following the smell, I was led into a dead end. That’s when I saw it.

Behind the glass, sitting at a large dining room table, was the mummy. Like the one that had gone missing from the Egypt exhibit, here it was. The mummy seemed to stare at me with its beady little eye sockets. It was wrapped in bandages from the base of its neck down to its heels, and its head was covered with a mop of unkempt hair. The mummy’s skin was a desaturated brown. The second I glanced away from the mummy, I heard a chair tip over from behind the glass, and when I looked back, the mummy was gone. 

That was more than enough for me; I was out of there. As soon as I heard that chair fall over, I turned and hit the ground running, not stopping until I reached the safety of my car. I didn’t go back into the museum until sunrise. I had forgotten my phone in my office, and even then, I couldn’t stop thinking about what might be lurking in the shadows waiting to snatch me up. I don’t know what to do, I’m pretty freaked out because either someone is in the museum, moving things around and playing tricks on me, or that mummy is moving itself. Personally, I can’t decide which is worse.
