*Tick-tick…tick-tick…tick-tick.*

My eyes snap open. What was that? 

*Tick-tick…tick-tick…tick-tick.*

Something’s outside my door. 

My pit bull, Chester, never comes upstairs because he’s afraid to climb the steps. Besides him, I live alone. So maybe it’s rodents. But…that can’t be it. Whatever’s walking out there sounds like it has two legs. 

Maybe it was a dream. 

I glance at the bedside clock.

*2:00 A.M.*

I get up, cross the room, and pull the door open. 

I check left. 

It’s empty. 

I check right— 

Also empty. 

Okay. Whew. I *was* dreaming. I crawl back in bed.

\*\*\*

*The next night.*

*Tick-tick…tick-tick…tick-tick.*

I jolt awake. It’s the same sound. 

I glance at the clock. 

*2:00 A.M.*

This time I stop by the corner of the room first and pick up my baseball bat. Then I push the door open. Flick on the hall light.

“Who’s there?”

Quiet.

I descend the stairs and peek into the kitchen. A light on my refrigerator blinks. Otherwise, nothing moves. 

I move into the living room. From behind the couch, Chester pokes his head up, ears perked. He’s probably curious about what the hell I’m doing. 

Alright. Maybe it’s a *recurring* dream. I return to my bedroom, set the bat back in the corner, and go back to bed.

\*\*\* 

*The next night—*

It comes again. 

And even though each room in the house is empty, I’ve had it. This is not my imagination. 

It may be a rodent. Or it may be an incredibly evasive squatter. 

Whatever the case—this ends now.

\*\*\*

The next evening after work, I stop by Lowe’s and purchase an indoor camera. 

On the drive home, storm clouds roll across the horizon and block out the sunset. Everything is colored in a muted blue. 

When I slow down and turn into my neighborhood, something *cracks* my windshield. 

Two more cracks follow. It’s hail. I pull into the garage and close up as ice begins pounding the pavement. 

I grab a ladder, head upstairs, and drill the mount into the wall near the ceiling. Then I install the camera. It aims straight down the hall. 

Anything moves here tonight, it’ll be recorded. 

Outside, the wind picks up. Ice and rain slam against the house. Chester and I perch on the couch and watch Netflix until 10:00. 

I let Chester out one final time, then climb into bed and close my eyes. 

The storm has softened. Gentle rain ticks on the roof. A breeze blows and stirs the branches outside my window. There’s always been something...about the sound of a storm…*that puts me to…*

\*\*\*

I hear bluebirds chirping.

My eyes pop open. 

Sunlight pours through an open window. I slept through the entire night. 

I shuffle down the hall, rubbing the sleep from my eyes. Maybe there wasn’t an intruder after all. But whatever footage was captured last night will put this to rest—once and for all. 

I climb the ladder, pop out the SD card, and head downstairs. Chester is sitting by the back door, wagging his tail. I let him out. Then I open my laptop up on the kitchen counter and slide in the card. 

A notification appears: *a new recording: 2:00 A.M.*

I feel a chill prickle across the back of my neck. Unless the camera malfunctioned, any motion at all means something was out there. I drag the mouse over the playhead when there’s a *bang* at the back door. 

Chester wants in. 

I open the door and Chester trots inside. When I sit back at the table, Chester curls up by my feet. I take a breath. Here it goes. 

I hit play. 

The camera’s night vision casts a green glow on the hallway. The image is grainy. Down the hall on the right, at the top of the staircase, there’s movement. Something peeks its head around the wall, then climbs into the hallway.

*It’s Chester.* 

But, as I’ve said, I’ve never seen Chester climb stairs. Never.

With his head hunched, he stalks down the hallway, calculating each step like he’s trying to move in silence. When he’s a few feet from my door, he stops. 

His body twitches. 

He flattens his chest to the ground, pushes off his front legs, and rises into a standing position. He finds his balance. Steadies. And *walks* forward. 

*Tick-tick*…*tick-tick*…*tick-tick.*

He’s outside my door. He turns and faces it. Then stares. He’s so still…the camera’s motion detection disengages. 

The recording stops. 

I glance down, off the computer, and below the table at Chester. His eyes peer up into mine with a look of guilt. Like he knows he’s just been caught. 

\*\*\*

I have since gotten rid of Chester. 

I don’t know what he was trying to do. Or how long he’d been trying to do it. 

But what disturbs me most is the recording I found later. 

It was taken at 3 A.M., when Chester finally sank back down on all fours and disappeared down the stairs.

While I was sleeping, he had been standing there for an entire hour. 

He was just…watching.
