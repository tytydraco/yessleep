I honestly don’t know how I should start this. I think I'm kind of still in some sorta shock after what happened yesterday.

I’m posting this in some random truck stop bathroom in some small town I’ve never heard of, using the little bit of data left on my phone hoping that someone can help me figure out what is going on or give me some idea of what to do. I’ve been on the road for a little over a day, my eyes are getting heavy and my whole body is sore. I can hear my heart pounding in my ears.

My mind is still racing with adrenaline and I'm not sure what I should do. I haven’t slept and I don’t know how long I’ll be able to run from this monstrous thing that’s chasing me. Anyway, I guess I'll start at the beginning. I'm Shaun, an eighteen-year-old quarterback from North Texas who saw something he shouldn’t have, and now what I can only describe as some sort of vampire is chasing me.

This all started this past Sunday night when my friends and I wanted to go out and celebrate us graduating from high school last week. My best friends Andy and John decided we should go to the local diner I work at and get some food before our night of celebration. We all met up about nine after I had worked my shift. We smiled as we sat down and ordered.

“So Shaun, are you excited about us going to Texas Tech next year?” John had asked with a hopeful look on his face. We had all agreed to go there because it was one of the few schools that had accepted all three of us and we'd all get to continue playing football there.

“Yeah man, I'm glad we are all gonna get to stick together. How are you and Sara doing? It's been forever since I saw you two have a moment apart much less a whole night,” I said with a laugh.

“Yeah, I swear a moment with just you has been as rare as catching Amber and Shaun in the same room without him staring like a love-sick puppy and stumbling over every word,” Andy said, patting John on the back and laughing heavily.

I tried steering the subject back to John. "So where is Sara?" I asked, scarfing down my food.

"I told her it was just us boys tonight. I wanted to tell y'all something," he said as he dug in his pocket, fishing a small box out of it. Then he opened it, revealing the diamond ring inside. "I'm gonna propose to Sara tomorrow. I know we are young but it's been 4 years now and she is gonna be going with us to college so I think it's time. I don't wanna mess this up," he said, beaming with a smile.

"Congrats brother, I'm glad y'all are so happy and I'm sure she'll say yes in a heartbeat," I said, smiling wide.

"Yeah she definitely will, so what’s up with you and Amber, Shaun? Are y’all going out yet?” Andy said with a smirk.

“Nah, not yet," I replied, not letting on that I had finally built up the courage to ask her out and had planned a date for next week.

We continued our small talk as we ate. After we finished, we decided to go mudding in my truck. We drank a little and blasted music, killing a few hours of the night, bringing back years of memories. Specifically of the first night I got my truck and of us enjoying our newfound freedom. After we got bored, I took John and Andy to pick up their cars and we decided to go to my house to hang out the rest of the night.

We arrived and walked inside and to my surprise it felt surprisingly colder than normal and it was oddly more quiet. I walked in and I instantly felt slightly on edge, goosebumps covered my arms and a pit formed in my stomach.

Just as I was considering why I felt this way, Andy asked me if they could spend the night and play some COD. This shook me from my uneasiness as I told them yeah, of course, and we just needed to ask my parents. My parents were old retired military of some sort, so they had always been strict growing up. Especially when it came to people staying in our house.

So I knew we needed to ask as we made our way into the kitchen. We were quickly greeted by my mom, my dad, and then some strange man dressed in an expensive-looking dark black suit. It was trimmed in a red silk and it even seemed to almost pull the very light from the room to itself.

“Hey, Mom, hey, Dad, do y'all mind if Andy and John stay over?” I asked. And as I did, the guy turned to look over at me and stare coldly, but he also held a smirk.

“Yes honey, of course, and this is your Uncle Alaric. He was really excited to meet you,” she said, her voice changing into an almost robotic, numb tone.

He quickly jumped in and introduced himself, standing quickly. The man towered over me at at least 6'6" to my 6'.

“Hello, Shaun. It’s fantastic to finally meet you. I've heard great things from your parents about you and, like your mother said, you may call me Alaric,” he said with a smile that chilled me to the bone and a strange accent I couldn't place.

He held out a hand to shake mine and as I took it, I realized just how strong his grip was. I thought it was strange that they hadn’t ever mentioned him before, along with the fact he had never been at any of our family reunions. I also got this weird feeling in my chest as he shook my hand. It was like an old instinct in me was screaming to run. Just like when I had walked in, only way stronger.

"I'm just here to catch up with your parents, it has been a very long time." He smiled and moved back to his seat at the table. I decided to shrug off my feelings and go upstairs to hang out with my friends.

“Damn John, your ass is at this game, Sara really is making you rusty,” Andy teased, laughing after destroying John in yet another one-v-one.

"What was up with your weird uncle, dude?" John had asked me, doing anything to change the subject.

"Uh, I don't know, never seen him in any pictures or anything," I said, stumbling over my words, just then realizing that.

"Yeah, did he give y'all the creeps like me?" Andy asked.

"Yeah man, honestly it was strange how much he creeped me out," I added, before we dropped the subject and continued playing. Then around three, John decided to go downstairs and get some water. As he did, I decided to chat with Andy.

“So uh, how has it been....at your aunt’s I mean?” I asked Andy, hoping my friend would open up to me. His mother had passed away a little over a year ago and he’d been living with his aunt since.

“It’s not too bad, though it’s basically like just having a roommate at this point. I told her I'm planning on moving out pretty soon and that next week I'd be looking at the dorms for Tech and a few other places. She looks a lot like my mom. I can see how much me being there is weighing on her. I'm assuming I remind her of my mom too,” he said with a dry laugh, a thin, fake smile on my friend's face.

Suddenly, we heard a rough crash and a loud, tortured, stomach-turning scream. It echoed throughout the whole house.

I started to feel sick as I quickly grabbed my hunting rifle from my closet, Andy and I moving to run downstairs. Another scream shot out, starting to fade. We rushed quicker only to be greeted by Alaric holding a now mangled John in his arms. Blood was pouring from John’s torn throat. The sickly red was pouring into nothing besides the mouth of Alaric.

John's yell had been reduced to a low gurgle. We watched the light fade from our friend's eyes right in front of us. I raised my rifle but I was stunned and filled with terror as my eyes met Alaric’s. Something was off, they weren't normal eyes. They were filled with this deep, blood-red color, only contrasted by dark black veins running through them to meet a pitch-black pupil. They were empty, completely lacking any emotion or remorse. Only replaced with what looked like an animalistic hunger—uncontained and uncontrolled.

Then I saw his mouth and my heart dropped as he pulled away from my friend, his blood still dripping from the man's mouth. He dropped my friend onto my hardwood floor with a loud thud, his body falling and landing in a broken and inhuman manner. As he dropped him, I saw four sharp, blade-like teeth stained with my friend’s blood. His face contorted into the same devilish smile he had held while shaking my hand.

It sent shivers down my back as the smell of rust filled my nose, and I pulled the trigger, aiming right at his face.

Everything was silent for a moment as I stared at the hole where his left eye used to be, and for a moment I felt relief. But it was short-lived, quickly shattered as the wound was quickly healed, leaving no trace it had ever been there. I froze, not believing anything I had just seen, but instantly I was broken from my trance by Andy. He grabbed my arm, yelling for me to run.

I noticed my parents' room slightly cracked, no sign of them anywhere. As I turned away hoping for the best we both took off up the stairs, knowing this thing was blocking the front door, we ran like hell up to my room, stopping only to lock the door before quickly moving to open the window.

My ears strained as I heard the slow stomping up the stairs, a demonic laugh filling my home. Then, quicker than humanly possible, I heard the laughing at my door and he punched straight through it, splintering the wood everywhere. I tackled my friend as if we were in practice again, feeling the rush of the fist just miss me. The monster reached for the knob as me and Andy jumped out of my window.

We both hit the ground hard, the instant shock standing me back on my feet, not noticing the pain at the time we ran to our separate vehicles. Andy whispered to me, "Split up and we can try to meet up later, he can't follow both of us."

I heard Alaric start yelling from the window in a language I didn't understand, only broken by his deep, old, corrupted laugh that cut through the night like a knife. Both of us dead-sprinted, Andy jumping in his car and me jumping into my truck. Both of us sped off in different directions.

I drove for what felt like hours until the sun finally came up. I decided to call my dad, some small part of me hoping somehow, someway, he’d be okay. The phone rang a few times before it was answered.

“Sorry, Shaun, your father can’t come to the phone right now. He is a little held up drowning in his own blood." He laughed as I heard him move the phone close to what I assumed was my parents until I heard their wet, sickening gargling.

"You can go to the police if you wish, but I have many connections and a sneaking suspicion they won't help you and that there will be no trace of you or your little friends. This is your first and only warning: I will hunt you and your friend down and when I do I will take great pleasure in slowly killing you both. Now our game has begun, so don’t disappoint me, kid. I'll leave here in, say, 30 minutes,” he said, laughing as the phone clicked.

The words kept repeating over and over in my head as I raced down some old back road. I kept driving for hours as I tried to process what I had been told. I wanted to break down and cry, but the fear overpowered my grief. I tried a few times to call Andy, but his phone went straight to voicemail every time I did.

I used some of the little money I had built up from working at the diner to fill up my tank and I kept driving, stopping only for gas, and continued like that until now.

I'm stopped at this rest stop to use the bathroom and write out this post. Please give me some advice, I don’t know what to do. I can't keep driving forever. I’m gonna keep trying to call Andy, but do I go to the police? Do I keep driving till I run out of money?

I’m honestly shaking as I write this out, my heart racing. Any little sound outside makes me jump. I'm terrified the moment I leave this bathroom that thing will be waiting for me outside. I don’t have anything but my wallet, my phone, and the clothes I’ve had on since Sunday morning. My truck has a shotgun in the back as well, but after the little the rifle did to this thing, I doubt it'll even leave a dent and Sara if you're reading this I'm sorry.

Please give me any advice y'all have.
