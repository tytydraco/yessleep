The man has been sitting outside of my house since before I can remember. Not always obviously. But frequently enough that he has fueled my nightmares since I can remember dreaming. 

I have never seen his face. He sits on the sidewalk out front. My parents had set up cameras and sensors for miles around our house to detect the man approaching. When he does, the family goes inside, locks the door and waits for him to leave.

My dad told me the story about my uncle who had recently been released from prison attempting to confront the man. 

His uncle approached the man with a kitchen knife. As he raised his hand with the blade in it, the man extended a freakishly long arm towards his wrist. He effortlessly broke the wrist holding the knife causing the uncle to release it into the waiting hand of the man. The man wrapped his arm around his uncle like an anaconda and squeezed him close before shaving his head with the kitchen knife until half of his brain was missing.

This story scared me and my sister enough that we never bothered to try to see the man. As long as we made it to the house before seeing his face we would be okay.

Yesterday he didn’t leave us a choice.

“He’s coming!” Our mom yelled with more urgency than usual.

“He’s doing the running thing!” She followed up with the reason for her urgency.

Down the road in the distance you could see him coming. He ran low to the ground and much faster than a regular human. He leans back like he’s sitting with his hands at his sides but his legs glide over the ground. We knew we were cooked the moment we see his face so we started running. 

We could hear faint footsteps as we approached the door which made my sister screech and we dove through the front door where our mother locked it behind us.

BANG!

Something his the door with such velocity that it cracked the frame of the door. The man made a low squealing sound that froze us all in our tracks. 

“Where’s dad?” I asked quietly.

“He’s walking the dogs. I tried to text him but he has always his phone on silent.” She replied. 

We waiting in silence for 20 minutes before we moved to the bedroom where we had the front lawn camera monitors. 

There he was. Sitting with his back towards the house.

We tried to call my dad over and over. Texting him from all phones. This was hardly unexpected behavior from him but the timing was terrible.

A few minutes passed and then we saw my dad and our dog approaching from the other side of the house than the one the man sat on. He did not see the man. Unfortunately the man did see my dad.

“NO!” My mom screeched as my dad approached the front door looking confused by the damage. She ran from the room and I was too scared to go after her. My eyes were glued to the man on the screen.

The man stood up and started to turn his body towards our front door. I shielded my eyes so I wouldn’t see his face but I knew where he intended to go. 

I opened this app to ask what I should do? Hide, run, give up?
