A couple years ago, me and my brother were exchanging ghost stories. Mine weren’t too great since most of the time I would either explain it away or just ignore it and assume it was my brain. As it probably was. But my view changed after what he told me. A little context: me and my brother had a really close relationship with one of our cousins who lived next door to us. He was older and a bit of an eccentric. Wearing long coats and fedoras sometimes. Borderline neckbeard territory, but this was the early 2000s so what can ya do. He ended up moving away to the west coast of the US and the story he tells me takes place right after he moved. I will be telling his perspective from the first person as I remember it. *Just after Chris moved, I remember waking up after midnight or so. Can’t remember. But you know how Mom’s house has that long hallway then a set of stairs down to the front door? Well, I was walking back from the kitchen when I heard Chris calling me from down the stairs in that room.* 



*Kept saying, ‘Hey man! I miss ya! Johnny, I miss ya!’ I turned and looked.* 

*The light was on downstairs and I could see the back of his hat brim and duster coat he used to wear disappear around the corner and head down to the basement. I was just barely able to watch the door close silently.”*

*Now me being the dumb guy I am, I walked down the stairs and approached the basement door. Keep in mind Chris is not even remotely close. Like I said, he just moved away clear across the country. Anyways, I lean up to the door. ‘Johnny! What are you doing, man? Come visit! Come on, man! At least give it back!’* 

*It was like he was joking and happy right up against the door. Then I heard footsteps walk down the steps and away from the door. The same heavy bootsteps he had when wearing boots. So... I opened the door. It was dark and you remember how the basement has like two separate lights with the pull string? Ok, picture the cinderblock room and chest freezer on the left and the light right in front of it turning on without the typical pull string sound.* 

*I walk down the stairs and back facing away from me, clear on the other side of the room, is Chris plain as day. Hat, duster and everything. ‘Johnny, come on now. I miss ya, bro! Bring it back at least. Why don’t you wanna come visit?’ That time his voice sounded sad. Then the light turned off. As soon as that happened I heard those same boots charge me through the darkness. I don’t know how fast I made it up the basement stairs before I closed the door and heard the boots collide with the door just behind me. And that was that.*

My brother’s name is Gordon. My name is John. Shortly after my cousin moved away, and soon after this story was told, he died in a freak accident. I was meaning to give back the saxophone he let me borrow, but I kept putting it off. But I guess it’s mine now. My brother keeps telling me I should give it back. But I didn’t have the heart to tell him what had happened. Even now, as I hear Chris calling me just before I fall asleep, I just keep ignoring it.
