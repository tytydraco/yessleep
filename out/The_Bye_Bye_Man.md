I wake up to the sound of my phone ringing on a Saturday afternoon. I yawn before picking it up, tired from the absurd amount of melatonin I took the night before. I check the caller ID to see my friend Andrew calling.

“Yo what’s up?” I instantly shot off.

“Aye man it’s been a minute, let’s skate! Jacob and Luna are coming too!”

I thought for a minute. Did I want to go outside? I checked out my window to see the sun pouring in, and decided I wasn’t going to waste the daylight. 

“Ya, give me 20 minutes.”

We met up on the old cul-de-sac on eldridge street where we played basketball, skated, and kicked shit on the weekends. We all had our skateboards, with Jacob’s board being objectively one of the shittiest boards any of us had seen. We teased him on it as a collective.

“So, now that we’re all here, what yall wanna do?” Luna paced with impatience, not liking the idea of sitting around. He was always an active kid.

“Thanks for bringing it up because I have an idea for us today” Andrew got the group's interests with this.

“What’s up?” Jacob questioned.

“You know that haunted house on the end of the road, by the forest right?” We all froze. We all knew what house he was talking about. The Baker house. Some poor old man moved in, claimed his family went missing, and was found dead, overdose. The weird part about the case was the police never found any relatives of him, meaning that his existence seemed to just be born out of nothing, his existence created nothing. Weird. “I wanna hit it up”

“Woah, that’s kinda far dude, somebody actually died in there” I tried to make my point; it didn’t matter if it was haunted or not, I just didn’t want to disrespect the dead. Andrew side eyed me like I just disrespected him instead.

“I mean, we got nothing else to do, and what, the case was like 30 something years ago? I doubt there’s anything and if something pulls up we’ll put belt to ass!” Jacob started laughing at his own joke contagiously, causing Luna and Andrew to laugh.

“Mannn, fuck it count me in” Luna agreed with the idea without much thought into my opinion. I just sighed, knowing I already got myself into some fuckshit.

“Let’s just go” I just wanted this to be over with quick.

The first thing that told me we were close was the feeling in my gut. Before I even saw the house, I could feel the negativity in the air like a static, some sort of dark aura. Then came the raggedy old lawn, then the beaten wood porch with snapped boards and hazards every step. Then the stench.

“It reeks like skunk in this bitch, you guys sure?” I asked one more time to confirm, with nods from all around. Andrew brought a lock pick for the door, so we wouldn’t have an issue getting in.

Stepping on the porch we agreed to split into groups. Me and Luna would go up, Andrew and Jacob would clear the main floor. We didn’t know exactly what we were looking for, we just had nothing better to do. Before stepping in I remembered something my dad told me when I first asked about ghost hunting in general. “Nothing will probably happen, but don’t tempt fate”. I learned from him not to try something like this but here I am.

The stairs were the first thing we saw walking in. We instantly took them, going to a little hallway on the second floor. 

“Imma check the bathroom, you check that desk over there” Luna pointed at an antique wooden desk near the end of the hallway. I nodded.

“Don’t take too long to shit brodie” I started walking to the desk. After opening up one of the cupboards I found a leather book, with a title written in red ink. I couldn’t understand what the characters wrote. I opened it up and saw a bunch of sketches, no comics. They all had some sort of writing, but it seemed to connect to something called “The Bye Bye man”.

I looked closer at the cartoons. It depicted isolation, but with the “bye bye man” always standing behind the main person. I was flipping through more before I heard Luna yell. 

“Dude what the fuck?”

“Luna what’s up?”

“Um- I think, I think we should head the fuck out” I turned back and jogged to the restroom to see what it was. As soon as I made it I swore the smell intensified. 

Then I see the bathtub; some sort of maroon star, with an ouiji board in the middle and candles.

 

Before I could even react we heard a step downstairs. Luna and I froze. Creak.

“Yo, you guys cooling?” Andrew? Andrew was in the house with us? I forgot. I now have a feeling somebody else was downstairs with him but I can’t place my finger on the feeling…

“Something’s wrong, that didn’t sound like Andrew” Luna whispered. I noticed it too. It sounded too flat to be him. Emotionless.

“Yo, you guys cooling?” Now it was weird, he said it the exact same way he just did, no change.

“Dude what is that?” I whispered over to Luna. He shrugged. 

We heard footsteps again. Coming up the stairs.

“Shit, move” Luna rushed quietly to the bathroom door, locking it. The heavy footsteps continued up and stopped at the top of the staircase.

“Why are you here?” This time it wasn’t Andrew talking. It was, Jacob? He was here too. I looked down to look at the book still in my hands. The page I was opened too changed, now reading “There were four of you when you came in”

“Hey Luna look at this, what could this mean?” I looked up to see Luna trying to open the bathroom window.

“Hold up man, I’ll check in a second but first we’re getting the fuck out”

The window jolted open, and Luna jumped out into the outside bushes to break his fall. I went to the window and looked down. It was so tall, I wasn’t sure if I could do it.

Knock Knock. Yep, that’s all it took. Something knocked on the bathroom door and I jumped out.

After landing, Luna and I went to the front of the house. We saw Andrew and Jacob outside.

“What are you guys doing here?” Luna asked. 

“What do you mean, we came here with you guys? You were in there for hours” Jacob seemed confused. I was too. I could’ve sworn it was just me and Luna.

“Ya dude, it’s getting late now, what were y’all doing?” Andrew asked.

“We-“ I thought before I spoke. “We just wanted to check the drawers upstairs. I found this” I showed them the page, drawing confusion from everybody.

“What is this supposed to mean bro, you had to have written that yourself” Jacob wasn’t buying it. Luna stood silent and pale. Andrew seemed, concerned.

“Let’s just get home man” I wanted this to be done.

The walk home was eerily silent. What usually would’ve been an ecstatic group turned lifeless. I turned to Luna.

“You good bro?” Luna looked over at me, holding his stomach, looking almost sick.

“Ya man, just a weird feeling I can’t shake off. I feel like we’re missing someone”

“Missing someone? Who? It was just us 4 when we went in the house?”

“I don’t know dude, just, I didn’t say nun alright?” 

“Alright bro”

That was that. We all went our separate ways to our houses shortly after.

Later that night before I went to bed I checked the page one last time. 

“There were four of you when you came in”

I rubbed my eyes. I felt cold fear surge through my body. A new sentence was written under it.

“3 came out.”

I rushed to check my phone. I looked through every photo I could, days in the neighborhood, late nights skating, sleepovers, and I couldn’t find evidence of anybody else.

I scrolled to the photos of the first neighborhood barbecue I had when I first moved and froze. It was all of us, me, Jacob, Andrew, and Luna. But Luna was holding his arm out like he was holding someone’s shoulder. Nobody was there.

Quickly, I tried to call Luna. His contact wasn’t on my phone anymore. I typed in his number. Welcome to Verizon wireless. This number has been changed, disconnected, or is no longer in service. 

I flipped the book back open, nearly tearing through the pages, before I saw it.

‘He didn’t forget, so he will be forgotten. Those who remember will be forgotten.’

I sat up straight. I couldn’t understand what was going on. Andrew called me, and I picked up still in shock.

“Yo, skate tomorrow?” That was it. No mention of Luna, or the house, nothing.

“Ya man, um, but do you know if Luna's good?”

“Who?” My stomach dropped. I sat in a moment of silence and realization before I answered.

“Nothing man, I’ll see you tomorrow”

I sat up all night trying to forget. How do you forget something you can’t stop thinking about? The only evidence Luna ever existed was in that photo, holding a mysterious nothingness. How was I supposed to forget?

I don’t know what happened. I must’ve fallen asleep at some point. But I woke up in hell.

I looked around. No no no, NO NO NO NO NO!

I was there again. On the second floor. Just laying on the bathroom floor. I peeked out the door. The book was still on the desk at the end of the hall. I looked towards the other end of the hall at the stairs. It was too dark to see any of the steps.

I creeped toward the book. I grabbed it and started flipping through it for answers. 

“Why are you here?” Luna?

I turned. 

“You didn’t forget, did you”

I froze. There was something, a shadow on the stairs. It was too dark to make out.

“What- what are you?”

“I’m what’s left”

Whispers shot through my ears. Adrenaline spiked in me. I ran to the bathroom. 

Screams echoed through my head, not from the house but inside my mind. 

The doors behind me as I ran started to slam open and shut. 

The lightbulbs started to shatter with force. I opened the bathroom door quick and-

I fell into my bed. 

“What the fuck?” I didn’t know what to think anymore. I looked over to see the book still on the desk.

I opened it. ‘There were four of you when you came in. I took one. 3 came out. 1 remembered. They are forgotten. Do you remember?’

Andrew called again.

“Hey, sorry for calling again, but have you seen Jose?” I stayed silent for a moment. 

“Who?” Andrew stayed silent for a moment.

“Nevermind” He hung up. I looked down at the book again.

I opened it. ‘There were four of you when you came in. I took one. 3 came out. 1 remembered. They are forgotten.’

I turned the page.

‘2 still remember.’
