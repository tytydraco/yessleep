I’m a pathological liar, always have been. Ever since I was a child I’ve found it easier to lie whenever I face a difficult situation. And even when I’m not I do tend to lie about the most irrelevant stuff. I may say stuff like “Yeah, my cousin is a fireman” when I don’t even have a cousin. I’ve never found a reason for why I do this. The point is this has always tended to backfire on me one way or another. Either my lies get tangled up or I simply forget I told them. This has made it really hard for me to connect with people, so I’ve never had many friends. This story is from when I was in the third year of middle school (I was fifteen at the time). I live in Mexico so the system here would make it freshman year of high school in the US. As I’ve said I had very few friends and none of them were really close. I hung out with a group of about eight kids, and it’s been a while since I’ve talked to them so I don’t really remember their names. The only one I do remember vividly is this one kid called Octavian (Octavio in spanish). He was a little taller than average, a little lanky and not really outstanding physically, except for his charming hazel eyes. But he was also smart. And I mean really smart, like he could do hard math without a calculator and read books we couldn’t even begin to understand. He was also really good at reading people, he could almost always tell when I told a lie, so he was one of my closer friends (If you could call it that).  
I live in Mexico City, so for those who don’t know I might need to make some clarifications. First of all, it’s a very big city, and I mean absolutely huge. I don’t think anyone has seen it in its entirety and I’m not the exception. It’s kind of like a maze, even for its own people. But I have a really good sense of orientation so I’ve never gotten lost, it’s kind of the only thing going for me. I’ve never been really booksmart or streetsmart, but if the group ever needed a guide, I was their guy. This is important because, number two, the city has a very convoluted public transport system. We’ve got buses, cable cars, cablebuses, metrobuses but the main thing most people use is the subway. I’ve used it since I was little so I’m really familiarized with it. My parents have never been the worrying kind so they didn’t ever put many restrictions on where I go. And even when they asked I always lied so it wouldn’t seem that bad. Looking back I wish they had because, number three, it is a very dangerous city. Just this year seven thousand people have gone missing in the city. And it’s worse in certain areas, like where I used to live. It was a very poor suburb (here we call them colonies so I’m gonna stick with that term), so even if someone could be found easily, police don’t really care unless you’ve got money. The last thing you should know is about this thing called “The Missing People Roundabout”. It’s fairly recent, a few years ago some families took over a roundabout and filled it with posters of their missing people. It’s kind of haunting to see but most people just drive past it.  
That’s what I’ve never liked about this country, everyone just ignores all the stuff that happens until it affects them. My dad used to say that the roundabout is just filled with delinquents and that the people who are missing did something to deserve it, it always makes my blood boil. Because the fear that you could go missing, it never goes away. It’s not only the roundabout, you see posters on the street, on TV, on the radio, everywhere, it’s like you can’t escape it.   
The point is, one day, near the end of the school year, my friend group and I went to a shopping mall near our school. It’s where most people from our school hung out after classes so we sometimes encountered them while walking. As usual some couldn’t make it, either because they had homework or more caring parents. So there were only five of us, including Octavian and me. We wandered through the mall for a while until we got bored. I think it was a Friday so we had plenty of time to get to our homes. So we decided to instead wander through the city. It is generally not recommended to do this, especially for teenagers. But we were in a group so we thought we might be okay. We hopped onto the subway, on line 1, and decided to get off on “Revolution Monument” station. We were headed for the Chapultepec Forest and even though there were other easier routes, Octavian said he wanted to check a store on our way there. I don’t even remember what we were going to the forest for. Even though it’s called a forest it’s really more like a huge park filled with museums and a zoo, and stuff like that. There was some daylight but not enough for us to get comfortable so we hurried through Reform avenue and ran to the forest.  
And on our way, there it was, as haunting as ever, the Missing People Roundabout. The other guys and girls didn’t really pay attention to it, most never do, but I’ve always felt repelled by its sight. Octavian was leading the race this time. I think he had something he wanted to see in the forest. He was always the one to propose the plans, and it was easy for him to convince us to join him. This wasn’t the exception. But this time I just couldn’t dare to even get near the roundabout. I told him I got injured on the knee and that they should go ahead while I got better, I was lying of course. But he instantly called me out on it, which was very strange of him. I mean, he always could tell my lies but he didn’t use to call me out like that, he’d just look at me complicitly, like he was the one telling the lie. It was like he was determined to get us to the forest, one way or another, but I just couldn’t bring myself to even get around that stupid roundabout. Another thing I should mention is that the families planted a tree on the roundabout, a cypress they like to call the “Guardian of missing people”. At the time I just couldn’t even bear to look at it, it made my head spin in all kinds of ways. Two of my friends helped me walk through the way, they didn’t even question why I was feeling that way or why we had to get to the forest, Octavian just told them “There’s something I’ve gotta show you”.  
And as we got past the roundabout, the mental noise got dimmer and dimmer, until it eventually faded. We got to the forest but something was off, almost no people were there, even the merchants that usually flood the zone were missing from their carts. Octavian said they were probably on vacation, which made no sense since it was a Friday and the forest should’ve been brimming with people. But as we got deeper and deeper I could feel how we were getting farther and farther away from people. When one of us eventually called him out he told us “We’ve just gotta get to the castle”. You see, in the first section of the forest there’s “The castle of Chapultepec”. It was built during the french invasion for the emperor Maximilian, right up until he was shot. It connects a lot of points in the city: The Pines Office, the Reform Avenue, The national auditorium, etc. But it was weird for us to be going there. It’s not a popular place to gather. Everyone knew Octavian liked history so we assumed he might show us something he found out but I blurted out something without even thinking “Octavian, we passed the castle ages ago, where are you taking us?”. I didn’t even realize it at the time but I was right, we had been walking for more time than even the forest required. I looked around and I couldn’t recognize which part of the forest we were in.  
He told me I was lying again, and everyone believed him. After all, I had always been a liar, but I swore I wasn’t lying this time. I told him we were nowhere near the castle and that we should head back. His voice started to grow sharper, telling me if I wanted to go so bad I should just go but even if most of the people there weren’t my friends I couldn’t leave them behind. He finally snapped and pulled me aside. “Why are you doubting me? I’ve never lied to you?” he said. “I just know you’re wrong, this isn’t the way to the castle” I responded. “And which castle do you think we’re going to?” He asked. I was confused. There’s only one castle in Chapultepec. He could tell I was getting scared so he grabbed my shoulders and looked at me with his grayish eyes. “This city is bigger and older than you, why do you think you know it so well?” I was petrified. All the noise had died, I couldn’t even hear my friends “I just do.” I responded “Everyone else is following me, why can’t you?” His eyes got paler and paler “I’ve been the only one defending you even with all your lies.” I couldn’t speak, his voice surrounded every thought I had. I tried to look back but the path wasn’t even visible. The trees seemed to be looking at me, like their swirls and branches made faces. Some cried, some weeped but they were all looking at me. I looked at him and he told me “Do you ever wonder how all the missing people can fit in one city?” I didn’t understand the question. “Everyday people die in this city, they get robbed and murdered but you rarely see any corpses. And yet people live all the same, they go to work, get on the subway, and live their lives. Why can’t you?”  
I looked at his hand on my shoulder. It felt like a grip such a lanky boy shouldn’t be able to produce. I looked at his feet, he didn’t have any shoes on. In fact, his feet seemed covered in dirt. “Do you think these people would care if you died? Let me tell you something. This city was built over a lake. When the founders drained the lake it left a hole underneath the city. People tried to get closer to what was underneath but humans were never meant to go that low. The city started sinking even before it got finished, the settlers had to fill the hole with something.” He explained “What are you saying? Are you going to fill the hole with corpses?” I asked “No, no, a human would never fill such a void. A tree’s roots on the other hand can go down miles. Every generation of gardeners has been tasked with keeping the void filled but it needs to be carried out a certain way. When a flower doesn’t grow right we just cut it, it serves for fertilizing. I don’t even need all of you for this year. So if you just shut up and lie like you do, I can let you go.” He let go of me. He got back with our friends. He told them all was right, and he looked at me with his hazel eyes and that complicit look.   
I told them I needed to go home, I just wasn’t feeling well. But as I was about to leave, I grabbed one of the girls by the arm and shouted we needed to escape. I ran for the exit of the forest. But when I looked back, the other guys were just standing there, acting like nothing had happened. Even the girl I had grabbed was furiously trying to go back. I looked at Octavian, his eyes were fully white but I could tell he was looking at me, not furious but rather complicit. The ground started to shake and I ran like I had never in my life. I don’t remember the rest very well. I blacked out and woke up in the middle of the Missing People Roundabout, just below the cypress, holding a bunch of tree branches. I got on the subway, not even conscious of which station I was on but when I got out I was right in front of my house, which is weird because no station leaves me directly in my house. When my parents asked me what happened I told them a group of robbers had left me unconscious and taken my friends. When the police asked for the missing students, I told them I knew nothing about it.  
To this day the memory of the incident haunts me. No one at school asked me about my missing friends, not even the ones that had survived just by not going. My parents got a little more cautious after that, not that I would want to go out anyway. I don't have any friends anymore. To this day I try to go out as little as possible. Whenever I do I feel like a pair of eyes are watching me from every direction, whether it is the missing people posters or the trees. The other day I went to see my cousin on his birthday. I decided to bring him a little plant. His little hazel eyes sparkled when he saw it, “It’s an orchid” he said. He’s very smart for his age. The shade of grey in his eyes looked just like someone I knew.
