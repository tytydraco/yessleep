I knew the rent was too good to be true. Four hundred a month for a studio in this city usually means someone died in it, or the plumbing consists of rusted hopes and dreams. I didn’t care. I was desperate, broke, and needed a place to sleep that wasn't my car.

The building is old, one of those brick tenements that smells permanently of boiled cabbage and damp wool. The landlord, Mr. Henderson, didn’t ask for a deposit and gave me keys that looked like they’d been chewed on.

For the first week, it was fine. I got used to the street noise and the radiator clanking like a dying robot.

It started on Tuesday. I was sitting on my futon, scrolling on my phone, when I heard a sound from the wall behind my head. It wasn't the skittering of mice—I’ve lived in enough cheap apartments to know that sound. This was slower. 

Deliberate.

It sounded like a fingernail gently tapping against the inside of the drywall. Tap. Tap. Tap. Then a pause, as if waiting for a response.

I ignored it. Old buildings settle. Pipes expand.

Wednesday night, I was brushing my teeth. The bathroom wall shares a partition with the hallway. As I spat into the sink, I heard a distinct exhale right next to my ear. It was wet and heavy, like someone with a chest cold sighing in frustration.

I froze, foam dripping down my chin. "Hello?" I felt like an idiot talking to a wall.

Silence. Then, a very quiet, very muffled sound. It took me a second to realize it was a giggle. A low, guttural giggle that sounded like it was coming through layers of cotton. I finished up quickly and slept with the lights on.

Yesterday is when I realized this wasn't just weird plumbing or rats.

I was on the phone with my mom, pacing the small living area. I was complaining about my new job. "The manager is a total tyrant," I said.

From the wall near the kitchenette, a voice echoed back. "...tyrant..." It sounded like someone speaking with their mouth pressed tight against the plaster.

I dropped my phone. "Who's there?" I yelled at the wall. "I'm calling the police!"

The scratching started up again, frantic this time. It moved along the wall, from the kitchen toward the corner where I was standing. It sounded like two hands scrabbling desperately for purchase.

Then, the wall bulged.

Just a little. A section of drywall about the size of a head pressed outward, cracking the cheap white paint. A dark stain began to seep through the center of the bulge. It smelled metallic, like old copper pennies and spoiled meat.

I grabbed a kitchen knife—a useless gesture, but I was panicked. "Get out!" I screamed.

The movement stopped instantly. The bulging plaster retreated slightly.

Then the voice spoke again. It wasn't muffled anymore. It was terrifyingly clear, vibrating the floorboards beneath my feet.

"I can't get out," the man's voice croaked. It sounded dry, papery, like old leaves crumbling. "I crawled in too deep. The studs... they shifted. They're pinching me."

I stared at the stain on the wall.

"I'm so hungry," the voice whimpered. "I can see your cereal box through the crack. Please. Just slide a handful through the gap by the outlet."

I didn't feed him. I ran out of the apartment and spent the night at a 24-hour diner.

I’m back now. I had to come back; all my stuff is here, and I have nowhere else to go. I’m sitting in the middle of the room, as far from the walls as possible.

I’m writing this because the bargaining has stopped. He’s not asking for cereal anymore. 

For the last hour, I’ve just heard a low, steady grinding sound. Like bone scraping against wood. And every few minutes, the wall by the door bulges out a little further, and the cracks get a little wider. He’s done asking for help. He’s trying to push his way through.
