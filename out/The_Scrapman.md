As an intern for a small, busy local news outlet, I rarely was able to indulge my interest in the supernatural or occult. With my often-limited free time I would frequent online forums detailing encounters with the other-worldly, eager to apply my journalistic skills and learn more. By sheer chance, I became friends with another forum member and occult enthusiast named Charlie, and even more fortunate that he offered me the opportunity I had been searching for. The first chance I was able to, I hopped on a plane and flew across the Atlantic.

Charlie had lived in the same large town for the majority of his life. Although the place itself was entirely unremarkable in every sense of the word, he described a period of two months in his childhood during the 90s that left a wound in the town’s history that would never truly heal.

The events to the best of his recollection began with a simple saying.

 

“Don’t be bad, or you’ll be Scrapman’s new toy!”

 

Who created the concept of Scrapman would remain forever unknown, but somehow, it became the new boogieman amongst the children in the town, spreading like wildfire, to the point even the parents picked it up and used the saying. The Scrapman itself was never given a concrete form, varying wildly from a fully autonomous machine to a serial killer with mechanical augmentations, but it was universally agreed upon that it was a malevolent entity. What made Scrapman stranger was that there had never been a mention of anything like it in local folklore or history of the town or its surrounding settlements. As such, it was dismissed as an odd trend that would eventually die out.

This would have been the case, if not for the first toy robot to appear on an unsuspecting family’s door overnight. The toy, shoddily made with pieces of scrap metal bent and welded together, featured a small button and speaker, that when activated let out a wet garbled static. Initially treated as a prank, the sentiment would soon change as over the following weeks, each day a different family would wake to their own small mechanical surprise at their door, each sporting an equally unintelligible cry.

Some other events had been transpiring simultaneously to the appearance of the toys. Much of this information I had gathered from old newspaper clippings, as well as a now retired officer who had been assigned to the case at the time. The officer, known as Scott, was at first reluctant to discuss the events with an outsider (understandably). It was from a combination of my persistence and a significant sum of my current savings, that Scott eventually relented and provided another perspective on the story. As he was recounting his experience, I saw that Scott never looked up as he spoke. His voice filled with regret and remnants of fear left to stew.

Roughly the same time the toys had started appearing, wildlife rangers in the nearby forest had been reporting an increase in unusual deaths of the local fauna. Scott recalls writing in his reports that the unfortunate animals had been viciously torn apart. However, increasingly peculiar was that the autopsies for each animal noted clean extractions of organs and bones. Someone, something, appeared to be harvesting body parts.

As time progressed, the townsfolk would continue to awaken to new robots outside their doors. In contrast to the awful quality figures at the start, the toys now appeared to be of better make.  Each iteration turned up with sleeker, cleaner metallic coats. The speakers that came with them however remained incomprehensible, until they weren’t. To the grim surprise of some unfortunate families, some robots’ speakers worked perfectly. Each sound recording lasted around 2 minutes and was always of their child mid conversation with family or friends, completely oblivious to the odd mechanical breathing and scraping. The mood turned sour quickly and soon enough people had set up cameras intent on catching the culprit leaving the toys. The police also became further involved, seeking a lead for what had previously been a public nuisance now turned to a serious harassment case.

The whole situation would reach a boiling point a month and a half after the first toy appeared, when, the body of a child was found in the middle of the road one morning. The body would have been completely unrecognisable had it not been for the nametag in the clothes they were wearing. Alongside them lay a robot, though not of the new well-made models, but a sharp and rusty malformed figure, the speakers completely dead. Upon deconstructing the robots, organic matter belonging to the victim was shown to have been used in its creation. All toys were collected as evidence and were never seen by the public again.

This tragedy would repeat itself twice more, before the townsfolk could take no more. They marched through the town and forest in search of any evidence of the identity of the culprit, to no avail. In the end, the robots stopped appearing, and the investigation wavered. All leads led to a dead end, and eventually most of the town would move on from the events. As my available time was drawing to an end, I would pay respect to the victims graves alongside Scott and Charlie before returning home, resolving myself to come back in the future and seek answers for myself and the others who had suffered.

As I reached the entrance of my flat, I found a small robotic figure outside my door. The world narrowed around it. I stood frozen in fear. After several minutes, I tentatively pushed the button upon its chest. My voice – a conversation, one I did not record. And beneath it, a slow metallic wheezing, almost like laughter.
