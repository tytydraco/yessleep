We were having a little get together with my old college buddies. Most of them talked about their kids and their jobs and the gas prices going up, the usual small talk when there’s nothing much going on in life. When the booze finally came, we started to get all personal. He cheated on me this, she’s taking the kids that, my boss is a pompous prick those. One of them talked about their golden retriever getting euthanized after biting an HOA neighbor, the kind that always cuts their bushes in this box way.

It was in the late evening that one of the guys started talking about his situation. Jake was his name and he was one of the richer dudes in the group, the richest one if you were being blunt. You can tell by the gold chain wrapped around his neck, his white Rolex that was encrusted in diamonds, and the red Porsche he had parked outside the bar. He was that rich

He was also the stereotypical trust fund baby, with the slick back hair and the polo shirts and yes, he was an insufferable brat who brags about his cars and his numerous trips to the Bahamas. We only hung around him for his money, and he didn’t seem to mind. Kind of like a slave-master dynamic. He was different now though.

His hair was less slick back and more stray strand filled, and his blue polo shirt had little stains of yellow and black that dulled away in the dim light of the bar, you can still notice them if your right beside the guy. A far cry from the usual preppy atmosphere.

He was always the one that talked at first, but he barely said anything up until this point, so when he had his piece to say we all went silent.

He had this full body mirror installed in his shower a week ago. A couple of days of use and nothing out of the ordinary happened.

“These guns still looked alright at the time, Hehe he.” he said flexing his biceps with a toothy grin.

You can tell that he was nervous with the way that he was looking. Moving his eyes side to side repeatedly to see if anyone laughed. No one did, it wasn’t even a joke.

It was all fine and dandy. Then while he was scrubbing his six packs of glory, his words, not mine, he looked up at his face and his eyelids disappeared in his reflection. The folds of skin that wrapped around the two eyeballs were now gone, leaving a face that stared endlessly at him with wide, red streaked eyes. He ignored it in true Jake fashion and went on with his day not before looking at his phone camera just to make sure. Eyelids are intact, no cause for concern, he thought.

The next day, he saw the same thing in his bedroom mirror. Eyeballs attached to the face, never blinking. This time little pieces of skin were removed from the reflection, near the eye holes. The naked eyeballs were now surrounded by dark red meat that made his face more sunken.

He jumped to the phone and his face was normal. It was just in his head he said to himself over and over and over again. He looked in his bedroom mirror once again and the same sight greeted him. He ignored the image and went on with his day.

The day went on and on until he went for his late-night shower. What he saw in that mirror made him tremble, every hair on his body stood up all at once when he saw his reflection missing an entire chunk of skin. His face was degloved, showing the stringing muscles of his cheeks and the bloody crater of his nose. The entire face was crimson red except for the teeth, which were perfectly white and placed. It mimicked the same jaw dropped expression of Jake.

This was the current state of his face he said. Everywhere he went he could see that skinless mug staring at him, even if he wasn’t even remotely looking, he felt those red eyes creeping along his body as if it was searching for something, something to take from him.

At this point he tried a shrink. Figured a couple of pills will get him back into shape but it didn’t work out. The drugs just gave him insomnia.

“I tell you, therapist are scammers first and foremost” he said slamming his beer glass on the table.

  His eyes were turning red and small drips of water started to form in his eyes. His mouth was trembling too.

All of us looked at each other in small, awkward glances, sipping away at our cocktails and beer bottles. We had to cheer this guy up otherwise he wouldn’t pay the tab.

We looked at each other again and something came up in our minds. Jake was always the type of person to take pictures in a group get together. Maybe a group photo would cheer him up. So, we suggested the idea saying it was getting late and we ought to cherish this memory with a photo.

Code for “I want to get out of here as soon as possible, please pay the bill, thank you”

“No thanks guys, I’m just not in the mood for this.” Jake said. But we cut him off with a flash of light from one of our phones.

A photo was taken and everyone gathered around the phone to see if the right angle was taken or the right lighting was implemented. I just wanted to see if I blinked or not. Jake didn’t even bother to look so one of the guys showed the picture to him while he was taking a sip.

His eyes widened and He dropped to the floor, falling off of the bar stool with a thud. He was breathing short breaths in a rapid sort of way. Sweat was dripping from his forehead. He paused there for a few moments before bolting towards the exit, almost ramming into it. The sound of an engine roared so loud it cut off every conversation in the bar. Then the screeching of tires.

“So, who’s paying?” one of the guys said.

We all agreed to split the bill and went out without a problem, waving goodbye and all that, except for Jake.

A couple of weeks passed after that. I was at work when the news broke out.

Jake was found dead in his bathroom with the shower still running. They found the room flooded and his body bloated and pale with little hues of light green on the wet side. His face was carved out of his body with the scissors responsible for the crime beside him, bloody with little strings of meat sticking to the metal blades. No prints they said, nothing from him or anyone. His eyes staring at the ceiling.

Nobody showed up to his funeral. Not even his parents or his sister, or the college friends that were in the bar. It was just me and the priest awkwardly looking at each other as the casket was lowered to the ground. Rest in peace Jake.

After the funeral I looked at the group photo we had together and a cold sweat came over me. The skin of everyone’s faces was gone. All of them, except Jake’s face.
