The thought hit me with the weight of a physical blow: Andy laced it. Asshole. The sheer, visceral shock of staring into my own blue-green irises within the head of that cephalopod was far too detailed, too undeniably real to be some mere byproduct of a bad batch. I needed a tether to reality—or at least some proof of the lie. I didn’t give a damn about the risk of another white-out; I only cared about what was currently coursing through my system. I stumbled across the room to where my backpack lay discarded, my fingers trembling with a raw, adrenaline-fueled desperation as I tore through the mess of notebooks and discarded textbooks. Finally, my hand closed around the small plastic pillow. I dragged it over to the kitchen sink, squinting under the flickering, harsh fluorescent light. The powder within looked pristine—white, crystalline, and deceptively perfect. I gave the baggie a gentle shake, watching the particulate settle. If he had cut it, the addition was invisible to the naked eye, save for perhaps a stray grain of something darker, like a fine, obsidian sand. I scraped a microscopic dusting onto my student ID and stared, trying to convince myself it was just a trick of the light. It looked too normal. The only remaining conclusion was that Andy hadn’t just cut it; he had fundamentally altered it. I shoved the baggie into my pocket and snatched my keys off the counter. Before I confronted Andy I needed more concrete proof, luckily I knew someone who could do an assay for me.   
Lucky for me I caught Matt just outside the chemistry building. He was on a walk between class and lab work.  
“Hey Matt, I need your help with something.”  
“Jeez Dan you scared me, you can't just sneak up on someone when you look like that.”   
I mumbled an apology to him, it was the first time it occurred to me that I hadn’t even changed my clothes in the last three days. My eyes were probably also sunken considering I'm not sure if I have actually slept in the past few days. “Matt, I really need you to check what something is for me. I got this from Andy a few days ago.” I held up the bag of coke. “Things have been really wrong both of the last two times I have taken it.”  
‘Put that away, christ we are on campus. Is that what I think it is, Dan?”  
“I don’t know Matt, that's why I am here. I know you have assay kits in the lab. Please for my sake can you do this?”  
Matt let out an exasperated breath and combed me with his eyes again. “Alright, but its gonna cost you, and I won't be able to get to it until later tonight but here,” He pulled an empty dime bag. “Put some in here, and don’t take anymore until I call you tomorrow.”  
Using my Student ID I transferred some of the powder into his baggy. Matt then took it from me and marked it with an A.  
“Thank you, Matt, I owe you.” I pulled out my wallet and handed him cash.  
Despite Matt's warning I decided that taking a bump would help me get through the night. Luckily this time I didn’t experience any of the hallucinations this time; it just made me feel as if I was walking through water. Before long I found myself, half walking half swimming home. By the time I had reached my apartment building it had begun to feel normal that I would swim up the staircase. As I pushed in the door, I dropped my bag and watched it slowly fall to the ground next to my coat hooks. I sat down to play some videogames, but before I could really start, I found myself struggling to keep my heavy lids open, my body dragged itself to bed before I totally lost consciousness.   
I awoke to my phone vibrating in my pants, I pulled it out and looked at it for a moment. It was 4:15 AM and Matt's name sat there on the caller ID.   
“Hey man, sorry if I woke you, but the assay just came back. It's pretty pure, there is some filler but I don’t have a test for that. Anyways, it's only 10%.” Matt's voice came out of the tinny phone speaker.  
“It's 4 am of course you woke me, but thanks, good to know Andy was skimping on me. I'm going back to sleep now.” I hung up the call. I was still dead tired but, knowing that it was cut, I needed to see Andy’s face, to look past that awkward smile and find the truth buried beneath his "friendly" warnings.

I banged on the rickety door of Andy’s run down house. I waited for 4 minutes on his porch before I started banging again. “Andy, I got to talk to you *now*! I think you gave me the wrong thing last time I was here.” I knew the shouting would make him let me in. Sure enough in a matter of moments the Door swung open, there stood Andy, with a respirator hanging around his neck, otherwise he was clad in his usual band t-shirt and dirty jeans.   
“Alright man come in, come in.” I step in and Andy shut the door behind me. “What the fuck is your problem man?” Andy was glaring at me.  
“Your shit has been making me see things. Twice now right after using your coke I have had serious hallucinations." His expression changed from one of annoyance to one of interest when I mentioned hallucinations.   
“Really, hallucinations? What did you see?”\`  
“Like the ocean? But like deep in the ocean.”  
“Actually, can you hold that thought, I'm in the middle of something. Come on, follow me.” He pulled his respirator up and started towards the basement, waving me to follow. Which I hesitantly did. As we reached the top of the stairs he pulled a second respirator from a hook and handed it to me. “You are gonna want to put that one before we start going down. It's not toxic or anything but it's just better to not breathe it.”  
“Breathe what?” I ask, but before I can get an answer Andy is descending the stairs in the darkness. Still weary of where I am being led, I slowly make my way down after him, where he is waiting outside a tarp door way. He pushes one of the flaps out of our way and beacons me into a room filled with fish tanks. Each fish tank was full of aquatic fungus, each glowing a different color, the water was being cycled between the tanks, some sort of particulate floated in most of the tanks. Andy approached one of the tanks, it appeared to be the final one in a chain of many tanks. The water was very clear and no fungus grew here. He reached down behind the tank and pulled forth two full bottles of water. Cracking one he handed to me before opening his own, pulling down his respirator and taking a deep swig.  
“Sorry man, I have to regularly drain this tank or it will overflow. This,” Andy gestured to the array of tanks, “is my new method of water purification. Once you've started the fungus colonies it's almost entirely passive.”  
Seeing he hadn't pulled his respirator back up. I took my own off. “Very cool man, but can we talk about what you put in your coke? And why did I have to wear a respirator?”  
“Look I cut it with baking soda, you wanted more than I had, and I thought it might help you ween. But I can’t see why that would cause hallucinations? Are you taking something else? Maybe it's a medicine interaction?”  
“No, you know I don't take any medicine.” I offered dryly.  
“Then maybe it's a sign you should quit or a stress symptom. You know more about the brain than I do man.”  
I doubted I was going to get anything more out of him so I pivoted back to my other line of questioning. “Ok fine, but what about the respirator? We seem fine here.”  
“The fungus won't produce spores underwater. So I have a cultivation lab down here too. You don’t want to accidentally inhale those spores when you walk by. So just a precaution.” He offered me his awkward smile.   
“I don't want to inhale it but drinking it is just fine?”  
“Well, no you wouldn't want to drink the spores either, but they don't spore in the water. The fungus is like a magnet to anything dissolved in the water, it feeds off that and in turn cleans the water. Then I just run it through a fine filter to pull any fungus particulate out, and voilà clean water.”  He walked over to a tank not in the chain. “Look at this one, it was the first tank where I started the cultivation. It's only been growing for 3 months, but it's covered the whole tank.” He was right, one could not see the gravel bottom of the tank as it was covered in technicolor mushrooms. Mushrooms I had seen before, in my water logged apartment.  “You can even grind it and use it as a dietary supplement.”  
“You sure some of that powder didn't make its way into my coke?”  
“Nah, It definitely didn’t, I wouldn’t waste it on you, I need it to show investors. Anyways it would probably not have any effect besides burning like hell, you know not a fine enough powder really. Hey, try the water.”  
Prompted on by him, I finally took a sip. It was surprisingly clean tasting, I didn’t even taste trace minerals.   
“How the fuck did you come up with this whole system?”  
“I had been working with the fungus for a couple of weeks for a lab, and then one night I had a dream about the fungus underwater. So I began testing it in water to see what would happen.”  
“Alrigth, so you dreamt all this up, Why show it to me?”  
“Dan, I'm going to need a hand, since I have gotten the contract with the sanitation department. I know you are a talented lab worker and you've been struggling with your PhD. Come help me.”  
His sudden generosity caught me off guard. “You want my help?’  
“I mean as long as you can stay clean. Sorry but I got to add that stipulation to make sure you are worth my money.”  
“I’ll think about it.” I could tell Andy wasn’t telling me the whole truth. I needed to talk to someone about what I had just learned but it had to be someone beside Andy. “Can I call you tomorrow about this?”  
“Sure, Dan, I start installation tomorrow so you may have to come visit me on site.”  
I pulled my respirator back over my face and went back up the stairs, and out of the house. I was about to do something stupid but Dana was the only person who would hear me out on this, of that I was certain.

I stood in front of an all too familiar door and took a deep breath before rapping on it.  A young Asian woman who once loved me answered the door, the way she glared at me made my skin crawl. “Hi Dana.”  
“What do you want, Dan?” She held her voice as flat as possible.  
“Look I need to talk to you about something, and I know it's gonna sound crazy.”  
“Dan, we aren't getting back together, especially if you still haven’t quit.” She began to close the door on me but I caught it before it got too far.  
“Its not about us. I think Andy is drugging me. Please just listen to me.” I could see her soften as my voice broke with my last plea.  
“Fine, come in.” I followed her into the living room before taking a seat in the arm chair, while she sat on the couch. “Alright, why do you believe Andy is ‘drugging’ you?”  
I hadn’t even begun to think about how to explain what was going on. “ Every time I have done his Coke recently I have been teleported to another aquatic world. So I had Matt test it for me, and it was cut, Andy never cuts his stuff.  And then when I was just there he showed me Fungus from that other world. Fungus he's gonna put into the water supply. The fungus has to be what's doing it.” I could hear my own voice accelerating as I spoke, to the point where the last sentence came out without any breaks. I could see fear creeping into Dana’s eyes.   
“Dan, this is why you need to quit, it's cooking your brain. Teleporting to another world? You know that makes no sense.”  
“Ok so maybe it's just hallucinations. It doesn’t change that Andy is gonna dose everyone with the mushroom.”  
“Dan, He offered you a job. He’s not trying to drug everyone, he's trying to go clean like you should.”  
“How did you know he offered me a job?”  
“Because I asked him to, Dan I still want what's best for you.”  
“Thanks Dana, you always know what's best.” My words dripped in venom. I stood and saw myself out. I didn’t need to be patronized by her. I needed help, and I could think of only one other person who might believe me if I could communicate with them. My cephalopod self. 

I pushed through the green door to my apartment, I looked for my backpack that I had dropped at the door earlier today, but it was nowhere to be found. My coke was in there and without that I won’t be able to see the other me. Just as I was despairing, I heard a slimy thunk from across the room. Slowly turning to face the sound I was greeted by my own eyes, hiding behind clear eyelids. The Cephalopod had clutched in one a hand a slate and the other some sort of stylus. Before I could speak he began to write in English.  
I write little of your language. Powder is dangerous, it makes my house touch your house. Too much powder makes your house my house. Then you're stuck with me.  
The Cephalopod skin pulsed primarily red as he wrote. “Can you understand me?” He nodded his “head” in an affirmatory gesture. “My *friend* is gonna add the powder to our water supply, everyone is gonna be consuming it soon.” Before I could even finish talking he had erased the slate and began writing  
Must stop, will destroy both houses. I help  
Before he could finish the sentence, he began to fade. At first I believed it was him changing color, camouflaging himself to my couch, but then the slate and stylus were also gone.  
“Fuck, I have to call Andy.” I pull out my phone, it rings for 30 seconds before I hit the automated voice box. “I don't have time for this Andy” I shout at no one in particular. I knew if I was quick down the stairs I could catch the next bus, and maybe catch Andy before he destroys our town.
