I used to think the worst thing about my apartment was the noise.

Every night at exactly 2:17 a.m., there’d be a soft knocking sound coming from somewhere inside the walls. Not loud—just enough to wake me if I was in that half-asleep state. Three knocks. A pause. Then three more.

I live alone, top floor, old building. I figured it was pipes at first. Or maybe a neighbor. Old buildings make weird sounds, right?

That’s what I told myself for the first week.

Then it started changing.

One night, instead of the usual pattern, the knocking came in a different rhythm:

Two knocks. Pause. One knock.

I remember lying there, staring at the ceiling, trying to map it to something logical. Pipes don’t change patterns. Not like that.

I got up, pressed my ear against the wall. Silence.

The moment I pulled away—

Knock. Knock.

Right behind me.

I spun around so fast I nearly fell. But there was nothing there. Just my dimly lit bedroom and the closet door, slightly open like I always left it.

I didn’t sleep the rest of that night.

The next morning, I decided to ask my landlord. He’s the kind of guy who shrugs at everything.

“Old place,” he said. “Walls settle. You’ll get used to it.”

“Do the walls usually sound like… patterns?” I asked.

He laughed. “Only if you’re listening for them.”

That answer didn’t help.

A few days later, I tried something stupid.

When the knocking started again—2:17 a.m., like clockwork—I knocked back.

Same pattern. Three knocks. Pause. Three knocks.

For a few seconds, nothing happened.

Then—

Three knocks. Pause. Three knocks.

Exactly the same.

My chest tightened. That wasn’t pipes.

That was… a response.

After that, it escalated.

The knocking got closer each night. It didn’t stay in the walls anymore—it moved. One night it came from the floor beneath my bed. Another night, from the ceiling directly above my face.

And always at 2:17.

Always answering.

I started recording it.

The first playback made my stomach drop.

Because it wasn’t just knocking.

Underneath the sound, faint but unmistakable, there was something else.

Breathing.

Slow. Uneven. Like someone trying very hard to stay quiet.

I stopped knocking back after that.

But it didn’t matter.

It kept going anyway.

Last week, I woke up before 2:17.

I don’t know why. Something just felt… wrong. Like the room was too quiet.

I checked my phone. 2:16 a.m.

I held my breath, waiting.

Nothing.

2:17 came and went.

No knocking.

No sound at all.

For the first time in weeks, there was silence.

I remember feeling relief… for about five seconds.

Then I heard it.

Not from the walls.

Not from the floor.

From inside my room.

Right next to the bed.

A soft voice, barely above a whisper:

“…you didn’t answer tonight.”

I couldn’t move. I couldn’t even turn my head.

The voice came again, closer this time.

“I waited.”

Something brushed the side of my mattress. Like fingers dragging slowly along the fabric.

“I thought we were talking.”

I don’t know how long I lay there. Eventually, I must’ve passed out.

When I woke up, everything looked normal.

Except for one thing.

On the wall beside my bed—where there had never been any marks before—there were faint indentations.

Grouped in patterns.

Three. Pause. Three.

And beneath them, scratched so lightly I almost missed it:

2:17

I’m writing this now because tonight it changed again.

It didn’t wait for 2:17.

It started early.

And it’s not coming from the walls anymore.

It’s coming from inside the closet.

The door I always leave slightly open?

It’s closed now.

I didn’t close it.

And something on the other side just knocked.

Two. Pause. One.

I think it wants me to answer.

I don’t think it’s going to stop if I don’t.
