I feel like I’m at my breaking point.  

My family’s world was shaken off its axis a while ago, and it hasn’t recovered at all. In fact, with each passing day, I’m beginning to forget what our old lives even looked like. 

Our household consists of myself, my husband, and our four year old son- Gideon. My husband works ridiculous hours at his office, so it’s often just Gideon and I at home. To be frank, he’s everything I’ve ever prayed for. We’ve suffered through infertility for many years before Gideon, so everyday I have with him is a blessing. When he was younger, my friends often joked with me that he was a classic “trick baby”, a child so well behaved they faux you into having more children. He’s sweet and smiley, but still saves extra cuddles for his mama. In fact, he was so well behaved, I actually found myself *bored* most of the day. 

Until Grandfather moved in.

Grandfather is my husband’s dad. He is mute, blind, and almost completely deaf. He was blinded from a house fire he was in as a young adult, and has hearing issues from old age. Surprisingly enough, no one knows why he stopped talking. He came back from the war completely silent. My husband had told me stories of his laugh, and how he could sing better than any celebrity- those are all distant memories now. Of course, it was chalked up to simple PTSD from battle (which it *very well* could be), but I think it was something deeper.

Anyways, Grandfather had come to stay with my husband and I temporarily as his permanent caregiver was out for medical reasons. Despite his melancholy nature, Grandfather refused to be assisted by just anyone, and would put up a pretty nasty fight. As soon as his usual caregiver was alright to work, he’d return home. Until then, we had a roomate. 

Living with Grandfather was pleasant. We communicated on a device that turns text into braille, and vice versa. Due to this, our conversations were often simple and slow. Day to day phrases were short, such as: “Eat” , “More blankets?” or “Nap”. Before Grandfather came, I was a rather lonely young woman. Our estate was vast, and It was only Gideon and I. It was enjoyable to have someone to sit with, even in silence, and his care did not require anything too strenuous. We’d go out in the yard and watch Gideon pick apples. Regularly, Gideon would lay on Grandfather’s lap, and sleep. 

One day in the fall, I was doing Grandfather’s basic care. He was very antsy that morning, even going so far as to fight back on the care I was providing. I was very frustrated with him. Defeated, I pulled out our communication device and began typing. 

“What need?” 

He took longer to respond than usual. I had assumed I had done something to upset him. 

At this moment, our picture perfect life- changed forever. 

“Outside now Gideon Outside Outside Outside now Gideon Outside Outsi-“

As the words flashed on the device, I felt my blood freeze in my body. 

Where *was* Gideon? 

I raced to our backyard. He wouldn’t go out there *alone, would he?* As I darted toward the apple trees in our yard, I was relieved to not see a little tuft of brown hair sitting up in the branches. 

That is, until I saw a tuft of brown hair on the *ground* instead. 

He had the little basket in his hands, the apples he collected scattered over the leaf-covered ground. His eyes were wide open, body limp as a rag doll, blood leaking from the back of his head.

His *Head.* I had thought. He landed on his *head.* 

To be honest, the next few hours or so were a bit blurry, but I can give a general rundown of what occurred. I know that I called the ambulance, and all three of us went to the hospital where my husband met us. I remember Gideon getting rushed into surgery. I remember him coming out of surgery, just for them to tell us he was brain dead. I remember shoving my husband out of the way to throw up in the bathroom. I remember when his heart stopped while I was sobbing on the toilet. I remember when they declared his time of death, 5:00 PM. I remember *all of this.* 

What I’ll never forget though, is what happened *after.*

It had been 5 minutes since Gideon was declared dead. The staff had given us our privacy. The only sounds in the hospital room were my screams. My husband was outside yelling at the doctors about something I can’t place my finger on. Grandfather was wheeled in by a nurse. I didn’t think he was aware of what was happening. He sat in his chair, looking straight ahead at Gideon, face unchanged. I was hiccuping, on the verge of throwing up again, when suddenly,

Grandfather stood.

He stood, and walked over to my sons lifeless body, growing colder by the second. If I had to paint a recreation of the scene, I’d give Grandfathers face a slight smile, which almost sent me into a rage in the moment. He grasped Gideon’s hand, and lowered his face towards my sons. He inched closer, to the point where their eyes were touching. I watched on in partial disgust and confusion. Their noses touched, and Grandfather placed a kiss on his lips, before looking over at the clock. 

5:07.

A beep. Then another beep. 

A medical miracle. 

Since then, our lives have been wracked with stardom and fame. Interviews, Documentaries, News segments- everyone wanted to hear the story of the boy who conquered death. Churches called us regularly to do sermons on how the power of love woke our boy. Gideon’s preschool wants to name a day off in his honor. Not just for the obvious reasons but for…stranger ones. Not only did Gideon wake up completely fine from his accident, but he woke up *better.* His language was more advanced, he’s grown several inches, his teeth are straighter. At one point, we were at the hospital everyday so the doctors could figure out *what the hell* happened to our son. The doctors themselves were in awe at the nonsensical nature of these results.

There were also some… not so ideal changes. Gideon was more aloof. He napped a lot more, and ate a lot less. He was shyer, as well. In the past, Gideon was pretty friendly, always making a new friend. These days, he sticks to me like glue, and doesn’t like leaving the house at all. Everyone said it’s probably leftover trauma, but I’m not so sure.

No one was in that room but me. Grandfather went home to his caregiver a day after Gideon woke up. I tried visiting in person several times, but every time I’d stop by, Grandfather was sleeping and wouldn’t wake. I’d ask my husband to check on him, and every time he went things were A-okay. I’d come to terms with not knowing. I was okay with shutting up and accepting my miracle, even if it didn’t make any sense.

Then there was the *rot*.

I was giving Gideon a bath one day, when I noticed a new bruise. Not too shocking, I mean, he fell out of a tree. That being said, this one was new, and each day it got a little bigger. It was a horrendous shade of green, concave on the skin on Gideon’s shoulder. Whenever I touched it, the skin seemed weak and flimsy. 

“I’m taking Gideon to the doctor’s tomorrow” I whispered to my husband over dinner.

“Why?” My husband gave me a look as if I’d gone mad. “It’s just a bruise. The kid’s been at the doctors for the past three months. Give him a break.” 

I immediately felt bad. Was I overreacting? Maybe my mom-brain was blowing things out of proportion. 

So, I started wrapping the rot. Sorry, bruises.

I began wrapping them in gauze daily as new ones appeared. Gideon didn’t seem bothered by any pain, so I did my best to keep them clean and covered. Recently, both of his arms are fully wrapped in gauze. Maybe it’s gone too far. 

As I mentioned, Gideon also hasn’t been eating. That wasn’t entirely true. He is eating,

Just not what you’re *supposed* to eat. It was a couple of weeks ago when I first noticed. Gideon was seeming …better. Still slower and more aloof, but he was brighter than he had been lately and was excelling in his studies. I hadn’t let him back into the backyard since the incident, but his father insisted that I try to get him back to doing “normal kid things” again. So, I watched him as he played in the grass. It brought a smile to my face seeing him laugh like he used to that it *almost* distracted me from the sight at hand. 

He had a squirrel in his mouth. He unhinged his jaw. He swallowed it whole. He went back to playing. 

I sat there stupefied. I must really be losing it. 

Call me the worst mother in the world. Please, do. Maybe I need to hear it. 

Once a day, I take him outside to play, and he does… that. I don’t pay attention to it. I act like I can’t see. Sometimes it’s a bunny, or a bird, or a kitten- but it’s always an animal and it’s always alive. Does he know what he’s doing? I can’t burden him with more. He’s been through so much. I have my son back. I cannot complain. I should be grateful. 

Yesterday night, tragedy struck our family once again. Perhaps if I hadn’t turned a blind eye for so long, none of this would have happened. 

It was a regular-ish weekday, and I had dinner in the oven. I went to tuck my son into bed, and I found him already in bed under the covers. 

 “Hey buddy,” I crept into his room, and shuffled next to his race car bed. I slowly took the covers off of him. “Ready for be-“

Underneath the covers, Gideon was contorted into a ball, gnawing at his leg. He had made it through half of it already before looking up at me with that same aloof stare on his bloodstained face. Wide eyes looked up at me as his face twisted into something unreadable. He opened his mouth. He cried. The fire alarm blared. How long had I been up here? I can’t remember. 

“I-I can’t see anything, mommy” Gideon croaked. 

I’m done. I’ll no longer be an agent in my sons decay. 

I miss my son, and I’m going to do whatever it takes to fix him. 

Maybe, to get answers on anything, I have to start questioning everything.
