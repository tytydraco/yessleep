Back when I was a little child in the early 2000s late 1990s, I encountered something that I cannot quiet explain how or why it happened. I have asked people about this occurrence in close circles and have read through several posts on the web looking for someone who may have shared a similar experience to this.

My memory may be fuzzy on some of these accounts, but this is the start of something that has seemed to bother me for decades. so... here is goes:

When I was a small child, I lived in a small house with my family and shared a room with my then baby sibling; my parent's room was not even fifteen steps from my bed to their bed just one room over. The kitchen space was what you instantly walked into when you went to their room and you could see the brown kitchen counters and cabinets, kitchen sink, and window that outlooked into the neighbor's pine trees. It was a relatively quiet neighborhood where nothing ever happened, but we still kept our doors locked in the evening hours.

I had problems sleeping at night as a child so I would often go into my folk's room and fall asleep in the gap between them. In all fairness, it was because I would get too warm in my room and wait a few hours till my bed cooled off enough to go back to sleep in my own room.  I had done these countless nights, and this night was like the start of any other. I went to my parent's room and crawled between them, sleeping on my side so I was staring at my dad's back while he slept facing the doorway leading from their room to the kitchen. Sometime had passed, but when I woke up my dad's spot on the bed was missing, the linens and sheets of the bed thrown haphazardly over; my assumption was he went to go have a smoke or he had gone to work if it was early in the morning, the reason for him being awake was not what bothered me. 

As I laid there looking into the kitchen, I felt this creeping cold dread that catches in your throat and lung; the feeling prickling at your teeth. I saw this almost physical embodiment of static wash from the side of the doorway into the bedroom and up the side of the bed to where I laid, the static like that of staring into the snow of a tv screen. It washed up past the tossed bed linens and sheets and landed in my hand. turning into a slim brick like that of a TV remote. I remember turning my head and looking above me at the ceiling fan. The glass covers of the ceiling fan blinking at me and the fan becoming this screaming face that quickly transitioned to these slim static rectangles that moved like a selection screen. I could pick any dream, close my eyes and go to that dream; upon waking up the screens were still there, and I could continue this for as long as I wanted it to happen. I remember waking up the next morning covered in a blanket from the end of my parent's bed and the sunlight streaming through the curtains of the kitchen window and the semi-closed plastic shutters of my parents' bedroom window. 

From that day forward, this static followed me in the dark, vaguely shaped like a silhouette of a person that is stuck on a loop. Every house I have ever lived at this static figure always runs at me and stares at me from above when I try to sleep only to restart as soon as I blink to this same figure running out the door and down the dark hallway. It is never the same silhouette, some are short and others are tall, but they always do the same actions. As a child I asked them to leave me alone, or to give me space while I slept and they seem to almost listen. That same cold feeling following me when I encounter this. 

I don't know if when I was a child, I had an overactive imagination or that I was watching too much tv; I had ruled out sleep paralysis and lucid dreaming long ago since I could move and was alert during this situation. But this still follows me to this day, and that odd event, which never happened again after this one time, of the static wave was the catalyst for all of this revolving the static people. I said something about this to a, now distant, family member once in my teens after having dealt with this for years without explanation. They said that it may have to do with supernatural stuff, like spirits since I did live in areas with cemeteries, etc. 

I thought initially it was a load of hog wash, but that changed when I encountered one of these static things and asked it questions in the quietness of my room. I asked it why it bothered me, if it was someone I knew. That cold feeling I had grown accustomed to feeling becoming prevalent during this conversation. I came to find out that this static being was my grandfather, who had died when I was very small. I thought it was odd and had gone to sleep not thinking much about it till the next morning. A little brown sparrow kept trying to fly into the window where my mom was at. Sparrows are usually this particular bird we associate with my grandfather.
