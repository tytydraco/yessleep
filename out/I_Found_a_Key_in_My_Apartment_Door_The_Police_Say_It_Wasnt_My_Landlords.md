About a week ago I heard something in my apartment that made me get up and check the front door.

It was already unlocked.

At the time I didn’t think much of it. I walked through the place, made sure I was actually alone, and figured it was probably nothing.

But the more I thought about it later that night, the more it started to bother me.

I work the morning shift at a small coffee shop in a college town, and since I’m enrolled at the college myself money is usually pretty tight. Because of that I ended up renting a cheap room in an old warehouse nearby. It’s not exactly glamorous, but over time I’ve made the space my own and keep it pretty clean. The landlord rents the rooms out cheap, and I always figured he was trustworthy since he’s on the community board.

On Monday, before heading into work, I checked the weather like I usually do while getting ready. It was already about 70 degrees outside, so I figured I’d just walk. After finishing my shift I decided to take a different way home so I could cut through a park nearby. I ended up staying out longer than I planned, and by the time I got back to the warehouse the sun was already starting to go down.

I ran into the landlord outside for a second, but it was just a quick “hey, how’s it going” before I went through the side door and up the stairs to my room.

When I got to my door and put the key in the lock, I noticed it was already unlocked.

I didn’t think too much of it at the time. I always lock my door, but I figured that morning I must have been in a hurry to get outside and enjoy the weather and just forgot.

When I got inside, I went through the same routine I always do. I emptied my pockets, hung my keys up, dropped my wallet in the bowl I keep by the counter, and set my phone down.

I started walking toward my room to grab a change of clothes.

That’s when I noticed my bedroom door was closed.

That immediately made me feel uneasy. I live alone, and I never close my bedroom door. There’s really no reason for it.

I slowly opened the door, half expecting something to be wrong, but nothing looked out of place. Everything was exactly how I’d left it.

Still, I couldn’t shake the feeling something was off. I grabbed a change of clothes and went into the bathroom to change, but instead of sleeping in my room that night I decided to stay on the couch.

When I woke up Tuesday morning, I had almost forgotten about it. I went through my normal routine getting ready for work and didn’t really think about the door anymore.

But when I was about to leave, something stopped me.

I had just locked my door from the inside, and out of habit I reached down to check the handle to make sure it was actually locked.

That’s when I noticed there was already a key sitting in the lock.

It wasn’t mine.

My keys were still in my hand… and I had never seen that key before.

For a second I just froze. I didn’t know what to do. After a moment I reached down, pulled the key out of the lock, and just stared at it.

Then I grabbed my things, got in my car, and drove straight to my friend Tyler’s place.

I told him everything that had happened — the unlocked door, the bedroom door being closed, and the key I’d found sitting in the lock.

He was the one who suggested I call the landlord. Honestly, in the state of mind I was in, that hadn’t even crossed my mind yet.

When I called him the next morning, he sounded confused. He said he hadn’t been back to the warehouse since Monday evening — the same time I’d run into him outside while I was getting home.

According to him, he had just come from a community board meeting that night and had stopped by the warehouse briefly before heading home.

He swore he hadn’t been back since.

By Friday morning I was still staying at Tyler’s place. I hadn’t gone back to the warehouse since Tuesday.

That morning Tyler shook me awake and handed me his phone.

There was a news alert on the screen.

The headline read:

“Man Arrested After Hidden Cameras Found Inside Tenant’s Bedroom.”

As soon as I read it, I felt sick to my stomach.

Had he been watching me too? Was Monday the first time something like that had happened in my apartment? Or was it just my neighbor’s place?

A mix of doubt, disgust, and fear flooded my mind.

Once I managed to calm down a little, I decided the best thing I could do was go to the police and try to get some answers for myself. I brought the key with me and handed it over as evidence.

After that I went back to Tyler’s place. I didn’t feel safe enough to even think about going back to the warehouse.

A few hours later my phone rang.

“Hello, good afternoon. Is this Peyton?” the voice asked. “This is Detective McKinley. I’m calling about the key you brought in earlier today.”

My stomach tightened as I waited for him to continue.

“I’m sorry to inform you,” he said, “but the key you turned in does not belong to Mr. Timmons… your landlord.”

There was a short pause before he added,

“If you find anything else that might help the investigation, please don’t hesitate to contact us.”

I’m still lucky enough that Tyler’s letting me stay with him, and news about the landlord is spreading everywhere now.

But there’s one thing that hasn’t let me sleep since.

If the key in my door wasn’t my landlord’s…

then who unlocked my door Monday night?
