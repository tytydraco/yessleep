When I was studying at Harvard. Me and twelve other students chose to start a research team. Focused in the observing, documenting and development of biology in the north-african region. Not only for the possibility of flying out to Africa and going on an adventure. But also because it was getting boring in homeland america. We had trailed down rabbit holes of the American wilderness for around a couple years now and wanted to see more.

After a couple hard days of waiting for a response from the school board. We finally got one. It was approved. But under one circumstance.  
The group would be cut in half. Only six got to go and the others needed to stay behind. It was hard to choose. I was definitely going. It was my idea in the first place.   
Some guys we barely knew opted out and some others got peer pressured.  
One didn't want to go because their lover also opted out so it ended up with five.  
Me Charlott. My best friend Lia. A German exchange student called Noah. Logan. And Will, Lia’s boyfriend.  
After a grueling 19 hour flight to Algeria we finally arrived



We had a long drive ahead of us into the savana. Every couple hours we stopped to get something to eat or drink.  
We arrived at the site at sunrise July 1th and started to set up our tents.  
As I looked around I noticed that it really was as beautiful as in the pictures.  
Savana plants and trees roamed the horizon. Not a cloud in sight.  
A bit further away there was a very small seasonal lake around a couple hundred yards away.  
As we were unpacking our tents and rations and planning for the next week will said  
“Uhh, where is my tent? Noah, I asked you to pack it?”  
“Shit im sorry, it must still be back home.” Noah said hastily looking somewhat embarrassed  
“What  is wrong with you!? You pack everyone’s tent but mine?! Is this because of the shower thing?” Will screamed  
“Look im sorry it wasnt intentional I-”   
“Fuck off” will said cutting him off.  
As will was about to push noah Lia stepped in  
“Calm down! Look you can sleep in my ten-”  
“No. Forget it. I'll sleep outside ”  
“That's too dangerous! There are wild animals here!” Lia screamed. But it was already too late as will stormed around a bush out of sight  
Lia sights and goes to help logan with his tent

After all of the tents were set up, and i had set up my internet disc, Logan, Noah and Lia were going to camp out on a hill around half a mile away from where we set up our tents to maybe see some wild animals.  
We all layed down with our cameras dug our limbs into the sand to not   
move

“Holy shit. Look at that" Logan whispered. Too amazed at the black and white striped zebra to say anything else

“Its going to drink that lake,” I said amazed.  
We took around a billion pictures of the poor animal until it blubbered off and we called it a day.   
On our way down to the camp. As it was getting dark I realized. We hadn't seen Will all day.  
I could see that this had also occurred to Lia who now also had a somewhat pale expression on her face.  
“Will!” lia screamed as we where approaching the lit up camp  
It was silent for a few seconds until wills voice screamed out   
“Yea!”   
As we got to the tank will crept out from behind one of the tents with a somewhat annoyed look on his face  
“Where the hell were you guys” will asked irritated   
“There was this cool zebra we took pictures of and-”  
“And what? You didn't even have the idea to find and tell me first before you left me all alone here?”  
Again Lia didn't even get to respond before he wandered off to where he had put his sleeping bag. Somewhat under a tree next to our camp.  
Dinner was potatoes and rice that day. But Will refused to eat.  
“That looks disgusting. I would rather shit in a coffee mug and drink it then consume whatever that is” will said   
He had also ignored Noah all day.  
We finished our meals and went to our tents.   
I remember that night clearly. It started raining.  
But something was off with the rain. I really couldn't put my finger on what it was until writing this.   
The shade of the rain wasn't quite right. I have no idea if it was my tent that crafted this illusion but the rain seemed a subtle brown-red hue.  
There was a subtle snapping noise. Which I threw up to be thunder. But now when I look back on it I realize it was much worse.

  


Waking up that morning was like any other one. Just that what I would see when I opened the tent wasn't only the beautiful sunrise of the savana. Or the gentle shadows that would cast along the dunes. Or even some dry sticks on the ground from yesterday's storm.  
It was Will's mangled body hanging in the tree he was sleeping under last night. It was the faint yellow colored sand now dark brown red.  
It was his intestine torn impossibly long from one side of the tree to the other. It was the scream I heard Lia out. Like she attempted it when she lacked air in her lungs  
It was Will's severed head laying right next to my tent staring at me.

The memories of this day are blurry. Nearly unrecallable from my memories.  
I faintly remember Lia crying over the radio telling our driver to come pick us up. Or maybe that the driver said that he couldn't for the next couple days.   
We didn't go watch the animals that day nor cook potatoes and rice.  
As I'm writing this in my tent. After the sun has set. 

It has started raining again
