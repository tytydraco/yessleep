I have always loved wandering through forests and meadows. The further away from people, the better. Even at the age of 10, I spent entire days searching for beautiful views. I often found abandoned houses or cemeteries, strange post-war structures, hidden lakes deep in the forest, worn-out archaeological sites, abandoned factory or mine sites.

&#x200B;

I spent hours in local libraries gathering historical knowledge about the area. All the scary stories, battles, and interesting facts about the towns and their inhabitants. I also collected all the maps of the area. I acquired maps dating back hundreds of years. I copied everything and divided it into folders. A hobby from the days before the internet.

&#x200B;

As I mentioned, I always travelled by bike. Local roads, forgotten paths, stops in nearby villages. Over time, I met many wonderful people. Somewhere in a shop, I met a woman who invited me into her home, showing me her collection of handcrrafts collected over generations. People who remember the war and local "historians" who remember the strangest and most terrifying stories about the surrounding cities and people leaving there. 

&#x200B;

One day, I found a map in an old book of a place I only vaguely knew. A very old map from a time when people still believed in the forces of nature, mixing Christianity with ancient beliefs. It marked a forest. But within that forest was a large "black spot." The description said, "unexplored location due to difficult access." Out of curiosity, I checked my notes and discovered that it still looked like "unknown land." That was all I needed; a new mission had begun completely by accident.

&#x200B;

The more information I gathered, the more I became fixated on this place. I learned that there were local legends dating back centuries that no one wanted to settle there due to a "strange energy." During World War I, there was never a battle there (which is unheard of in this region) or people trying to start a life there to escape the war. During the interwar period, a large part of the forest was cleared, but the black spot on the map was never touched. However, there were witness testimonies/local legends that spoke of "strange forces" occurring in the area.

&#x200B;

I found information about groups of N×zi officials responsible for researching the occult for the Reich. They appeared unexpectedly. They evicted the surrounding villages. Only a few residents remained to help. Apparently, the Germans were eager to reach the depths of the black spot. Even them evacuated themselves after some time, kil××ng all witnesses.

&#x200B;

Armed with this knowledge, I set off on a three-day trip there. As always, I planned to spend the night in a tent. I usually went from farmer to farmer asking if I could pitch my tent in their yard. This always seemed like a safe option, and I could meet some very interesting people this way. Oh, those days before social media.

&#x200B;

That's how I met the X family. They agreed to make their garden my base of operations. They invited me to dinner. Everything proceeded in a friendly atmosphere. Suddenly, Mr. X asked where I was going. I answered truthfully. There was such silence that only the buzzing of mosquitoes could be heard.

 After a moment, X said:

"It's late. Time for bed." 

Everyone in the household rose from the table without a word, giving me strange looks.

&#x200B;

Early the next morning, I set off on foot towards the black spot. Around noon, I was very close to my destination. And that's when it all began. First, I felt I wasn't alone. I increasingly felt that "the forest was watching me." I noticed I could see birds, but it was very quiet. Suddenly, I felt resistance. It was as if the air had become thick. I looked straight ahead. I saw a dense, dark forest of massive trees. Not a forest. A strange wall of trees connected by thick branches. These trees were so massive, so sprawling, it was impossible to see inside. I checked the map. I was in place. "Black spot" was in front of me.

&#x200B;

I took a deep breath and tried again to move forward. But something held me back. I began to understand the meaning of the words "a place with strange energy." That's when I got truly scared. I turned around, and my heart stopped. Before me stood an extraordinary figure. His posture resembled that of someone very old. Strange markings/tattoos on his face. He wore fur and some fabrics combined into something resembling a cape/poncho. His hand rested on a wooden stick. He stared at me with his black eyes with such intensity, as if he were peering into my soul.

&#x200B;

He grabbed my hand and, holding it with great force, stared at the lines etched on my skin. Then he walked around me, examining me with his eyes. 

He stood before me again and spoke: 

\-"It's been a long time since someone like you came here. I know you want to go there, but you can't. This is no place for  you."

 Like a fool, instead of screaming, hoping someone would help me, I blurted out:

\- "Who the f are you?" 

The figure looked at me, completely ignoring my words. 

\- "You were born into a family where you weren't wanted. You prefer the forest to people. You don't hurt anyone. You're looking for something, but you don't know what. Like you don't belong in this era." 

\-"How the hell he knows this?" I whispered. 

The old man look around and continue:

\- "I've been here forever. I guard the entrance to the forest. But you. You're different. There's no hatred in you. I won't punish your desire to discover the truth about this place. But I'll never tell you the secrets hidden within." 

&#x200B;

And suddenly it got darker. The wind started blowing. Out of nowhere, a multitude of crows appeared. They made an increasing noise. I covered my ears. I closed my eyes. I thought I would die of fright.

&#x200B;

Suddenly, silence fell. I was in my tent. I nervously looked around. I saw my packages, my notebooks. I opened the tent. I was in the host's yard. Had I dreamed of this? Am I going crazy? What day and time is it? What's this all about? How did I return to the tent? I began nervously packing. I knew I had to get away from this place. Suddenly, to my surprise, I saw a necklace. Exactly the same one I'd seen around the neck of the strange man I met . Well, this wasn't packing anymore. This was an evacuation mission.

When I was ready, I grabbed my bike and was ready to go. Mr X stopped me. He said:

\-"I don't know why he let you come back, but if he had done this, he saw something in you."

&#x200B;

Oh my, how I felt then. Like in some scary movie, I was cycling like crazy. My poor bike was barely holding up. I didn't even know I could move that fast. I made it home. I didn't leave my own house for three days. I kept wondering what had happened. For years, I came up with many explanations. But nothing seemed logical. Years later, the most plausible explanation was that it was just a dream. But this necklace found in the tent is proof that it really happened.

&#x200B;

First, I moved to a big city. Then, even to another country. I haven't been back to my hometown in so long that I don't even remember when. 

&#x200B;

But sometimes, walking through the park, I see crows. They always charge and stare at me. Just like that old man, the forest guardian. They stare at me with those black eyes deep into my soul.
