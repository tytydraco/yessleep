So, I’m a writer. I like telling stories, and I love sharing my stories with the world. That being said, I’m also a workaholic, kind of a scatterbrain, and a wife and mom. I’m in my 40s now (early 40s, thank goodness) and I think how far I’ve been pushing myself all these years is starting to take a toll. I’ve got a day job, a side hustle and two amazing kids that need my attention, so by the time I get to sit down and actually get some writing done, it’s pretty late at night. 

What nobody tells you is that even on the early side of 40, your body can just go to sleep whenever the hell it wants to. Comfy chair? Time for a snooze. Right in the middle of watching a video stream with friends? Sleep comes for you with a gentle but firm cuddle you can not refuse. Lately it’s been sending me to snoozeville while I’m trying to write. 

It usually starts out normally - I sit down, sip some coffee I let get too cold, and clack away at the keyboard, off in my own little world. An hour or two later, my head jerks backwards and I gasp back to consciousness, then zombie walk my way to the bed, finally ready to admit defeat. It happened a few times to me before it started to get weird. 

So the first time this happened, I was sitting at my desk, settled in my overly comfy extra wide office chair I can sit criss-cross applesauce in, with Sill on my lap. Her purring and warmth must have tipped the scales because I don’t remember falling asleep. I don’t even remember going to bed. 

I woke up to the sound of my obnoxious alarm, and the kids running all over the house scrambling to find their school uniforms. Confused and groggy, I wandered back to my desk and grabbed some tylenol for my pounding head (a common state of the under-rested.) I chugged the old cold coffee and swallowed the pills. As they washed down I looked up at the screen and saw the following words (pasted directly from my writing program.)

She gazed at the never-ending expanse of desert and managed to put one grimy unshod foot in front of the other. Struggling for every step, panting but not sweating. Sweat means that your body has water in it, and hers decidedly did not. She was a dry wandering desert spirit. Dry dry dry. Her bones cracked. Her teeth bit down on her dry paper tongue. Her dry dry dry eyelids scratched down

It ended there. It was supremely weird. Certainly unusual and not at all like my writing style. I mean, it was up to the part where “hers decidedly did not.” - I use the word ‘decidedly’ far too often, so that was definitely me. I remembered the scene where my hero, a young desert dwelling magical girl was dragging her dry feet through the desert, but I *decidedly* did not go into the details of her bones, teeth and scratchy eyelids!? That was kinda spooky. It was much too much for my YA story - yikes I didn’t want to give kids nightmares. 

I asked the kids if they were messing with my computer at all and got somewhat honest sounding no’s. My husband was completely passed out so there’s no way he typed it. I chalked it up to being super tired and just…like stream-of-consciousness blurbing out anything my dreaming brain decided to type. Yikes, though, what was wrong with my sleepy brain?

We went about our days as usual, and as usual I was up late burning the midnight oil. It was probably about 1 or 2am when I started to get tired again. But, being the dedicated hack I have always wanted to be, I opened Obsidian and tried to employ the “write a little every day” practice. I really needed more practice and less preaching, so I started tapping away. Of course, after deleting the last couple sentences that just made no sense. 



I was damn proud of myself. I knocked out a thousand words and was gearing up for a thousand more, when Sill jumped in my lap. That should have been the warning sign that sleep was coming for me, whether I wanted it or not. I blinked, then woke up in my bed, 4 hours later. 

Ignoring the pounding headache this time, I raced to my computer, just on the odd feeling that maybe there was something there again. 

There was something there. 

As Runa lifted the shimmering gem to the light, rainbow colors cascaded across the sheepskin walls of her trading stand. The rainbow however, seemed a little off. Instead of the usual 7 colors blending into one another, it was blood. Bleeding, thick, decaying, corpusculent blood. It dripped from her face, her chin, her breasts, her rotting navel. Her bones cracked and her teeth bit down on her bloody tongue. Blood blood blood. Blood on the table. In your coffee. Do you like the taste of blood? Try it, don’t be a pussy.

What. The. Fudge. 

Ok, first of all, this is disgusting. Did I…don’t be a *pussy?*  I hate that word. I never use it, it’s demeaning to women. Blood in my coffee? What - I couldn’t even imagine what dream-me was thinking. A tiny little voice behind me sounded out “cor-pus-cu-lent?” 

I whipped my hand out and hit the monitor power button. 

“Hey! That was getting good!” my 10 year old grinned a devilish grin. 

I was breathing hard by now, trying to batten down the panic I could feel rising in my throat. “Ronnie, that is private!” I managed to gasp. She looked at me sideways then wandered back to her routine. While the morning chaos reaffirmed its noise level, I sat and stared at the blank monitor, just trying to breathe slowly and puzzle out what the heck was going on.   

Why were those words so disturbing? Why did they twist my innocent little fantasy novel into abject horror? I didn’t have time to ponder too hard, because I had to usher the kids off to school. 

Another busy day to look forward to, on not enough sleep. I wonder if it’ll happen again tonight?
