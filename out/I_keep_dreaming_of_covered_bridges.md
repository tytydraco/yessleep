I keep waking up drenched in sweat for the past SIX NIGHTS despite the shitty heating in my outdated farmhouse. It's not exactly see-your-breath-cold in my bedroom, but the low sixties shouldn't be leaving me with swamp-ass in the morning. Every night, I have the same weird dream that leaves me in a panic.

It all starts the same way. I'm walking along some dirt road in the woods, gravel crunching beneath my shoes and the damp, earthy autumn air filling my lungs. There's the occasional mud puddle. It SHOULD be winter, but hey... it's a dream. And like most dreams, you don't really question that shit in the moment. Knobby, fat sycamores hug the unpaved road, their near-bare branches intertwining overhead like lovers' laced fingers. The road must be well used despite it not being paved since I only crush the occasional leaf underfoot. 

After coming around a bend, there's an old covered bridge over a rocky stream. Some moss grows here and there on the planks, but for the most part, the red paint is in pretty good shape and it looks sound. I can't make out what's on the other end when I look through the entrance. It just looks... black on the inside after a couple feet in. That's where things start to go wrong. I can SEE the opposite bank of the stream, I can see the dirt road disappearing amongst the bramble, brush, and trees. The stupid bridge isn't huge or anything. It doesn't curve. It's an overcast day, sure... but it's mid afternoon, not midnight. It doesn't make sense and my stomach churns to just look at it. Usually, when something spooks me that badly in my sleep and seems so out-of-place, it snaps me right out of the dream with little more than a lingering pit in my stomach. I don't get so lucky with these dreams...

And of course, because fuck me and fuck dream logic, my body starts to move on its own. Some unseen bullshit dream magic starts pulling me towards that darkness at the mouth of the bridge. The first few nights, I would try and fail to fight my own legs into turning tail and running. Then I tried standing still. 

Nothing. Works.

Step by step, willing or not, I walk into that inky darkness within the covered bridge. The first night, I was dragged just a few steps in. Maybe five or six horrible, muscle-ripping, awful steps were forced out of me before I woke up kicking the air like a lunatic. The next night, I'm driven deeper into the inky blackness. Then deeper. By night five, it feels like I'm being puppeted miles into an endless black, my footsteps loud on shadowed planks. I can hear water below, babbling under the endless bridge. The air is ice-cold. These are the longest feeling dreams I've ever had, and the only company I get for these walks are my own terrified thoughts, unshakable dread, aching legs, and the dark. 

I always wake up feeling like I've ran a mile.

Night six is different. After the usual long, painful forced march into the bowels of the bridge, I finally see the other side. I would have ran for that sweet grayish light, but the silhouette of someone on the far end makes me pause. I am so distracted by whoever it is that I don’t even notice that the pull in my legs is gone. I take another step on my own volition. Then another.

As I draw nearer to whoever or whatever is at the exit of the bridge, I can make out their shape a little more. It looks like a woman. A curvy woman, maybe? After spending so much time in the dark, even the overcast light from outside is blinding, so I can't make out much more than that. The silhouette shifts a little, like she's holding out a hand. I can't really SEE her doing it, but again, dream logic. I just KNOW she is. Before I can get any closer, I wake up again, sweaty and breathing hard.

And no, you chucklefucks, it isn't because I almost touched the hand of a pretty lady. I'm engaged to a very sweet (and patient) man that could keep snoring through an apocalypse. Forgot to mention that I'm a woman, by the way. 

So... what should I do tonight? Should I go willingly into the bridge? Should I approach her? Should I take her hand? I just want this awful dream to stop. I feel so fucking stupid, being scared of a dumb fucking bridge, but now I can't even look at one driving around my area without wanting to vomit out my beater's window, and the lack of good sleep is starting to affect my work. Please, if you have any advice or insight, share it. I'm getting pretty desperate here. I'm just doom scrolling paranormal subs and ghost videos at this point and I don't know where else to talk about this. I feel like a little kid, whining about bad dreams to anyone that will listen. My fiancé doesn't really know what to make of this, but he sounds like he wants to try and find this bridge irl.
