I didn’t want to write this, not because I’m scared of how it sounds, but because I think it confirms something I’ve been trying to ignore. I’m not the only one.

After my last post, someone messaged me. No introduction, no small talk, just one line asking if I was in the flood. I ignored it at first, but then they sent another message asking if I still feel hungry even after eating. That’s when I replied.

We didn’t talk like normal people. It felt more like comparing symptoms. They told me they had memory gaps too, that they wake up exhausted, that something feels wrong inside them. Then they said something I never mentioned before, that they can’t eat normal food anymore, especially cooked food. That part hit me hard, because I’ve been dealing with the same thing and never told anyone.

After a while, they asked if I hear knocking sometimes. I asked what kind, and they sent it exactly like this, tap taptaptap-tap. I didn’t respond for a while after that. Then they said they think something is following them, and asked if we could meet.

I knew I shouldn’t go, but I did anyway.

We met near the area where the flood happened. That wasn’t planned, but it felt like it had to be there. The place is still wrong. The ground is dried but it smells like the water never really left. They were already there when I arrived, just standing still, facing away from me.

At first, they looked normal. But when they turned, I felt it immediately. Not fear, not shock, just recognition. They looked like me, not exactly, but close enough. Same tired eyes, same thin face, same kind of emptiness behind it.

We didn’t greet each other. The first thing they asked was if it had started yet. I didn’t need to ask what they meant. I told them it comes and goes, that it gets stronger at night, that sometimes I feel like I’m about to lose control. They just nodded like they already knew.

Then they said something I can’t stop thinking about. They said it doesn’t stop.

I asked what they meant, but they didn’t answer. Instead, they looked past me, like something was behind me, and asked if I could hear it. I couldn’t at first. Then I did.

Tap taptaptap-tap.

Not from a door, not from anywhere normal. Just somewhere out there in the dark.

We both turned, but there was nothing. When I looked back at them, they were smiling. That’s when things stopped feeling normal. It wasn’t a natural smile, it was too wide, too calm.

They said, “See? It comes for us.”

Then something shifted. Their body didn’t change, not at first. It was their eyes. That same empty look I’ve seen in myself, but deeper, like there was nothing behind them at all.

They said they tried to stop. That they stayed inside, avoided people, did everything right. Then they stepped closer and said that one night, they didn’t stop.

I stepped back without thinking. I didn’t need details. I understood exactly what they meant. That feeling I had in the water, the one I tried to ignore, they didn’t ignore it.

That’s when I noticed the smell. Not rot, not decay, something fresher than that. Then I saw their hands. There was something dark under their fingernails, just like mine.

The knocking came again, louder this time.

Tap taptaptap-tap.

And suddenly their expression changed. The smile disappeared and for the first time, they looked scared. Not of me, but of something behind me.

They stepped back quickly and said not now, almost like they were being interrupted. I turned, but there was nothing there. Still, I felt it. The same presence from before, the one from the door, the one from the water.

When I looked back, they were already moving away. Before leaving, they said not to let it stop me, and then louder, almost angry, they said next time I won’t want it to.

Then they ran.

I didn’t follow. I couldn’t. Because in that moment, I realized something I’ve been avoiding.

That feeling in the water, the one where everything went quiet and right, that wasn’t just survival. That was me.

And whatever that thing is, the one that knocks, it’s not hunting us.

It’s stopping us.

And I don’t know how many times it can do that.
