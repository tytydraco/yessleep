Aaliyah first noticed it in things.

Not the kind of things you point out. The kind you quietly dismiss.

Like how the ceiling fan in her room would slow down… not gradually but suddenly—as if something had interrupted it mid-motion.. How her phone battery drained faster on certain nights even when untouched the screen cold but somehow awake.

She blamed the wiring. The old building. Stress.

Midterms always made everything feel a bit off.

The library stayed open late during exam season. Aaliyah liked the silence it offered after midnight. It was different from the quiet—thicker. As if the building itself had settled into something once most people left.

That night she had taken a corner desk by the windows.

Outside the campus was still.

Inside the lights hummed overhead.

She had been reading the paragraph for the fifth time when she felt it.

Not a sound.

Not movement.

Just the awareness that she wasn’t alone anymore.

Aaliyah didn’t look up away. She told herself not to. The feeling would pass if she ignored it. It always did.

Then—

A chair shifted somewhere behind her.

Soft. Deliberate.

She turned.

There was someone seated two rows back.

She hadn’t heard footsteps. Hadn’t seen anyone walk in.

He wasn’t doing anything. Just sitting there one arm resting on the table his posture relaxed in a way that didn’t match the hour.

For a second she thought she must have missed him earlier.

People. Went all the time.

Still…

There was something about the way he was looking at the room.

Not at anything in it.

Just… through it.

Aaliyah turned back to her book.

Ignore it.

A few minutes passed.

Maybe longer.

Time felt strange in that silence.

When she glanced back again the chair was empty.

That should have been the end of it.

A forgettable moment.

Except that night she dreamt.

She was in the library again.

Same desk. Same book. Same stillness.

The lights were dimmer—too dim to read comfortably—and the windows showed nothing outside. Not darkness. Not sky.

Just… absence.

She could feel someone

Not behind her.

Closer.

“You stay long.”

The voice wasn’t loud. It didn’t echo.

It simply arrived.

Aaliyah turned.

He was standing between the shelves now.

Closer than before.

Close enough that she could see his face—almost.

It didn’t. Distort.

It just refused to settle into something she could remember.

“I have exams " she said, her voice steadier than she felt.

“That’s not what I meant.”

Something in the way he said it made her chest tighten.

“Then what did you mean?”

He didn’t answer away.

Instead he glanced toward the end of the library.

As if listening to something she couldn’t hear.

When he looked back at her his expression had changed— slightly but enough.

“Nothing " he said. "Not yet.”

She woke before Fajr, her heart beating faster than it should have.

The room was quiet.

Normal.

Her roommate asleep.

Aaliyah sat up slowly pressing her palms against her eyes.

“ a dream " she whispered.

She got up to make wudhu.

Routine steadied her. Grounded her.

By the time she finished praying the unease had faded—mostly.

The day she avoided the library.

Not consciously.

She just… found reasons not to go.

Studied in her room. In the hall. Anywhere else.

It worked.

Nothing strange happened.

No dreams.

No lingering feeling of being watched.

Yet by the third night the silence itself began to feel wrong.

Too clean.

Like something had stepped back—. Not left.

The power went out after 10 PM.

Half the hostel groaned in unison as the lights cut followed by the glow of phone screens flickering to life.

Aaliyah stepped out into the corridor leaning against the railing that overlooked the courtyard. A few students were gathered below laughing, calling out to each other in the dark.

Normal.

Grounded.

She exhaled.

“You stopped coming.”

The voice was beside her.

Aaliyah froze.

Slowly she turned her head.

He was leaning against the railing as if he had always been there.

Same calm posture.

Same unreadable expression.

Her first instinct was denial.

“You’re not actually here.”

A faint smile touched his lips—not amused, not mocking. Just… acknowledging.

“That makes it easier?”

She swallowed. "I didn’t say that.”

For a moment neither of them spoke.

The courtyard noise faded into the background muffled.

“You shouldn’t be here when it’s like this " he said quietly.

“Like what?”

He didn’t answer.

Instead his gaze shifted past her.

Aaliyah followed it.

At first she saw nothing.

Just the dark outline of the wall the faint shapes of trees swaying slightly in the night breeze.

Then—

Something moved.

Not a person.

Not an animal.

The shadow itself seemed to shift, stretching in a way that didn’t match the direction of the wind.

Aaliyah blinked.

It stopped.

“You saw it " he said.

It wasn’t a question.

Her pulse quickened. "No.”

A lie. Immediate. Instinctive.

He didn’t call it out.

Something, in his expression hardened.

“You should go inside.”

“. You?" she asked before she could stop herself.

A pause.

Then simply "I’m already where I need to be.”

The lights flickered on.

Bright. Sudden.

Aaliyah flinched.

When she looked again—

He was gone.

That night she didn’t sleep easily.

Every small sound felt louder.

Every shadow sharper.

At some point she sat up in bed, restless and reached for her phone.

2:17 AM.

She hesitated… quietly recited, her voice low but steady.

The room felt calmer after.

Not safer.

Just… less exposed.

The morning everything seemed normal again.

Too normal.

As if the night had folded in on itself. Erased any proof it had ever happened.

When Aaliyah stepped out into the corridor—

She stopped.

Near the wall, where the light didn’t fully reach—

There was a mark.

Faint.

Like something had brushed against the surface…. Pressed into it.

It hadn’t been there before.

She knew it hadn’t.

Aaliyah stared at it for a moment.

Then slowly she looked around.

For the time—

She didn’t feel like she was imagining things.

Somewhere just beyond the edge of where she could see—

Something shifted.
