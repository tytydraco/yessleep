I know this isn’t the best place to vent, but I’m not venting. This is going to come off as a weird request but I want to FORGET about my wife.

We were married for 5 years, she died 10 months ago. I should've paid more attention to her. Earlier this year we were both going through a rough patch. She’s always talked to me about how she wanted to leave, how she wanted to be just free. She’s told me about how her workplace doesn't treat her well, how she doesn’t get along with her friends anymore. I told her that it’s all going to be fine and that I’ll be there for her and I’ll help her get through it. 

After that, the things that she started saying got weirder. She started talking about how she’ll sometimes see herself run out the house and disappear in the forest. Like an out-of-body experience. My poor wife must have been so stressed and tired from work that she started hallucinating. I asked her to take time off from her job and that I’ll take care of everything for a while until she feels better. She refused and insisted that she was fine and that she’ll stretch it out more until her next pay. 

One evening. I received a call from her. I answered the call and said “Hey darling, are you off work already? want me to pick you up now?”   
Silence followed…  
“Darling? Can you hear me?” I asked, wondering if her signal is cutting her off.  
“I’m leaving..” Her voice came through the phone.  
“You’re leaving?” I repeated. I hurriedly put on my jeans and said “Oh okay, I’ll be on my way there now I–” before I could finish my sentence she cut me off.  
“I’m done..I’m sorry..I’m going to get help. I can’t do this anymore, I love you..” she hangs up the phone.  
I stared in disbelief at my phone screen, my beautiful wife’s picture displayed on her contact page. “What’s happening to my wife?..”  
I called her number again. No answer. Adrenaline rushed through my body and I sprinted out of our house without a second thought. Everything was a blur from there.

All that’s left are the missed calls I made to her phone and one final outgoing call. The last call that we had of her telling me good-bye. The terror that I felt upon seeing my dead wife on the stretcher being carried inside the ambulance as I sat on the road unable to move a single inch. The realization that she’s gone felt like the whole world around me collapsed.

Fast forward a few months, all I could think of was her. All of our memorable moments I’ve had with her kept playing in my head over and over. My nights were filled with thoughts of her—untouchable, yet right there. I remember how she would smile at me lovingly, every night. How her warm soft skin invigorates my whole body and her laugh that fills the room. Her hair that slides through my fingers like silky sheets woven only for me, I could even hear her delicate voice that whispers throughout the night. All of these thoughts make me desperate for her even more—I still need her every night. But now that’s gone. Everything else is gone. What was once the happiness and pleasure she once brought into my life is nowhere to be found. I miss her, I really do. After what seemed like months of emptiness, I decided to try and slowly pick myself back up. I couldn’t bear to see myself spiraling down, I know she wouldn’t want to see me like this too. So I moved out of our place and settled in another town–I had to. The ‘friends’ she had kept blowing up my phone and dropping in unannounced. Almost as if they only started caring about her now that she’s gone, what hypocrites. I had to get away, so I changed my phone number, deleted my socials, packed my bags and started a new life.

For a while, things seemed to calm down. I was managing my days much better than I had during the first few months after the accident. I started volunteering at a nearby community center, helping people who were struggling with grief and loss. Around the same time, I received a promotion at my sales job. For the first time in a long while, it felt like my life was moving in the right direction. I still dreamed about my wife every night, but I didn't complain. Those dreams were all I had left of her. No matter how painful it was to wake up afterward, it was worth it just to see her again.

Until recently, I no longer want to dream about her. 

Across the street from my house is a bus stop that hardly anyone uses, except for a woman I occasionally see waiting there. During the night, it's barely visible except for the patch of ground illuminated by the streetlight. That’s where I see her. At first glance you’d think she has white hair, but no. Her blonde hair appeared almost white beneath the harsh light shining down from above.

“Just like her’s..” I whispered under my breath. I quickly shook that idea off. “No.. what am I thinking, she looks nothing like her!” I grumbled silently and took a quick glance at her again. She looks like she’s wearing a red scarf, her gloves were red too. My wife’s favorite color. I think I got caught staring for too long because she gave me a small wave. I awkwardly waved back and hurried inside, nearly tripping over myself from embarrassment. I shut the door and leaned against it, my heart pounding. It felt as though I had just met my wife for the first time all over again. But as time passed, she began to look less like my wife and more like herself.

I know that probably sounds strange, but it's hard not to notice her when the bus stop sits directly across from my porch. Every now and then, when our eyes meet, we exchange a silent greeting. Somewhere along the way, I found myself checking the clock throughout the day, waiting for the time she'd be standing by the bus stop. I'm not sure how I feel about it, but I can't deny how nice it feels.

That's when everything started to go wrong. The dreams of my wife that visited me every night began to change. At first, she would simply lie beside me, looking at me with the same warmth she always had. Then, little by little, something became wrong with her. Her face would twist and distort. Her jaw would unhinge in a way no human jaw should. Yet despite the horror of it, her eyes remained the same. In those dreams, I could hear her voice clearly. The words seemed to come from her impossibly wide, unmoving mouth.

"Why are you doing that? Don't do this to me."

I would wake with a violent jolt, drenched in sweat. My heart hammered so hard that I could hear it inside my ears. I thought everything was finally getting better. I felt alive again. A change of scenery, a promotion at work, new friendships, and that woman from the bus stop. For the first time since the accident, I thought I was feeling it again—the feeling of love I thought I had lost.

But I was wrong.

I've always been a skeptic when it comes to anything supernatural, yet I couldn't shake the feeling that my wife was haunting me from beyond the grave. How else are you supposed to explain the nightmares that began the moment I started taking an interest in the woman at the bus stop? Every night, they grew worse.

"Don't do it, please. I thought you loved me."

She would scream the words with such desperation that even after I jolted awake, I could still hear them echoing in my ears. I’m sorry, this might sound shallow, but do you think my dead wife doesn’t like the fact that I might be moving on? I’ve already gone to therapy for this, but my therapist keeps saying the same thing. So I’m left wondering if it’s just my conscience, or my emotional state distorting my dreams instead.

Either way, I want the dreams to stop. The wife I see in my dreams isn’t the wife I remember. That’s not her. I want to stop whatever’s happening with these dreams. I’m starting to see her outside of them now too. Sometimes I catch her in my peripheral vision and hear fluttering footsteps behind me, though I’m not sure it’s really her anymore. She looks like her, but wrong. Bloodied, her skin raw and red like flesh, her hair dull and grey, almost white. If anyone knows what this could be, please help. I don’t care if it’s beyond logic anymore, I just need it to stop. I want to stop seeing my dead wife, I want to move on from my dead wife. I just want to feel love and normal again, but this grotesque looking woman that I see in my dreams haunting me isn't helping.
