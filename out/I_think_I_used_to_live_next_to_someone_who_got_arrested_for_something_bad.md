I wasn’t planning on posting this anywhere, but a friend told me I should get other opinions because I might be overthinking it.

I’m not saying this guy was anything like that. I don’t have proof, and I know how easy it is to connect dots after the fact. It’s just… some things don’t sit right now that I look back on them.

**I moved out a few months ago, but something’s been bothering me ever since.**

The guy who lived next door to me was… normal. That’s the easiest way to describe him. Quiet, polite, kept to himself. We’d nod to each other in the hallway, that kind of thing. I never even learned his name.

Looking back, there were a few small things that felt off, but at the time I didn’t question them.

He worked odd hours, I think. I’d hear his door open late at night sometimes—like 2 or 3 AM. Not every night, but enough to notice. Always just the door opening and closing. No voices, no footsteps coming back later.

He also got a lot of deliveries. Not unusual on its own, but it was always the same type of boxes. Medium-sized, plain, no branding. They’d sit outside his door sometimes for hours.

One time one of them got delivered to me by mistake. Same building, wrong flat.

I knocked on his door to give it back. He answered pretty quickly, like he was already standing right there.

I remember he looked… surprised to see me holding it.

Not annoyed. Just surprised.

He took it straight away, said thanks, and shut the door a bit faster than I expected. I heard multiple locks clicking after.

Again, didn’t think much of it.

The only thing that actually stuck with me at the time was a smell.

Not constant. Just every now and then, usually late at night. It wasn’t strong, just… strange. Kind of metallic, maybe? Hard to describe. I assumed it was something from outside or pipes or whatever.

Then one night there was noise.

Not loud, just different. Like something heavy being moved across the floor. It went on for a while. I almost knocked to ask if everything was okay, but I didn’t want to be that neighbour.

The next morning, everything was quiet again.

A few weeks after that, I moved out. Different area, didn’t think about it again.

Until last week.

I was scrolling through local news and saw an article about an arrest. No details really—just that someone had been taken in from my old building in connection with a “serious investigation.”

No name released.

But the photo they used of the building entrance was mine. Well, my old one.

I don’t know if it was him. It could’ve been anyone in the building.

But I keep thinking about those boxes.

And that smell.

And the fact that I never once heard anyone else in that flat.

Not a TV. Not music. Not another person

Just him

At least, I think it was just him
