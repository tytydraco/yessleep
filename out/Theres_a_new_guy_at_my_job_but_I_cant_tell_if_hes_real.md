He started at my job around a month ago. I work at a Tex-Mex restaurant called Piñas. The whole thing is outside and on bad weather days it can be a real drag to get through. It takes around 50 people to keep the place going, sometimes less. Our business varies day to day, depending on the season. Summer and winter are our slowest times, and we have a lot of turnover once the money leaves. I stick around because I can't seem to find anything else. The hours are flexible and my seniority lets me get away with a lot, so it's the perfect slacker job. Not that they want me to slack off; almost every week I have to train someone new, and almost every week someone else quits. 



That's why it didn't faze me the first time I saw him. That first shift I didn't think much. A blue t-shirt, jeans and black shoes. Just like the rest of us. He was a bit taller than average with short hair, but nothing about him stood out to me. He seemed like the kind of guy to stay for a month before he could land something better. I saw him a couple of times that first shift, shadowing Jordan. I would seat a table and look up and there he’d be, right behind Jordan's shoulder watching him navigate the POS interface.



I didn't see him again for about a week, but that's normal when people are still in training. Our managers never schedule you well until they think you'll stay. When I saw him again it was Tuesday night, one of the slowest, I mean seriously nobody comes in. This time I saw him shadow Rochelle. The second day of training as a server you're supposed to greet the tables on your own, and your trainer hangs back just in case you need help. But the new guy followed her like it was still his first day, staying a couple of steps behind while studying her as she input orders. My job as the host is to stand in the empty parking lot and wait for parties to come in. I instead spent most of it wandering around the restaurant pretending to clean things, smoking cigarettes, and making small talk with some of my coworkers. Later that night I found myself poking through the walk-in looking for the bin of chocolate hidden in the back. I climbed down from the and turned to face Rochelle and the new guy walking into the freezer. She’s closer to management than I am, and I knew she would say something. Quickly, I apologized for getting in their way, and made for the exit while trying to force the chocolate into my pocket. I glanced back to see if either of them had noticed and I found the new guy staring right back at me. I hadn’t noticed his eyes before. They were big and wet like a kid’s. 



“Are you alright?” Rochelle asked. “You’re looking kind of spacey…”



I apologized and left the freezer as fast as I could. I didn’t think much of the interaction then, I just felt relieved not to be caught.



After those first couple of shifts they began to schedule him more frequently. But he was still just shadowing. He never took his own tables, greeted customers or did much of anything, really. I realized I had never even seen him run food before. I think it was the fifth or sixth shift since he started that I got curious. Whenever management wants to hide from their responsibilities, they come to the host stand and pretend to help out while they talk or go on their phones. Denise was on the floor that morning and she loved to stand around and chat. We were talking about our cats, when I asked her about the new guy. 



“New guy? What do you mean?” She seemed surprised.



“He’s been shadowing a bunch of servers the past couple of weeks. Tallish?” 



“I think I would know if we hired someone new. The last guy we hired was Tom, and that was a while ago.”



But I knew who our bartender was, Tom had been at the restaurant for over two months at that point. It had only been two weeks for the new guy. I brushed it off. Denise wasn’t our best manager and if she hadn’t hired him there was a good chance she genuinely had no idea he worked here. I saw him again that afternoon, where I had hidden behind the dumpsters for a cigarette. I could hear him coming by the sound of his shoes. Each step the tops of his shoes seemed to disconnect from the soles, making a slight squeaking sound. He walked by following Jordan, but he wasn’t paying attention to him. He was staring right at me. Through the crack between the two dumpsters he caught my gaze. Our eyes connected for just a split second in the gap, and he smiled. I stamped out my cigarette and hurried back to the stand. 



I idly looked for new jobs on my phone; something about the guy seemed off. It’s the middle of summer though, and the heat makes hiring slow. I had to stick it out at Piñas for a little while longer. Thankfully though, I didn’t see him for a week. That week was nice, just slow summer days with nothing to do. My last shift of the week had been on Sunday night, normally the slowest second only to Monday. I had closed up everything early, leaving out only the essentials for any last minute customer. I had promised my boyfriend Eric that I’d be by his place hours ago. But after a call out, I had been made to stay until close. I was annoyed. Nobody had come in for over two hours, and they could’ve let me go home whenever. 



During times like these I liked to hide in the alley in the back. It’s less of an alley honestly and more of a patchy dirt and grass path between the back of the building and the fence separating us from the restaurant next door. There’s a single tree in a courtyard space a little ways down. You have to shimmy behind a ladder to get back there, so most of my coworkers only know the area from the view of the window in the pass. A stoner that used to work here told me about the alley a long time ago and it had become my spot. I liked to pace along its full length, smoking and ruminating on how the week was going. Car headlights from next door flitted through the slats in the fence and lit up the mossy, clover covered walls. That night I walked back and forth, the space entirely dark and lonelier than usual. I was halfway through my cigarette, and using my other hand I texted Eric to apologize and say I’d be there soon. Walking toward the courtyard I looked into the leaves of the tree, and pacing back to the entry I looked past the outcrop of the wall and toward the sky. I went like this at my usual pace, fuming at my bad luck. One length back and I found myself once more in the courtyard, looking through the window to the pass. My manager for the night, Kate, looked back. Now there was no chance of me getting off early. I scrambled to turn around, and tripped over myself getting to the exit.



Two glistening eyes met mine at the end of the alley. He had eight fingers clamped around the wall like an insect. Only the top of his head down to his wide, moon-like eyes showed.   
Then he barked. Short and raspy, like a small dog pretending to be big. He barked again and again and I stood frozen. Then he released his fingers, and the top of his head followed. I heard the squeak of his shoes grow quiet, and then silent. He was gone.



I didn’t see him for the rest of the shift, and after I clocked out, I ran to my car. Soon after I arrived at Eric’s apartment and gave my usual knock. We stood on his balcony in the humid air, flicking ashes into the wind. I told him everything about the new guy.



 “So?”



“What do you mean, “so”? This guy is really freaking me out, I don’t know what to do.”



“Why does he scare you so bad? He seems a little weird, yeah, but not dangerous.”



“You don’t get it.”



“I care about you. Ok? I just don’t think this is something to worry about sweetheart.” 



I glared at him. I stayed at his apartment for a little longer but left earlier than normal. I started to feel like it was all in my head. Maybe I was having stress hallucinations or maybe it was just schizophrenia finally hitting me. 



After that night I tried my best to ignore him. The only issue is that he wasn’t shadowing people anymore. During the shift he would appear suddenly behind tables, trees, and windows. He followed some of the servers, but never for the whole night like he used to. He wasn’t on the map for the server's sections, and after asking more of my coworkers, no one knew who I was talking about. I had officially lost my mind. But I didn’t have any other mental issues. No other hallucinations, no emotional changes, no drugs. I couldn’t understand what was happening to me.



This last part happened two nights ago. After everything that had happened with the new guy I had given up my alley and taken to smoking along the creek behind the restaurant. It ran along the back row of patio tables, separated by a small fence and a steep hill. To get back there you had to circle around our parking lot’s fence, and take the concrete stairs in the abandoned lot next door. I hadn’t seen him that night and I was feeling better than I had in a while. In the lot there’s a single streetlamp set among the trees that dimly lights most of the area, ending only at the edge of the creek. I like to sit at the edge and let my legs hang above the water. During the day you could see small fish swim by, and occasionally a large heron. At night there’s only the hum of the restaurant, and shadows swaying from the leaves above. 



*Squeak.*



The footsteps moved messily over the gravel lot. I sat completely still. There was no escape, and my eyes began to tear up as I held my breath. A small tendril of smoke from my dropped cigarette was the last sign of life in the lot. The footsteps came to a sudden stop directly behind me. I could feel him standing there. I could feel his eyes on the back of my neck.



Then there was a thud, as two mangled shoes landed neatly next to where I sat. After that, nothing. Slowly the sweat on my neck began to cool, and I felt the air rush back to my lungs.  
It took me a long time to stand up. I refused to take a second look at his shoes. Carefully, I made my way back to my stand, and back to the cheery lights of the Piñas entrance. I didn’t tell anyone what had happened, and after I closed everything I walked to my car. After getting inside and locking my doors, I called Eric. I said I needed to talk, and he told me to come over.



After parking in his complex, I made my way up the stairs and gave his door my knock. As I waited outside, the stairwell felt unfamiliar. Everything did. Finally he swung the door open and smiled. I had never been so happy to see him. For most of the night I completely forgot about what had happened earlier. We made dinner together and laughed about the guys at Eric’s job. 



It wasn’t until the end of the night that he broke the peace. He brought up the call, and asked what I wanted to talk about. I didn’t think he would believe me if I mentioned that none of my coworkers noticed him. I decided only to tell him that the new guy had snuck up on me by the creek, and that it made me slightly anxious.



“Have you told any of your managers about him?” 



“No. I don’t have any proof that he’s doing what he’s doing.”



“If you’re not gonna tell someone who can do something you should just let it go. Some people get off on that kind of thing, making people scared, so you can’t give him what he wants.”



“I guess.”



“Not everything has to be a thing. You can fix it, or let it happen, ok?” He sounded tired.



“Sure. Whatever.”



He changed the subject, but I mulled over his advice for the rest of the night. It hurt me that he didn’t take me seriously. Or even pretend to. The night faded into a blob of colors on his TV at the foot of the bed. The scene was familiar, comfortable. I felt safe with him, away from my apartment and my job. I fell asleep to Eric’s snores.



After what felt like only a short time, I woke up. Looking over at his alarm clock, the red LEDs flashed 3 something. He was still asleep, but his snores had been replaced by steady breathing. Stars found their way through the gaps in his blinds, and the moon cast a dim glow on the room. The TV had turned off on its own a while ago, and Eric’s dog slept with her tail twitching near the door. The carpet was neatly vacuumed and although the room was nearly bare, it was clean, and quiet. His bathroom door was left open, a now dark hallway ending at his closet. Then I heard it. 



*Swish.*



From deep within the dark of the bathroom came a slight shifting of clothing hangers. Then the creak of the door. I sat up and squinted past the doorway, lit up pale white by the moon behind me. He was there. Standing just at the edge of my sight, the moonlight fell only on two eyes and two bare feet. For several minutes he stood there. In silence. Then he put a finger to his lips, and smiled. I shouted, trying to wake Eric, and his smile dropped. Immediately, he sprinted through the apartment and out the front door. The dog had woken up, and whined slightly as she came to the side of the bed. Eric groaned and comforted her, before turning to me, upset.



“He was here, he was just here, the guy from my job, and he ran. He ran out the front door.” I could barely get the words out.



“It’s 3 in the morning. I can’t do this.”



“You have to believe me, please. He was here I swear, he was in your bath-”



“I’m sorry sweetheart. I think you should leave. I can drive you home but you can’t stay here.”



“Eric please. He could come back.”



“You need to talk to someone. You aren’t right, and you haven’t been.”



He shifted off of the mattress and pulled on the pants he’d left on the floor. I asked him to at least walk me to my car, and he agreed. I paused as we reached it.



“Why don’t you believe me?”



“I’m not trying to be a dick, ok. Just find a psychiatrist or something.”



I drove home slowly. I couldn’t bear the idea of being in my apartment alone. The whole drive I thought about what happened. About what Eric said. Did I wake up his dog? It was a good possibility I was losing my mind and he was right. When I got home I started writing this whole situation out. I’m not sure how helpful it’s going to be for a doctor but maybe it’ll explain something I can’t see. As I’ve been writing, I can’t help but feel even now that he’s real. That I’m not crazy. So I’m posting it here too. I made an appointment with a psychiatrist, but just in case what’s happening to me is real, this place might be the only one that can help. Am I crazy? Has this happened to anyone else before? How do I make things normal again?



I took a break from writing this just now to have a smoke on my patio. When I came back in, I found wet footprints by the front door. I don’t know where he is.
