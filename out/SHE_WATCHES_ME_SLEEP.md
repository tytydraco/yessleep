 

It was Friday, October 29th, 2005, at 12:03 a.m.

I’ve always thought my roommate was a bit weird. I mean, she wasn’t always “out there”. I hardly knew her, and when I'd see her around, she always looked sad. Anyone could tell she had no friends, and she stayed to herself, so in a way, you couldn’t judge her. I want to mention when I did see her , it would normally be around the art department, so I thought it was nice to know she had hobbies and stuff.

Her name was Rika Lang. She was a very beautiful girl. She had long black hair, dark green eyes, and was pale, so the dark circles under her eyes were visible. She was pretty tall— maybe 5’10? I do see her sit under the tree in the courtyard during my free period, though. With all that, you’d think she had nothing bad going for her. She had A LOT of rumors— from being a runaway to starting fires in middle school.

Everything I heard about her was awful. So.. Naturally I kept my distance. I tried anyway.

I had recently moved into dorms for this semester, and Rika was my roommate. For the first two months, everything was surprisingly fine. We hardly spoke, and I would try to invite her out, but she just stared, and I just left it at that.

 Lately, she’s been watching me a lot. When I get out of the shower, the dorm would be dark, and the stove light would be on, and I would notice her just watching me, and she wouldn’t speak.

   I want to mention her art was admirable. Creepy and unsettling? Yes, but I try not to think much of her art because, again, it was impressive. There was a painting that always made me uncomfortable. Rika had placed a medium-portrait-sized painting right in front of my door. One day, I finally took the initiative to closely look at the painting, and I felt my stomach knot up. 

   It was me sleeping in an empty, dark room, with this… Thing? sitting next to me. As I looked at it longer, I think it was staring back at me. It  looked as if it was decaying; the eye sockets were sunk in, it had an unsettling smile and perfect teeth. Oddly enough, the way I was sleeping, rather the whole ‘me’ was too accurate, from the way my hair fell onto my pillow to the partial skin showing from my hand being under my shirt. 

I decided to take it down and sit it at her door, in a boundary way. The next morning, I saw another painting up. I had the same pajamas that I slept in on. The thing wasn’t smiling— still making eye contact, but it had a frown this time and it looked more angry than sad. Regardless, I took it down and knocked on her door, but I got no answer. I left it at her door again and left for my classes.

When I came back home around 7, the wall was empty, and Rika had music playing, but her door was cracked, so I knocked and peeked in. “Hey Rika, I really think your art is cool, but it makes me kinda uncomfortable, so…” She sat her paintbrush down and stood staring at me, and she slowly nodded as she walked over, pushing the door closed in my face. I was shocked. I couldn’t get mad, though I did invade her space.

That night, I had sat up rubbing my eyes, and I felt a bright light shining above me. As I leaned back, I saw that thing from the painting. I jolted up, holding my chest, and the sun was shining in my face, and I could smell food being made, leading me to walk out my room. I hadn’t noticed the painting yet, but I was distracted by Rika talking to one of my friends, and he waved, and she was smiling too. I sat down, and she sat plates down, and we were all talking, and I would notice her staring at me a few times, but I tried to ignore it.

Later that evening, I walked to my room and noticed the painting I ignored earlier. I thought I was dreaming. I mean, no way. Right?

I was awake in the painting. That thing was behind me. I was rubbing my eyes, and again, it stared at me. One big stage light shone down on the both of us, leaving everything around us black. Meaning I saw EVERY horrific detail about that monster. The eyes were wider, and the nose was missing. That sinister smile kept getting more eerie, and I finally snapped. I took it down and threw it away. Normally, I’d sit it in front of her door, but I was sick of it. I even told her I didn't like her work in front of my fucking door.

The next few weeks, I decided to sleep at my parents’ house. Unfortunately, I came back to the dorm, and I hadn’t seen her around or anything. My room was how I left it. I felt a little bad about just leaving without trying to talk to her, so I decided I would when she came back. After doing some classes, I decided to take a shower.

 Around 10:47pm I  got out and saw her sitting on the couch staring into the hallway. I couldn’t tell if she was looking at me or her room door. It looked like she was staring for a while, then she spoke. “Sleep well, Violet Lenn.” It sent instant chills down my spine. It just seemed so unsettling hearing my full name leave her mouth since we hardly knew each other. “You too.” I managed to get out before closing my door and locking it. Even though I was uncomfortable, my door was locked, so I would be okay. I scrolled for a few minutes and fell asleep around 11:20.

Around 2:03am I was able to hear my door creaking open, and since I’m a light sleeper, I peeked up.  Since it was really dark, I grabbed my phone, turned my flashlight on, and what I saw made me scream. The thing from the painting. I had so many questions, but it slammed my door once my flashlight went on. I still was able to get a decent look to know it was for sure the thing from the painting. I was up for the rest of the night with the room light on until the sun came up. I went to see if my door was still locked, and it was. Honestly, I didn’t feel safe in my room, but eventually, I passed out.

Around 11 am I woke up. I finally decided to leave my room. It felt like an eternity. I was glad though… in a way, I was free? no one was home. I did the dishes and cleaned. By the time I was done, it was around 1 p.m. I sat on the couch and let out a sigh of relief and started watching tv pushing all my stressors to the back.

30 minutes in, I heard a loud bang.

The noises came from the bathroom, and I felt everything stop. Regardless, I decided to check the noise out. My first mistake was going to check the noise out alone. My second one was bringing the tv remote. How the hell was that going to protect me? But anyways, I knocked on the bathroom door, and no one answered (obviously). I should've left then, but no, I opened the door. There was absolutely nothing waiting behind the door. I let out a big sigh and turned around, and my soul left my body. I saw it there. It wasn’t a monster; it was my roommate.

I sat up, sweating and huffing, and I grabbed my phone. The time was 4:07am and I noticed my door was cracked, and I saw someone staring at me. I couldn’t move or scream. I knew it was Rika. The light from the kitchen broke into my room, and I saw her long nails at the top of my door, but her body was lower, so there was no way she was standing, and there was no possible way her arm could stretch that far. 

I slowly stood, and I felt the eyes following me. I slammed my door shut after hearing a blood-curdling scream. I broke down crying, too scared to move , too scared to do anything.

The sun came up, and I started packing. I knew I wouldn’t stay here any longer. I called my dad to come get me, and he said he’d be there in 20 minutes. I quickly started packing, and as I went to the door, I saw Rika on the couch. “ You’re sick,” she hadn’t replied, and she kept staring at the blank TV. I noticed her hand was wrapped, and I knew last night wasn’t a dream. I got chills from thinking about it and quickly  left the dorm.

When I got home and unpacked my bags, a piece of paper fell out, and I slowly opened it. Everything made sense. The ‘thing’ was Rika. That aside, the paper had a drawing of me standing and reading a note, and my closet door was cracked open. Moments later I heard my closet creaking.
