This has been eating at my mind for a year now. I'm struggling to even find the words to begin. I guess the best place to start is when I got transferred to USP Marion in Illinois. USP Marion is the first "super-max" prison-unit built to basically replace Alcatraz. You see, the only reason I got this job is because my father was a guard. His father was a guard and so on. And coming from a line of guards, my father was determined to make sure I did what I had to do to become a guard myself. It was almost forced. I don't want to be a guard. I'm not even sure how I got this position at such a high security facility.

You see, before USP Marion I was basically just the coffee jockey for the head CO's in their blocks. Maybe it was a joke. Or I just got the right coffee for the right CO. Regardless I was unfortunate enough to land a job here as a guard for a block, and the north tower night guard position. I did my training. I did what I could do to become an officer. It baffles me still as I write this. But all the training, all the classes I took, and all the coffee I delivered could never get me fully prepared for what happened at this prison.

It was April. I always had the morning shift. I liked working early because when I was up in the guard tower I could drink my energy drinks while watching the sunset on the rolling hills. Although with sixteen hour shifts.. Sometimes more, it just became a burden. Not the sunset but this job. This damn job. I promise I'm not here to complain about my work. In fact because of the high security the job was mostly easy. My shift goes as follows, Clock in, do a head count, an inner-perimeter check, talk along with my fellow guards about inmate gossip, do a last check on the outside perimeter, then I'm off to my block for eight hours until the shift change, and for the rest of the night until early morning I'm in the guard towers.

Although I enjoyed the towers during the mornings and nights the most interesting part of my days are usually the inmates in the block I'm responsible for. The stories they tell are some of the most outlandish things. Other times its the usual insult calling me a pig or a cry for how they're innocent or didn't do anything.

The most interesting story teller of them all was "Cotton Ball", I won't share his real name for legal reasons but the guards and inmates always called him cotton for short. He gained this nickname due to his hair and beard. Cotton was the oldest inmate, both in age and how long he's been in this prison. He had long straight white and silver hair that was so puffy, it was like he never heard of a brush in his life. His eyes looked slightly dull but had a remnant of blue in them, and his beard practically matched his hair, only more curly and short.

He was in prison for a first degree murder in the late 60's. A case so foul and disturbing it landed him here when he was around 19. He's now in his 70's. For now all I know I'm allowed to say is that he invaded a cabin in some national forrest.

In his old age, Cotton came to accept what's coming for him. Although he doesn't have the death penalty, due to lack of evidence, he realized long ago that he accepted the fact he would die in this prison. But death in prison isn't the only thing he looked forward to. All the inmates were allowed to leave their cells for about two hours, twice a day, and three times on Saturday and Sunday.

On my way in for my first day in my block, rec time was just starting, I could tell because I could see inmates playing chess and reading from the all too small library that was basically just a small shelf with maybe 150 books tops. and over 30 of them alone are different variants of the bible. There was also a massive hoard of bright orange from their jumpsuits huddled around the TV area.

Just before I entered the block I was greeted by Dante who seemed to have a new inmate with him. Dante, who is basically my manager and the head CO of our block. Is a Mexican gentlemen with a short black and curly beard, his eyes were a stark, almost bright green, and he had a buzz cut. The new prisoner was a tall skinny man, pale white skin, dark brown eyes, and his hair was a stark contrast to Dante's hair, long and golden. He looked me up and down with a shifty eye and almost instantly looked away from me. I wasn't really too excited for a new inmate not just because it was my first day but it also meant more paperwork for me to do.

He handed off the new inmate to me because he had some tasks on the cat walks of the towers. once dante was gone i rolled my eyes and led the new guy into the block. I gave the new inmate a breakdown of how we run things and assigned him a cell. As soon as he went to put his bedroll on his bunk he already had Cotton following him.

After a good minute I pulled out my first Zappt' energy drink for the day. Love this stuff, it's made local meaning better caffeine content. a cup of coffee is nothing compared to Zappt'. after a good chug of my drink I began to process our newest guest into our database. while processing him I had a chance to check his case. I read that he was in for manslaughter with a vehicle against a crowd of protesters. after a minute of processing him into our system I noticed him and Cotton not too far from my desk talking, me being the snoop that I am, I listened in.

"You know greenhorn, I bet you've already heard all the classic excuses, about how 'I didn't do it' or 'I was framed' but I promise you. With me? Its true." I laughed to myself at that statement, sure Cotton, you and everyone else in here. It always makes me laugh hearing the stories of how they didn't do it, watching them try to rationalize the terrible things they've done. And every time it always ends with them being one hundred percent innocent. Greenhorn laughed a bit too, then proceeded to ask him how his situation was different.

"Ahh you see, I was framed! I swear it. Not by someone, but something! A nasty beasty I swear it!. I tried to stop-" Cotton stopped mid sentence and had this thousand mile stare, then snapped back like nothing happened. "It. I tried to stop it from killing that family, but it knocked me clean out and when I woke up. I was covered in blood and I had handcuffs on" I looked at Cotton for a good moment trying to determine what could have made him come up with such a ridiculous story. Greenhorn looked at Cotton like he was completely uninterested "Okay dude whatever you say."

"The guards and the other inmates call me Cotton Ball. You can call me Cotton though" he gave Greenhorn a playful wink then his expression got serious in the snap of a finger, and pointed his long, large knuckled finger at him and said "But I'll tell you this. Greenhorn. I ain't ever told a lie in my life." then he trailed off still staring at Greenhorn as he made his way to play chess with a fellow inmate. Dante came in and took his seat, after taking a large sigh. I looked to him and asked "Is Cotton always like that? Like kind of crazy?"

"Yeah, poor old Cotton, he's close to losing his mind. Saying stuff like 'I need to share the truth' or 'if it goes unknown it'll happen again' he is all kinds of backwards." I just slightly chuckled and then continued my paperwork for the rest of the day.

A few weeks went by before it was my first shift in the tower. or as the other guards called it, The Dead Shift. apparently everyone hated night shifts in the towers because they always felt dead after working it. So much of a terrible shift that the night guard before me quit.  I've been assigned two Dead Shifts a week. It was around nine pm before I made my way to the tower I was assigned to. The guard before my shift gave me a quick breakdown of how to handle the job and wished me luck.

As soon as he was gone I wasted no time and pulled out my portable TV and sat down my six pack of Zappt' into a small cooler I brought from home. It wasn't too eventful of a first night. But it was definitely hard to stay awake. Luckily I had the six pack of Zappt' and television. Tower work was pretty easy. Every 20 minutes check the perimeter, be ready for any alerts,  and send an update on the status of the perimeter every hour. Simple, Tonight's Dead shift started out normal. Chug a Zappt', watch some shows, and keep my attention on the perimeter. When after a few hours at around one thirty in the morning, my radio started making this high pitched frequency sound that turned into a low almost growl like fuzziness. Then my small portable television popped and fizzled out.

"Damn thing" I smacked it a couple times as a fatal last attempt to get the small TV to turn back on. In my frustration I slammed it on my desk and walked out to the catwalk around the outside of the tower, there was a lightening storm coming our way, I could smell the wet concrete from miles away from how heavy the rain was. The wind was blowing at a good pace as well. As I leaned on the railing and sipped my drink I checked the perimeter.

Watching out at the near darkness passed the lights from the prison, feeling the wind through my hair. almost better than TV in my opinion, but just not the same. Then after about a half hour I heard my radio go off again, it made loud screaming noise this time, i assumed it must have been some kind of interference so I ran back in trying to contact whoever was on the other end.

"Alright, if this is some dumb teenager I need you off this channel right now. This channel is meant for prison personnel only. " Silence. Nothing but a slight buzz until I heard a subtle clicking. It kept going off \*click, click, click\* three clicks. Then it stopped, then repeated over and over again. I pressed the transmission button to send another warning out "quit messing around with this channel now or I will send officers out to find you"

Then I heard a deep scratching sound coming from below me. Like the building was hit with massive stray tree branches, Whatever it was, it was loud, and it was shaking the whole tower. I practically fell down into my seat. The lights went out within the tower and the entire prison, then a heavy gust of wind violently forced the door open. After a moment of shock I rushed over to shut the door and locked it. Then the power came back. And Dante came through the radio.

"Everything okay? The grids are down but luckily the backup generator is working" I grabbed my radio and replied "sounds good Dante, yeah everything is fine. Hey, before the power went out were you messing around with me? I heard a scream through my end then I kept hearing a clicking sound."

"Screams and a clicking sound? No, I was too busy rushing to the generator to get the power back up. The storm out there might have interfered with our radio towers." I paused for a good moment. Then remembered the scratching sounds too. I don't know what it could have been because there weren't any trees at the base of any towers here now that I think about it.

As I sat in the middle of the tower for good long while, the uncomfortable poor excuse for a cushion was getting unbearable. So, I stood up and looked out towards the tree line. Everything looked good but suddenly, I felt extremely tired. I began to think of tonight's events. Still being slightly shaken up from the wind and the storm. I sat back down and popped open another can of Zappt' I began to think about the radio and the interception I heard. I had to keep reminding myself to calm my nerves "Two more hours, two more hours."

Finally after my shift was done I ran to punch my code in to clock out and went home. I had small and simple cabin not too far from the prison. It was more of a dwelling than a home because the state had issued it to me so that I could have a place to live closer to the prison. the state practically provided everything for me. from clothes to groceries. My cabin is a small, one bedroom. one bathroom home with a tiny living room and a very small, very basic kitchen that I hardly ever used. Just the bare necessities needed only to live in rather than to make an actual home out of.

As I walked in and got ready for bed I laid there, staring at the ceiling.  I couldn't sleep. I was too mad about my portable TV blowing a fuse, feeling determined I got up from my bed and grabbed the TV. After a long and arduous few hours, I finally gave up and threw it away.

As I plopped down in my lazy boy within my bedroom I begin thinking about Cotton and his outlandish story was. After long hours of contemplation about cotton and the weird events from that night. my alarm went off. I didn't get any sleep. "great" I think to myself  with heavy regret. I got up from my lazy boy chair, threw on my uniform, put a fresh coat of deodorant on, grabbed my keys, and ran out the door. Still thinking of Cotton and how much his stories are beginning to intrigue me.
