“Hey Troy! It is Liz, your old neighbor, not sure if you had my number. I am just checking to make sure that you are still able to watch the cats and Lucky tonight? Your mom told me you could. I put instructions on the counter and you can help yourself to any food you can find in the fridge!”

Shit, I forgot I said I would watch Lucky. 

Not that I really mind watching him, I’ve done it enough times before. But it was Friday night and I would have preferred not spending it with a senile old dog. Lucky is a seventeen-year-old pug-beagle mix. He was extremely lively when he was younger, however, ever since Liz moved out he has been just different. He is completely blind and deaf in both ears. He’s still kicking though, but I can only hope he doesn’t... *you know*... on my watch.

\* \* \*

Liz lives in an apartment on the other side of town and it was about a ten-minute drive from my house. The apartment backed up to a steep hill filled with trees. It’s beautiful on bright summer days, but creepy and cold at night. 

I got to Liz’s apartment in the evening with my overnight bag packed. I look under the mat and there’s a keychain with a big “E” and a key attached. 

“Never change Liz.” I said through a smirk.

I unlock the door and await what is in store for me. I walk directly into the living room where I do not see any animals. I then walk to the kitchen to see Lucky himself, licking the floor of his own poop. 

“Oh come on Lucky,” I said.

I picked him up and he immediately began protesting as if he hadn't eaten in days. I had to throw him in his cage. I feel bad about punishing him but I had to clean it up, even though he was kicking and screaming. This dog will eat anything in front of him so if I didn’t, he would have kept eating. Liz keeps a mop handy for situations like this so I grabbed it and started mopping away. I could hear Lucky yelling from his crate, almost like I was denying him of his feast. After I cleaned his mess, I let him out of the crate. He immediately inspects the area, looking for anything else he could get. He does this with old slanted hips and unblinking blind eyes. 

As he was inspecting the floor, I remember I needed to feed him and the two other cats, Shadow and Snowball. One cat was all black and the other was all white (Can you guess which is which?). The cats are not an issue. Liz got them after she moved out and they were pretty self sufficient. All I need to do is put their food in their bowls, and they hang out in separate rooms. I found the note Liz left me on the kitchen counter.

“Hey old neighbor! Hope you are doing well. I already fed Lucky so you just have to make sure you feed him at eight o’clock. He usually needs to go to the bathroom every hour just to keep him from going inside.” 

Well there goes that rule. 

Liz then explains the mountain of medications that this dog will need for assorted purposes. This dog is needy; I am only watching him for one night and he needs eight different medications. But I set up Lucky’s bowl and put the medicine in for him to eat. Before I even put the bowl down he is scratching at me and howling for his food. If this dog is anything, he is hungry. He needs to be separated from the cats so he won’t eat their food. Liz’s solution to this was a gate with a gap under that was high enough that the cats can get under but Lucky cannot. This allowed for the cats to have their own space without Lucky, you know, being Lucky.

After feeding the animals, I find some dinner for myself in the fridge and bring it to the couch. Lucky was right behind me every step of the way. As I sat on the couch he was there, begging for my food. Sometimes whimpering, sometimes barking. I was not going to let up though, this was my feast and Lucky had already had his. Lucky climbed up his ramp next to the couch and started howling in my face. His first howl jolted me so much that I almost lost my food. I take my food and put it on the side table so he can’t reach it. He immediately goes over to the side table and tries grabbing the food. Doing this as best as his frail little body could. After several attempts, he knocked the plate over before I could catch him and chicken nuggets began to fall onto the carpet. 

“Lucky! What the fuck!”

Immediately I ran over to keep Lucky from eating the food. As soon as I put my hand on him, he went into a fit of rage, and his mouth covered my hand. I felt the pressure from his small teeth and pain shoots through my arm. I yelp out and look down to see that he drew blood. After this, I let him have the chicken. Liz had never said anything about him being hostile. I took my hand and put it under the sink to run water under it. While rinsing, I look under the cabinet for a first aid kit where thankfully there’s enough gauze there for me to wrap my hand. I know he is a small dog, but he bit me deep enough that a simple bandage would not do the trick. After I fix myself up, I see this dog lying on the floor of the carpet. He was smiling with pleasure as I could see my blood on his teeth. I didn’t know what else to do, so I called Liz. 

Ring. Ring. Ring. 

“Hello?”

“Hey Liz, Sorry to bother but I was watching Lucky and bit me. I was eating food and he took it and when I tried to take it from him he bit me.”

“Oh god, I am so sorry. He is probably used to me giving him something for everything I eat, I am so sorry. Are you okay?” 

“Yeah, I just wanted to let you know, not sure what to do now.”

“Yeah, thanks just… just make it through the night with him I’ll be back in the morning.” It sounds like this was out of character for him, she was clearly flustered and embarrassed. “Have you taken him out?”

Damn, I forgot to and I wasn’t going to tell her about me coming late enough for him to eat his own shit. 

“Yeah, he pooped and peed”

“That’s my Lucky! Let me know if you have any more problems.”

“Okay see you Liz.”

Click.

\* \* \*

It had been an hour since I fed him his own food. Now I have to take the dog out. His leash sits next to the door and fits loosely around his neck. In the note Liz left for me, Lucky had needed to be outside for at least ten  minutes in order to do his business, and because it was an apartment, we were in the front yard. I had to be right there with him holding his leash as we walked outside into the dark on the edge of the forested hill.

The light outside of her apartment building is the only light for yards. Right now it was only me, Lucky, and the woods. Lucky started pulling me on his leash; he had found something that he wanted. He walked to the edge of the hill and darkness and wanted to go further, but I would not let him. He went down on his elbows, on an army crawl to try and drag me with him. 

“Lucky stop. What in the world are you doing?” I said as I yanked him back. Soon after this struggle, Lucky had given up and I had won. Pleased with myself, we walked around a bit and he didn’t poop or pee but he decided he was done so he began walking back to the door. As I followed, I quickly caught up with him, opening the door so he could walk inside.

As soon as the door opened he slipped out of his loose collar and went running for the woods. He hobbled but it was fast nonetheless. I immediately dropped the collar and ran after him but by the time I caught him he slipped into the darkness. Shit, this was my fault and I had to get him. 

I went down the unmarked hill with my phone flashlight, this dog was not going to die on my watch. I ran down yelling his voice. Why was I yelling his voice, he never listened to me before, why would he now? I went straight down the hill so I could find my way back. I could never tell Liz that I lost her dog, I don’t even know how long Lucky would last out here without anyone to take care of him. 

As I went deeper and deeper into the woods, it was not long before I heard something. It was sloppy crunching. Not the same as wet leaves or grass being stepped on, it was a sort of animalistic noise. Along with this crunching I could hear a low growl from a beast. A growl with a kind of hunger. It was then that I turned my phone flashlight to face a lifeless squirrel being eaten by the dog. He was right there having his way with another animal. Lucky had already torn through its stomach. I am not sure if he killed it, no, he wasn’t able to because he was too old. But he was doing it nonetheless and he no longer looked like a domesticated animal, this was primal for him. It was almost like he reverted back to his basic genetic DNA, as a hunter not a dog. His snout was covered with blood, eyes were lifeless, and he was harshly ripping out the intestines of this poor woodland critter. With each snap back of his head I could hear the squish and snap of the squirrel’s extremities. It felt as though I couldn’t interrupt, that Lucky had let me bear witness to his primal senses. After the initial shock I had had enough, I ran over to him yelling at him. 

“Lucky! Fucking stop, what are you doing?” Then reached for him to stop him, he bit back, but I had known better. I snapped my hand away and then bear hugged him and suspended his squirming body by my chest so he couldn’t reach me. I then walked back up this steep hill and put his ass back in the crate. He was objecting harshly to this punishment, showing teeth, growling, but I couldn’t keep him out. I couldn’t call Liz to help either, I just needed to keep Lucky away from the outside. I locked the latches on the crate and let him yell at me. Nothing else he could do without his body giving up on him. I decided to keep this secret to myself, wait for the morning to clean him up, and simply go to bed. I had fed Lucky and the cats and now my job was done for the night and I was exhausted. I crashed on the couch watching the television waiting for Liz to come home.

\* \* \*

I woke up. Not knowing what time it is. As I look outside I can only see the dark woodsy hills. The television turned to the screensaver and I could hear one of the cats hissing and screaming coming from the other room. I went into the kitchen for Lucky, but there was no dog in that crate. Lucky had broken through, the bars of the crate were bent, and the door itself was bent like a beast had broken out.

How? How could such a frail and dying dog be able to manhandle his way through the gate? But there was no time for that so I had to investigate the screaming cat. I walked down the hall, past the broken baby gate used to separate him and the cats. I finally walked into the bedroom to see Shadow hissing at Lucky with his tail up, ready to defend. Lucky was defending back, growling with his bloody teeth and some newfound energy. I was too tired to act, so I just watched. Upon watching, I found that Lucky’s face had been even bloodier than before. His face was covered with a dark shade of dried up blood and new blood. This new blood was accompanied by claw marks from the cat. But there was only one cat. Where was the other cat? Liz said Snowball liked to hide but not in a situation like this. Before I had time to think of the answer to this, Lucky had noticed me. He went from growling at the cat to peering his cold empty grey eyes at me. For a split second he just stared at me, as if he knew he was in trouble. I felt like he was able to see me. But how? He was a blind little dog.

Suddenly, he moved his front paws up and stabilized on his back paws. He was standing on his back two legs as best as he could and as human as he could. His shoulders unnaturally sank back for a dog, almost like he was mocking me. He started walking towards me. One foot in front of the other. Now this was with hunger in his eyes. His mouth was smiling and watering, almost savoring this new hunt he had found. 

I sprinted away. What was he doing? I couldn’t answer, so I went into the bathroom just to escape him. I shut and locked the door and Lucky was clawing on it. I was shambling in the bathtub awaiting my fate. I think he started trying to bite and eat the door through because I heard. Then, when clawing didn’t work then he started ramming his head. Bang. Bang. Bang. With every bang I knew I was getting closer to him breaking down the door for him to do whatever he wants to me. The banging wouldn’t stop. Should I call Liz? Is this all in my head? What could I do? Suddenly the banging stopped. There was a feeling of comfort, I could finally leave this house and never return. I reached for the handle and that was when I heard a voice from outside the door. 

“Tttttroy?” the small voice shudders. “Pppppeaas l-let meeee innnnnnnn.” Was that the dog? It was like this for almost 30 minutes. Just tripping over words and trying to use its voice for anything to get me out. Saying more things like “be gooood ffffurrr ttttttrooooyyyy” or “let. meeeeee innnnnnn.” The only save I got was the front door opening. I heard keys hitting the kitchen counter and the inhuman voice had stopped. 

“Hey Lucky! How are you doing, my little stink! Were you good for Troy?” It was Liz. I walk out the bathroom. “Oh- Hey Troy I didn’t realize you were still here. Is your hand okay? Was Lucky good for you?” I was speechless. The only remnant I saw of the beast was his blood-stained body laying on the couch, sleeping, with a full belly. 

Liz was (understandably) pissed. She left for one night and I lost her cat and Lucky was all scratched up. A few days later Liz noticed a strange smell coming from her closet. That’s where she found Snowball. She never gave me details but from what I knew and her telling me, I could only imagine how graphic it was. When I asked her if she thought Lucky did it, she just hung up. This was about a year ago and she never asked me to watch Lucky again. I hope she is doing okay, but I also wonder if Lucky is still alive. Or if Liz is still alive.
