Listen, I’m not trying to bash people who work the night shift, alright? Do what you want. I mean, if night shift is far more convenient than just working the day shift, then all the power to you, but it’s just not for me. Which is why I thought telling you all about it would maybe help someone in their career picking going forward.

So a couple months back I was between jobs and couldn't seem to settle on anything. Each job I went for had something about it that ruined it for me. Call me what you want, but if I don’t vibe with you, then I don’t work with you. Also, I’m not working in any place that is completely alright with having its employees work in terrible conditions. So with that being the case, it’s a wonder as to why I decided to take a position at my local Walmart. A place known for being where all the numbers of a strange and batshit crazy client tell tales to frequent. And yes, if I didn’t make it too obvious, the position was for the night shift. 

10 pm to 7 am was the shift I forced upon myself because it seemed to be the only place around where I lived that paid subpar enough that it wasn't some stupid factory job. I worked there for a whole month before anything weird happened. Well, weird compared to my coworkers because most of them had to be either on something or had serious issues. Not that either of those is a bad thing. Hell, I probably looked as “good” as they did; I barely slept as is back then, so getting that job practically made me look like a zombie.

I made some friends there, which was hard to believe considering my previous track record, but no, these guys were cool. I even still keep in touch with them. Garron and Holly were their names. Good people, truly. Anyway, the three of us had stepped out of the store during our break. We were chatting about any odd thing we thought of, mostly to keep ourselves awake. We stood out by the entrance, making sure not to wander too far from it. Mainly because we feared what might lurk in the darkness of the parking lot.

Holly said she once sat on the bench close to the entrance. And swears she saw what she thought was a murder taking place across the parking lot at the gas station. If there was one, no police report, body, or anything was reported and filed, so if she did see something, she’s the only one who knows. Then again, she's told me stories I have a hard time believing, so she could have just seen something else and mistaken it for a murder. Like, would you really trust someone who believes she's met John Cena? Like, come on. How’d she see him?

Shit, I’m rambling, aren’t I? Fuck it, so we were standing by that entrance, again talking about random stuff. When Garron started poking me on the shoulder.

“What?” I asked, angry that every poke felt like a bullet.

“You hear that?” I didn’t have a clue what he was talking about.

“Hear what?” Holly asked, cupping a hand over one ear.

Garron began to look around frantically like a mad man or a dog who caught a scent. “Sounds like screaming.”

“Screaming?” We stood there for a moment. Listening to the eerie sound of a quiet night. The parking lot lamps buzzing certainly didn’t help with that.

“Maybe I'm more sleep-deprived than I thought.” Garron finally said, taking a sip from his powerade.

I’m not sure if the combination of noise and no noise was what I thought I was hearing at the time. But despite what Garron had just said, I was hearing the screams too.

“No, I think you’re fine… or we both need sleep.” I listened harder, and without a doubt, I heard the distinct sound of someone screaming or something close to that.

“I think I might hear something. Can you describe it to me?” Holly asked.

“I don’t know. Maybe like a very low pitch. Like someone screaming without putting heart into it. But they're doing it loudly. I guess a monotone scream is how I should word it.” I really didn’t know how to describe it, like I couldn’t tell if this person or whatever was hurt or anything. Hell, I could barely tell if it was human.

“Is it getting louder?” Garron had the hairs on my neck standing up when he said that. I even smacked him in the arm.

“Don’t say shit like that, dude," I said.

“Just listen…” A cold nightly breeze began to blow by. I thought at first maybe that was carrying the sound closer; I was wrong.

“Who is that?” Holly said, pointing towards a man standing far off close to the gas station. I hadn’t noticed him there prior, but he stood there as still as a statue. His back faced us, and he stood like he was ready for a military march. What’s worse is he stood where no lamplight was above or near him, so maybe that’s why I didn’t notice him. And I didn’t notice immediately, but the screaming had stopped.

“Probably some crackhead. You two want to go inside or what? Holly and I silently nodded, agreeing that going inside sounded like the safer option.

"Weirdo dude, always has to ruin the peace.” I said, pulling from my vest pocket a bag of skittles I had been saving.

We still stood near the entrance, just inside and near where people grabbed their buggies and where produce was. That Walmart was very minuscule compared to other Walmarts; the only saving grace it has is the fact that where it’s located is a convenient place for locals and travelers. Otherwise, it’s hard to believe people even shop there.

“Oh look, creepy's gone.” Holly said, leaning uncomfortably to the side just to see the parking lot.

“Must’ve run off when he realized we’re too smart to stay outside.” Unsure why I said this, honestly. I mean, the guy could’ve been normal. Maybe I was assuming the worst too easily.

“Oh shit," Holly said.

“What? Is he there?” I peeked alongside Holly, joining her in the uncomfortable stance.

What I saw out there was the man again. At least I’m assuming it was a man. He was closer now. Closer like in the horror stories you’d read online. His stance hadn’t changed; he simply moved closer, now under a lot of lamps. Under the light, I could see his features much clearer. He had on a simple clean shirt with blue jeans on. His hair was neat and he didn’t seem to have a single wrinkle anywhere on his clothing. What was a guy like that doing outside a Walmart at this time of night?

“Is that his car?” Holly said, once more pointing to the gas station.

I hadn’t noticed it either till now, but a car was in fact parked at a pump near the gas station.

“Great, he probably thinks we’re open because our lights are on. Probably stopped to get gas, and since there's no clerk, he figured he’d come over here for something.” While I appreciated Garron's theory, it didn’t explain why the man was standing there like he was.

“Do you think he’s screwing with us?” I asked.

"Well, he saw us, that's obvious… I don't know who he could be.” Garron yawned, folding his arms behind his head.

“How much trouble would I be in if I got out there and just beat that guy up?” Holly asked. Don’t mind it; she's said odder things. “Our breaks are over now, and we wasted too much of it because of him.”

“Holly, you're the one who peeked out there again. Besides, I think he’s done messing around now." My heart skipped a beat or two when Garron pointed out the fact that the man was now standing at the entrance.

He still had his back facing us, but he was still standing statue-like on the other side of the entrance doors.

“You locked those doors back right?” I asked Garron.

“I did.” He said.

“Good to know.” I decided I’d go up to the man, confront him, and tell him the store is closed. No more twenty-four-hour convenience, thank god. Couldn’t imagine working the night shift with customers of all sanities coming in in the middle of the night to bother us as we worked. And I’d be damned if this dude disturbed our peace outside of our breaks.

“Sir, we’re closed. I’d like to please ask you to return to your vehicle.” He didn’t respond, let alone react to what I said in any way.

“Sir, did you hear me? I said we’re closed. Go home.” Again, he didn’t react. 

“Garron, could you get Keith? Maybe this is a manager's job. They get paid enough.” I began to walk away when suddenly he spoke. 

“Please let me in; I need food for my starving stomach.” The way he said it made me uneasy.

“Sir, please, sustenance is what my body needs. Can you please provide it?" Dude must be a social reject if he talked like that.

“Could you at least turn around, dude?” I regretted saying that.

"Please just open the doors.” The more he spoke, the more I heard the monotone. Maybe this idiot had been the one screaming. Probably in his car or something, which is why it was so faint.

“My manager is on his way if anyone has a say in you getting in here. It’s him.” The man slouched a bit; his demeanor looked less confident now. I thought maybe I had relaxed him with some possible hope. I’m not saying I would’ve let him in, but whatever his deal was, he seemed like he really just needed help. He even began clutching his stomach like he was actually hungry.

Now I say all of that, but all of that was thrown out the window once you consider again he looked clean, he looked healthy, and he looked fed. Can’t say I know what exactly “fed” looks like, but I’ve seen starving, and that wasn’t it.

"God, he’s still here.” I heard Holly say behind me.

“Some people just don’t take no for an answer.” I heard our manager Keith say, also behind me.

“He says he wants to buy food because he’s starving.” I explained to the two of them.

"Well, I’ll take it from here.” I couldn’t have been more relieved when Keith said that. Holly and I walked back to our aisles and continued working stock onto the shelves. Surprisingly easy night all things considered; Keith’s usually on my ass about stuff, but I hadn’t seen him since Holly and I left him to deal with the guy. So the rest of that night was smooth sailing.

That is until we were on our way out to go home. Holly, Garron, and I walked out of the building and were walking to our vehicles, which we parked on the side of the store where online shoppers go to pick up their groceries.

“Have either of you seen Keith? Like at all tonight. "I remember asking.

“Not since sending him to deal with that weirdo. I hope he didn’t try confronting the guy by opening the doors.” Holly said, I worried the same.

"Well, the doors were open.” Garron then said.

“You did not just say that.” I said, revving up a punch aimed at Garron's arm. I was getting tired of him freaking me out.

“You guys didn’t notice? Doors weren’t locked when we went through them. So maybe he did unlock them.” I punched Garron in the arm as I shivered at the idea of that guy waltzing in at any moment.

“I’m too fucking tired for that shit.” The sun had started to rise as cars began to park in the lot.

“Do you guys hear that?” Garron said suddenly.

“Oh Jesus, not this again…” I was hearing it too. Screaming again. This time, like bloody murder. Bloodcurdling. The three of us slowly turned our heads in the direction of the screaming.

In the middle of the parking lot. We saw Keith. Lying on the ground. He had rope tied around his neck that had snapped god knows when. He looked as if he’d been hitched to the back of a vehicle and drug all around the parking lot. I sometimes wonder to this day what might’ve happened if I did open the doors for that guy. I didn’t like Keith as a manager, but I wonder if I or Holly had stayed with him, would that have prevented what happened? That guy was odd, but I felt no ill intent from him. I simply saw a guy who was hungry and deranged, maybe, but the night brings out the odd ones. I quit that job a few days later. Mostly out of guilt, but because I couldn’t handle seeing that guy again. What would he have done to me? Or Garron. Or Holly. 

I like to say I see the good in people. Maybe too much for my own good. Not everyone has the purest of intentions.
