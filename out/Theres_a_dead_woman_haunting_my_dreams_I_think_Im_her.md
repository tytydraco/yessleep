I don’t know who’s going to read this. Maybe no one. Maybe that’s better.

But if something happens to me, at least this will explain why.

The date is **April 12**. Time on my clock reads: **3:08am.**

I really don’t know why I’m writing this, but my therapist (Julia), says it might help. ‘*Grounding*’ is what she calls it.

My name is Charlie Holloway—and no, Charlie isn’t short for Charlotte—it’s just Charlie. My mom wanted a boy. The women in our family tend to go a little… insane.

I used to think I’d be different, but now I’m not so sure.

For the past eight years I haven’t had a single normal night of sleep. And it’s because of a woman.

A woman whose face I can’t see, whose voice I’ve never heard.

She doesn’t say anything. She just stands there and looks at me. I can feel her looking at me.

The Shadow Lady (that’s what I call her) started appearing in my dreams a short while after my 16th birthday. So… eight years now.

It started with one dream. Just one.

You know how sometimes in dreams you’re not really in your body?

Like you’re watching everything happen from the outside?

This one wasn’t exactly like that.

I was in it. *Inside her head.*

Or at least I think I was.

Her name is Angel, I know this because in the dream she’s not alone.

There’s a man, she calls him John.

She knows John, she loves him. I can feel that.

I can feel everything she feels and thinks in the dream like I’m her.

I can feel her pain too. And there’s a lot of it.

When the dream starts off, she’s a bit disoriented. She doesn’t know where she is. But the place feels  familiar to her.

There’s the smell of sawdust, and paint in the air. And something else. Something metallic, rusty almost. That smell is what I taste on my tongue whenever I wake up.

The taste of blood.

There are other things I—she can hear.

The sound of footsteps, a heavy gait. It belongs to the man she calls John. Bird calls, and plastic rustling. There’s a louder sound—a saw.

She wants to call out to John, but she can’t feel her tongue—I can’t feel my tongue. I can’t feel anything.

John tells me it’ll be alright. That he’s going to take the pain away. 

But then the saw starts again, and then another sound. Wet. Flesh tearing. It’s at this point I realize… I’m being cut up. 

Taken apart piece by piece on a table in a shed. 

I can feel the tears on her face. I can feel her urge to scream. But she can’t. 

He took her tongue first.

He’s murmuring sweet nothings as he continues to cut, and cut. When it’s almost over, I can feel the jagged teeth of the saw on my neck, just as he starts to decapitate me. 

Then the scene fades out, and I’m in that void again, with the shadow lady watching me in the distance.

And then I wake up. Screaming, and grabbing my throat, with the distinct metallic taste of blood in my mouth. And the feeling of someone watching me.

I haven’t told Julia this part yet—the part about the sawing—she might be a psychologist but even that would sound absolutely insane to her. And the last thing I need is to end up in an asylum. 

So during our therapy sessions, I tell her I’m simply being watched in my dreams. I tell her I can’t sleep, and it’s affecting my life. I’m hoping she’ll prescribe drugs for me. Something to knock me out. 

I just need to sleep. 

My clock reads **3:27 am**.

There’s no more sleep for me at this point. If I try to again, I’ll be pulled right back to Angel, and her death.

And honestly not sleeping is better than that.

But even now, wide awake and with every light in my room turned on… I can still feel it. 

Eyes watching. Always watching.
