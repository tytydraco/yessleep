For most of my life I have lived in a place called ‘The Holy Island’. That’s what tourists like to call it and annoying teens like I used to be, but locals will usually stick to Lindisfarne. The name is nowhere near as in your face but it makes people feel less like they’re stuck in some weird crusader esque time. 

The island is only actually sometimes an island, like half the time you can walk there from the mainland. The other half of the time the tide comes in and then you're just stuck there. I’ve gotten used to it but it is weird when I type it out. So many tourists have gotten trapped here overnight and I would always laugh at them. Sometimes I would pelt their cars with eggs but that’s a story for another day.

Why I’m even talking about the island is because of all the weird experiences I had growing up. A lot of this weird stuff I kinda forgot about but when my father died it all resurfaced. So what follows is my account of the first memory I have of what the island does. I have a lot of stories to tell but this is what I would consider the first domino.

19/08/2026: Jimmy In The Sky

I was fourteen years old, it was about eleven o’clock at night and I was feeling a tad rebellious. I snuck out of my house and met up with the rest of the gang. It was me, Michael, Mikey and Madison. Now it would be great if my name also started with an M but sadly I’m the Anna of the group. 

We met by the dockyard overlooking the castle. We’d sit there and just skim rocks into the ocean whilst listening to obnoxiously loud music. No-one would ever bother us though, everyone knew but we weren’t doing any harm. 

I did a generational skim, about seven bounces and we all freaked out. Lots of cheering and Mikey sulking because he held the previous record. Then the actual point of this story happened, the music turned off. The silence was stunning, we all stopped what we were doing and looked around frantically. 

No-one did this, the surroundings were completely devoid of humanity. Just us, the ocean and the sky. The silence didn’t last long, but what came back through the speaker was the most peculiar thing. There was a loud burst of static that pierced our eardrums, I grabbed at my ears and by the time I could hear properly again, a tiny voice came out. 

There was a discordant piano underlying the croaky voice of an elderly man. He sung a song none of us had ever heard before, “When dear old Jimmy comes around, better lock your doors or you’ll lose your crown. When dear old Jimmy comes to play, don’t look to the sky and you’ll be okay. Dear old Jimmy oh dear old Jimmy, don’t look now it’s dear old Jimmy. Dear old Jimmy oh dear old Jimmy, don’t look now it’s dear old Jimmy.”  
Immediately after it cut out violently, sparks flew from the speaker. We all stumbled back, afraid to even approach the speaker. None of us moved, I think we were waiting for it to continue. The song itself wasn’t scary but I don’t know, we all knew that this wasn’t a prank. There was an anxious energy in the air, none of us wanted to move or address whatever the fuck this was. That was until Michael glanced up. 

His eyes hit the sky and his head quickly moved to follow, snapping up as if he were in a trance. He let out a horrific scream, like a man being burnt alive. It took everything I had in me to not look up with him. We all shouted at him, asked him what was happening but he just kept fucking screaming. 

I walked towards him, put my hands on him to comfort him, to pry his head away from the sky but all he could do was look and scream. I was crying, begging him to just look away and eventually he did. His head snapped back, looking me dead in the eyes. Michaels eyes were different, his eyes were always blue but they were swirling like the ocean, the tide coming in. 

The last thing I remember him doing was smiling at me and then jumping. He dove head first off the platform overlooking the bay. 

His head smashed into a rock, splitting open immediately, dashes of blood spattering the shoreline. When he hit the water, he sank like an anchor, we were all too afraid to dive in after him. We told everyone what happened and they called it a psychotic break. Even his parents didn’t look into it further. Looking back I think they all knew something we didn’t. 

Since then I’ve had my own run in with dear old Jimmy. I’ve dealt with a lot of shit this island has thrown at me but god, I think losing Michael is the one that sticks with me the most. Seeing my friend die, the ocean reflected in his eyes. Whenever I’m away I see it, the tide coming in, and as much as I try to ignore it, I always feel the need to return here.
