My brother is in rehab. To my knowledge he’s not allowed to leave, or take any phonecalls for  
the next few days, at least. Voluntary hold and isolation, detox. So I’m trying to figure out why I  
saw him in his bedroom last night.

He and I are two years apart. I’m the eldest daughter of our family, (we have a younger sister  
too, but she and him don’t get along as much,) and we’re both in our early twenties still living  
with our parents. Where he’s always been a sort of aimless ‘ideas man,’ the typical stoner, I  
know who I wanted to be from a young age and have always done my best to be a good  
example for him. I think he’s always resented me for that, and for the sort of parental role I took  
on with him to help our parents.

I don’t know. He’d trusted me enough to be honest about smoking all the pot. And right before  
he left, I took him to a rave (we both love dancing) and learned he’d been experimenting with  
cocaine. It was only then I started wondering what else he’d been ‘experimenting’ with. I got  
really mad at him about it, but he obviously didn’t remember the next day. He was high.

We’ve fought plenty of times, but never like right before he left. Walking into the house at all  
hours, shouting obscenities and saying nonsensical shit. Creepy whispering and laughter in the  
middle of the night coming from the wall our bedrooms shared. I kept waking up at all hours,  
startled awake by the noise. It kept all of us up. Him and our sister fought every day. He’d rage  
at the drop of a hat over the smallest bit of pushback from anybody. And at this point we all  
knew he was high out of his mind, not himself. I just never expected him to ever shout in our  
mother’s face and swing his arm up like he was about to punch her. Hard. I went ballistic.  
Pushed him half way across the kitchen before the hit could connect and shouted like I’ve never  
shouted at anyone.

He was basically on his way out after that little spat. Our parents gave him an ultimatum for the  
first time in all their years of dealing with his addiction–go to rehab and therapy, or move out.  
They would pay for his recovery but he would not be stalking around the house tweaking  
anymore.

It’s been three days since he left. He gets his phone back this weekend, and I already have a  
million questions to ask him. I miss him so much, which is the only reason I’m still trying to  
convince myself I was dreaming. I must have been. 

What I saw last night couldn’t have been  
real.

I woke up in the middle of the night. I still don’t know from what, but I remember blearily rubbing  
my eyes and turning to see the hallway light click on and then off from under my door.

Footsteps. Might be my little sister grabbing something to eat from downstairs.

I cleared my throat and rolled over to check the time. Phone was dead. The distant yellow  
streetlights outside my window were the only light left in the room. I’m guessing it was  
something like 2am so I crept out of bed and opened my door. Passed the waiting open door to  
my brother’s bedroom. It was as still as he’d left it. My mom liked to leave it open because shehated the idea of leaving it closed for some reason. Like she’d be forgetting him, she said. I  
didn’t argue at the time, but it looked creepy and dark in the moment. His room used to be so  
full of activity. He’d be bumping music until midnight. I glanced into the pitch black square that  
was his doorframe and was struck by how lonely it looked. I sort of understood what she meant,  
then. It looked like it was waiting for him to come back.

Too late to dwell on it.

I walked into the bathroom, did my business and stared at myself in the mirror for a while. I  
looked awful. My hair was a mess from sleep, but my skin has gotten so dull and sallow. I think  
I’d forgotten to brush my teeth too, so I was just picking at my gums idly, hating what I saw.  
There was no time to take care of myself when my sister needed homework help, or to be  
dropped off someplace. When Mom needed me to go grab groceries so she could pick up the  
slack on my brother’s chores. I work pretty long, fast-paced shifts where there isn’t much time to  
invest in being pretty. I don’t usually care, either. But that night I did. It hurt to see physical proof  
of how bad I was doing.

He was such a sensitive, sweet kid when we were growing up. I promised to always protect him.  
I promised to be there for him like a big sister is supposed to. And I was staring back at  
undeniable proof that I’d failed miserably. No restful sleep, no peace at home, and still no baby  
brother.

I didn’t wallow too long. Turned back on my heel and closed the door behind me, tiptoeing back  
toward my room before I could wake up my parents. And then I heard my brother’s voice.

“*Hey*.”

“Oh *shit*–!” I caught myself on my doorframe, reeling around to look back at his room. It still  
looked empty. I even cautiously peeked around its edge, trying to see inside. He has blackout  
curtains in there, it was always impossible to see further than a foot in front of you at night.

What the hell.

I heard his voice again, somewhere in there.

*“Hey. Come here.”*

“What… what are you doing home?”

…No answer. I stepped toward the door again and heard him moving inside, taking a step  
toward me. His silhouette was the same, it was definitely my annoyingly tall, skinny, curly haired  
brother. I just couldn’t see his face. I asked him again what he was doing home.

He replied,

*“I’m back. Come here.”* I remember hearing the distinct wet noise that a person’s mouth makes  
when they’re smiling. “*I wanna show you something.”*

“Okay, that’s cool. Um… dude I’m happy you’re home but like–“

“*I have everything I need here.”*

I didn’t know what to say to that. I just took a step back and asked,

“Are you allowed to be back so soon?”

“*I’m back. And now I have everything I need.”*

I sighed. He wasn’t going to answer me. And I didn’t care enough to investigate further. I  
wouldn’t argue with someone who was clearly high in the middle of the night.

“Do you want a hug or—?”

“—*No*.”

His shadow quickly sidestepped back into the dark and I heard his bed creak from the corner of  
the room. The corner of his room which was nowhere near where he’d been standing.

“Okay.”

And then I just… walked back into my room and closed the door. Fell asleep in a few minutes to  
the sound of him scratching around in there, dragging something along the wall we shared.

The reason I’m writing this now is what happened the next morning.

For one, no one’s seen him and his room is spotless. His backpack is still gone and his phone  
location is still reliably sitting right over the grey square that is the rehab center. Still radio  
silence, but that kid doesn’t go anywhere without his phone on hand because he likes to post  
videos of himself dancing. He promise he’d dance with me again when he came home.

Mom told me when I finally came downstairs that he’d be calling us later today, and looked at  
me like I was crazy when I said,

“I thought he came back last night?”

Apparently not. Because he’s been stuck in an intensive care unit since he got there being  
slowly weaned off of the meth he’d apparently gotten off a friend. Another experiment. I was  
disappointed, because I missed him so much but I wrote off the night before as a stupid dream.

I was wrong. He’s home alright. But in the light he doesn’t look the same. And from what I can  
see staring back at me through the crack in my bedroom door, he’s gotten much taller since the last time we spoke.

I have to go now. I think he’s still waiting on that hug.
