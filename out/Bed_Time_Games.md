There were rules.

Not written down. Not explained.

Just… understood.

Some of them were normal.

Don’t touch the stove.

Don’t go outside after dark.

Go to bed when you’re told.

But some of them didn’t feel like rules.

They felt like… boundaries.

Lines drawn in places I didn’t know I wasn’t supposed to cross.

Like nights when people came over.

I remember the way the house would change.

It didn’t look different.

It didn’t sound different.

But it felt different.

Like the air got heavier. Like everything was waiting for something.

Cars would pull into the driveway.

More than usual.

Doors closing. 

Voices I didn’t recognize.

Adults.

And sometimes…

Other kids.

That part never felt strange to me, but

not back then.

Kids meant playtime. Noise. Distraction.

But I wasn’t allowed to stay.

“Bed,” my mom would say.

Earlier than usual.

Always earlier.

“I’m not tired,” I’d tell her.

“I know,” she’d say, smiling.

“Go anyway.”

She never argued.

She didn’t need to.

My dad would already be standing behind her.

Not saying anything.

Just… there.

So I’d go.

Every time.

From my room, I could hear everything.

That’s something people forget about kids.

They think walls are thicker than they are.

They think closed doors mean silence.

They don’t.

At first, it was just voices.

Low. Controlled.

Like conversations that didn’t want to be overheard.

Then… movement.

Footsteps. Faster than walking.

Not quite running.

Then something else.

Laughter.

Not adult laughter.

Kids.

I remember smiling the first time I heard it.

Lying in bed, staring at the ceiling, thinking about how unfair it was.

They were still up.

Playing.

And I wasn’t.

I almost got out of bed.

But then…

Something changed.

The laughter didn’t stop.

It shifted.

It got louder.

Higher.

And underneath it…

Something else crept in.

A sharpness.

The kind of sound that doesn’t sit right in your chest.

I sat up in bed.

Pulled the blanket closer without thinking.

The footsteps were faster now.

Running.

More voices.

More kids.

And then—

A scream.

I froze.

It didn’t sound like playing.

It didn’t sound like someone pretending.

It sounded real.

I waited for it to stop.

For someone to laugh after. For an adult to say something. For it to turn back into something normal.

But it didn’t.

Another scream.

Closer this time.

Then more running.

Something hitting the floor.

I got out of bed.

I don’t remember deciding to.

I just… did.

The hallway was dark.

The light from downstairs flickered against the walls, shifting like something was moving in front of it.

I stepped closer to the stairs.

Slow.

Careful.

The sounds were clearer now.

Running.

Crying.

Voices I didn’t recognize calling out over each other.

And something else.

A kind of laughter I didn’t understand.

Not happy.

Not loud.

Just… wrong.

I reached the top of the stairs.

I should’ve gone back.

I knew that.

But I didn’t.

I looked down.

At first, my brain didn’t understand what I was seeing.

It tried to make it normal.

Tried to turn it into something familiar.

People moving.

Shapes crossing the room.

Bright colors.

But it didn’t fit.

There were figures in the living room.

Tall.

Too tall.

Their clothes were… strange.

Loose. Hanging. Mismatched.

And their faces—

I couldn’t make sense of their faces.

Not fully.

Just flashes.

White.

Dark shapes where eyes should be.

Smiles that didn’t move right.

They moved too fast.

Chasing.

The kids.

They were running.

Trying to get away.

One of them fell.

I recognized him.

The red hoodie.

For a second, everything slowed.

He tried to get back up.

Hands slipping on the floor.

One of the figures reached him.

I don’t remember exactly what happened next.

Not clearly.

Just movement.

A struggle.

And then…

Stillness.

The room didn’t stop.

Everything else kept moving.

Running. Laughing. Chasing.

But that moment—

That one moment—

Burned itself into me.

I couldn’t breathe.

I stepped back.

Too fast.

My foot hit the wall.

The sound felt loud.

Too loud.

I turned and ran.

Back to my room.

Door closed.

Lights off.

I didn’t think.

I just moved.

The closet.

I crawled inside.

Pulled the door shut.

Curled into the corner like I could disappear if I stayed still enough.

The sounds followed me.

Faint through the walls.

But still there.

Running.

Screaming.

That laughter.

I covered my ears.

It didn’t help.

I don’t know how long I stayed there.

Hours.

Maybe all night.

At some point…

It stopped.

Not slowly.

Not fading.

Just… gone.

Like someone flipped a switch.

The silence that followed was worse.

I must’ve fallen asleep.

Because the next thing I remember—

Was sunlight.

Thin lines of it slipping through the closet door.

Everything was quiet.

Too quiet.

I opened the door slowly.

Half-expecting…

I don’t even know what.

But my room was normal.

The hallway was normal.

When I went downstairs…

The house looked exactly the way it always did.

No people.

No kids.

No signs of anything.

The furniture was in place.

The air was still.

Like nothing had happened.

My mom was in the kitchen.

Making breakfast.

She smiled when she saw me.

“Good morning,” she said.

Like it was just another day.

I didn’t say anything.

I couldn’t.

I looked around the room.

Trying to find something.

Anything.

Proof.

That’s when I saw it.

On the hardwood floor.

Near the living room.

Faint.

Easy to miss.

Small handprints.

Red.

Smeared.

Trailing across the floor.

Toward the basement door.

I followed them without thinking.

Each step slower than the last.

They stopped right in front of it.

The door was closed.

Of course it was.

I stared at it for a long time.

Then I heard my mom behind me.

“Don’t go down there,” she said.

Her voice was calm.

Gentle.

But something about it…

Something underneath it…

Made my skin feel tight.

I nodded.

Of course I did.

Because there were rules.

And some of them…

You didn’t break.
