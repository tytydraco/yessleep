This is fucking ridiculous but I'm desperate

This is fucking ridiculous, but I’m desperate. A priest and a therapist both suggested I keep a journal of events. That sounds like the start of a bad joke in the making: a priest, a therapist, and a car salesman with a “maybe a demon” ruining his life walk into a bar…

I don’t know how to construct the middle or the end yet, because I’m still in the beginning. 

I think.

Jesus, this is so fucking weird. I’ve never kept a journal before. What do I even say?

I guess I’ll just explain from the start, or where I think the start is. Honestly, it feels like there’s very little I actually know other than something is happening to my pregnant wife, and I’ve never been more afraid.

I’ve been married to Bonnie for about a year now. We dated for three months before getting married. I know that doesn’t seem like long enough to really get to know someone, but when you know, you know.

I had only dated one girl before Bonnie. 

We’ll call her Lucy for the sake of anonymity. She was my high school sweetheart, but as they usually do with teenage romances, things turned ugly when we hit the real world around twenty.

Bonnie had everything Lucy lacked. She’s a good ol’ girl—a woman who desires to be a mother and homemaker above all else. She goes to church every Sunday with her well-to-do white-collar parents.

They never approved of me. I’m just a grease monkey with no real prospects and a bad temper.

I do alright though. I’ve climbed the ladder at work and make a decent amount, so when Bonnie told me she wanted to travel for our honeymoon, I was stoked. I’ve never left the States, and with the amount I’m making, not only could we afford to travel abroad, we could afford to do fun shit while we traveled.

Bonnie and I are a bit of thrill seekers. We’d both always wanted to take acid or mushrooms or something like that. She mentioned Indonesia as a destination, so I did a bunch of research on all the cool things to do there. That’s when I learned you can pay some shaman from a small village to give you a guided trip on something called ayahuasca.

Bonnie was iffy on it. She said she had a bad feeling. But I was able to get her on board, so after our little courthouse wedding we set off.

Bonnie was sick the entire time we were traveling. We’re talking puking, shitting, looking like all the blood had drained from her pretty rosy-cheeked face. Even sick she looked angelic. She had beautiful long blonde hair that always smelled like rosemary, green eyes, freckles, and alabaster skin that I loved because it was so soft.

Sorry, I’m getting off track.

I can hear her standing outside the office door right now, and I don’t know if it’s her… her… or if it’s not her.

Anyways, we land in Indonesia and make the journey to our hotel. She showers and goes straight to bed, so I go do some exploring, get some food, and ask around about finding a shaman for our adventure.

I was pretty surprised to find someone who could take me to the right place almost immediately. Guess this is their tourism trap.

When I got back to the room, Bonnie was leaned over the toilet throwing up again, but I managed to get some soup in her and then we both fell asleep.

I had a terrible nightmare that woke me up in a panic. 

Bonnie wasn’t asleep next to me anymore.

I scanned the room and saw her standing at the window, staring out. She was so oddly still it kind of freaked me out. I asked her what she was doing and she slowly turned around. Her face was shadowed, but I could tell she had an unusual expression.

So I got up and gently coaxed her back to bed.

The next morning she was feeling much better. I was worried though and told her we could cancel our adventure if she wanted. But she insisted, saying she was really excited about it, I could tell she was lying.

We found our guide and headed to the village.

It took hours to get there, and about halfway Bonnie started saying she had that bad feeling again. I told her we were way too far into it to turn back now.

By the time we arrived, it was just getting dark.

I don’t know what I expected of the process, but it wasn’t for them to give us very little information and basically just make us drink the nastiest shit on the planet and then tell us to get comfortable.

Bonnie gagged multiple times while trying to drink hers. At one point I thought she was about to give up, but I encouraged her and she was able to get enough down.

It did not fucking stay down.

Dear God, it did not stay down.

We puked and puked and puked. I started to get scared I’d have a heart attack with how hard my body was vomiting. Poor Bonnie had it so much worse because she had already been sick for two days, so her body just didn’t have much fight left in it.

But the shaman kept having the translator girl tell us the puking was normal, so we weren’t too scared.

Well… I wasn’t anyway.

Once the throwing up finally quit, we almost instantly felt it. It was like I started melting into the mat I was sitting on. My body felt all tingly and good. My vision blurred in and out, but I felt incredible.

I asked Bonnie how she felt but didn’t get an answer.

So I looked over.

She was sitting up board-straight, staring slack-jawed into the woods.

I asked again, and this time I swear I heard her say something, but I couldn’t make out what it was.

Then I noticed the shaman and the translator both walking toward her with what looked like dried leaves in their hands. They started chanting and hitting her with the leaves.

I couldn’t tell if she liked it or not, so I didn’t know if I should intervene.

But then my trip started to change.

I began feeling this overwhelming sense of dread. It felt like I was inside a creek, like I was actually submerged in water—but I wasn’t, and I knew that.

I could even smell the stagnant water of the creeks around my hometown.

I was not having fun anymore.

I tried to keep my eyes on Bonnie, but my head kept rolling around like I couldn’t control my neck.

 On one of the rolls, I did manage to see her.

And for fuck’s sake, I wish I hadn’t.

She was still sitting upright with her body facing the woods, but her head was turned at a disgusting angle like some tortured, fleshy owl.

 She was staring right at me—eyes huge, pupils so dilated they were pitch black, jaw opened so wide it looked broken.

Her eyes were full of fear.

I could tell she was crying.

It scared me fucking shitless.

There was a weird sound all around me, but I couldn’t tell where it was coming from. It sounded like a deep moan mixed with a crackling noise. I thought maybe the shaman was blowing a horn, because he was no longer hitting Bonnie with the leaves.

Neither was his translator.

I didn’t see where they went, and that scared me even more because they were supposed to be our guides during this shit.

The rest of the night is blurry. I can only remember little bits here and there.

All of them terrifying.

Bonnie staring at me like that.

That noise.

I think maybe it was coming from her.

I woke up in the fucking woods. No clue where I was or where Bonnie was. Dirt caked under my fingernails, head pounding, clothes soaking wet, and I was only wearing my underwear.

Yeah, I know. Fucked up, right?

I staggered to my feet, looked around for which direction to go… and saw her.

Sitting in the same rigid posture, at the edge of the woodline.

Staring right at me.

But instead of slack-jawed, she was smiling ear to goddamned ear, her eyes were still pitch black.

I froze in fear seeing her like that. I didn’t know what to do, so I just kept calling her name while slowly walking toward her.

She stared me down the entire time.

When I got close enough, I gathered all my courage and touched her head.

The second I made contact, she let out a guttural scream I have never heard before in my life.

I jumped back, trying to calm her down, my heart racing.

When she had expelled all the air in her lungs, she slouched, and it was like watching someone’s soul come back into their body.

She looked around frantically and tried to stand up, but her back was killing her from sitting so ramrod straight and tense for so long.

I leaned down and helped her to her feet.

Then we held each other.

And something felt so different about that hug.

We followed a trail back to the village only to discover no one was there anymore. It looked like they had packed up and left in the night.

We drank some water and began walking back.

Eventually we caught a ride to the hotel, but neither of us had spoken a word to each other since that morning.

There was the weirdest feeling between us. I just can’t explain it.

At the hotel we ate and showered. I asked her if she wanted to cut the honeymoon short and go home tomorrow. She just didn’t seem to be having a good time at all.

I didn’t really expect her to say yes.

That night she started getting sick again.

I woke up out of a nightmare and found her standing there.

But she wasn’t facing the window this time.

She was facing the fucking wall.

I watched her for a minute. She didn’t sway or move a muscle. So I called out to her.

Her head snapped quickly in my direction.

I heard a loud CRACK as the moonlight illuminated her face, like God himself shining a light to show me that tears were rolling down her cheeks, soaking them.

Her jaw hung open.

She was drooling all down her nightgown.

My instincts told me to run.

But where?

And it’s Bonnie.

I can’t run from her. I can’t leave her here.

So I got up, fought my revulsion, closed her mouth for her, and put her back to bed.

The next morning I woke up to her handing me coffee and asking how I slept.

Now cut to a few weeks later.

Once we got home, she seemed to start feeling better. We literally never talked about our little adventure again, and life just continued like normal.

Then one day when I got home from work, I saw a pregnancy test on the counter.

No—we were not trying or planning to have kids for many years.

So this was not an exceptionally fun surprise for me.

Bonnie seemed stoked about it though. And although I lightly tried to ask about an abortion, I could tell she wouldn’t be letting this pregnancy go.

I wasn’t happy, but whatever, right?

Who cares if a man is unhappy, especially in situations like this.

I even tried to convince her that she was clearly already pregnant during our trip, so the baby might have some kind of issues from the ayahuasca.

She didn’t take the bait.

The very next fucking night, I had another nightmare.

I can never remember what they’re about.

I sat up in a panic and, to my absolute shock, Bonnie was standing at the foot of the bed.

Staring at me.

Slack-jawed.

Face wet.

But instead of drool dripping from her mouth this time…

…it was stomach bile.

And it smelled familiar.

It smelled like creek water.
