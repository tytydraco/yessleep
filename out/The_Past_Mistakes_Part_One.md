Hello. My name is Robert. And I’m in trouble. And it is all my fault. I don’t know how much time I have left, but I need to get this out before HE gets to me.

It all started in August last year. It was a regular day like any other. Summer was about to end, so the kids were getting ready to go back to school. I woke up to see my wife, Loretta, making breakfast in the kitchen and my kids were eating what she had made. Loretta saw me, giving me that cheery smile she always made in the mornings. “Good morning, honey” she said. I glanced back at her with a small smile. “Morning” I said. I kissed her, and I sat beside my son, Jack, who was using Loretta’s phone to watch some random videos online.

“Morning, daddy” Jack said, looking at momentarily before going back to his video. The way he said dad was very funny to me. I rubbed his hair as Loretta got my food. Bacon and eggs, like usual. I thanked her, beginning to dig in, when I heard my oldest son, Daniel, coming downstairs, still in his pyjama pants. “Mom, dad” Daniel said as he grabbed a slice of bacon from the plate and ate it. “Morning, sleepyhead” Loretta said in a playful matter. I could tell he was up late once again with friends. I didn’t blame him. At his age, I used to hang out with my friends all the time late at night.

“Got anything planned today?” I asked Daniel with food still in my mouth, much to the disgust of Loretta giving me a look. It had been a while since my son and I got some father/son bonding time, and I didn’t have work today, so I needed something to do. “What do you have in mind?” Daniel responded. I thought for a minute. But before I spoke, I heard my daughter, Trina, calling out to her mother. “Mom! I can’t find my jacket!” “It should still be on the chair where I left it last!” Loretta said, calling back to Trina from the stairs. Trina then came walking down fully dressed, like she was going out somewhere fancy.

“I’m heading to Jessie’s house. I’ll be back later” “Hold on, sweetie” Trina stopped to look at Loretta like she was in trouble. “Did you clean your room like you were told to?” She sighed in annoyance. Loretta, knowing what that meant, told her to go back upstairs and clean her room before she could go to her friends house. “This isn’t fair!” She announced her thoughts. “Neither is being a parent” I responded. This got a few snickering from my boys. As I finished up my food, I heard my phone ringing in my pocket. I saw the number wasn’t one I recognised. I thought about cancelling the call, but something in my gut was telling me to answer it. So I did.

“Hello?” I said in a calm manner. “Robert? Is this you?” That voice. I remember that voice. It was my high school friend, Norman McKay. I hadn’t talked to Norman since me and him graduated high school. “Norman? Holy sh- crap! It’s been a while, man! How’s it been?” “Listen, Rob, I need to talk to you about something important. I can’t say much over the phone, but I’m getting the old group back together. Meet me at the Flowing Rivers Coffee Shop at Downtown around 10 am” “Uh… Sure. I can do that. I’ll see you then” After that, he hung up, without saying bye. Well that was something. “Who was that?” Loretta asked, nosing in on the conversation. I was shocked busy her sudden appearance, but I calmed down, responding “Oh! It was Norman McKay, a high school friend of mine. Apparently he wants me to go meet him at the coffee shop in Downtown” “Huh” she said rather unsure. “Any reason why?” “Didn’t say. He just needs to meet me and the others in our group” I checked the time on the clock, and it was 9:22 am. I had to get going now so I wasn’t late.

“Sorry, honey. I need to get going to see him now. I’ll see you when I get home” I went upstairs to get some proper clothes on. I kissed Loretta bye, rubbed Jack’s hair again, and headed out to the car. The distance between my house and Flowing Rivers Coffee was about a 20 minute drive. I listened to some music, singing along to it. I’m a pretty good singer. When I arrived, I entered the store, finding Norman. He had changed a lot the last time I saw him. He had long brown hair which was starting to grey, a clean shave, and now had glasses. To be honest, I almost didn’t recognise him when I saw him. I only did when I heard Norman’s voice. There was already two of our other friends. Billy Powell, and Cary Hickman. “Norman! Shit, man. You’ve changed a lot last time I saw you. Billy? Cary? Still look quite good”. Billy and Cary smiled at that, but Norman kept his poker face, not changing his expression much.

“Hey, Rob. Long time no see. I’m glad you could make it. We’re still waiting for Ray and Israel to arrive, so just sit down and order something if you want to. It’s on me” Norman said very calmly. I was very confused by his attitude, but I didn’t think much of it. I sat next to Cary, ordering myself a hot chocolate, because I don’t really like coffee. Then I remembered what he said. Ray Beasley and Israel Poyner, our other friends, were also coming. He didn’t say that our last friend, Ford Evans, was on his way. “What about Ford? Is he not coming here?” Norman didn’t say anything. “I’ll explain when everyone’s here. Right now, just sit down and relax”. So I did. 10 minutes later, Ray and Israel finally arrived. They were excited to see us again, and I wa happy we were all reunited after over 20 years.

Norman cleared his throat, getting prepared to give us the news of why we’re back together. “Alright. Now that everyone’s here, there’s something I need to tell you all. And it’s not good news. You may be wondering why I’m starting without Ford. Well he’s the reason why we’re here today” I saw Norman attempting to hide his face, like he was about to start crying. “Last night, I saw on the news that someone was found dead in their apartment with their girlfriend next to them. Then they showed names. It- I-It was Ford. Ford was murdered alongside his girlfriend”. Hearing those words, everyone went silent for a moment, not knowing what to say. I looked up the news article of the murder the night prior, and it said the following: ‘Last night at around 7:30 Pm, the bodies of 38 year old Ford Evans and his girlfriend, 28 year old Heather Downes, were found in his apartment dead. Ford’s intestines were removed from his body from a large cut in his abdomen, while Heather’s eyes were gouged out and multiple slices were made across her body. Police are sure this was a homicide, but it is unknown yet if the killer is one of the victims or not. News will be updated in the near future’.

I showed the article to the rest of the guys who were still trying to figure out what Norman said. Cary and Israel began to tear up a bit from this discovery. Billy still refused to believe it was true, and Ray was frozen in shock of learning his old friend was gone. Norman just sat still in his chair, already done grieving the night prior. I sat back in my chair. I couldn’t believe it. Ford was the youngest one in the group, and he was dead. Murdered. I don’t know what we’re going to do now.
