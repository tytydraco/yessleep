I did not sleep. I stayed in my room with the door locked, listening.  
The sound didn’t stop. It came and went, never staying long enough for me to get used to it, never stopping long enough for me to forget it was there.

Slow. Deliberate.

At some point, the light outside started to change. The dark shifted into something softer, and I realized it was morning.

I heard the kids and went downstairs. Jeremy was already sitting at the table, eating breakfast.

He did not look at me. He did not say anything.

For a moment, I just stood there before sitting down across from him. He kept eating, quiet, like he had been the past three days.

I didn’t say anything either. We sat there for a few minutes without speaking.

After a while, he finished, got up, and went upstairs. He didn’t look at me when he passed by. I waited a bit before moving.

I found Josh and Jonathan later, getting ready.

I stayed by the door for a second before saying anything.

“Hey,” I said.

They both looked at me.

“Does Jeremy ever… go outside at night?”

“Sometimes,” Josh said.

“Yeah,” Jonathan added. “Why?”

I shook my head.

“I don’t know. He asked me something yesterday.”

“What?”

I hesitated.

“A game,” I said. “Something with leaves and branches.”

They were quiet for a second. Then Josh nodded.

“Oh. That.”

“He plays that sometimes,” Jonathan said.

“Sometimes?” I repeated.

“Every now and then,” Josh said.

“Only at night,” Jonathan added.

I nodded slightly.

“Does he always ask someone else to play?”

“Yeah,” Josh said. “He doesn’t do it himself.”

“What do you mean?”

“He doesn’t pick anything up,” Jonathan said. “He just tells you what to do.”

“And then?”

“He fixes it,” Josh said. “At the end.”

They said it like it didn’t matter. Like it was just something he did.

“And the stuff?” I asked. “The leaves, the branches…”

“It’s just there,” Jonathan said.

I hesitated. “Do you know where he learned it?”

“Some kid showed him,” Jonathan said.

“What kid?”

“I don’t know.”

There was a short pause.

“I think it was someone from school,” Josh said.

Jonathan shook his head.

“No, it wasn’t.”

They didn’t say anything else after that. I didn’t ask anything else either.

I went outside after that.

The yard looked the same as it had the day before. The grass. The dirt. The edge of the woods.

I walked to the spot where Jeremy had arranged everything.

There was nothing there.

No leaves. No branches. No beak.

I stood there for a bit, looking at the ground, expecting to notice something I had missed. There wasn’t anything to notice. Not even a mark.

I moved around the area, checking from different angles, like that might make something appear. It didn’t.

I tried to remember exactly how it had looked. Where each piece had been. How close I had been standing.

I knew it. I was sure I knew it.

But the more I tried to picture it, the less clear it felt. Like something I had already gone over too many times. Like it had been worn down.

I stayed there longer than I meant to.

I stopped trying after a while.

The image didn’t really go away either. Even when I wasn’t thinking about it directly, it was still there. That same spot in the yard. That same shape I couldn’t quite put back together.

Every time I got close to it, something didn’t line up. Something small, but enough to notice.

I sat down inside and tried again, this time slower. Just the branches first. I could see those. One against the ground. The other slightly raised. That part felt right.

Then the leaves.

I knew there were three. I tried to place them, but it didn’t work. I adjusted them in my head, moved them around, tried different positions. None of it felt correct.

I stayed like that longer than I meant to, going over it again and again, trying to get it right.

After a while, it started to feel harder to focus. Not suddenly. Just a slow pressure, building behind my eyes.

I leaned back slightly and closed them for a moment, but the image didn’t go away. If anything, it stayed clearer there. The branches. The empty space where the leaves should have been.

That was when I noticed the sound.

Faint at first. Easy to ignore.

I opened my eyes. The house was quiet. I stayed still, listening.

I shifted in my seat and tried to let it go.

Then it came again.

A soft tap.

I looked around, trying to place it. It didn’t come from the walls. It didn’t come from the floor. I turned my head slightly.

Nothing.

I pressed my fingers lightly against my temple without thinking. The pressure there had changed.

I kept them there for a few seconds.

Then it happened again.

A tap.

Under my fingers.

I pulled my hand away. The room stayed quiet.

I sat there for a while, trying to think of a reason. Lack of sleep. That had to be it. I stood up, walked around, listened again, then sat back down.

The sound came back.

This time it didn’t stop after one.

Tap. Pause. Tap. Longer pause. Then again.

Slow. Even.

I stayed still, listening, trying to follow it without thinking about it. After a while, it started to feel… familiar. Not the sound itself. The spacing. The pauses between each one.

Like there was a pattern to it.

I closed my eyes again, not on purpose this time. Just to focus.

Tap. Pause. Tap. Longer pause. Then again.

I stayed like that, counting without meaning to, trying to match it to something.

At some point, I realized I had already heard it before.

Not like this. But close enough.

I opened my eyes. For a second, I couldn’t place it.

Then it clicked.

The way he had made me say it. Out loud. Each part separate. Each one spaced out.

I didn’t move. I didn’t say anything.

But it was already there.

Three leaves. Pause. Two branches. Longer pause. One beak.

I tried to stop. I didn’t say it out loud. I didn’t move my lips.

But it didn’t matter.

It kept repeating.

Matching the sound.

Tap. Pause. Tap.

It’s still happening.

It hasn’t stopped.

I don’t know if it’s going to stop.

I don’t know if I did something wrong. Or if this is just part of it.

I keep thinking about what Jonathan said. That he doesn’t pick anything up. That he just tells you what to do. And then fixes it.

I don’t know what that means.

I don’t know if it’s finished. Or if something’s missing.

I haven’t gone back outside. I don’t want to. I don’t think I should.

The sound changes sometimes. Not faster. Not louder. Just… closer.

Like it doesn’t need to travel as far.

I don’t know how else to explain that.

I keep thinking about that question. The one he asked me. The first thing he ever said.

I didn’t think it mattered.

It feels different now.

So I guess I’ll ask it too.

Have you ever played Three Leaves, Two Branches, One Beak?
