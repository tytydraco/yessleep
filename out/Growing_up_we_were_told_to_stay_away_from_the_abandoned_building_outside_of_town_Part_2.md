About a month ago I [posted](https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/comments/1s10exi/growing_up_we_were_told_to_avoid_the_abandoned/) about an experience I had growing up in my hometown.  I mainly posted it for therapeutic purposes.  I’ve been told by my therapist I need to share more.  Be more expressive.  The sad reality is I lead a solitary life. I work from home, rarely go out.  Most of my human interactions come from the internet.  That’s why I figured my experience would be best expressed in this sub-Reddit.  Just another story for those with a mild curiosity to look at before moving on.

  
However, since posting about that experience I’ve gotten a few messages that have intrigued me.  Others who have lived in my hometown, past and present, have come forward to share tidbits with me.  I know they’re authentic based on the fact that those messaging me either mention their own name or the name of the town.  Obviously, I’ve used redactions where necessary.  

  
I have heavily debated whether or not to share these messages.  As I stated last time, I am comfortable in my ignorance.  What happened to me as a teenager was deeply traumatic and I fear that if I dig deeper I may only find more unexplainable horror.

  
Unfortunately, I feel a familiar curiosity building within me.  The same curiosity that drove me to investigate that abandoned building as a teenager.  That curiosity led me to share this message.  If there’s any interest I’ll share more in the future.  I’m still nervous about all of this.  I guess we’ll see what wins out in the end; my nerves or my gnawing curiosity.



Message 1



“Hey man this is \[REDACTED\].  I’m not sure if you remember me.  I was there that night.  I was one of the freshmen who was hazed.  I didn’t see much.   I was in the back of the group.  I only ran because everyone else did.  For a long time I didn’t really believe all the stories.  I wrote it all off as a local legend.  Mass hysteria.  Whatever.  I’m not so sure anymore.  Things around here have changed.

  
A few months back, the county (I’m assuming it’s the county since it’s their land) put a fence around where the abandoned house is.  The fence is no joke either.  It has to be 10 feet tall.  A serious amount of barbed wire sits at the top.  The county put it up due to a hazing ritual gone wrong.  That’s the rumor anyway.  I can’t believe the kids were still using the house as a way to Haze.  Some things never change.

  
Apparently, what happened was a kid just walked inside and never came back out.  One of the freshmen went right up to the front window and stared directly into the eye.  The other freshmen were too scared to approach so they stood a few feet behind him.  Then, without warning, the kid walked straight through the front door!  Insane.  The other kids waited, hoping he’d come out.  He never did.

  
Granted the story is from the local rumor mill.  But something had to have happened.  Before the fence went up, people said they saw men in black walking around the forest.  FBI, CIA, something like that.  I never saw the men in black themselves but I’m pretty sure I saw their vehicles.  On my way back from work I saw three nondescript black cars parked on the side of the road near where the abandoned house is.  A few weeks later, the fence was up.  Never saw a construction crew.  It’s like it just appeared overnight.  The whole thing feels straight out of a movie.

  
I’m glad you got out of this town.  I heard you moved to \[REDACTED\].  My family and I will be moving near there soon!  Never been a city person but we’ve got to get out of here.  Luckily my wife \[REDACTED\] works from home.  She can hold down the fort till I get a job.  You remember her?  She was way out of my league in high school.  Still is!  I’m a lucky man.  We’ve got a couple kids.  That’s the main reason we’re moving.  I don’t want to raise them here.  This place has always been a bit of a ghost town but even more so these days.  Everyone’s leaving.  The only people that seem intent on staying are the older townsfolk.  Like your parents.  Whenever I run into them, they mention you.  I understand wanting to stay away from this place but you should at least give them a call.  They miss you.  Wishing you the best brother.  Peace.”
