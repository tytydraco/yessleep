I didn’t even want to go.

That’s the truth.

Xander planned the trip. He always does stuff like this. Says we need a break. Says we spend too much time inside.

Benji agreed right away. He always does. Doesn’t think things through.

Nico didn’t want to go. Said that from the start. He doesn’t like the woods. Never has.

I went because I didn’t want to sit at home alone.

So we went.

The drive took a while. The road got worse the deeper we went. Pavement turned to gravel. Then dirt. Then just a narrow path between trees.

No signal. No houses. No people.

Just woods.

Xander said it was perfect.

We parked near a small clearing. It was getting dark, so we moved fast. Set up tents. Gathered wood.

We built a fire before the sun was gone.

That fire mattered more than we knew.

At first, it was normal.

We sat around the fire. Talked. Ate. Benji told dumb stories. Nico stayed close to the flames.

The trees were all around us. Tall. Close together. You couldn’t see far past them.

But it felt fine.

Then Benji threw a stick into the woods.

We heard it land.

Then we heard something else.

Another sound. Like something picked it up and dropped it again.

Benji laughed. Said it just bounced.

No one pushed it.

The quiet came next.

Slow at first.

The bugs stopped. Then the leaves. Then the wind.

It didn’t go silent all at once. It just faded.

Nico noticed first.

“Do you hear that?” he said.

Benji smirked. “Hear what?”

Nico shook his head. “Exactly.”

We all listened.

Nothing.

Xander shrugged. “Animals moved off.”

Maybe.

But it didn’t feel right.

We kept the fire going longer than we planned.

No one said why.

We just did.

But wood runs out.

And people get tired.

Xander stood up. “We’ll be fine. Tents are right here.”

Benji agreed. “Yeah. It’s not a big deal.”

Nico didn’t argue.

That was strange.

We let the fire die down.

Then we went to sleep.

I woke up to a sound.

Something brushing against my tent.

Soft. Slow.

Like fingers.

I stayed still.

It moved along the fabric.

Then stopped.

Then tapped.

Light. Careful.

I grabbed my flashlight but didn’t turn it on yet.

“Benji?” I said.

No answer.

The sound moved again. Other side of the tent.

I turned the light on.

For a second, I saw a shape through the fabric.

Right outside.

Then it moved away.

Fast.

I unzipped the tent and looked out.

The fire was almost gone. Just embers.

Xander’s tent opened.

“You heard it?” he asked.

Benji came out next. “What’s going on?”

Nico stepped out last. He looked off.

“I didn’t like that,” he said.

None of us did.

We rebuilt the fire.

No discussion. We just did it.

Xander grabbed wood. Benji helped. I kept my light on the trees.

Nico stayed close to the fire.

As the flames grew, the feeling eased a bit.

Not gone.

Just held back.

Then we heard it.

A voice.

From the woods.

“Hey.”

It sounded like Benji.

Benji froze. “That wasn’t me.”

The voice came again.

“Guys?”

This time it sounded like Nico.

He grabbed my arm.

“No,” he said. “That’s not right.”

The fire popped. Flames jumped higher.

The voice stopped.

We didn’t leave the fire after that.

Not even for a second.

The voices came back when the fire got low.

Always just outside the light.

Always someone we knew.

But off.

Time dragged.

The fire started to drop again.

Xander stood up. “We need more wood.”

Benji shook his head. “No. Stay here.”

“If it goes out, we’re blind,” Xander said.

“And if we leave, we’re dead,” Benji said.

Nico spoke low. “It wants us away from the fire.”

We looked at him.

He didn’t explain.

The fire made the choice.

It dropped lower.

The shadows stretched.

The trees felt closer.

Xander grabbed a flashlight. “Ten feet. That’s it.”

“I’m going with you,” I said.

Benji stayed. Nico didn’t move.

The second we stepped past the firelight, it felt wrong.

Colder. Heavy.

We grabbed what we could.

Then I heard it.

Right behind me.

“Don’t go too far.”

My voice.

Exactly my voice.

I turned fast.

Nothing there.

Xander looked back. “What?”

“Nothing,” I said.

But it wasn’t nothing.

Then Benji screamed.

From the woods.

Not the fire.

The woods.

We ran back.

The fire was still there.

Benji was still there.

He looked at us. “Did you hear that?”

Nico was gone.

We searched the edge of the clearing.

Called his name.

The woods answered.

His voice.

Crying.

Asking for help.

But always further away.

Never closer.

Xander started toward the trees.

“I’m getting him.”

Benji grabbed him. “That’s not him.”

“You don’t know that.”

We did.

The fire dropped again.

The voices got louder.

More of them now.

Not just us.

Different voices.

All calling.

All begging.

All outside the light.

Something stepped into view.

It looked like Nico.

At first.

Same shape.

Same size.

But it stood wrong.

Too still.

Its head tilted.

“Xander?” it said.

He stepped forward.

I pulled him back.

“Don’t.”

The thing smiled.

Too wide.

Wrong.

More shapes behind it.

Dozens.

Between the trees.

Tall. Thin. Not right.

Watching us.

Waiting.

The fire flared when a log broke.

Flames shot up.

They all stepped back.

At the same time.

That’s when we understood.

The fire wasn’t just light.

It was the only thing keeping them out.

We burned everything.

Branches. Bags. Parts of the tents.

Anything.

The flames got high.

The clearing became a circle of light.

They stayed outside it.

Moving.

Watching.

Waiting.

We made it to morning.

Barely.

When the sun came up, they were gone.

No voices.

No shapes.

Just trees.

Nico’s tent was still there.

Untouched.

No signs of anything.

He was just gone.

We left fast.

Didn’t pack right.

Didn’t talk.

Just left.

Benji won’t talk about it.

Xander says we panicked.

Says Nico wandered off.

Says we imagined it.

I don’t agree.

I know what I heard.

I know what I saw.

And I know this.

Those things didn’t show up that night.

They were already there.

Watching us.

Learning us.

And if that fire had gone out...

None of us would have made it back.
