We all had something we were craving. Some vague idea In the back of our subconscious. Something we desperately sought after, only to be left disappointed upon typing our concept into a search engine. I too was in this situation. I had to find a birthday present, for my roommate. He liked video games, old video games to be exact. He wasn't the retro gamer type, more of a early 2010s gamer. I tried looking for some game he hadn't played before. I pulled up google. typing in 2000s video games. I got typical games as the top results. He played those already. I mumbled. Ive got to find something more niche, more unknown, something that would surprise him and be a testament to the fact that through all the years of friendship, I hadn't neglected his interests, as he never had mine.So I began to scower the web. I came across multiple games I thought were suitable candidates to gift to my friend. But he had a very specific taste. Something a simple google search couldn't satiate. So I went onto the *Im Looking for* Forum.

I typed the website into my search bar and was greeted with a small animated version of a detective holding a magnifying glass, the words "Im looking for" sprawling out of his side in a minimalistic and sleek fashion. Below him was the usual registration box. Asking for

Username:  
Password:  
Login

I typed in my Username and logged in.  
Immediately a beige box filled my screen: The Words *IM LOOKING FOR* on the top left. It prompted me to finish the sentence. So I typed in. *IM LOOKING FOR* a early 2010s video game about artificial intelligence going rogue and fighting against humanity.  
The title felt too long, so I shortened it. IM *LOOKING FOR* a 2010s video game about rogue AI

With the title of my post done, I quickly wrote a description of why I wanted to find this game, with a desperate plea to find the game quickly so I don't turn up to my roommates birthday party empty handed.

To my surprise a hour after I had posted the question I received an answer:

"*This game might be what hes looking for*"

Someone had commented and posted a image of a video game, not belonging to any established studio or console.

"*Its for PC don't worry*" the commenter had annotated.

Atleast he wouldn't have to buy a new console. I looked up the game. Zarathustra was its name. It was indeed about some mysterious AI, that harvests peoples brains for processing power. Not quite what I was looking for but it would do. I asked him where I could find the game.

*"Do I need to buy it second hand?"*

I replied. Within a few minutes he wrote

*Yeah cuz ts not in stock on any conventional shipping or media site.*  
*But youre in luck. Ive got this game like 3 times cuz I thought it be rare or something. I*  
*Ima give it to you for 10 bucks.*

After paying the money, he sent me a screenshot that the game would be arriving at a post office in 20 minutes or so. They probably live close by. I walked to my front door and took my shoes off my shoemat. My roommates shoes were no where to be found. He must be out with his friends or something.

Good thing he cant catch me buying him a birthday gift. I went to the post office, it was uneventful. I got the game and some wrapping paper and walked back. Crossed the street, got honked at by some angry drivers and found my way back home. When I arrived I put my shoes back onto the shoemat and strutted back into my room. My PC was still on. Still logged into the forum. I browsed a bit, bored and exhausted. Then I found a bizarre yet popular post on the front page.

*IM LOOKING FOR* this person  
The title intrigued me. Was it allowed to look for people using a forum like this? Surely that wouldn't be allowed. I clicked on the post wanting to report it. Maybe it was a troll or something. The Post opened:

I only have this picture of them

And attached to the post, was a picture of me.  
After the shock wore off I was baffled. Why had someone taken this photo of me? It was remarkably candid.  
Showing me looking over to my side as I crossed the road.  
For what its worth It was incredibly zoomed in, illuminating my side profile.

Looking at the clothes I was wearing and the state I was in. I could only come to the conclusion that someone had taken this photo roughly 30 minutes ago.  
From what angle did they take this? From a car? I have to do something about this. I clicked on the three dots on the top right corner of the post and clicked report: *Sharing personal information* and wanted to call it a day. Probably just some weird guy who saw me today, nothing more.

But I didn't click off, I looked at the comments. Instead of a flurry of condemnation, I saw cooperation. People were typing possible names, locations and workplaces that would identify me. Most were far off and probably trolling. But then I heard it: A *Ding!*

Ive been mentioned somewhere. I clicked on the link that brought me to the comment.

*This is likely their username @\[my username\]*

This was under a post, which was similarly looking for me.  
Attached was a image of me. Showing me, sitting on my chair logging into the Forum on my desktop.  
 The image had a caption:

*Any Ideas who this user could be:) ?*

I felt my spine chill as I saw a pinpoint and exact answer, with my full name and location.

*We found them!!*

People celebrated.

I rushed to my front door. I had to report this to the cops.  
 I put on my shoes and then noticed something. It weren't only my shoes that stood on the shoemat but my roommates shoes were there aswell. When the realization hit me I heard a

*click*

I turned my head quickly  
and saw the flash of a smartphone flick through a window before disappearing.  
I got into my car and drove to the local authorities. I showed them everything. The weird posts, the breach of my privacy, the possible breaking and entering and they listened. They interviewed my roommate, who was pissed off that I had gotten him involved with this fiasco. But I couldn't trust him anymore. A week later the cops told me that the website had been taken down, similar complaints were made across the country. My roommate was let go scot free. I moved out a week later, went across the country, to a new city and new life.

Yet as I stand infront of my new apartment.  
I noticed it: A small click and flash.
