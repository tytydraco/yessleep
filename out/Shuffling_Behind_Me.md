The first thing I noticed about the shuffling was that it never followed me outside.

I didn’t realize that detail mattered at the time. I only noticed it because I started testing the sound once it became impossible to ignore.

Inside the apartment, I would hear it.

Outside the apartment, I wouldn’t.

If I walked down the hallway toward the front door, the sound would follow one step behind me. If I opened the door and stepped into the building corridor, it would stop immediately. Not fade away. Just stop.

Like something had reached a line it couldn’t cross.  
At the time I assumed it was my imagination.

Old buildings make strange noises. Pipes shift, floorboards creak, neighbors move around.  
None of that explains why the sound matched my footsteps.

It was always a little late.  
If I stepped forward, I’d hear the faint drag of something behind me half a second later. A soft shuffle, like someone walking carefully on the balls of their feet.

The first time I noticed the timing, I stopped walking.  
The shuffling stopped too.

That was when I turned around.  
There was nothing there.

Just the narrow hallway, the flickering light above the kitchen entrance, and the smell that never seemed to leave the place.

I had only been living in the apartment for a week at that point. It wasn’t a great place, but it was the only one I could afford. The previous owner had left behind some furniture and a few broken appliances, and the walls had that dull yellow tint you only see in buildings that haven’t been renovated in decades.

The smell had been there since the first day.

I cleaned everything when I moved in. Floors, counters, cabinets. I even scrubbed the inside of the closets.  
The smell didn’t change.  
It reminded me of old laundry.

The shuffling started two days later.  
I tried to ignore it.

That lasted until I realized the sound stopped whenever I stopped walking.  
I started testing it after that.

If I walked slowly down the hallway, the shuffling stayed behind me. If I sped up, it sped up too. If I suddenly spun around, the sound cut off in the middle of a step.

Like something had frozen.  
There was never anything there when I turned.

I checked the mirrors.  
Nothing.

I set my phone on the kitchen counter and recorded the hallway while I walked away from it.  
The video showed me walking normally. No second shadow. No movement behind me.

Just me.

The apartment building actually has cameras installed outside every unit door. I asked the landlord if I could check the hallway footage once, just to make sure no one had been entering my place when I wasn’t home.

He pulled it up on his computer.  
The recordings showed me leaving in the morning and coming back in the evening.

No one else.

No one following me.

The shuffling still followed me inside.

A few days after that, I noticed some of my clothes were missing.  
It wasn’t obvious at first. Just a sock that never turned up in the laundry, a t-shirt I couldn’t find.  
I assumed I’d misplaced them while unpacking.

Then the smell started getting stronger.  
Especially near the hallway.  
And especially on the back of my shirts.

I didn’t notice it until one night when I took off my shirt before getting into the shower.

The collar was wet.  
Not damp like sweat.  
Wet.

Like someone had dripped water on it.  
Except it smelled wrong.  
It smelled like drool.

I washed every piece of clothing I owned that night.  
The smell came back the next day.

The shuffling got louder over time. Not by much, just enough that I couldn’t pretend it wasn’t happening anymore.

Sometimes when I walked through the apartment I felt something strange on the back of my neck.

Not a touch.  
Just warmth.  
Like someone standing too close.

A few nights ago the shuffling stopped completely.  
I was walking from the bedroom to the kitchen when I realized I couldn’t hear it anymore.

I walked the hallway again.

Nothing.

I walked faster.

Still nothing.

For the first time since moving in, the apartment was quiet.  
I thought it meant whatever had been following me was finally gone.

But things haven’t gone back to normal.  
I keep doing small things without deciding to.

Yesterday I opened the front door and stepped into the hallway before realizing I hadn’t planned to leave.

This morning I stood in the kitchen for a long time before noticing I was facing the wall.  
I don’t remember walking there.

The smell hasn’t gone away either.  
If anything it’s stronger now.  
No matter how much I shower.

A few minutes ago I stood up from this chair and walked to the door again.  
I don’t remember deciding to do that.

I keep looking at the handle.

I’m not sure why.

I keep thinking about the hallway outside my apartment.

About the stairs.

About the street.

My hands have been hovering over the keyboard for a while.

I don’t know why.

Just now I feel compelled to stop.

To stop writing.

I keep looking at the door.

I want to go outside.
