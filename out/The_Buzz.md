It started low, barely audible. I mean, how many people even use wired headphones anymore. Outdated technology for Lotharios and technophobes. But those people who did started to complain about a buzzing sound. Humming feedback emanating from the little speakers in their ears. Now, plenty of people have had that problem. It can be caused by dozens of things. Easily dismissed. But it kept on happening. Online tech boards became flooded with complaints. People thought there was some sort of problem with the manufacturing. Mass recalls were sent out. But it didn’t matter if they were the most modern, or the earliest models. All wired headphones were affected. The only unaffected devices were those that didn’t require an ongoing connection to the grid. If it ran on batteries or had an external power source, then it was free of The Buzz.

Of course, investigations were launched. Power stations the world over were studied, in hopes of identifying the source of the problem, but nothing came of it. Wired headphones were soon phased out, and the problem seemed to go away. At least on the surface. But from dank little corners of the internet, something was starting to stir. People who had held onto their wired headphones. It was mostly meme groups in the beginning. Pages like r/Buzzboys and r/Buzzed started to pop up. They mostly consisted of joke posts, ironic conspiracies and remixes of The Sound. Harmless really. That was until r/Thosewholisten emerged. Founded by a man named Lionel Coles. Coles believed that he could hear something in the noise. His profession as an audio engineer lent him some credibility, at least to those that paid him attention. He actually started on Buzz Boys. Posted semi regularly, fun little audio gags originally. His remix “Flight of The Buzz Boys” was a minor viral hit, which gained him some attention. Then he posted, “Listen to The Word” to YouTube. It was a two-minute video of Coles messing around with a 10 second sample of The Buzz. The usual shit. Messing around with pitch, speed and tone. But then a word started to emerge, strained but audible. A few basic refinements and it was clear as day. One simple word, “Listen”.

People dismissed it at first. We had all watched enough ghost shows to be skeptical when words appear from static. But we could all hear it, that much was undeniable. However, just because somethings undeniable, doesn’t mean people didn’t anyway. Coles started appearing on podcasts. His group, “The Listeners” as they called themselves, started analyzing every second of The Buzz. For a while their efforts were fruitless. The more they studied it, the less they learnt. Sometimes it sounded like screaming, but mostly it sounded like nothing at all. After a while people simply lost interest. New trends came and went. r/Buzzboys and r/buzzed went dark. But The Ones Who Listen only seemed to be galvanized. More dedicated to their cause of listening. Deciphering the meaning of The Sound. Eventually they were mocked underground. Into websites with strange names and obscure domains. And there they sat, below the surface on their unintelligible mission.

Then the second word came. Feedback emanating from speakers. Music systems, Tv’s, laptops. Anything with a speaker. Much louder than before. Harder to ignore. The world really started to pay attention. It was everywhere, always in the background. Some carried on regardless, most simply unplugged their entertainment systems from the grid. Home generators started to gain popularity. Anything to avoid the sound. But it was not the only thing people were trying to avoid. The Listeners had grown dramatically. Surveys from the time suggested that 5% of the global population saw themselves as Listeners or at least open to the idea. They deciphered the second word in two days. “Time”. What it meant was up for interpretation.

Tech companies, afraid for their bottom line, invented the power stabilizer. In essence, a condom for power plugs, that filtered out The Noise. The handy device allowed for the resuming of relatively normal life. But The Listeners started to grow more bold and more public. If you went into a town or city, you were almost guaranteed to see one of them handing out leaflets, evangelizing The Word. The first Listening Station was set up. A temple for those who wished to listen and study The Sound. Within a few months, Listeners became the 5^(th) biggest religion in the world. They became part of normal life. Something that could be ignored, but only just.

The third word is when people started to become really scared. It destroyed the power stabilizers. It was loud. It was all that could be heard, cancelling out everything else that was being played. Nothing that was tried to combat it worked. People abandoned the grid entirely. Fights would break out in the street over batteries or the ever-prized generators. By this point, listening stations were becoming ever present. They taught that The Noise should be embraced, rather than ignored. To the point that each station blared it out of speakers 24/7. Communities started to become segregated into Listeners and non-Listeners. The value of soundproofing skyrocketed. The Word itself was far less exciting, but no less important for the believers. “is”. It was clearer too. You could almost make it out without enhancement. Everyone was on edge. But they adjusted. Silent movies started to make a return. Live acoustic music became an important community staple for those uninterested in The Word. Slowly things escalated, bands were stationed at the borders, in an attempt to counter the sound that leaked from the Listener territories.

The fourth word was the tipping point. A deafening sound that emanated from anything that could produce sound. The last remnants of digital audio were trashed. Littering the streets of the non-Listening areas. Even previously unaffected items like mobile phones had to be silenced. The global communication network ceased to exist. The Listeners became much more erratic, violent even. Some said it was from the lack of sleep or the pain from their self-induced tinnitus. Others suggested that The Noise itself was causing the madness. Whatever the case, hard borders started to be set up between the two factions. But try as we might it became increasingly difficult to avoid the new Word. Which this time was “running”. Noise cancelling speakers were set up on the walls of fortified cities. Then they started to build the sound weapons. Speakers of incredible power. Able to be carried and directed. Finding yourself in the path of one of these things meant death, if you were lucky. If you were unlucky something worse was in store. We called them the Quiet Listeners. They were people caught at the edges of the blasts. Their external blood vessels erupted, causing blood to weep from their bulging eyes and burst eardrums. They are mostly silent. But sometimes they scream. It’s the worst sound I’ve ever heard, worse than The Buzz, much worse. They’re hollow somehow. They don’t seem to know who they are anymore. They just wander into the outskirts of the cities, past the walls. Sometimes in packs, sometimes alone. You better hope they’re alone, anyway. Encounter a large enough group of them and their collective screams can be enough to make you into one of them.    

The sound wars were swift and brutal. Some say as much as 20% of the global population died in the 3-week conflict. Africa became a stronghold for survivors, mostly because of the lower technological density, meaning that the original wave spread slowly and was more easily contained. Russia, Australia, New Zealand and The UK also managed to fortify, or at least so we were told. A handful of “Safe communication” methods apparently still exist. But I’ve never seen them.

You hear stories now and then. Of the US and mainland Europe. Spies wearing specialized noise cancelling equipment. They say that all those who resist hearing The Noise are killed. Placed in soundproof chambers and bombarded by so much of The Noise that they literally explode. Other stories, of cities built by the Quiet Ones. Cathedrals to sound. Dedicated to amplification. All who get too close end up becoming absorbed into the fold. Straight lines of blood trailing to the city. I wonder if one could see it from above would it look like a child’s drawing of the sun.

The last word decimated The Listener cities. It was a sound so loud that it could be heard reverberating around the world. All technology from the old world was rendered unusable. Those who didn’t die joined The Quiet Ones in the cathedrals. The cathedrals started to grow. Their radius got bigger and bigger. Russia fell. Africa followed suit. Australia and New Zealand are still safe, as far as I know. The UK is barely hanging on, and we won’t last much longer. I’m part of a group that’s been tasked with carrying out humanities’ last plan. Every functional aircraft in the country has been fitted with a nuclear warhead. If we can take out a few of their mega speakers, perhaps it’ll buy humanity some more time. But even with the precautions we’ve taken, most of the vehicles will simply disintegrate from proximity to the sound systems. Hell, even if we succeed the world will be thrown into a nuclear winter. An unideal situation. I hope something survives. Perhaps one day this account will be studied in classes. At the very least I can give context for the last word. For anything that might discover it. The true message. “Listen, time is running out”.
