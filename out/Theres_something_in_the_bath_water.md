Last March I was fortunate enough to finally move in with my boyfriend. I lost my job in the winter and ended up getting evicted from my apartment. It was a shit hole anyway, so I wasn't too upset. My boyfriend, on the other hand, hasn't been in the best of spirits.

We've been dating for 3? Maybe 4 years now? These past few years with him have been a blur. These have probably been the best years of my life, but, at least up until a few days ago, I wasn't entirely sure how he felt.

Despite the time we've spent together, very little of it was at his house. Early on, he was super pushy about meeting up at my apartment or some third place; never at his house. At the time, it was a bit of a red flag, but he's been so wonderful to me otherwise. Because of the kind and caring person he was, I chose to turn a blind eye. Maybe he just had a messy place or he didn't want me to know of some secret wealth that he had. God, I wish that latter option were the case.

I had been at his house a few times before fully moving in. I may not have many options, but even I wouldn't move in blindly. The first time was because he had left his wallet in my car after our 1st anniversary. The morning after, when I had found it, I knew he would need it before his overnight shift.

I'm not exactly proud of how I found his address, but he wasn't responding to any of my texts. I'm sure he was asleep. It was a long date after all and with work the next night, he'd have to sleep during the day. However, I couldn't let him forget his wallet. I flipped the brown, tattered wallet open and rummaged through it until I found his driver's license. I glanced it over until I found his address and put it into my phone.

At this point, I knew he wasn't hiding some secret mansion from me. He lived on the North side of town, and while I'm not entirely judging, it's not exactly where you would find ravish abodes or even white picket fences.

His home looked fairly big for one person—maybe a 2 bedroom house. It was off-white and baby blue, though I'm sure the colors would've been a bit more vibrant a decade or two ago. It looked worn down, but generally in good shape. When it came to my fear that my boyfriend might be a secret hoarder, it was a relief to see that his lawn was neat and free of any tacky decorations. I had to knock multiple times and really loudly before anyone answered the door.

"Quinn?" Despite his bed-head and the dreary, dark bags carrying his eyes, he looked more alert than ever. "What're you doing here?"

"You left your wallet in my truck." I held out the chestnut colored wallet in front of him. "I thought you might need it for your routine Taco Bell visit before work." His face did not mirror my own gleaming smile.

I know it's probably impolite to look into someone's private area that they seem to be guarding, but can you really blame me for trying? His taller and more stocky figure blocked most of my viewing pleasures, but I was able to get small glimpses inside. Just like any other southern house built in the late seventies, his living room had a shit-brown carpet with the same colored wood paneling on the walls to compliment it. Refuting my previous fears, the interior seemed well kept too. It wasn't perfect of course, but it looked recently vacuumed and most things seemed to be in their place.

"How... did you find my house? I don't really remember giving you my address." His words snapped me back to where I was and my eyes met his face once more. I couldn't quite tell if he was irritated, confused, or fearful. His expression gave nothing away.

"You uh... told me it a while back. I don't really remember when but I wrote it down in my notes. It's a good thing too! Otherwise you wouldn't have this." I waved the wallet in his face since he had still yet to take it from me. It seemed my teasing seemed to successfully decept him from my less-than-honest response because he clicked his teeth and snatched the wallet from me. He pulled me into a quick side-hug and kissed the top of my head.

"Well, thanks for bringing it back." His tired eyes seemed to relax due to our brief embrace.

To let him get more sleep, I chose to leave after kissing him goodbye. I often laugh back on this memory, remembering how despite how tired and frustrated he was, he was a true gentleman through and through. In only a T-shirt, boxers, and house slippers, he walked me back to my crimson truck, letting me use his arm as leverage to get in. He slowly walked backward as I pulled out of his drive, waving at me. He didn't go back inside until my vehicle was completely out of sight.

There were a few times after that I had visited, usually for the same kind of thing. Returning lost items, delivering mail, and other small errands like that, but he never invited me inside. About a year back, after dropping off his jacket on my way to work, I asked to use his bathroom.

"It's broken." He said promptly. His quick answer made my mind run a million miles per hour. If he wasn't secretly rich or a hoarder, then why was he so adamant about not letting me in the house. Not even just to use the bathroom? My mind tried to race some more but the urgency of needing to piss outweighed my suspicions. "There's a gas station half a mile down that way." He pointed East. "It's right past the Taco Bell."

The urgency surged and before I knew it, I was running to my truck, too quick for him to follow. Though, from the looks of it, he hadn't even attempted to escort me. He stood at the door, only his eyes following my Chevy to the stop sign.

A couple months ago, my boyfriend and I had a long and serious talk. I told him about my job and living situation. I hated to be the one to do it, but I brought up living with him. Just as I had assumed, he seemed less than ecstatic about the idea, however he agreed to it in the end. No matter how hesitant, I was still excited to finally live with him.

Giving me a break from packing my belongings, he finally let me in. He showed me around the whole place, except the bathroom of course. "You won't want to go in there." He would comment as we walked by. Scared of the answer, I chose not to question why.

After the tour, he sat me down and went over some general house rules. This was completely fair. At the time, I was unemployed and he would be providing for both of us, so I could only agree to everything.

"If you want to bring friends over, please let me know beforehand. I'm not too much of a stickler about that kind of thing, but if you don't warn me, I will not apologize for walking around in my undies. Just saying."

"No one wants to see that anyway." My mind wondered to the best of places. I had always imagined living with him, but seeing his house laid in front of me brought true bliss to me. I envisioned my favorite flower pot sitting next to his on the window sill, towels with each of our initials hanging right next to each other, and our shoes now accompanying each other by the door. The golden hour peering through the window helped establish the memory fondly into my mind.

"This is my mom's old house so as long as if you treat it with some respect, we shouldn't have any problems. Not that I'm worried about that with you." It seemed like he added that last part to make sure that I wasn't offended. I love how thoughtful he is like that.

"Oh, and about the bathroom. Don't go in there past 10. It fine to go in after sunrise and until then, but never go in after 10 o'clock."

My head snapped to look at him. "You got it working again?"

"Yeah, my landlord fixed it up for me a while back. Not very well of course. That's why you can't go in after 10."

I couldn't stop myself from squinting at him. Why would the plumbing stop working from 10pm to sunrise? It made no sense, but I truly knew nothing about plumbing. My sense of novicity detered me from questioning.

"Unfortunately it still smells pretty bad in there. I've tried everything and it just kind of won't let up. I could get nose plugs if you want."

"It can't be that bad." I laughed, looking over at the closed door that supposedly shielded my nose.

"You say that now..." He trailed off. "Oh, and no baths. Ever. Just use the shower." 

This was a bit disappointing for me. I had chronic muscle aches that seemed to only be relieved by nightly baths and Aleve. Beggars can't be choosers though, so I'll just have to buy some extra Aleve.

That night, during my test run of the house, I woke up to a light knocking noise. It sounded kind of like a branch knocking on a window, but it seemed to be further into the house rather than outside. Liam was already at work so when I woke, the bed was empty. Irritated, I stripped the sheet off of me and stumbled through the house, guided by the soft knocking. The further I went, the louder it got. When I made it to the end of the hallway, where the bathroom door starts it off when entering through the living room, the knocking stopped.

The home was almost eerily quiet, only a few fans filling the audio-less void. I peek out past the hallways into the living room and through to the kitchen and didn't see anything. No matter where I looked, I kept being drawn towards somewhere— the bathroom. I stared down at the brass-colored handle, seeing my own upside-down reflection in it. The more I looked at it, the more I thought it might move... do something, but nothing came.

Taking a deep breath, I pressed my ear up against the door. I remembered the smell from having used it before bed. Liam wasn't kidding in the slightest about the smell. I almost took him up on the offer for nose plugs, but at this point I was too prideful after turning him down. No smell could ever be that bad, but this one was very, very close.

From outside the door, a subtle, almost inaudible creak was heard. I tried to press my ear closer, but couldn't. My fingers slowly wrapped around the knob, tempted to pull it open, but I paused.

It was just the plumbing that was bad, right? If I just looked inside, that shouldn't be an issue. I debated it in my head for almost a full minute before loosening my grip on the handle. I would have to ask him tomorrow more on the rule. Not wanting to upset my Love, I turned my back to the bathroom door and headed to bed.

He woke me up earlier than I would have liked, lightly shaking my shoulder. "Quinn... It's wakey wakey time." He sung, his voice cracking on the higher octave.

As frustrated as I was, I didn't get much time with him during the day. In four to five hours, he would have to sleep before going to work. That meant that I spent most of my time at the house by myself. I cherished every moment with him. "Good morning, Liam." I croaked, my throat thirsting for water. Hearing this, he passed me my Stanley.

"Good morning, Quinn." His voice was nearly a hum, close enough to almost lull me back to sleep. "How'd'ya sleep?" 

I rubbed my eyes before stretching. "Fine... for the most part. I woke up in the middle of the night."

His face paled for a moment, though his smile didn't slip. "Oh really? How come?"

"There was some kind of tapping noise but it went away. I think it might've been in the bathroom? Probably just the pipes." I yawned, recovering the previous night's voyage.

"You didn't go in there, did you?" His voice wavered, the chipperness fading.

"Nah... I didn't." I looked over my shoulder and towards the door. What would have happened if I did? "I really can't go in there after 10? Even if I just want to put on deodorant or something?"

He held onto the blanket, his knuckles whitening as he thought his response through. "If you need deodorant in the middle of the night, just keep it in the room."

This answered my question well enough, but brought up another. "Why? Why can't I just go in there?"

He looked away from me, so I couldn't see his face. He stood and turned towards the door. "I'll make you breakfast." He's never been so cold to me.

Cinco de Mayo has recently become one of my favorite holidays. Mexican food, music, and drinking? Hell yeah. Liam was even sweet enough to take off of work to celebrate with me, so of course, we started drinking later into the night. I don't usually drink this much, but when I've got a six pack of Modelos just to split between me and my boyfriend, you bet it'll be gone by Seis de Mayo.

All of that drinking led me to the stinky bathroom. It was clean, almost spotless actually. It was hard to imagine that this kind of smell could be coming from it, especially considering the amount of bleach I could smell as well. I swayed as I sat on the toilet, barely able to hold myself up. Like every good American in the golden year of 2026, I pulled out my phone to entertain myself. Upon opening, the bright 10:16 blazed into my eyes, staining them with fright.

It was past 10, and I was in the bathroom. I wasn't sure what to expect. I looked around, almost as if I was going to find something. The more I looked, the less I found. The bathroom was clean, silent, and stinky. I finished up what I had begun, washed my hands, and left. Maybe it wasn't exactly 10pm. I mean, it would be ridiculous for the plumbingi to mess up at exactly 10 anyways. I pushed the thought aside and returned to my loving boyfriend. He pulled me into an embrace as he attempted to dance with me once more. I didn't bring it up with him, fearing his resentment.

I once again awoke to knocking coming from inside the house, this time much louder than before. I looked over at Liam, surprised that it hadn't woken him up too. He must've drank more than me. I attempted to peel the sheets off of me, however my stomach turned as I moved.

'I drank too much.' I thought far too late before vomiting. Most of it fell off the side of the bed with some of it onto my shirt. The muddy substance dripped from my chin and my olfactory senses were flooded with the horrific, sour smell. Part of it reminded me of how the bathroom smelt.

Once I got a grip of myself and finished, I took a gander at my boyfriend, observing how he was somehow, still asleep. I wanted to groan, but my throat hurt too much. I was grown, so I would just deal with this myself—no need to wake him up.

After a long time of stumbling around and finding the cleaning supplies, I was able to clean it all up in under 15 minutes. Thankfully, none got on the bed, so I just had to clean the floor. Myself and my clothes were another story though. I smelled horrid and it was nearly unbearable at this point. I had no choice but to clean myself up.

I grabbed some PJ's and a towel real quick before heading for the bathroom. I already broke the rule, so what was the point of abiding by it? Nothing happened. At this point, I was nearly convinced that Liam just had some weird phobia of going into his own bathroom at night. I couldn't let that stop me and I desperately needed this shower.

Out of habit at this point, I didn't even consider bathing. If I had thought about it, I might have, but the smell made it hard for me to think about more than just getting it off of me.

The cold water hit my skin, shocking me awake. I could see goosebumps consume my flesh and shivers take over me. I closed my eyes, finding it hard to enjoy the shower. So, the hot water cut out after 10? That wasn't really the biggest deal. He could've just told me that. Frustrated, I scrubbed my body, probably unintentionally exfoliating it in the process.

My eyes opened wide as I felt the water graze above my ankle. They shot down to my feet, and sure enough, the water was rising. I looked at the plug, that I knew I hadn't put in, sitting on the arm of the tub. The most disturbing part was the 'water' that had now encompassed my feet up to my upper ankle was the same muddy color as my vomit from earlier. The color and the stench almost made me hurl on the spot.

I hurriedly, turned off the water and snatched my towel. Before stepping out, I could see that despite turning the water off, it was still rising. Not only that, but peeking out just over the surface was a thumb and index finger, gripping my ankle. The scream I let out was finally enough to get Liam up.

To look at things optimistically, I started walking more. I'm sure the gas station clerk is tired of seeing me so often, but I don't talk to him so I wouldn't know. I've been telling myself that there was just something wrong with the plumbing, but I'm not sure. Has anyone else on the North side of Deaten Town had the same issue? If so, please, please reach out. The smell still haunts me.
