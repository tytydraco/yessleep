Happy Summer guys! Thought it’d be cool if I shared a fun little story about my family here. My sister and I come from a, well, not the most affluent family. We grew up in a very small, rural, and simple community where everyone knows everyone — which was a rare blessing  — but it really wasn’t the place to grow up in if you ever wanted to change the world, start making money, or even to attend college. It was the old Mississippi farming corner where even the Summer sunrise felt languid and laid-back and the local Church was there since the days of yore. I enjoyed it, I’ve always been quite popular in town and I loved the idea of humble schools, living next to my second cousins, having the local Priest as my godfather, and throwing sleepovers at my friend’s house on School nights. My sister, on the contrary, was never too fond of that very vehemently provincial environment. That girl had the brains and guts ample to conquer the world. When we were kids, she had to explain to me why Helium balloons don’t fall on the ground like the regular, nitrogen-oxygen ones. Helium.

Anyhoo, she did. She self studied for the SAT and, well, she was admitted into some very reputable and prestigious schools. My parents couldn’t afford most of them, so she went to one on (almost) full scholarship. My parents weren’t too thrilled about their angel daughter’s departure, but they agreed to support her little journey, with a community-wide faith in the tenet that she would come back eventually, Lord willin’ and the creek don’t rise. Don’t remember which institute she’s gone to, but I believe it was California Institute of Technology; because that was where she met her future husband. A bright fellow. He did something important in… oh, Radium, yes, it was Radium studies. He won an award for it. Radium. 

After she left town, it was a great matter of time until I again saw my baby sister. I worked the farm on my own after my dad got sick, which was alright with me because it meant higher wages. Ha-ha, I am kidding. I love my old man and I wouldn’t have  jest had he been unwell now, when I write this story. He’s good and healthy now, but back then my mother and I really had it hard. We wouldn’t have gotten through if it weren’t for our abundant supply of good ol’ Mississippi beef, praise the Lord. Our beef was both what helped my dad manage his low blood pressure (thanks to the Phosphorus in it) and get some income flowing. Phosphorus.

After a year of hardly communicating through letters with my little sister, she finally visited. Her husband… well, back then, they were not yet married, drove her to our farm. She introduced him to my mom and my recovering dad, and to her very charming and beloved older brother. He seemed a noble man, a fitting one. A good Christian too — an Irish Catholic, to my funnily embittered mother’s dismay, but an honorable man nonetheless. It was then that he asked my father for his blessing. Good heavens, I remember the exulting spirits of that summer. The whole town was jubilant as they saw their young and distant daughter back home, and with a fiancé with her. They met at a lecture at the California school earlier mentioned, as I recall, a lecture about a newly discovered synthetic element called Einsteinium. Einsteinium.

It was certainly a great summer of reunion and joy. He was an upright fellow, it appeared to us all that he would make a good husband to my sister. He was a mannered, dignified, and tempered man. And, granted, he was a resourced California Irishman. He had social connections and his family had an established amassed fortune. And, for a moment, the town was so drunken of cheer that it was as if there were something in the water. Of course, there wasn’t anything in the water, it was still simply basic Hydrogen and oxygen. Hydrogen. 

However, one night, as I spoke to my sister, she told me about her relationship with that man. She loved him, and it seemed that he loved her, but she spoke to me so much regarding their dynamics that I have grown to realize that he uses his status as a mean. As a weapon. Almost as if… if he were to abuse her, in any way, she would bite her tongue and look the other way. They are fine, of course. I am not here to divulge any mind blowing information, I wouldn’t want to break my sister’s privacy. They are a very loving and sweet couple, that love nowadays is as rare as Erbium. One last time. Erbium.
