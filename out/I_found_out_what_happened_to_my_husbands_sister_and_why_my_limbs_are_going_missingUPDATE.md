I am so disgusted at myself right now.

I’m disgusted by how I judged my mother-in-law [earlier](https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/s/eBVX33dXOX). I used to think she was just an eccentric, paranoid old woman. Now I know the truth: she was a simple woman caught up in the middle of an ancient horror - one that took away her daughter years ago, and is now coming for mine.

Needless to say, I dare not go anywhere with my husband until I have a solid game plan.

Earlier today, I faked some baby duties just to avoid going out with him. He looked patient, not too bothered by my refusal, but there was something hollow about him.

“It’s alright,” he said, his lips stretching into his usual, robotic smile. “Next time, we will take our baby there too. Both you and the baby can use some fresh air. Plus, you’ve been acting strange as of late.”

Now I finally understand her multiple amulets. Now I understand her overzealous praying. She wasn't crazy. She was just protecting herself, and in a way, protecting us.

The next time my husband went down to the lake, my mother-in-law finally broke her silence. She told me about the  *Bacchachor* — the child stealer.

I won't bore you with all the folklore, but it started with an ancestor of ours who first moved to this land. Her infant daughter died suddenly, and the mother went mad with grief. She refused to let anyone touch the dead body. The whole village could hear her agonizing cries echoing through the night.

But the next day, her family found her bedroom empty. After hours of searching, they found her by the edge of the lake. She was sitting under a massive tree, her hair unkempt, a blank smile plastered on her face, gently rocking her child.

And her child was very much alive.

As happy as the family was, dark things soon followed. Death and misfortune plagued the village. Crops failed overnight, livestock rotted in the fields, and then... a village child went missing.

Those were unscientific times. It was easier to blame a grieving woman than changing climate conditions or freak accidents. The village men dragged her to the lake. They made her watch as they burned her resurrected child alive, and then they hung her from the branches of that same tree.

But she never truly died. She still lives through her eternal grief. She takes female infants, but only when they are offered willingly by both parents. In return, she blesses the men of the family with boundless good fortune.

“You offered your girl willingly?” I asked, my voice trembling, unable to believe what I was hearing.

“Have you noticed something wrong with yourself lately?” she countered, her sharp eyes scanning my face.

“Yes!” I blurted out, tears stinging my eyes. “My fingers and toes... they're missing. I look down and I can’t see them. And earlier, I saw you smiling at an empty corner of the room.”

“It always makes the mother see things first, I didn't experience anything like that in my first pregnancy” she explained grimly. “I heard strange noises. The pressure cooker would go off right in front of me, but all I could hear was the beautiful chirping of birds. Your father-in-law , curse that man’s soul— had already made a pact with her. That’s why our crops never failed. That’s why his businesses thrived and we were able to build this mansion.” A flash of pure, unadulterated anger crossed her weathered face.

“I am glad he is dead," she spat. "I am sure he tried to bargain something else after his cancer diagnosis, but it didn’t work out for him." she was relieved. "He convinced me that my hallucinations after childbirth would be cured if I just prayed to the tree by the lake. He took me there one night, and like a fool, I believed him. He asked me for a lock of my hair, and a single drop of my blood.

The symptoms stopped the very next day. My hearing was completely back. But then, she came for Ashi.

I saw it. I held my baby girl, crying, when that thing entered the nursery at night. At first, I didn't even know what I was looking at. It stretched out a rotten, elongated hand and touched me. The moment it made contact, I saw visions... I saw her life. I saw her child’s agonizing death, I felt the rope tightening around her neck as she hung from the tree... it was almost as if she was justifying her horrific actions, explaining her rules to me.

And then I blacked out. When I came to my senses, my sweet baby girl was gone. Everyone sympathized with my 'loss', but nobody believed what I actually saw.”

My mother-in-law stopped speaking, the heavy silence of the house settling in around us.

My daughter is already four moons old. According to the timeline, I still have a little bit of time to figure out a plan.

My husband thinks he's going to trade our daughter for a lifetime of wealth and success. But I am going to make that man pay.
