I woke up in the middle of the night to the sound of people talking in hushed voices, but this wasn’t an uncommon occurrence, when you have young children you get used being woken up. My first thought was that my daughter had experienced a nightmare and that my wife had gone to her room to comfort her and help her get back to sleep. So I closed my eyes and was getting ready to doze off again, when I noticed something peculiar about the tone of the voices coming from Olivia’s bedroom. I could pick out my daughters voice, but then it occurred to me that the other voice did not belong to my wife, which brought a sinking feeling to my stomach.

Now startled I rolled over on to my side and saw that my wife Jessica was still snuggled up in bed next to me. I held my breath and listened intently, thinking that I had just imagined the second voice, after all my daughter sometimes talked in her sleep. There it was again, the second voice was quiet but high pitched, a child like voice, but it wasn’t Olivia’s. I scrambled out of bed and made a bee line for her bedroom, although I was quietly panicking my parental instincts took over and gave me a shot of courage. I was ready to confront whoever or whatever I found in her bedroom.

I opened the door and quickly reached for the light switch, then flicked on the lights. My eyes darted across the room, looking for an intruder, but to my relief I only saw my daughter. I thought that I must have imagined the second voice after all. Olivia was sat upright in bed and staring at me with wide eyes, she was clutching Frosty, a stuffed toy Penguin, in her hands.

“Daddy, I’m scared,” she said.

“Who were you talking to honey? Have you had a bad dream?” I asked.

She glanced nervously towards the corner of her bedroom, where Luna, her favourite doll was propped up against the wall in a sitting position.

“Luna was talking to me daddy, she woke me up,” Olivia explained.

Suddenly it all made sense, how could I have been so stupid? Luna was a vinyl plastic doll that my wife had purchased from a thrift store for my daughters eighth birthday, like many toys it had the ability to ‘talk’, in other words it could utter pre-recorded phrases. The doll was designed to respond to physical touch and motion, but if the components were defective then it might well be malfunctioning and ‘talking’ unprompted.

I wandered over to where the doll was sitting, then picked it up and began to study it. This triggered the sensor inside of it and the doll promptly said “I’m tired, will you read me a story?”.

The doll had been made to resemble a small child, it had shoulder length blonde hair, blue eyes and chubby plastic cheeks. It was wearing a pastel pink dress, like something that a little girl might wear. In many ways it was an unremarkable toy, it looked like it might have come off an assembly line at a factory.

“Are you sure you didn’t touch her?” I asked.

“No, she woke me up,” Olivia re-iterated.

“Well I’m sorry honey, but I think she might be broken,” I explained, “she’s only supposed to talk when you touch her, but if the sensor inside of the doll is broken, then that might cause the doll to say things when she isn’t supposed to,”.

Olivia shook her head, then said “no daddy, I think she’s alive,”.

“She’s just a toy Olivia, there’s nothing to be scared of, she’s just broken,” I replied.

“Daddy please don’t turn off the lights, I’m scared,” Olivia whined.

I walked over to my daughters bedside and placed my right hand on her back to comfort her, but she didn’t take her eyes off of the doll in my left hand.

“It’s okay honey, I promise I’ll leave your light on,” I said, then I stooped down and kissed the top of her head.

But Olivia was still staring at the doll, then she whispered “daddy, I think she’s real,”.

“I know you’re scared honey but believe me it’s just a doll, she has a tiny computer inside of her that makes her talk,” I explained, “It’s like when you hear people talk when you’re watching a movie, it’s just a recording,”.

“But...but she said things, like different things...things that she doesn’t normally say,” Olivia stammered.

“What did you hear her say?” I queried.

“Luna said that she wishes that she still had a body,” Olivia answered, “she said that she wishes she could walk and play like I can,”.

I’d be lying if I said that didn’t scare me, it actually made the hair stand up on the back of my neck. It’s not that I believed that the doll had literally said those words, but in the dead of night a child’s imagination can be scary in of itself.

“You were probably still half asleep when you heard her talking, that frightened you and then you misheard what she was saying,” I suggested.

Olivia shook her head, “can you buy me a new doll?” she asked, “I don’t like her any more, she’s scary,”.

“Sure thing honey, I’ll tell you what I’ll put Luna downstairs for tonight so she doesn’t wake you up again,” I replied.

When I left her bedroom I almost instinctively turned the light off, then stopped myself when I saw Olivia flinch.

“Don’t worry the light is staying on, now get some sleep. I love you,” I said.

“Love you too,” Olivia replied, before snuggling back under her bed covers.

I carried the doll downstairs and briefly considered throwing it in the trash, then decided against it. Olivia can be quick to change her mind and I had a suspicion that when morning arrived her fear of the doll might have subsided. So I decided to place Luna on the living room sofa, far enough away from our bedrooms that she hopefully wouldn’t wake us if she malfunctioned again. The movement must have triggered the sensor again because when I released her she said “I love you very much,”.

Then I went back to bed, hopeful that I would be able sort everything out in the morning. Either the doll could be fixed, in which case no replacement would be required. Or more likely, I wouldn’t be able to fix it, in which case I would simply buy a new one. I wondered if the thrift store that my wife had purchased the doll from might have more of them in stock, then it occurred to me that the doll might have ended up in a thrift store because it was defective.

I snuggled up next to my wife, who was sill sound asleep, then closed my eyes. The house was almost silent now, apart from the sound of rain pattering on the window. I started to drift off to sleep, my thoughts gradually becoming less coherent, soon I would be in dreamland.

I found myself sitting in a rowing boat, drifting in shallow waters along the shoreline of an island. It was night time and a crescent moon illuminated the sky. Then I realised that I wasn’t alone, there was a doll in the rowing boat with me, it was Luna. I could hear the distant cries of my daughter and I frantically looked around for her, then I saw her running along the shoreline of a white beach. She was trying to follow the boat along the shoreline, waving her arms and screaming in anguish.

But the boat was drifting away from the beach, moving further from the shoreline and Olivia was getting smaller, her cries becoming more distant.

“Don’t leave me here daddy, don’t leave me,” she screamed.

Then I woke up with a jolt and sat upright in bed, almost crying out in fear myself. I was breathing heavily, then I realised that it was just a bad dream and I let out a sigh of relief. But the house was no longer silent, I could hear a quiet murmuring voice coming from somewhere. I guessed that it was probably that damn doll malfunctioning again and thought that I ought to forget about it and go back to sleep. But after that terrifying dream I just had to go check on my daughter.

So I climbed out of bed and wandered into Olivia’s bedroom, the light was still on and I was relieved to see that she was sound asleep. Then I crept downstairs to investigate the source of the noise. The living room was dark but I could still make out the silhouette of Luna sitting on our living room sofa, the doll was babbling away to itself.

At first I couldn’t make out what it was saying, I thought that it sounded garbled, as though its batteries were dying. But there was something odd about the tone of its voice, if it were a human I would describe it as panicked. I approached the doll slowly and listened intently, at first I could only pick out individual words, but as I moved closer it became clear to me what the doll was saying. Then my blood ran cold.

“Daddy help...I can’t see...why did you turn the light off...it’s so dark I can’t see...I can’t move…help...help,”.

My first instinct was that I was still dreaming. After finding myself in that rowing boat I had never actually woken up, one nightmare had merely transitioned into another. I pinched myself and willed myself to wake up, but I was still in my living room.

“Daddy...mommy…frosty...help me, I can’t see...what’s happening to me,” the doll said.

I groaned in terror and tried to make sense of what I was hearing, still hoping that it might be a dream. It had to be a nightmare, because if this was reality then I was losing my mind. I was about to bolt out of the room when the doll suddenly fell silent, which gave me time to regain my composure. I stood there in silence, watching the doll intently and waiting for it to speak again, but it didn’t.

Had I really heard the doll talking? At first I was sure that I had, but as the minutes passed by and I continued to stare at it, doubts started to emerge. It was late at night and I was tired, surely I could have imagined the doll talking. Except I didn’t really believe that it was just my imagination, it was more that I wanted to believe that I had imagined it.

But what if my mind hadn’t deceived me? What then? As I struggled to make sense of it I came up with a rather silly idea that the doll might have a wireless connection and that someone might be playing an elaborate prank on me. My wife had purchased it from a thrift store after all, could someone have reprogrammed it?

Then my thoughts circled back around to some of the things that the doll had said.

‘why did you turn the light off…..daddy...mommy...frosty,’.

I shuddered, if someone was playing a prank on us then they knew the name of my daughters stuffed toy penguin. They also knew that my daughter had asked me to leave the light on, which meant that they were spying on us. And didn’t that voice sound different to Luna’s normal voice? Didn’t it sound a bit like…no...I probably just imagined that bit.

I wondered whether the doll might have a concealed camera or microphone inside of it, that sounded crazy, but it made a lot more sense than a possessed doll. I stooped down and looked the doll in its eyes, as though I was looking directly at whoever was on the other side of the hypothetical camera.

“I know you’re watching us,” I whispered, “this isn’t funny, I’m gonna find out who you are,”.

I continued staring at it for a few moments, when I received no reply I stood upright and turned to face the door to the hallway. But before I could leave I was startled again by that childish voice.

“Who’s watching us? Daddy where are you? I can’t see,”.

Now I was sure, that voice wasn’t Luna’s normal voice, it sounded like Olivia.
