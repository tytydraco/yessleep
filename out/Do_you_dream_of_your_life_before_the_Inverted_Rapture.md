I think it's  been a week or more since The Eye appeared above us. Every day and every night I stare up at it, praying that someday soon it will go away. My town is nearly empty. Scorch marks litter the roads, and I rarely see people outside during my food runs. After the blackout, I thought the whole world had ended and that The Eye had nearly wiped out all of humanity, but today our lights and even the Internet came back on, and with it I hope that means there are people still alive on Earth.

I don't know how long the electricity in our town will last or even if this message will reach anybody, but if you're still alive please let me know. If it helps anybody, I will write down everything that's happened, everything that I have learned, and every horrific thing I have witnessed. Maybe the world won't feel so lonely if we have this shared experience. For those who can see this, do you dream about your life before The Eye too?

Every night I have the same dream, my life before The Eye. Before what I've heard some call "The Inverted Rapture."

I dream of movie nights with my parents, high school exams, walking home with my friends, and giggling at our "secret code" of dangling earrings and matching purple shoelaces. A life I was happy to live. One where I felt safe, felt seen even through closed doors. It was a life I never wanted to wake up from. Yet the dream always ended the same way.

I'm on the couch staring out the window, waiting for my mom and dad to get ready to leave. It's the morning of Easter Sunday. For such a small town with a population of a bit under 200 people, almost 100 percent of whom are die-hard Christians, it is basically mandatory that I go. I'm groggy and cranky since I slept late the night before. My mom comes out of the hallway in a flowery Sunday dress. She gives me a kiss on the forehead. I reel back, feigning disgust, but it really makes me feel better. A few minutes later my dad comes out as well, freshly shaved but with mismatched suit pants and a blazer that's too short for his growing belly. He's never been good at dressing himself like Mom is.

"Alright, honey." He stretches and fakes a yawn.

"Are you awake enough to go to church?"

He laughs and pinches my cheek, and I almost bite him with how much it ticked me off. 

I wish I joked with him more.

He grabs a water bottle and heads for the door, my mom motioning for me to get up from my grouchy ass. As soon as he reaches for the door we hear the loudest rumble of our lives. The house begins to shake and I hold my hands over my ears, feeling as though they are about to burst. My mom holds onto me to try and console me even though I know she's in as much pain as I am. It's unbearable. I can't even hear myself sob under the immense noise, like a moon made of thunder is hurling itself toward the town.

Then there is nothing.

My ears are ringing, but slowly my hearing comes back. My mom and I sob,  my dad comes and hugs us both until we're ok enough to get up.

"Oh my G \_ D. What was that?"

In my dream, His name is always drowned out by screaming.

I hold my mom for dear life as we fearfully creep behind my father. He opens the door and reveals a peaceful neighborhood, as though nothing has happened. Our cul-de-sac is untouched, and a couple of our neighbors start to walk outside a little bit after us.

"Hey, Saul! What was all that commotion about?!"

My right-side neighbor, a bigger guy than my dad but much more timid, stands behind his wife when he calls out to ask what's going on.

"Uhh... No clue, Tom. That's what we are all here to figure out."

My dad answers back, full of sarcasm and annoyance. I never figured out why he didn't like him.

Murmurs grow around the cul-de-sac until a shrill scream cuts through the thick fog of noise.

"My lord Je\_\_s, WHAT IS THAT?!"

My neighbor's wife falls to her knees and breaks down instantly. We all look up, but none of us can believe what we see.

The sun is massive. It looks like it got closer to us but it doesn't feel any hotter. Dark wisps form around it and turn into thick puffy clouds. The clouds above us swirl unnaturally around the massive morning sun until they stop, fixed in position creating what looks like an eye with the sun itself as its iris. The faint symbol of a cross bores itself into the middle of the sun, the pupil of this Eye of Judgement.

We gaze in astonishment and fear. Some kneel down to pray while others do nothing but stare and weep. Before we can act any further, the cross in the middle of the eye grows darker, and the rumbling comes once more, this time beneath our feet. Fire and brimstone, sobs of the damned, screams of the innocent. I watch as burning cracks form beneath my neighbors, burnt tarry arms forcefully drag nearly every one of them down into the depths. Through the commotion I see blurs of numbers etched upon their foreheads, too many to count but so bright and bleeding I cannot mistake them.

"PLEASE NO! G\_D NO! HELP!"

 I snap my head to my right side. Tom's wife is already halfway down into the molten hole with the number 2 branded on her forehead.

"Fuck! No no no, Lisa!" 

A helpless Tom paces back and forth as his wife claws at the sidewalk, scraping blood and pieces of her nails onto the sidewalk. Lisa does not stare at her husband but instead looks toward us, toward my dad. Without hesitation, my dad leaves us behind and sprints toward her. He isn't able to go too far before he begins writhing in pain, clutching his forehead. Beneath him, the earth begins to tear away and melt. Arms without bodies, as long as the Earth is deep, spring out and dig into his sides.

My dad never looked back at us during his final moments.

My mom covers my eyes until I hear my dad scream for help. I struggle to break free from her grip, and as soon as I rip her hand from my face, I wake up on the couch staring out the window.
