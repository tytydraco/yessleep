\[I am alone, at my resting place, the harsh sun off my skin, the shade of this familiar attic offering me life.\]

We are late getting to the house, I have not been here since Nana passed. The sun is setting  
behind the trees as my husband and I unlock the door, the rusted key hardly turning the locks. The dust is unsettled as we make our way through the rooms; no one has been here in years.

\[The time has come.  
I am awoken by the sound of these humans, and I am reminded of my mission.The smell of  
fresh blood has reminded me that I am hungry.\]

We make our way through the house, the light pouring through the broken windows, the taste of  
decay is all around. My husband is on edge, his affluent upbringing has not prepared him for my  
world, or where I came from. He has the bluest eyes I have ever seen, but his money reaches  
far beyond his hairline. His penny loafers are coated in dust; I am reveling in his grimace over  
his italian leather, but for now I am immersed in the memories of this house. I know what it is to  
feel. But his feelings are not my concern at this moment, as mine are never his.

\[My colony has migrated; for the moment. We have a different taste for nutrients, fruit has never  
sustained our appetite; I do the best hunting for us on my own. This sunset has always lead me  
to this place, this house, this most perfect banquet. I stretch my wings\]

My husband stomps up the stairs, designer luggage in hand, Blackberry out of service. No  
business will be handled tonight. I leave him with his stress; he has always been the anxious  
one. The attic is my hideaway, my safehaven. My future and my end. I lower the ladder and am  
hit with a strong smell of the forest, the trees, the memories, and the knowledge of what's to  
come.

\[My hunger is immense as I watch her walk in. I am salivating. The time is not now, but will be  
soon.\]

No one has been here in years; my family has been long gone. Nana was all I had left; the  
weathered roof is showing the neglect of time, and the moon is now shining through the rotted  
wood. I see something move, is it Nana? My heart knows the truth.

\[I am watching her. I am hungry.\]

Peering through the shadows and boxes, I hear a faint sound. Looking up, I watch as the bat  
comes straight to my neck, unrelenting and quiet, so quiet; he has done this before. I feel the  
blood drain from my neck, an oddly satisfying end to an unsatisfying life.I do not struggle. I  
quietly fade, passing my anger on, knowing my husband's demise will be worse. I give the bat  
my life; knowing he will take vengeance for mine.

\[I absorb her life; I feel her in my veins. I fly down to the bedroom; my colony is already arriving.  
We will let him bleed slowly, as she did for him\]
