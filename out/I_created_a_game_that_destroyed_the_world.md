We're all doomed. You just don't know it yet. Our world is slowly falling apart, like the liver of an old alcoholic.

It doesn't matter if you believe me or not, this process can no longer be stopped. But if you still need proof, take a closer look.

The signs, they are everywhere, and an attentive person can already see confirmation of my words in the world around us.

Solar flares are becoming more frequent, the Schumann resonance fluctuation is intensifying, and strange, inexplicable events are increasingly coming to light. I'm afraid that I am the one who started this whole process.

Since childhood, I've loved horror stories, horror movies, everything connected to horror.

 I don't even know why I was drawn to them, especially since I wasn't even scared! On the contrary, I found a certain comfort and solace in those melancholically dark, terrifying worlds.

For a while, I tried to find the scariest movie, game, or story, but without success. Although I enjoyed the process immensely, nothing in this world frightened me. That was until I ended up in the ICU.

Now that was true horror. It was a hospital in some remote backwater, so the doctors were practically uneducated, and the hospital had no money. I was surrounded by crumbling walls, and the food was potato gruel with cockroaches.

Patients walked the corridors dragging bags filled with blood behind them; they were trying to use ultrasound to crush their kidney stones, but the local orderlies, who were like werewolves, ended up crushing their kidneys instead. 

And my roommate had a catheter fashioned out of tourniquets and syringes because there were no real ones.

Anyway, I don't really know how I got out of that hospital. I was probably lucky that nothing serious was wrong with me and the stones passed on their own. But this experience affected me so deeply that I decided it was time for me to do something in this life, to leave something behind in this world.

I had made horror games before, but nothing on a large scale. They were small horror stories with their own small audience, but now I wanted to make something truly significant, something that would reflect all my love for horror culture.

The ultimate horror, one that would include all the elements from different genres!

But how could I possibly combine all that?

And then I understood: I needed to make an OS. A horror operating system that would contain different games, stories, and media files. A true portal to hell.

For the design, I started using real symbols from ancient magic. On the desktop, the Key of Solomon spins, a circle for summoning demons.

The main logo is Dingir. The Sumerian symbol for god, and that became the main name of my game.

Dingir OS.

At this stage, that name is already burned into my subconscious.

Dingir OS... Dingir OS... Dingir OS... I repeated it every day like a mantra.

I have to finish Dingir OS. It became my obsession.

Day and night I sat at my computer, creating the cursed files, images, and sounds that would fill this system.

No AI. Everything had to be real. Behind every cursed file is its own story written by me.

I sat at the computer until I was dizzy. 12 hours every day, molding the organs of this digital monster with my blood and sweat.

I also created other characters people who were supposedly playing Dingir OS. I wrote their stories and biographies, trying to make them feel real.

At night, I would wake up every two hours and write down new ideas, which I would immediately start implementing in the morning.

Until one day, they came to me... 

In a dream, the as-yet-unwritten characters of Dingir OS appeared before me. Souls trapped inside this system. They started giving me tips on how I should organize things, what new functions to add.

Six months passed, and I made the first demo.

Not many people played it. There were only about a hundred downloads, but even that was enough...

A week later, when I opened Dingir OS again, I saw that new stories had been added, new anomalies, portraits that I hadn't put in.

At that moment, of course, I thought I was going crazy from overwork.

At night, the spirits of Dingir OS came to me again. They urged me on, forcing me to work even harder on the game.

No, I couldn't go on like this. I needed to rest. I decided to set aside one day for rest, to clear my head of these endless thoughts about Dingir OS, but there was nowhere to run.

In the news, on forums, on random YouTube videos, everywhere there were messages from my game. Events described in the game were happening in the real world. Files I had created inside the game were appearing on various forums.

The solar flares were intensifying. I returned to the computer and discovered that the game had already been released. Released without my knowledge.

YouTubers are gradually starting to make videos about my game.

A game that I didn't finish and didn't release.

So far, only a few thousand eyes have seen it, but even that is enough.

Pandora's box was opened.

I started frantically going through the plot of my game in my head.

A group of netstalkers creates an operating system to find and sort anomalous objects within the deep web.

Gradually, by processing all these files, they perform an information overload ritual to travel to another dimension and merge with the information flow.

This causes the system to gain a life of its own and exist in many different forms, whether as a game, a scary story, a YouTube video – it lives and spreads, gathering people's souls.

The game's creator writes a post on Reddit about his game, convinced he has started the end of the world.

After this, natural disasters gradually begin, solar flares intensify, the Schumann resonance fluctuation changes, and images from my game appear on creepypasta forums. YouTubers who made videos about it disappear without a trace or become too afraid to talk about it further.

The game becomes an information virus and spreads further and further.

The principle is simple: a person comes into contact with this game, this operating system, with its files, or even just a story about this game, and at night, in their sleep, \*they\* come to them... Those who now torment them, forcing them to keep thinking about Dingir OS. And the more I think about it, the more it grows, gaining new abilities.

These entities, they take the person into that hellish dimension where they continue to fuel the system.

If you've read this far, you already understand that it's over for you. They will come for you in your sleep, just as they came for me.

I'm sorry. I created the game that destroyed the world.
