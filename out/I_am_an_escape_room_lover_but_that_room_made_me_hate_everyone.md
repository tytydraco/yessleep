Hello everyone. I’m Nick. I am writing from my secret phone while hiding in a closet; outside, there is someone feeding on my friends.

​I don’t know where to begin, but it all started yesterday, Tuesday, while I was having dinner and scrolling through Facebook. An advertisement for an escape room caught my attention: it claimed to be the scariest room ever and challenged anyone with enough courage to come.

​I ignored the ad and kept scrolling, thinking it was just another cheap marketing tactic playing on one's ego. But I stopped again when I found a video of an influencer talking about the same room. She was challenging any team that wins by offering them $5,000. I checked the comments; they were all warning each other, with people swearing they would never risk going to this room. I was convinced—no sane person would pay for more than 100,000 comments.

​Fear gripped my heart, ego took hold of me, and curiosity led me back to the official post. I contacted one of the managers there and booked four tickets: for myself, my friend Mark, his girlfriend Lola, and my crush Lara.

​In the morning, about half an hour ago, we were sitting in the waiting room. We were greeted by a staff member who told us the rules: find the body before the hour ends.
​Lola asked, "What happens if we don’t find it?"

She answered with an artificial calmness, "It will be over. Are you ready?"
​We followed her to receive our uniforms. She took almost everything from us and put them in a locker.

Mark whispered in my ear, "Did you bring the other phone?"

I smiled at him and nodded in agreement. The lady who welcomed us left us, and before the door closed and the game began, I asked her, "What is so scary about this room?"

"You'll see," she said with a cold smile, and the door shut.

​We started walking; Mark was kissing Lola and putting his arm around her shoulder. Meanwhile, Lara and I were the complete opposite of them.

Mark winked at me and said, "Lola and I are going to check the bathroom. Maybe the body is taking a hot bath." They left; I was sure he wasn't going to check anything, he was going to do something else—that’s just his nature, he fears nothing but God.
​I smiled at Lara, and we walked on, searching.

A disturbing voice came through the speakers: "Welcome everyone. I am the hidden corpse. Let's play hide and seek."

​I looked at Lara and smiled, thinking the matter wouldn't be as scary as expected. Then, darkness suddenly fell. The voice returned: "I will count to ten, and then... let’s make it a surprise. Don’t let me find you." The counting began, but I don’t remember the song; however, it was exactly like the one in the movie (Ready or Not).

​As soon as the lights returned, I heard nothing but Lola screaming in a loud, annoying voice. Lara and I ran toward her. We found her standing at the bathroom door, her face covered in blood.

"What’s wrong? Where is Mark?" She didn’t answer. Suddenly, a chainsaw pierced through her intestines. Blood splattered across my face. He looked strange; I didn't focus on his face, I only focused on kicking him hard in the leg and closing the door. Lara and I ran, terrified and panicking. We reached a room. Lara hid in a closet, and I hid in the one opposite her because the space was small.

​The silence was deafening, no sound but our breathing which we tried to muffle. Suddenly, we heard the sound of footsteps approaching. It was him, holding Mark’s palm and eating it. He was standing next to the closet where Lara was hiding. She was crying, and I tried to calm or warn her, gesturing with my fingers: "Shhh!"

​But she couldn't; the sound of her crying broke out. The strange man knelt down and looked into her eyes. He opened the closet, grabbed her forcefully by the neck, and opened his mouth in a terrifying way—a strange horror like (Pennywise). He ate her head violently, without even threatening her or giving her a chance to pray. He dragged her by her feet and left the room.

​I tried to contact the police, but there was no signal even though the bars were full. I was in the same room facing a window. I didn't hesitate, I wasn't afraid. I ran with all the strength I had and threw my shoulder into the window; the glass shattered under my weight, and I fell outside. I broke my ankle, but I didn't stop; I kept running. Then a car suddenly appeared; the driver tried as hard as possible to stop but hit me slightly, making me fall. People gathered around me; I gave no answer, no crying. I just stared at that red gaze from the man who devoured my friends... as it faded into the darkness.
