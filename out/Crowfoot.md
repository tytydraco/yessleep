Urban legends. Stories shared around campfires to spread fear, think bigfoot or the loch ness monster. Some are famous, others are stories shared between four friends. I have one of the latter. It's a bit long, so I’ll explain it in three parts. It’s up to you to believe whether or not it's true, but I believe it is.

  
  


Back in the mid-1800s, about ten years before Canada would be founded as a country, strange occurrences started happening in what would become modern day Hilcaster. Around the area of what is now known as Crowfoot, dead deer would be found. That would be normal if it wasn't for how they died. No external damage, but when hunters tried to carry the bodies home, something felt off. The deer didn’t feel right. Too soft. Like there was nothing inside them at all. When the hunters would open them, the deer’s bones would be shattered, almost like gravel.   
Near the site where the deer would be found dead, they would find multiple markings in the snow. Three lines, one long line and two lines coming out from it. It looked like a crow's foot, giving this area its name.  
This would happen every single winter for one hundred   
years. People were frightened by it, but they wouldn’t question why it happened. Food was provided on Crowfoot and people didn’t have to hunt for it, it was overall a major good for the locals.  
But in 1965, developers would come to Hilcaster. Their plan was to turn this forest area into a large city, similar to the nearby city of Adams, but have Hilcaster be an export of wood. Due to how much forest there was, they would be selling wood for hundreds of years if they did this. But obviously, the locals didn’t want this. They were scared of the idea, because it would get rid of the area's natural beauty and disrupt wildlife. That's what they told the developers anyway. The real reason was because they were scared of what would happen if they got rid of the forest in Crowfoot. The fear wasn’t unfounded, but to know why we have to go back to the early 1900s.

In 1905, a family bought land in Crowfoot. They were going to build a house in that area. So they would start cutting down trees in an area far away from town. They made a massive clearing, the yard alone was fifty yards. They started work on the cabin, but every night when they went to a friend's house to sleep, something felt off. On the way there, they would hear footsteps around 150 yards behind them and once they would get inside, they would hear things in the front yard. Not wolves or coyotes, but something much closer to the sound an elk makes.

A few months later, they would finish the cabin. After they moved in, they would stop talking to those friends who provided them shelter. The friends were offended, so they went to the cabin to confront them. But when they got inside, no one was there. And it seemed like no one had been there for weeks due to the rotting food on the counter. After that, the family was never seen again.

Back in 1965, the developers didn't listen. In fact, just out of spite, they would start in Crowfoot. They went into the deepest part of Crowfoot and started cutting down the trees. As more and more trees were cut down, less crowfoot patterns would appear in the snow. In its place, there would be more dead deer. But rather than just having broken bones, the deer would have their limbs removed from them and inserted into their mouth. They would also be gutted of all their organs other than their heart.

The developers were horrified, but they kept going. They kept cutting down the trees.

One day, they all went deeper into Crowfoot to get whatever wood remained from that old family's cabin. They would never leave those woods.  
Now, my story.

I remember these few days like the back of my hand. It was back in 2015 and I was visiting my Nana and Papa at their cabin out in Hilcaster. It was the middle of summer. I remember it was during a heatwave, mainly due to the fact I was sweating my ass off inside with the AC on.

That day, my Nana and Papa brought me down to the Spruce Creek flea market. It was the last day it would be there, so I was given fifty dollars to spend on whatever I wanted. I don't remember everything I got, but I did get this panda made from cereal boxes and rubber bands that would jump when you pushed down on it.  
I also remember them telling me that a cousin from Alberta I hadn't met before would be coming out. His name is Kyle, awesome dude.

Back up at their cabin, I still had a bit of time before he would arrive so I passed the time the way I always do, taking a walk in the forest. I remember this time it felt weird. I always enjoyed taking walks in this forest, but now it felt creepy. I felt like something was following me. I would turn around at one point to head back to the cabin, but when I did, I saw a tall lanky silhouette quickly move out of the way, its arms and legs moving at inhuman angles. I heard its footsteps crunch as sticks and branches broke. Then, I heard it start sprinting at me.  
I turned back around and started running deeper into the woods. After who knows how many minutes of running, I would see the cabin.   
I kept running until I was on their lawn and collapsed on the lawn. I turned back and saw the figure moving back into the woods, silent now.  
I heard a voice behind me laugh.  
“What the fuck are you doing?” The voice asked.   
I quickly stood up and looked at the person.  
“Oh shit, you’re Hunter?”  
I stuttered out a half-baked answer.  
“Y-yeah, that's me. You’re Kyle, right?”  
Kyle laughed.  
“Yeah, obviously.”

Me and him would play for hours, talking about random stuff that 11 year olds talk about. I tried playing normally, but the thought of that thing kept following me.  
“Hey, Kyle… do you believe in like, monsters and shit?”  
Kyle stopped.  
“Uh, kind of I guess, why..?”  
I sighed and explained what happened.  
The color drained from Kyle's face.  
He would look at me and dismiss what I said.  
“Man, it’s probably a deer or something. I-I’m going inside to grab a slice of pizza.”

We both went inside to get some food. I stayed inside longer than him, in order to have a bit more food. I was a bit worried, but I brushed it off. He was now alone in those woods.  
Once I finished eating, I went outside to find Kyle. I spent too many minutes trying to find him, but when I did, I knew something was wrong. He was running out of the forest, sobbing.  
“Thank fucking god, I thought it f-fucking got you.” He managed to get out through tears.  
“What got me? Kyle, what are you talking about?”  
He sat down on a log, breathing heavily.  
“N-nevermind, it doesn’t matter.”  
I let out a sharp breath.  
“For fucks sake Kyle, just tell me.”  
He looked at me and hit the log with his fist.  
“Dude, fuck off. It’s not important.”

After that, I would go to the tent trailer I was camping in that night and Kyle would go into the cabin. I didn’t fall asleep. He didn’t either. I know this because we were texting each other about the elk sounds coming from the forest.

The next day we wouldn’t play. We would mostly keep to ourselves, but around mid-noon, we would meet up on the lawn.  
“Hunter, I just looked up information and about an hour's walk away, there's this abandoned cabin. A ton of people went missing there, we need to check that place out.”  
I blinked at him.  
“Are you fucking insane Kyle?”  
Kyle looked at me, shocked.  
I continued with what I was saying.  
“Because I sure as hell am, lets fucking go there.”

We would spend half an hour gathering food, drinks, knives for protection, and a camera. Not much happened on the walk there, mostly just conversations about random stuff.  
“Wait, so the g-spot is where?”  
Kyle was laughing his ass off while asking the question.  
“Yeah, it's up the… nevermind.”  
A chill went down my spine mid-sentence. The forest had suddenly gone quiet, no birds, no wind.  
Kyle grabbed my shoulder and pointed at a clearing.  
“Yo, dude it's the cabin!”  
I looked forward, reaching into my bag to grab the camera, but pulled my hand out without it.   
Something felt wrong about this cabin.   
It was built in 1905, but it looked pristine, almost like someone had been taking care of it.   
No cracked windows, no crumbling wood.  
Kyle looked at me for a second before looking back at the cabin.  
“So… why is the grass mowed?” He asked, taking a careful step forward.  
I didn’t answer.  
I reached into my bag, my eyes still hyperfocused on the cabin.   
I slowly pulled out the camera and two knives. I handed the camera and one of the knives to Kyle, my hands shaking ever so slightly.

We entered the cabin, slowly. Kyle had been recording for a few minutes already, his hands clearly not steady.  
“What the fuck..?”  
Kyle's voice was shaking heavily.  
The inside of the cabin was destroyed.  
Rotting wood, broken windows, floorboards falling apart, moldy food. We went outside again to look at the cabin again.  
It had changed.  
It was falling apart from the outside now.  
The grass was tall and rotting.  
I was trying not to cry, but tears escaped my eyes nonetheless.  
“Y-you’re being a bitch right now Hunter. Let's just g-go inside… now.” Kyle was trying not to show his fear, but he was failing.  
I was going to argue but decided it would be useless.

We went back inside, our bodies slightly shaking. Every creak made us flinch. We walked past the kitchen as fast as we could, the smell coming from it was fucking abhorrent. Once we did pass, we saw a staircase leading to an upstairs.  
We looked at each other, both of us agreeing to go upstairs.

Once we were upstairs, we sat down on a bench.  
Kyle sighed and looked at me.  
He was fidgeting with his fingers and started stuttering.  
“You wanna know what I saw yesterday..?” Kyle's voice was shaky, sounding like he was on the verge of tears.  
I blinked, not registering the question at first. When I finally registered what he said, I simply nodded. I was too tired to speak.  
Kyle closed his eyes and breathed deeply before speaking.  
“The thing I saw yesterday wasn’t an animal. It was like seven feet tall, and had greenish skin.”  
He was crying now, his voice sounding raw.  
“It had no face, only a mouth.”  
He had to take another deep breath before continuing.  
“It had small dimples where its eyes should have been, one of its arms was growing out of its back… out of its fucking back.”  
I was crying now. That was the exact silhouette I saw the day prior.  
Kyle looked at me, his eyes red and his voice raw.  
“And it touched me. It touched me with that fucking back arm.”  
I was about to say something to him, but as I opened my mouth to talk, I heard the front door open.  
Me and Kyle both said nothing.   
We looked around the room to try and find a place to hide. We saw a bed and we both scrambled to it.  
The second we were fully under the bed, it started walking up the stairs.  
‘Kyle., Hunter. Get home right- right now.’  
It was our Nana… but her voice wasn’t right.  
Suddenly, loud elk sounds came from the stairs. Screaming, gurgling, elk sounds. And then it… stopped. It would go back down the stairs and close the door. Me and Kyle waited probably half an hour, but I can’t be sure, before coming out from under the bed. We would run outside and I don’t remember what happened after that. All I know is that it was dark when we got back, despite us leaving at noon and only being there for under six hours.

We tried telling our Nana and Papa what happened, we begged for them to believe us, but can you really blame them for not believing us? They were focused on why we were gone for so long. No matter what we said, it didn’t matter. They didn’t believe us.

We both went out to the tent trailer for the night. There were two beds thankfully, but neither of us went to sleep that night. We heard the elk noises again tonight. And it couldn’t have been an elk, no matter how much we wanted it to be. Elk don’t live in Hilcaster. They have never lived in Hilcaster.

We got up the next morning, exhausted. Kyle and I would be leaving at noon, we’d be going to Adams. I looked at my phone, barely registering the time. 5:47 A.M. I sighed, but it sounded more like a yawn. Kyle was about to say something, but he stopped himself. That's what I thought until I heard his breathing stop and I saw something tighten around his neck. A large, lanky hand. No, more like multiple tendrils.  
They wrapped around his neck, his face turning purple, I looked at it in shocked horror.  
Whatever it was, it made me scream. My Nana and Papa came running out, but the thing had let go of him before they got into the trailer. We tried explaining what happened, but like before they didn’t believe us. Until they went to the back of the trailer, and saw multiple hits against the trailer. They assumed it was a bear, but knew whatever happened could’ve killed Kyle.

Nothing of note happened after that. We left for Adams at noon and I had my headphones on the whole time.   
I had fallen asleep until we got to Adams, where Kyle had to wake me up.  
Adams at one point was considered a bustling town in British Columbia, but after multiple disappearances, people stopped moving there. Half the town was abandoned and the other half was either the ghetto or wooded areas filled with redneck hicks. I lived in the wooded areas. That didn’t mean it was any better than the ghetto, it just meant there were more places to hide when you got robbed.   
My family had the best house in town, but that wasn’t saying much considering it was still only a three bedroom two bathroom house not built to have three bedrooms. We were the richest people in the poorest town.  
Me and Kyle said goodbye to our Nana and Papa before going inside. My parents were not home very often so we could do whatever we wanted for a bit.  
We went into my room and sat on my bed. Neither of us had said a word to the other since this morning.  
“You wanna play some Minecraft? I got a PS4.”  
I handed him the controller, not wanting the silence to go on any longer.  
He didn’t respond, but he took the controller. I loaded up a new world. I don’t remember what happened while we were playing. We just played for hours. It was dark by the time we finished.  
I stood up and looked out the window. The woods were dark but thin. It got thicker about ten kilometers from here, but I could easily see the lights from the less wooded side of town.  
“The woods are never this thin in Alberta.” Kyle said, breaking a silence that lasted about half an hour.  
I didn’t reply. He was right, Alberta has thicker woods than BC… I wonder how big the woods in Crowfoot are. They go for miles and miles, I wouldn’t be surprised if they leaked into Alberta a little bit.

We would go to bed not too long after that. Kyle's dad would be there to pick him up in the morning. And I went on with my life. I would return to my Nana and Papa’s very often until I turned about 17. After that I would work as many jobs as I could so I could find an apartment as far away from Adams as I could. I would find one in Vancouver around the time I turned 19, just a few weeks after my dad would pass away. I would only return to Adams twice after that, when my sister got married and when my Papa would pass away. And I vowed to never return to Crowfoot, even though my Nana wanted me to. I saw the devil in those woods.  
But vows aren’t the same as promises and one day, not too long after I would turn 22, I would receive a text from an unknown number.  
“Hey Hunter, it's Kyle. Nana only has a few months left and I’m driving out to Crowfoot to spend a few more days with her. U can come if u want. Pls come out Hunter.”  
That single text would bring me back to that awful place.  
I would spend about four and a half hours driving to that place. My girlfriend had tagged along because she wanted to meet my family. She didn’t know why I only talked to my Nana anymore.  
“I can’t wait to see your Nana! I wonder what type of food we’ll have. Deer, moose, elk?-” I cut my girlfriend off.  
“Elk don’t live in Hilcaster Sarah, elk have never lived in Hilcaster.” My voice was monotone, not wanting to go there.  
Sarah grabbed my arm and shook it gently.  
“You’re no fun Hunter.” She was giggling. I was glad at least one of us was happy.  
I looked at the gas tank. Almost empty.   
“Hey hun, can you check where the closest gas station is?” I asked, trying to sound cheery.  
She nodded, grabbing her phone.  
“Theres one about five kilometers ahead in this small woods town.”  
My hands tightened on the wheel.  
“What’s the name of the town?” I asked, trying to stay calm.  
“Some place called Adams, a small town.”  
I looked at her, trying to stay calm.  
“We’ll find another gas station.”  
She looked shocked, but didn’t stop me.  
Unfortunately, our gas tank would go empty right by the gas station.  
“God damn it.” I muttered under my breath, teeth gritting.  
Me and Sarah would walk to the gas station.  
“I’ll find someone to help us with gas, you get food and snacks.”   
Sarah nodded before putting her headphones on and heading inside.  
I would go across the street to the tow truck service I knew all too well from my time as a teenager. When I walked in, the old man from across the counter looked at me, at first with confusion, then shock, then frustration.  
“Where’s the fucking car Hunter?” His voice sounded like how a cigarette smelled.  
“Hey Roy, long time no see.” I tried lightening the mood.  
“Where’s the motherfucking car?” I could smell the cigarette he was smoking all the way from the entrance.  
“Like 400 yards from the gas station.”  
Roy stood up and patted me on the back.  
“Thank’s for not getting stuck up on the highway again.” He still wasn’t smiling and the pat felt more like a hit with how the words were said.  
Roy drove the tow truck to the car and pulled it into the gas station. I pulled out $100 to pay him but he pushed it away.  
“You’re living in fucking Vancouver, keep your money.”  
I was about to go when he called me back.  
He brought me inside and passed me a hat.  
“You forgot it here the day you left for Vancouver, thought I’d return it.”  
It was my dads old hat. The one he gave me before he died.  
I thanked Roy before going into the convenience store. I would quickly grab a pack of cigarettes and a lighter before paying and getting in the car. Sarah looked at me shocked when I got in the car.  
“I thought you quit hun?” She said, clearly disappointed.  
“I did. I bought them just in case I need one.”  
I buckled in and turned on the music. Before I started driving, I lit a cigarette.  
“And I sure as hell need one.”  
She would take an edible and zone out for the rest of the car ride. It was another two hours until Hilcaster.

We would arrive at my Nanas at about 10:30 at night. Shockingly, everyone was still awake.  
I would head inside and see my Nana. She almost cried when she saw me. We both cried when we hugged.  
“Oh my god, I didn’t think you would come out.” Her voice sounded exactly how it did back in 2015.  
“I felt I had to.” I said, trying to straighten my voice out.  
I would briefly introduce my girlfriend to everyone before grabbing a beer. There were more people here than I thought there would be. Aunts, uncles… no kids though.  
I would go outside onto the deck to look at the stars while I drank my beer.  
Kyle was in the pool. He was swimming with another person.  
“Hey Kyle.” My voice was oddly shaky.  
He looked over and quickly hopped out of the pool.  
“Yo what's good man?” He had taken up smoking younger than me, I could tell just by his voice.  
“Not much, life's been absolute shit for me.”  
Kyle laughed. He didn’t find anything funny.  
“Real. Anyways, that's my boyfriend in the pool. You still single?” His boyfriend was waving from the pool.  
“No, I got a girlfriend. She’s inside socializing.” Sarah had always been a social person.  
Me and Kyle would talk for about five minutes before I went inside. I would quickly chug my beer and find my girlfriend.  
“Hey babe, I’m going to go upstairs and crash.” I said, kissing her cheek right after.

The bed was just as soft as when I was a kid. I looked at my arm. All the mistakes I made were on that arm.   
What went wrong? I was two months clean… and now I’m back here. I’m back to the place that ruined my life… and now these marks would never leave. I reached into my bag and grabbed the melatonin gummies. I grabbed three and that was barely enough.

I woke up around 5 A.M. Kyle was the only other person up. I could see the living room from the bed. I slowly got up, groggy from the night before.  
“Hey Kyle.”  
He simply waved.  
I sat down beside him and noticed he had something.   
“What’s that?”  
He sighed and replied, barely awake.  
“Weed.”  
I just nodded before looking at the ceiling.  
I went to the kitchen and poured myself some wine.  
“I’m going back to the cabin.” Kyle’s voice was shaking.  
I spilled a bit of the wine.  
“You’re coming.”  
I looked at him, shocked.  
“You fucking expect me to go back to the fucking shapeshifting cabin? What’s next, you invite me to a building with a sign that says ‘Evil Ass Rape Building’? Like hell I am.” I was trying to laugh. It came out as crying.  
“It’s not like that. We didn’t fucking die there. The worst part was the cabin changing-.”  
I was crying now.  
“What about the elk noise Kyle?! Elk don’t fucking live in Hilcaster. They never fucking have! Elk don’t just choose to mimic fucking voices in the middle of a place they’ve never been Kyle.” My voice was raw.  
Kyle stood up and got in my face.  
“I don’t want to go in there anymore than you do. But three people came with me and now there's only me and my boyfriend. The others went for a fucking walk in those woods and never returned.”  
His breathing was heavy yet controlled.  
“And I’m not dragging my boyfriend into this supernatural bullshit.” His mouth was quivering while his voice was cracking and shivering.  
I was distraught.  
“I don’t fucking care Kyle. I came out here for my Nana, I’m leaving Crowfoot, Adams, and Hilcaster behind after this visit. That means I’m leaving you behind Kyle. I’m not going to the cabin.”  
Kyle slapped me.  
“I fucking know. I fucking know Hunter! You think I wanted to come out here? I hate this place more than you because at least you were freed from this thing. It…” Kyle swallowed hard, trying not to cry.  
“It followed me home.”  
I finished drinking the wine and started pouring another one.  
“I didn’t escape it. I escaped the fucking creature but I never escaped this place. This place follows me wherever I go. I can’t fall asleep without remembering hiding under that bed.”  
Kyle looked at me, about to say something, but he went quiet.  
“I’m going alone you fucking coward.”  
That was the last thing he said to me before he entered that forest.  
He would be in those woods for three days before I had the balls to start preparing to enter the woods. And I wish I could say I entered the second I was done packing but it would be another twenty four hours before I would even step foot onto that path that led to the cabin.

I dreaded walking down this path. I was wearing my headphones and drinking a beer. I hoped that I never arrived at that cabin, that instead I would find Kyle and the others lost in the woods. But you never get what you hope for in Crowfoot.  
The cabin entered my sight. It was perfectly normal looking, pristine. Just like the first time. I threw up when I saw it, only tasting the multiple beers I drank that day. I slowly approached the cabin and opened the door. I didn't bother looking back outside, I knew it was just as rotten as the inside now. It looked identical from the first time I was there. The only difference was that at the bottom of the staircase there were two dead bodies. I didn’t recognize either of them but who I did recognize was Kyle. He was standing at the top of the stairs, looking at something other than me. I didn’t dare look. It already saw me.  
“Hunter., what are you doing out this-this-this late.” It was my dads voice.  
I started crying.  
The elk noises came back.  
I started mumbling to myself, my voice getting louder with each word I said.  
“Elk don’t live in Hilcaster, elk don’t fucking live in Hilcaster!”   
I closed my eyes.  
The thing walked right in front of me. Its breath smelled awful, like blood and piss.  
It started touching my face. Its dry hands scraping against my face… but it didn’t hurt me. And then it left. It walked out of the building.  
Kyle ran to me and started sobbing. He said things to me but I didn’t listen. I just walked home with him. I would pack up my bags that night and go home.  
I wish I could say this story had a happy ending where everyone lived and the creature died but I can’t. I can’t because it followed Kyle home again. Seeing his boyfriend sob at his funeral was the worst thing I’ve ever seen. The pain in his voice was worse than when the creature touched my face.  
My Nana died a week ago and that’s why I’m writing this post. I went to Crowfoot for her funeral. My girlfriend came for me, so I wouldn’t feel so alone. But I haven’t seen her in a full day. I think I have to go back to the cabin. If I don’t make a second post, you know what happened to me. And Kyle, I’m sorry I couldn’t be there more. I’m sorry I didn’t go in with you the first time you asked. I’m sorry for letting you down. I’m sorry for meeting you, because you wouldn’t be dead if you hadn’t met me. Crowfoot takes what it wants and gives what it can in return. It smiles at you while you sob. Crowfoot wants us and it will give more deer. But never elk. Elk don’t live in Hilcaster.
