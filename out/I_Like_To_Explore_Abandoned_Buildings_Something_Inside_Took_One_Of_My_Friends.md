\*\*Part 1 - Dave's Auto Shop\*\*

I checked a local urbanex forum I frequented one night. There was nothing I hadn't seen so far. The only post that caught my attention was one about Wiseman Mine.

George Wiseman was said to have started a mine somewhere on the outskirts of town sometime in the 1800's.

He died a rich man, never having told a soul the location of his mine.

He is said to have sworn an oath on his death bed.

He swore to take up his pickaxe once again and continue mining until he had found all the gold in the ground. He also swore that if anyone came looking for his mine he would use his pickaxe to defend his land.

I thought it was just a silly story. Something to scare newcomers to the scene.

I finally saw headlights coming through the trees and put my phone down. I checked the time, 10:23pm. Late as usual.

A bearded man called to me from the passenger seat.

\>"Hey skinny, We had to get gas!"

Skinny was as much a description as a name. Not my real name. We decided to keep our identities a secret when we met up. We were breaking and entering after all.

The parked next to me and I heard the sound car doors opening and closing. 

I got out of my car and grabbed my bag.

I saw The Groundskeeper grabbing her bag and a handheld lantern from the back of the suv.

The 3 of us walked towards a large building slowly being reclaimed by the trees.

The groundskeeper handed her bag to the bearded man, Wire Cutter, and pulled her hood up.

The old car factory shut down about 30 years ago. The owner, Dave Hardwell, died of a heart attack and didn't have any family alive to claim his business or assets. Nobody ever came to demolish the factory. People just stopped showing up to work.

Whoever was the last to leave didn't even bother to lock up.

We walked through a hole cut in the fence and into what once was a delivery yard. It was overgrown with flowers and bushes now.

Wire Cutter asked who we thought planted them all here. Me and The Groundskeeper just looked at him and sighed.

We made our way through the factory floor and up the still stable stairs. We went further to the locked door to the roof.

Wire Cutter dropped his bag to the floor and zipped it open. Inside were an assortment of tools. He pulled out a drill and hammer. He then asked The Groundskeeper to hold her light closer and got to work.

Eventually he had the door off its hinges and we stepped outside.

The view from the roof was beautiful. We could see the whole town of Westbrook.

We went on for a time pointing out buildings we recognised all faintly lit by street lamps and spotlights throughout town. 

We could also see the stars, better on the other side of the roof facing away from the city.

A few hours went by. I pulled some drinks and snacks out for us. We talked about the other places we had explored.

We had all met online. We shared a few spots back and forth and decided to check out a place together a few years ago. After that it became routine. 

We had all been friends since.

Then The groundskeeper told me that she and Wire Cutter thought they had found a place for us to explore.

I was obviously intrigued and asked them what it was.

They told me it was a gold mine. Wiseman mine.

I thought they were joking and laughed. When they didn't join in I raised my eyebrow at them and they told me they were serious.

I asked them what made them think they had found \*the\* Wiseman Mine.

They told me a source they trusted told them it was somewhere 2/3 down Bowler Ave and 6 miles west into the woods.  

Bowler Ave was a straight road that took you out of town. It connects you onto an interstate highway heading towards the coast.

I told them I wasn't sure and Wire Cutter told me that we would be rich if we found it.

I asked him how he planned to sell the gold and he told me he watched a documentary that said he would just need to go to a gold broker.

I told him you had to prove where you got the gold first and he told me he would just tell them he found it digging holes somewhere.

The Groundskeeper interrupted us. She said she didn't care about the gold or whether we could sell it or not. She just wanted to prove it was real.

She asked if I wanted to see if Wiseman Mine really existed. 

I did, So I told them I was in.

We made a plan and 2 weeks later we set out onto Bowler Ave in search of a ghost story.

\*\*Part 2 - The search\*\*

A storm rolled in the next day after we made our plan to search for George Wiseman's Gold Mine

We had all taken the time to make maps with locations circled we believe could be the mine. 

I found an article from 1897 of a local man, Joshua Briggs, who died somewhere in the area from falling into a collapsed mineshaft. They say he must have fallen onto something sharp in the fall and impaled it right through his skull. 

He was beyond recognition. If it weren't for his wallet and his ID inside they wouldn't have identified his body. 

I warned the other to watch their step during our search.

We started early. 6am. By some miracle they weren't late for once.

I said as much to them when they arrived 5 whole minutes early and The Groundskeeper fixed me with the meanest look. 

I never understood why she had that name. I asked her once and she said it was an inside joke between her and Wire cutter.

We drove for about an hour. I was behind the other 2 and pulled off the dirt road when they did, coming in behind them. The paved road had ended a few miles back.

The 3 of us walked into the woods that day and when we walked out we had found nothing and covered half of the ground we thought we would.

I got bit by ants. Wire Cutter got stung by a bee and we were all exhausted.

We went home defeated but determined to carry on the next day.

2 days of searching later and we found it.

It was late afternoon on the third day. We were on our way back and starting to lose hope.

A tree had fallen over and rested right in front of us. The ground around its roots was sunken several meters into the dirt in an odd pattern.

I recalled the article I had read several days prior and told the others to stop.

I looked around at the tall grass and thick bushes. The taller trees and large rocks on the forest floor. 

Wire Cutter saw it first. A hill to our left with a hole dug into it. The foliage around it was flattened by the tree trunk that had fallen presumably in the storm.

We went home and prepared for the next day to explore Wiseman Mine.

That night I dreamed of hearing the rhythmic sound of metal banging on stone underneath me. A faint voice called out for me to stay away. I woke up in a cold sweat.

The next day we almost couldn't find it. We searched for 2 hours before stumbling upon it again.

The Groundskeeper took a step forward and peered down into the dark. She turned back to us and said,

\>"You boys coming? It looks like it opens up further inside."

With that she began to crawl into the makeshift mineshaft.

Wire cutter followed after her. 

I went last.

There were 3 of us that went to look for Wiseman Mine. Only 2 of us would leave once we found it.

\*\*Part Three - Wiseman Mine\*\*

Even being the shortest I had to crouch as we made our way through the winding tunnel system.

I don't know what condition I expected to find the mine in but at least the main tunnel was in strangely good condition.

The air was damp and all I could smell was the dirt all around us.

We were carefully making our way downwards further into the earth. We saw a few side tunnels as we explored. Some of them were caved in.

We discussed turning back after the second or third caved in tunnel we saw. 

We decided not to. We wanted to find something to prove we had been there.

We heard something, a faint echo and we all stopped dead in our tracks.

The Groundskeeper turned her lantern's brightness higher and asked what that was. Wire Cutter asked if we thought it was George Wiseman come to get us.

He looked scared, panicked even.

At that moment I laughed. Maybe it was my nerves or hearing my friend of several years so earnestly ask if a ghost was really there but I laughed.

I had never asked either of them if they actually believed in the old ghost story.

I did so now and Wire Cutter gave a defensive reply that he did believe. He said weird, unexplainable things happen everyday.

The Groundskeeper was silent. She stuttered when she finally spoke and said she hadn't made up her mind.

My laughter seemed to have defused the tension and we continued to walk further down. Continuing our conversation to distract ourselves from the nerves.

I don't know how long we walked for but eventually the main shaft ended in a cave in. 

I think this was when we knew we had gone too far. 

We turned around to leave without much discussion and Wire Cutter tripped.

He hit the ground with a large thud. 

There was a rumbling sound and a sharp crack as the stone beneath him fell away. 

The floor beneath us all opened up and we hit the ground below hard. 

The lantern broke. 

We were in the dark.

I called out and to my relief the others replied. We had luckily survived without any major injury. 

Thankfully Wire Cutter had a spare flashlight. After a few minutes scrambling about in the dark for his bag we could see again.

We had fallen about 10 feet and didn't see a way to climb back up.

We found ourselves in what seemed to be a deeper part of the main shaft. It continued to our left and right. We couldn't tell which way went up.

We went left as Wire Cutter announced left is always right and confidently strode in that direction. 

He had the flashlight so we had to follow. 

We walked for awhile, applied what little first aid we had and found ourselves at the ledge to a pit.

It went down for what looked like forever.  Further than the flashlights beam could show at least.

The Groundskeeper threw a rock down.

We all leaned in, listening closely. We didn't hear it hit the bottom. But we all did pause.

We could hear something else. A repetitive sound like something hitting against the rock down below. 

Wire Cutter called out a single word, his voice echoed down the bottomless pit.

\>"Hello?"

We listened again. 

The sound of his voice faded. The sound below stopped. 

We turned around and walked the other way. 

As we walked we occasionally came across intersections of 4 paths. We would check to see which way seemed to be leading upwards and otherwise hoped we picked the best route. 

Wire cutter, who was in front of us all, stopped suddenly. He asked if we heard anything. 

I said no.

The Groundskeeper said no. 

We listened.

We heard nothing.

We continued on slowly. We came across a sharp turn in the shaft and Wire Cutter turned ahead of us.

He froze. We caught up a second later and saw why.

The torch light illuminated a figure standing just beyond the turn in the shaft. 

He was pale. He held a pickaxe in his hands and long mangled hair covered his face.

He took a step forward and swung his pickaxe at Wire Cutter's head. He didn't even try to move out of the way.

I saw the pickaxe come out of the back of his skull.

I think The Groundskeeper screamed. Maybe it was me screaming, I don't know. The few seconds after felt like 10 hours. 

Then the torch fell from Wire Cutter's hands and the light flickered.

His body fell limp to the ground. 

The figure reached down and grabbed Wire Cutter's leg. 

The torch light flickered and finally turned off. 

We were in the dark again. I could only hear the sounds of breathing. My own, The Groundskeeper and what I now believe to be George Wiseman.

His breathing sounds laboured and each breath was accompanied by a wheeze.

He spoke in a low, raspy voice.

\>"Leave."

His command hung in the air. I could hear the sound of something being dragged across the floor and heavy footsteps.

I ran in what I belived to be the opposite direction, back the way we came. I held my hands out in front of my to try avoid running into walls head first.

I could hear The Groundskeeper running behind me as we tried to find an exit in the dark. 

We must have ran for hours.

We dared not say a word for most of the run. Only The Groundskeeper spoke once towards the end.

\>"Skinny, I can see light over hear!"

I scrambled towards the sound of her voice and I saw it. I ran faster still and soon we were crawling and soon we were bursting free from the mine. 

We were safe. 

\*We\* were safe. Me and The Groundskeeper.

We stood there, hands on our knees, hunched over catching our breath. It was sunset. I made it out alive. We made it out alive. 

My friend was dead.

I turned to look at The Groundskeeper. I didn't know what to say, so i said nothing. 

\>"His name is Hunter, mine is Thea"

She walked away. I followed her. I still didn't say a word.

I haven't since. I don't know what the right words to say are. My boss called on monday and my voice got caught in my throat evertime I tried to speak.

I texted him that I had a throat infection.

I'm posting this here as a warning, don't go looking for George Wiseman's Gold Mine.
