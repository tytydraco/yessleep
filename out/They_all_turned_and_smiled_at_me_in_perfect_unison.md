I don’t know if I should even be posting this, but my mind has been running in circles for hours. Something happened earlier this evening that didn’t feel normal, and I feel like I’m losing my grip on reality just thinking about it.

I was at a small, independently-owned grocery store near my place. It was just a normal evening run. I only needed to grab a few basic things before heading home and calling it a night. The store is usually pretty quiet, bathed in that harsh, flickering fluorescent light. There were maybe six or seven other people inside, including the staff. Just a completely mundane atmosphere.

I remember walking down the dry goods aisle, looking at the pasta, when it happened.

All at once, everyone stopped.

It wasn't a gradual lull in activity. It wasn't people pausing to look at their phones.

At the exact same fraction of a second, every single person in my line of sight completely froze.

A woman walking past the end of my aisle stopped mid-step, her foot literally hovering an inch above the linoleum tile. A guy a few feet away from me holding a basket froze with his arm extended, his fingers inches away from a jar of sauce. Even the cashier up front, who I could see through the aisles, stopped scanning.

And it wasn't just the movement. The sound died too. The low hum of the refrigerators, the soft music playing on the overhead speakers—it all just vanished. A thick, heavy silence dropped over the entire store.

It only lasted about a second. Maybe two. But it was enough for my stomach to completely drop.

Then… they all turned.

In perfect, mechanical unison. I heard the synchronized squeak of their rubber soles pivoting on the floor.

And they looked directly at me.

It wasn't a casual glance. It wasn't normal, curious eye contact. Every single one of them had their bodies angled toward me, their heads tilted at the exact same unnatural degree.

Completely still. No expressions. Just… dead-eyed, unblinking, watching me.

I felt this immediate, crushing pressure in my chest. All the air was sucked out of my lungs. Every survival instinct I had was screaming at me that something was deeply, fundamentally wrong in a way I couldn’t logically explain.

I didn’t move. I was paralyzed. I just stood there, gripping my basket so hard my knuckles were white, while all of them kept staring.

Then one of them smiled.

It was a man near the end of the aisle. But his smile didn’t look normal. It was entirely in his mouth, not reaching his eyes. It stretched too wide across his face, the skin pulling taut, like he was forcing muscles to move that he didn't quite know how to use.

Then the others started smiling too.

One by one. The woman with the hovering foot. The guy reaching for the sauce. The cashier in the distance.

Until all of them were standing there, completely frozen, smiling at me.

No one blinked. No one spoke. Just those same empty, predatory smiles.

My brain finally snapped out of it, and I took a step back without thinking. My shoe scuffed loudly against the floor.

And the very split-second I did—

They all stopped smiling. All at once. They turned away and just… continued what they were doing.

The woman’s foot hit the floor. The man grabbed his jar of sauce. The hum of the refrigerators and the background music snapped back into existence.

Like nothing had happened. Like I wasn’t even there. Like the last terrifying thirty seconds had just been wiped from reality.

I stood there for a few seconds, my heart hammering against my ribs, trying to remember how to breathe.

I didn't care about the rest of my list. I just wanted to leave. I walked straight to the counter, keeping my head down, terrified to look at anyone else.

When I got to the cashier, he didn’t look at me. He didn’t offer a greeting. He just scanned the items in total silence.

His hands moved normally… but his head stayed slightly tilted in my direction. He wasn't looking at me with his eyes, but I could feel his awareness locked onto me, like a radar tracking my movements.

I threw cash on the counter, didn't wait for my change, and practically sprinted for the exit.

But right before the automatic doors slid open, I couldn't help myself. I glanced back.

Everyone in the store had stopped moving again.

And this time… they weren’t smiling.

They were all just standing there in the aisles, perfectly still.

Watching the door.

Waiting.

Like they knew I was about to leave.

I haven’t gone back since. I've been sitting in my locked apartment with the blinds drawn, jumping at every sound in the hallway.

Because the part that bothers me the most isn’t what they did. It isn't the eerie synchronization or those horrible smiles.

It’s the overwhelming, suffocating feeling I had when they were staring at me.

It didn’t feel like I had walked into a normal grocery store.

It felt like I walked into something… that was already waiting for me
