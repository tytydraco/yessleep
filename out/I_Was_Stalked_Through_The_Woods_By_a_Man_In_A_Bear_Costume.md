This happened to me on a walk I took about three weeks ago. It was a snowy Saturday morning, a blanket of fresh snow covered the ground, and, as usual, I decided to take a short hike through the woods. This is something that I often do, as I find that it helps calm me down. There’s just something about being out alone in the forest, with just you and your thoughts; it’s something I still do, although it’s not as enjoyable anymore. I thought this would be like just any other walk through the woods, little did I know what was in there.   
  
Now, something that I should mention is that this was in the middle of bear country. We don’t have large grizzlies where I live, but there are plenty of black bears. Because of this, I was pretty alert and made sure to make some noise. Black bears aren’t usually very aggressive and usually run away from humans, but you won’t want to startle them. As I walked, I began to notice the large number of footprints in the snow, not animal footprints but human footprints. It was not uncommon for other people to walk this trail, but not many people hiked at this time of morning. The footprints hardly stayed on the trail; they would wind in and out of the woods, loop around and cross over each other as if someone had been aimlessly wandering back and forth. I thought nothing of this at first and just continued walking.

On the trail, there is this steep part that descends into a gorge. As I walked through, I could hear the packy snow crunching under my feet, *crunch, crunch, crunch*. After a while, I decided that I would stop for a rest, but as soon as I did, I heard another sound that told me that I wasn’t as alone as I thought. Coming from off the trail was the sound of someone else's footprints going *crunch, crunch, crunch*. I listened. They were coming towards me, I could tell it was a person because the footsteps sounded even, whereas an animal's footsteps would have been more frantic. I listened for a good minute as the footsteps got louder and louder and louder and then stopped. There was a minute of silence before I called out.  
“Hello?”  
The stranger didn’t answer, but I could hear him burst up the slopes of the gorge towards me, his footprints crunching rapidly through the snow. This freaked me out, and my fight or flight instinct must have kicked in because the next thing I knew, I was running right back up the trail I came from. I dared not look back as it sounded like they were right behind me. A guttural roar erupted from behind me as I was chased right back up the trail. The chase must have lasted around 5 minutes, but it felt like hours. eventually I realized that the sound of footsteps was gone, and I breathed a large sigh of relief. Unfortunately, that wasn’t the end of it.  
  
I was making my way back to my house when I looked up ahead and saw something that made my blood run cold. About ten meters ahead of me waited a man, in a bear suit, holding an axe. The Man blocked off the path, looking me dead in the eye. It was as if he knew that I would have to get past him. The man walked back and forth, swinging his fire axe around casually before resting it on his shoulder. I looked around, assessing my options. I could turn around and go back down the trail away from my house and come back once that freak had left, but who’s to say he won’t just wait there forever, or follow me? I could turn off the trail and try to find a different way home, but that would mean tramping around in the woods for a couple of hours. I gulped as I realized my only option was to try to sneak past the bear man. I slowly wandered towards the man, watching as the man stared me down, not moving. My whole body tensed up as I got closer to him. Suddenly, the man swung the fire axe towards me, just missing by an inch. I bolted away from him. The bear-man, sensing that I was getting away, grabbed my ankle. I yelped and kicked the man’s hand, causing him to lose grip. In a second, I was back onto my feet and out of there.  
  
The next day, I was awoken in the middle of the night. I’m not sure what woke me up, but something definitely felt off. I lay still in my bed staring up at the ceiling when I heard it. A low guttural growl coming from outside. I turned my head, looking up towards the window and saw the bear man, staring at me from outside, the fire axe in his hand. I screamed in shock and backed up from the bed. The man raised his axe and burst through the window.  A long arm stretched through the broken window and grabbed me by the shirt, pulling me towards him. The bear mask made the man look like he was grinning with sadistic pleasure. I grabbed a broken piece of glass lying on the bed and jammed its sharp edge into the bear-man’s arm. I heard a yelp of pain from under the bear mask as his arm recoiled. I jumped out of bed, found my phone, hid in a corner and dialled 911. When the Police arrived, they found axe marks all over the house, but the bear-man had vanished. He must have followed me when I ran home and waited for me to fall asleep. The police were able to follow his footprints through the woods, which brought them to a tent, where he must have been living. Inside the tent were tools, old food wrappers, and the decaying, mutilated remains of dogs and cats. Disturbingly, many of the remains appeared to have been consumed, suggesting the bear man had been feeding mostly on stray animals. The thing they did not find was the bear man. The trail of footprints ended at the bear man’s camp and didn’t go any farther. For all I know, he is still out there. Be careful.
