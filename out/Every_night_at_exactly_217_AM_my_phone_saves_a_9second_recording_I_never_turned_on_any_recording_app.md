Three nights ago I woke up to a notification on my phone that I’ve never seen before.

It said:

“Audio memory saved.”

At first I thought it was part of some random update. Phones add weird features all the time and half the time I don’t notice them.

But the notification looked strange. It didn’t come from any app I recognized.

When I tapped it, it opened a single audio file.

The timestamp said 2:17 AM.

The file length was 9 seconds.

I pressed play.

For the first four or five seconds there was nothing except a low static sound, like a microphone picking up an empty room.

Then I heard something shift close to the phone.

Not loud. Just the sound of fabric moving. Like someone leaning over a table.

After that, a voice whispered something so quietly I had to replay it multiple times to even understand it.

The voice said:

“Not tonight.”

Then the recording ended.

I live alone.

My apartment is small — one bedroom, one bathroom, kitchen and living room. If someone had been in the room with me I would have woken up.

My phone was on the bedside table charging the whole night.

At first I assumed I must have triggered something accidentally while half asleep. Maybe Siri or voice recording or something.

So I ignored it.

The next morning I checked my phone settings just in case.

There’s no recording app installed except the default voice memo app, and it definitely doesn’t start recording automatically. There’s nothing scheduled, no automation, nothing running in the background.

It felt weird, but I forgot about it after a few hours.

Then it happened again the next night.

Same notification.

Audio memory saved.

Another file.

Again recorded at 2:17 AM.

Again exactly 9 seconds long.

This time the recording sounded different.

At first there was that same faint static.

Then I heard something that sounded like light tapping.

Three taps.

Slow and spaced apart.

Like someone knocking gently on wood.

Then the whisper came back.

Same voice.

But this time it said something else.

It said:

“Still locked.”

I checked every recording setting on my phone after that.

Nothing explains this.

No background apps. No automation shortcuts. No sleep tracking app with audio recording. Nothing.

Tonight I decided to stay awake and see what would happen.

I sat in bed with the lights off and my phone in my hand watching the time.

At 2:16 AM nothing was happening.

Just the clock on the screen.

When the time changed to 2:17, the screen flickered for half a second.

Then the notification appeared right in front of me.

Audio memory saved.

A brand new 9-second recording.

Recorded while I was literally holding the phone.

I haven’t played it yet.

Because about five seconds after the notification appeared…

I heard the exact same three taps.

Not from the phone.

From somewhere inside my apartment.

And the worst part is…

The tapping didn’t come from the door.

It came from the wall right behind my bed.
