It's been a long time since I've talked about it. I haven’t really thought to tell anyone. There are all kinds of evil in this world, you know. There's evil at every corner you don't see. I've seen evil. The purest display of evil you'll hear that isn't covered up by some conspiracy. As long as I'm alive, truth will no longer distract from the point.

It's been years now, twenty years, I'd say it's been. I'm older. Less caring. I've no desire to live with this any longer. So if you're willing, I'd like to tell you all the story of my very first job. A job I soon grew to resent. A job, I thought I'd forgotten.

February 2nd, 2005

I remember this day vividly. I was twenty at the time, and I'd just landed my first job at this local market store that had recently opened back up for business. I applied to be a cashier, and in no time I went from coming in for a short interview to, not even ten minutes later, bagging groceries for paying patrons.

Getting your first job can make or break your work life experience. For me, I realized very quickly that customer service wasn't my cup of tea. The only thing that kept me from verbally assaulting someone was the image of the game I hoped to buy with my paycheck. 

I got to a point where I couldn't handle the immediate overload of stress. I remember practically stumbling my way to the bathroom.

"You alright?" I heard someone say as I was wiping my mouth of puke.

That someone I heard was a friend of mine who at the time was my boss. The store was divided into three sections. The front end, the backstage, and the showstage. I'm not kidding; that was how the manager referred to each part of the store. Basically each section was led by a team lead, and this guy happened to be mine.

"Sorry, I just arrived earlier. "You're the new hire, I assume?" he asked.

"-grk- Yeah..." I said, lifting myself to my feet.

"Well, I can't say I blame the shameful display of utter hopelessness... but it's like... your first day, man," he said, handing me a paper towel.

"I'm Zach, by the way. Your boss."

The man looked like he'd never seen a shower in his life. Though I couldn't tell because of the smell of cigarette smoke. His hair was long and disheveled. He damn near looked homeless. He even had his name tag display his name as BallinCough. Even if that were his last name, what kind of last name is that?

Zach talked with me for a bit; surprisingly, I calmed down afterwards, so I guess the talk was beneficial. Afterwards he brought me back to my registrar, and I continued working. It was an overwhelming first day, but one I wish I could go back to. Simpler days, simpler times.

May 21st, 2005

Four months went by with barely any problems. Customers still annoyed me, but having Zach there helped me get through it. We talked about all kinds of random things. He'd been slowly introducing me to areas around the store. Almost giving me a personal tour of the place. Believe it or not, but for such a small-looking place on the outside, there were a lot of Backrooms-like corridors. 

One room Zach had shown me before this was one filled with lost toys and various other items. Zach called it the room of "Lost and Never Found" because, for all we knew, all these items had just been forgotten.

"Are you looking for something?" Zach asked while I was searching every wall in the breakroom.

"No. Just. Just a socket or plug-in or whatever. I need to charge my phone," I said, holding my dinky old flip phone.

"Well, instead of wasting time, just follow me. We should still have some spare batteries. Swap one battery for another, and suddenly it's good as new." Zach said, leading me down a long hallway I'd never been to before.

The further we went, the more the walls around me began to show age. Cracks and mold were everywhere. Eventually we made it to a small room where Zach gestured for me to hand over my phone. The room was small but filled with desks and random boxes. Hanging on the door handle was, strangely, a really old belt.

"So what do you use your phone for?" he asked.

"What... like, I don't know man. Calling, music maybe. What do you do with your phone?" I asked snarkily.

"I take pictures of my feet and send them to random numbers." The instant response nearly gave me whiplash.

"I'm going to need you to repeat that. Maybe in Spanish too," I said as he handed me my phone back.

"No, anyway, want to see something cool?" He said excitedly as he pulled out from under a desk what looked like a large speaker.

"This old baby is hooked up to every speaker in this place. You connect an aux cord to your device, and you can listen to whatever you want on the store radio. Want to try it out?" He asked.

While reluctant at first, I didn't begin quickly scrolling through my phone for all of my downloaded songs. I even knew exactly what song I wanted to play. Zach started saying something to me, but I tuned him out completely as I plugged in my phone and pressed play on the song.

From the hallway I could hear the song I chose...

"Feel good

Sha, sha-ba-da, sha-ba-da (feel good)

Sha, sha-ba-da, sha-ba-da (feel good)

Sha, sha-ba-da, sha-ba-da (feel good)

Sha, sha-ba-da, sha-ba-da (feel good)"

I sat on one of the boxes. Tuning out the world and just listening.

"Sha, sha-ba-da, sha-ba-da (feel good)

Sha, sha-ba-da, sha-ba-da (feel good, change, change, change, change)

Sha, sha-ba-da, sha-ba-da (feel good, change, change, change, change)

Sha, sha-ba-da, sha-ba-da (feel good)"

Before I could drift fully into bliss. I looked up from my phone to realize that I was now completely alone. Zach had left the room.

"City's breaking down on a camel's back

They just have to go 'cause they don't know wack"

I looked out of the room to the long hallway. Staring up and down, yet I couldn't find him. I slowly walked out of the room, walking in an unknown direction. You know the feeling of being alone but not alone. That's how I felt in that hallway.

"So, while you fill the streets, it's appealing to see

You won't get undercounted 'cause you're damned and free"

I came across a branching hallway. It was dark, too dark. I could swear I heard noises. Muffled cries or maybe something else. I don't know what compelled me, but I went down that branching path.

"You got a new horizon, it's ephemeral style

A melancholy town where we never smile"

Wherever I was, it showed its age well. Graffiti art lined the walls. Each piece of art got more and more disturbing the further I went. The hallway led to a dead end. At least that's what I thought at first. I felt around until I found what felt like a doorknob. I didn't think twice before turning the handle.

"And all I wanna hear is the message beep.

My dreams, they got her kissing 'cause I don't get sle-"

The music suddenly stopped. I felt cold. I knew something was behind that door. I could feel it. I could sense it. I couldn't see it.

"Holy shit, where the hell have you been, dammit!" I heard from behind me.

"What's this thing?" I asked, expecting my friend to give me the honest truth.

"What's what? You mean the floor mopper robot thing," Zach said with a chuckle. "It mops the floors so we don't have to."

What I was looking at didn't strike me as "for cleaning." 

"I heard crying coming from here," I said.

"Oh, you too. Damn, I hoped I was schizophrenic." Zach chuckled again.

"You're a piece of shit, you know that." Zach smiled, likely feeling accomplished.

"Oh yeah. I grabbed your phone for you. It's about time we head back." Zach handed me my phone, and we walked straight out of there.

In all my years I've never felt the same way in that moment before or since. Turns out Zach has said to me that he was going to go to the bathroom. Unfortunately, I had already tuned him out. We walked all of the graffiti again. One I had missed the first time stuck out to me. It was a simple small art piece. It depicted a figure that looked vaguely familiar. The figure was depicted surrounded in darkness, and next to it was the word "Damnatio Memoriae."

August 17th, 2005

Months again went by with nothing of note. I started to think I really was just hearing things. Coming up with every scenario imaginable in my head to explain what happened. Maybe there was a gas leak? I'm not sure.

This day I don't remember like the other two. I remember it being late in the evening. My shift was almost coming to a close. Zach and I sat in the breakroom talking our usual nonsense as I watched the clock's time tick by.

"You like ghost stories?" Zach asked out of the blue.

"Depends. Is it actually scary?" I asked.

"Maybe."

Zach stood up. He walked to the light switch, flipping it off while seemingly summoning a flashlight from nowhere. He sat back down and began his ghost story.

"This place had been abandoned for twenty years until two years ago. In those twenty years a multitude of stories came like a flood. Everyone and their grandmother has a story to tell about this place. One of them was a dear friend of mine. She went missing four years ago. The last place she was seen was at the entrance of this store. She'd been inside this place before. I think she watched Blair Witch one too many times. She wanted to make a movie just like it using this building. So she put together a small team of partially willing participants and began to explore. If it wasn't hard to tell, this place is well over sixty years old. Kelvin can make as many improvements as he wants, but nothing can erase the passing of time. My friend told me before her disappearance that they found something during their exploration. Something that, as far as I know, is still here." Zach took a sip from his soda and carried on.

"They entered, and from what my friend said, this place wasn't immediately inviting. A ceiling panel nearly caved in her skull. They scoured the entire front of the store as well as every aisle. Well, I say that, but back then, this place wasn't a store."

"What was it?" I asked.

"The room we go to to play music over the radio. The reason there was a whip in there was because this place was once secretly a KKK private church. They'd bring in those they deemed not human and beat them. Whipped them. Dehumanized them. The graffiti on the walls you saw was from children they had here. That's the secret my friend found. It's the horror she had to bear."

"Not much of a ghost story," I said. Zach just looked at me.

"The day my friend went missing. She claimed to be hearing things. Voices and sounds. She called me one night screaming that she could hear something whispering in her closet. I came by the next morning, and I found that she had boarded up her closet door. Saying that even then she could still hear it. Later that day she was last seen walking into this building alone. She hasn't been seen since. I've searched almost every room in this place, yet all I've found is nothing."

Zach's friend was named Kayla Veero; she went missing July 6th, 2001. 

Her body was found January 18th, 2006.

I was the one who found her.
