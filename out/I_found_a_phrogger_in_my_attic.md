This is going to sound insane, let me say that before anything else. I barely believe it's real, and I'm living it.

It all started with this new stupid job. I got an offer to help run an archive in a city a few states over, I was ecstatic! Hell, in this economy? I'll take anything, especially in a city with a lower living cost than goddamn San Fran? Yeah, sign me up. Anyways, I moved a few states over to some middle of nowhere town. I found this nice house, small, cheap, and being rented out for a fraction of where I just moved from.

The landlord only asked I had no pets, I assured I didn't, a lie. Technically, I did, an ant farm. I didn't think it was a big deal, so i took my colony with me. It was warm and sunny the first afternoon I was unpacking my shit. I sat my antfarm on a windowsill, it was one of those cheap, crappy, gel ones. The light illuminated through the transparent blue, and I remember being almost awestruck at how pretty it was. I must've stood there for about 15 minutes just...staring til I heard some noise. I brushed the old creak off as the house settling. I kept unpacking afterwards and shoved the rest of my boxes I wasn't ready to touch into the hallway closet.

I was so wrong, man.

About a week or two after moving in, I noticed some things I left packed up in boxes would be unpacked and moved out of the way once I got home from work. I brushed it off at first, really, I thought I just forgot doing it. It was a stressful move, and I wasn't fully adjusted. I brushed the noises off the same way, somehow. I remember, one night, I woke up around 4 am. I had to piss, you can't blame a man for that, and I started to sit up in bed until I heard it. It was this stupid, fucking breathing noise that I just convinced myself it was the vents! Vents don't have distinct inhales and exhales, they don't rattle like they're breathing out so hard they run outta air, and they sure as hell don't blow hot air on my face in the middle of summer. Not the way I've got the AC cranked. I told myself it was just vents and stress, no big deal, and laid back down. I didn't get up until 7 am, but I laid there awake listening all night. When I woke up the hallway closet was open. That nagged at me wrong, especially when I noticed a small little hatch(?) in the closet. A hole in the top part with a piece of painted plywood as a cover that you could push up to enter. I didn't touch it. I had work to do.

I never touched it after. Didn't wanna know what I'd find, I guess.

I guess in that moment was when I started to get worried. After that night I bought a stun-gun and got a little neurotic about locking my windows, and for a while it stopped. No unpacking happening without me home, no breathing in my ear, and no weird dread in my chest. I let weeks pass without noticing how quickly I had to buy groceries. I'm a single man, I live alone, and yet I was buying shit like a house of 5. I was so focused on work I didn't question it. The archive wasn't a very fucking good one, it was full of historical books all beaten and battered up, no proper fucking catalog of them or summaries, so for the past weeks I'd been (and still as of writing this) focusing on classifying based on age and genre with little help from the other bastard I worked with.

By the time it happened it was nearing the end of summer. I had stayed late, reading this one historical book I couldn't find any logs for. It was folklore, but man, it was weird. I had stopped on this passage about some myth from the local area. I was preoccupied when I got home, probably quieter than usual, I get slow when moving. Well, I was a whole lot less distracted when I looked up in the entryway.

There was a tall, skinny man(?) perched on the fucking edge of my windowsill, the lid off my antfarm off. His fingers were half my fucking forearms, man, and he dipped them into the gel like it was a fuckin' instinct. I guess it's weird to some people to care so much about bugs, but, man I felt tears in my eyes just watching. It was probably also outta fear even if I don't wanna say that shit. It dipped it's long fucked up fingers into the gel and brought it up to its face. I couldn't see it, it was facing away from me, but I recognized that rattle in it's breathing.

I'm not necessarily a scary guy, but I'm decently build, and yet this thing almost dwarfed me when it stood up. It picked up my hand farm and I watched this long almost sticky fucking.. tongue i guess, lick the rest of my poor ants out of the glass before it put it down crookedly on the container. It wasn't my favorite time to realize that stun gun was still in my room, so I just stayed quiet and didn't fucking move. I watched it amble awkwardly to the little hatch in the closet hallway leading to the crawlspace and disappear into the darkness. I stayed at a hotel and called in sick the next day. I didn't wanna call the cops just incase I turn out to be just... stressed. I stayed at the hotel for a week.

I just got back home, finally decided to check it all out. I peeked inside before jogging back out to my shitty truck to type this before I risk it all. I'm honestly trying to procrastinate it all, when I looked in there, the closet was open, and everything in there smelt like copper and dirt. It looked like there was hay, or something, all over the floor. It's probably just a raccoon, or a phrogger (just found out about this recently,  who the hell does this for fun?) but... a part of me can't shake a feeling that something's watching me from the crack in the front door right now.

Wish me luck.
