Eighteen dollars didn’t feel worth the dust in my throat and two day ride. Horse was worth more than that. Probably knew it too. Damn bastard who owed money to a ranch got up and left with the advance pay. This whole chase feels pointless - three years ago I would be going after bounties known nationwide, but now? It's just fools who don't pay debt.   
I’ve set camp in places worse than this before. The ground was flat enough, the wind was blocked by an overhang, and the horse settled easily. The tent goes up the same way every time, and my hands seem to know how to do it better than my mind can keep up with. Enough dry branches were in the area to keep the fire going for days, but it initially seemed to struggle to catch. The sound of the fire crackling is of peace to me. It provides warmth. Safety. The smell of cooking meat. Not that I had any. Checking my bag only left me with a few half eaten crackers, a can of beans and a discoloured apple for the horse. Greedy beast usually tries to steal a bite of mine, but she wouldn’t even look at her apple for a good while. Stared off looking at the sunset. Or something like that. I took a bite myself and she moved, finally, but not like she normally does. She took the apple slowly, like she had forgotten it was there. 

The food was dry and bland. Nothing more I can expect really whilst out here, but there are worse things I could eat. The fire was loud tonight - not because it was any bigger than usual but because of no other competing sounds. Humming bugs usually fill the silence but they weren’t around tonight. Just means I’ll get bitten less. I finished packing up the bag. Sun was lower in the sky now, and the dust in the air looked heavier than it should’ve been. Thought I saw something out past the ridge when heading back to the camp. Just a shape. Could’ve been a rock, could’ve been nothing.

The horse still had its head set on the distance. Damn thing didn’t seem to be doing alright, I’ll have to check her. I gave her a brush and tightened the rope securing her and then saw it again, tall and too thin to hold its form. I thought it was further left. It was to the right before. Or I’m wrong. After a few seconds I looked back at the out of place figure. It hadn’t moved. I went back to the horse but the figure wouldn't leave my mind. I found myself taking longer than usual to secure her in place.

Sleep usually greets me quickly, but tonight it seemed to take longer. Twenty minutes of nothing but rocks in my back and troubled thoughts. It nearly came until the horse got unsettled and started thrashing around. I got out of the tent and went to settle her. At first I didn’t notice it. The horse did. It was glaring at this spot behind me, refusing to look away. It was there again. Black as ever even against the night sky. It looked sort of like a rock once I stared long enough. It also looked different to how I remembered it before. The horse got anxious and hit me in the back, breaking my line of sight on it. I whipped back around and saw it again. Well, I saw something. The figure seemed to have changed. The thin mid section now appeared taller. It grew features that even from afar I knew weren’t naturally forming - not a face, not exactly, but something that implied a face the way a hole implies the ground beneath it. The horse felt my unease as I tried to calm it further. I couldn’t stand being outside any longer and started to reach for the tent again. I stopped. I could taste the salt from sweat which had rolled down my head. Not long after I’d ripped the tent open and clambered back inside.

Silence kept me awake. The horse was not okay; it kept snorting and blowing and galloping. I couldn’t help her. I couldn’t. I had to. If she was harmed then I would have no way out. I clenched my revolver and burst out. 

Nothing.

The figure was gone. The stars could be seen again where its black form had blocked them before. The noise of the night returned - crickets, foxes, wind. I turned to the horse to attempt to comfort her. Her eyes were bloodshot and hoof marks patterned the ground. I turned to secure the rope again but couldn’t.

There it was. Atop the overhang. Impossibly tall. Same wrong thinness. Only now it didn't blend in with anything. It blocked the stars behind - but it blocked them wrong. I kept looking. Thought that maybe it’d settle into something familiar again. Rock. Shadow. Anything. 

It didn’t.

The longer I stared the less it matched the ground it stood on. In fact, it no longer even stood on the ground. I ran. Didn’t turn to check. Didn’t want to see it somewhere else when I looked back. Didn’t want to know what would happen if I did look back.
