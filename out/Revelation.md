You may be familiar with the quote from a famous poet: “This is the way the world ends, not with a bang but a whimper”

Well I am here to say that is half right. The world did not end with a bang… but with a scream.

When the first one appeared everything went silent. One moment the skies were clear, the next a bright mass of light swarming with eyes, folding in on itself forming incomprehensible shapes, hung above the crowded Times Square. Everyone stood in awe of the entity that suddenly manifested before them. Then the screaming started.

Suddenly beams of light shot out from various eyes, colors of every spectrum illuminating the already bright summer skyline, and began to strike civilians in rapid succession. All that was left was a pile of ash where they stood. People ran over one another to escape this grisly fate, but it was of no use. It didn’t matter where you were; on the streets, in your home, on the subway deep underground—nowhere was safe. Soon the skies were filled with the soot of the fallen.

For seven days, more of these unstoppable beasts arrived. They policed the globe day and night searching for their victims. Towards the end you could still occasionally see flashes of multicolored light in the distance as another innocent was struck down. Then finally—on the seventh day—they left, seemingly satisfied with their work.

It didn’t take the survivors long to piece together what had happened…

The Rapture.

The angels had finally come to take the righteous to their kingdom in heaven. At least that’s how the pastors, left behind, had worded it. For others it was aliens, with some prime directive to exterminate half of all life. And still some believed it to be a government conspiracy. Truthfully, no one had an answer. But what we do have is the aftermath.

Disenfranchised Televangelists who took to the bottle like a lost lover, Corporate CEOs trying to scheme a way to make a profit on the tragedy, grieving mothers whose cribs were filled with the ashes of newborns, angry fathers who screamed relentlessly to a deaf God, and scientists who spent decades trying to peer into Pandora’s box only to be faced with an answer that left them dumbfounded. These were the ones left behind to lament the losses we had suffered.

Some tried to make peace, and spread compassion to their fellow man. Others took this as an opportunity to unleash their inner demons with little fear of consequence. However, a vast number of people chose another path altogether.

Mass suicides swept the world, with people jumping from buildings, finding the nearest firearm, or simply giving up and withering away in a corner. Two weeks later, you couldn’t walk the streets without needing to step over the bodies of neighbors, friends, or family. Bodies left to decompose next to the buildings covered with the grey stains of their loved ones.

I am writing this in hopes to pass along the knowledge of this dark time to whatever poor souls continue to thrive in this wasteland we used to call home. And as a warning to those that have forgotten, or are too young to even know. If you see that light appear in the sky, don’t run—it’s useless. But do scream, so hopefully the end won’t be silent.
