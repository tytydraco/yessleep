Hello everyone! Weird question, but do you, any of you, daydream? If so, then I'd love to know what you daydream about. Though maybe you'll want to stop once I tell you all about something I experience when I daydream.

We all know what daydreaming is like. We've all stared into space like the lights are on but nobody's home. One could call it a brain glitch. Others could say it's other worlds tapping into our brains while we're awake. We've all seen the typical depiction of a daydream in media. Suddenly, in a matter of seconds, you go from one scene of a film to a whole "what if" scenario. What if?

Most, when they daydream, think about the past or the future. Skewing the narrative how you see fit. Let's say you daydream about an embarrassing event in your youth. You remember how you felt, how the whole ordeal had affected you. Though you only remember your point of view. That's the terror of daydreams; you can easily miss what you chose to ignore.

It was a very boring class. Math has never been my favorite subject. So I did what anyone would do in any situation that required attention. I tuned it all out. I had an earbud in my ear, and the last thing I wanted to think about was the teacher babbling about equations I'll never see again.

I began to drift. Suddenly the world looked less cold. Everything was as bright and colorful as my imagination could manage to perceive it. I was in true bliss. The weight of the world felt like it never mattered to begin with. Momentary bliss at its finest.

My classroom that was once filled with tired classmates was now a bustling and fun learning environment. It was my perfect world. Then I saw my teacher. He looked out of place. Like he'd never belonged despite this being a classroom. My imaginative version of the classroom, sure, but even in my imagination a teacher should be included. What's the point of going to school if not to learn and if the teacher is out of place in it? Why be there? Why care? 

I started to wonder if some people aren't meant to be teachers. I don't believe in nonsense self-aura stuff, but then again, this could be proof. The question was, of course, why he seemed so out of place. Why is he here if he doesn't belong?

I spiraled in that moment. Dumb question after dumb thought. I thought of stuff that didn’t make sense to justify why he didn't belong.

The human brain is a fascinating thing. It can do things even we still can't explain. 

My teacher looked at me. Not in a curious way or in an angry way. He didn't have the mannerism of a teacher who's caught their student spacing out. He had the eyes of a predator. He smiled. He knew I saw him. He knew I saw what he was. I just couldn't figure it out at the time.

"Kaitlyn? Can you see me?" He asked as he stood up from his chair.

I didn't answer. I couldn't. I had no idea if what I was seeing was real. His voice sounded clear, too clear. Like the excessive noise of the world had finally subsided to where my brain could focus on him.

"Kaitlyn?"

"Kaaaaaaaaitlyyyyyyyyyn?"

"Why do you ignore what you see, Kaitlyn?"

"Is the truth too much for your head?"

His words were like a bad salad. Too much and too little emphasis on any word. No emotion carried his words. Emptiness is the best way I could describe it.

"We... See... You... To..."

I snapped out of my daydream. Everyone around me was staring at me. I almost thought I hadn't escaped it.

"Kaitlyn! "My teacher shouted. "Come on, can you tell me the square root of 24?"

I didn't know the answer.

"Sorry, I don't remember," I said.

"Really, Kaitlyn, is this really too much for your head?"

I haven't dared daydream since. I've even tried to prevent myself from dreaming when I sleep.

It's... Just too much... It's too much for my head to bear... My head hurts...

Thank you. I hope my story helps. With what you may wonder. We'll that remains to be seen. 

Will it all be to much for your head as well?
