The incident happened a long time ago, back when I was still just a kid attending primary school, no older than 8 or 9 years old perhaps.



I want to clarify that these strange occurrences may just be happenstance, I don’t know. Memory from a decade ago are prone to be hazy, I could just be misremembering things, or my brain just fabricated most of it. Back then I didn’t think about it too hard, still too young and stupid, or too scared to.



I am hoping that by sharing my experience with you, I might alleviate some of that feeling, isolation might be apt? Something of that sort. Again if the details don’t seem to make sense, I apologise. I am merely recounting a time of bygone days.



I lived somewhere in Hong Kong, in a private estate, not going to give details about my location, just know that if you aren’t familiar with Hong Kong, it is a pretty urban place full of people. I lived in an apartment with my mom and grandma on the fifth floor.



The school I attended to was near me, literally just the road across my building, from my bedroom window I can see the school playground, that level of close. So naturally my parent didn’t escort me to school sometime after we got used to the routine, I was then alone during the period from home to school and back.



Everyday I would wake up, get dressed up by my grandma, took the elevator and walked to school. A life so simple I couldn’t remember most of it.



There comes a day in recess, where classmates grouped together to share with each other ghost stories, a popular kids thing in Asia. I was not a kid who believes in ghosts in general, no religious upbringing and such, being told Santa isn’t real was the final nail in the coffin. So all the creepy stories kinda to flew by me, one ear in and one ear out type of stuff, including the one this story is about. Didn’t pay much attention to it, at least at first.



The tale goes a little something like this. The tale of an elevator woman who longs for attention.



If you are alone in an elevator, watch out for a woman who might be waiting at a certain floor. When the door opens and you see a lone woman walking in, do your best to ignore her, she may try to distract you, do not attempt to communicate, gesture, or even acknowledge her presence. Her actions may differ depending on the situation. Some say that she hums, others say that she weeps. But no matter what do NOT react. Do NOT look at her face, not even a glimpse.



Once she enters you are in a test or a game. Nobody else can go in the elevator anymore, and you should not get out either. Just you and her. In the elevator. After a while, once she noticed that you are ignoring her, she will attempt to ask you which floor you live on. Think about your tone before you speak, don’t be too rude or too polite, act like she is just a normal person along the ride. Say any random floor will do, except the floor you actually live on. Never give her that information about you.



She will then say which floor do you think she should leave at, do NOT answer that question. Simply wait, she will leave at after some time has passed, on a floor the building never had.



The game is now over and you may resume a normal ride.



Of course I never even recalled this ghost story after I heard it, shelved it in the back of my head, that day pass by and nothing happened inside the elevator, I would press the fifth floor button, wait like 10 seconds, and I would get out. Same thing same timing day after day, to say that I wasn’t fazed by the tale would be dishonest, I was still just a kid after all. But every time the elevator door opens to the lobby I took a breath of fresh air, a sort of reassurance that nothing weird is going on.



No weird ghost, no weird tale, just an elevator.



The elevators they had back in the estate was nothing special, maybe a bit antique. Remodeling private estate properties are a lot harder than government owned ones. The elevator still had those fence looking things outside the door, first the door would open then the fence would, never really sure what the purpose of those things are. They would make a loud sound when they shut, making a stop between floor that much more irritating. 



The chance of people intercepting the elevator I took was low, I lived on the fifth floor. Floor “one” was for parking, indicated by the character “P”. Floor two was a garden where people can stroll around, one where every private estate in Hong Kong has, it even had a swimming pool. So only Floor 3 and 4 are floors where apartments lies. And even if someone did come in the elevator, we would be seconds away from reaching the lobby anyway.



Days went by, our school forgot about the story, me included. Children pick up gossip fast and drop them just as fast. Soon the tale was just a mundanity among the other tales shared at recess, nothing more than a nagging feeling each time I took the elevator.



The day was normal, at least it started out that way. I get dressed up, went into the elevator, wait 10 seconds, and be at the lobby.



Well.



Except that this didn’t happen.



All I remember was a ding sound, indication of arriving at a floor and stopping there, I used to always look at the sign displaying floor I am currently on, just watching the number go down. Something about that comforts me, knowing that soon I will be out the elevator. Sometimes I wasn’t alone, plenty of people use that elevator everyday. But it wasn’t a lot either, again private estate. In addition to multiple elevators each serving a different section of the building.



This time the elevator didn’t stop at the lobby, did I even pressed the lobby back then? Maybe I forgot to, considering the fact that I was sleepy. The door opened, then the fence, accompanied by loud thuds.



There stood a woman, shoulder length black hair. I jolted internally. The tales suddenly came rushing back into my memory. First thought that came to my mind was maybe, “Is it really happening?”. But the first action I took was none other than to immediately look down to avoid staring at her. While thinking to myself things like “Why me?” Or “Am I psyching myself, it’s not real is it?”. Endless stream of what I can only describe as lamentation takes hold of me.



A moment of silence. Never knew the absence of sound can be deafening.



All thoughts drown out by the sound of footsteps getting into my elevator, sound of high heels, the grown woman was a lot taller than me, I wasn’t even half her size. Not that I bother to fact check such details. My vision is glued to her feet. Scared to make a noise and attract her attention.



Slowly, trying to make as little noise as possible, I backed into a corner of the elevator, just far enough from her to make personal space, but close enough to the door to bolt when she tries something, at the time it felt like a smart choice.



I was recalling the tales and rules of the scenario as fast as I could, a thing about not telling her where I live all the while assuring to myself that she is just a normal resident living in the building. Never seen her before, but I was a child, I could look at people then turn around and forgot about their face. Or maybe she was a visitor.



Trying to rationalise the reason behind her appearance isn’t helping me feel secured. Deep down, perhaps I feel like I was in a life or death situation.



Two, then three seconds went by, that was when I noticed the woman was weeping, imagine a grown up crying to the extend of bawling their eyes out. My mind as a kid couldn’t imagine adults doing something like that. It was the first time I could feel cold sweat dripping down my back. Things are starting to get dire inside my mind. It felt uncomfortable listening to her whimpering.



The woman kept at it, and I continue to kept my distance away from her, which isn’t much since it’s an elevator. I was practically trapped inside with her. Anything inside the elevator is within arm’s reach.



It doesn’t help that she is acting more and more like the woman I heard of in the tale. I desperately wanted to take a peak at her silhouette, remembered the rules. I discouraged myself from doing that. What good could come out of looking at her?



I recalled the moment of arrival. Maybe I already looked at her when she first came to my elevator. I don’t know.



Should I know?



Maybe she was already trying to grab onto me. I wouldn’t notice until it’s too late when I am constantly looking downwards.



When I was ruminating on that possibility I heard her gasping. A sound of pure anguish.



Which floor am I at right now? A mere 10 seconds stretch on and on, until time lost its meaning, the only thing I am concern about now is what she will do next.



She went silent then, sniffling was the only I sound I could made out of. Did she calmed down?



Or was it a red herring?



I looked at the ground floor button still lit up. What was supposed to be 10 seconds feels like minutes, then hours. The only indication for the passage of time being her breathing noises was awful.



I felt her glaze lingering on me.



It was sudden. I couldn’t tell if the gaze carried malice.



Right the rule. Amongst the panic I almost forgot.



Do I have to say something? Or should I keep my silent? A game of play along.



That’s when the sound of the elevator came back. A pleasant sound of a ding. Never thought I had to be thankful to an elevator.



The elevator had finally reached the lobby, the silent seemed to subside.



When the door opened I never felt so relieved, even though the woman never did anything to me. I felt like the only reason she didn’t was because I somehow followed the rules.



I let her walk out first, I don’t know why but I felt like I had to. A moment of awkwardness pass by. She walked out to the lobby. Still sobbing mildly. Only when I was sure people are outside, “real” people so to speak, did I also come out.



I took a look around the lobby. The woman was nowhere to be seen, even thought I was sure that she couldn’t have gone that far.



I soon noticed the people’s behavior inside the lobby was, for the lack of a better word. Distant.



I noticed that none of them paid attention to that woman. It was weird, the sight of a crying woman apparently didn’t alarm anyone.



Then again I am living in Hong Kong, a place where everyone minds their own business even if someone yelled and thrashed in the middle of the street. A woman crying isn’t that big of a deal.



That had to be it, I mean what other explanations could there be lest I start believing in the supernatural.



Later that day, I told my classmates what happened, only the kids around my age of course. About how there is a woman in my elevator that morning and stuff, being a kid didn’t help me formulate a coherent story at the time. Most of them whose faces and names I can’t remember now, said nothing more than words that carries a similitude to “Wow, no way”, or “You are lying”, sentences to those degree are common for children.



I knew it’s one of those, you don’t believe it until you see it type of stuff.



Yet I feel alone in that moment, sure it was hard to believe the elevator thing happen even for myself. But at that moment, I felt stranded within that elevator again.



No understanding was established, only empty platitudes and amusement. 



At the time I didn’t know how to clarify what happened there, so I didn’t told any adults about it, not the teachers, not even my parents. I mean, just imagining a kid saying about stuff like an elevator ghost woman sounds crazy. Eventually I tried to convince myself that the lady was just having a terrible day, nothing more. And it was just coincidence that I stumbled upon her.



With half hearted salutations, days returned to normal. Every time I revisited the memory of that elevator ride I tried to make sense of it by using hypotheticals. Stuff like what if she had a breakup with someone? Or she was grieving someone? Anything to subside my fear of the tale being true. It helped just a little bit, there weren't much to do inside the elevator anyway.



Suffice to say I didn’t want to be alone in an elevator after that experience, started to only take a ride when I saw another person also go in. The lobby always had people in it anyway.



I started to become aware of how many people are in the lobby at any given moment.



The leftmost elevator is for odd numbered floors, the middle one is for even numbered floors, rightmost one is for all floors. I never took the one other than the leftmost one of course. All of them have access to the parking and garden floor too.



A week or so gone by, I was convinced the whole thing was behind me, only way to move on. Went along with the days, the routine still hasn’t changed, I was not about to walk the stairs everyday just because of a weird encounter.



Except there was that one time, the lobby had a red flower pot that day, not sure why that was the only detail I can recall. Anyway the elevator I usually take, the one where the “game” took place in was closed for maintenance. So it’s either the stairs, which I am not going to take, school backpack is tiresome already as it is, or the rightmost elevator that has access to all floors.



Ironically the elevator with the access to all floor is the one taken the least. I figured people are afraid of the traffic. So I patiently wait for a long time for a person to take the ride with me.



Some time later I was in that elevator, one I never took, with a stranger. I didn’t look at the sign displaying the floor at the time, not anymore. I developed this habit after that day.



Looking at it made me anxious.



Then the sound of a ding.



To my surprise, the door would once again open, then the fence.



It was weird, again I emphasize I lived on the fifth floor, with only a couple of floors below me. But it wasn’t that strange, maybe the person was just going to the parking lot. I thought to myself. Brushing off the feeling of discomfort.



The parking floor was blue, I was sure of this, even though I never set foot in it. The color was unmistakable. That’s all I could see anyway before the door closes when people get off on that floor. I didn’t glance at the person nor the floor he stopped at.



He kind of just disappeared. Into the parking floor I assumed.



After taking so many ride you sort of has a mental note of how long everything takes. So I assume it was the parking floor judging by how short the ride had been.



Well I shouldn’t have assumed back then, a mistake in hindsight. not sure when it happened, similar to the time when the woman came in. Time seemed to blur inside the elevator, I waited for longer than usual.



Ever so slightly.



At first I didn’t notice, but I lived on the fifth floor. Yet the ride has been slightly longer than the 10 seconds I was so used to.



Maybe this elevator moves slower?



Another ding, another thud that came from the fence. I stare at the entrance, strange was the first thought. My floor, the fifth floor. Was colored in dim yellow, odd numbered floors are all in that color, and even numbered floors are in another color. That way people can recognise whether they have reached their floor or not even if people lived on adjacent numbered floors took the same ride. This I knew for sure, even as a child.



My mom told me about the color coordination after all.



The only exception was the parking floor which is in blue, and the garden floor which is in pink.



I mean the only exception.



A loud thud.



I was staring at a floor with orange themed wall.



I try to recall if there was a floor like this. Maybe I took the wrong floor? If I accidentally pressed the wrong floor I can just go up or down one floor with stairs, I thought to myself.



So I stepped into the orange floor, the smell wasn’t that different from normal. At least.



That’s when I noticed there was no floor sign for this floor.



The floors should have a number right? I mean mine was the number five, Parking floor is “P”, can’t recall what the sign for the garden floor was but it was definitely there. It’s the first thing you should see when the door open.



Except this one had no sign.



I need to go back into the elevator, I thought. I couldn’t explain it, but it felt like it was my last chance to get out of here. Even trying to articulate the thought would be too late.



The fence was closing in.



As if it had a will of their own, trying to keep me in here. In this orange tinged floor.



But it’s just a fence.



I could have pressed the button to recall the elevator, but when I tried to do so a sense of dread came over me. I recalled the day when that woman came into my elevator.



Which floor did she came from again?



I don’t know, I was too busy looking at the floor by the time it happened. So I didn’t look at the sign.



Was even one?



The elevator fully closed. I was too hesitant. The chance to go back in slip me by. But I wasn’t sure if going back in the elevator is the solution. A nonsensical decision I made.



The recollection of the day with that weeping woman came crashing down on me once again. Accompanying it was a sense of urgency. That’s the only way I can describe my actions.



I had the option to wait for the elevator to go back up again. But that elevator in particular is the one that has access to all floors. And waiting for it could take a long time.



Time that felt precious, time that I didn’t feel like I had.



I took a step back and looked carefully around this time. Still no floor sign, I don’t where I am. The structure of the floor is still the same, one narrow hallway, branching at the end of each side. Usually housing 3 apartments at the tail end of each branching hallway. I looked at each branch.



No apartments.



Only a door at the end of the hallway. Signs on the door that read something, I don’t know what since I haven’t reached the age where I can read anything more complicated than basic Chinese characters back then.



I didn’t trust those doors, probably better to not trust it. Even if they appear no different from the doors you would see in a building.



Doors that could lead to stairs.



That train of thought lead me to search for stairs on this floor, the atmosphere had grown far too suffocating for me. The narrow hallway, strange orange wall and the melancholic lighting.



The fact that I didn’t see anyone in this floor. All accumulated into panic mode, I scanned and judged the doors and where they would lead. To no avail of course.



Trying to remember what my floor looked like. I reminiscent upon the times when I was at the stairs. It is located at the right hallway of the floor, a grey staircase often filled with the ashes of burnt cigarettes.



My mom used to hide behind the stairs, to avoid my reprimands regarding her smoking habits.



I learnt at a young age that smoking is bad for your health, so naturally I tried to discourage my mom from doing it.



She always told me she would quit after this time, she never did. Said something about how taking a drag helps her calm down.



I don’t know why she needed it, but unknowingly, moments like those became one of the only things I can still recall about this place. About my childhood.



I thankfully located a door leading to a similarly designed staircase. One from those days.



Well except that this one seemed, smaller. More dampened.



Like it hasn’t been used for a long, long time.



But it wasn’t the time to ruminated on trivial things. Not even I was still trapped betwixt the orange floor. I started to sprint down the stairs, as fast as a child can run.



Each staircase composed of ten steps, then a turn. Then another staircase of ten. That’s when you descended a floor.



I walked a set of ten, turned around, then another set of ten.



No indication of where I am, the grey staircase remained grey. Nevertheless I kept the pace. I wasn’t sure if anything was chasing me, but I wasn’t going to wait around.



One step at a time, after what felt like an eternity to me.



My legs are aching, the only sensation I can recall, alongside the smell of ashes. I coughed.



I would always cough like this whenever mom was smoking.



She said how old habits die hard. Started it after the marriage.



Another set of ten steps, when I turn around. Stairs and floors filled with the butt of cigarettes. Each one has a different length to it, I pictured my mom twisting the orange butt of the cigarette against the stair before going back to the house with me.



I would always manage to convince her to stop, even just for a moment.



After another set of ten. I saw it.



There was a door, unremarkable in anyway shape or form, but it was the only way I have to get out of here. Away from this mess I got myself into.



Away from the orange floor.



Away from the memory of that weeping woman.



I pushed open the door with all the strength I could muster.



With a loud bang, I stepped outside. I looked around, and everything was normal. I returned to a world I belonged. In a sense it lacked finality to it, but I dreaded looking back.



It wasn't long before the voice of a woman calling out to me from my right. It was the security guard.



She told me that I activated the fire alarm earlier. I didn’t know what that meant at the time. But she escorted me back into the lobby. The pot of red flower was still there, it’s petals reminds me of scrunched up paper.



Leftmost elevator is still in maintenance. Almost akin to a reminder of what had happened manifested in physical form.



The security guard asked why I went to the emergency escape stairs. I give her a retelling of the experience I just had. Being fresh in my mind and all.



As best of a recount a child at the age of 8 or 9 can give anyway. Most the description amounted to something like, “Floor, Orange, Lost”. 



She laughed at my story. Said I just pressed the wrong button, or gone to the wrong elevator. Brought me to my apartment and went back to her post. I didn’t insist on the story.



It didn’t matter much to me at the time anyway. The why and how is brushed aside. I just wanted to get back to my apartment. On the fifth floor. My home.



I told my grandma about the experience. She told me there is no floor with orange. She mused that I must have gone to the floor beneath.



I didn’t pursue the “truth” or whatever, I was a kid back then. I was just glad I was back to the normal, mundane life I was living.



Nothing ever happened in those elevator again, even when I rode it alone. My family decided to move to a new estate when I graduated primary school. Said something about an financial settlement.



I never shared the story of the orange floor with classmates at that time. Partly because I was embarrassed, partly because I believed in the explanation the grown ups have given me. It’s better to believe in that I was a stupid kid then to believe the elevators are haunted anyway.



Wasn’t until recently I remembered this weird occurrence back in my childhood. I reiterated that it has been more than a decade since then. An experience I repressed for so long that even I forgot most of the details. Only the broad strokes.



I haven’t gone back to the estate since then. I might never know whether the woman is a ghost or a person in distraught. What is the strange orange floor and the numerous doors in it. What connection does these two things share. Is not a thought I wish to linger on. Nor can I.



I am just glad that I never saw the woman wept again
