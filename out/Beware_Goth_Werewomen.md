I tried to shout for help down the empty alley, though I knew the derelict buildings would swallow every sound. My voice came out ragged and faint, nearly lost beneath the low, throaty growls closing in from behind.  
The tallest one vaulted over a rusted dumpster with terrifying grace, landing lightly in front of me and cutting off my escape. Her heavy black eyeliner framed eyes that glowed a vivid, unnatural green. Pale skin contrasted sharply with her dark lipstick and the silver rings piercing her lower lip. Her fangs had lengthened just enough to peek past her lips when she smiled; her nostrils flared as she drank in my scent. Those glowing green irises caught stray beams from the distant flickering streetlamps and threw them back in sharp, needle-like points.  
I slammed my shoulder against a boarded up door, desperate for any way through. My hands met only solid wood. She did not rush. Instead the rest of the pack melted out of the side streets, their athletic bodies forming a tightening semicircle of shadow and heat that trapped me against the crumbling brick wall. The rapid, heavy thud of their hearts echoed in the narrow space, each beat strong and deliberate, far quicker than my own panicked pulse. I felt the tension in the air: that steady, unstoppable rhythm speaking of hunger kept barely in check.  
She stepped closer. Her breath washed hot across my face in slow, deliberate waves. I flinched when the tip of one sharp fang brushed my cheek, leaving a thin sting.  
“You should have stayed on the main roads,” she murmured, her voice low and velvet-rough. “Most men hear the howls and quicken their pace toward the lights. You chose the shortcut through our territory like a fool who thought the city belonged to him.”  
I shoved past her, trying to break through the gap between two of the others. The muscles in my legs burned with the effort. Her hand caught my arm with easy strength and yanked me back against the wall. Long fingers wrapped around my bicep while her black nails pressed lightly into my skin. I knew one quick flex would tear through muscle. She studied me the way a hunter sizes up a struggling catch, deciding how long to let it fight.  
I should have trusted my instincts the moment I heard the first distant howl echo between the abandoned factories. I should have remembered the whispered warnings about these forgotten districts after dark, where people vanished and were never found whole. Instead curiosity and a stubborn desire to prove the stories wrong had pulled me deeper into the maze of empty streets. Now the alley felt like a trap closing shut. The faint glow of the few working streetlamps seemed to dim as if the city itself was turning away. Shadows lengthened in the broken windows overhead, and I imagined more glowing green eyes watching from the rooftops.  
She shifted her stance. One powerful arm pinned my chest to the bricks. A long black tail I had barely noticed earlier curled around my leg like a living chain. The tufted end tightened just enough to pin me in place. Her free hand traced the line of my throat down to my collarbone. She pressed there, feeling the frantic pounding beneath. A soft, pleased rumble rose in her chest, almost a purr.  
“Such a racing heart,” she said. “It will burn so brightly when we chase it down.”  
I twisted violently, pure instinct driving me. My knee connected with her thigh. The blow landed hard, yet she only let out a short, amused breath. The others surged closer; their agile, buff bodies tensed, blocking every possible exit and scattering broken glass across the pavement. She caught my wrists in one powerful grip and forced them above my head against the rough wall. Cold brick scraped my knuckles. Her strength flowed effortlessly, almost casual in its overwhelming force. I felt the massive gap between my human limits and the raw power that moved through their limbs.  
“Keep struggling,” she whispered near my ear. “The more you fight, the sweeter the chase becomes. Your fear smells incredible on the night air.”  
Her tongue flicked out, longer than it should have been, tracing the pulse in my neck in slow strokes that left wet trails behind. Each pass sent a confusing jolt through me: raw terror mixed with the strange thrill of being hunted. My body still remembered the distant sound of their laughter that had first drawn me off the safe path; it still betrayed me with shivers that had nothing to do with cold alone. She noticed. Her dark lips pulled back, revealing the full curve of her fangs.  
I tried to speak again. “Let me go.” The words cracked in my throat. She laughed, low and liquid, the sound bouncing off the brick walls as if the empty city were joining in.  
“Let me go,” she echoed mockingly. “After you came looking for trouble in our streets? Did you think the howls were an invitation? Did you believe you could wander these ruins and leave untouched?”  
She released my wrists only to grip my shoulders and spin me around, pressing my chest flat against the wall. My cheek scraped against the cold bricks. She pinned me there with her body, her strong frame molding against my back. The pack closed in on either side, their glowing green eyes reflecting in the puddles at our feet. Darkness swallowed the alley except for the heat of their powerful forms and the slow, deliberate rhythm of their breathing.  
She leaned in. The firm curve of her body pressed against my back through her torn black lace. Her mouth found the side of my neck. Fangs grazed skin, testing pressure without breaking it, yet. I felt the promise in every careful scrape, the certain knowledge that she could end the hunt with one decisive bite.  
“I will tell you a secret,” she murmured against my ear. Her voice dropped to a register that vibrated through bone. “Every man who thought these streets were empty learned the same lesson. The city does not forgive intruders. And neither do we.”  
Her tongue traced the rigid line of my spine and down my back. My muscles locked tight. I could not tell if I was still fighting to break free or simply bracing for what came next. The confusion twisted inside me. She knew it. She fed on it.  
When her mouth reached the base of my back, she paused. I felt her inhale deeply, drawing my scent into her lungs until it seemed she might take the strength from my body. Then she bit. Not deep. Just enough to break skin. Pain flared white-hot. Blood welled. She lapped at it in slow, luxurious strokes that turned agony into something worse, something that blurred torment and the dark thrill of the chase.  
I gasped sharply, a broken sound I barely recognized. She hushed me gently, her fingers stroking my hair with surprising tenderness. The contrast cut deeper than her fangs.  
“Hush, love,” she said. “The hunt is almost over.”  
She spun me around to face her once more. Strength was draining from my legs. I sagged against the wall. Blood trickled down my legs, staining my pants. She pressed close, straddling one of my thighs to keep me upright and trapped. The others stayed near, their glowing green eyes watching with patient hunger. Their silver rings and black nails caught faint streetlight and threw it back in dull emerald glints.  
“Look at me,” she commanded.  
I obeyed because resistance was fading. Her glowing green eyes filled my vision. Inside them I saw myself, cornered, breathless, already marked. She smiled, dark lips pulling back to show her fangs and the dark violet of her gums.  
“You ran so well,” she said.  
Her head descended. The first real bite landed at the join of neck and shoulder. Fangs sank deep. Muscle parted with a wet sound I would never forget. Pain roared through me. My body jerked. A cry tore free only to be muffled against her shoulder as she held me close. Blood flooded her mouth. She drank in long, greedy swallows. Each pull sent fresh weakness spreading through my limbs.  
The world narrowed to the rhythm of their feeding. Draw. Swallow. Breathe. My heartbeat stuttered, tried to keep pace, then faltered. Vision dimmed at the edges. Sounds grew distant: the distant drip of water from a broken pipe, the faint howls of more sisters echoing through the district, the slow wet sounds of my blood on her tongue.  
She lifted her head at last. Crimson glistened on her black lips and dripped onto my chest in warm drops. She licked them clean with slow care, savoring every trace. Her glowing eyes softened, almost regretful.  
“I’ll miss you my love. ” she whispered.  
She kissed me and I almost willingly kissed her back, I could taste my own blood when she pulled away.   
My chest rose once more, shallow and uneven. I tried to speak, or perhaps only to beg. No sound came. She leaned in one final time. Her dark lips brushed my ear.  
“Sleep now, handsome. The streets will keep you.”  
Her fangs found my throat again. This time she tore a deep gash. A bright, hot burst of pain then nothing. Darkness rushed in fast and complete. The last thing I felt was the gentle stroke of her hand across my cooling cheek, the soft croon of her voice fading into the fog.  
The abandoned streets held their silence afterward. Farther down the block, a lone streetlamp still flickered weakly. Deeper in the ruins, a distant siren wailed once, mournful and alone. My last thought is all that is left behind before I blacked out, “I think I love her.”
