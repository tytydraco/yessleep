My friends and I experienced one of the most horrifying things on a road trip we went on nearly ten years ago.

We were just trying to go and see The Black Dahlia Murder. They are our favorite band, and they still mean a lot to us. Hell, we all became good friends because of going to those shows in the first place. Had I not had that group of dudes with me, I wouldn’t be here typing all this out to begin with. There would be no witnesses.

This is what I remember from that night.

My buddies and I were all carpooling together to go and see The Black Dahlia Murder during their Nightbringers tour back in 2017. The line-up was so stacked that it was insane. The Black Dahlia Murder, Suffocation, Decrepit Birth, and a bunch of others we loved were all on the same bill. A night full of death metal and zero responsibilities was way too good to pass up on. 

Everybody secured their tickets the second they went on sale and took time off work. We weren’t missing that show for anything in the world.

The trip itself started out harmless enough. Looking back, it felt like every other road trip we had ever gone on together.

“Everblack is still their best album man, I don’t care what anyone says.” Ryan stated, wiping the leftover Taco Bell crumbs off his Frozen Soul shirt.

Brandon looked legitimately heated. “No, Nightbringers is their best by a country mile and it’s not even close.” 

“That album JUST came out.” I shook my head disapprovingly. “That’s recency bias talking. Besides, you’re both wrong anyway. Nocturnal is the greatest album this genre has ever seen.”

Our debate was being heavily scrutinized by a set of wild, brown eyes in the rearview mirror. 

“Y’all need to go touch some grass. You’ve been going ‘round and ‘round about this same topic for the last hour.”

“Pull over and I’ll happily oblige Brian.” Ryan snickered, earning a laugh from the whole group.

Brian smiled. “Don’t tempt me.” 

We pulled into a gas station off the interstate that our GPS had directed us to go to.

“Dude, are you serious?” Brandon groaned. “This is the fifth time we’ve stopped on this trip. We’re going to miss the show at this rate.”

Brian pulled up to one of the gas pumps illuminated by the bright lights above. “We’ve still got plenty of time. But keep complaining. I’ll make sure we stop a sixth time.”

“You’re such an asshole.” Ryan joked, already unfastening his seatbelt.

“What? I gotta piss like a race-horse.” Brian parked the car and turned in his seat to look at all of us. “Any of you ladies care to join me?”

I raised my hand like a student getting ready to answer a question. “I’ll go.”

Brian opened the car door and began stretching in an exaggerated manner. “Perfect! Let’s go, Lucas!” 

“I can’t wait to get my paws on a Dr. Pepper,” I admitted with a smile. “I’ve been fiending for one.”  

Brian either didn’t hear me or didn’t care to respond. His focus was on Brandon. “It’s your turn to fill up the tank Brandon!”

“I filled it up last time!” Brandon called out, but Brian ignored him, and both of us walked toward the gas station.

I surveyed our surroundings, and the first thing that stood out was how deserted it was. For seven o’clock in the evening the place was dead. There was only one other car besides ours parked in the lot, a beat-up Chevy truck whose better days were very clearly behind them. 

Had a tumbleweed rolled through the parking lot, the place would have looked like your stereotypical abandoned building in the middle of nowhere. 

“Where the hell did we stop?” I asked, holding the door open for Brian to walk through. “There’s like nobody here.”

Brian pulled his phone out of his pocket and inspected it. “Some place called Brindlewood. I don’t fucking know.”

“I’ve never heard of it.”

“If we didn’t stop here I could have gone the whole rest of my life not knowing about this place.” 

I nodded and made my way to the soda fountain while Brian walked to the counter nearby. With a styrofoam cup in hand, I pressed the Dr. Pepper button and watched the soda nozzle gush the soda I had been craving. 

Out of my peripheral, I watched Brian wave his hand to get the cashier’s attention. 

“Hey man,” he rapped his knuckles on the counter. “Where are your bathrooms?”

I didn’t hear a response which only peeved Brian and his pea-sized bladder. “Good talk. Guess I’ll go fuck myself.”

I had to stifle a laugh at the sight of him stomping around the gas station until he found the men’s restroom door and disappeared out of sight. When I went up to the counter, I still had a smile on my face.

“Sorry about my friend,” I apologized and fished my wallet from my pocket. “How much do I owe you?”

But my question went unanswered as I fished a five dollar bill out of my wallet. When I went to present the money to him, I noticed that the cashier was standing in the exact same place that he had when Brian and I had walked in.

“How much?”

Once again, no answer. His eyes were wide-open, bloodshot, and staring off into the distance. It was like the guy was in a completely different universe. Was he tripping balls in the middle of a shift he had mentally checked out of halfway through? That was a possibility. But dealing with not-so-sober people was out of my wheelhouse.

“Hey, you okay?” I waved my hand like Brian had earlier to get his attention. The guy didn’t even blink. It was like he was in a catatonic state.

“What’s going on?” 

I nearly jumped out of my skin. “Brian, you fucking dick. Don’t sneak up on me.”

“I didn’t. I just walked over here.” He placed some candy on the counter and gestured to me. “Put it on his tab.”

The cashier’s eye began twitching, signaling some sign of life, but not enough to make me feel comfortable again. 

“Dude, I think we should call someone.”

“Why?” Brian shrugged. “This guy is probably enjoying the best trip of his life. Who are we to interrupt that?”

I ignored Brian about halfway through his sentence to study the movement within the cashier’s forearms. Something was writhing and swaying underneath. 

“Hey,” Brian’s eyes drooped down to read the cashier’s nametag. “Chase. Can you ring me and my friend out so we can be on our way? We got places to be.”

The shapes in Chase’s arms moved sinuously. It was a disturbing visual made even more disturbing by the fact that pearlescent outlines fidgeted behind the irises of his eyes.  

“Let’s get out of—” 

I never got to finish my suggestion as Chase suddenly gripped the counter tightly, his breath alternating between wheezes and growls. The veins and squirming silhouettes bulged under his skin. His body wracked violently with spasms. I dropped my soda on the floor, the Styrofoam cup spilling soda all over mine and Brian’s shoes. We stared in horror at Chase’s bizarre behavior.

The color in Brian’s face had left. “What do we do?” 

I couldn’t pull my eyes away from the vast number of protrusions underneath his skin. “Call the fucking police man!” 

Brian pulled out his phone and began dialing but cursed at the realization that he had no cell service. “We’re fucked dude!” 

A moment later, Chase collapsed to a heap on the floor. Everything went silent.

“Oh my God.” Brian gripped his hair in distress. “Is he dead?!” 

“He’s not dead.“ I shot Brian a look. “Let me check on him.” 

I inched closer toward the counter to peer over it, and what I saw made my stomach churn. Lying on the floor was Chase. His limbs were splayed as if the bones within his body had been forgotten. His eyes were rolled back, revealing white, zombified membrane staring back at me. Foam spilled out of the mouth of his seizing body. It looked like something straight out of a Cannibal Corpse album cover, and I had to suppress the urge to vomit at the sight.

“Dude, is he okay or what?” Brian’s voice called out from behind me.

“I…I don’t know. But he needs to get to a hospital.”

The ding of the doorbell alerted us to another presence entering the gas station.

I recognized Ryan’s voice immediately. “Guys, what’s the hold up?” 

“Yeah man,” Brandon added. “We need to leave now if we’re planning on getting to the show on time.” 

Before I could reply to either of them, tendrils crept up from the depths of Chase’s throat and pulled his lips apart like dental instruments. His cheeks expanded and stretched as something began emerging from the depths of his body, splitting the flesh like it were a mere cocoon.

 “Jesus…” 

Chase didn’t even look like he was breathing anymore. His chest wasn’t rising or falling anymore…it was pulsing. Bones and cartilage compressed like debris in a compactor. Whatever it was continued its push up from the inside of Chase’s body.   
“What? What do you see?” Brian asked from the beef jerky aisle.

Chase’s jaw clamped shut, not from pain, but from pressure. Something nacreous pressed beneath the thinning skin of his throat. His body was splitting at the seams at an uncontrollable rate. 

“Lucas! Brian! C’mon!” Ryan shouted impatiently. 

And then—Chase’s head exploded. Brain matter and chunks of skull splattered against my clothes. Blood dripped from every visible surface as an elongated shape consisting of pure muscle emerged from what remained of Chase. 

It was a worm.

That was the only word my brain could find for it. It was far larger than I originally thought. Segments of pale flesh dragged behind it in slick coils, each section flexing independently beneath translucent skin. 

Before I could even process what I was seeing, Brian screamed and ran for the exit.

I stumbled backward without thinking and bumped into the counter. The opalescent mass reacted to the noise I had created by lashing out and slamming into my shoulder. I fell to the floor hard with a thud. I extended my hand upward in an attempt to grip the counter and pull myself up, but before I could, I felt its gore-soaked, cold body squeeze my wrist. 

I howled in pain as the constriction grew increasingly tight. 

“Lucas!” Ryan screamed. I saw him and Brandon rushing back through the aisle, but the worm already had me. I tried to move but it was useless. Every movement I made was weighed down by the worm pressing down on my arm.

“Get it off me!” I pleaded, my arm growing numb from the amplifying pressure.

Brandon vaulted the counter effortlessly and landed beside me. Using the counter as leverage, he grabbed my shirt and began yanking me backward so hard I thought that my arm was going to rip from its socket. 

Despite his best efforts, I was still stuck in place. Ryan placed himself on the right side of me and repeatedly kicked the worm’s body as hard as he could. But the kicks did nothing to make the worm relent. 

Realizing he was going to need to do more damage, Ryan grabbed a nearby fire extinguisher off the wall next to the cash register and began clubbing the worm’s side.

The worm rasped instantly, its grip loosening just enough for Brandon to pull me free.

“Go!” Ryan yelled, discarding the fire extinguisher and running away.

Without hesitation, Brandon and I sprinted toward the entrance. The Dr. Pepper I had dropped earlier splashed underneath my feet as I looked behind me. The worm carved through the store behind us, folding metal shelving like cardboard. Bags of chips and pretzels flew overhead, raining down a hailstorm of crumbs.

An entire display of beer crashed to the floor beside me. Cans burst against the floor and sprayed foam across my pants as Brandon grabbed the back of my shirt to hold onto me. It barreled through another aisle, launching Pop-Tarts and a rack of discounted sunglasses into the ceiling.

Ryan practically dove through the doors first while Brandon shoved me ahead of him. We stumbled out onto the concrete of the parking lot, and we rushed toward the car.

Brian’s head was leaning out the driver’s side. “Move your asses!” he barked. “C’mon!” 

I risked a glance over my shoulder and saw the worm erupt through the front of the store. With the force of a semi-truck, it crashed through the doors. Glass detonated outward as we scrambled toward the car. The worm’s mouth split open like petals of a flower unfurling. Its screeches were growing closer. 

Ryan was the first one to reach the safety of the backseat. 

“Go, go, go!” he commanded as the rest of us piled in. 

Before Brian could take the car out of park, the worm threw itself against the passenger side window. Its circular maw snapped ferociously as the glass spiderwebbed and struggled to remain intact. Its saw-blade-like teeth clamped down repeatedly against the window. 

The creature’s body thrashed against the side of the car like a livewire. Every impact rocked the vehicle hard enough to shove us against each other. The sustained blows took their toll, and the passenger window imploded, spraying glass across Brian’s lap. We screamed in fright and watched the worm try to crawl inside with the hook-like appendages along its underside.

I gripped the seat and reared back my leg to kick the creature out of the car. “Drive! Get us the fuck out of here!”

Brian threw the car into drive, and with the momentum of the car jerking forward, I thrust my foot into the side of the worm. Its body fell with a wet splat to the parking lot, and we peeled off into the night.  The tires screamed against the pavement and before long, the horrifying scene faded into the distance behind us.

Brian wiped the anxious sweat from his forehead. “What the fuck was that?” 

I said the only thing I could muster in that moment. “I don’t know.”

“I don’t know either, but I’m glad we all made it out alive.”

“Uh, guys? What’s that behind us?”

Brandon’s question sent a shiver down my spine. I looked at where he was pointing, and saw a shape slithering in the darkness behind our car.

“Oh fuck!” I cried out. “It’s followed us!”

“The hell it has!” Brian swerved the wheel in a desperate attempt to shake it from the car, our car veering to and fro across the lanes of the empty road.

Amid our panic, red and blue lights flashed in the darkness behind us, revealing two things. One, our antics had drawn the attention of a police officer, and two, that wasn’t the worm behind us, it was the gas nozzle that we had accidentally pulled away with.

“There’s no way this is happening right now.” Ryan sighed in relief and annoyance.

As Brian brought the vehicle to a stop, metal and asphalt ground together, creating a trail of sparks behind us. 

Brian massaged his temples. “I can’t believe we took the fucking pump with us.”

I couldn’t believe it either. I wanted to laugh it all off, but I couldn’t. The adrenaline was still flowing through me, and all I could think about was how the hell we were going to explain all the blood.

Nobody uttered a single word as the officer walked up slowly and swept his flashlight across the dangling gas nozzle still clinging to the side of the car.

“How are we doing tonight?” he asked with a mix of confusion and cordialness. 

“We’re doing alright officer.” Brian lied with a smile. 

“Right…do you want to explain what you were doing back there? You were swerving all over the road, and you got a gas nozzle still in your car. You in a rush?”

Brian looked at all of us before looking back at the officer. “Yeah, we’re going to see The Black Dahlia Murder.”

The officer raised a brow. “The Black Dahlia Murder? Is that a group? A rock group?”

“You could say that.” 

The officer turned his attention from Brian to me; his flashlight revealed all the blood caked to my clothes.

Even from where he stood, I could tell he was visibly shaken. “What happened?”

“There was a giant worm that attacked us at the gas station. It destroyed the whole place and we barely escaped. I swear I’m not high. I haven’t had a single beer either. You have to believe us officer.”

He clicked his flashlight off and glared at Brian. “Alright, you boys are taking me back to that gas station right now.”

And so, we did just that. 

The gas station looked even worse coming back to it. Red and blue lights strobed across the shattered storefront while gasoline and a trail of slime pooled beneath the pumps in shimmering puddles. One of the fluorescent lights above the entrance flickered in and out periodically like a strobe light.

There wasn’t a whole lot to prove our story about a giant worm nearly killing us, and I could still see the skepticism on the cop’s face as he rummaged through the rubble of the broken aisles.

“What did you say happened here again?”

“I told you, there was a giant worm,” Brian explained, pointing toward the front counter area. “It burst out of that guy over there.” 

We all nodded in agreement, and the cop walked toward the register. He started to say something but stopped dead in his tracks when his flashlight landed on what was left of Chase behind the counter.

He gagged and turned visibly pale at the sight. 

“Christ…”

A moment later, he lowered the beam slightly and spoke into the radio clipped to his shoulder. 

“Dispatch,” he said quietly, never taking his eyes off the body. “This is Officer Johnson. I need medical and backup out here at the Dean Co. gas station over in Brindlewood immediately.”

After he called it in, we answered the barrage of questions they asked us to the best of our ability. They thought that we were out of our minds, high, drunk, or maybe all three. But we complied with every test they gave us, including a breathalyzer. When they realized that we were in the right frame of mind, we were given medical attention, and then discharged not too long after.

By the time we were finally able to leave, the sun had come up, and most disappointingly, the show was over. We had missed it. All that time we spent planning and driving was for nothing. The tour had moved on to the next town. All because of a chance encounter with a monstrous worm in a gas station in the middle of nowhere. 

The same car that had been packed with jokes and album debates the night prior, now sat in silence the entire drive home. We didn’t even bother trying to find another date on the tour, we just accepted the financial loss and told ourselves we would see The Black Dahlia Murder on the next tour.

I’m not sure what became of that Dean Co. gas station in Brindlewood. I tried looking it up a couple years back and other than some articles saying it was permanently closed, there was no mention of Chase or the worm the guys and I saw. 

I still think about that night every now and then, and while the guys and I treat it as nothing more than an inside joke, it still scares the shit out of me knowing that we nearly lost our lives. Sometimes, I still can’t help but wonder what exactly happened after we drove away. 

A couple of nights ago, Ryan sent us a news article in our group chat about a truck stop in a neighboring state. That truck stop in addition to a series of other gas stations had suddenly shut down after employees “disappeared” during their night shifts.

The photos from every single one of those places all looked exactly like the Dean Co. station after we had escaped—torn shelving, blood on the floor, and long trails of slime leading out of frame.

Judging by the trail of carnage spreading across the nearby states of Wisconsin, Indiana, and Ohio, whatever crawled out of Chase didn’t die in Brindlewood. 

It’s still out there, and it’s feeding.
