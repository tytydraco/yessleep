I work as a gas station attendant and, to this day, I remember everything that happened the fateful night my life was turned upside down.

I watched the clock on the wall torture me with its absurdity, time passing through my fingers like sand escaping a shell. There I was, standing behind the cash register, in an ill-fitting XL-sized uniform shirt. A big smiley face above my nametag. *We bring happiness to your everyday needs!* To say this job drained the life out of me would be an understatement. But it kept the bills paid, food on the table warm, and my crippling nicotine addiction at bay. A large man with a huge sweat stain on his back burst into the gas station, a ring of a bell alerting me to his presence. Slipping my black hair into a bun, I put on a smile and warmly yelped a greeting. He gave me a slight nod and made his way to the beverage section. Grabbing a plastic cup, he filled it with ice and pressed the double espresso button of the coffee machine with his chubby finger. The machine started grinding the coffee beans and making the milk scorching hot. We made eye contact for a second. I politely smiled again and watched as the ice was partially melted by the dark substance. He looked around the gas station, locking eyes on the cigarettes behind my head. He seemed to be thinking which brand would appease his lungs. I could relate to that. I took it as an opportunity to do my job.

“Which brand of cigs are up to your liking, sir?” I said, turning my back to him. I readjusted my septum piercing and scratched my back. “A pack of Golden Marbs is fine, peach.” The coffee machine clicked, announcing the coffee was done pouring. I reached for the Golden Marlboros on the top shelf and rang them up for him. He made his way to the cash register and put the iced double espresso on the counter. I slid the cigarettes toward him. “That is thirteen dollars with tax, sir,” I declared. He started rubbing his head while searching his pockets for change. He took out a musty twenty-dollar bill and, with a mad smile, said to keep the change. I let him go with his midnight refreshments and went back to studying the clock.

Suddenly, the bell rang again. A short girl entered the gas station. She was heading straight to the cash register. A parent teaching their child how to be independent. My thought was confirmed when I saw a crumpled bill in the child's hand. She was sweating profusely, eyeing me up and down. I leaned against the counter, trying to make myself less intimidating. “Hi,” I said, smiling. The smile felt genuine, a nice contrast to the previous customer. She looked at the ground and mumbled the number of the pump. I looked out and saw a patient mother sitting in her car, looking at her phone. She handed over the bill and, as I was preparing to give her the change and send her on her merry way, I realized something about her felt off. She seemed to realize I was studying her, so she looked at me intensely.

“Which brand of cigs are up to your liking, sir?” were the words that came out of her. I was in shock, in awe, but also felt like I got stabbed in the gut. The voice from her throat did not resemble the trembling high-pitched voice she spoke with before. The voice was monotone, with a fake politeness to it and a harsh aftertone of a smoker's breath. It was my voice.

“Thirteen dollars,” she said. I squirmed and shouted: "What the fuck are you doing?”. Her left eye twitched, and an uncanny smile formed on her face, revealing a perfect set of adult white teeth. “With tax, sir.” I felt reality slipping away from me. I could feel myself shrinking into nothingness, as the child in front of me rose. Before I flew to the ether, I looked out to the pump to realize the car was no longer there. The child kept growing, and a circle of metal appeared in its nose.

I awoke to the sound of my manager shaking me awake. He asked if I had taken any drugs or alcohol. I just said I must have passed out and denied any of his accusations. He sent me home and I am now on paid sick leave. I do not know what the child was or what happened after I passed out. I just have this terrible feeling that something was taken from me, like I was robbed of my identity.

My worst fear came true when I was watching the news yesterday and saw a picture of myself on the TV with bright red bold letters underneath it spelling out “WANTED”. I will not list the atrocities this "thing" has committed under the cover of my appearance. I have hundreds of missed calls from my family, friends, and even my boss. I must go into hiding before the police track me down. I am already risking so much by posting this story here. Please, if you see a small child with adult human teeth, do not interact with it. Your safest bet is to run.
