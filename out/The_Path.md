Throughout all of my childhood and most of my teen years, my family used to live in a relatively small town located somewhere in upstate Pennsylvania.

To be more specific, they lived in a two-story house that was located close to the edge of town. That didn't really change much for me except for the fact that I had to walk roughly 10 minutes to and from the nearest bus stop.

Now, because the houses that were built near the edge of town were built in such a way that they didn’t really have any concrete roads leading to or from them, they’d usually split off into these thick dirt roads that would extend for roughly 20–30 minutes before you’d arrive home. Now, some people would describe walking down these roads as "creepy" or "unsettling"; however, I’d say that, for me, walking across these paths would bring me some sense of comfort and peace.

One day, while I was returning from a summer camp-like program, I came to see the turn that would eventually lead me to my house. While visually everything looked the same, I smelled a sort of... stench that stuck onto the trail like a skunk's odor. Now, I wasn’t unfamiliar with odd smells coming from the forest at that time. I’d smelled the stench of death a few times before while walking on the trail, which was probably caused by a decomposing animal carcass. However, unlike the carcass smell, which would ultimately pass after you’d walk a dozen or so steps away from the supposed source, this one seemed to linger throughout the entire duration of the trail.

The smell itself was hard to explain, mainly because there isn’t a proper way to describe it without it sounding like something else entirely, but, in a rough sense, it smelled like spoiled eggs and cheap laundry detergent. It smelled worse than that, but I digress.

As I started walking further along the trail, I noticed that the trail seemed to stretch on for much longer than I had originally remembered it. Now, normally the way I’d know that I was approaching the end was when I saw a red arrow pointing to the left on one of the bigger trees that was next to the path. For me, that would indicate that there was about 10–15 minutes of trail left, and once you’d walk through that final section of the dirt trail, you’d be led to this clearing, where, in the middle of it, was the house. However, after walking for about 20 or so minutes past the arrow, I did not see any sort of clearing in sight, which, at the time, really creeped me the fuck out...

I started to backtrack a bit, and as I did, I noticed that the way I walked forward and backward seemed to defy the laws of physics as a whole. I’d walk back a few steps, and now I’m back at the red arrow. It didn’t matter if I had walked for 10, 15, or 20 minutes; if I were to take a few steps back, I’d end up in the same place.

After about seven times of going forward and turning backward, I decided to go left, which was where the arrow was pointing. I remember that me and one of my old friends from grade school made that mark so we wouldn’t forget where we decided to build a tree house near the path.

As I started to go left, I felt the odor that had stuck to the trail slightly fade, bit by bit, and by the time I came to the tree house, the smell had vanished entirely. Upon looking around, I climbed up the three-foot ladder and onto this small, barely stable platform that had a wooden plank for a wall. That’s the aforementioned tree house...

While this did calm me at first, deep down I felt a slight unease, as if this place, this platform I was on, wasn’t really the one I’d built a year prior. Sure, it looked the same. It rattled in a similar fashion, but something about it didn’t feel right.

As I could see the sky above the trees turning into a warmer color, I carefully climbed down out of there and began my way back to the path. However, as I started to walk back, the ever-growing feeling that something was wrong started to dawn on me again. However, before those thoughts could fully settle, I felt as though I wasn't alone out here.

I started to doubt the sound of my footsteps even being mine. I stopped for a few minutes to listen, and I mean really listen to myself walking. Every time I would stop, I’d hear, for just a split second, someone stopping as well. When I walked, it sounded like there were two pairs of feet. It was just barely noticeable.

By this point, I was sent into a full-blown panic. I was running back and forth, ending up at the red arrow over and over again. If I decided to go on the tree house trail, I’d end up in the same place I was just a few minutes ago, even after I’d passed the tree house. It didn’t help that it was getting dark and the stench was getting worse. At times it even made me gag and pinch my nose.

Something didn’t want me to leave this trail, but it felt like whatever force was dragging me back in didn’t know what was past the tree house...

So, I decided to walk backwards again, and sure enough, I ended up back at the red arrow. This time, however, I decided to turn around and walk back to where I came from. As I expected, the trail ended up stretching beyond what I’d expected, and I ran for about seven minutes before stopping to fully catch my breath.

However, as I turned my head to face the path, I noticed someone... or well, more specifically, something. It was pitch black at this point, so discerning how it actually looked was borderline impossible, but I did manage to make out some things about it...

It was tall, like, freakishly tall. Its skin looked glossy, slimy almost, but I couldn’t tell for sure. It looked like that from the moonlight shining along the trail. I guess that whatever it was didn’t like being seen because, as soon as I lifted my head up, it made this low, rumbling sound that almost sounded like someone gurgling or choking underwater.

As I started to back up, I turned around to start heading the other way, the way I was originally going, and as soon as I did, I heard it.

Sprinting.

It sounded wet and unnatural, forceful even. But I didn’t look back. I couldn’t look back... I didn’t even have a second thought in my head to consider what this thing was. All I was concerned about was getting home and getting the hell away from this thing, this monster that had been following me for God knows how long.

I heard footsteps catching up with me. I tried to quicken my pace, but my legs just couldn’t. I was so scared I started to sob out of the fear that this thing might catch up and do God knows what to me.

As soon as the thought crossed my mind, I felt something forcefully yank me back, and I fell hard on the ground. I screamed in fear as I tried to cover my face so I could shield myself from whatever horrors this thing might do to me. I closed my eyes, expecting the worst, when I suddenly heard something...

"Oh God, oh my God, it’s him!"

That voice sounded familiar, warm almost... my mom's voice. I was hastily picked up from the ground as she embraced me in a hug... It felt slightly cold, but it was her... After hours of walking, I finally reached home. I cried on her shoulder for a while before she asked me a question.

"It’s been five hours, where were you?"

"I... I dunno, I was walking home, and then I guess I-I got lost," I replied, slightly stuttering from the faint sobs that came out between words.

"Don’t ever do that to me again... please," she said, still tightly hugging me.

"I won’t... I promise," I replied, barely managing to hiccup out those words while I leaned my head on her shoulder, finally feeling safe after hours of unending hell.

"I still have some leftovers from dinner. Uncle Jowey saved them for you."

"...I don’t have an Uncle Jowey... I don’t—"

Before I could finish my sentence, my heart sank, as it all came back to me at once. The smell... it had become stronger than ever now. Suddenly, her hug didn’t feel as comforting as it did seconds ago. It felt so unfamiliar again... so cold...

I could feel her fingers digging into my back as the deafening silence stretched between us. My back felt wet all of a sudden... A slimy, cold feeling that I hoped wasn’t what I thought it was...

*But it was, wasn’t it...?*
