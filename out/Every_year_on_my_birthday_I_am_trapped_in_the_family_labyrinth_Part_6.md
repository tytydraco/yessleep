I sprint along the path until exhaustion forces me to slow down. I don’t hear the rumbling sounds of that stone monstrosity following me, so I hope it is safe to catch my breath. I resist the urge to stop completely. Instead, I follow the oddly glowing tunnel a bit further until it opens into a strange room.

I am amazed by the sheer size of the space, I cannot even see the other side. There are no visible branching paths in here, just a large open space. I notice that there appears to be actual soil and dirt covering the ground all around me. It’s strange, but I don’t ever remember seeing normal earth here before. Every time in the past, I have only seen that cold unyielding stone on every surface.

I look around to see the other hallmarks in the room, and they are far more disturbing. Dozens of tomb stones are scattered around the area. It appears I have come upon an entire hidden graveyard.

Unsure of what else to do, I move closer to investigate. I hold my breath and examine the first stone.

I’m not surprised to see my family name on the surface, after all who else would wind up dead here? But what I can't figure out is how or why they were buried here, never mind who could have done it.

I know many people over the years have been lost, but we never knew where the dead would end up. We just assumed that the labyrinth claimed them, never to return. Yet now it seems there has been a graveyard in the place of their death this whole time.

I continue searching the chamber and walk from stone to stone, looking at the names of my slain family members. Each stone has a name, and birthdate. Disturbingly, the stones also seem to even have the specific dates of their eventual demise.

I still don't know who could have interred them here, or how anyone even knew the date they eventually succumbed. The implication that someone or something here is keeping such particular track of my family's mortality is disturbing.

I continue moving through the shadowed and silent graveyard. I noticed the sites here seem old, close to a hundred years ago. Moving further down the grim line of resting sites I begin to see more recent dates.

I start to see names I recognize. I pause when I see a stone for Lydia. I look closer and see that her birthdate is on the weathered stone, but I am shocked to see there is no death date. The area below the stone is a shallow pit that has an empty coffin laying inside.

I consider the voice I heard, it sounded so much like her. It must have really been her. She is not dead, somehow she survived after all this time! But how and why is she not helping me to find the way to stop all this? Why is she just pushing me to leave her here and save myself? I have so many questions, but I know I need to keep moving.

I press on and look at the next row of stones. I see more of the semi empty plots, with waiting coffins still inside, no doubt ready to receive those that fail to return.

I gasp aloud when I see a stone that bears my own name. The birthdate seems to be glowing faintly as I look upon the horrible sight. Someone here has already set a resting place for me in case I fail. I see a disturbing flickering next to my birthdate and I think I see the outlines of light arranging itself into various numbers. It is very unsettling to see the stone cycling through dates, almost as if to anticipate my eventual death date.

Suddenly I hear a strange shifting sound. I look around in a panic, half expecting to see the giant stone snake again. I see nothing and relax for a moment, only for the ground to start shaking.

To my horror the entire room feels like it's experiencing an earthquake. I try and steady myself but I fall down. As I look around the grave room I see a nightmarish sight. The older graves that are buried start to emerge. They are pushed by the tectonic force and thrust out of the ground. Just as they emerge the coffins burst open releasing a noxious fetor into the air. Then I see the dessicated bodies of my ancestors regarding me with skeletal grins.

To my horror they begin to move. I start forcing my terrified body to run, I have almost been killed by one undead ancestor this trip, I am not staying around to see what these ones want. I start running towards the far end of the room and the last few rows of gravesites. Fortunately they all seem new and empty. Though the sight unsettles me and I dimly consider what that lack of space implies for the future of my family.

I put the idea out of my mind and run. I reach a wall and see no obvious way out. There is no path, no door leading out, only more gravesites behind me and the shuffling sound of reanimated bodies moving closer. I look back and see the lumbering forms of my departed family slowing moving towards me, arms outstretched and with a hideous light glowing in their empty eye sockets.

I desperately search for some means of escape and eventually see something. It seems to be a strange hatch, almost like a cellar door. It was almost invisible, covered by dirt, with just the smallest bit of wood paneling visible. I frantically brush off the dirt and see a metal handle. I try to pull it open but it wont budge, I start stomping on it and I think I can feel something begin to buckle.

As I furiously stomp at the door I hear the guttural moans begin to take shape. They seem to be speaking to me through some horrible death rattle. Destroyed lungs and decayed mouths, impossibly form words that rattle me sanity,

“Release...us......you....only....you....can....set......us....free”

I can barely consider the meaning of their words over the sound of my own heart racing. They shamble closer and closer towards me. Then I hear a loud crack and see I finally managed to break part of the hatch. I target the splintered pieces and break several more off. I am almost through when I see a withered foot right near the broken hatch.

I look up and as soon as I look into the glowing orbs of the ghoulish figure I feel like my mind is slipping away. It is being flooded with horrible images of pain and desperation. The agony of slow death by dehydration and hunger make me feel like I am going mad. It feels as if everyone's memory of succumbing to the labyrinth presses into my mind in one instant.

Through the pain I hear one desperate message. I focus on the terrible dead voices and it becomes clearer,

“Free us!”

Suddenly the neck of the undead figure snaps back and I hear a shout that brings me to my senses.

“Get back!” I see a lean, almost emaciated figure with long hair. They are not amongst the dead, they are moving too quickly and seem to be striking down the horde of my dead family, or at least incapacitating them. The horrible death wail recedes and my mind no longer feels as if its under attack. Then the figure who helped, turns back to regard me. When I see her face I can barely believe it.

Its Lydia, she is alive! Before I can say anything she grabs my arm and pulls me through the fractured door. We fall down several feet into a must-covered tunnel. I stand up, and look above. None of the undead come tumbling down after us, so we must be safe, at least for now.

I look back at my sister, who I thought had died years ago and only manage to mumble the word,

“How?”

She regards me directly, no smile, no warmth just a rictus of focus, concentration and perhaps pain. She holds up a hand to silence me and responds in a raspy whisper,

“No time, you have to leave this place, what you are doing will.....”

She clutches her head and I see an odd flicker of light in her eyes, like something is going on. Then she seems to calm down and her temperament changes entirely.

She looks at me again and seems completely calm. She raises her finger to point toward one of the two directions the tunnel we were standing led towards. She spoke again, in an eerily calm and unnerving tone.

“The way is open, the Ouroboros revealed it, free us all. Open the way, the curse can be ended.” She stops speaking and stares blankly at me for a moment. I take a step toward her as she starts to sway. It looks like she might faint and fall down for a moment.

Instead she starts to scream out loud. She sounds like she is in pain and I reach out a hand to help, she slaps it away and I see a manic, almost deranged look in her eyes. She screams again and shouts,

"Get out!"

Then in the next moment she flees down the opposite direction she was just telling me to go.

I call out to her to wait, but she is too fast and as soon as I give chase, I stumble and fall down hard on my side. I sit up and by that time she has vanished down the tunnel.

I have no idea what is happening with Lydia. Something is wrong with her. I am grateful she is alive, but being here this long has changed her somehow. I can't even trust her advice, because what she had told me is contradictory. In one instance she urged me to leave and save myself, then just after, she tells me to press on to the hidden chamber and stop the curse.

I consider the way she spoke. The way she said, "Free us all" I know she would want everyone to be free from the curse, but she sounded....off. Worst of all, the way she said, "Get out" did not sound like a warning to me, it sounded like she was shouting it at someone, or something else.

I do not like my options now, but I know I have to decide if I should press on and find the hidden chamber or if I should follow Lydia and see what the hell is going on. I start to walk down the path she had said the chamber was, but after a few steps, doubt starts to gnaw at the back of my mind.

I know I might be making a mistake, but I have to be sure. I turn around and follow Lydia. I need to know what is going on and what she really knows about the chamber and what will happen if I really do, free us all.
