*The officer*, *whose lips were stained with rust, exited containment and started latching onto others' mouths.*

─*Security log of the Rusty Lung containment breach.*

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[Previous](https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/comments/1s7rncd/im_a_contract_cargoescort_driver_a_passenger/)

I raced back to my vehicle. I did not want to be fired.

Mere seconds after I arrived, the Representative appeared. Did it want to see if I would be late?

It cracked its jaw before it spoke. Moving it in a circular motion. Counter-clockwise. Clockwise. "Call this your last probationary delivery. Few of our other contractors ever made it past two deliveries. None have succeeded in this one." His words escaped through lips far too narrow. The thought of asking more about the failures was banished by the Representative's glare.

The rusted bond which tied us was sweeter than the emptiness of my housing.

"You will have a passenger as well as inanimate cargo. He is waiting in the back seat. The trailer has been changed. You are to drive to the same location in Gloves again. You are granted six hours to do so." Two more hours for the same distance.

I crawled into the driver's side. The rear-view mirror reflected an average man in a brown suit. Unlike everyone else I have met here, there was no bond that had formed. No chain or thread. I could not stop staring at him.

His lips were covered with rust.

I was consciously choking myself. For the cargo, I found some relief in the security of the trailer─but this man was in here.

The vehicle was made for this. I was not. The man's expression never changed.

Once on the main road, bumps and swerves dominated the drive. The Rusty Lung's file monopolized all of my thoughts. The Representative would gain nothing by sentencing drivers to a cruel death mindlessly. There had to have been a secret.

Suddenly, after thirty minutes of driving in silence, each muscle fiber in my face tensed. My eyes burned. The effort it took to keep my head straight pulled all of my joints up. The slime of my gums became grainy.

The man was now in the passenger seat.

I nearly crashed. He still had empty eyes staring straight in front of him. The GPS claimed I had ninety minutes left until arriving to the destination.

My mind was full of hurt I could not arrange. My hands and feet could not match my mind. I had pulled over without noticing.

I jumped out of the car and curled in the dirt. I screamed with a pitch I could not do before. Bugs and grass found themselves in my mouth. I had stopped blinking. Each muscle seized. My fingers tried to scratch the flesh off of my skull to pull my brain out.

I did not know how much time had passed until I regained control. I noted that the vehicle had not moved from where I had left it.

The man had returned to the back seat, looking as he always did.

I checked the time. I was tortured for two hours.

I buckled my seat belt and pressed on the gas. The threads that tugged between me and all I had spoken to at Hilltop directed me forward.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When I pulled into the destination, the man immediately exited and walked into the unloading dock. Employees came to take the trailer. Unlike the previous drop-offs, one decided to speak to me.

"What happened to you? You've dirt everywhere and look like you were stuffed into a barrel and rolled down a hill." I checked my reflection.

"Do you not know what the cargo and passenger are?" My words were messy and chopped.

"No, our team is strictly transfer. I'm not sure why that is. It seems inefficient, but the pay is good." I looked down at the creases in my palms. Sweat mixed with dirt.

"So... I am not the only one?"

"Things have always been secretive here. At least I have a team to vent with. Plenty of alcohol around to stop ourselves from thinking too hard." He hit the roof of my vehicle and walked away.

My pants were wet and malodorous.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

It was dark by the time I returned to the museum. I was the only one there.

I walked to my housing. The door resisted my force. As soon as I stepped inside, I was again slammed by the walls' ire. My veins pulsed. The road was less hostile.

The need to feel clean hid the worst of it.

I noticed the shower head was rusty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

I did not wait for the Representative's call the next morning─I went to the staff entrance of the museum. While it was never told to me, there had to be someone who coordinated my orders before telling the Representative.

There were so many people. I did not know it was necessary for museums to have so many staff members. It took me several tries to enter the crowd.

I made it to some kind of office secretary. It took him some time to notice me, as usual.

"Hi, can I help you?" Acknowledgement.

"Hi. I am a contract transport driver. I was wondering if there was a group that coordinated the objects and people I drive? I have not met them yet."

"Ah yes, I know of you. That's probably because you were probationary. Hm. But it looks like you're official now. It took us quite a long time to get someone firm like you." Appreciation.

He took his unnaturally clean phone and dialed. "Michael F. passed probation. Do you want to meet with him? OK, will do." He hung up and smiled at me. "Follow the signs labeled 'Export Department' down the hall and to the left."

"Thank you." I took note of his name placard. Sam C.

My shoes squeaked against the floor. My face flushed and neck tensed. I took slower steps after the first three squeaks. The floor itself was, unsurprisingly, immaculate. It looked like it was purchased brand-new every day. The lights above me all shone with the exact same brightness.

In following the signs to exports, I happened to walk by a strange office. The door was not post-modern like the rest of the staff floor. It looked almost like a barrel lid. The windows were tinted. I did not see any labels.

I happened upon the entrance the museum receptionist pointed me to when I asked for the Representative yesterday. His office was missing.

It occurred to me that I had not passed a single person in the halls. The thought was pushed aside by the receptionist of the exports wing greeting me. She had an unwelcoming expression, but nonetheless had initiated contact. With me.

"It's fortuitous you came by. The Representative was about to page you. We need you to drive our department manager to these coordinates, about eight hours away." She handed me a slip of paper. Our fingers brushed together briefly. While I was looking at the paper, I noticed her name placard. Sam C.

I shifted my focus back to the coordinates. "Why a set of coordinates rather than words?" Sam appeared impatient.

"Some things are better quantified." I began walking out to the location of my vehicle. My steps were lighter, and the floor was not squeaking.
