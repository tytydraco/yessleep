Hello everyone. I am Sarah Adams. It started a little while ago—about an hour and a half—when I was sitting on my couch, crying because my boyfriend and I broke up.

​After a few minutes of crying, my phone rang. I smiled, thinking he wanted to fix things. I wiped my tears and smiled, but it was a strange call, and the smile left my face. I answered, and before I could speak, a voice said: "Hello, Sarah."
​I said: "Hello."

He answered in a whisper: "Why?"

My expression turned to anger, as did my sad voice: "Why what? Listen, you scoundrel, if you’re joining those phone harassers, now is not the right time."

​Before he could respond, I hung up on him and blocked his number so he couldn't call again. I scrolled through the photo gallery; I was crying over the pictures with Ethan. Then, I received another call, but not on my mobile phone—it was the landline. I wiped my tears as I picked up the receiver.

​The same voice that had been talking said: "Hello, Sarah."

"You again," I said angrily. "You bastard. If you call again, I’ll call the police."

Before I could hang up, he said mysteriously in that same whisper: "I’m not harassing you, Sarah. I want to know why you are crying."

​I froze in my place. How? How did he know I was crying? "How did you know I was crying?" I asked, looking at the walls of the house in fear.

"It’s Ethan, right?"

A lump formed in my throat, and I spoke with difficulty: "How do you know it's Ethan? Where do you know Ethan from? Who are you?"

"Don't worry, Sarah. I’ll make sure he’s no longer a burden to you."

I stood up from my seat with exaggerated anger: "You son of a bitch, if anything happens to Ethan, I’ll call the police!"

He let out an evil laugh and said: "Not him. It’s you something will happen to."
I replied: "Hello? Hello?"

​My voice was coming out of the receiver as if both phones were in the same room. My hand trembled as I put the receiver down and looked around me. "Hey, you!" I said in a loud, broken voice throughout the living room. "Believe me, I have the police’s number."
The voice that made me spin toward its source came from right behind me: "I have it too, Sarah."

​The landline rang again. I grabbed the phone quickly: "You bastard, I will kill you!"
"What?" It was Ethan’s voice. "Sarah, are you okay?"

"Ethan... Ethan..." I said with relief. "There’s someone watching me. He wants to kill me."
"Really? Or are you just joking?" Ethan said with his usual coldness.

"I swear to you, Ethan, I’m not joking," I said, crying so he would believe me. "There’s a cursed bastard who wants to kill me."

"I know you’re not joking, Sarah." Ethan’s voice turned into the voice of that person. "For I am the killer."

"But to be a fair killer," he said, smiling with madness and distortion, "I’ll give you 72 hours. Three day, and you'll be out of the way..."

​I was crying and terrified as he continued: "You can use them; give money to the poor, pray in church... but I want to reassure you, this won't make me reconsider. I will kill you, Sarah."

​The sound of him hanging up made my heart stop for a second, then beat slowly. I threw the receiver down. I took my keys and my phone and left my house without even turning off the lights. I am writing now while driving, fast and in fear, heading toward Ethan’s house. I hope he can help me...
