I’m not going back to that forest.

I know that sounds dramatic, especially since the police went out there the next morning and came back telling me they didn’t find anything unusual, but I don’t care what they say. I don’t care that they told me my friends are fine, or that I should try to sleep and “process what happened.”

They weren’t there.

They didn’t hear the sounds.

And they didn’t see what those things did to people.



We went there to film something stupid.

Sven found the place online—some abandoned bunker hidden deep in a forest just outside town—and of course he thought it would be perfect for one of his videos. Nothing serious. Just the usual: walk around in the dark, scare each other, maybe catch something weird if we got lucky.

There were four of us: Sven and Iris, and me and Dana.

It was already dark when we arrived.

The forest didn’t feel wrong at first, not in any obvious way, but the deeper we went, the more it felt like the place was holding its breath. No wind, no insects, nothing moving except us. Even the sound of our footsteps seemed to die off too quickly, like it didn’t belong there.

We found the bunker sooner than expected.

Half buried. Concrete. The entrance just a low opening that swallowed the light.

Inside, it was worse.

Blankets on the floor. Burn marks where fires had been lit. Food wrappers that weren’t old enough to have been there for years.

And in the far corner—

bones.

Not scattered.

Stacked.

Long ones. Small ones. Some still dark in places like they hadn’t been picked clean all that long ago.

Dana didn’t say anything, but I saw the way she looked at them. Like she already knew we shouldn’t be there.

We left.

We should have left the forest completely.

We didn’t.



We saw the light deeper in.

At first it felt like relief—someone else out there, something normal—but that feeling didn’t last long. There were people standing around the fire, maybe six or seven of them, all wearing long, dark cloaks that hid their faces.

They weren’t talking.

They were chanting.

Quiet. Repetitive. The same words over and over again in a rhythm that made the air feel tight.

Dana told me we needed to go.

Sven told us to get closer.

And then he stepped on a branch.

The crack cut through everything.

The chanting stopped instantly.

Every single one of them went silent at the same time, their heads turning—not toward us—but past us, into the trees behind where we were standing.

And then they started to panic.

Not confused. Not angry.

Panicking.

One of them dropped to their knees. Another started backing away from the fire like they were already too late.

I heard one of them say it.

“They’re coming.”



The forest answered.

The wind hit all at once, but it didn’t feel like wind. It moved through the trees in a straight line, like something forcing its way forward. Branches snapped overhead, and then the sounds started—fast, erratic movement circling us from every direction.

Something slammed into one of the cloaked figures and dragged them sideways across the ground.

They didn’t even get a full scream out.

Their body hit a tree with a dull, wet crack before disappearing into the dark between the trunks.

Then another one went down.

This time I saw more of it.

Low to the ground. Moving too fast to follow. It hit the back of someone’s legs and folded them instantly, dropping them face-first into the dirt before something yanked them backward so hard their arms snapped straight out in front of them.

The sound that came out of them didn’t sound human anymore.

After that, everything broke.

They were everywhere.

We ran.



Sven didn’t make it far.

Something moved across the ground behind us and hit him full force, knocking him forward so hard he slid across the dirt. He tried to push himself up, but something grabbed onto him—low, tight—and dragged him backward.

His fingers dug into the ground, leaving deep lines as he tried to hold on.

For a second, I thought he might.

Then something pulled harder.

His grip broke.

And he was gone.



We kept running.

Iris was right behind us, I could hear her breathing, fast and uneven, and then there was a sound above us—branches shifting, something heavy moving through them.

I barely had time to look up.

Something dropped.

It hit her from above, hard enough to drive her into the ground. The impact knocked the air out of her completely, and before she could even scream, something wrapped around her and lifted.

Not clean.

Not smooth.

Her body caught on the branches as she was dragged upward, her legs hitting, twisting, bending in ways they shouldn’t before disappearing into the dark canopy above us.

The last sound she made was cut short.



It was just me and Dana after that.

I don’t remember how we found the bunker again.

I just remember the feeling of concrete under my hands as we stumbled inside.

And for a few seconds…

it felt safe.

Nothing came in.

We could still hear movement outside, things passing by, something brushing against the edge of the entrance—but nothing crossed into the bunker.

Dana kept saying we were okay.

I didn’t believe her.

But I didn’t say anything.



Then everything stopped.

The movement outside.

The sounds.

All of it.

The forest went completely silent.

And then something blocked the entrance.

Not fully.

Just enough to change the light.

It didn’t rush.

It didn’t make a sound.

It just moved closer, slow and heavy, until part of it pushed into the bunker.

I couldn’t make out what it was.

The light didn’t reflect off it properly. It just… disappeared into it.

Then it reached in.



It took Dana.

Not like the others.

Not fast.

It closed around her middle and lifted her off the ground like she weighed nothing. The pressure hit all at once—her body folding slightly around whatever had her—and the sound that came out of her was short, sharp, cut off before it could become a scream.

I grabbed her.

I tried to pull her back.

My hands slipped.

Everything felt wet.

It pulled.

Slow.

Unstoppable.

For a second, I was still holding onto her arm.

Then I wasn’t.



After that, I ran.

I didn’t think, I didn’t look back, I just ran until I reached the car and got out of there.



I went to the police.

They went back the next morning.

They told me they found all three of them.

Alive.

At home.

Like nothing had happened.



I didn’t argue.

I didn’t go back.



I checked the footage later.

From inside the bunker.

There’s something in it.

Behind us.

In the dark.

It was already inside with us.



And when I watched it again last night…

I noticed something I didn’t see before.



It moves.



And now I don’t know if it ever stayed in that forest.
