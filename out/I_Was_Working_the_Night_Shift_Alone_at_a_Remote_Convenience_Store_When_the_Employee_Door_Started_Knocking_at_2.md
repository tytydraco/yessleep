I used to work the night shift at a tiny convenience store on the outskirts of town back in late 2022.

The place sat right beside an old highway surrounded by forest. No gas station nearby, no houses, nothing. Just the road, the trees, and our flickering OPEN sign.

Most nights were dead silent.

Around 2 AM, I was usually alone.

That night started normally.

A few customers came in before midnight. Some truck drivers. A tired couple. Nothing weird.

At around 1:47 AM, the power went out for about three seconds.

Everything shut off.

The refrigerators.  
The lights.  
Even the buzzing sound from the neon signs disappeared.

Then it all came back.

I remember laughing nervously because the silence felt… wrong.

That’s when I noticed the man outside.

He was standing near the edge of the parking lot.

Completely still.

I couldn’t see his face because of the darkness, but he looked tall. Very tall. Hoodie, hands in pockets.

I figured he was just waiting for someone.

But after around ten minutes, he still hadn’t moved.

Not even once.

I tried ignoring him.

Then my phone buzzed.

It was a message from my coworker Tyler.

>

I texted:

>

He replied almost instantly.

>

Before I could answer, another customer walked in.

An older woman.

She looked nervous.

The second she entered, she quietly asked:

>

I turned toward the parking lot.

He was gone.

I told her I didn’t see anyone anymore.

She just nodded slowly, bought a bottle of water, then left quickly without even taking her change.

At this point I was uncomfortable, but not terrified.

Not yet.

Around 2:26 AM, I heard knocking on the side employee entrance.

Not the front door.

The employee door.

Three slow knocks.

I froze.

Nobody should’ve been back there.

I checked the security camera monitor.

Nothing.

Just static for half a second.

Then the camera came back.

And for one frame…

I saw someone standing directly in front of the camera.

Too close.

Like their face was pressed against the lens.

I nearly fell out of my chair.

I grabbed the store phone and called Tyler.

No answer.

That’s when the knocking started again.

Slower this time.

*Knock.*  
*Knock.*  
*Knock.*

I locked the register and stayed behind the counter pretending nobody was inside.

Then I heard something that made my stomach drop.

Footsteps.

Inside the store.

Slow footsteps moving between the aisles.

But I never heard the front door open.

I looked up carefully.

Nobody.

The aisles were empty.

Then aisle 4 motion sensor turned on by itself.

The light flicked on.

I could see the end of the aisle from the counter.

Empty.

But something felt wrong.

I don’t know why, but I slowly walked closer.

And I noticed the shelves on both sides were shaking slightly.

Like somebody had just brushed past them.

I whispered:

>

No response.

Then I heard breathing.

Right beside me.

I turned instantly.

Nothing.

But the breathing continued.

I backed away toward the counter.

That’s when the store speakers crackled.

At first I thought it was broken audio.

Then I realized it was whispering.

Very quiet.

Almost impossible to understand.

Until it suddenly became clear.

>

I ran.

I didn’t even grab my bag.

I sprinted out the front entrance into the parking lot.

And standing near my car…

was the tall man.

Except now I could see his face.

Or at least I think I did.

Something was wrong with it.

His eyes looked too dark.  
His smile too wide.

Like someone trying to imitate a human expression without understanding it.

I got in my car and locked the doors.

He walked toward the window slowly.

Not aggressively.

Almost calmly.

Then he tapped the glass once.

And said:

>

The police searched the store later that night.

They found nothing.

No fingerprints.  
No forced entry.

But they did find something strange.

The security footage from aisle 4 was corrupted.

Except for one frame.

One single frame showing me standing alone in the aisle.

Looking directly at someone standing inches away from my face.

Something the camera somehow captured…

but I never saw.
