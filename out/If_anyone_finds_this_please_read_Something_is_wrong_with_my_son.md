I don’t know where else to put this. I haven’t slept. I’m writing everything down exactly as it’s happening in case something happens to me.
If this sounds crazy, I understand. I thought the same thing an hour ago.

[6:03 AM]
It was a bright, sunny Monday morning. The kind that makes everything feel normal, safe… predictable.
I got up to make my usual coffee, filled the kettle, and set it to boil. The hum of it filled the kitchen. While I waited, I went to wake my son, Jake.
Just like I do every day.
But something felt… off.
I can’t explain it. Just a tight feeling in my stomach. Like something was wrong.
I knocked on his door.
“Sweetie, wake up. It’s time for school.”
No answer.
I figured I’d let him sleep a bit longer.

[7:02 AM]
I went back.
Knocked again.
“Jake, honey, you’ll miss the bus.”
Still nothing.
I tried the handle.
Unlocked.
That’s not normal. He always locks it. He’s scared of the dark. Says locking the door “keeps it out.”
I don’t know what it is.
I didn’t ask.
I wish I had.

[7:04 AM]
He’s in bed.
He looks like he’s sleeping.
But he won’t wake up.
I tried shaking him. Calling his name. Nothing.
Then I heard him.
Not out loud.
In my head.
“Mummy… I’m stuck.”
I know how that sounds. I know.
But it was his voice.

[7:06 AM]
He says it’s dark.
He says he can’t move.
He says something is watching him.
I told him he was in his bed. That he just needed to wake up.
He started crying.
He Said, “It knows you’re there.”
The way he said it… it didn’t sound like him.

[7:08 AM]
His eyes are open now.
He’s staring at me.
He hasn’t blinked once.
“Mummy,” it said.
But his mouth didn’t move.

[7:10 AM]
I’m not alone in this room.
I know that now.
Jake is still talking to me in my head.
Crying.
Begging me to help him.
The thing on the bed is talking too.
Telling me that’s not really him.
I don’t know which one is my son anymore.

[7:12 AM]
Jake says he’s still in his room.
But everything is wrong.
The walls move.
The door won’t stay in place.
He says something gets closer every time he closes his eyes.
I don’t know what to do.
The thing just said:
“You opened the door.”

[7:13 AM]
Jake screamed.
He said it was right next to him.
Then it went quiet.
Too quiet.
Now he’s whispering.
He says he can see me.
But I’m not where I think I am.

[7:14 AM]
He says I’m in the bed.
That I’m not moving.
That I’m not waking up.
Something just grabbed my arm.
I didn’t see it move.
It’s holding me.

[7:15 AM]
I can’t move.
Everything just… flickered.
Like the lights went out for a second.
Now I’m lying down.
I can see the ceiling.
Jake’s ceiling.

[7:16 AM]
I can see the door.
I can see… me.
Standing there.
Watching.
The thing is standing now.
It’s looking at my body.
It just smiled.

[7:17 AM]
It just spoke.
In my voice.

If you’re reading this, I didn’t make it out.
And I think it’s going to need a new voice next.
