I was sent to my village for the holidays. There, at 3 AM, I saw a 'Barat' (wedding procession) that no one was ever supposed to see. I will die, but I will never go back to that village again. What I saw with my own eyes was not a ghost, but it was a thousand times more terrifying and disgusting than any ghost.

Since my holidays were going on, my family sent me to the village. The weather was good, and everything was going fine. But on the third day, I noticed the village elders sitting together and whispering about some 'Barat'. I asked my uncle if there was a wedding in the village.

My uncle’s face instantly turned pale. He scolded me angrily and said, "Mind your own business. And remember, no matter what happens tonight, do not open your bedroom window and do not look outside."

Night came. Around 2:30 AM, a strange, suffocating silence covered the entire village. No dogs were barking, and there was no sound of crickets.

Then, I heard the sound of drums from a distance. But it was not a normal wedding drum. Its heavy thumping sound was slow, strange, and scary. To the beat of that drum, some people were singing a strange song, and I could not understand the words.

My psychological fear started growing. I could not control myself. Forgetting my uncle's warning, I opened a tiny crack in my wooden window and looked outside.

A procession was moving down the dark, muddy road of the village. Torches were burning. What I saw made me want to scream, but I quickly slapped my hand over my mouth.

It was a naked procession.

All the men and women walking in it were completely naked. There was not a single piece of cloth on their bodies. Their entire bodies were covered in dark, wet field mud and ashes.

They were not walking like normal human beings. They were dancing to that strange drum beat, jerking their necks, hands, and legs in unnatural ways, as if they had no control over their own bodies. They were just dancing with madness in their own rhythm.

At the very front, a horse was walking. A groom was sitting on that horse. That groom was also completely naked, and he was wearing a necklace of human bones. But when the horse passed right in front of my house and the torchlight fell on the groom’s face, the ground slipped from under my feet.

That groom was not a living person. It was a corpse tied to the horse with ropes. It was the dead body of a young guy from my own village, who was found dead in the fields just two days ago.

My mind went completely numb. That entire naked procession went ahead into the street, laughing strangely behind the dead body. Their attention was not on me; they were just lost in their terrifying ritual and slowly moved toward the fields.

Terrified, I closed the window and fell onto my bed. I shivered with fear the entire night.

The next morning, I came back to my city. Everything is normal here now, but now I know that such strange and terrifying things actually exist in this world.
