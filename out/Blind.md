The park was empty; not even birds live there. The area was mostly flat aside from the oak trees. The oak trees were tall but the wood still looked young. The sidewalk was run down. The trees were slowly overtaking the once large sidewalk. The cracks were massive and slowly grew with time. It was almost fully quiet. What little noise there was got drowned out by the screaming wind. It was too hard to make out. It was almost impossible to even hear but it was still there. 

There I stood on the sidewalk listening to the quiet sound. Once I was able to finally tell what it was, my heart stopped. It was someone else's voice. I screamed out, “Is anyone there?” I heard nothing back. I screamed out a second time, “Is anyone there?”  Again no response. The voice then turned into quiet whispering. I started running immediately. I did not know why it was probably just a prank or just some sort of animal. After only a few seconds of running I sat down on a big rock almost as if it were a chair. 

I looked over my shoulder and laughed it off. My laugh felt hollow, almost like what a dead person's laugh would sound like. I took my phone out and started listening to music. I couldn’t remember a single song, the music was very forgettable. After a few minutes of that I turned my phone off and let everything get quiet. 

There was next to no noise but it still did not feel quiet. It was nothing but it just felt like something was off. I always enjoy the quiet. That is why I have always enjoyed coming here. After a moment looking at the sun going down trying to act like everything was normal, I slowly resumed my walk.

Everything was normal at first but then I saw it. On the side of the broken sidewalk was some sort of dead animal. It did not look like any animal I have seen before. 

Its skin was pale. The eyes were tiny compared to the creature's insane size. It was about ten feet tall and just two feet across. Its arms were long even when compared to the rest of its body. Its head was strange. The creature's ears were tiny; it was hard to tell that they were even there. It did not have a nose, just faded marks where it should be. Its legs were long and skinny. The thing had weird marks all across its skin. It was hard to describe but it looked like blue areas of the skin but still pale. Its mouth was close shut. For a second I did not think the thing had one because of how well its face bled together. 

After a moment of shock I started backing up. I could take a picture with my phone but that felt wrong almost as if it was wrong to even know what that thing is let alone look at it. I finally turned around to open the park gate. The park gate was big and wide. It was made of rusted metal. On the top was a large sign. It read as follows: “Welcome to Everwort Park.” I always hated that name. 

My thoughts got interrupted with the faint sound of the whispers returning. I immediately left the park and the sound stopped as soon as I left. Everything seemed to be normal again but it did not feel normal. 

As I entered the sidewalk that led back to my apartment I heard the same weird noises as I did at the park. I froze in my place as I heard it get louder and louder. This time I was able to make out what the voice was saying, “Don’t fear the infinite.” The words were quiet but loud in my mind. 

I turned around to see if I was being watched. I saw no one, the whispers didn't stop, it only continued the same phrase being repeated, “Don’t fear the infinite.” I started running and the message only got louder: “Don’t fear the infinite.” My heart only beat louder and my hand began to shake as I heard another voice. It spoke slowly and softly, “David Woodblock.” It knew my name. 

Before I could respond I fell to my knees. I immediately looked down to see a rock by my foot. As I stood up the whispering stopped and a different sound appeared, a loud ringing sound. I slowly turned around to see nothing. The ringing sound stopped. I just stood there in silence waiting for the ringing to return. I did nothing; I could not think, could not fear, just stood there waiting for what was coming next. After what felt like an hour, I finally unfrozen and started to think. I knew I was standing in the same spot for an hour but I could not recall a single thought. 

I then began walking again and the ringing returned, even louder this time. I stopped again after just walking a few feet. The sound stopped the moment I turned around. My hands began to shake as I just stood there in the dark. After a moment in panic I started walking backwards. The sound did not return, at least not at first. A few minutes later it returned. It was quiet this time but still felt more intense almost as if the intentions changed. 

I started speeding up as it grew louder. It continued to get louder as I walked backwards. I then stop dead in my tracks. This time it didn't stop, it only grew louder. The sound felt violence. My ears started to physically hurt from the sound. I felt a hint of blood flow out my head from my left ear. I then started to look straight in front of me. It did not stop. I then turned to my side. That is when it finally stopped. 

There was nothing there but an old street light. I turned around again and the noise came back. Then I looked back and it stopped again. I then started walking while still looking in that direction. It stayed quiet. My eyes were the key. It could not follow me as long as I looked at it. I can’t see anything but I know it there. It is trying to find me and I know how to stop it. 

I then started to run the final stretch to my apartment while trying to look in that direction. I never ran so fast in my life. I could feel the sweat drip down my face. After about 20 seconds of that the noise returned quiet again. I stopped running and I immediately turned behind me and began to look around. 

After looking in around the same direction as last time the noise stopped once more. I was just a few blocks away from my apartment. I slowly took a step towards my apartment while still looking over there my legs were shaking with fear. I then repeated that process again and again. I could not let whatever that thing is catch up to me. When I finally made it to my apartment I immediately opened the door and ran inside. 

The ringing did not return. A small smile crossed my face as I ran towards the elevator. I quickly pressed the button. After what felt like an hour the door opened. I just slowly went inside as the ringing returned. It was quite, barely noticeable but still there. I scream as the elevator closes. I heard nothing back other than the sound growing louder. 

Then things went quiet as the elevator began to move up. I heard nothing other than my own breathing. The pain in my ears still refuses to stop. I just stood there in silence. I blacked out doing the rest of the ride up. All I remember was the elevator door opening back up again.

I ran into my apartment without thinking. It was small, a tiny kitchen in the center and a small bathroom and room to sleep in. The apartment was all I had to hide in. I immediately grabbed my chair out from the kitchen and put it against the door. After that I grabbed a bunch of books I had left on my desk. Then I grabbed the entire desk.

That's when the ringing came back. This time it did not wait to get loud and violent. I looked down to the floor to see the blood flowing out my ears, turning the floor blood red. The ringing then immediately got quiet as fast as it came back. I started walking backwards, still looking at the same spot.

After a short moment of walking down the hall I heard a loud bang by my door or at least I think it was loud, it was becoming hard to tell with my bloody ears. It sounded more like a scream than anything. The door then slowly opened as the ringing came back. All the stuff that blocked the door was violently knocked down like it was never there. 

On the other side was the dead body of my neighbor. His eyes were ripped out of his skull. The rest of his body was untouched. The insides of his head was visible from the hole that was once his eye. His brain was also untouched. It was only his eyes. That thing had eaten his eyes. I had no time to process that as the ringing stopped as I heard more screaming down the hall. 

I immediately ran back to my room as everything went quiet. I then stopped dead in my tracks as I saw that the window was open. I kept looking over as I tried to slowly walk out. I then heard a thud behind me and began to turn around. Behind me was another one of my neighbors dead. 

Her eyes were ripped out of her skull as well, but this time there was more blood coming out the hole. Then the ringing stopped and the voice returned. This time the voice was clear it was barely above a whisper. It sounded smooth almost as if it came from a human voice. It simply said, “Don’t fear the infinite.” Every single door opened as soon as the voice stopped. The dead bodies of the rest of my neighbors fell out of each room one by one. All of their eyes were missing.

This is just what I can share so far but I promise you my town has had much more mess up thing then this. This is only one piece of a puzzle, a puzzle that left better off unfinished.
