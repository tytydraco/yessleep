I learned that the trees have souls

and just like ours some souls have holes

widening roots have a heavy toll

when I came here to leave the race

I rarely sought another face

Instead I found a line to pace

The nearby fields and lakes and wood

where few others have trod and stood

my own haven for thought and brood

so now on daily out I venture

through vibrant lake and verdant pasture

to seek my own absentee rapture

when I pass the cows just graze

all the sheep and lambs don't gaze

and the wolves stay past the furthest haze

but the further in I go

older and deeper the things that grow

ever greening the midday glow

when the path becomes too thin

or I feel a fading in my grin

I load my heel and make a spin

but today when the weeds start warding

and the vine rivers too thick for fording

I sense a gap in the green that's hoarding

the space in my eyes is cut by red

it tugs my heart and hope and dread

from forest to clearing I have fled

and when I regain my sense

my eyes alight but head is dense

it takes time but I focus my lens

I see the tree

I see the monarch of trees

I see the Traitor Tree

a hundred times of my height and my breadth

crimson defining of blood and of depth

to avert my gaze would take my life and my death

this tree stands truly alone

leaf of fire and branches of bone

carving out it's lonely throne

no other green approaches the royalty

and I rest low to be certain of loyalty

knees are after all crafted for fealty

here in the clear is where I make rest

uncertain if this is a reward or a test

here the wind and sky are sweetly blessed

yet under the gentle breezing flow

there is a fading in the glow

was the sun really already that low

this is when I start to feel

the need to run and not to kneel

why is there flesh where bark has peeled

why do the branches bend and grasp

why do the twigs become hands in a clasp

why are there faces locked in a gasp

why is it surrounded but by dirt and spores

beast skulls and bones counting in scores

why do my eyes keep wanting more

now the wind whispers and lies

hell is to leave and to leave is to die

climb up the tree and I'll let you fly

I tear away my soul and myself

and leave this tree on a memory shelf

I tap some swift now and hope later for health

I fight out back to the fields

begging for speed without any yields

and yet when I saw it I reeled

across the path a poor beastly Bull

who seems to have bursted from being so full

and all the gore through the fence pulled

yet what sort of wolf would make such a kill

this wasn't for food but only for thrill

fear in me races while the body does still

and on either side of my barbed way

the cows stare as if I were fresh hay

why don't they flee why would they stay

then I spot almost a man through the mist

now so thick that I nearly missed

how a hoof turns to hand and hand into fist

some deeper mind knows to recoil

that's not a farmer at work in the toil

its neck is too long and its limbs are all coiled

then all the close cows shed their four legs

these are the things that don't hunt for dregs

no time to weep and no point to beg

my scream comes all too quiet

heart wants surrender but no flag to fly it

I need to flee but my legs won't try it

I tear at my feet and again and now thrice

crush down all my fear in a vice

terror has weight but sloth has a price

I force a sprint back up the path

lungs suffering of my legs wrath

snapping out a greenery swath

I am heard, and they are herder

pushing me into forest further

now I'm a home and safe deserter

I pause at the wicked trees clearing

and hear the trampling of nearing

never thinking above my fearing

so when not knowing either devil

I choose what seems the lesser evil

the tree will grant a lighter level

I scamper up the lumber ladder

but that isn't the sounds of leaves that scatter

in my fear and shame I loose my bladder

up I tear and claw and scramble

blood pours from me or the bramble

the beasts into the clearing trample

and from the wooded perch

the spire of a deadwood church

I find a truth that makes my guts lurch

the ringed sentinel trees form a shield

around this scar that never was healed

and which sought more than just leaves to wield

I am but a hare in a trap

The man things form a circling wrap

as the boughs all bend and snap

they just stare with mouths agape

and branches shear through their too-tall napes

how could I think this would grant an escape

I turn to look at what I fear to see

the faces in the traitor tree

their eyes are open just for me

In each eye a hollow mourning

mouths reaching for silent warning

the dread of my plight dawning

die a man or live a thrall

My only choice is whether to fall

I ready a step but then I stall

am I steeped enough for my death

I still find a sweetness in breath

only this choice then no choices left

is this meant for my final grove

no more woods to love and rove

... I should have dove

the creeping twigs find me

branches bind me

vines climb my eyes and blind me

I don't sleep and then I wake

a puppet made of vine and stake

which my struggles could never break

I have new strings and ties on me

find me cut me so I may flee

my lumber loom from the traitor tree

the wood won't rot and the strings won't fray

just so long as I must stay

my blood my bark will not decay

this elder tree is a vile traitor

a seed and sapling excavator

banished now until all later

I feel still the seeds and young

crying out for dad and mum

never to live or come undone

the good trees pass through branch and root

and live beyond their ash and soot

the forest bond is absolute

but the traitor tree demanded more

choking out the forest floor

until its siblings closed the door

the trickster tree must now take people

to build and bolster its hell bound steeple

from the paths it will greedily pull

how many others before me and us

We are its roots its branch its forest

and no green thing will join our chorus

I beg pray plead to find me burn me

a release from this rootwork apogee

release us from the Traitor Tree
