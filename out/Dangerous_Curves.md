After spending a child hood of being traded from one house to another while the families occupying them used my presence as an excuse for extra government assistance that eventually lead to a young adulthood borrowing dingy alleys and cold park benches to sleep and self medicate on I was rather exceptional at being alone. Spending 8 months in a county jail for a possession charge allowed me to finally decided that an apartment and a job could do me some good since the dirty needles and brown paper bags didn’t. I spent 6 weeks getting my CDL before getting a job with the help of my parole officer driving water trucks for a local oil company.

“Beginners always start on overnights, they don’t let you work days until they’re sure you’ll stay for a while.”

The words from a grizzled overweight man with large hairy forearms and a gut that sticks a little too far out the bottom of his fire retardant shirt echoing in my mind. David was a large man not just in weight but also in height, he towered over me, his salt and pepper hair was a stark contrast to the Irish orange color of his goatee. Though he had a beer gut he was also obviously strong. Like a father. At least like the so called fathers that used to hold me down or throw me across the room on a whim. He was also going to be the guy I ride with for my first week before I was able to drive on my own, his words meant little to me at that moment as my body hadn’t yet acclimated to my new vampiric sleep schedule. Large dark bags rested just below my eyes and a nearly empty coffee thermos remained glued to my hand given to me by Dave saying.

“Here son, I have a spare you’ll need it” -as he handed it to me

From the mirrored bags under his eyes and coffee thermos glued to his hand I deduced that nobody acclimates to the sleep schedule, at least not easily. We went over a pre-trip check and climbed into the cab of the truck. The drive was slow at first it was early evening around 7pm so there were still people out and we had at least an hours drive until we got to the first pad.

“There’s a few rules all us drivers abide by to stay safe especially at night. Don’t drive to a pad on your own, most of these oil pumps are out of cell range and if you break down or wreck you’re gonna need a way back. That being said we only take three breaks a night, one lunch and two refuels. So take your piss and grab your snacks then. Lastly don’t drive in silence. Not at night.”

His rules all made sense the last thing any one needed was to end up flipped over in a ditch or worse be the reason someone else is. We worked for a one off oil company that mostly did gas extraction in the Appalachian mountains right on the border of WV and OH. It was a common rule of thumb to avoid highways and interstates at all costs due to our trucks usually being over weight and the DOT doesn’t typically set up weigh stations on one off backroads. I nodded at his words, my eyelids heavy with sleep as my body relaxed against the passenger seat. I took a big swig of my bitter black coffee and sat up straight trying to fight off a nap. Suddenly blue and red lights lit up the side mirrors as Dave blew through a red light. I noticed he didn’t even slow down. In fact he sped up, I spoke up trying to get his attention.

“Hey…. I think we’re being pulled over.”

My words seemed to fall on deaf ears, so I turned to look and Dave just kept facing the road. I began to say something again about the cop on our tail but out of the corner of my eye I noticed the lights were gone and the only thing behind us were a distant set of headlights from a second tanker following us to the pad. I stared into the mirror, inching my face closer to the glass as if it was an optical illusion and if I got close enough the cruiser would suddenly reappear behind us. It never did. The hair on my arms and the back of my neck stood on end. My heart began to race as my pulse drummed in my ears I turned back to Dave and in a tone that demanded an explanation I said

“What the fuck was—”

My stomach sunk to the floor and my breath hitched in my throat cutting myself off as my mind slowly unraveled what I was looking at. Dave’s body was slumped back against the seat his hands clawing at his own eyes. He pushed his fingers into the sockets and leveraged each eyeball out letting it dangle from the optic nerve. Then he wrapped his hands around each eyeball plucking it from the nerve like some forbidden fruit and tossed them both into his gaping maw that occupied too much real estate on his face with rows upon rows of jagged yellow teeth. He clamped down on the eye balls with a quickness and ferocity of a bear trap causing them to pop and leak down his chin. His lips pulled tight at the corners creating a smile that seemed too big for his face. I couldn’t move. I couldn’t breathe. I couldn’t speak. As my mind tried to decipher the show of pure horror that befell my very own eyes all I could do is watch with my own mouth agape as my body refused to move. My mind doing Olympic level gymnastics trying to rationalize what was happening in front of me. 

Then Dave’s jaws separate once again, his skull hinging backwards loading like a spring as his bottom rows of teeth stayed perfectly still. Then faster than I could perceive they were wrapped around my forearm, a force harder enough to snap bone. Finally I screamed half in terror half in pain. Then I awoke Dave’s vice like grip was his hand and not his jaws as he shook me awake. Dave flashed a light in my eyes, and I saw a blue and red neon “open” sign lit up in the window of a gas station right outside my door. My entire body tensed up as the warm blinding light of the flashlight passed my eyes again, causing me to instinctively shield my eyes with my hands.

“Hey what the hell man”

My words were sharp and left my mouth with an evil bite. I immediately regretted, I pulled my arm away and tucked it close to me as if his hands were hot embers.

“Listen kid, I’m gonna let the sleeping slide for tonight on account that you’re new and all. but if I catch you drifting off even once tomorrow night I’m strapping you to the hood. We’re at our first stop for the night, go refill your coffee, take a piss and meet me back here in 10 minutes.”

Then he put the flashlight into the pocket along the inside of the trucks door and clicking it off before stepping out of the truck and slamming the door shut behind him. I sat there for a few moments staring out into the inky blackness that engulfed everything around us. It was suffocating. Even in the hard times when I would bounce from park bench to park bench and use discarded classifieds as makeshift bedding in damp dingy alleys I at least still had the city lights to keep me company. Out here on tree canopied back roads that ribboned from hilltop to hilltop there was no light. Nothing to illuminate the all consuming darkness that was always just a few feet away. 

I stepped out of the truck closing the door behind me and walked into the gas station. The buzzing lights grounding me, letting me know that Dave’s jagged teeth and vice like grip were all part of an all too common phenomenon I’ve learned to live with. The clerk behind the counter gave me a half hearted grin but her eyes conveyed the absolute exhaustion I had seen on everyone else’s face since I clocked in. I walked over to the coffee pots and refilled my thermos. Then my thrift store work boots clicked against the linoleum floor as I I approached the cashier. The bitter sweet aroma of the dark brown liquid sloshed around in my cup as I set it on the counter and pulled out my wallet.

“Is this it honey? We got a sale on beef jerky. I know how much you truck drivers like your snacks” -she said with a light humorless chuckle.

“This and a pack of Marb reds please” -I retorted. 

This was the part of normal life I hated the most. The simple monotony of kind gestures between plain strangers. The extreme highs and lows of addiction made the normal day to day seem bleak. I smiled kindly with my lips as my eyes remained dead, then I paid before leaving back out the door I came in. I pulled out the pack of cigarettes putting one in my mouth and lit it. Leaned against the grimy brick wall I took a long well deserved drag of my cig. Then without reason the hair on the back of my neck raised as I scanned the tree line across the empty road hoping to find it was just my mind playing tricks on me. Some residual fear from the nightmare I just experience a few minutes ago. I was wrong, there was a break in the typical oak and cedar trees. A small clearing surrounded by evergreens, and in the center of that clearing stood Albert Hockney.

Albert Hockney was the name of one of my many foster parents he was just as cruel as all the others but in a different way. Most of the parents would neglect or emotionally abuse us since it left the least visible bruises and scars on the off chance someone from CPS showed up. Yelling at you and denying you meals for days on end didn’t leave physical marks… just mental ones. Albert was different, he lived with a mostly indifferent woman I always assumed to be his wife in a secluded cabin just outside the city. No visible neighbors meant there was no one to call the authorities if something seemed off. I was the oldest thirteen at the time of the five foster kids who stayed there during my stent. I had been in the system since birth for reasons that were never given to me, so I knew how to survive. Being the oldest meant it was my responsibility to teach the younger kids how to as well. Albert and his wife had two kids of their own, they always referred to us as “pigs” because we laid in the barn at night.

The foster kids would best be described as the Hockney’s help, we often cleaned the house and cooked meals all while the family sat and reveled at their tv all hours of the day. At the slightest hint of insubordination Albert would grab a leg from an old discarded dining room chair and tap it against the floor, like a warning. He named the wooden torture device Big John (Albert was a Jimmy Dean fan). The tapping of Big John was your only warning, if you didn’t fix your ways he’d leap out of the stained recliner he spent most hours of the day and night in. Before you had the chance to hear him move he’d have one hand holding you against the nearest surface and the other swinging that dreaded hunk of wood in your direction. 

I never made a sound not a cry or the faintest whimper would escape my lips. I’d already run out of tears from years before, some of the younger kids did. They were too young and less emotionally jaded than I was. I never forgot those deep set dark brown eyes, so when my eyes met his gaze from across the empty desolate road I froze. I pulled my cigarette from my mouth and dropped it to the ground. Albert hated cigarette smoke. My chest heaved up and down as I lost control of my breathy and it became rapid. My thermos fell from my other hand making a clanking sound against the rough concrete. Albert raised Big John resting it on his shoulder as he began taking large steps towards me. His steps became a jog, then a sprint. I squeezed my eyes shut, my heart nearly pounding out of my chest. I could hear the loud stomps of his dirty boots hitting the asphalt, then the concrete of the gas station parking lot. They were so loud, and at any moment he would be on top of me striking down as hard as he could. A strong hand clapped against my shoulder and for a moment I thought my heart stopped. 

“Hey man you okay?” -Dave asked, his words breaking me free of my trance like state.

“You okay? There’s nothing out there”

I opened my eyes and saw a dimly lit parking lot and a wall of oak and cedar trees holding back an all consuming darkness from the forest just across the road. At some point I crouched down holding my knees to my chest. After seeing that Albert was no longer here I straightened up and calmed my breathing.

“Yea… I’m good. I just thought I saw something.” I said as my hands shakily picked up my discarded thermos, most of my coffee poured out onto the ground.

“The hills will do that to ya, that’s why we drive in pairs now. You’ll see things that don’t make sense, things that will shake you to your core” 

Dave’s tone has changed from a man just working to live, now he sounded like a man who’s just barely surviving. The tone of a man who’s seen things that couldn’t accurately be described in words and only live in the deep recesses of his mind. We walked towards the truck together and climbed in, I didn’t bother refilling my thermos again. Between the nightmare and my vivid hallucinations I won’t be sleeping anytime soon. Dave continued rambling on about his many years driving and how in the good ole’days it used to be a lot easier to go missing so if something happened it would often get swept under the rug. We sat in the cab of the truck for another five minutes waiting on Jordan the driver of the other truck in our three man caravan. Once we saw him get in and the truck rumble to life we pulled back out onto the road and continued towards our first off load for the night. Realizing I was fully awake and had no plans of falling back to sleep he explained the day to day monotony of the job to me.

“It’s pretty simple kid, you get your truck from the shop, drive to your pad to fill the tanker and drive to the designated disposal point for that night. Rinse and repeat until 6 am.” 

Though what he was saying seemed important, I couldn’t manage not thinking about what happened at the gas station. He looked so real, sounded so real. Even though I wasn’t satisfied with Dave’s explanation of the hills will do that to ya it really dug at me. I spent the next hour and a half picking at a frayed thread in my memory I’ve never hallucinated like that, it bothered me. Then in an effort to push the image out of my mind I searched my brain for memories. Like the beginning sequence of an old school film projector images of my life flashed against the back of my eyes. As I recalled things that were previously buried under a thick layer of heroine addiction we pulled into the offload spot and both stepped down out of the truck. Dave was showing me how to hook up our pump to the holding tank using a large 4 foot long pry bar to open the manhole cover that concealed the pump to the holding tanks in the ground. Suddenly Dave looked down at his watch and back up to me quizzically then stated plainly.

“Jordan should’ve been here by now. Why don’t you hop in the truck and radio him.”

So I did, I stepped up into the truck leaning over my seat and pulled the radio off of its receiver. It hummed with a short burst of static just before I said

“Truck two this is truck one. Status update?”

There was silence, I waited a few minutes and spoke again

“Truck two were at offload, what’s your ETA?”

More static, I stood up, climbed out of the truck and jogged back to dave telling him that I couldn’t reach Jordan. Dave unclamped the hoses and hooked them back up in the truck before walking back prybar in hand to the cab and I followed. We both jumped in and Dave used the radio one last time.

“Jordan! JORDAN! Pick up dammit. Where the hell are you?”

After a few seconds of silence Dave threw the truck into gear and headed back down the way we came. Almost two miles back Jordan flipped his truck going around a particularly dangerous curve. Dave stopped on the side of the road our headlights illuminating the fresh red drag marks leading from the shattered window of Jordan’s truck across the road and into the woods.

“Radio the shop, tell them it’s a code 211. And no matter what do NOT get out of this truck until they get here.”

 Dave’s words were to the point and said with such ease, like he had spoken them countless times before. Like this was a situation he had encountered countless times. I picked up the receiver and called over the radio back to the shop.

“Truck number 8446 to shop we have a code 211. Requesting immediate assistance.”

“Shop to truck 8446. Copy. Sending shirt foreman and emergency services. What’s your location”

“Two miles south of offload site 167, one truck is flipped. We stopped to assist.”

“Copy, please remain in your vehicle until emergency services arrive”

I put down the receiver and went stiff as I watched Dave pull out a snub nose .357 revolver that was tucked into his waistband. I watched him examine the smeared blood in the road and walk into the woods following its trail. It didn’t take long for me to hear the unmistakable crack and boom of his gun shots ringing out. Then another. Then two more. Fear crept into my mind, no longer buzzing with adrenaline all I could imagine was being the sole survivor of whatever horrible fate happens to those other two guys. Most of my life was a series of cowering in fear and finding habits to medicate my broken mind. I was given the chance to change… to stop cowering and in some small way try to help. My eyes searched the cabin, if I was going to follow Dave into those dreaded woods my bare hands wouldn’t be much use. I grabbed the four foot long pry bar, that was nestled between our seats and the back wall of the cab a few minutes ago. My other hand grabbed the door handle and I pushed open the door. I heard a fifth gunshot. He had one left and still no sight of either of them. 

I slammed the door shut and walked past our headlights that lit up the road. I heard faint sirens approaching us, they weren’t going to make it. Not at the pace Dave was shooting. I hurried into the woods trying my best to follow Dave’s tracks. It was infinitely more dark under the tall trees than it was on the road and Dave took our only flashlight with him. I got about 50 feet into the thick greenery when I head a sixth and final shot. My pace quickened and so did my breathing, I only had a loose idea of the right direction but I was running that way at a full sprint. 

“DAVE! DAVE WHERE ARE YOU?”

 I screamed as loud as I could my voice breaking in the midnight air. I heard no response and kept running, then I saw a pin prick of light. The flashlight, I ran in its direction to find it a few moments later lying on the ground. I picked it up and looked around shining it into the trees all around me. Still no sign of Dave or Jordan, then I heard a noise a noise. The sounds didn’t fit my surroundings. I was in the middle of some state owned patch of forestry, why was I hearing the cry of a small child. It wasn’t quite right, like something trying to imitate the wails of a child lost and missing its mothers warmth. I switched off the flashlight and stood in the darkness, calming my breath and my heartbeat to try and listen harder. It cried again this time closer, I took a step toward the noise.

“Dave?”

 I whispered into the dark, though my heart had stopped beating so hard my mind was racing. There would be no logical reason for a grown man in his early fifties to make these sounds, but then again nothing out here would logically make these sounds. Whatever was making that noise wasn’t a child, at that moment I wasn’t even sure it was human. Then the cry turned into a low growl produced from behind a terrifying row of sharp teeth. I turned on the light just in time to see a coyote lunging at me from between two oak trees. I dropped the flashlight and held the pry bar out in front of me just in time for the animal to wrap its teeth ridden jaws around the bar sending us both to the ground. I kicked it off me instinctively my body reacting before my mind had the chance to comprehend and scrambled backwards. 

Holding the pointed end of the pry bar towards the coyote my mind began to realize what was standing in front of me. It quickly scrambled to its own feet and walked in a crescent shaped path from my right field of view passing by the flashlight and stopping on my left side. My heart raced, I could hear my pulse drumming in my ears. I lost control of my basic motor functions as fear consumed me. Once again like every time before, I froze in the face of despair and danger. The coyote leaned back on its haunches getting ready to pounce on me its yellow eyes glowing in the darkness telegraphing its every move. In the darkness I could just make out its snarl as the moon reflected its blood stained teeth. Then just before its spring loaded legs released sending it flying towards me a thick tree branch swung down cracking it across the top of its head splintering off with a sickening crunch. The animal fell to the ground its legs crumpled underneath it. 

I didn’t watch it long enough to see if it was still breathing before i looked to see where the branch came from. Dave who was carrying Jordan on his shoulder emerged from the darkness behind the animal, he was covered in blood scrapes and cuts covered his arms and torso. Grabbing the flash light and walking past me he’s words filled the inky night around us.

“I said to stay in the fucking truck”

I sat in the soft leaves and grass for just a moment longer before the realization that if I didn’t follow he may just leave me out here. I scrambled to my feet and followed a few steps behind him with my head down and the pry bar loosely in my hands. After about  10 minutes of hiking we saw red, white and blue flashing lights. As we emerged from the wooded roadside the EMT’s took Jordan’s body from Dave and laid him down on a stretcher, they checked for a pulse and some breathing but after seeing his throat which had been ripped out and multiple large bites taken out of his arms and legs they called the time of death. The rest of the night went by quickly, after some questions from authorities Dave drove me back to the shop where we were tested for drugs (company policy) and filled out incident reports. Then I went home early. 

This all happened three nights ago and I’ve tried not to think much about it since. Until a call I received  from the detective working the case not long ago. Detective Harmon called me to two things first Jordan’s truck at some point before his accident was tampered with, and second my drug screening popped for sedatives. When I saw Jordan’s lifeless body on that stretcher, it didn’t bother me at the time but the chunks taken out of him were much larger than the maw of a coyote. Also the only thing I ate or drank that night was the coffee Dave handed to me when I clocked in. So many questions are swimming in my mind. I have to get ready for work tonight. I’m driving with Dave again. I’ll update you tomorrow if i survive.
