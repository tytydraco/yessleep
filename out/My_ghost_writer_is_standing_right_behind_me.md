I think something is inside my laptop. 

I’m not crazy, I promise. 

I’m a writer, and I don’t know where else to post something like this. 

It all started last week. 

You see, I spend most days at the public library and use the free WiFi to write my novel. 

I bring my laptop everywhere with me. Just in case inspiration strikes. I always make the joke that my laptop is my best friend. 

My father made fun of me for saying that at a family dinner once when I had to run out and write something down before I forgot it. 

He said I had an obsession. 

He didn’t like technology. Never trusted it. 

He told me I shouldn’t trust anything if I can’t see where it keeps its brain. 

The thing is, that phrase never made much sense before last week. 

Now, it fucking haunts me. 

It just did it again. 

Anytime I’ve tried writing about anything other than that night, that damn line just sits there. 

Pulsing. 

No. Not pulsing. Smiling. 

Every third blink I swear it curves into a smile. Like it knows I’m watching. 

It almost feels like it’s breathing. 

I just stare at it and try to think of the next word. The next phrase. The next paragraph. 

But, it won’t come. 

And even if I try to force it, the keys won’t register. 

I guess I should start at the beginning so, again, you won’t think I’m crazy. 

Because I’m not. 

This past Thursday, I made it to the Hillvale Library just after 5:00 and took up my usual spot away from the desk. There was a corner where I like to sit in a banquette near the stairs. 

I spread out my laptop, coffee, favorite pen and scratch pad like I always do. 

I start working on my novel. It’s kind of an experimental piece, to be honest. 

It’s about a man who stumbles into something supernatural. An ordinary man. 

Which is kind of ironic, because that is exactly what happened to me that night. 

I met Bobbie a couple weeks ago when I started coming in after work on Tuesdays and Thursdays when the library had extended hours until 9:00. Sometimes I’d stay till close just getting one more round of edits in. 

Bobbie and I became fast friends. 

He worked at the library up on the third floor, but covered the ground floor that stayed open late. We’d chatted several times about the writing process. He handled the reference questions so I was always pestering him with some research or wanting to run an idea by him. 

When he came down the stairs that night to start his shift, I waved to him like I always did and he gave me a little thumbs up and slid behind the desk. 

He didn’t look like his usual self, though. 

He didn’t come over and talk. He didn’t smile. 

Something was off. 

I figured he was just having an off day so I went back to working on my book. 

I really liked this particular spot in the library, not only because I tended to get my best ideas here, but also because it was the best view in the house. 

To my left and at an angle, the main stairway for the library rose four floors. It wasn’t just some ordinary set of stairs, though. Behind the railing and along the wall that went all the way up to the fourth floor, there was a giant obsidian mirror. I’d never seen something so beautiful before. Or so massive. I’d heard from someone in town that it was installed back in the ‘80’s and donated by a private art collector that specialized in indigenous Aztec glass. 

There’s something about it that appeals to me. 

That mirror is why I am attracted to this spot in particular. From this seat, I can see almost the entire first floor. 

I just didn’t know, until that day, that the mirror might have been looking back. 

I didn’t even notice anything odd at first. I was busy editing a scene with my MC. 

I heard Bobbie talking behind me at the desk and assumed it was someone he knew well because he was laughing. 

Usually sound doesn’t bother me when I’m writing, especially here at the library since it’s a public place. 

But there was something odd about Bobbie’s laugh. 

It was *unsettling*. 

It’s like there were two voices laughing at the same time. Each slightly off from the other so it sounded dissonant. It made the hair on the back of my arm stand up. 

I turned around to ask them to lower their voices, but I only saw Bobbie. 

*He was talking to thin air.* 

I whipped my head back around and my eyes met my reflection in the stairway mirror. 

Then they slid to the side and over to where the desk was. Where Bobbie stood. 

There *was* someone with him. 

I turned back around in my seat and twisted my head a bit to see if I’d missed something around the corner, but it was still just Bobbie. 

Talking to himself.  

Except that wasn’t quite right, was it?

I turned slowly back in my seat. 

I didn’t mean to look back at the mirror. 

I don’t know why I looked. 

But when I did, the thing talking to Bobbie stopped moving its mouth and turned. 

Our eyes met in the mirror. 

A sound played loudly through the speakers of my laptop. 

It was like a screech. Metallic. Almost like a guttural scream if it originated through electronic vocal chords. 

I searched everywhere in my settings on how to turn it down. 

I tried turning it off. 

Unplugging it. 

Shutting the lid. 

Nothing helped. 

I started to panic. 

Then I felt a tap on my shoulder. 

I nearly jumped out of my skin and dropped the laptop. 

“Hey, Nate, are you ok man?” 

Jesus, it was just Bobbie. 

“Yeah, sorry, I can’t get this sound to stop playing.”

“What sound?”

It had gone eerily quiet. 

“Oh, my laptop was making this high pitched sound just a minute ago.”

I showed him the screen of the laptop, but it had gone dark. I pushed the power button, but nothing happened. 

It had full charge just a minute ago, but now it was completely dead?

“Did it have any kind of message pop up before the crash?” 

Bobbie went into full diagnostic mode. 

“Nah, just made that sound and died.”

“Well, if you want, you can leave it here with me overnight and I’ll check it out and give you the verdict tomorrow.”

“Sure, that’d be really helpful. You know that laptop is my life.”

“Yeah, no worries man. I should be able to back it up and do a restore on it and you should be good by tomorrow. Just swing by on your way to work.”

Bobbie gave me a clap on the shoulder and I put the laptop in the bag with its cord and handed it to him. 

I left shortly after and gave him a wave as I left. 

That was the last time I saw Bobbie before his “accident.”

I read about what he did to himself in the paper. 

When I went back to the library on Friday, there was crime scene tape blocking the path to the lobby and police lined the sidewalk. 

I asked one of them about my laptop. If I could come in and get it and leave. That it was important. 

One of the deputies made me fill out an inquiry form and said they would be in touch if they found it. 

I didn’t hear anything until yesterday when the police finally contacted me. 

They had the laptop downtown and I could pick it up. 

I used it most of the day yesterday. 

It worked like normal. 

Fired up just like it did before last Thursday. 

Before what I saw in the mirror. 

I picked back up on my routine of stopping by the local coffee shop then heading to the library. 

Everything was fine. 

Same booth by the stairs. 

Laptop. Coffee. Pen. Writing pad. 

Except something was different. 

My thumb caught on something stuck on the trackpad. 

I looked closer. 

Crusted over and almost completely indistinguishable from the plastic now, a dime-sized stain sat between the edge of the trackpad and the wrist rest. 

I picked at it with my finger and flecks of a dried reddish-brown substance spread across my finger. 

I kept this laptop pristine. It was my baby. 

I was 100% sure this was not on it before I gave it to Bobbie. 

I lifted my fingers up to my nose and smelled the feint copper smell of blood.  

I don’t know what made me look up at the mirror at that moment. 

It’s not like I had a real reason to. 

But I did anyway. 

At first I only saw myself. 

My laptop screen made my face glow an unnaturally pale color in the dark glass. 

I blinked. 

There, just behind my shoulder, stood someone else. 

I saw hands come up and around my shoulders and a face lean down. Right next to my ear. 

I felt coldness seep into my shoulder and run down my arms and into my hands and fingers. 

When I looked back at my screen this is what I saw:

*Hi Nate. My name is Angie. Do you want to hear a scary story?*

I slammed my laptop shut and left as quickly as I could. 

That was yesterday afternoon. Sunday. 

It’s Monday. I haven’t slept since then. 

I think something or someone followed me home from that library. 

I’m not sure if it’s attached to the laptop or to me. 

I’ve tried stretching, showering, massaging, even writing by hand. 

None of it has helped the itching in my fingers. 

They keep curling down like they want to type on a keyboard. 

I haven’t touched the laptop since yesterday. Not since that creepy message. 

It’s broad daylight, though, right? 

Nothing scary happens in broad daylight. 

I risk looking at the laptop. 

It looks normal. Even inviting. 

I get up from the couch and pick it up. 

When I open the screen, it’s dark and the power button isn’t working. Even though it’s been on the charger all night. 

I was about to shut it and consider it actually broken this time, but then something flickered on the screen. 

I looked closer. 

It was that damn cursor again. 

Just one in the middle of the screen. 

Blinking. 

Then the words started to appear letter by letter. 

My blood ran cold. 

*Bobbie has such beautiful eyes. I really enjoyed taking them out by hand.* 

*Don’t you want to see?*
