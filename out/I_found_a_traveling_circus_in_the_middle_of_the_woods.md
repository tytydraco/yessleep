My name is Amy and I’m not a superstitious person. I never have been, but lately I've been questioning if that statement is even true anymore.

I'd like to give some additional context before we get into this post.

 

I’m part of a YouTube channel called No one & Nobody with my best friend Karlie. We met in my sophomore, her  freshman year, when we got paired together as dorm mates. We were quick friends and bonded over both wanting to work in some form of entertainment or film, as well as a shared hobby in urban exploration. 

 

Karlie and I decided to start our YouTube channel after we documented a particularly interesting visit into an old abandoned theater in our city. I’m not going to lie and say that our first couple videos did crazy numbers or anything, but it certainly didn't do bad. 

 

After that we realized that using our combined skills in audio engineering and videography, with Karlie and I pursuing degrees in each, respectively. We could make decently high quality videos cataloging our ‘ventures into the unknown’ as Karlie likes to say. Personally I would argue that while there is an element of mystery, most of the places we go are entirely known as they were once inhabited, but she also likes to say. 'There's no excitement without some intentional whimsy.' I'm coming around to it.

 

At first only people within the urban exploring community in our area and a few surrounding cities watched, with our videos averaging somewhere within 200-300 views per video. About 6 months in though, we got mentioned in a commentary video by a much larger channel than our own. After that the views on the next couple of videos skyrocketed. 

 

Thanks to this, after the hype died down we maintained a small but steady viewership that slowly grew over time. 

 

Now 2 years later I'm a senior in college and we’re still going strong, still making videos together, and still roommates. Things are going great, well, at least they were. 

 

Let me not get too cynical, things are still mostly great, and I guess everything that’s been happening recently isn’t bad just…. Different. Different weird. I'm not generally a fearful person, my only major fears being dolls, and falling, but this stuff has been scaring me. 

 

We had a break from school about 3 weeks ago and it just so happened that a few hours and a couple of towns over that there was a mall we had been getting a lot of comments asking us to go see. So we went, nothing crazy and nothing unusual. We explore abandoned malls all the time. It’s often one of the first kinds of abandoned places you end up exploring. Honestly it’s shocking we hadn’t been through this one yet with it being so close.

 

We don’t always share the names or locations of the places we visit as a courtesy to the other people who explore them. Hopefully it spares them from some more extreme vandalism as well. But seeing as this is a mall and we’ve already posted the video of this venture I see no issue with going into more detail in my description of these events to you. 

 

Let me precede this story by mentioning that this day is not the one that I’ve primarily come here to discuss. Nonetheless we’ve received countless accusations of doctoring footage, staging, and lying after this video. And I'm expecting much the same after I post this.

Pulled on top of each other, sticking out at odd ends, this isn’t right there’s just. So. Many. Some of them are missing arms the other legs but a good portion of them are fully intact and holy shit that one doesn't have a head and—
