I stayed up all night listening to the box. Whatever was inside it would move every hours. It was not moving all the time. Just a little shift every now and then. It was like something was getting comfortable. I put the box in the hall closet around two in the morning. By four I could hear it again. It was not making a lot of noise. It was just moving.

By the time the sun came up I told myself I was being silly. There had to be a reason for all this. Maybe someone sent me a prank package. Maybe there were broken electronics or a clock inside. I kept telling myself that while I made some coffee. I looked at the microwave. It said 4:18. I unplugged it. Plugged it back in. It still said 4:18. I reset it. It still said 4:18. I got tired of looking at it so I covered it with a towel.

Then I decided to open the box. Inside the box there was another box. This one was smaller. It was wrapped in paper. Inside that box there was another one.. Another.. Another. There were seven boxes in total. Each one was smaller than the last. I remember laughing. I was not laughing because it was funny. I was laughing because I felt stupid. I was scared of a bunch of boxes inside each other.

The last box was very small. It was a little bigger than my hand. Inside the box there was a brass key and a note. The note said "RETURN AFTER 4:18". I read it six times. Then I called my supervisor. He came to my house twenty minutes later. When he saw the key he turned white. He was not surprised. He was terrified. He asked me where I got the key. I told him. He sat down. Did not say anything.

Then he asked me something. He asked if they knocked three times. I stopped. I asked him how he knew that. He closed his eyes. He said they always knock three times. Always. Not just once. Always. Before I could ask what he meant he stood up. Took the key from my hand. The brass key burned him. He dropped it immediately. I heard it hit the floor. It did not make any sound. The key had vanished.

We both stared at each other. My supervisor looked older. He looked tired. He sat back down. He said he had hoped to retire before this happened. I asked him what happened. He did not answer my question. Instead he asked me another question. He asked if anyone in Valewick had ever talked to me. I said no. He asked about the woman in the diner. I said no. He asked about the boy with the bicycle. I said no. He nodded. He said that was good.

I asked him why it was good. He said I did not want them to talk to me. I asked him why. He looked at the microwave in the kitchen. He whispered that if they start talking it means they are trying to warn me. I felt something in my stomach. I asked him what they were warning me about. He just shook his head. He said he could not tell me. I said he clearly knew something. He said he knew enough.

I asked him what he meant by enough. He said enough to know that if they sent me a key they want me to come back. I laughed. I said obviously I have to go to Valewick. I deliver mail there. He said no not during the day. His voice was shaking. My phone rang. It was a number. Neither of us moved. It rang again and again. Eventually I answered it.

I expected to hear static or silence. Instead I heard the sounds of a diner. There were dishes coffee brewing. There was music playing. Then I heard a woman's voice. It was the time I had ever heard someone from Valewick talk. She sounded calm and kind. She said I was late. I almost dropped the phone. My supervisor stood up quickly that his chair fell over.

He whispered what she said. The woman's voice continued. She said I had left early the time. There was a pause. Then she said he had been waiting. I could not speak. The woman sighed sadly. For the time someone, from Valewick sounded afraid. She whispered please do not come back after 4:18. The line went dead.

My supervisor looked more frightened than I had ever seen him. He asked what she said. I told him. He sat down heavily. For a time neither of us spoke. Finally he looked at the microwave. He quietly said she spoke. I nodded. He stared at me. He was not shocked or confused. He was terrified.

Then he whispered something I wish I had not heard. He said that means they remember. Then something knocked against my front door. Three times. It was slow and patient.. This time it was not coming from outside. It was coming from the hallway closet. The one I had emptied hours ago.
