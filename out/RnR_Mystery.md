This is just a personal diary of mine, something I experienced going back to my hometown.

I think this was in 2024 and it was the end of Ramadhan in Malaysia, so everyone and their families are getting ready to go back to their hometowns to celebrate Hari Raya. I personally do not have a hometown anymore as both my grandparents have passed away years ago, so we just celebrated it with our extended family in the city.

I just finished the last physical class right before it switched to online classes for the students who are journeying home earlier since 1) It's gonna be extremely jam and 2) Raya preperations are gonna be hectic as hell. I decided to go back 2 days before Raya and took the night time bus since it'll be slightly less jam and I can sleep it off the few hour bus ride.

The journey back home from my uni usually is about 6 - 7 hourse depending on traffic and there will be multiple rest stops (We call it RnR) along the way for those who wants to use the bathroom or grab a snack/drink. This time around, my bus was supposed to be at 8PM but somehow the bus got delayed to 11.30PM and it wasn't until about 12.40AM-ish when the bus departed from the station. The bus is the normal modern long-distance bus and there was quite a lot of passengers in my bus but still a few empty seats.

The highway that my bus usually use is the more modern one, meaning bright lights (Most of the time) and more cars on the road but about an 1 hour in; I noticed that we took the back roads, meaning the old roads along the forest area. This road is usually the best to beat traffic but rarely would a bus use this route since it's much smaller and darker due to the lack of street lights.

We've already passed 1 RnR and will be stopping to another one in a short while. However, the nearest RnR is right smack dab in the edge of the forest. It's normal to build RnR at the edge or sometimes inside the forest but this particular RnR is at the edge but looks extremely run down. Like the building in of itself is those old style *pondok* types, like it has the old school "Jalousie" type windows and an open air roof and this RnR is mainly for bathroom and prayer purposes with a few vending machines scattered nearby.

I decided to go to the bathroom and quickly realised, I was the only male passenger that got off the bus and with the dimly lit building, the quiet forest and vast bathroom stalls? Yeah, perfect horror movie scene. I quickly went to the bathroom and do my business but I heard someone got into the stall next to mine, normal and I ignored it. But the second I went out, the stall beside me was empty like the door was not closed.

I reallt got spooked but remembered it's Ramadhan, all the demons or bad spirits have been tied up so nothing should happen but... Somehow it still did cuz at the very last stall at the end, suddenly slammed close. It was loud and echoed throughout the bathroom. Nope, I got the hell out of there and ran inside the bus. Once I think everyone got in, the bus attendant was doing head count and what made my blood run cold is when he counted the seats next me... There was no one in that seat, it was an empty row... Only me...

I kept paying attention to the attendant and hoped he miscounted the people but when he came back to count again, he still included the seat next to mine. I remember he counter 24 so I quickly glanced to count the number of passengers and the one I can see is only 22... And the two seats next to me is empty... I panicked and couldn't sleep.

About 2 hours of trying to take my mind off of the empty seats, suddenly the attendant approached me asking if I'm willing to switch seats. He asked in Malay but I will translate to the best of my ability.

"*Sir, is okay for you to change seats really quick?" - Attendant*

*"Erm... Switch where Bang?" - Me (Bang is shortened Abang, meaning elder brother/honorifics)*

*"The seat beside you only, just now the makcik asked to switch seats... She wants to look outside" - Attendant.*

My heart stopped cuz he was pointing at the empty seats beside me and there were no *makcik* (Old lady) in the bus, mainly students and foreign workers. I looked at the attendant and told him something that made him pale instantly.

"*Bang... The makcik... Was always seating at the seat across from mine?.." - Me*

*"Eh? Yelah, you didn't notice Sir? There's a makcik with her child..." - Attendant*

*"No... Because from uni until now, no one has been sitting there... I'm the only person in this row, those seats were always empty..." - Me*

The attendant froze and looked back. He immediately began doing a head count and I heard him count only 22, the amount I counted. He visibly shaked and apologized to me before darting back to the front of the bus. I stayed still, knowing damn well what that meant. I saw an empty seat in front of me and migrated there. I'm not seating at that row anymore.

The remainding of the bus ride had turned tense. Like somehow it felt extremely unwelcoming, like the air turned gloom and hostile and everyone felt it since it wasn't long after my encounter with the attendant that everyone woke up suddenly and refused to sleep. There would be numerous tapping noises on the windows or sometimes a faint *mengilai* (High pitched laughter) from a distance.

The bus didn't stop at any RnRs after that and went straight to the city. Once we arrived at the station, we all noticed multiple scratch marks on the window and the side of the bus and how the windows are all fogged up even in 32°C weather. But one thing I did not expect to see was a makcik at my original seat, smiling with a tombstone on her lap, waving at us with hollow eyes. And that's when I heard it, a faint crying of a child from inside the bus.

Never did I take the night bus afterwards.
