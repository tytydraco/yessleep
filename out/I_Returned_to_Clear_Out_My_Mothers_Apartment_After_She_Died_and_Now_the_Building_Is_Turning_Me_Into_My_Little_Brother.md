I’m writing this from room 17 of a crappy motel about fifteen minutes away from the building. Its 4:17 in the morning. The neon sign outside keeps flashing pink through the thin curtains and my hands already look wrong on the keyboard. I dont know how much longer I can even type this. I left everything behind in the apartment. Clothes, charger, the box with moms ashes, all of it. I just ran.

Three weeks ago my mother died. Cancer. On her hospital bed she made me promise never to open the little door at the end of the hallway in her apartment. I said yes to calm her down. I thought it was the drugs talking. Im thirty four, I repair old computers for a living, I dont believe in that kind of stuff. I took a week off, drove up north, and let myself into the fourth floor unit with the spare key she always kept under the mat.

Its one of those ugly five story concrete buildings from the seventies. Long gray hallways, thin walls, you can always hear people walking upstairs or flushing toilets but you never really see anybody. Half the apartments are empty now. It smelled like damp and old food the second I walked in. Familiar in a depressing way.

The first three days were just sad. I packed up her clothes. They still smelled like those violet candies she sucked on all the time and her old cigarettes. I found half empty wine glasses, piles of unopened mail, pictures of me and my little brother stuck to the fridge. In every photo Im smiling. Hes not. I kept looking at that closed white door at the end of the hallway. Paint peeling at the bottom. I left it shut.

On the fourth night I opened it.

I dont even know why. I had drunk two beers and I was exhausted. The door was stuck so I had to shove it hard with my shoulder. Behind it was this tiny narrow room, maybe two meters by one. Nothing but stained carpet and a window that had been bricked up years ago. The smell hit me right away. Sweet and rotten at the same time, like melted violet candy mixed with blood. The carpet felt warm under my socks. Almost body temperature. I backed out fast, closed the door, and told myself it was nothing.

That night I woke up at like 3:11 convinced someone was standing there watching me sleep. The whole building was quiet except for this slow breathing sound coming through the walls. In and out. Perfectly matching my own breathing. If I held my breath everything stopped. When I started again the walls answered.

Day five the little red truck showed up.

It was my brothers favorite toy. I threw it away myself the week after he died, I remember the garbage truck taking it. But there it was in the middle of the living room floor, same scratches on the wheels and everything. I put it in a box. Next morning it was back on the floor. I recorded a video on my phone. My hands were shaking so bad the picture is blurry but you can hear the building breathing behind my voice if you listen close.

By day seven my veins turned dark. Not just visible, they were raised like thick black roots under the skin on my arms and neck. Every time I thought about my brother they throbbed. I wore long sleeves even though the place was boiling hot. The carpet in the hallway felt thicker. My bare feet sank in a little and made this wet sucking noise when I lifted them.

The hallway started getting longer.

I counted my steps every morning like an idiot. Fifteen from front door to kitchen on day six. Twenty one on day eight. The narrow room behind the forbidden door had another door now on the far wall. I opened it and saw the exact same hallway stretching out way farther than the building should allow. Same flickering lights. Same ugly carpet. It just kept going.

I started talking to myself in a higher voice. Kid sentences I hadnt said since I was nine. Youre not supposed to push. It was an accident. I caught myself and slapped my own face hard enough to make my nose bleed.

Day nine my nails grew back thicker and faster than I could cut them. They looked yellowish and hard. I scratched the wall without thinking and left little handprints at the exact height my brother used to be. When I looked in the bathroom mirror my left eye blinked half a second after the right one. I smiled at myself. I wasnt smiling.

Day ten the bumps started. Hard little lumps under the skin on my stomach and thighs, like teeth growing in the wrong places. If I pressed them I could feel something move underneath. I threw up in the bathtub until I was dry heaving.

The building was changing with me. I tried to leave on day eleven and the stairwell had nine landings instead of five. Every other apartment door was sealed from the inside with tiny handprints all over the metal. When I finally got to the ground floor the lobby was dark and the front doors were gone, just brick wall where they should have been.

Last night was the end.

I woke up on the floor of that narrow room. My clothes didnt fit anymore. My arms were shorter. My face felt loose like the bones were sliding around under the skin. In the corner there was a shape that looked exactly like me at nine years old wearing the same pajamas from the day he died. He smiled with my adult teeth still in his mouth.

I ran.

I dont remember getting down the stairs. Just the sound of small feet chasing me and my own feet making wet slapping noises because they were getting softer and smaller. I made it to my car and drove until the gas light came on. Checked into this motel under a fake name.

My hands are shrinking while I type. The fingers are short and chubby now. My voice cracked earlier when I whispered Im sorry and it sounded like a little kid. The violet candy smell is coming out of my own skin. I can feel new teeth moving behind my ribs.

Im still me. For now. I can still type this. But the building is calling me back. I can hear it breathing through the motel walls, getting louder. It wants me to take his place so the accident can be fixed.

If you still have parents dont ever go back to their place after theyre gone. Especially if youve got something heavy you never told anyone.

I have to post this right now. My fingers barely reach the keys anymore.

Im sorry mom.

Im sorry little brother.

The neon sign outside just flashed exactly in time with my heartbeat.

I think Im going back tonight.
