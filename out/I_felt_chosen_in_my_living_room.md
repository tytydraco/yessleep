It didn’t make me feel scared.

It made me feel right.

The living room was the only place it happened. It started out of no where one evening. The rest of the house stayed normal. But the moment I crossed into that room, the air thickened and the temperature sank subtly, like the house was drawing a slow, deliberate breath around me. It wasn’t sudden. It crept in. A cold that didn’t touch my skin so much as it did my thoughts.

At first it felt like dread. A warning pressure in my chest. But underneath it was something else, something intoxicating. My heart didn’t race. It slowed. My mind didn’t scatter. It sharpened. I felt heavier, like gravity had decided I mattered more there. I felt important in a way no mirror had ever given me.

I started going in on purpose.

I would sit in the dark and let the cold soak into me. The shadows didn’t behave. They leaned toward me. They thickened in corners where they shouldn’t exist. The ceiling felt higher and closer at the same time, like the room was folding inward around me. When I caught my reflection in the blank TV screen, my face looked different. Not distorted. Commanding. My mouth curved slightly upward and I didn’t remember telling it to.

The feeling wasn’t fear anymore. It was power.

Not loud. Not violent. Quiet and ancient and patient. It filled my chest until my breathing felt ceremonial. My thoughts became crueler and clearer at the same time. I felt like I could see through people. Through their little reasons and soft lies. I felt above them. I felt permitted to think things I never used to think.

I started smiling at those thoughts.

That’s when I knew it wasn’t just happening to me. It was changing me.

The room encouraged it. When I resisted the feeling, the cold deepened and the walls seemed to press inward. When I relaxed into it, the cold softened and something like approval settled over me. It felt like standing in a cathedral built for something that hated light and had finally found something worth shaping.

I stayed longer.

The doorway began to feel far away. My legs felt thick when I tried to leave, like wading through syrup. The walls sounded close, like they were listening. I felt drawn to them, not violently, but curiously. Like something behind them had leaned forward first. Once, I placed my hand against the paint and it felt warm beneath the surface. Not room temperature. Body temperature. When I pulled away, there was a faint indentation where my palm had been.

That was when the thought formed fully.
This wasn’t fear. It was an offer.

It didn’t promise safety or comfort. It promised elevation. It made me feel like I could be more than human. Like something old and heavy was lending me its posture and waiting to see if I would keep it. My ego swelled in that room until it felt holy. I felt chosen. I felt deserving. I felt like the rules bent slightly when I breathed.

But every time the feeling peaked, another thought followed it.

“What will you give me?”

It didn’t say it out loud. It shaped the question inside my chest. What part of you is unnecessary. What part of you can be burned away. What will you leave behind to become what you are meant to be.

At night, the walls looked thinner. The shadows pooled at the baseboards like they were waiting their turn. Once, I stood up without remembering deciding to and walked toward the blank wall behind the TV. The paint looked soft. The darkness around it pulsed faintly, like something breathing through it. I felt the urge to step forward and let it take me. Not into the wall, but into whatever waited where the wall was pretending to exist.

I stopped myself.

That was the last night I sat in the living room. But the feeling did not stay there.

Now I catch myself grinning at things that used to bother me. I feel taller in empty rooms. Sometimes I wake up standing in front of other walls, my forehead inches from the surface, my breath fogging faintly against the paint. Sometimes I dream that I finally accept the offer and feel something slide into me like a second spine.

I don’t think the living room was haunted.

I think it was a mouth.

And I think whatever tasted me is still deciding how much of me it wants.

Because the worst part isn’t that I’m afraid of what it will take.

The worst part is that when I feel that cold power rise in my chest, I still want to know what it would give me if I let it finish.
