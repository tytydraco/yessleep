Not sure if this is the right place to post this, but something weird has been happening in my building for the last couple of weeks.

I live alone in a small apartment. It’s just a quiet place on the third floor. My neighbor across the hall is an older guy named Mr. Mehra. We don’t really talk much, just the usual good morning in the hallway.

About two weeks ago I found a small package outside my door when I got home from work.

There was no sender name. Just my address.

I assumed it was something I forgot I ordered. That happens sometimes.

Inside was a black pen.

Nothing special, just a normal pen.

But the strange part was the note inside.

It said:

“You’ll need this tomorrow.”

I laughed it off. I thought maybe a friend was messing with me.

The next day at work my manager suddenly asked me to sign some urgent documents because the office printer broke and we had to fill them manually.

And yeah, the only pen I had on me was that one.

Still weird, but I didn’t think much of it.

Then another package appeared the next evening.

Same thing. It was sitting outside my door.

Inside was a phone charger.

And another note.

“Your battery dies tonight.”

That actually made me pause.

But again, I ignored it.

That night around 11 PM my phone dropped to 2 percent and I realized I had forgotten my charger at the office.

The only charger in the house was the one from the package.

Now I was starting to get curious.

So the next morning I asked Mr. Mehra if he had seen anyone leaving packages outside my door.

He looked confused.

“Packages?” he asked.

I told him what had been happening.

He just shook his head.

“No idea,” he said. “But if you find another one, don’t open it.”

That response felt strange.

“Why?” I asked.

He didn’t answer directly.

He just said, “Some things are better left unopened.”

I laughed it off.

But that same night another package appeared.

This one was heavier.

Inside was a small flashlight.

And a note.

“You’ll hear something tonight.”

I almost threw it away.

But around 3 AM I woke up to a loud crashing sound in the hallway outside my apartment.

The power in the building had gone out.

Everything was completely dark.

I grabbed the flashlight from the package and opened my door.

That’s when I saw Mr. Mehra standing in the hallway.

Just standing there.

Staring at my door.

I asked if he heard the noise.

He didn’t respond.

He just looked at the flashlight in my hand and said quietly,

“Good. You opened it.”

I asked what he meant.

He sighed and rubbed his forehead.

Then he said something that still doesn’t make sense to me.

“You’re getting the packages now.”

I asked what that meant.

He looked at me like he was deciding whether to say it or not.

Then he said,

“Before you moved in… I used to get them.
