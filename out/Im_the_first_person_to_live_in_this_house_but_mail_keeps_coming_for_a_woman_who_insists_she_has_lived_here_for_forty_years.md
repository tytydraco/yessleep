I am writing this at three in the morning with every light in my house turned on because I cannot sleep and I cannot stop walking from room to room and I do not know what else to do with my hands.

I bought a brand new house in March. It was a new build, finished construction three weeks before I closed. I am the first person to ever live here. I have the certificate of occupancy. I have the builder's warranty. I have the county records showing the parcel was vacant land until October of last year. I have all the paperwork.

The first piece of mail arrived in May.

It was addressed to a woman named Margaret Calloway, at my address. Junk mail. A coupon flyer from a grocery chain. I threw it away. The next day there was a credit card offer for her. The day after that, a mailer from an auto dealership.

I did what anyone would do. I wrote "Return to Sender. Wrong Address." on the envelopes for two weeks. The mail kept coming.

Then a hospital appointment reminder came.

It was from a real hospital, the regional medical center in the next town. It was addressed to Margaret Calloway at my address. It listed her as a patient since 1986. I called the hospital because I wanted them to update the address. The records department told me Margaret Calloway had been listed at this address since she registered as a patient. They confirmed the address. They read it back to me. It was my address. I asked if she could be moved to a forwarding address. The clerk said there was no forwarding address. Margaret had been at the same address for thirty-nine years according to their records.

I told the clerk that I had bought this house in March and that I was the first occupant. The clerk paused for a long time and then said, "I am sorry, I am not sure how to help you with that. The patient is listed at the address you just confirmed."

I hung up.

I want to stop here and explain how rational a person I am. I am a tax accountant. I read paperwork for a living. So when something does not match the paperwork, my first move is always to find the paperwork that is correct. I assumed the hospital had bad data. I assumed somewhere there was an old patient file that had been auto-renewed without anyone checking the underlying address.

I drove to the county records office.

I pulled the parcel history myself. Until October of last year, this lot was vacant. There is a satellite photo on file showing the empty land. I have a printout of it. The lot was sold to the builder in November. Construction started in December. The certificate of occupancy was issued in March.

There has never been a house at this address before mine.

I want to say that very clearly because I keep needing to remind myself of it.

I drove home with the printout. I sat at my kitchen table for a long time looking at the satellite image of my own house's location, as an empty field, dated fourteen months before I moved in.

That night a utility bill arrived addressed to Margaret. I went outside to my mailbox to get it after I saw the mail carrier leave. My own utility bill for the same month arrived the same day. Both addressed to my house. Both for active accounts. I called the utility company. They said yes, there are two active accounts at this address. One has been active since 1985. One has been active since April.

The one from 1985 is in Margaret Calloway's name.

I am going to skip ahead because if I go through every piece of mail in order I will be writing this until morning.

I have found Margaret in the voter rolls and in old phone directories. I have found her name on a property tax record from 1987 that lists her as the homeowner at my address. There is a photo of her in a community newsletter from 2003 that describes her as a long-time resident of my street. The photo shows her standing in front of a house. The house in the photo is not my house. The address printed under the photo is mine.

I called the number I found for her last night.

A woman answered on the third ring. I asked for Margaret Calloway. She said, "Speaking." Her voice was older than I expected. Cheerful. The kind of voice that has spent decades answering the phone politely.

I asked her where she lived.

She gave me my address.

I told her I had bought that house in March. I told her I was the first owner. I told her the house was eight months old.

She laughed. Not unkindly. She said, "Sweetheart, I have lived in this house since 1985. I think you have the wrong number." Then she said, "Are you alright? You sound very upset."

I could hear the television playing in the background of her call.

It was the same show that was playing in my living room.

Same episode. Same volume. There was a delay of less than half a second between what I heard from my television and what I heard through the phone.

I hung up.

I have been walking through my house with every light on for the past four hours. The kitchen phone receiver has been on the wall since I moved in. I had assumed it was decorative. I never plugged a landline into the jack. The receiver is a beige rotary phone, the kind I have not seen since I was a child. I never thought about it. It has been on my wall for eight months and I never once asked why a new build would have a rotary phone hardwired into the kitchen.

It just rang.

I have not picked it up. It has been ringing for three minutes. I am writing this in the hallway, away from the kitchen, with the lights all on, and I can hear it. It does not stop. The show on the television keeps changing on its own. The volume keeps going up and down without anyone touching the remote, which is in my hand.

I am not sure who lives in this house.

I am starting to think it might not be me.
