I am currently writing this within the outskirts of town, sitting in my car as I type this out on my cracked, black phone. I am out in the middle of nowhere because I fear that something very bad is going to happen once I spread this story to the greater public, but I understand that if I keep this churning inside of me, I would be better off dead. I have no idea if the government is even aware if I am still alive, nor do I care; I suspect that they are the ones that are trying to hunt me anyways. 

For the sake of my anonymity, I will not be sharing my real name as I share my story, but will instead refer to myself as “J”. To give some background on this whole situation, I live in the small town of Eldword, which is located inside of Brinswood County, Missouri. The town has a population somewhere around 6,000, and is primarily known within the area as a huge agrarian hub. Many farmers inhabit the surrounding area, and rely on the town to help transport their goods across the state, which is how the township makes most of its revenue. Within the town, St. Joseph Hospital resides around the center of it. Although rather small, it has been the area of many rumors – ranging from kidnapping to barbaric medical experiments – that have given the hospital a notorious reputation to those local to the area; almost nobody goes there unless they cannot help it. These rumors didn’t show up from nowhere, but were carefully developed after news of the hospital originally being a sanitorium for the mentally unwell was exposed to the public after records were found proving what was previous mere gossip to be true. After going to to the place after an especially bad incident there, I fully support those that believe those theories.

A little over 18 hours ago, I was sipping a hot, steaming cup of coffee – an americano – in the newsroom of my local office sector, sitting down in the middle of the room at one of the many tables. I continuously glanced over at the notes and citations that I had gathered from a previous case involving a scandalous representative, growing more bored by the minute. I expected another calm and boring day that would go by in a breeze, and would end in me being home by 5 PM – god I wish that was true.

Instead of that now bygone dream, a fellow journalist friend of mine (let us call him Frank) busted into the newsroom, huffing as he began to sputter out an assortment of sentences and stringed-together words that didn’t make any sense to me at first, giving me a great shock. To allow you to picture him, Frank is a stout, burly man that’s around 6 foot, and weighs somewhere near 200 pounds. His hair is red, curly, and bushy at the very top, and he has a very freckled face, with many centered around his upper cheeks and nose. He is normally very composed and proper – the main reason why I was surprised at his sheer lack of properness. After he calmed down, caught his breath, and sat down across from me at the same table, he finally began to speak clearly:

“J, I think I got something big.”

I peeked up from my coffee, squinting at him as I beckoned him to go on with a nod of my head.

“Something really bad is going down at the hospital, and none of the news anchors have made their way there yet. We can finally get the big scoop that we – or, me at the very least – have been chasing!”

I glanced at his face fully now, a look of shock and suspicion on my expression evident as his grin faltered a little.

“Frank, are you on something? What are you talking about?”

Listening to my words, his grin quickly dropped more, curling into a frown as he slowly lost most of his excitement, instead replacing it with a sense of seriousness.

“Some sort of ailment broke out in the hospital – a plague or some sort – that’s causing the PD to quarantine the entire area. However-”

He paused, clearing his throat as he gazed at me fully.

“-the whole exclusion area hasn’t been closed up properly, which means that we can still get in if we sneak around.”

I now stood up, leaving my coffee – which I had finished long ago – cup on the table, as I looked at him with a stern look.

“Frank, what you’re suggesting is not only illegal, but incredibly dangerous. What if we get caught by someone? What if we get that goddamn plague you’ve been rambling on about? What if the whole thing is a hoax to get- fuck.. I don’t know- a tax write-off?"

Upon hearing my words, Frank would grimace, clearly expecting me to say that, but hoping that I didn't, as he reached into the pocket of his khaki pants, sliding out a picture that I still can’t get out of my head:

a man, with crimson, bloody drool dripping from the right side of his unhinged, broken-looking jaw, was leaning out of a window – presumingly out of his hospital room – while he looked down at the groundfloor below. The picture was obviously taken from a moderately-long distance, but it was close enough to get nearly all of the man’s details clearly. His face was not a natural color, but appeared as if he was sunburnt from head-to-toe, with cracked, dead skin peeling off him along his head and arms. The most shocking part, however, was his hands. Barely visible from the hospital window, his right hand – if you can even call it that – was gripping the window with such an intensity that it appeared to be making an indent in the wall. The hand resembled more of a claw, with the fingers limber, and the fingernails protruding outwardly, a forceful extension of them being evident with the blood stains along the areas surrounding the hyponychium that were only distinguishable from the man’s skin by its darker shade of crimson and horrifying shininess.

Just looking at the picture nearly made me vomit, a large overwhelming wave of anxiousness overtaking me and making the room spin for a few moments before I was able to calm myself down. As I steadied myself, I was finally able to croak out:

“Frank.. Please.. Please tell me that that was edited.”

My lord, I wish he just lied to me.

“J.. sorry to say, but that is real.. took it myself."

He'd pause once more, sighing deeply.

"I had the same reaction as you when I saw the man, but I knew that you weren’t goin’ to believe me because you’re.. well.. stubborn. I’m sorry.”

I gazed back up from him, now noticing that my hands were positioned on the table, and that I was looking down; I was so zoned in on the memory of that picture, and Frank's words, that I had momentarily forgotten what was happening around me.

“It’s.. goddamn it.. I get it Frank.” 

I was able to leer at him once more, his hazelnut irises looking into me deeply; I knew that he was genuine. Eventually, I was able to move my hands away from the table, and stand up straight; I glimpsed back at Frank once more, and sighed.

“Well.. I guess we ought to get down there.”

The area that I am in is no longer safe; I must leave and tell the rest of this story later. Stay safe, everyone, and remember: don't trust them.
