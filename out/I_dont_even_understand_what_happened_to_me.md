This happened to me while I was still living in my old apartment.

I want to make it clear that even though the story seems unbelievable, I really did experience it. And even today, I struggle to find a rational explanation for this event, because several of the details I’m about to share—taken together—seem inconsistent, but each one makes perfect sense on its own.

All of this happened just two years ago, in 2024. At the time, I was living in a shared apartment in a small three-story building, located in a rather isolated village in Normandy, surrounded by fields and forest. The village was tiny: a primary school, a bar-restaurant/butcher shop that was almost always closed, located near the village’s only truly busy road—a national highway—and a small grocery store that also served as a bar, located near the church on a street perpendicular to that highway. Nothing else. It was my first modest but peaceful apartment.

I only stayed there for a year, and the building was a “transit” building: tenants only stayed for a few months. Most of them left for work around 7 a.m. and came home between 4 and 7 p.m.  As a result, during the day, there was almost no one in the building except me, my roommate, and the neighbors above me.

Before long, a routine had settled in. I lived on the ground floor, and since we were always there, we had sort of become the building’s “caretakers.” When a neighbor forgot their keys, the doorbell wasn’t working properly, or the mail carrier needed to deliver a package to a neighbor who wasn’t answering, they would knock on our living room window, since it was right by the entrance to the lobby (you only had to reach the edge of the few steps leading up to the building’s entrance to get there).

My roommate rarely went out, the complete opposite of me, who went out quite often: to run errands, walk my dog in the nearby forest… But most of all, I loved going out very late at night. I particularly enjoyed walking at night, taking in the silence and the nocturnal sounds of the sleeping village; moreover, since it’s very isolated, the streetlights turn off around 10:30 p.m., plunging the streets into pitch darkness, allowing me to gaze at the stars.

The neighbors upstairs, on the other hand, were very quiet. We could sometimes hear their footsteps, but rarely. However, every day at exactly 3 p.m., they played the accordion. Always the same song.

Neither before nor after.

It had almost become a regular part of my day, and honestly, they played really well. To be clear, I only met them once, a few months after we moved in, sometime between March and May 2024. The neighbor had come over to ask for my help because the seal on her washing machine had failed, and that’s when I discovered that they lived in a small shared apartment, just like us. She was very nice; we chatted a bit while I helped her with the machine, and I complimented her on the accordion piece that she or one of her two roommates played every day at 3 p.m.

Looking back on that moment, she didn’t seem surprised or taken aback that I mentioned it; she simply said: “Oh, does that bother you?”

I told her no, and the conversation continued as usual.

Then one day… nothing and months went by without the accordion.

At first, I just thought they had moved out. As I said, tenants changed often. And a few days later, when I started hearing furniture being moved around in the evening, I concluded that new tenants had indeed moved in and were refurnishing the apartment. So yes… late at night it seemed a little suspicious to me, but since most of the tenants left early in the morning and came home late, I just figured they hadn’t had time to do it before.

A few months after the accordion went missing, one night around 12:30 a.m in november, while I was in bed, I was woken up by sobbing.

At first I thought it was my roommate. I could hear it really clearly. But as I listened more closely, I realized he was talking in the living room: he was playing online with friends (Helldivers, for those who know it).

When I got up to check where the sobbing was coming from, I went into my roommate’s room, right next to mine, and there… strangely, the sobbing was much fainter (almost inaudible), as if muffled by the walls. In my room, on the other hand, it was the exact opposite; you could hear them so clearly that you’d think the person was crying right in my room. But the strangest thing was that you could hear them perfectly above my bed... when I moved away from it, they sounded a little more muffled... however, I had placed my bed in a small alcove, which normally served as a closet for the room. Since the apartments were all identical, that meant the person must have been crying in theirs.

At the time, although I found it strange, I wasn’t alarmed. I told myself it was probably the old, poorly insulated building that made the sound seem so clear, and I didn’t think too much of it. I grabbed my dog and went out for our usual evening walk.

And there, for the first time in a year, the lights were on in my upstairs neighbors’ living room.

It was unusual. The residents of the building all turn off their lights around 10:30 or 11:00 p.m. Some watch TV, others sleep—except for my neighbor on the top floor to the right, who is often out on his balcony smoking (yes, I go out so often late at night that I’ve noticed these details). And since the accordion disappeared, I have NEVER once seen their living room light on in the evening, let alone at such a late hour. But now, not only was the light on, but a dark figure was standing motionless in front of the window. At the top of the few steps leading to the entrance of my building, I put on my headphones while observing this detail that was completely out of the ordinary for me, and I admit that it started to unsettle me.

I couldn’t make out a face or even a clear outline. Given the contrast between the pitch-black night and the bright light from the ceiling fixture behind, that wasn’t really surprising… but given my proximity and the total darkness of her outline, it looked as though she were glued to the window… stoic.

I thought it was strange, and it intrigued me enough that I stood across the street from my building for a few minutes, pretending to look for my dog with my flashlight and “accidentally” shining the light on the figure a few times. My goal was simply to catch a glimpse of something—a piece of clothing or even a reaction from the figure I was “unintentionally” blinding multiple times—but… nothing. No reaction.

Strange... but I didn’t linger long, a little afraid of really annoying someone, and I continued my walk.

As I walked along the road behind the building, I noticed something even stranger: no light was coming through the kitchen window of that apartment.

Normally, even if the living room door is closed, the light is so bright that you can always see a little bit of it through that window. But there, absolutely nothing.

And suddenly I remembered a detail: when I’d seen the silhouette as I left the building and stood across from it, the living room door behind her was open. So the light should have been visible from the kitchen.  
What’s more, at that moment our living room, located right below, was also lit up because, as a reminder, my roommate was playing online with her friends, so I could clearly see the light from our living room through the kitchen window, which gave me a point of comparison.

At the time, I didn’t think much of it. I told myself that maybe I’d seen things wrong in the dark or that the person had simply turned off the light and gone to bed.

But when I came back from my walk over an hour and a half later, the figure was still in the same spot, standing motionless in front of the window, with the light in their living room still on.

I went back into the building… and then something felt off. That feeling in the hallway… It’s impossible for me to really explain what I felt; all I know, what I’m certain of, is that the very moment I opened the front door, my instinct started screaming at me: “Something’s wrong. **Don’t stay here**.”

Yet I’m a real night owl. Walking alone at midnight or 3 a.m. doesn’t bother me. Strange noises, dark hallways… I’m used to it because, as I said, I always take my dog out during those hours to enjoy the quiet of the night, even when there’s a storm outside (which has given my roommate cold sweats on multiple occasions).

But that night was different. I’ve been scared by some of those noises before, or at least felt anxious… but that night… that feeling… even today, it makes my blood run cold. Especially the first thing I did when I walked in… was to look up at the staircase leading to the upper floors; I felt like I was being watched from that spot, on the small landing of the first flight of stairs before reaching the apartments above. I didn’t even dare turn on the light, as if I were afraid someone would see me. Who? I don’t know; everyone in my building is asleep at this hour. All I know is that he was on the stairs, on that small landing... and that he was getting closer... that’s what my survival instinct was screaming at me. It was as if my brain had sensed that someone dangerous was in front of me, but my eyes couldn’t see him.

The problem is that my apartment door is right next to those same stairs... (literally right next to the first few steps), which meant I had to get close to the source of the danger I felt just to get home...

I’ve never been in such a hurry to get into my apartment; with the key in my hand, I practically leaped in front of my door, and just as I was turning the key in the lock... I really had this unpleasant feeling that something was dangerously closing in on me and was going to grab me, seize me from behind... that I was in immediate danger, that I really had to get to safety quickly. But above all... I **FELT** that it was coming from the staircase. I couldn’t explain it, but I was deeply convinced of it.

Even my dog, who was with me, started scratching at the front door so I’d open it faster (something she’d never done before). When the door opened, I practically slammed it shut and locked it again just as quickly, my heart pounding so hard it hurt. I still remember pressing my body against the door, as if I were convinced someone was going to try to force it open. It wasn’t until I was sure both locks were securely fastened that my body relaxed with a sigh of relief as I felt the adrenaline leave my body.

It was around 2 a.m. My roommate had gone to bed, I was alone in the dark, and I stood in front of the door for a few minutes before going into the living room to put my things down. That’s when I noticed through the window that the light in my upstairs neighbor’s apartment had finally gone out...

There was no way I could sleep that night, especially since for the rest of the night until 5 a.m., I could hear furniture moving in my neighbor’s apartment…

Of course, my roommate didn’t believe me when I told her all this the next day: the sobbing in my room, the silhouette at the window, the sensation in the hallway… especially when I said to her:

“The furniture moves every night, yet yesterday I could see in the light that their living room is empty. **So what is moving every night**?”

Honestly, I ended up thinking that maybe I’d hallucinated. I sometimes suffer from insomnia, and fatigue can play tricks on you. Plus, darkness is always a good catalyst for the imagination...

In fact, the following evening, when I came back from my night walk, this time I didn’t feel any strange sensations or anxiety as I entered the lobby of my building. It was… exactly the same as usual. **Everything** was just as it always was.

I know this sounds like a tall tale, a simple story meant to give you the creeps... but it really happened, even though no one believes me, and unfortunately... I’m not the only one who’s experienced this.

A few weeks after that fateful evening, my neighbor stopped me as I was coming back from walking my dog in the early evening and she was taking hers out, accompanied by her husband.

She asked me if I’d seen anyone suspicious around the building, because their basement had been broken into.

I told them no, but I mentioned the neighbor upstairs, because since he seems to be there all the time, just like me, he might have seen something.

And then, she looked at me with an almost relieved expression.

She told me she’d experienced events similar to mine, even though I hadn’t said anything about it—I’d only mentioned the neighbor. She told me about furniture moving, sobbing… She and her husband had complained to their upstairs neighbors because it kept them awake some nights—it was that loud—and the neighbors had replied that it wasn’t them. They, too, heard the noises… coming from the apartment across from theirs. (That is, my upstairs neighbor.)

Then she asked me:

“Have you ever seen anyone in that apartment?”

I told her about the figure I’d seen a few weeks earlier during my nighttime walk.

She turned pale.

A few days earlier, she had seen the same figure… except it was around 4 p.m. and it was still light out. But she told me it was IMPOSSIBLE for her to make out who the person was; she could only tell that the figure looked like a man. When she approached the building to go home, the figure started screaming and banging hysterically against the window. She was so scared that she turned around and locked herself in her car while waiting for her husband. And even from there, she could still see that dark figure thrashing against her living room window...

They called the real estate agency... and the agency claimed that the apartment had been vacant for months and hadn’t been re-rented. (And I had indeed found the listing on their website at the time, which had been posted a few months earlier and stated that the apartment had been available immediately for a month prior to these events.)

Honestly, I don't know what to make of all this.

Ever since I caught a glimpse of that figure, there hasn't been a single sound—quite the opposite of my neighbor, who kept hearing furniture being moved around even more violently. She also claims that the figure yelled at her, but as I mentioned at the very beginning of my story: My roommate and I were there the whole time. Yet we didn't hear a thing.

The agency finally sent someone to check, suspecting a squatter and having received several complaints from other tenants in the building about furniture moving at night. No one was there. (And I swear, it freaks me out to know that.)

I finally moved out in early 2025.

On the day we left, we had finished emptying the apartment around 11 a.m. to noon, and while my roommate was talking to the driver of the truck we’d rented for our furniture, I was waiting outside near our car in the parking lot behind the building. I looked up, just scanning the area while waiting for him to come back… and I saw it.

The silhouette.

Half-hidden behind a blanket draped over the bedroom window.

The facade faced south, the sun was high in the sky, it was only the first floor, and I was once again just a few meters away... the light was strong, illuminating the corner of the room visible through the slightly lifted blanket, and yet I couldn’t make out anything about her at all—not a face, nor the color of her skin or her clothes. Just that dark shape pressed against the glass, seeming to spy on what was happening.

After a few minutes, she moved away. The corner of the blanket she’d lifted as if to peek fell back in front of the window.

And a few seconds later, I began to hear the accordion tune. The very same one I hadn’t heard in months.

I was... frozen. It wasn’t even fear; I think I was so stunned that I simply forgot to feel any emotion. I thought I was hallucinating, so much so that I asked my roommate if he could hear it too when he came back to me.

He replied:

“Yes. The neighbor must have started training again.”

Then he got into the car. I didn’t say anything. I didn’t dare tell him what I’d seen; he probably wouldn’t have believed me, just like the first time.

All I know is that I didn’t dare move until the song ended. Then I got into the car almost on autopilot to drive away with that knot in my stomach… Before telling myself, “The thing I saw last time saw me… And I think it’s saying goodbye.”

Even today, I still have absolutely no idea what happened. I know what I saw, what I heard, and I know I wasn’t alone, even though no one around me believes me.

The one thing that bothers me the most is why that figure seemed so calm around me.

And I think I’ll remember forever that feeling of “imminent death” I felt when I walked into the lobby that night.
