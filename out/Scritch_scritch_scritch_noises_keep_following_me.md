There is something very soothing about living in the suburbs. The fresh air, the lack of noise, the serenity of nature… suburbs are in an entirely different pocket dimension of the universe, compared to cities.

I moved to my new home in Lavender Park around a month ago, after getting tired of the concrete jungle with all the incessant sounds, noxious smells, and ceaseless traffic, coalescing into one big blinking mess.

I was more than ready for some change of pace after all the drama at work, recently.  
   
And it has been a good decision, over all!

I was welcomed with open arms to my new apartment, and everyone was quite friendly.

The neighbors Karan and Sasha helped me move my stuff into the new apartment and even invited me over for dinner. It was more kindness than I had hoped for as an introvert, who made it throughout life just writing for a living and hiding behind my screens.

The lady at the bakery offered me a cookie on the house, for not using plastic covers and bringing my own tote bag!

The landlord, Mr. Hubert, was understanding and supportive about my anxiety and even took the time to help me figure out my electricity, water, and the thermostat. 

All in all, my first week in my new possibly forever home, was quite pleasant.

Most of my time went into arranging and organizing my house. Rearranging my bookshelf again was quite exciting, and knowing one day that one of my books could be in someone else’s bookshelf thrilled me. 

I also paid a visit to the local grocery mart and farmer’s market to stock up my fridge.

But the rest of my time? I took long walks amongst the woods. Mostly along the hiking trails. The fresh air settled me in a way that the day-to-day frenzy of the city never let me feel.

Even my boss, who is usually an incessant nag, let me take my time to set up before I get back to my pending works and deadlines. 

All-in-all, life couldn’t have been more perfect.

I even found socializing that did not exhaust me… I found that there was a Tuesday night Trivia event at the local pub and the people there were fun and kind!

The problems started on January 18, around 2 AM in the morning. I remember the date and time because I checked my phone when I heard the distinctive sound, like an animal’s wounded whimpers… as though they were trying to escape. My heart lurched into my throat, and I could literally feel each pulse at my throat.

I don’t know why, but I felt like I was able to understand the raw, guttural, and LOuD sounds being made, yet my heart went out to the creature.

I had just made up my mind to go investigate when the sounds suddenly stopped. I kept my ears peeled for any other noises, half in fear, half in hope to hear the sound again. Perhaps, it had outrun whatever was chasing it… and was safe now?

But I heard nothing and found myself falling back into a dreamless slumber.

The next morning when I woke up, I tried to enquire if anyone else had heard any weird noises in the night, but none of my neighbors seemed to know about it. The only odd thing I heard about was the local FM station reporting a brutal murder, which I switched off, not wanting to hear the gruesome details about.

Nevertheless, life went on in Lavender Park, and the world still spun around.

I decided to take my work for the day to the local Fisherman’s Paradise trail and work on completing my editing for the day there. The brooks gushing, leaves rustling, and the bird calls put me in a unique mind space, and I ended up writing two chapters of the book I was currently working on. The weather was warm, and the sunshine perfectly offset the previously cloudy and gloomy days that the town had been experiencing.

I decided to walk the long path back home. I wasn’t someone who was very fond of walks, but nature beckoned, and the weather was too good to pass up the opportunity. I was halfway back home when I heard something.

*Scritch scritch scritch.* 

Something very…. Not dead leaves were making a just-audible-enough sound.

*Scritch Scritch Scritch.* 

I tried to ignore it, but it repeated again, and again, and again.

*Scritch scritch scritch.* 

Now I was not one to discount that, animals exist in the woods and are capable of various noises. But the two discomforting aspects of this particular series of sounds was that it kept increasing in frequency, faster and faster by the minute, and getting louder and louder.

*Scritch scritch scritch.* 

As though getting closer and closer. 

*Scritch Scritch Scritch.* 

To me.

*Scritch scritch scritch.* 

I decided to pick up my pace and see if I could out-walk the sound, and when that didn’t work, I decided to run. I am not an athletic human, and I was definitely worried that the source of the sound would eventually catch up to me.

I ran through the thickets of thorns, my breathing off kilter, my hair a disarray, my backpack bouncing up and down with the weight of my laptop. I could feel a cramp slowly but surely developing in my abdomen, but the adrenaline rush let me run further, instead of collapsing.

It took a while, but after almost slipping, and definitely grazing my hand on some bushes, I did successfully make it home, panting for breath, hoping, and praying that whatever it was, had stopped following me a while back.

Everything was too loud to hear over my heartbeat and my fast breaths trying to suck in air, and I realized my eyes were watering… with red spots in them.

I wiped my eyes and blinked it back open to see… normal tears…

I guess it was the adrenaline?

I closed my eyes, leaning against my door, trying to catch my breath, and trying to hear if I could still hear the scritch scritch noise. I could still hear it in my head even though I didn’t know if I actually could hear it out loud now, over my lungs trying to choke on a breath.

That Tuesday, the trivia night had a new and unique round- Urban Legends, perhaps inspired by the recent reports of another unsolved and unexplained murder in town. 

As a fiction author, I was more than interested to hear about some of the interesting local myths and perhaps find a morsel of inspiration from a different perspective.

Apart from the super popular ones like faeries, the yeti, werewolves and what not, the most intriguing tale of the evening was that of the mystical Whistler. The Whistler seemed to be an undead local haunt, someone who whistled a particular 5 note tune in succession of each other, crescendoing into operatic levels of volume, deafening their victims, for life. The victims were always frozen in fear, and their eventual fates were to keep re-hearing the whistle for the rest of their lives, even in their now “silent” worlds.

More than the eerie whistle that one of my trivia-mates executed, it was the “successive repetition” aspect that kind of spooked me out. It reminded me of the distinct “scritch scritch scritch, scritch scritch scritch” sound that seemed etched into my memory now. 

I went back home unsettled that night, hoping to stave off the nightmares with some background music and tried to get some work done till I had exhausted myself.

Halfway through rewriting the third paragraph of the night, I heard a sound.

Surprisingly, it was not the “scritch scritch scritch” sound.

Instead, this time I heard loud wailing, and the breaking of glass.

Someone seemed to be in trouble!

I rushed to the source of the noise, hoping to stop whatever it was that was happening.

I found myself outside of Karan and Sasha’s house, and paused, not knowing if I would be intruding, but the sounds continued on the other side of the door, and I decided to take a chance…. And found the door open!

I entered the house to find it pitch dark.

I slowly made my way along the wall, pausing to stumble across something on the floor before I could see something on the table from the light through the window.

I pawed the walls until I could find the switches and turn on the light. 

And I found…. A cat.

“Mrowwww” It wailed almost like a human baby. Surrounding the cat were pieces of glass and assorted porcelain.

“Maybe it was just hungry?” I thought, as a relieved chuckle escaped me.

My laughter grew as I stumbled my way to the refrigerator and brought out some milk and a saucer from the shelf nearby to feed the cat.

The cat stared at me, its eyes glinting in the moonlight as it slowly bent down to sample the milk, still glaring at me.

And then, it started drinking the milk.

I laughed again, shaking my head, looking up from the cat into the mirror behind it…. To find my long hair in shambles, like a disgusting curtain over my… bloody face.

I cleaned my eyes and checked again, only to find the same creepy reflection staring back at me in abject horror.

Terrified, I quickly made my way to the next room, knocking, hoping to find either Sasha or Karan to stop myself from spiraling.

On the second knock, the door just gave way and opened, and I entered to find my neighbors’ bloody bodies, without their heads.

A loud high-pitched scream shattered the silence of the night.

Belatedly I realized that it was me who was screaming.

Their bodies were on the bed, and both were missing heads, their necks haphazardly cut, as though clawed by nails rather than a weapon, the blood somewhat dried. The rest of their body was somewhat still in the same clothes I had seen them 3 days back, but in tatters, a pool of blood around them both.

The room reeked of blood and death. It did not smell unpleasant, it just smelled sad.

Then I noticed something- Sasha and Karan’s heads were on top of their dresser cabinet. As though staring at their own decapitated bodies. Their hair looked like someone pulled on it, their eyes and face seemed to be frozen in fear, looking somewhere up, above.

As though my breath was kicked back into me, I ran outside of their house trying not to cry. My neighbours…

I ran to the Landlord’s unit on floor 2. 

“Mr. Hubert”, I knocked on his door and waited, hoping to get someone to help.

Need to go to the police, need to collect evidence, the cat, she needs to be adopted…

After waiting 10 mins, out of sheer frustration I tried the doorknob, and surprisingly, it opened. 

I dreaded walking in… realizing with dawning horror about what this could mean for the residents of this house.

Entering the unit, I could hear their FM radio switched on, to the local channel.

“-Local authorities report a suspicious individual around 5 ft tall, long hair, possibly female, with a pen in hand. Reports are flooding in about a manhunt-” I ignored the report, trying to figure out what to do. 

Should I wait for the police? Should I try and see if Mr. Hubert was still alive?

I walked further in the messy house, heart sinking as I took note of all the things strewn on the floor. Signs of a struggle.

Perhaps I am too late…

And then I heard the television from 3 rooms down.

Perhaps, not?

What if the killer was still in the house?

What chance would I have? Against an actual Killer?

But if Mr. and Mrs. Hubert are still alive, would it be worth the risk to face this evil by myself?

Steeling myself, I took the baseball bat nearby in my hand, clutching it like the lifeline it may be.

I slowly made my way towards the room, the voice from the TV being that of the News reporter of Channel L News, which I tuned into at times.

I slowly opened the door, only to find the Landlord on the bed, in the same state as my neighbors. His head was on the table, bleeding from the neck, eyes unseeing, face frozen in shock, fixated on something above….

An involuntary whimper escaped me as I tried to not collapse right there.

My head turned to the news on its own accord…. To find…

Myself on-screen...

“A potentially dangerous and feral individual has been reported of massacring individuals with just their hands and apparently a single pen. A video has been leaked, showing them to have been-”

It was me, on the screen.

With my long hair, not combed or braided. My teeth were set in the largest grin, which I did not realize that my face was capable of making. My good luck charm, the pen my father gave me, which I usually tucked into my pocket before I started working every day, was clutched in my hand like a dart… or an injection. I was facing 4 policemen in what I recognized to be the county police station. When the first one came at me on-screen, I ducked, and punched them from the behind. They crumpled, instantly.

When the other two moved, the person who resembled me, parkoured on screen and scratched the eyes of one of the policemen while kicking the other. Another 10 seconds, and the footage sped up to show all four policemen on the floor, collapsed. The footage cut just as the on-screen me was aiming the pen at their neck, an inhumanly laugh… sounding just like me, from a few moments ago.

And then I heard it.

Tap tap tap.

Tap Tap Tap.

Tap tap tap.

A faint sound. Repeating again and again, from the closet. I spun around, slowly, the baseball bat still in my hand, as I heard the wretched repetition again.

I opened the closet door to find…. Mrs. Hubert. Deaf and mute Mrs. Hubert, her mouth wide open in a silent scream as she tap-tap-tapped, again and again frantically, as she saw me.

My hand suddenly let go of the bat, as my vision whitened. My hand was suddenly clutching my lucky pen in hand, and I could see the maniacal grin on my face in the reflection of the wooden closet’s varnished surface.

And then the world turned red.
