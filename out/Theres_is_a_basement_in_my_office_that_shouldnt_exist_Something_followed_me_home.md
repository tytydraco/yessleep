I first noticed something was very wrong when the time skipped 15 minutes. 

It was not like I dosed off, felt dizzy, or anything like that. 

One moment I saw my computer monitor say it was 2:32, I glanced away, and then saw that my monitor said it was 2:47. 

My heart skipped a beat when I noticed. I knew that barely a few seconds had passed and I quickly checked my email and documents to see if I did anything but found nothing. 

Moments like these kept on happening throughout the week. 

One time I was unloading my groceries from my car and then found myself putting the last item in the fridge. Another time I found a basket of dirty laundry I knew I just cleaned. 

Of course I thought I was going mental, but I was busy with work or what not and didn’t want to deal with a psychologist. 

It got really bad when I needed to retrieve files from my office basement.

 I got the email from a higher up saying that the files were needed for an upcoming project. I sent a reply mentioning I was busy but he didn’t take my excuse. 

So, I did what anyone who was experiencing episodes of memory loss would do, I went down there. 

I checked my watch as soon as I left my desk. It was 1:14pm.

 I then opened the door to the staircase and went down two stories, my feet echoing down louder than they should’ve been. 1:15pm.

 I proceeded to walk through a series of corridors leading to the basement elevator, pressed the button, and went inside as soon as the doors opened. 1:17pm. 

That was when things stopped being normal.

As the elevator began to descend, I felt a sudden pressure in my head, and felt everything become heavier as if the air itself became denser. 

I grasped the railing with my right hand as the dim light on the ceiling began to flicker. 

*You’re better than this.* I thought to myself. *Don’t let this episode control you. Not now.* I glanced at my wrist for reassurance. 1:18pm. 

The elevator then dinged signaling that it finally reached its floor. 

I looked up at the floor display on the elevator wall and saw that it said B3, even though there should’ve been only two basement floors. 

At least the pressure in my head had subsided. 

I looked out the elevator and groups of filing cabinets and shelves scattered throughout a medium sized room, with corridors leading out from each wall. 

As I walked out of the elevator into the room the pressure in my head increased exponentially. 

My vision blurred and saw a blinding flash as I tried to walk further. My head felt like it was about to cave in on itself, and then burst out in a giant eruption scattering all of brain matter in the process. 

The pain in my head then transformed into nausea, with a slurry of a yellow-green liquid being ejected from my innards. 

I then briefly gripped reality again, and tried to figure out what I needed to do. *Cabinet 36B. You need to go to 36B.* 

I kept on repeating this to myself as I stumbled around the room, with sharp flashes of brightness, color, and pain occurring with each step. 

After what seemed like ages I finally stumbled to what I believed was the right cabinet. 

I was able to gleam that the number was in its 30s, and that was good enough for me. My hand fumbled around the cabinet until I found the handle and opened it. 

The pain then increased even more and my legs collapsed from under me. I gripped the cabinet so tightly that my fingers became numb, and my eyes closed tight with no intention to open. 

After a few moments I was able to get a slight grip on myself, and reached into the cabinet. 

What I felt wasn’t a file, it felt round like a cylinder, with areas that were rough and smooth. 

I then mustered all the strength I could to lift it out, ignoring every feeling of pain, nausea, and weakness which all reached a level I never thought remotely possible.

All of a sudden I found myself at my desk, midway through typing an email to my coworker John. 

The pain that was so strong had made me think I was about to die, was now completely gone. 

With a feeling of dread my eyes slowly drifted down to the bottom right corner of my monitor. 1:14pm.

I tried to continue through my day normally after that, finishing all my tasks for work and what not. I kept on checking the time as I went through my work but the time acted, well, normal.

When the clock finally hit 5:00pm, I did not hesitate to pack my things and leave the building. 

Whatever happened, or didn’t happen, to me in that basement, I needed to be as far away from there as possible. 

As I walked through the parking lot to my car I looked around in paranoia, but the only thing I saw were other coworkers walking to the cars. 

That was until I glanced at the back exit of the office building. 

Standing against the wall was a man in a dark suit. The lighting was dim but I was able to make out the man was narrow and had short hair. 

I shrugged it off, saying to myself that it was just a guy waiting for a late night meeting. I then unlocked my car and began the commute to my apartment.

With the whole mental breakdown that happened to me in the afternoon, I thought my day couldn’t get any more weird. 

I then saw myself cooking dinner through my apartment window. 

It was not me seeing myself in a third person perspective. I was standing outside my apartment. 

But there, walking through the kitchen, was a man who shared every physical aspect of me cooking the chicken stir fry I’ve been making nearly every night.

I took a deep breath, reassuring myself that I was just hallucinating, then unlocked my apartment door. 

The man did not disappear, nor did his appearance change. He just continued his cooking, shaking the pan every so often and not even glancing up as I walked in.

“What are you doing in my apartment?” I said, quieter than I intended to. “I said, what are you currently doing in my apartment right now?” 

He still didn’t look up, now adding various seasonings to the pan. 

“Listen, if you don’t get the hell out of here, I’m going to call the police.” I said, finally with some reassurance as I pulled out my cellphone. “Don’t make me do it”

I proceeded to dial numbers 9 and 1 on my cell and made the display face my copy. “Get, out, right, now” I said in a hushed yet angry tone. 

I don’t know if he caught my bluff, since there was no way I was going to tell the police I was experiencing yet another psychotic episode, but he proceeded to do my routine and pull out a plate to serve himself. 

It was then when I finally snapped.

“Stop this crap! Right now! You’re going to put the plate down, and get out of this apartment! I don’t why the heck you look like me but if you don’t get out of here I am going to call the authorities! And if they don’t come, I’ll deal with you myself!” 

I then grabbed a kitchen knife and held it threateningly. 

My whole outrage didn’t even faze him. The whole time, he was transferring the food to the plate and then put it on the kitchen counter. 

However he must have gotten the message though, because he walked across the room, went through the door, and just walked away. I followed him to my front door to see where he went, but he was gone. 

I proceeded to put the kitchen knife away and then called my work.

“Hey Melinda, hope your night is going well. I am going to miss the staff meeting tomorrow morning, just some family things which came up.”

“Oh, that’s ok. Just be there when you can.”

“Thank you, have a good night”

I then proceeded to call a psychologist.

Dr. Eric Weston. 

I only heard of the guy because of a conversation I’d eavesdropped on by one of my coworkers. 

The conversation was about some relative losing his grip and that the doctor treated delusional patients responsibly without sending them to a mental hospital. I fit the criteria quite well. 

When I found the number for his practice the receptionist told he was booked for a good few weeks, but when I told them about the incident in the basement and how I saw a duplicate of myself, I got an appointment for the following morning.

I drove into the strip mall ten minutes before my appointment, eyeing the various storefronts which may have been thriving a few years ago, but were now either abandoned or struggling to maintain business. 

The front of Dr. Weston’s practice was the only place which didn’t have various stains on its exterior. 

I opened the door and heard an electronic chime as I walked through. The receptionist gave me a smile and told me Dr. Weston would meet with me shortly. When my name was called, I was led into his office.

Dr. Weston had a relatively slim frame and was a good six inches taller than me. He had some gray strands in his short brown hair, but looked to be in his mid 40s. He gestured towards a cushioned chair and told me to take a seat. 

“Hello,” he said in a calm tone of voice. “How have you been feeling lately?”

“Not great,” I said hesitantly. “Your receptionist must have told you about my recent experiences. They have been quite extreme, and I want them to stop.” 

“Yes, of course. I do know a few things about your recent experiences, but it would benefit me greatly if you elaborated on them further. If you don’t want to, I would understand.”

“No, I’ll tell you,” I then proceeded to go on a 30 minute monologue about all the ways I was slowly losing my mind.

After I finally finished talking about my encounter with my duplicate. Dr. Weston looked at me with a look of concern, and maybe a hint of fear. After a long period of silence, he responded.

“What did the object look like?”

“What object?”

“The object that you found in the filing cabinet, what did it look like?”

“I don’t know, I couldn’t see. The pain was so strong I couldn’t even keep my eyes open.”

“Do you have any recollection of what it could’ve felt like? Did it make any sounds? Did you appear in your office immediately after touching it?”. 

He asked these questions in an increasingly rapid succession and seemed rather desperate for an answer.

“Why the heck do you need to know all this?” I said in an angry tone. “I am currently telling you that I am going crazy, and the only thing you care about is what a random thing I touched felt like!?”

After I said this his look of desperation receded. 

“I’m sorry,” he responded. “I can’t give you a diagnosis at the moment, but I will examine my notes and will contact you shortly. These episodes should not escalate, but if they do, call me a call.”

“What?” I considered going on a rant about how he barely did anything but I decided to hold back. “Ok, make sure to contact me alright?”

“Don’t worry, I will.”

As I walked out of his office, I could’ve sworn I heard the doctor whisper “Lock your doors. Lock your doors”

I didn’t give those words much thought, after all, I was getting used to seeing and hearing things all the time at this point. 

I made it to my work shortly after my lunch break, and went through my day without the time acting weird or me going into another episode. That all changed when I came back home.

As I drove home from work, I considered the possibility of it being there, but I hoped that my mind wouldn’t do that to me again. 

I was very wrong. 

It was sitting at my dining table, digging into the stir fry he never got to eat the day before. Around him were the various pans it used to cook, put in similar but not quite the same places I always place them. 

I hesitated before opening my door. I knew it had to be all in my brain. If I could ignore it long enough, maybe it would just disappear. 

I opened my door and walked over to my freezer, taking out a microwavable dinner and ignoring the duplicate of me sitting to my right. A

s my food began to thaw in the microwave, my duplicate paid no attention to me, but when my dinner had around 30 seconds to go, it twisted its neck unnaturally far to make eye contact with me. 

“Get, out, right, now”. It managed to say these words with the same exact tone of voice I had said them the night prior. “What are you currently doing in my apartment right now?”

I clenched my fist and allowed myself to take a breath. I wasn’t going to lose my composure again, not after I snapped the way I did the night before. 

“Don’t make me do it.” it said randomly.

I proceeded to give it no response, turning my head away so I couldn’t see it stare at me.

My duplicate then began to say things I never said to it directly. It said various phrases I had said to coworkers, things I mumbled to myself, even some of the things I said in my appointment with Dr. Weston. 

I continued to ignore it, but it was getting increasingly difficult even as I took the food out of the microwave and picked at it with my fork. 

After a few minutes it ran out of phrases of mine to say, but after a few moments it spoke again.

“You’re a screw up aren’t you”

The sudden and unexpectedness of the sentence caused me to instinctively look at it. I then quickly shifted my gaze back to my plate.

“You are a worthless person who no one cares about. You will lose your mind, you will be institutionalized, and then everyone will forget about you.”

As it said those words I was filled with a feeling of sudden dread. I tried to continue ignoring it, but I couldn’t help the words from getting under my skin.

“You can’t get rid of me. You have no control. You will never have control. You will never be norm-”

“Shut up!!” My sudden outburst finally got it to stop talking. “Get out of my fucking head, or I will kill you. Understand?”

I grabbed the kitchen knife, but not as an empty threat. I had every intention to stab that thing and finally get rid of it.

“So you’re going to just sit there or what!?” I yelled.

It did not respond, but instead chose to get up from its chair and stare at me.

I felt a thick film of rage drape over me. My face became hot and my fists became clenched. I then lunged at my duplicate with the knife.

In one nearly instantaneous movement it swung its fist back and struck me in the chest well before I could even bring the knife down.

I felt myself being launched across the room, with an eruption of pain and an audible crack occurring as I hit the back wall. 

I sat there on a floor with my back against the wall in a daze of pain as my duplicate slowly walked over to me and kicked me so hard in the stomach that stars filled my vision. 

I then fell over onto my left side with a series of coughs and gasps coming from me as I attempted desperately to get air back into my lungs.

As I tried to regain my focus, I saw the kitchen knife laying on the floor only a few feet away. 

My duplicate drew its foot back in an attempt to kick me again, when I gained a burst of energy and swung my legs away so that it kicked the wall instead. I heard a loud crack and saw in the corner of vision that its foot broke through the drywall. 

I then swung my arm out, grabbed the knife, and scrambled onto my feet, all the while my duplicate was trying to get its foot out of the wall. 

When it did,  it gave me an expression which still unsettles me now.

 I wasn’t rage nor was it fear, it was the most neutral expression one can make. It was as if it was staring at a tv screen, with it having no emotional regard for me whatsoever.

We stared at each other for a few moments, my duplicate staring at me with its straight, neutral expression while I held my stomach trying to keep my feet on the ground.

It then started running towards me and I threw my knife towards it. The knife hit it in the shoulder and I heard it make a shriek that managed to be in both a high and low tone at the same time.

 I used the opportunity to run down the hallway and slam my bedroom door. I scanned the room and quickly used my office chair to barricade the door. 

Not long after I heard a series of thuds one after the other against the door. It made no yells or screeches, only a constant banging produced in perfectly even intervals.

I knew I didn’t have much time, so I opened my window, squeezed through it, and scampered down the fire escape. 

As I jumped into the alleyway below I felt a surge of pain redevelop from my abdomen, and a swell of vomit ejected from me. 

I stumbled through the alleyway, with my plan being to get near a crowd so that I wouldn’t be followed. 

I then got blinded by a pair of headlights from a car which emerged ahead of me. When it got close enough, I saw that it was a black car with tinted windows. 

The car slowed to a stop and a man emerged from the driver’s side. I was quite surprised to see the face of Dr. Weston looking straight at me.

“Get in the car, now” said Dr. Weston. He managed to keep a similar professional tone he had with me at the therapy session, but I could detect a sense of urgency in his voice. 

I heard objects being thrashed around in my room above us and I made the logical decision to jump in the passenger side. The car then accelerated rapidly and turned onto the main road. 

“Why are you here? How did you know I was in trouble?” I asked.

“Your session with me gave me most of what was needed to know.”

“What’s that supposed to mean?”

The doctor glanced towards my direction, then looked back at the road. “Let me break this down clearly for you. You have been experiencing what we call anomalies, things that are supposed to be hidden, but end up revealing themselves one way or another.” 

“Ok, so I haven’t been hallucinating?”

“Well, you have, everyone has, but I digress. The point of instability occurred right underneath your office, and for one reason or another, you ended up heading down there despite the horrid physical symptoms you must have experienced.”

I nodded, trying to make sense of what I was being told. Dr. Weston took a pause, and then continued.

“Whatever you did down there made the anomaly much worse, and now has expanded significantly beyond the premises of the building. You now need to do what needs to be done in order to deescalate this situation and prevent much unneeded devastation.”

I then noticed that the route we were headed was all too familiar.

“No, we’re not heading back there!” I responded. “If what you’re saying is actually real, then going back down there will most likely kill me. I’m not taking that risk.”

“If they could’ve sent an agent like me to deal with it they would’ve. But the fact they directed me to send you indicates that only the source of escalation can fix it.”

I sat in my seat in a state of anxiety, contemplating if I should open the door and run.

“Of course we can’t force you. But you must know that if you don’t comply, many people will die.”

I took a moment to respond, looking out the window at all the familiar buildings I may never see again. “Fine, I’ll do it, but only if you give me some answers first. How is it possible that a duplicate of me exists with super human abilities, and that a metal canister can apparently risk the lives of an entire city?”

Dr. Weston continued to direct his gaze at the road, but did finally respond back. “I am legally obligated to not tell you much. What I can say though, is that the physical characteristics of our world that we thought we knew for sure were stable started breaking as of five years ago, and that this isn’t the first time an incident like yours has occurred.”

As he said this, the car pulled into the familiar office parking lot and parked right next to the office entrance.

“Well,” I said. “If that’s all you’re going to tell me then I better be on my way”.

I opened the car door, looking back at the doctor who was managing to keep a calm expression, and then walked through the front doors of the office building.

Surprisingly, the office was empty. 

Whoever Dr. Weston was working for must have had the premises evacuated. Luckily, or unluckily,  the electricity was running, so I knew I could take the elevator down into the basement I dreaded entering. 

When the elevator doors closed, I felt a strange sense of calmness. 

Either way, my situation would soon be over. Either I would die from the awful symptoms produced by whatever that thing was in the basement, or I would somehow survive and save many lives. 

Either option was much better than being a hallucinating psychopath for the rest of my life.

The lights in the elevator began to flicker rapidly and I felt strong sharp pain emerge in the front of my skull. It wasn’t nearly as debilitating as it was the first time though, so maybe I stood a chance. 

The elevator chime then rang with the doors opening soon after. Besides the pain in my head and a slight feeling of nausea, I was feeling pretty good.

I looked up at what I thought would be the room with all the filing cabinets, but only saw a long narrow corridor with a concrete floor and fluorescent lights at even distances. 

I walked down, noting how the pain was slowly escalating the further I got. 

The corridor was surprisingly long. Ever so often I found a door, but it was either a storage closet or it was locked. 

After around a few minutes of monotonous walking, I heard an all too familiar screech. I looked back and there it was. 

While I could make out the figure down the hall behind me did have the same face as me, it was now noticeably taller to the point where its head was only a few inches below one of the fluorescent lights. 

It stood there for a moment, staring at me with its neutral expression. It then started sprinting. 

At that moment I let all of my thoughts leave my consciousness and focused solely on the fact that I couldn’t let that thing catch up to me. 

My legs began pistons, accelerating and increasing my velocity with every step I took. My vision narrowed, focusing must be the vague outline of a doorway at the end of the hall. 

Even with my surprisingly fast speed, I heard a series of stomps behind which only increased in volume and frequency. 

I focused my attention on my legs, using every ounce of willpower to make them move just a little bit faster. 

The pressure in my head however was worsening to the point where my vision began to blur. A deep burning began to spread through my lungs and legs because of my prolonged and unexpected burst of physical activity. 

It wasn’t long until one of my legs finally gave way and I crashed onto the cold concrete floor.

I put my arms forward to block my fall and tried desperately to get back onto my feet. 

It didn’t take long however for the clattering of footsteps behind me to reach my position, and I felt myself being lifted by jacket and thrown against the wall. 

My chin hit the hard surface with a loud thud, and I felt the metallic taste of my own blood in my mouth. 

I looked back to see my duplicate. Its appearance was now significantly different. 

While it did maintain the same facial features, its limbs and torso were significantly lengthened beyond what should be normal. 

It looked like it maintained the same amount of body mass, but was stretched in every direction to make a more lanky spindly figure. Its skin was also more pale, with its veins a sickly green. 

The long twisted arm which was grasped on my jacket pulled back and violently shoved me into the wall again, this time with my head crashing into it. 

The pain in my head got exponentially worse, and a flash of light blinded me momentarily. 

As it drew its arm back to shove me again, I came to my senses and reached into my jacket pocket. 

My duplicate let out a loud screech as I drove my pocket knife into its wrist. It was a good thing I grabbed it before jumping out my apartment window. 

As I stumbled away from my duplicate, I saw that its blood was not the dark red I expected, but instead an oily black. Its blood began to spew all onto the floor, but I knew it wouldn’t be stalled for long.

 I stumbled through the hallway, seeing how the doorway ahead couldn’t be more than 50 feet away, but the more I willed my legs to move faster the more my head pain began to swell.

My duplicate began to make a series of uneven steps towards my direction, giving the occasional screech which was more in anger rather than pain.

 I continued willing my legs to move despite the blinding pain, but I knew it was gaining on me. The doorway began getting close, but the lumbering footsteps behind me couldn’t have been more than a few feet away. 

Right when I grabbed the door handle I felt myself being yanked back, with a cold wet hand brushing against the back of my neck. 

My hand lost its grip on the door handle right when I twisted it open. Out of instinct, I shoved myself forward pulling myself out of my jacket. I then dashed through the open doorway and slammed the door with all the strength I could. 

It was too late though, with a long spindly arm going through the doorway before I was able to close it. 

The arm began to thrash violently trying to pry the door open. I pushed against the door extra hard and began hearing the mashing of flesh and the crunch of broken bone. 

After an excruciating amount of seconds, I felt the door click into place and the once thrashing arm became limp. 

I took a second to catch my breath. My duplicate was still screeching behind the door, and I felt its thuds against the door. The lock wouldn’t hold for long.

I walked around the room, noting how the blinding pain in my skull got worse in certain parts of it. I looked at each filing cabinet, looking for the numbers 36B. 

I then saw it, right on the floor. I could tell it was a round cylinder, but the more I tried to make out its details the more blurry it became. 

The door then burst open from behind me. My duplicate emerged, now with a long hanging piece of flesh which was once its right arm.

It charged towards me with a terrifying speed, and even before I could even turn to run I felt it slam into me to shove me into the ground. 

I looked up at it, and saw how its eyes were bloodshot while its face remained neutral. I tried to squirm my way out but to no avail. It then brought its functional arm high above the both of us, and then brought it down in one powerful stab. 

As I felt its hand dig into the flesh of my back, the pain that was once so strong in my skull became barely noticeable. 

After what seemed like ages, I was able to concentrate enough to drive my knee straight into its abdomen. 

It gave off another loud shriek and fell on its side, giving me the opportunity to crawl on the floor, slowly making my way towards the cylinder. 

It was not long though until I felt a strong tug on my leg. I quickly grabbed the bottom of a cabinet to make sure I wouldn’t be dragged back. 

The tugging transformed into a series of yanks which got progressively stronger. I kept my grip though, doing anything not to let my duplicate drag me.

 I then heard a loud pop and an explosion of pain on my hip as my leg dislocated. 

I looked behind and saw that my duplicate began to stand. I used my good leg to kick at it, making it fall on the floor once again. It was now or never. 

As the duplicate was tugging on leg, I noticed that the cabinet was off balance. I hoped to use that to my advantage.

 In one motion, I managed to get my arm to the other side of the cabinet, and pulled on it as hard as I could. 

The next few moments happened very slowly. The cabinet slowly leaned forward, as if questioning whether it should succumb to physics or defy them entirely. It then began to fall towards the ground, gaining speed in each passing second. 

As the shadow fell on top of me I was hit with the realization that I was going to be crushed as well. At the last moment though, I swung my legs around, ignoring every bit of stabbing pain. 

The cabinet missed me by an inch, but I heard a series of muffled screams coming from below the cabinet.

I shoved myself through the final couple feet and grabbed a hold of the cylinder. The cabinet behind me then flew into the air and I saw my duplicate get onto its two feet, for once, with a look of rage in its eyes.

 I looked to my side and saw that cabinet 36B was only a few feet away. 

With one excruciating drag I made my way closer. My duplicate began to make a series slow stumbling steps with thuds occurring after each movement. 

I felt my injured torso meeting the grittiness of the concrete floor as I dragged myself again. The thuds became louder.

I launched my hand onto the cabinet handle and threw it open. A cold foot pressed upon my back blinding me with agony. 

I thought about Dr. Weston, his look of concern and absolute fear when I told him what happened. I thought about the time skippages, and how it began my downward spiral. 

I wasn’t going to let some mutant who happened to look like me ruin everyone else's life. 

I shoved the cylinder in with one fluid motion. I heard a click and saw a flash of blinding white light just as my duplicate made its final shriek.

The buzzing of the familiar fluorescent lights occurred above me as I found myself back at my office. I patted down my torso and leg and noticed that they were both completely fine. 

On my computer screen was a half written email to my co-worker John. As my eyes drifted towards the bottom right corner of my monitor, I noticed the familiar time of 1:17pm.

I haven’t seen Dr. Weston since then. The building that was once his office was an abandoned graffiti covered storefront which looked like it had been that way for years. 

So far, the time skippages have stopped, and it looks like everything that happened after my first trip in the basement got reverted somehow. 

Because of all this you may think I would be relieved. I was at first. 

But when I got out of my car to walk to my apartment, I saw a figure with my appearance washing dishes through my window.
