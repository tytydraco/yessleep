My husband Mark and I always loved going to the annual church Easter egg hunt- we brought our little girl Maisie with us as she was very competitive and looked forward to 'crushing the other kids with my sick egg finding skills', according to her. Usually the church committee comes together to organise it but I'd been asked to play a part in it, to hide some of the eggs and then announce the winner once I'd counted their eggs. I was ecstatic, it felt nice that the community trusted me to be a part of this and I knew Maisie would be happy. So, Mark, Maisie and I made our way up to the church to help set up.

The area was a lovely little grassy field next to the church that was dotted with picnic tables and swaying trees. I left Maisie with Mark and made my way over to where the committee was crowded around one of the tables. Suddenly, one of the girls in the huddle turned around and squealed with glee.

"EMMA!! Oh my god, I'm so glad you could make it!"

The girl who turned around was Natalie, one of my best friends and one of the church committee members. I turned and gave her a tight hug, smiling and giggling whilst we quickly caught up with each other. 

"Yeah, it turns out I'm helping with the eggs as well! I'll be hiding them same as you and helping announce the winner!" she beamed, grabbing my hands in hers.

I laughed and broke away from the group. Half of us were designated to go and hide the eggs whilst the other half tried to distract the gaggle of kids for as long as possible. I could see Maisie trying to snoop but I gave her a frown and she quickly turned around again. Natalie giggled and went back to hiding the eggs.

After a while, the kids were unleashed on the eggs, felt baskets swinging wildly as the ran from place to place, rummaging through bushes and lifting anything they could carry to check underneath. All the parents and committee members sat on the benches at the side, laughing and chatting whilst keeping an eye on the kids.

"Mummy! Auntie Natalie! I found an extra one as well!!"

I frowned a little. There weren't any 'extra' ones as far as I knew, but there was a farm nearby so I guessed Maisie must have found a chicken egg that was dropped over onto this field. I thought to go and tell her to drop it, but thought otherwise and just let her run around the field trying to find the rest of the eggs. I'd be looking through them at the end anyway, so I could check what it was then.

A little while later one of the committee members blew a whistle and the gaggle of kids came bounding towards the table where we'd count their eggs. I could already hear my daughter bragging about her win to the other children and arguing childishly about how she'd definitely found more eggs and she'd even found the special secret one.

I rolled my eyes and huffed a laugh at Maisie's bravado and went to count her eggs. I rifled through the bright plastic eggs and shielded them from the grabby little hands that tried to reach for them. There, at the bottom of the basket, was a slightly smaller sickly-looking egg. I reached my hand down and brushed a finger against the shell- a faint greyish colour that felt smooth and sticky to the touch, covered in a dark brown film. It stained the pale pink felt of the bottom of the basket a faint dark brown. I saw that there was a small hole in the bottom which left a fine crack around the side. I looked around and met Maisie's eyes, frowning deeply.

"Where did you find this egg honey?"

Maisie peered over the rim of the basket and excitedly pointed to one of the older trees surrounded by bushes in the far end of the field.

"I told you mummy, it's an extra special one! You must've put it out specially for me, it was super hard to find but I'm super good at finding." 

I looked at Natalie for answers, but she just shrugged her shoulders and looked as lost as I did.

Natalie picked the egg up to look at it closer. She spotted the hole and moved it closer to her eye to try and see.

The egg suddenly burst open, fine spores spraying the air around her. I jerked away from her and yanked Maisie back in tow. A jet of dark brown spores shot into Natalie's eyes and open mouth, covering them entirely. She let out a piercing scream, chucking the shards from her hands and clawing at her eyes and throat. In a panic she rammed a finger down her throat and started gagging into the grass- but nothing came up. Tears streamed down her face in an effort to clean her eyes as she wailed for help but all we could do was stand there in horror watching her retch with all her might.

"HELP ME! ugh... it feels... it feels l..like" 

She paused to retch harshly, her whole body trembling wildly,

"IT... FEELS LIKE THERE'S SOME..THING IN MY THROA-"

 Natalie coughed and coughed and coughed but nothing would come out.

All of the kids had now run screaming, either hiding behind their parent's legs or running into the church behind.

I broke out of my stupor and shouted for Mark to take Maisie and the rest of the kids away. I grabbed a water bottle off of the table and went to hold Natalie's bawling face. The spores had covered her tongue and throat entirely in a dark black fur that sprayed out wildly with each cough and trailed down her chin with drool. Her eyes had turned a shocking bloody red, harsh tears spilling out of her eyes spreading the spores over her face. 

I tried to wash her eyes out with the water but nothing would work. She went from shoving her fingers down her throat and scratching at her eyes wildly and screaming in horror and pain.

All of a sudden, the remaining colour drained out of her sweaty face and she jolted forward and heaved into the ground. I could hear something coming up and looked away as I felt my own stomach turn. She gargled and convulsed until I heard something heavy thump against the grass. She passed out in my arms, slumping over and turning into dead weight.

Covered in black spores and a foamy red bile was an egg- a plastic, bright pink egg. I brushed the hair out of Natalie's face and tried to splash the water in her throat to wake her up, but all she was doing was lying limp and leaking red foam from her lips. I picked her up with all of my might and tried to sling her over my shoulder, but that just caused her body to convulse and shudder once more as she choked up another egg over my back, this time a bright yellow- leaving a waterfall of black vomit on my top.

I screamed for Mark to call an ambulance- he came rushing towards me from the church and watched in horror as Natalie's lips turned blue as she began to choke. I looked inside of her mouth again and could see a bright pale blue egg was lodged in her throat. I sighed, apologised to Natalie and reached my hand down her throat to try and dislodge the egg. In my efforts, I cleared the blockage slightly and she coughed and gasped for air- spraying a fine black mist of spores directly into my face. I dropped her in shock and went to wash my own face as Mark picked up the sagging body of Natalie to bring her to the ambulance.

Later, in the hospital, the doctors did an x-ray of Natalie's stomach- in the ambulance ride her stomach had bulged and bloated grotesquely and all she could do was cry for help. In the x-ray, according to the horrified retelling of my husband, Natalie's stomach and oesophagus was packed full of shiny plastic easter eggs. The doctors had tried to get a sample of the spores that covered her eyes and mouth but they seemed to fuse to her skin, turning the inside of her mouth a dark, muddy brown and her eyes a pallid red.

She didn't last much longer.

Since all this happened, the atmosphere in my house and our community has been dire. I had to gently explain to Maisie what had happened, in the best way I couldn't- even I didn't understand how it happened. In all of this, I've felt my own health deteriorate. I guess the stress of dealing with Natalie's affairs and arranging her funeral has taken its own toll on me. I've been dealing with such a sore throat all I do is cough these days. It had gotten to the point where my coughing and hacking was keeping me up at night and I couldn't keep any food down in case I choked on it. 

I was trying to fall asleep one night when dread filled the pit in my stomach. I lurched forward and fell over my own feet trying to stumble to the bathroom. I made it in front of the toilet and coughed with all my might, feeling something hard come up my throat. I cried for my husband and he came rushing towards me and rubbed my back with worry. 

After what felt like forever I finally choked up what I'd been dreading it would be.

A shiny orange easter egg covered in blood. I panicked and asked Mark to check my throat with his phone torch for me. 

All the colour drained out of his face.

"Emma... you... your throat..."

He took a photo and handed his phone to me.

There, in the back of my throat, a large patch of furry, dark brown spores.
