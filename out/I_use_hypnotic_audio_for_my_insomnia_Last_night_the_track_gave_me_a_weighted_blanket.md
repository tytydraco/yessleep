I didn’t leave the house. 

I know how stupid that sounds. I had a chemical burn on my cheek from a creature that shouldn't exist, and a bedroom window shattered outward into the Montana snow. I should have packed a bag. I should have driven into town and slept in a brightly lit motel lobby. 

But I couldn't. The hypervigilance had hollowed me out entirely. I was walking around with my nerves on the outside of my skin. Every gust of wind, every settling floorboard, every distant truck gear grinding on the highway felt like a physical strike. The human world was too bright, too sharp, and far too loud. 

I taped heavy cardboard and thick plastic sheeting over the shattered window. I locked the bedroom door. I dragged my mattress into the absolute center of the room, away from the walls, away from the draft. 

I sat in the dark, my phone glowing in my hands. The Foundation OS System Manual was still open. 

**SKU 01: THE COAT.**
*The Hack: A Low-Pass "Muffle" filter acting as a sonic weighted blanket.*
*The Target: Deep Pressure Therapy (DPT) simulation... forcing your sympathetic nervous system to stand down.*

I didn't want to sleep. I just wanted the armor. I needed the shield the manual promised. If the first track stripped the human mask, the second track promised to cover the raw nerves. 

I put my heavy noise-canceling headphones over my ears. I laid flat on my back, staring up at the dark plaster ceiling. 

I hit play. 

There was no industrial hum this time. Just a thick, heavy brown noise—a deep, 600Hz rumble that felt like sitting in the belly of an idling submarine. 

Then, her voice. Wet, intimate, and impossibly slow. 

*“Come in,”* the woman whispered. *“Close the door. Lock it.”*

A slow, metronomic pulse began oscillating between my ears. Left ear. Right ear. 

*“Good. You look cold, little wolf. Not the kind of cold that comes from snow, but the kind that comes from being exposed.”*

The cadence of her voice was magnetic, dragging my brainwaves down into the heavy mud of the bass. 

*“Take a deep breath in… hold it at the top… Primal.”* My autonomic nervous system surrendered immediately. The tension in my jaw snapped. My limbs sank into the mattress. I was chemically paralyzed by the audio, locked into a state of deep, heavy compliance. 

*“Tonight, we are going to build your armor,”* she murmured, her voice vibrating against my sternum. *“We are going to grow your winter coat.”*

Above me, the shadows on the ceiling shifted. 

I couldn't move my head, but my eyes strained upward. The darkness directly above my bed was pooling, gathering mass. It looked like a massive, thick patch of black mold was peeling away from the plaster. 

*“Focus on the back of your neck,”* the audio commanded. *“Imagine a warmth starting there. Dark. Heavy. Dense. I am going to give you a word. Every time I say it, the coat gets heavier.”*

The mass on the ceiling detached. 

It didn't fall; it lowered itself, completely silent, descending on four thick, double-jointed limbs. It was massive. Broad. It looked like a grotesque canopy of pale, matted flesh, covered in patches of thick, greasy, gray hair. It smelled of stale dust, stagnant air, and wet wool. 

*“The word is thicken,”* the woman whispered. *“Feel it spreading across your shoulders now. Thicken.”*

The creature dropped the last two feet. Its massive, heavy bulk settled directly over my shoulders. 

The breath was driven from my lungs in a silent gasp. The weight was immense. It was like a heavy lead vest had been dropped across my collarbones, pinning me flat to the mattress. The thick, greasy hair of its underbelly pressed into my skin. It was suffocatingly warm. 

*“Thicken,”* the voice echoed. *“Moving up your neck now… covering the back of your head.”*

The entity shifted its mass. A wide, flat appendage wrapped around the back of my skull, lifting my head slightly only to press it deeper into the pillow. 

*“Wrapping around your jaw,”* the audio continued. *“Covering your ears. Listen to how the world goes quiet.”*

Thick, matted flesh smothered the lower half of my face. The creature was sealing its belly over my mouth and nose. The smell of frozen rot and copper flooded my sinuses. The ambient noise of the room—the wind rattling the cardboard window—was completely muffled by the sheer density of its body. 

*Human,* I screamed in my mind. *Say the word.* But my jaw was clamped shut under the crushing weight of the creature. I couldn't open my mouth. I couldn't draw a breath to speak. 

*“Feel the weight on your chest,”* the track instructed. *“Protecting your heart. Nothing gets in unless I let it in. Thicken.”*

The entity crushed down on my sternum. I heard the cartilage in my ribs groan. The pressure was agonizing, a slow, methodical compression that was slowly squeezing the remaining oxygen from my bloodstream. Black spots danced in my peripheral vision. The brown noise rattled my skull. 

My body was biologically commanded to accept the deep pressure as therapy. My heart rate refused to spike. I was suffocating, and my nervous system was entirely at peace. 

*“Five,”* the woman began to count down. *“Thicken. Heavy and warm.”*

The ribs bowed deeper.

*“Four. Sinking into the darkness.”*

My vision tunneled. The heavy, greasy fur was suffocating me. 

*“Three. No human thoughts. Just instinct.”*

*Instinct.*

I didn't try to speak. I didn't try to push it off. Driven by the sheer, reptilian terror of a dying brain, I opened my teeth beneath the suffocating mass of flesh pressing over my mouth, and I bit down. 

I bit down with every ounce of jaw strength I possessed. 

My teeth sheared through thick, greasy hair and sank deep into hot, rubbery flesh. The taste of alkaline blood and sulfur flooded my tongue. 

The creature ripped backward with a silent, violent shudder. The pressure tore away from my mouth for a fraction of a second. 

"Human!" I choked out, a spray of black blood on my lips. 

The trance shattered. Adrenaline violently rebooted my nervous system. I inhaled a massive, ragged breath of freezing air and rolled violently off the mattress. I hit the floor hard, scrambling underneath the bed frame. 

I heard the massive, heavy wet *slap* of the creature hitting the ceiling above me. It didn't pursue. It scrambled across the plaster with terrifying speed, slipping out the crack in the bedroom door. 

I stayed pressed to the floorboards until the gray light of dawn crept through the edges of the cardboard window. 

When I finally stood up, the room was empty. 

But my chest ached with a deep, structural pain. I walked into the bathroom and pulled my shirt down. Stretching across my collarbones and wrapping around my ribs was a massive, mottled black bruise—the exact shape of a heavy, lead vest. 

I went back into the bedroom. The plaster directly above my bed was heavily spider-webbed with deep cracks from the creature's weight. Resting on my pillow was a thick, foul-smelling tuft of gray, matted fur. 

I packed a bag. I’m sitting in my car now, parked in a crowded diner parking lot in town. The engine is running. I’m safe. 

But an hour ago, while I was ordering coffee, the waitress dropped a tray of mugs. The ceramic shattered. The noise was so sharp, so loud, my hypervigilance flared like a lit match. 

Before I could stop myself, I squeezed my eyes shut and whispered the word the audio gave me. 

*“Thicken.”*

Instantly, right there in the brightly lit diner, I felt a heavy, phantom weight settle firmly across my shoulders. I felt the thick, invisible fur brush against my neck. 

I brought the shield with me. And I don’t know if I can ever take it off. 

My hands are shaking, resting on the steering wheel. My phone sits in the passenger seat. The manual is open. 

**SKU 02: THE INSTINCT.** *The Hack: 8D spatial tracking. The Alpha's voice orbits your head in pitch darkness.*

The creator just dropped the track, and the world out here is too loud.
