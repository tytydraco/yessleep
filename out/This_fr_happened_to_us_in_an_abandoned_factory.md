I’m writing this now because it’s been about eight months since we went there and I just wanna share my experience with what happened there that night. 

There were four of us that went there. Me, Evan, Jess, and Tyler. 

Of course, these aren't our real names and I won't share exactly where this happened so people don't go snooping around this place and get hurt. Looking back now I realized how dumb of an idea this was in general, and we were lucky none of us got hurt or had any lasting side effects from any of the toxins there... anyways.

The site we went to that night was called "the Towers", it sits about forty minutes outside the city we live in, out past the old rail yards and the last stretch of warehouses anyone still uses. It used to be part of a chemical processing complex that shut down sometime in the late 90s. Nobody redeveloped it after that. Jess said the cleanup alone would probably cost more than the land was worth.

She’d been inside once before during the day with another group and told us most of the outer structures were stable enough if we stayed out of anything labeled storage or containment. She also said there were still hazard placards on some doors warning about ammonia and solvents, but she figured those were left over from before the shutdown.

I honestly don't know why she wanted to go back there, since she was a total puss and couldn't even finish a scary movie but somehow the four of us agreed to check it out at night to get high and mess around.

We got there a little after midnight. Tyler had already opened a beer before we were even out of the car, and he lit the joint while we were walking along the access road toward the fence line like this was just another normal weekend thing.

The fence had been cut a long time ago. You could tell from the way the metal curled outward and rusted smooth along the edges. People had been going in through that same gap for years.

Inside the complex everything felt bigger than it looked from the road.

The processing buildings were connected by narrow service corridors and pipe runs that forced you to zigzag around corners just to move between structures. Even standing in the open yard you couldn’t see very far in any direction without something blocking your view.

The first thing I noticed once we were inside the perimeter was the smell.

It wasn’t strong enough to make anyone nervous right away. Just this faint mix of wet concrete, old metal, and something sharp underneath it that reminded me a little of ammonia.

I remember Jess noticed it too.

Tyler said it smelled like someone had left batteries in a flooded basement for twenty years.

We stayed outside for maybe ten minutes before going into the first structure attached to the eastern tower. The door we used was already open. There were still faded hazard diamonds bolted beside the frame, but the colors were almost completely gone.

I had my camera running when we stepped inside.

We watched that footage again a few weeks ago.

There’s a noise right before we cross the doorway that none of us reacted to at the time.

It sounds like something heavy being dragged across concrete somewhere deeper inside the building.

You can hear it clearly once you know to listen for it.

None of us said anything about it that night.

Inside, the air felt different almost immediately. Not thicker exactly. Just still. Like the building hadn’t had proper airflow in a very long time. Our flashlights picked up dust hanging everywhere, but it wasn’t drifting the way normal dust does. It just sort of stayed suspended where it was.

Jess kept reminding us to watch where we stepped because some of the floor grates were rusted through. There were pipe bundles running shoulder height along parts of the walls, and more than once one of us had to duck under something that probably hadn’t moved since before we were born.

About ten minutes after we entered that building, Evan said he was going to try to find a bathroom.

We called him the R-word and told him he should've pissed before we went inside but he was insistent and told us he’d be right back.

At the time, none of us thought it was a big deal but just like what kind of idiot was this guy?? This is exactly how a horror movie starts, and we honestly were too far off because of what happened next. 

Factories like that are basically small towns once you’re inside them. People split up for a few minutes and then get lost when exploring somewhere that big. We were passing around Tyler's joint and joking around saying he's probably dead already and whatnot when we heard some sort of metal squeal, not anything like super loud just an abrupt sound somewhere the factory.

We realized he was gone already for like 5-7 minutes and decided to go look for him just as our high was peaking and we all started tripping out and feeling super paranoid. 

I remember Tyler said he saw him walking on one of the upper catwalks above us.

I remember looking up when Tyler pointed. 

At this point we were a little pissed that he left to exploring without us.

We decided excitedly to just sneak up on the catwalk and try to scare him a little as revenge, but when we got up to the catwalk and Evan wasn’t even there.

Tyler called around for him and got nothing at first, then we heard his voice from below saying “yeah?” like he was answering from somewhere deeper in the building or below us.

We got up to the catwalk and Evan wasn’t even there.

Tyler called down for him and got nothing at first, then we heard his voice from below saying “yeah?” like he was answering from somewhere deeper in the building or below us.

Tyler said “what the hell?” and Jess immediately started crying and saying that this wasn't funny and she wanted to leave. I remember saying it wasn’t possible because we had all seen him above us a minute before.

I'm not sure if we were all just tweaking from the joint but I swear me and Tyler heard Evan’s voice came again, quieter, saying he was coming up.

Tyler swore under his breath and said he just saw someone moving below the structure, so we all looked down.

There was a figure down there that looked like Evan, but we had no idea as it was so far down and our flashlights were too dim to see well. Tyler waved, and It waved up at us when Tyler shouted.

Jess told us not to engage with it, like she thought it was a ghost or something, but Tyler yelled for him to come up anyway.

The figure started moving like it was climbing, but then just like freaking vanished or something and everything went dead quiet in a instantm

Then Evan’s voice came from right above us, like he was standing over the catwalk, saying “I’m here.”

We all looked up at the same time and there was nothing there. Like I mean nothing. Just straight air. 

At this point we were all freaking the hell out and Tyler said he was done and wanted to leave. I agreed immediately and Jess grabbed his arm in fear.

On the way down, Tyler kept saying he saw Evan in different spots ahead of us, but every time we reached those spots, there was nothing there.

Jess told him to just stop and that we need leave now, and he snapped back that he wasn’t imagining it.

At some point Evan’s voice stopped matching where we were. It would answer from places we hadn’t reached yet, or from behind us when we were all facing forward.

That’s when Jess said we needed to leave immediately.

We agreed without arguing.

We stopped trying to find Evan and just headed for the exit pissing our sh\*t out.

When we finally approached the exit, everything was already falling apart in terms of what we thought we were seeing.

Tyler kept saying he still saw Evan behind us in the corridors, like just out of sight, but whenever we stopped and looked, there was nothing there. Jess kept bawling and begging him to stop pointing it out because it was messing with everyone and making her scared.

Evan’s voice came again at some point, closer than it should’ve been, asking where we were going. Nobody answered it.

We just kept moving.

I remember at one point someone—maybe Jess—said we should call the police, because this had gone far enough and Evan was actually missing at that point, not just separated. Tyler immediately said no because we were all high and drinking and there was no way we were explaining any of this without getting ourselves in trouble. Jess didn’t argue, just said we needed to get out first and figure it out after.

The weird part is how little I remember the actual layout on the way out. Jess said the same thing later, that it felt like parts of the building just didn’t register properly, like we were moving through it faster than we should have been.

Tyler kept turning around every few steps like he expected Evan to be right there, and Jess kept telling him if he kept doing that we were going to lose each other instead.

Then we reached the outer section near where Evan was just standing inside the doorway like he had been waiting for us.

Nobody spoke for a second.

Tyler said his name and immediately started walking toward him.

Evan looked normal. A little confused at our reaction, he claimed like he’d just gotten turned around. He said he was checking out a few of the abandoned machines after his piss and heard a whisper and thought it was us, but couldn't find us and decided to just head for the exit because he was a little freaked out and didn't want to be there alone in case he got hurt or something.

Evan claimed he didn’t hear us calling for him and that he never went upstairs or left the lower section.

Jess asked him where exactly he had been, and he just repeated that he stayed on the same level the whole time.

Tyler looked at Jess and said he had seen him on the catwalk earlier. Evan said that wasn’t possible.

Nobody really followed up on it. It just stayed there and we decided to gtfo as soon as possible.

It wasn’t until we were almost at the fence that Jess stopped again and screamed at the top of her lungs.

We all turned to see wtf she was screaming at.

Way up in the upper structure of the tower, there was a window that should not have had anything behind it anymore. That section had partially collapsed years ago, at least according to Jess.

But there was something there.

A face, looking down at us. I swear on everything that I saw it, so did everybody else. It couldn't have been just a illusion because when we all focused on it, it pulled back like it had been noticed.

After a second of shock Tyler began to sprint and we followed after him and didn’t slow down until we were outside the fence and back at the car.

Nobody looked back again after that point.

Not until later, when we started going over the footage, trying to figure out when Evan was actually separated from us—and realizing none of us agreed on when that even happened.

I honestly have no idea what actually happened in that place.

We’ve gone over it a few times since, and even now none of us tell it exactly the same way. Jess is convinced Evan never went upstairs at all. Tyler still insists he saw him moving around in places that don’t line up with the layout. Evan thinks we're still pranking him or we were too high and the chemical residues there were messing with our perception or something.

Honestly, I don’t know if it was someone in the building like some homeless guy or something messing with us, or just our brains filling in gaps in a dark place we shouldn’t have been in to begin with, or the weed or all of the above.

I had footage from that night, but part of it got corrupted before I could really save it properly. The pieces I did recover mostly look normal until you hit certain sections where the audio just becomes this distorted mess. There are moments where it sounds like someone is responding before anyone speaks, but it’s hard to make anything out clearly enough to say what’s actually there.

I stopped trying to figure it out after a while. We haven’t gone back, and none of us plan to.

Sorry if that's not interesting enough or anticlimactic as some of the other stories here, but it's a true one and I hope mybe I can maybe figure out how to uncorrupt the footage I got to share with you guys.

 Anyways peace, just be careful exploring these abandoned places, you never know what's gonna happen...
