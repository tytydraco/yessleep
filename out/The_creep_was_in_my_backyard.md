This is a real story, it really happened to us. I've always wanted to tell it so here I am. Also, English isn't my first language, so apologies for any grammar and spelling mistakes (please correct me so I can get better). 

So, about five years ago (early 2021), my best friend Sam lived with me and my dad, in our old house in the countryside of France. It's a really small village where nothing ever happens, I live right in front of one of the two primary schools of said village, old earthen walls, beams that have visibly lived through at least one fire incident, house older than my generational trauma, the works. 

That year of 2021, like every year in the late winter, my dad went to his annual ski trip with his colleagues. Like every year, he was supposed to be gone for a week, and me and Sam were fairly happy to have the house to ourselves even though we didn't deviate from routine at all. 

At the time, the sofa bed Sam used as his bed was downstairs, and so was the TV, so we would spend all night smoking magic weed and watching this or that. 

That night was no different, we were high, and maybe even watching one of Squeezie (most popular French YTBer)'s horror thread Reddit videos (where he reads reddit posts from r/letsnotmeet mostly), which is kind of ironic given what happened. Then, past midnight, maybe around 2-3AM, while everything was dark outside the windows save for the school's permanent light (the rest of the street lamps turn off at 11PM here), my dog started acting funny. If you have a pet, and you love them and know them well, you know when they're not acting normal. Millie was acting really weird. She often kind of whines, and maybe sometimes barks at the windows because cats outside are fighting and she can hear them, or irrelevant things of the likes. So when I told Sam, "Millie's being weird," he said, "She's always weird. It's Millie." 

But I knew it wasn't her usual weird, it was \*weird\* weird. 

She was going from window to window, sniffing around, but she was being awfully quiet, while still apparently being excessively interested in what happened outside. She seemed stressed out, was whining low like she was trying to be discreet, sniffing the air, her tail wasn't wagging, everything about her demeanor was off. 

So, naturally, my instinct kicked in. I knew it wasn't normal, but at the same time, what could it be? My brain was saying it was nothing, my gut was screaming to fucking run.

If you're looking directly at the TV in the angle of the walls, you'll have two windows on your right, one in front of you behind the TV screen, one on your left, and then further back on the left wall is the entry door, which has a small window right on its left, over the first steps of the stairs. Then, almost completely open on the living room is the kitchen, with its own door that opens to the backyard (that's technically a sideyard I guess), that door has a large window and is visible from the couch. The third door isn't visible from the living room, it's on the same wall as the kitchen door, but in another room, the cellar. 

Also, note that the backyard is closed from the street by a very low mesh-type fence and a small wooden sort of door, which latch could be opened from the outside very easily but made a horrible screeching sound when opened.

Back to our story, from that point on, I wasn't even paying attention to the TV, I was watching the windows and the kitchen door like a hawk, somehow I knew I was going to see something. And I did. 

At first, I thought I was crazy though. I saw a shadow pass in front of the fourth window. It wasn't even a shadow, I saw something pass between the school's light and my window which for half a second masked the reflection of the light on the metallic frame of the window. I thought I was having paranoid hallucinations, but I still said, "I think I saw something outside." Sam brushed it off again. I kept staring at the windows. Millie was still being weird. And then I did see a shadow, behind the first window. This time my heart missed a beat. The movement of the shadow was going from the direction of the second window towards the school, so in a direction they couldn't possibly have taken unless they'd circled back to the crossroads after passing in front of the fourth window where I'd seen them the first time. 

My brain worked very quickly and I sobered immediately: they weren't just passing there by mistake. First of, we were under curfew cauz' of the pandemic at the time. Second, there's never anyone in the streets at night in this village where there's a majority of elderly couples and couples with young kids, unless it's noisy groups of pre-teens wandering around. And third, they'd fucking circled back to our house. 

So I said, "There's someone outside, I'm sure of it." 

I think this time Sam picked up in my tone that there maybe really was a problem and I went on to explain those observations to him. He tried to rationalize that it was maybe just someone taking a stroll, but I don't think even he believed the excuses. Anyway, there was nothing much to do, so we tried to convince ourselves that everything was fine. 

But I wasn't buying it, and I started staring straight at the kitchen window. I think Sam had muted or turned off the TV by that point, or maybe he was still watching, I don't know. 

All I can remember is watching the frame of the window, a big square of dark beyond which I couldn't see shit, while the rest of the kitchen was lit by the strong yellow artificial light. 

And then I saw what to this day remains the scariest thing I've ever seen (and I'm a horror movie enthusiast, mind you). I saw a face—no, not even a face, it was just a bright white oval, floating through the frame of the window, from right to left, so towards the back of my backyard and away from the street. Someone's face, on which the kitchen light was reflecting so brightly that I couldn't see any facial features, not even eyes. It happened really quick. I thought I was hallucinating. My whole body froze in place. And then, I saw it again. It came back in the frame, hesitated for a fraction of a second, then creeped out of the frame, towards the back again. Even though I couldn't see this person's eyes, I'm pretty sure we did make eye contact, at least from their perspective. This, again, will become relevant. 

Also, there is no way this person ended up by mistake in my backyard, they would've either needed to go over the fence (not hard at all, but you can't do it by mistake), or open the portal (same). There is also no way to get out of the backyard but through the street side, the rest is surrounded by neighbors' own backyards and tall fences. 

Now I knew I wasn't crazy. One hallucination, maybe. But this much, plus my dog's behavior? No way. I said, "There's someone in the backyard. I saw a face behind the window. There's someone in the backyard, I swear." 

I think Sam really realized the enormity of the situation as I jumped off the couch and made a beeline for the kitchen. There was one thing in my mind: make sure those goddamn doors were closed because they were the only things standing between me and my family and whoever was in the backyard. My heart was pounding in my ears and I understood in that moment that the expression "blood running cold" is an understatement. It felt like my veins were filled with ice, I'm not even kidding. But I did reach the kitchen door without passing out, and to my utmost horror, it was unlocked. I turned the key, grabbed a knife in the drawer, and practically ran to the cellar door to make sure it was locked, then to the entrance door. Both were locked. 

I told Sam about the exact weird move of the face coming back in frame then backing out towards the end of the backyard, which he hadn't seen, and we were both horrified that the kitchen door had been unlocked. We debated what to do. My dad was on the other side of the country (which is probably smaller than Texas, but still), I did have my uncle's number but he was surely sound asleep at this hour, like any legit adult I knew. We were both AFAB, fem-presenting seventeen-year-olds, alone in a big house with an ADHD dog who has the personality of a golden retriever on steroids to defend us, needless to say, we had no idea what to do. 

We were also high, which made calling the cops kind of a risk, and also... ACAB. We were both as scared of the cops as we were of the creep in the backyard, even without the magic weed problem. 

So, we settled on locking ourselves in the bathroom with the dog. My idea was that if the creep managed to get in the house, I'd have enough time to warm up the water at its max before the bathroom door broke down, and boil his eyes out. 

Sam was calling his family (who lived about an hour away from my place), and I was debating waking up my grandparents, who lived about 15-20min away. We didn't know what to do, so we decided to wait. Silence fell. 

It was awfully heavy, and I never stretched my ears quite as much as I did that night. I have a pretty good sense of hearing, what with being autistic and all, I can pick up on really small noises. And I was listening for a particular sound coming from one of the doors... Which I did ultimately hear. In the silence, I'm absolutely positive I heard the kitchen door's handle rattle like someone was trying to open it. I know that sound perfectly from the gods know how many times I opened and closed that door since we've lived here. The creep did try to open it. Had I not checked that it was locked, it would've been unlocked, he would've gotten in, and I probably wouldn't be writing this post right now. 

So when I told Sam what I'd just heard, we decided that avoiding whatever consequences we would face for the magic weed along with our fear-driven hatred of the cops wasn't worth being serial murdered. We did call the French equivalent of 911. 

What did these people do to prove us wrong about the cops being absolutely dangerous leeches of society? Nothing. We were told that "all their agents were busy on another case" and the dispatcher even went so far as to say she "thought it was about a suicide or something" (which is in no way something cops are trained to deal with, and situations in which they more often than not make it worse), anyway, there was no one out there to help us. She did take my address but said she couldn't do anything. She told us to call back if he got in the house, and hung up. 

So much for "the cops protect us" or some shite. Now we were confused, angry and terrified, and nowhere near finding a solution. So, as a last resort, I did give my grandma a call. Her and my grandpa were reluctant, and seemed to be treating the problem as a minor inconvenience, despite us actively explaining that someone was trying to get in the house. But they decided to come check on us anyway. 

We spent about twenty to thirty minutes in near complete silence, and they felt like they passed really quick. I was listening to every single noise, but nothing. 

After some time, Millie started acting up again, but this time, much to our relief, she was her usual overly excited self, and I knew she was just hearing my grandma's car. We unlocked the bathroom door, I had my kitchen knife secured in my hand and ready to strike, but the house was perfectly silent. We got downstairs quickly, opened the door, and thankfully my grandparents hadn't gotten jumped outside our front door. Again, they seemed very dismissive of the issue, but we did feel safer. 

I was back to checking every window, insisting we kept the lights off so I could see outside, and that's when I saw what looked like a minivan turn in the street and going away from the house, but very slowly. When I saw someone, who looked like a man from the stature and the way they were walking, on the sidewalk, level with the car, I understood the car was the cops. Yes, the guy was still around, after at the very least 20min of waiting for my grandparents. 

A few minutes later, the cops rang the doorbell. I didn't wanna see them, so I didn't talk to them directly, but apparently they said that they "knew the guy quite well" and "had his identity" but "couldn't do anything since he hadn't actually broken into the house". He was trying to though, but whatever. Cops will be cops I guess. 

I didn't want to leave the house cauz I had no idea where my cat was, and didn't want to leave my lair undefended, so my grandparents ended up staying the night. My dad was back maybe 48hrs later, and so was my brother, Raphaël. 

The creep did come back after my dad and brother got home. Both me and Raphaël saw him in the street, through the fifth window by the front door, but we didn't see him get close to the house and this time I didn't feel scared. My brother and dad are both really tall men, especially Raphaël for a fifteen year old at the time. They seemed menacing enough, and we were four in the house, I think (not sure) I remember going out in front of the door to check if the creep really was gone after he disappeared from view. 

This was the last time he made an appearance. Sam doesn't live here anymore, but I've never left the house for more than a week at a time and very rarely, there's never a night when this house is empty, and none of us have heard or seen anything weird beside petty pranks from the school's kids. 

Needless to say, the door-being-unlocked part gave me a pretty serious obsessive compulsion about locking the doors, and I began flipping out at my dad and brother even more than before when they forgot to lock it. I would check it multiple times a day, sometimes multiple times in a row, all three doors and the kitchen door always at least twice. 

There's one thing that sticks to my mind like chewing-gum and fills me with utter horror when I think about that unlocked kitchen door, it's the eye contact. 

The guy knew the house wasn't empty, he obviously saw us inside, there was light in the house. He was alone and unmasked. Our house is visibly shabby, an unfinished renovation, we obviously look like the most valuable things anyone will find in the house are a barely-working TV screen and maybe an old computer and one or two smartphones. In comparison, our left neighbors' house looks way more shiny and like one that could have a pretty nice jewlery drawer for burglars to find. 

That guy was NOT here to rob us. I know what he could've possibly have been after, and this is one of the reasons why I'm so thankful I don't live in the US. Had we been, the guy would've had a gun, would've maybe shot at the window or the lock and gotten in. He would've been able to hold us at gunpoint and do what you and I know he was probably here for. Again, two fem-presenting and visibly AFAB, young adults at best, conventionally attractive, alone in a big house in the quiet of a countryside French village. 

The cops wouldn't have saved us, the showerhead turned all the way to hot water and a dumb kitchen knife maybe would've. 

There's no conclusion to this story, I have no idea who the guy was, who else he's traumatised, and just have a general horrific ideas of his intentions. 

This probably isn't as scary and well-written as other stories on this subreddit, also cauz my memory is kind of fuzzy, but it is very real. I'd put a pet tax below if I could, but rest assured, Millie hasn't been traumatised by this event in the slightest (unlike me lol), I truly believe she's saved our lives that night and it's the only time in hers that she's ever behaved like the shepherd breeds she descends from.
