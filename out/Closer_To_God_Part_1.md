I’m in my 20’s now, but I was 12 years old when my brother was turned into an angel. For clarification, I was born and grew up in a cult that was hidden in the Missouri Ozarks. I won’t give away names of the cult, let alone myself since they are still very active. I just feel like I need to tell someone other than law enforcement about this, hopefully it’ll bring me some closure.

Obviously I managed to get out, I was only twelve but had family that still wrote us. Few of the letters got through after the cult screened them. Secret codes though, had a few phone numbers so I stayed with relatives for a bit but on my own now. I don’t fully know how to delve into this trauma completely but i can start from the beginning, fake names and such, refer to me as Jericho.

It was 2016, early August, when they came and took Logan away from our home in the cult. He went quietly, accepting of what he was chosen for. Our parents were crying, joy, not sadness. I on the other hand, was a mess. They told me such sweet lies,

“Logan will come back soon, we just cant see him for a while.” My Mom said on loop weeks after they took him.

If I was good at anything, it was listening. Probably even when I shouldn’t have been, but it got me out of and into plenty of trouble growing up. When the farms would have a poor harvest and rations had be divided out, i made sure my parents had blackmail to get us more. When one of the other kids stole something, I was the first know and second to tell. Sure It didn’t make me the most liked kid by the others, but it’s what God wanted me to do, right?

So my listening skills came in handy weeks before they came to take Logan from me. Mom and Dad talking about how they, “made such good stock for our Lord” and how, “the other families are jealous of our boys.” Bragging, Prideful. Nothing to be proud of sending your sons to the slaughter by radicals.

Our parents believed that, based on appearances alone, we were closer to the biblical depiction of angels according to the cults doctrine and text at least. Cornsilk like blond hair, vibrant icy blue eyes, featureless skin, athletic. Their pride in our features was baseless, everyone in the camp looked the same, enough so that if you didn’t know, you could mistake us all as family.

My best friends, Alex and Zach, twins had nearly identical appearance to me and every other twelve year old running around barefoot on the compounds grounds. They often adopted me as their unofficial triplet and would frequently use to bate Old Man Morgan from his little shop so they could steal candy, theft. I didn’t mind, free candy, and all the adults trusted me so when I say they didn’t do anything, they didn’t.

My brother on the other hand, he knew what I got into, constantly reminding me that i shouldn’t be helping sinners commit crimes and should instead report them for proper reeducation. I would then also remind him of what he does behind closed door, what he did be closed doors. I loved and still do love my brother, miss him everyday.

The way he talked about The Bible, how he used to read the scriptures around the house or when i was really little and had a hard time falling asleep, he’d lay in bed with me and tell me stories from the good book till i fell asleep. Childproofed though, some of them were pretty raunchy after reading them now.

The compound wasn’t one in the traditional sense. It was an old corn farming town, most of the land was bought up by the cults leader, and his name I will use, Cassian Creed. What little i could dig up on this man was hard to find, usually everyone has some sort of digital footprint but his was washed clean. Formerly the CEO of small Texas oil company he vanished for 5 years after filing for bankruptcy.

He was a warm man, well spoke and knew how to get people to listen to him. Every syllable sliding out of his mouth like warm butter on toast that attracted people like ants to sugar. His sermons would echo through the church and the town so those that couldn’t attend would still be able to hear his words. Creed wasn’t ugly either, women found him attractive clearly as he had no issue claiming any women in the town as a wife or mistress.

Obviously in the non-perverted version of The Bible, polygamy is a big no-no. But for Creed, as well as other cult leaders from my research, God made an exception for them and them alone. Load of bullshit. I can’t get worked up over this now, just need to get it out, from the start, a few days before.

I was gently shaken awake, Logans hand resting on my shoulder. He was leaning was over me in my little twin bed. The morning light shining in my room and catching his hair in a way that made his face practically glow. “Hey, sleepy head. Mom says it’s time to get up. Little lambs that sleep all day miss the shepherds words.” He said to me in a soft voice as I stretched out in my bed, yawning. He always woke me up like this when I overslept.

“But, fine…” I yawned and sat up, blinking the sleepies from my half closed eyes. As my woven quilt draped off my shoulders and pooled around my waist. “There ya go J, don’t fall back asleep. The Sloth might get you.” He smiled, perfect white teeth, as he straightened out his white t-shirt, pulling a strand of hair from it and dropping it in the floor. “I know, I know. Im getting up…have Zach or Alex been by?” I asked, pivoting to the side and putting my feet on the carpeted floor.

Logan scratched at the little patch of hair in his chin he’d been sporting, somehow convincing our father that, “God intended me to have facial hair, otherwise I wouldn’t be able to grow it!” He would say, nobody argued him either. “No, cant say I’ve seen your friends. They’re probably robbing poor Mr. Morgan again.” He said as I stood up, stretching again, reaching my arm around to scratch my back and ruffle my own hair to wake me up a little.

“Dang, okay. Well they need me for Mr. Old Man Morgan. So they’re probably in the river or the barn.” I said padding across the soft flooring to my dresser. Perfectly folded pairs of blue jeans and white t-shirts stared back at me as I thought about what to wear from my options.

“After chores though, most important is the service to our Lord.” Logan said as he watched me change, taking off what i had in and replacing it with jeans and a t-shirt. “Well yeah, of course.” I said, pulling the shirt down some, baggier than it should be. “Good, I think Mom left you a chores list on the fridge. I won’t be around much today. I get to help with Ryan’s ascension today.” He said as he turned, pausing in the doorway of my room waiting for my reaction.

I of course, whipped around still struggling to pull the shirt on over my head at the mention of Ascending. “They’re opening the gate today?!” I ask, excited, and pulling my head through the sleeve of my shirt. “C-can i come and watch?” I asked, more calm now that i was stuck. Logan chuckled, soft and warm as he walked over and helped me out of my predicament. “Sorry kid, you know you know can’t. So I got a job for you too today.” He said as he remove my shirt and handed it back to me.

“What is it?!” I asked, dressing myself properly, my eyes drilling into his as he stood straight up. “Keep your friends Alex and Zach, away from the church today. I know them as much as I know you, they’ll try and sneak a peek at The Gate.” He took my hands, clasping them in his. “Promise?” He asked, I nodded, not wanting to disappoint my biggest hero.

He let go and messed my hair up before turning and leaving my room, i found my sandals a followed after, determined to finish my chores as soon as possible so i could enjoy the hot summer days that was blessed to us. Logan had left already, his truck pulling out of our driveway and down the road. Now i was alone in my family home, which is usually empty during the day. Mom was at the children’s school, teaching the littler kids the word of God and of our faith. My Dad worked closely with the leader of our sect, Father Creed.

I padded across the hall, dropping my sandals by the front door for when I was able to leave and then towards the kitchen. Light green cabinets with cream colored counters and an old white fridge met me as i stepped onto the warm tiles. Pictures of me and Logan on the fridge or from last month’s birthday party with Alex and Zach. Set at eye level for me, magnetized by a small wooden lamb magnet as a brief chores list.

Take out the trash, put away the dishes, fold the throw blankets in the living room. All fairly simple things for me to handle, could all be done in about 20 minutes i thought to myself as i took the list and walked to the radio on the counter. A small little boombox my Mom would like to turn on when cooking. We only ever got three stations. KTRU, the news talk station I hated listening to, but it was better than the other two. The gospel music station was usually the same five hymns on repeat and the prayer station was only ever on on Sundays.

I turned the radio on to KTRU since being alone irked me a little, felt like someone was always watching me especially with how close our house was to The Wormwoods. The knob felt smooth as I twisted it, static flared for a moment from the mouth of the radio as i adjusted the knob to KTRU’s station, a little smack to the top and the clear voice of Brother Isaiah and Hunter came through. Laughing about something from a previous caller I must’ve missed. Decent enough background noise for chores.

I quickly put the fragile glassware away in the cabinets and removing the trash from the can, tying it off and setting it by the door. Blankets….I dislike folding. I grumbled and headed into the living room. Decent sized space. Wooden coffee table with removable glass top pieces, A sectional, recliner and projection TV, a few plants, and a lemon scented candle. I folded the blankets and threw them neatly over the back of the sofa and one on the recliner. Thought I would do my due diligence and vacuum a little as well. Acts of kindness is something that’s taught and encouraged when we’re in early school.

I have half-listening to the radio as i wrapped the cord of the vacuum back up,

“That was Brother Dale again predicting the world ends on Thursday.” Brother Hunter said, holding back laughter

“Again.” Brother Isaiah replied, more disappointment on his tongue.

“Yeah, he’s been wrong twelve times, but hey — lucky thirteen.” Brother Hunter quipped, it got a chuckle out of me.

“If he’s right this time, we’ll issue a formal apology.” Brother Isaiah said, ready to move onto the next call.

“And if he’s wrong, we’ll see you Friday morning like usual.” Brother Hunter played a sound on a sound board and then answered the next caller, i tuned out at this point.

Finishing up, I slipped into my sandals and grabbed the filled trash and mint-scented bag, unlocked the door and stepped outside. The warm wet heat of the Missouri Ozarks summer intermingled with the buzz of locusts clinging to trees and screaming for dear life. The sun felt good on my skin at least as i walked towards one of the large dumpsters each cul-de-sacs in town. Pulling the metal panel aside i tossed the bag into the container and turned around to hunt down my friends.

The walk to where Alex and Zach usually spend hot summer days like these is just on the border of The Wormwood. A small bit of a low but wide bed river that flows partly through town, we just call is Dirt Creek since most of the year its too shallow to do anything but mud wrestle or look for frogs. The Wormwood definitely scares us off though, it’s not a place we like being near at night and barely during the day. Mom says It’s where The Deceiver hides and plots with The Betrayer to overthrow Father Creed and our town.

The one time we were out there at night we saw so many eyes just looking back at us, we thought they were just wolves or smaller animals but they moved too funny. I thought, dreading going near Dirt Creek as I walked in the direction, following the side walk. My train was derailed however when I collided with a taller man in formal white robes. I recognized him immediately as Brother Joseph, one of Father Creeds disciples and usually lead the town sermons on Wednesdays. “Ope, good morning young Jericho. Pleased to run into you today.” He said, folding his hands into his robes sleeves.

“I’m sorry Brother, I was thinking and not paying attention where I was going.” I said, offering a bow of my head in apology. He laughed and gently pat the top of my head, his hand lingering a little too long, but allowed me to raise it. “Remember, Proverbs 4:26. Ponder the Path of thy feet, and let all thy ways be established.” He said, fully taking his hand off me.

“Thank you Brother, Logan told me that Ryan was ascending today, is that true? Are you going to help as well?” I asked, the concept of ascending sounded so fun, to become an angel in Gods army bathed in His grace to fight evil down here on Earth. It would be like the ultimate super hero.

Brother Joseph nodded with a smile, “yes of course. It’s not for a few more hours however. Ryan I still undergoing all the proper rites and sanctifications needed. I trust you will be there to watch?” He asked me reaching out to put his hand on my shoulder, it felt heavy and sweaty.

“Nuh-uh, Logan told me that i had to make sure Alex and Zach didn’t try and sneak in. You know how they are.” I said, a smile on my own face. Brother Josephs hand tightened when i mentioned Alex and Zach, his grip starting to hurt. “Yes of course, please do be vigilant for them and those around you.” He said through gritted teeth, noticing the uncomfortable look on my face he left go and apologized.

“I shall see you around dear Jericho, say hello to your mother and father for me will you?” He said more as a statement as he walked away, further down the main strip of our town and into Old Man Morgans general store. “That was strange” i thought, “I know Alex and Zach can get on people’s bad sides some times but Brother Joseph seemed legitimately angry. I’ll ask them when I find them.” I said in my might and continued walked through town towards Dirt Creek.

Familiar shouts of two identical voices echoed nearby accompanied by splashing sounds of water. They were easy to find, my friends, their clothes separated into two piles on the bank of the river as they splashed waves of water at each other in the chest deep water. I whistled at them to get their attention as I removed my shirt, tossing it into my own pile. “Hey guys!” I shouted, removing my jeans and sandals, putting everything off to the side.

“Hey man! Whats up dude?!” One said then the other. I waded into the water, it was cold and flowing softly, perfect for this kind of hot day. “Nothin’ had some chores and now im out here. Ran into Brother Joseph on the way. He seemed PISSED at you two.” I said wading over and quickly going under to acclimate my entire body to the cold water.

“Yeah…we fell asleep again during remedial education again. Your Mom doesn’t believe in physical punishment so she just keeps giving us homework.”

Alex said, digging in his ear with his finger, wiping whatever off on his brother who just did the same. Weirdos.

“But then the homework is easy so we finish it usually right before bed and turn it in the next day.” Zach smiled, still missing one of his front teeth from when he dared Alex to hit him with a frying pan.  Again, weirdos. Thankfully it was a baby tooth still.

“And then we’re over here with mister goody-two-shoes. I dunno why you choose to hang out with us.” Alex said wading over and hooking an arm around my shoulder using the other to wave in-front of us. “Just think J, you could be incharge of all this one day.” Zach came over next and did the exact same thing, hooking his arm around my shoulder, resting atop Alex and a similar “imagine!” Motion. “And me and Alex as your counsel, man we’d be literally kings.” They laughed, and I admittedly did as well, but still pushed them off.

“Yeah no kings though, thats not a title for us. Obviously you haven’t been paying attention to the homework my Mom gives you two.” I said as the floated off for a moment before sandwiching me in the middle again. “Nope! Not in the slightest.” The said together before splashing me with a whole bunch of water.

We spent a few hours out there, Alex talking about Sister Susans…nevermind, but he was definitely talking about them when we heard something move from inside The Wormwood. We went silent as the dense fog the permeates the woods approached the bank of the river, touching the water slightly causing it to turn a a faint blood color. We scrambled out, wet, mud clinging to our hands and feet, our hearts racing as we sat soaked in the bank closer to town, where our clothes sat.

“Ryan must’ve ascended by now.” I said and both of them looked at me. “WHAT?” They shouted, pivoting to both have a hand on my shoulder and my legs locked between theirs.

“Ryan was ascending today and you DIDN’T tell us?” Alex asked, getting close to my face.

“Yeah J, you know we were cool with Ryan. He was like your brother but a little less stuck up!” Zach said and then made a face like he regretted it. “I mean, Logans cool and all but like-“

I frowned, I didn’t like withholding the truth from them but I promised Logan. They would’ve tried to intervene or ruin it as a prank to Ryan and ascension means a lot to the people who get to do it. They removed themselves from over too of me, sitting on their knees and frowning.

“We didn’t even get to say goodbye.” Alex said with a sniffle.

Zach pouted too, “yeah. He used to buy us candy all the time from Old Man Morgan before we got you involved. He still bought us stuff too.”

“You guys can still talk to him through prayer though. Thats what Mom says to do to talk to those that Ascend, it’s how i still talk to my Grandparents and my cousin Stephanie.” I said trying to cheer them up, it wasn’t working very well, but I was also distracted by something dragging my attention from inside the fog of The Wormwood. “You guys could come sleepover at my house tonight, i’m sure Logan will tell you stuff he can.” I added as my eyes followed something moving around through the trees.

“Yeah, I’m sure our parents would be okay with that.” Alex said standing up and pulling on his clothes. Zach did the same and since it seemed like we were done here. I did as well, facing The Wormwood the entire time, watching as the tall figure stood just on the edge of the fog wall, watching us. Its features nearly impossible to determine aside from its curly hair, robe and apparent wings.

“Guys, we should leave now. Something’s in the woods watching us.”
