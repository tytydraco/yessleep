I was in the cafeteria when I first noticed her. She was on the other side of the room, sat at a table by the wall, alone as I was. She stood out among the rest of the crowded scene. I think it was her face that made her so distinct. Her pale skin clung onto her bones with little padding. Her hair was perfectly straight, black and shiny like wet ink, with bangs that stopped at her eyes. As I peered out across the long hall of tables full of my peers, it felt like I was in an art gallery. There she was, a portrait hung in a dark corner of the room with no one stopping to analyze it. Except for me. I couldn't take my eyes off her. It was like I had been starved of her strange quality of light for my entire life.  A dark vignette formed around her in my vision. My mind became quiet. It was as if for a moment I had become a pinhole camera with no sense other than sight.   
Inconsiderately, the bell rang and snapped me out of my trance. As everyone else got up, I felt a sudden wave of sickness wash over me. Blood rushed to my head, and my senses were being overdriven. I staggered for a moment, quickly getting a grip on myself. I went on the rest of the school day with a faint headache. Whenever I closed my eyes, I envisioned the same portrait in the same gallery. I couldn't help but think all I needed to do was see her again.  
I left the campus feeling a dull, throbbing pain as my usual thoughts began trickling back. I stopped at one end of the crosswalk where everyone else had congregated. In the dozen or so faces that made up the mass, I immediately found hers. The sighting felt like a hit off a cigarette. Soon the wave of relief dissipated, and a familiar hunger gripped me. The mass of people began to make their way across the street, and she went along with them. I followed from behind. I felt this premonition that if I got too close, she'd disappear. It was like trying to catch a butterfly. The crowd slowly dispersed as each person went where they were needed. Soon it was just me and her.  
She had a very similar route to mine. I wondered how I hadn't found her sooner. Up to this point, I had been diligent in keeping my distance. However, she turned the corner where I would have gone straight towards my house. She was getting away, and I needed to decide quickly whether or not I was going to pursue her. But to my surprise there was no deliberation. I looked down to see my legs had already begun to move without me having to instruct them. This made me smile. Just one less distraction I had taking up space in my head.  
It was only until I saw the sky getting darker that I realized how long I had been following her. She led me far past the streets and houses of my neighborhood,  and into a forest I had never visited before. The deeper into the woods she took me, the more the trees began to look grey and lifeless, almost petrified. I couldn't feel any pain in my legs though we had walked for what must have been miles. In fact, I had lost sensation almost completely just below my neck. The only pain I felt was when I took my gaze off her.  
At long last, we reached a small shack. Judging by the condition of the dark, spongy wood, it had to have been very old. She went inside, but as soon as she did I lost all momentum in my legs. It felt as if I was unplugged. My legs gave way and I fell onto the ground. Using what little strength I had, I managed to crawl towards the shack. As I inspected her home more closely, I saw that the wood appeared to truly be a massive growth that had sprouted out from the ground. It was more of a soft mound, almost like a big toe. It was caked in thick layers of dirt. The spots that were less obscured by grime showed a bricklike web of fibers. A single person sized opening in the front took the place of an entrance. Through the dim moonlight, I peered past the threshold. There was no floor or ground to be seen. Inside there was only a pit. I couldn’t see the bottom, and I certainly didn’t see her. There was a warmth coming from deep down, and the air inside was wet and heavy.

I was so happy.
