I didn’t believe the message at first.

It came at 2:17 AM on my phone, from an unknown number with no profile picture. Just five words.

“Don’t turn on the hallway light.”

I stared at it for a few seconds, half annoyed and half curious. Spam messages were getting weirder lately. I almost deleted it, but then another message came through.

“I saw you get up.”

That made me sit up in bed.

My room was completely dark except for the faint glow of my phone. The house was quiet in that deep way that only happens at night when everything is asleep and even the walls feel still. I live alone with my mom, but she was on a night shift. No one else should have been awake.

I typed back.

“Who is this?”

The reply came instantly.

“I’m in your hallway.”

I froze. My eyes moved toward my bedroom door without me meaning to. It was slightly open, just a crack, like I had left it earlier. The hallway light outside was off.

I told myself it was a prank. Someone messing with random numbers.

Then I heard something.

A soft creak from the hallway floorboards.

My body went cold. The house is old, so it makes noises all the time, but this was different. This sounded like weight shifting. Like someone had just stepped closer to my door.

My phone lit up again.

“Don’t look directly at the door.”

That made no sense, but it made my breathing shallow anyway. I didn’t move. I just stared at the crack in the door, waiting.

Another creak.

Closer.

Then silence again.

I should have called my mom. I know that now. But at that moment I just sat there, reading the messages over and over, trying to find logic in them.

Then something changed.

The crack in my door got darker. Like something was standing just outside it, blocking what little light came from my room.

My phone vibrated.

“Good. You see it now.”

I felt my stomach drop. I hadn’t looked directly at anything. I had just been staring at the door. I tried to convince myself it was impossible, that I was imagining shapes in the dark.

Then my phone sent a final message.

“Whatever you do, do not turn on the hallway light.”

And right after that, I heard it.

A whisper, right outside my door.

Not loud. Not dramatic. Just close enough that I knew it was real.

“Open it.”

My hand started shaking. I didn’t want to move. Every instinct told me to stay still, to pretend I was asleep. But then the whisper came again, slightly louder.

“Open it. I know you’re awake.”

Something about the voice was wrong. It didn’t sound like it came through the air. It sounded like it was inside the wood of the door itself, vibrating through it.

My phone screen suddenly glitched. The messages disappeared for a second, then reappeared with one last line.

“You have already looked at me too long.”

The hallway floor creaked again.

Right in front of my door.

I couldn’t take it anymore. I grabbed my phone tighter and slowly reached for the lamp switch beside my bed. I don’t know why I did it. Maybe I thought light would make it stop. Maybe I just needed to see something real.

My finger touched the switch.

And the voice outside said, calmly,

“Wrong choice.”

I turned the light on.

The hallway light flicked on at the exact same time, even though I didn’t touch it.

For one second, everything looked normal. Just an empty hallway.

Then I saw it.

There was someone standing at the end of it, perfectly still. Too still. Not leaning, not breathing, just upright like it had been placed there.

But the worst part was not the shape.

It was the fact that it had no face.

Not blurred. Not hidden in shadow. Just nothing where a face should be.

And it was already closer than it should have been, like the hallway had shortened.

My phone buzzed again.

“No more lights.”

The hallway bulb exploded.

Darkness came back instantly, but not complete darkness. Something was still there. I could feel it moving closer without sound now, like it didn’t need footsteps anymore.

My door handle slowly started turning.

I backed away from the bed until I hit the wall.

The handle stopped.

Then a soft knock came from the door.

Not forceful. Not impatient.

Just polite.

Three taps.

Then a voice, right outside again, even calmer than before.

“You were warned nicely.”

The door began to open.

I don’t remember deciding to run, but I did. I threw myself toward the window, pulled it open, and climbed out onto the roof outside. The cold air hit me hard and I nearly slipped, but I managed to get onto the slanted tiles.

Behind me, I heard the door fully open.

No footsteps followed me outside.

That was worse.

Because it meant it didn’t need to.

I looked back toward my room through the window. The inside was dark, but I could see my phone glowing on the bed.

A new message appeared on it, even from outside.

“You looked anyway.”

Something stood behind the glass.

Not pressing against it.

Just there.

And for the first time, I understood something that made my chest go completely tight.

It wasn’t in my hallway anymore.

It was in my reflection.
