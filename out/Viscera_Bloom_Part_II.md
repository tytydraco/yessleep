Finally I have found some safety to sit and provide more of my story. I appreciate the encouragement from you all. I will provide context later on why I am in the dangerous position that I am in. For now, I will tell you more of my experience.

Victoria and I didn’t bother waiting to see if the shambling abomination above us could make it down the stairs. We were throwing open doors running to the exit of the university research lab. Once we cleared the exit door, we sprinted to the emergency response call box with a big blue LED light attached to it. I have never seen anyone use these placements, but I am glad AST’s administration cared enough to put them in. 

The dark night cast shadows that covered the modern architecture of the campus buildings in an inky contrast, with only small portions of the wall illuminated by lights left on in lecture classrooms. The silhouette of every building and tree around us was visible, but the details remained shrouded in the striking darkness. 

Victoria slammed on the button and a short time later, a voice responded through the holes of the speaker. I figured she should handle the call and I would make sure the thing was not in our vicinity, although I doubt I could do much to prevent its pursuit. 

“Officer Patterson, Four Rivers PD. I am moving to your location now, can you speak?” The confident voice cut through the tension nicely and helped ease a tiny bit of my nerves. 

“We need help right now, something is wrong with our friend! He looks like he is in horrible pain. We don’t know what to do!” Victoria nervously shouted into the box. 

“If you can stay put at the call box please do so. I will be there shortly.” At the time he said that, we heard the siren in the distance coming closer. I was able to release a small sigh of relief as the siren rounded the corner farther down the street beside the campus walking path. I glanced back at the biology lab, the door didn’t look like it had moved. Maybe Braden, or whatever he was now, was still in the lab stalking the hallways. The fact that the doors of the entrance were push-to-open doors did eat at the back of my mind like an incessant angry shouting. 

*What was stopping it from coming outside?* I thought as the police car whipped up to the curb of the street next to us. 

Officer Patterson threw open his door and quickly made his way toward us. Victoria still had tears streaming down her face. He looked around surveying the scene. The two sweaty college students standing before him did not provide him with any obvious clues to the emergency at hand. 

“Are you two hurt? What is the emergency?” Officer Patterson probed as he looked us up and down. He focused mostly on Victoria, who was still slightly hyperventilating. I decided to step in to help her catch her breath. 

“Our friend,” I pointed toward the lab. “Something is wrong with him, he looked like he was going to hurt us.” I know some of you may wonder why I didn’t immediately tell him about the violent metamorphosis, and my answer to that is simply, how would you vocalize that to a police officer and make it sound believable? “He is dangerous, and something is very seriously wrong with him.” I expressed this to the officer. I was leaving out a few details, but I think he caught my meaning.

“Was he on something?” Officer Patterson asked with dead seriousness. “Drugs? Amphetamines?” We confidently shook our heads at his inquiry. He stepped back from us and radioed something to his dispatch. Then he started cautiously walking toward the lab, putting his hand at his belt right around his gun. 

Our breathing was very shallow, afraid to contaminate the silence of the nigh with our sound. Me and Victoria had our eyes secured to the entrance of the lab as Officer Patterson made his way up the steps. I decided to check our surroundings before he made it in. I glanced around and noticed that, even for a relatively small university, the campus looked empty, like a ghost town long abandoned. While getting a read on our surroundings, I noticed something around a tree in one of the decorative campus landscaping off to the side of the walking path. When my eyes focused a little more, my blood ran cold. I didn’t move a muscle or even think about what was around me, just what was in front. 

In the landscaping bed, the silhouette of a tall twisted figure stood just out of what could be seen. A stray light from a lecture hall casted just enough light onto the figure to show its face. 

  
It looked straight in our direction with dark voids where its eyes would be. His jaw still remained violently cocked to the side. The light also illuminated the edges of the large spikes jutting out from its body. It was no longer twitching and seizing like before. It just stood and stared at us in complete silence. 

*How did it get outside without us noticing*? I couldn’t think. Adrenaline was already drowning out my thoughts. Victoria looked like she was starting to recover slightly as she watched Officer Patterson. I felt bad that I was about to ruin that short-lived serenity. I carefully jabbed her in the side. She whipped around with a confused slightly angry expression, but before she reacted to me audibly, I covered her mouth and nodded toward the landscaping bed. She slowly turned to look and saw it. I can imagine she had a similar reaction to mine. We were starting to step toward Officer Patterson when things took a drastic turn. It started running toward us, in a way that sent shivers down my entire body. It looked as though it was running, but its legs were moving as if it was in a video being rewinded. It stretched its arms wide as if it wanted a large hug. A scratchy growl could be heard emerging from its throat as it took more steps. 

It wasn’t running very fast, sticking around was not in my deck of cards. We took off toward the lab. 

“Officer! Please!” I called to the officer. He whipped around at the sound of my voice.

“I thought I told you kids to stay . . . Jesus Christ!” He quickly pulled his weapon from his hip and aimed it beyond us toward what was left of Braden. “Stop right there! On the ground! NOW!” The abomination didn’t even flinch. 

At that point we made it up the stairs to be next to Officer Patterson. The monstrosity continued charging toward us from roughly 100 meters away. By now it was fully visible in all of its abhorrence. It looked like a terrible personal portrait of Braden painted by Pablo Picasso. Some physical traits still looked like they belonged to Braden, but most of it had been mangled beyond recognition. Officer Patterson aligned the barrel directly with its path of travel.

BAM! BAM! 

Two shots exploded out of the officer's weapon and collided with the horror. Each shot splashed with red, ping-pong ball sized chunks at each collision point. But the abomination did not waver in its pursuit. 

Officer Patterson shooed us inside and followed right behind us. Our group ran to one of the ground floor labs. Victoria shut the door and locked it. Officer Patterson radioed to his dispatch frantically calling for backup. They kept telling him there was something other officers were responding to that took higher priority and that he had to wait. He cursed and then angrily walked toward me and Victoria. 

“Answers, now!” He pointed a finger straight in my face. 

“We don’t know! We came to check on our friend and he mutated into that horrific thing!” she covered her face with her hands as the words came out of her mouth. Almost as if saying it out loud helped the gravity of the situation really set in. 

“What caused him to do that?” Officer Patterson asked a question that we hadn’t had time to think about yet. Victoria was in no shape to play detective. 

“We would have to investigate the lab upstairs to really know.” I said with my best educated guess. It looked like Officer Patterson was going to say we should investigate it, but a heavy slam hit the door of the lab at that moment. From behind the door, a sound rattled under the doorway that was what I imagined a walrus that was being strangled by a chain would sound like. We pushed a workstation table in front of the door and backed away. 

It kept slamming itself unrelentingly against the door. The heavy wooden door temporarily warded off the attempts to get in, until we heard the sound of splintering and splitting. Our eyes were glued to the door not wanting to look away. Like a lumberjack swinging a great ax at a tree, it threw itself against the door. Officer Patterson held up his gun ready to fire. 

The top right corner became exposed first. A disgusting claw stuck its way through. Now it was not slamming against the door, it was tearing pieces off. It stuck its head in the exposed corner, and Officer Patterson rocketed two more shots into the thing’s head. Each shot rang with a deafening roar in the small lab room. I covered my ears and observed the scene in front of me as if it was in slow motion. 

In front of me was my best friend of three years sobbing with a facial expression that knew nothing but fear in that moment. Next to her was this police officer who responded to help us knowing nothing about the situation, now pointing his weapon ready to fire it again. Then there, viciously tearing at the door as if it were styrofoam, was what used to be my other best friend. 

What snapped me out of the momentary internal pause was a clawlike hand flying from the creature’s arm through the hole in the door. Its claw-like hand latched on to Officer Patterson’s ankle and pulled tightly. It took his leg right from under him, and he fell on his back. He screamed in agony as the claws buried themselves and writhed as they dug deeper. It also began to pull the officer toward the door. 

“Victoria, we need to help!” I shouted to her. She got up and started sifting through the cabinets. I did so as well and started grabbing vials and bottles. I hurled individual bottles and powders through the hole to damage the abomination. It responded with a variety of screeches and spasms. It didn’t budge until Victoria came from the back of the lab with a large glass beaker. She threw the clear liquid through the hole and steam emitted from its flesh immediately as the liquid scalded the creature. It sent out an earshattering screech in response and let the officer go. We could hear the monster take off down the hall as if cowering in fear. 

We ran to tend to the officer’s wounds. Victoria was grabbing things to wrap and clean the wound and I cleared the area. I lifted the officer's pant leg and jumped when I saw the affected area. Four huge lacerations went up his ankle, and around them, the skin and muscles under the skin were writhing and twisting. Victoria had appeared behind me and was observing behind my shoulder in shock. 

“We have to cut it off.” Victoria said sternly. This was in stark contrast to how she was reacting previously in the situation. “It can’t take another person.” She looked me straight in the eyes. I struggled to argue with her. 

“But…I am not qualified to do that. I don’t know how to do that at all!” I frantically yelled as the realization of what we were about to do set in. The officer was struggling on the ground as his foot began to contort and twitch. Victoria went into one of the dissection cabinets, and found a small bone saw and some rubber tubing. She lit a bunsen burner and used a striker to set it off. 

“Use this to sanitize the saw. Hurry! He doesn’t look like he will have much time before we have to cut off more than just his foot.” I was a little disoriented by her request, but looking at the officer’s seizing foot, I grabbed the saw and held the length of the blade through the flame. . 

“Victoria this is an officer of the law, are we sure this won’t come back to screw us over?” I protested one more time as she used the tubing as a tourniquet below his knee. I already knew what her answer would be, but I just hoped and prayed there was something I hadn’t considered yet.

“Leo, soon this man will no longer be a human let alone an officer of the law and that will screw us over a million times more.” That was some unfortunately strong reasoning. I held the freshly sanitized saw and lowered it to the officer’s leg. He was frantic and unresponsive to any outward stimulus, but he was not stopping me. There was a definitive line on his skin where the muscles were slithering underneath. *The point of no return.* The phrase echoed through my head as I put the blade of the saw against his leg. 

I will save you the gritty details of how an inexperienced college student sawed a police officer’s leg off. But when the final motion took the last connection of skin off, something highly unpredictable happened. The foot wriggled and twisted as what looked like fingers started to emerge from its sides. At this point, I grabbed some tongs from a drawer and grabbed the foot as it squirmed. I threw it in a lab sink, and Victoria started dousing it with some isopropyl she found in a cabinet.

It made no sound, but the vigorousness of its movements indicated its discomfort. I tipped the bunsen burner to ignite the foot-creature in the sink. The awful smell of burning flesh filled the room, and Victoria turned on a fume hood to let some of the fumes out. It jerked and thrashed in the sink as the flames scorched it layer by layer. We watched in horror as what once was a part of a human now became a twisted, charred mess. 

Officer Patterson, now with only one foot, was no longer seizing in pain, but it looked as though he was starting to lose consciousness.

“I should have believed.” He kept repeating very quietly under his breath with his eyes closed. Victoria had found a first aid kit in one of the cabinets and was applying bandages to his wound. As the fire died in the sink, I sprayed some water onto the charred mess of flesh and blood to prevent any chance of any campus building fires. I can say pretty confidently that it would not be moving anytime soon. 

“Let’s get him to a hospital.” I said out loud, and Victoria agreed. We called an ambulance, and got them to understand the severity of the emergency without using any phrases like “evil flesh monster” or “foot turning into a meat eating crab.” We struggled to carry Officer Patterson to the front door of the lab. Still on edge from our recent encounter we checked every corner carefully. As we got closer to the door, we saw an enormous window shattered completely into pieces. The lights from the ambulance showed through the gaping hole in the building casting our shadows in a blue and red pallor. 

I think I heard something. That was faster than last time. I am going to do some more maneuvering, and I will check back with you guys soon. Your support has been greatly appreciated as I give you more of this information. There are people who would do horrible things to me if I tell this full story. But you all deserve to know.
