Prologue – The Town That Remembers Blood

SunnyField was a town that seemed trapped in eternal autumn. Even in summer, the sun rarely warmed the asphalt, and shadows lingered longer than they should. People laughed, went to school, fell in love—but beneath it all, the town breathed something rotten.

The first murders weren’t connected.

The second were already strange.

The third became legend.

And then the masks began to appear.

Chapter I – Coming Home

Tiffany Miller had sworn she would never return to SunnyField.

But 1995 had other plans.

She came back for her younger sister Jessica, for the old house at the end of Willow Drive, and for a feeling that crawled under her skin—as if someone was watching her from the darkness.

Jessica greeted her with a smile, but there was something broken in her eyes.

"That attack..." she whispered that very evening.
"I still dream about it."

The attack on her boyfriend, exactly a year ago. Blood, an axe, and a masked man who should not have been alive.

Chapter II – Jam Keeper

The legend of Jam Keeper had been passed down through generations in SunnyField. He was said to be a giant of a man, recognizable by a mask made of twisted wood and a massive axe he had carved himself. The story went that Jam Keeper had once been a local lumberjack, condemned to death after killing several villagers who had destroyed his life through lies and sabotage. His spirit never found peace; he returned from the grave at night, awakening those who followed his bloody trail—his allies, new killers, born of hatred and darkness.

Another killer, rarely mentioned by the townspeople, was known as The Silencer—a man whose victims were always found with their hands and feet bound, in mute silence. His face was never seen; he remained in the shadows, and his symbol was a red ribbon tied around the victim's neck.

Chapter III – The Night of the Attack

1995 brought the darkest nights to SunnyField. Tiffany, Jessica, Melissa Granger, Jake Finch, Ben Boone, and Jed Rich decided to spend a weekend at an abandoned cabin on the edge of the forest. Night fell, and silence was thick.

The first attack did not wait long.

Melissa was found in the kitchen, split open by Jam Keeper’s axe. Her body hung from the ceiling, blood dripping onto the wooden floor, forming a gruesome mosaic. Ben Boone tried to escape through the back door, but The Silencer intercepted him—handcuffing his wrists and strangling him with a rope, whispering words in an unknown language. Jed fought and survived but was severely wounded.

Tiffany, Jessica, and Jake realized they couldn’t escape. The attack was systematic, almost ritualistic, and it became clear—SunnyField was no ordinary town, and they were no ordinary people. They began to explore the town’s history, confronting the dark truths of their own pasts. Everyone revealed their deepest secrets, but Jed’s was the darkest: he confessed to causing the death of a newborn by smothering the baby and blaming his ex-wife.

Chapter IV – Jed’s Sacrifice

Tiffany and Jessica thought Jed’s guilt was the reason the killers were after them, believing that sacrificing him could stop the curse. They laid his body in an old cave, prepared for a ritual. Jed resisted with all his strength, but Jessica mercilessly drew a knife and stabbed him through the heart.

They thought it was over—but the attacks continued, and they realized they had killed an innocent man.

Chapter V – The Finch Family

In panic, they began researching the town’s history. The shocking revelation struck them—the Finch family had been responsible for all the evil, practicing bloody rituals for centuries in exchange for power and fame.

The Finch homes were decorated with old trophies: skulls, bloody emblems, seals of long-forgotten rituals. In the family basement, Tiffany and Jessica found walls inscribed with the names of victims spanning centuries, each meticulously documented, each life recorded in a black book. The tradition was simple: sacrifice the innocent, preserve the family’s power and wealth.

Chapter VI – The Final Ritual and the Truth

While searching for an exit, Tiffany and Jessica captured Jake Finch—the only family member who had refused to follow the bloody traditions. In the ritual cave, surrounded by family symbols, Tiffany and Jessica didn’t know what to do.

Anger and grief consumed them—they thought that by killing a family member, they could end the long-standing terror over the town. Somehow, they captured him and killed him brutally, believing they had finally broken the chain of death.

But the shock was devastating—Ted Finch, Jake’s brother and the sheriff of SunnyField, entered the cave, the keeper of the tradition. He revealed the truth: Jake had been the only one to refuse the tradition. The sheriff shot Jessica, but Tiffany, overwhelmed by rage and sorrow, engaged in a bloody fight.

The confrontation was merciless, but in the end, they managed to kill the sheriff.

Chapter VII – Curse Broken

In the rest of the cave, they found the Finch family’s old book, a tome that held all the secrets and power of the family. Tiffany and Jessica carefully recited the words from the book, erasing the bloody symbols and nullifying the curse that had kept SunnyField in fear for centuries.

As the first rays of sunlight pierced the cave’s darkness, Tiffany took Jessica’s hand. Tears streamed down their faces as they looked at the town outside the cave—SunnyField was quiet, peaceful… but in their hearts remained a deep wound, a reminder of blood, loss, and the courage they had needed to survive.
