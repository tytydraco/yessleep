I wake up at exactly 3:00 a.m. every single night without fail.

And it’s starting to scare the hell out of me.

Before I went back to my hometown recently, I slept like a rock.

No nightmares, no random wake-ups, nothing. I was a heavy sleeper through anything.

It all started with something I thought was harmless.

I was at a local market back home, and I found a vintage crystal photo frame at a secondhand stall.

Inside was a childhood photo of my old primary school classmate.

We used to be super close, so I thought it’d be a funny little gift, so I bought it.

We were inseparable as kids. Climbed trees, stole bird eggs, all that dumb kid stuff.

Then we just… lost touch. I hadn’t heard from him in years.

So I asked around about him.

Turns out… he drowned in the river the summer after we graduated elementary school.

I had no idea. Not even a little.

The next day, I went back to my city, back to my run-down old apartment.

This place creaks and groans all night — pipes, walls, everything.

But I’ve always slept through it all.

Until that night.

Now I wake up at 3:00 a.m. sharp every single night.

Sometimes I hear faint rustling in the pipes.

Sometimes a short, muffled thud outside my window.

Once, I swear I heard birds chirping. At 3 a.m. That’s not normal.

Last week, I woke up groggy, headed to the bathroom.

Then I heard it:

A dull, heavy thud.

From under my bed.

My entire body went cold. I didn’t make a sound.

I slowly walked to the living room, closed my bedroom door, and texted my friend.

He showed up around 5 a.m. with the police.

They searched everywhere. Nothing.

Just a few old boxes under my bed.

“Probably just a rat,” the officer said. They left politely.

But I knew it wasn’t a rat.

I went through the boxes myself. No chewed stuff, no droppings, nothing.

All my books and notebooks were untouched.

And I found something weird: an old childhood diary with a combination lock.

I don’t remember the code.

Ever since then, it’s only gotten worse.

3:00 a.m. like clockwork.

The noises get louder every night. My head feels like it’s splitting.

My friend begged me to see a psychiatrist.

I laughed it off. Blamed the apartment. No mental health issues in my family.

He looked like he wanted to say something… but didn’t.

I stopped hanging out with people. I’m exhausted. Depressed.

My lease isn’t up for another month, and I can’t afford to lose my deposit.

Then one weekend morning, my mom called.

I’d just fallen asleep, so I snapped and complained about the apartment, the 3 a.m. wake-ups, everything.

“I’ll be out soon, Mom. Lease is almost up.”

“Honey… if it’s that bad, just come home.”

I told her I was fine, that I had friends here.

I joked about how my friend thought I was losing my mind.

“Funny, right? No one in our family is like that.”

The line went dead silent.

“What’s wrong, Mom?”

“Do you really not remember… anything?”

“Remember what?”

My vision started to blur. A heavy, sleepy fog.

Then her voice changed.

It went sharp, twisted, not human.

I pulled the phone away from my ear in shock.

The pipes screamed.

Another thud from under the bed.

Birds outside started screeching like crazy.

“Mom…” I whispered, trembling. “What are you talking about?”

A long, terrible silence.

Then a little boy’s voice came through the phone.

“How could you forget me?”

It was him.

My dead childhood classmate.

I stared at the faint white light by my window.

It kept getting bigger.

And bigger.

Until it swallowed everything.
