I just got back from a trip and walked up to see a piece of paper saying **"EVICTION NOTICE"** in bright red on the front door to my apartment. I didn't have much money at the time, so I thought booking a temporary, cheap hotel for the month was a good idea. But if I knew the truth, I would have never done that.

I did a lot of research, but every hotel charged at least 30 bucks a night, which I couldn't afford. Then, I scrolled all the way down and saw a link to a cheap hotel that only charged 5 dollars a night. I felt like the luckiest person alive, But I was wrong.

When I got to the location, it felt a bit unusual for a hotel to charge only 5 dollars a night. But since I had absolutely no money left after the Georgia trip with my besties Azzah, Sadhika, Riya, and Andrea, I just brushed the thought aside. I wish I had turned back. When I went to the registration counter, the clerk handed me the keys with a bright smile that looked so innocent. I got to my room, and the door read Room 501.

The first five days were great. I had the comfiest bed that I almost fell asleep on the second I laid down, an active internet connection, a great quality TV, and a comfy, beautiful couch with a fancy coffee table. I thought I was living every brokie's dream. I thought it was too good to be true.

On the sixth day, I noticed some unusual things going on. I found healthy black hair in the sink, but my hair is brown. I just thought maybe it was normal and I was being paranoid. During the evening, I was just having my coffee when I heard someone close the door to my bedroom. I knew something was wrong, but I kept silent because I didn't have the money to go anywhere else.

On the tenth day, it got worse. I was sleeping, knowing something was wrong, when I heard a screeching, ear-bleeding scream coming from somewhere—but I definitely knew it was inside my room. As the days went by, I couldn't sleep. There was scratching and screaming coming from somewhere in the space, and the lights started flickering. But it was different from a normal light flicker caused by an electrical issue. It was almost like... it was flickering in a rhythm,and i have studied morse code, it was almost like the rhythm was saying.... H-E-L-P?

I barely slept those days. I couldn’t handle this by myself anymore, so I invited my besties over to the room. When I told them what was happening, Azzah asked, *"Are you sure your mind hasn't gone crazy?"*

Andrea agreed, saying, *"Maybe the hair was just left over from the past guests and you didn't really see it. And the lights are probably just an electrical issue—I mean, it is a cheap hotel."*

*"Girl, I told you not to watch those horror videos and paranormal investigations,"* said Riya.

Sadhika just shrugged. *"Guys, you are all wrong. Aurora, all you gotta do is eat some food that tastes amazing, like what we had in Georgia, and you'll feel better."*

I groaned. *"But the lights were flickering in a rhythm and I have studied morse code when i was bored,the lights rhythm was saying help ! And how could I not notice thick, healthy black hair? Riya, I swear to you I don't even believe in paranormal things. And Sadhika, because of that Georgia trip, I’m stuck here!"*

Just as we were arguing about all these unusual things, we finally switched the topic and started talking about random stuff like we always do. Suddenly, we heard heavy footsteps coming from the living room, but if me and all 4 of my friends are here then who is out there? But my friends were so busy talking and laughing that they didn't notice . I asked *"Guys did you hear that ?"*

*"Hear what ?"* azzah asked,

*"not this again"* said riya.

*"See Aurora, you told you barely slept right ?"* asked sadhika

*"yeah? because of these stuff happening"* i said

*"your mind is just making it up,just forget about it "* said andrea

I thought for a second and said *"maybe your right, I should forget about it"* after a while, they all packed up and left.

I was cleaning up the trash from our hangout when I noticed something strange sitting on the fancy coffee table. It was an old, dusty silver pocket watch that was ticking aggressively fast. I thought it belonged to one of my friends, but then I remembered none of them brought anything like that. I just brushed it off, figuring my friends are notoriously forgetful and someone must have dropped it.

I went into the washroom to wash up before bed, but the moment I turned on the light, my heart stopped. Written across the bathroom mirror in a thick, dripping red liquid were the words: ***GET OUT***.

I finally couldn't take it anymore. I definitely knew this was not my mind making it up. The scratches on the walls, the night screams, the blood on the mirror—none of it was a hallucination. It was real. Every single horrific thing I had suffered in Room 501 for the past month was actually happening, my stomach dropped when the realization hit me all at once. Panic took over. I grabbed my bags, not caring where I went, as long as it was far away from this room.

I ran down to the front desk, and screamed at the clerk about the scratches on the walls, the ear-bleeding cries, the blood on the mirror, and the horrific things I had endured in Room 501 for the past month.

The clerk just stared at me like I was insane. *"Lady,"* he said, rolling his eyes, *"we don't have a Room 501. Our floor plan ends at room 500."* He thought I was just another crazy scammer trying to get a refund, and he had security throw me out onto the street.

Shaking, I sat in a coffee shop across the street and pulled up my phone to search the history of the building. The very first news article that popped up made my blood run cold.

The hotel had caught fire exactly one year ago today. The entire top floor was completely gutted, killing everyone trapped inside.

I’m looking across the street at the building right now. To everyone else, it’s just a blackened, abandoned ruin. But my clothes still smell like the room. My skin is still covered in dust. If that place burned down a year ago... where the hell have I been sleeping for the last thirty days?.....
