In my late teenage years, I worked at a golf course. I have since left this job, but there was one occurrence that I cannot explain no matter how many times I have gone over this exact memory. It isn't the usual old rowdy golfers that are drunk trying to start fights with you, nor was it the geese that liked to attack everyone every season (though that was a funny in retrospect). I haven't really told many people of this, maybe three people at tops in close circles that knew about my high school job. 

I worked at a golf course as a ground's keeper of sorts, starting my job normally in the middle or so of March and ending sometime in December if I stayed later in the season before the weather soured and no one wanted to freeze in the cold playing golf. The occurrence happened around March, the day had been slow, and I had been working late until the sun had set over the horizon and all the carts were stored in the barn overnight. I locked up, told my boss I was leaving, and headed out into the empty parking lot to head home; this was not out of the ordinary for me... at typical night, like any other. 

I drove through the dark countryside, winding through the small village that this course was closest too and cut through the back side streets that took you down areas filled with spaced out houses and farmland. I didn't have my high beams on; driving several miles under the speed limit as I puttered along with my driver's side window rolled down just a crack since it had gotten too hot in my car with the heat going. 

The only light on this route was a single light above a three-way intersection near a school and church. The light slowly growing distant as I passed the normal crossroad intersection and drove further into the rural area. I had my radio playing, I put on the "AM" Album by the Arctic Monkeys before I left and had been listening to that on my drive. 

As I drove further up the road, getting closer to the main roads that I usually cut to. I saw something reflective swaying back and forth in the middle of the road. I assumed it had to have been a cyclist in the area; however, it made no sense since it was fairly cold outside and usually cyclist have lights or some sort of reflective clothing on, but this person didn't. The little reflective bar at the back of their bike swaying along the road before it dove into the ditch on the side of the road. 

Mind you, this is rural farmland, ditches are not some small little dug out section that is like going over a small hill. These ditches are deep and wide, about the width of a one lane street and a depth similar to that of a trench you see in WWI films and photographs. So, a guy on a bike plummeting into one of these deep ditches would be a problem; especially if there was any water that had frozen at the bottom. 

I stopped my car, sitting there for a moment debating on getting out of my car to see if whoever it was is alright. I saw something emerge from the ditch, and it wasn't a person carrying a bike. What emerged from the ditch was this massive black furred creature that resembled that of a dog. It's eyes reflecting yellow in the headlights and its face mangy. It did not look right, its limbs too long. it bared its teeth at me, its greyish pink lips curling back as it crept into the darkness of the baren farming field.

I rolled up my windows and drove home in silence. I turned off my radio and kept glancing behind me to make sure whatever it was wasn't following me. I never knew what it was. I don't want to know what it was. It never happened again.
