**Part 1.** 

 

My dad died when I was 8, I can't really describe him much better than he was a cruel man, with cruel thoughts, and even crueller ways of punishment. It is important to know that; because of my dad I was a bit “different” compared to most normal children. I was a recluse, kept to myself a lot, this made it hard to find friends and even harder to keep them, before he died, I would occasionally bring someone over for tea. And my father being the way he was, would make it very uncomfortable for everyone there. 

I remember the last time a friend ever came over, we were all sat at the dining room table, a plate of microwaved food laid in front of me (we were what you could call a lower-class family). My friend sat next to me happily eating as my dad watched her from the head of the table. “So...Clementine, you got a boyfriend?” she turned to look at him and smiled politely as she spoke “nope, well josh asked me but he's gross” she quickly glanced at me and we both laughed softly. My dad put down his fork and turned his body more to face her, “Oh really...that seems odd, a girl as...pretty as you, I would assume you'd have boys all around you”. She looked back up at him and smiled once more, but this time with a hint of uncomfortableness hidden inside “Thank you Mr Marshall”. My father's face slightly twisted into an oily smirk as he leaned more forward “Yeah...I bet any boy would love to get close to you...play with your shiny black hair...watch you dance in that red dre-”. Just then my mother called from the other room “John, could you come here please”, My father paused for a second sighing, as he stood up he continued to look at Clementine with that smirk, the one I hated, it was always plastered across his face whenever he was doing or thinking of something vile and sick. 

He walked away and into the other room, I saw Clementine from the corner of my eye, she was looking down at her feet as she slowly hit her leather school shoes together. Her hands lay in her lap, picking the skin at the end of her fingers, I paused for a moment and leant in a little. “You okay Clem?”, it took her a few seconds to respond “Yeah...I just...didn't like the way your dad looked at me...” I didn't really know what to do so I just put my hand on her back and rubbed it gently. I knew what she meant, my dad would look at me and some of the other friends I brought around my house the same way. “No Mary I'm not doing anything; how dare you even accuse me of such things” I heard my mum and dad speaking in the kitchen, I guess my mum must have caught on to what he was trying to do. “John...I'm not fucking stupid yknow...I can tell...”. She lowered her voice to a whisper “You can't...not again...not here...” suddenly there was a loud metallic crash as I heard something large hit the wooden floor of our kitchen. “Listen here you little bitch, don't you ever, ever! Say that shit again, or you know what happens”. The soft whimpers of my mother were drowned out by my dad coming back into the dining room. 

He sat down and let out a long breath; he took a sip from his can of whatever lager he was drinking and looked over at me and Clementine “Sorry if you heard any of that kids...sometimes adults...disagree on certain things and need to sort it out”. Clementine looked up from her shoes and to my father “It's okay Mr Marshall we didn't hear anything”. My father stayed silent for a few seconds, taking another sip of his drink, “Good...good...your both good kids yknow...I bet your parents are proud of you Clementine...so sweet...such a good girl”. Clementine didn't respond just nodded softly, I tapped her on the shoulder and motioned my head, signalling to leave and head to my room, she got up and started walking behind me, just as we were about to turn the corner into the corridor my father spoke up. “Connor...come here a second” I turned and gave a half smile as Clem continued towards my room, I walked over and sat on the chair next to him. 

“What did you forget...” I looked up at him and didn't say anything, **BANG**! his fist slammed against my face, I flew back into my chair, the wooden slats almost breaking against the force. “You didn't put your plate away did you...” He spoke through gritted teeth, little flecks of spit pushing through his off centre and yellow teeth. “No, I didn't...I'm sorry dad...” I could already feel my adrenaline starting to bubble, my brow quivering as my vision became blurry, he looked at me for a few seconds before turning towards the table and taking a long gulp of his drink. “You know what happens now don't you...” I didn't even look up I nodded my head as I wiped my face with my red school uniform jumper. He leaned over really close to my ear, the smell of his cheap lager permeating my nostrils and the hotness of his breath gave the feeling as if I was being suffocated, “later...when your friend goes home” I didn't say anything, my face began to sting as my skin turned red, “now piss off...” I stood up and shuffled my way out of the room and down the corridor until I reached my room. 

I opened my door and walked inside, shutting it behind me I noticed Clementine sat in the middle of the floor, she stood up as I walked in and spoke softly, her hands covered by her sleeve as she fiddled with it. “Connor, I think I'm going to head home...Its late and you know how my moth-” I interrupted her and stepped closer “please, stay...just for a bit longer”. She lowered her head and grabbed her puffy red coat as she walked towards my door, “I'm sorry Connor”, she walked out of my room and I followed her as she made her way to my front door, my dad however caught her just as she was about to leave. 

“Hey Clementine...you leaving?” she paused and turned around looking up at him, but her eyes were looking anywhere else “Yes Mr Marshall, my mum will be very worried, it's getting dark”. My dad who was leaned against the door frame now stood up straight and walked over, he knelt down, “Call me John please...now its rude to not say goodbye right?” Clementine looked over at him and nodded softly. My dad smiled as he opened his arms, she reluctantly walked over, he wrapped him arms around her, “such a good girl aren't you Clementine” I watched as his hand was slowly going up and down her back, his stained yellow fingers contrasting against her shiny, beautiful red coat. 

After what felt like hours, he eventually let go of her and opened the door letting her out, that was the last time Clem spoke to me, after that she avoided me like the plague. He turned to me after a few seconds, his smile completely faded as he looked down at me, he walked over grabbing me by the scruff of my neck, I kicked and screamed as he dragged me down the hallway and towards my room for my “punishment”. This wasn't the first time my dad had done this to me, I knew what was coming and so did my mother, but she never stepped in or even tried to say anything, I would often catch her crying in the kitchen afterwards in the late hours of the night. 

My dad tried to do the same thing to many of the people I brought over, but thankfully never succeeded, and after a while, through a mix of hushed whispers in the playground and me isolating myself I never had anyone over again, but my father still continued and for a while before his death it was getting worse and worse, more frequent and more violent, some days I had to miss school because I was either too bruised or could barely walk. 

It was around 2 in the morning when the first police car pulled up outside of our house, I don't remember much other than my mum crying in the kitchen as two police officers surrounded her, then a few ushered me back into my room, they tucked me into my bed and told me “it's gonna be alright lad, don't worry, just get some sleep”. It was all a blur after that, the funeral passed by like nothing, I don't even really recall when we started packing. But I remember very clearly being sat in the backseat of my mums small black mini, the raindrops hitting the window as I watched them race against each other, betting on which one would reach the bottom first, the occasional speed-bump would knock me out of my daze and I turned to the front of the car, I leaned to my side and saw my mum. “Where are we going again mum”, she turned down the radio and spoke very softly, like the words would shatter her if she spoke too loudly, “somewhere new Connor, a fresh start...”. I turned my head back out of the window and saw a large rusty green sign pass by, it said “Welcome to Oxford!”
