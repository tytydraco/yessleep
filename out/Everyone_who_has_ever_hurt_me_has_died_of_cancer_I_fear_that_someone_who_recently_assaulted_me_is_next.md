Maybe this isn’t the right place for this post, but I don’t know where to put this. Every person who has ever caused me great harm has gotten cancer. Many have died rather quickly (and terribly) after having done something malicious, violent, or sexual to me.

When I was young, I lived in a small community. Death hits hard when you know almost everyone or at least know someone who knows someone who dies. It seemed strange to me even then that so many people I knew died of cancer in my hometown. Especially since it was a very healthy community and most people had active lifestyles, ate locally grown/raised foods etc. (lots of wealthy outdoorsy hippie-types in the PNW).  
When I got older and moved to bigger communities/towns/cities I found that this same trend of people around me dying quite suddenly of a cancer kept happening. The news always came as a shock even though, as I started to realize, most were also people who had done me great harm.

These have been particularly cruel people; in once case they were an ex-husband of a friend of mine who threatened to kill both of us when I helped her leave him, and later harbored her away from him for a bit. He got a diagnosis of pancreatic cancer a couple weeks after attacking me at a gas station quick-stop. He dropped weight so fast it was truly horrifying. He was all alone since the divorce went through, and his children wanted nothing to do with him. My friend wound up caring for her abusive ex-husband during his last weeks of life out of her sense of Christian good will. I supported her decision after seeing how sickly he was.

I had a cruel boss who humiliated and abused me. He threatened the security of my job if I told….a few months later he got diagnosed with leukemia. And two co-workers at the same company who made my life a living hell (and who very nearly killed me on a job site on two separate occasions) both got diagnoses of cancer in the same month! One passed very quickly of a brain tumor (less than a month after learning of the diagnosis) the other had another type of blood cancer. The community rallied together to support him and his family (he had a wife and two young boys and came from a well known family). I don’t know what became of him as I moved away around d that time and cut ties with everyone at the company.

There was even the sudden death of my landlord’s dog that bit me! It was a three-year old cattle dog that ran up behind me while I was headed to my apartment (over the top of the main garage) and bit me hard enough to break the skin through my jeans. Three days later it died. My landlord told me they were in shock because it just laid down one day and died. It was so bizarre they had an autopsy done on him. The vet found the dog’s organs to be completely covered in tumors. They never saw any signs that it was sick. That really freaked me out and became the impetus for my revisiting my past and the various characters who have impacted me over the years.

When I finally sat down and really thought about all the people I knew who hurt me (badly) I realized they had all been afflicted with cancer. All unexpectedly and most died rather quickly after the diagnosis. I swear, other than thinking nasty (sometimes poisonous) thoughts in my grief and anger, I never did anything to these people—certainly nothing that could cause a disease like cancer.

Recently, I was assaulted rather brutally. I will be undergoing physical therapy to regain some impaired mobility due to the neurological damage I suffered. I worry that my cognitive state and mental capacity might not be what it once was ever again and this has left me feeling incredibly depressed. I keep experiencing waves of rage and anger as well as feelings of frustration about the injustice of what happened to me. Spiteful thoughts keep over taking me; sometimes I find myself thinking about their suffering and then I start to worry….what if they actually DO get sick? What the hell is wrong with me? I’m either bat-shit-crazy, or, somehow very very dangerous.
