About 6 months ago my dog went missing. He’s a grey Great Dane, I’ve had him for almost 8 years. His name is Leo. He has lovely big brown eyes and whenever I call his name or talk to him, he cocks his head with curiosity or excitement. The sheer whisper of the word walk would send his head swinging from side to side or up and down with excitement. He was a lovely boy. A good boy.

I thought he was gone for good. It had been 3 weeks. I had gone on holiday and left my brother in charge of looking after Leo. He said he took him for a walk in the woods by my house and Leo just started to go crazy and managed to get out of his collar and ran into the woods.

It was hard to believe Leo would do something like that, as he was usually such a calm dog especially in his old age. But I also didn’t believe that my brother would do something to him, he loved Leo. There was a big age gap between us, so he had essentially grown up with him. 

He was heartbroken about what had happened. He called me crying after hours of looking. Saying who couldn’t find him. Mum and dad said he was freezing when he got in. They had look as well but no luck. Which was odd, the woods weren’t that wood big, you could easily walk them in an hour and width about 20 mintues.

I missed that little goofy ball so much, but I didn’t want to let on to my brother how heartbroken I was. It made me feel even worse seeing him.

But then three weeks later, there he was. At the end on my garden. The woods that my brother had taken him too wormed around to the back of my house. There was no fence so I could just walk from my house straight into the woods and I often would with Leo.

I was shocked to see him. I was just about to sit down for tea when I saw a glimpse of him in the corner of my eye. I stood up immediately and ran to the back door, calling his name as I ran out. He stood there for a moment, still. My run slow down a bit as I saw him. He seemed silent, still.

‘Leo! Leo come here boy, it’s me. It’s mummy,’ I said holding out my hand. ‘Come on lovely boy,’ He walked towards me. Not slowly but not quickly, just casually. I thought it was odd, especially with how excited he would usually get when he saw me after I had been on holiday, or just away at work.

Then he walked past me to the back door and waited for me to open it.  I watched him for a moment, taken back then open it for him and he casually walked in.

I watched him casually walk around the house. Staring at everything, his head staying up right as he did, he looked stiff. *He’s probably cold* I told myself and grabbed him his blanket to put over him. I attempted to wrap it around him, but he stood stiff as ever and then continued to walk around the house, the blank lazily lying on him, like a cape. I chuckled a little, it was funny seeing him with it again. Then I remembered my brother. So, I grabbed my phone from the living room and called him immediately.

‘Lydia?’ He said hoarse.

‘Did you just wake up? It’s 8 o’clock at night.’

‘Yeah, I took a nap. Everything okay?’

‘Better than okay. I found Leo.’ I said as I paced around the house searching to where Leo had gotten to.

‘What?! Really? Where?’

‘In the garden, he must have found his way home,’

‘Wow, I can’t believe it. How?’

‘I don’t know, I mean I’ve walked him in those woods his whole life,’ he wasn’t in the living room, or the kitchen.

‘How does he look? He must be hungry?’ 

I hadn’t even thought.

‘Umm… well honestly he looks how he did when I last saw him’ I said, a growing worry now started bubbling in my stomach. 

*I hadn’t of imagined it. Had I!? He was there? I held him?* I doubted.

‘Really? What did he eat rabbits?’

Then saw him, in the dining room. Facing the wall. His ears standing straight and his eyes fixed on the wall.

‘Yeah, I don’t know. I gotta to go, I might see if I can take him to the vets just to check he’s okay.’

‘They won’t be open this late will they?’

‘Some do…bye.’ I hung up the phone and tried to pull his head away from the wall. Eventually he did. He had this vacant expression on his face. 

‘Are you hungry, boy?’ I asked as I went into the kitchen to get him bowl, though I hadn’t been able to throw his bowl away, but I did throw his dog food away. I opened the fridge to see what I had. Bacon. From the butchers. His favourite.

I heard his claws scratching against the tiled floors. He starred at me as I unwrapped the meat and put it in his bowl. He stood there for a moment looking at me. I pointed at his bowl.

‘Food.’ I suggested, he looked down at the bowl then leaned down. I picked up my phone again and began to call the vet. I walked into the hallway as I did, leaving Leo.

‘Hi, I was just wondering if you guys are open still?’

The sound of Leo eating the meat was louder than normal. I could just hear his chomping over and over again. 

‘Till 9? Yeah, that’s great.’

Chomp. Chomp. Chomp. 

At times it even sounded like he was biting the bowl. He must be hungry I thought.

‘Yeah, it’s Lydia Taylor and Leo.’

Chomp. Chomp. Chomp.

‘Yeah, I found him. I can’t believe it. That’s why I want to bring him in to see if he’s okay.’

As the receptionist was questioning me, about Leo. I looked back into the kitchen.

Chomp. Chomp. Chomp. 

‘No I don’t see anything wrong with him…’

Chomp. Chomp. Chomp.

‘Yeah, I’ll be right down.’ I hung up the phone

Chomp. Chomp. Chomp.

‘Leo. Leo?’ I moved him away from his bowl. He hadn’t eaten anything. He had just been biting it over and over again. The dents of his teeth had made the meat look even more mushy than usual.

We went to the vet. I was greeted by the receptionist Raya she had gotten 3 new piercing since I last seen her, she already had twelve. She mentions that Dr Rehman was away this month, he was sick with something. They weren’t sure what, but they think it was one of the animals that gave it to him. She said Dr Rain would see me.

‘I thought she retired?’ I asked.

‘Yeah well with Dr Rehman gone we need someone and she was the only option,’ she said with a roll of her eyes. Dr Rain was one of the stricter vets. Never smiled, harsh to talk to. I remember what she said to me when we had to put our cat down. 

‘It happens. Part of life. Can you leave room?’

I expected the worst when I walked in. Dr Rain was facing the wall when I walked in. She turned around with a smile on her face. Nothing is scarier than seeing someone who never smiled, smile. Her loose skin hung over her face like a bulldog. As if it was trying to force her into a frown.

‘Oh, hello,’ I said. ‘You look…chipper.’

‘Always my dear, always,’ she replied her voice was hoarse and scratchy, not surprising as she used to smoke a pack a day, I could always smell it on her, and since retiring I’m guessing it went up too 2 packs.

She said Leo was healthy, nothing major. I mentioned some of the weird behaviour. She said it was normal for an animal to act different after something like this. 

‘He needs you to show him who he was and who you were to him,’ the vet smiled a toothy smile. ‘He just needs some taking care of, he’s been alone in those woods,’ She cooed petting him. He seemed to respond a bit.

‘Animals get trauma too.’ She sang. I had never thought an animal could get trauma like humans. But it reminded me of my childhood cat, Billie, she was an outdoor curiose cat until we moved house, then she refused to go outside or leave the house at all. She hated the garden.

The next day my brother came over and ran to see Leo. He still lived with my parents, and they lived on the opposite side of the woods.

‘Hey boy! Remember me.’ He said as he patted Leo on the head. There was no reaction. He simply stared at him. ‘What have we got beef now? Come on boy it’s your uncle.’

‘The vet said he has trauma or somet?’

‘Trauma?!’ My brother exclaimed. ‘Why what happened to him out there? He’s an animal, he’s supposed to be in the woods.’

‘I don’t know, it’s no like he can tell me.’ I looked down at Leo.

He cocked his head.

‘I didn’t know animals could get trauma,’ my brother said distracting me as he walked into the kitchen. ‘What the hell is in his bowl?’

‘Dog food, but he doesn’t sallow it, he just bites it,’

‘Weird.’ As he jumped on the counter to grab some snacks. 

My brother and I walked Leo that lunchtime. Leo was still and silent for the most part. Every now and again I’d see him cock his head. The entire walk he would look back at me. My brother even noticed. He joked saying that he must have missed me and wanted to make sure he wouldn’t lose me again.

That night I ordered a pizza; I wasn’t in the mood for cooking. My head was everywhere at the minute. My parents were away for the week, so I was also in charge of my little brother not being too stupid while they were away. I bit into my pizza, gulp.

*Gulp.*

I looked up, turning to where I had heard the noise. Leo was sat in the living room staring at me as I sat in the dining room. He wasn’t eating anything. 

I took another bite.

So did Leo. But of nothing. Just the air.

I swallowed.

Leo swallowed.

I went to bit again but paused before hand. Leo didn’t. He chomped at the air. The clashing of his teeth echoing slightly.

I looked at him and then at his bowl in the kitchen. He still wasn’t really eating his food. I grabbed his bowl; it still had dog food in it. I put it down in front of him. He leaned down, took a bite. Then swallowed. And continued to do so, till there was nothing left in his bowl. 

I sat down at the table and continued to eat my pizza. Leo watched for a moment before turning and leaving. He had become a lot more isolated, didn’t want to spend time with me, just observe. I thought about how he was before he went missing. Confident friendly dog always trying to sit on my lap despite his size. If I let him on the couch, he took up most of it, his legs hanging off the end as he slept.

I cleaned up my plate and his bowl, then sat in the living room. I was happy to rest, I hadn’t been sleeping much, and it was getting to me. I sat down on the couch, relaxed sleepily let out a ‘Mmmm.’ as I closed my eyes.

*‘Mmmm’*

I heard echo from behind me. Making me turn around as quickly as I heard it. But there was nothing there, nothing but Leo. He looked at me. And I looked at him. He cocked his head and his eyes were… white? Not all white but the bit around it. He still had his big brown eyes, just not as big and now with a white part, like a human.

I hated to admit it; I wished every day for him to return; I wanted him back so badly. But now, he was scaring me. What could I say though? My dog has different eyes? He just stares too much? He’s just weird but in a vibe sort of way, nothing physical? He eats when I eat, he gulps when I gulp? Something wasn’t right. 

I thought about leaving him at a rescue centre. But every time I got close to doing it, I remembered what the vet said*. It’s trauma, it’s normal for animals to react like this.* And I think how alone he must have been out there. How cold and hungry. Then I think about the little puppy I brought home 7 years ago. That sweet little puppy I raised. And shared so many memories with. My lovely boy. My good boy. 

I missed him so much. I thought maybe he needed more time. I called the vets to see if I could get another appointment. But they were full that month. Raya said some of the other vets had gotten sick too. I asked if the sickness was contagious to dogs. Dr Rain had told her no, dogs were fine. It was more wild animals.

A few days went by and nothing new happened. He started to swallow more of his food. He was still observing a lot. And every now and again I’d hear an echo. A sigh, a cough or a gulp. Right after I would. Nothing that a dog couldn’t do. But still. 

Until one weekend. I had gone out with some friends and stayed over at a mate’s house. I arrived home late the next day. I probably overstayed my welcome, but I didn’t want to go home. I didn’t want to see Leo. It was already dark when I got in.

‘Leo? Leo?’ I said as I slowly crept around the corner of the living room. Trying not to make a sound. ‘Leo?’ I whispered almost hoping he wouldn’t’ here. I moved to the dining room, looking around every corner. I knew he’d be hungry. Then I heard a low growl from behind me. That’s when I saw him. 

It was Leo. But he was taller. His legs and arms much longer than before. The toes on his paws were elongated and disfigured, they were bleeding. Some of his claws had fallen off. And his eyes. His eyes were whiter now with small piercing brown irises, one larger than the other seemingly pulsating. His fur was longer around his head, but not all over, it was patchy. Its snout was letting out deep heavy breaths, as its mouth opened a gap. Breathing from it, moving up and down opposite to its snout. His fangs were bigger and some poking out of his mouth, some looking more round than others. I had never seen anything like this.

‘Lyyddiiaaa…’ it whispered with a hoarse burst in the middle of my name as if something was moaning within. ‘Lydiaaaa…’ it whispered again ending with a spurting cough that also sounded like a grunted low bark.

I began to walk slowly into the kitchen. I could hear what was left of its’ claws hitting the tiled floor as it followed me.

‘Lydiaaaa,’

I placed my hand on the handle of the back door. I heard its claws again. But now in a rhythm as if it was tapping every individual finger on the floor, one at a time. I saw a smile stretch across its’ face.

‘LooOOoovely,’ it hissed. A few of its’ teeth splatted out, as blood dripped down its’ mouth. It stretched a toothless smile across its’ face once more.

I ripped open the back door and ran into the woods.

I ran straight for my parents’ house. I didn’t look back once but I could hear something clumsily chasing me through the woods. The pace of its’ legs seemed off. Every now and again I could see a shadow rise from behind me, lit up by the moon. Before crashing down. It ran like a newborn deer, I could hear it smashing into trees and rocks, moaning as it did. Calling my name every time. 

I managed to get to the house and banged on the door.

‘Let me in! Let me in! It’s me!’ I yelled

I could hear heavy breathing growing louder from behind me. In the reflection of the window, I could see it creeping from the woods. Attempting to stand on two legs to only fall back down on all fours. Its’ shoulder-blades rolling back as it did. By brother eventually opened the door and I pushed past him slamming the door as I did.

‘Chill out.’ He lazily replied. He was high. I could smell it.

‘Twat.’ I replied.

‘What? What’s up? What’s happening?’ He said looking through the window of the door. I grabbed him before he got too close

‘Leo… Somethings… wrong with… Leo.’ I spat still trying to catch my breathe.

‘Yeah, he’s traum-‘before my brother could finish his sentence the creature smashed through the kitchen window.

‘Lydiaaa…’

It hung in the window, the sound of its’ back paws clawing on the wall to push it in. It front claws scratching at the inner walls to balance itself. Its’ eyes scorching into mine as its’ hair covered its grotesque face. I could see its spine moving underneath its’ skin as it did. Shards of glasses carving into its’ back.

‘Lydiaaaahhhh’ it cried spitting blood, while even more poured from its’ wounds. Its’ eyes widen and burning. It looked as though it was crying. Seemingly feeling pain for the first time.

I grabbed a knife from a draw in the kitchen and began slicing at its face. It screeched with every strike. A human-beast like screech, piercing my ears.

My brother awkwardly threw books at it from my dad’s bookshelf. 

I dove the knife into its’ skull. It moaned and whined as blood gushed from its head down its face, blanketing its eyes, which pulsated and stretched even more. 

It fell back out of the window and attempted to crawl back into the woods. But its’ wounds were too much, it left a bloody trail as it slid on the grass crawling towards the woods with it two front arms. Seemingly howling at something. Its’ body laid limp at the end of my parents’ garden. 

We called the police and no one knew what to make of it. They said it was rabies or a tumour that made him go crazy. I don’t know what it was.

But I moved away from those woods as quickly as I could and managed to convince my parents to do the same.

And I’m glad I did, as when the creature was howling a human beast like howl into the woods, I swear I could hear something howl back.
