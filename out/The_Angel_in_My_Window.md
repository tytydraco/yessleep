This is not really my favorite story to tell, but, it's the easiest one I remember.  
  
For some context, I was born ill. It was something that has been present since the first day, ever since I began to breathe, as if my mom's unwanting of getting a baby was transferred into a baby unwilling to live.   
  
I was ill, am ill and will die ill. It is merely the way of life, how I was constructed and how I shall die. Between the effects of my fathers alcoholism and my mother's lack of care, I soon found myself with a litany of illnesses, all chronic and products of an immunodepressed immune system. MRSA, hay fever, things no one should experience in the first pair of years of life. I was supposed to be chewing cables or something, not connected to a respirator, you know? 

And yet, my first memory will forever be stained by the heavy veil of E. Coli. I can't say for sure, what matter of issues led to me ending up infected, as far as I've been told it was a combination of unforeseen circumstances and my mother wanting her two year old toddler to eat a hamburger that costed only a dollar. All I can say for certain, and as good as it was explained to me, I had a 'tornado inside of me that keeps destroying everything in its path'. It was certainly painful. I do remember, when they took me out for tests, I felt like I was laying on a cloud, so sleepy. And so, when I came home, it was as easy as that, I fell asleep, right in my bed.

Even if I slept in my mother's bedroom, in a separate bed, I never reached for my mother in the night. I always laid down, my face right against the wall below the window, with the wooden frame perfectly slotted above my temple. It was cold, and it did soothe me best. With the window facing the street, if my mom opened it, I would feel the cold chill of the night, or better yet, hear whatever neighbor walked towards the corner store. If I was unlucky, I'd hear my mother going to the store without me. That day, around the night, it did sound like that, with how close the person walked near the window.

And yet, I heard my name. It called for me, by name. 

Even if I won't tell it, for privacy reasons, it was weird to hear it. It is the diminutive of a family name, and yet, this person called for me, not for my mother, not for my grandmother. I knew they called for me, for they were talking so, so gently. As such, I opened my eyes, to see who was it that had found the shape of me enough in the dark so they would distinguish my form so near the window. Even as such, the sight was so bright that I couldn't quite understand it.

"Come with me." They said, for I couldn't tell if they were a boy or a girl. Long hair, wispy and light, brighter than the lamppost behind them. They sounded so soft, so kind to me, as they touched the window pane with that beautiful pale hand of theirs, attempting to pry it open. They had gorgeous eyes, the color of honey, that seemed to glisten and shine in the dark.

"I can't." I said, shaking my head, even if it made me dizzier, it was the only way I knew my mom understood I didn't want something, like eggs, or meat.

"But you have to, it's time." They said, their smile and voice soft as they gently opened the window further, trying to touch me. Even if they looked good, even if by all senses I felt better, something was wrong. Something didn't quite make sense, even if everything felt good. 

I screamed, as loud as I could, my voice hoarse and unsure. And then, like nothing had ever happened, she was screaming at me, telling me far and wide how I couldn't just scream at her like that.   
  
I can't quite remember all of the details, mostly because even then, all I could focus was the memory of the stranger's face. 

So many years passed. Mostly since I learned it wasn't quite a 'tornado' what was passing through me, nor was I supposed to speak to strangers in the way that I had with that stranger during that night, even if my mother attributed it to a particularly pleasant night terror.

... Pleasant night terror? No, it was not pleasurable.

The truth is, so many years passed by, and between my family wishing I was a good catholic girl and my parent's waning interest in my being, the birth of my siblings, I was relegated to the role of a good older sister. Permanently single, of course, but that was by choice. Not a thing felt good, to me. Every relationship I had fell flat, and that is precisely what rattles me to my core now that I think about it.

No matter what I did, no matter how much I cared for myself, whether it was surviving through influenza or a bad bout of shingles, there was not a moment when I found respite. That amount of respite was not even given when I got my first boyfriend. A douche, if you ask me. Not a douche if you ask the one who needs a partner to fall back on, to show and justify as the real reason why you are cool and totally not a virgin loser.

Anyways. he was the perfect decoy. Like a face that you can show and yet claim no reconnaissance for. He held some resemblance to the kind of blokes that some girls like, vaguely ethnic and yet white enough that no one doubts that he's in the same league as you. Small, beady eyes, perpetually whiny tone of voice, pale sickly skin. I didn't quite fancy him. He knew it, that I didn't quite like him *like that*, and yet, he still did quite well fit the spot of what my classmates considered an acceptable and totally not fake boyfriend.

Which is good. 

Except when it wasn't. See, in all of my infinitely wise, chronically congested glory, I didn't particularly partake in alcohol, less so any other matter of drugs. Not as if I shame other people, only when they spend far too much on it, and so, when Sebastian began to outdrink my father, I thought that perhaps there was no way to salvage that relationship. It wasn't as if my friends ever knew him, after all, how could I pride myself on dating a drunk? So, before I knew it, I had made my mind. I had to break up with him.

... Without quite knowing how to break the news to my friends, that my fake boyfriend was now a ex-fake boyfriend, not graduated to real boyfriend, but, well, you know what I mean.

"When are you going to introduce him to us?" One of my friends said, as we were eating our lunch, without knowing how much anxiety her question was causing me.

"Ah, oof, soon. Soon, I hope." I said, nodding my head, after all, with simple ways of physical, non-verbal language people always knew better, right? Surely they knew better than to ask more of me, whilst I am so--

"How does he look like?" That same friend, turned to look at me, chipper as can be... How does he look like, she asks to the one who definitely does not want to admit her boyfriend is a drunkard who looks like his middle name is Hennessy. 

"He's blonde and tall and handsome. Really handsome." I said, trying to sound sure and as if I had not been bullshitting and describing some pretty lad I once saw at a Calvin Klein ad. 

Of course no one believed me. Who could?

And that was only the beginning! Then, some weeks after that whole dilemma, my grandmother fell grievously ill. It was not even something I had planned, or, well, not something anyone could've foreseen. You know how sometimes people make the excuse that their grandmother has died and therefore they deserve to be exempted from work or school? That happened to me, only of course, the days before the first day of my third semester of college. Hadn't it been bad enough, had I not been forced to assist monday, yesterday, and yet...

Yesterday night, I dreamed another nightmare. I went to school, and yet, I could not find my path, nor a way to defend myself as I looked all through the darkened hallways, the smell of formaldehyde and antiseptic almost cold to my nose as I walked, almost endlessly. My legs ached, nevermind how cold I was in my dreams. And then, I saw him. Or who I thought must've been a way out. A mere dream, I knew, as I ran after some figure in my dreams, bright yellow breaking through the serenity of the blues of my dreams until he turned, extending his arms before I ran directly into him. He caught me, he caught me, even if I had woken up. He caught me, and I woke up. Who even was him?

... What had I dreamed? Only the color of gold and the scent of death seemed to cling to my bones. Maybe a mere reminder, an echo of what I had lived through my grandmother's funeral.

Today was a whole new beast. 

I can still quite remember, my idiotic train of thought. I didn't even bother taking my backpack in the morning, knowing that I had only a class and we were meant to do a debate, so, no use for my notebooks. I thought, since I have quite a hefty history of fainting spells, I shall eat breakfast. Breakfast of champions it was, burritos and a coke bottle. Half a liter of coke and three burritos later, I was happy as could be. Really, you never know how happy and dandy you are until you eat your favorite food and then leave all dandy for your class. I didn't even bother remembering where I left my phone, just went and entered the classroom, and sat down for my third day of class.   
  
Around twenty minutes into the class, I was overcome with *that* feeling, that weird feeling when your stomach is doing backflips, almost a reprimand for having eaten food meant for someone twice your size, it almost ached inside of me. Nevermind, might need to take it easy. Needless to say, it was not easy, since I ended up fainting. Over a tummyache! Just a tummyache! The ride to the nurse's office was not a blast, as I got wheeled out of my room and brought to the nurse's office, with the medical students doing their practice work and so much more. Between all of it, they asked if they should ring someone to fetch me. Even if I knew I had myself as my own emergency contact, I wasn't quite lucid enough to protest and explain myself, not even able to protest as I gagged over nothing.

I sat down, whilst pondering my options, for I could not even remember a thing. Could they call my mother, if she had no way to contact me? Had any of my siblings seen me get wheeled into the infirmary? Worse yet, what was I supposed to even do, to justify the sheer course of events that I had gone through that landed me into the infirmary? As such, there I was, on the nurse's office, drinking water with electrolytes and getting treated like an princess, all over a simple fainting spell and some general malaise. And yet, as I pressed by face against the cold wall of the nurse's office, I heard a knock at the door.

"Oh, he did come! I thought he just wouldn't answer." The head nurse said, as she got out of her office, as she went to open the door, whilst I didn't even bother tilting my head, not moving from my perch as I stared right to the door. And there, with the backdrop of the sun against his back, a beautiful stranger, tall with long blonde hair, with one hand holding my cellphone and in the other my backpack, as the nurse gestured off to the side with her head, at the handsome stranger that was making his way inside the room. Bright eyes broke through the cold light of the infirmary, as he stared intently at me, just as the nurse chirped. "Your boyfriend's here."

I couldn't even move, just staring up at him without being able to say a thing.   
  
It was time, after all.
