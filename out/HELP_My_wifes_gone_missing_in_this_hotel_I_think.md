"Are you sure?"

Those words never felt so empty.

"What d-do you mean? O-of course I—I'm sure." My stutter got in the way of many things, but never before has it caused someone to completely disregard a word I'd said.

"Sir, there's no record showing you or your wife ever entered the building, let alone acquired a room. Honestly, are you positive this was the right hotel?" I felt my mind race in circles as the clerk was simply laughing off any notion of my memory being true.

My wife and I have been off and on for a while. Played with the idea of divorce. And damn near got close to it. That was until she one day mentioned a trip. An abroad vacation to wherever our hearts desired. I desired Japan; she desired Ireland. After toying with where to go, I ultimately decided I'd follow her lead for a change. To be honest, I figured it was an insistent "my way or the highway" mentality that led us into our spousal indifference.

So we came to Ireland. Explore every number of places she wanted, and I admit now that I had the most fun I'd ever had. All that fun... All with her. After a few days' worth of doing as many bucket listesque things as we wanted, we decided to book this very hotel. Our room number was 310. I know it was. And no, dammit, I'm not crazy.

I remember vividly staying in the room with my wife. We even slept in the same bed, something we hadn't done in so long. I felt happy. Only for that very happiness to be torn away when I woke up the next morning and she was gone.

I went down to this very clerk I've been speaking to. This piece of fucking shit told me he'd call someone to help me find my wife. Who arrived was some guy from the local police.

"Heard you lost your gal, lad." He said to me with a stern but thoughtful smirk.

"Y-y-yes, sir. I-I woke up a-and she was just g-g..."

"Gone, sir?"

"E-exactly."

He tipped his hat down in a strange way. Almost as if he were silently sending me his condolences. But for what?

"Couldn't have gone far, son. Ride with me. We'll see if we can find her."

At first I was hesitant. Why go anywhere? In my mind she likely just got lost in the hotel and could be wandering the halls. Hell, maybe she sleepwalked. She's done it before, so maybe she's done it now.

"Nothing on the cameras, sir." The clerk said when I mentioned it.

"N-nothing?" Not a single camera caught where my wife went. She wasn't in the room, and now they're telling me she was never spotted on any camera.

"No point in getting worked up, laddy," the policeman said.

"Come, like I said, maybe we'll get lucky and find her not far from here."

God, why did I agree to go with him? GODDAMNIT WHY DID I GO WITH HIM.

We must've driven around for hours before he finally said he was needed back at the station.

"Gonna need to call yourself a cab."

"What, n-no, why? J-just drive m-me back!" He didn't even look at me. Just unlocked the doors and silently urged me out. Which I begrudgingly did.

Taking that cab ride back to the hotel was just as awful. I couldn't remember anything. Hasn't helped that I've slept maybe three hours at most. Road signs looked different. The ways this cab turned and the directions he went, none of it looked familiar to me. Even turning down the same street as the hotel looked totally different. Maybe I am at the wrong hotel.

The clerk just called for me. He apparently called the police on me. I'm a madman now. Can you fucking believe that? 

I know, right. I KNOW! This was the hotel. Not a single one of them can convince me otherwise.

The clerk just asked me another of a thousand empty questions.

"Do you have a description of your wife? A picture maybe?" I did. I most certainly did. And... that would prove this was the hotel because we took several pictures in here.

I...

The clerk just asked me if something was wrong. I guess he's finally acknowledged me typing this out as frantically as I am. I guess he's right to call me a madman.

But no, that's... that's not it... I... our photos. My wife's and my photos.

"Sir, is something the matter?" The clerk asked again.

I've...

"Missing something?" he just asked again.

Sorry... It seems I was wrong. This, this is the wrong hotel, after all. I've never been here in my life. I've never...

Again, I apologize for such a rampant whatever this is. I've been mistaken.

The clerk has asked me to tell you all that business is always open. And that if you ever need a place to disappear, then this would be the place for you.

Honestly, that doesn't sound too bad, actually. What could be wrong with disappearing?

"The police are here for you." The clerk just told me. 

"Now get the fuck out... Madman..."
