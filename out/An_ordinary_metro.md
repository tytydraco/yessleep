I felt the gunman too. I didn't want to, but I did.  
As I walked the length of the empty compartment, I felt my feet upon the floor and understood that the floor was also me. As I breathed, I felt myself enter my own lungs — not air, but something older than air. As I blinked, I became the dust floating in the light, the light itself, the window it passed through, the city beyond the window, the people in that city carrying their own metros inside them.  
The beast. Elijah. The burning hand. The slow meal eaten to make it feel like enough.  
All of it — me. All of it — here. All of it — always.  
The metro kept moving. It always had. It always would. Not toward a destination but because movement was its nature, and its nature was mine, and mine was everything that had ever boarded and departed and forgotten they were never separate to begin with.  
I was not the observer anymore.  
I was the observed.  
I was the journey and the track and the dark tunnel between stations and the light that appears when the tunnel ends.  
I was Station Zero.
