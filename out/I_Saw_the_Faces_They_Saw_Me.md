I had my first dream in what I call the wasteland two months ago. I saw myself standing in an overcast desert, the sky above me was grey but cloudless, like a large grey blanket had been thrown over the land and had yet to settle. The earth below my bare feet was dry and cracked, as far as I could see around me not one hint of vegetation or life interrupted the parched soil. The air around me hung heavy, as if anticipating rain, the still air clung to me in anticipation, the humidity giving nothing to the dehydrated earth below me. As long as I stood there though, the rain would never come.  
I stood there for a great deal of time before I finally woke up. The dream itself was not all that memorable, I likely would have forgotten all about it had it not been for the fact that I had the same dream for two weeks straight. Every night, I stood there in the wasteland, and was keenly aware that I had been there before, that this was a dream. It all felt so real, the soil poking into the soles of my feet, the heavy air around me never yielding the promised rain, my naked body taking all of it in as if I were in the waking world. By night three I had started walking forward, to see if I made any progress and found something new. For many nights, I made none. Everytime I fell asleep I would find myself in that same wasteland, seemingly the same spot. Of course, I may have been making progress, it would have been impossible to tell without any landmarks besides the ground under my feet.   
The dreams continued like this for a while, until one night when I found myself in the wasteland again. I was sitting down after a long period of walking had worn me out. It had felt like hours, even though I knew in the waking world only minutes had likely passed. Sitting on the soil, I began to look around absent mindedly, when I saw something new to my left, far off in the distance. I had never seen any landmarks here before, and simply took in what I was seeing. It seemed to be a mountain, even from the distance I was at I could tell it was far taller than anything I had ever seen before. It rose high above the ground and up into the sky, eventually disappearing into the featureless grey above. It was as if it was piercing through a solid sheet of storm clouds. I could not make out any features of the mountain from here other than the height of the thing, but wasted no time in standing up and starting toward it. I made some progress when I woke up, and found that as I went about my day I could not get the image of the mountain out of my head. It had been so oddly shaped, the form of the thing giving it an almost artificial look, like something you might see drawn in a child’s art book. It was too perfect, a pyramid rising to the sky.  
I remember this day as well because it was the first time my friends had started to ask about me. I hadn't realized, but I was looking haggard, exhausted, as if I had not been sleeping a wink. I told them I was fine, that I just wasn’t sleeping great, but they only seemed to concede for my sake, as if not wanting to pressure me into talking about it further. One friend came to me though, a man named Jamie. He looked at me and asked if I had been having any strange dreams lately. I went silent, something felt wrong about telling him. It was as if the wasteland was my own personal world, it felt wrong to let someone else into it. He told me he had been having strange dreams himself, ones that left him feeling worn out and unrested come morning. I didn’t say anything until he mentioned being in a desert, that’s when I knew that for whatever reason, he was having the same dreams as I was. We talked about them for a bit, just how strange it was that we were both having them, how real they felt. I left out the fact that I had seen the mountain. I felt it best for him to get there on his own, if our dreams were going to continue to follow the same path.   
A few nights after I first saw the mountain in the distance, I had made good progress. I was much closer to the mountain now, and could see more of the details of the thing. It couldn’t be more than a couple night’s journey away now. The rock face of the thing seemed eerily smooth, with few rough patches or grooves visible, and still no vegetation of any kind seemed to be present. It was when I blinked that I would see the second change in the wasteland. I opened my eyes and a short distance in front of me emerged a hut, appearing without sound or sign of disturbing the dusty earth it sat on. It was as if it had been there the whole time. I stopped in my tracks, taking in this new change to the environment.   
The shack was undoubtedly old, the weather worn boards it was made of were full of cracks and holes, resulting in walls I thought surely could not hold up the structure they formed. The roof of the thing lay flat over the top, essentially making this hut a wooden box with a door. I came up to the hut and peered into it. The muddled light from outside streamed through the holes in the walls to illuminate the structure enough for me to see. The floors were much the same as the walls, except smoother, as if years of walking inhabitants had worn them down, dipping and sagging in various places. I stepped into the building, and took it in for a minute or two, looking around in fascination. I wondered who could have lived there, was there more life in this wasteland than I had previously thought? I turned back toward the door when that last question was answered for me.   
A raven stood silent in the doorway, impossibly large. It must have stood at about three feet, with wings that could have carried away a small child. The feathers on it were pitch black, almost purple in the hazy light of this place. It had its head bowed down, peak pointed solidly at the floor, as if it was debating a particularly interesting grain of wood in the floor, all focus on that. I took a moment before I spoke, asking it where it came from. I had never spoken in the wasteland before, and it seemed as if the weight of the air served to muddle my voice, hiding a tremble of fear that was surely there and resulting in my producing no more than a whisper.   
I don’t know what I was expecting, but somehow I was not surprised when the raven opened its beak and a voice came tumbling out. “You were not supposed to be here yet” the raven said in a coarse, unnerving voice that the air did little to dampen. I didn’t say anything to this for a moment or two. There was something in the voice of the raven that filled me with a deep sadness, as if the voice was that of someone who had lost all hope, had no spark of life left to them. “When was I supposed to be here?” I asked the bird in the doorway. “Not yet, Not before, but after” the bird replied. “After what? Should I have stayed out of the hut?” I asked, suddenly afraid that coming in here had been a horrible mistake. The bird shook its head, never taking its beady black eyes off of the floor. “Not till after all things, not till long after they woke up. Not till long after the One that Watches began and ceased the viewing”. I was perplexed, I wanted to ask so many questions but I didn’t know where to start. “Where should I go now that I’m here? Should I go to the mountain?”.”The mountain is the end, but every end needs a beginning” the bird replied. I didn’t know what the raven meant by this, so I asked where the beginning was. “You will find the beginning soon enough, that is the only way the end may take place”. With that the bird turned itself in the doorway, keeping its head down the whole time as it did. The raven stepped outside, and spread its massive wings to take flight. As the raven took off I took after it, running out of the hut and watching the raven soar away into the sky, veering towards the mountain and into the grey sky above.  
I woke up now, a cold sweat covering my body. I got up from my bed and closed the curtains that were letting in sunlight. The dark of my room was soothing to me, allowing me to think clearly. I didn’t know why my conversation with the raven had unnerved me so much. I had gotten little information from the thing, and honestly that might have been for the better. I had no idea what I would have asked, and knew that every question would just lead to more questions, more confusion.   
I decided to call Jamie, to see how he was doing. He told me he was doing alright, but I could tell from his voice he wasn’t sleeping well. Was that how I sounded to others? Did I sound worse because I had been having them longer? He told me he could see a mountain in the distance now, I knew it wouldn’t be long till he reached the hut. I didn’t let on that I knew anything more than he did. Maybe I should have, but I doubt that would have changed anything. Even if he knew, he was still going to go to sleep eventually, He would make his own way there, ask his own questions of the raven. He would face his own dilemma when the time came.   
I had nowhere to go that day, so I took the day to rest at home, spending a good deal of time in the shower. I couldn’t stop thinking about the raven, what it had meant by the beginning and the One that Watches. The day went by painfully slowly, I had tried impatiently to fall asleep again but to no avail. It would be well after midnight when I finally fell asleep once again.  
I was outside the hut, staring up at the mountain. Something was different now though, there was a breeze blowing gently from behind me, carrying with it a smell. It was like that of a beach at low tide, pungent, earthy. I turned toward the breeze and could see a glimmer in the distance behind me from where I had come. I considered my options, looking back and forth between the mountain I had been walking towards and the new occurrence behind me. The words of the raven echoed in my head, how the mountain was the end. I don’t know how, but I knew whatever lay behind me was the beginning. I started walking away from the hut and the mountain, walking towards the source of the breeze.   
I walked for a great deal of time, seemingly far longer than I had walked before. It felt like days, and all though I grew weary I never grew hungry or thirsty. The smell of the air gradually grew stronger, and I could make out better what I was walking toward. It seemed to be a sea shore, and as I came to it I discovered that to be true. The waves lapping gently at the craggy land were pitch black, soaking into the dry earth I had been walking for so long. The dark waters were not what held me attention though, it was what I saw some distance out into the water. Standing perfectly still, I saw figures hunched over. There were seven of them, all standing separate from each other by a short distance. I could make out a few details of them, their wiry bodies matching the water in their perfect pitch blackness, their arms long and dangling hung far lower than they should have, drooped so low that the gentle motion of the waters lapped at their fingers while they themselves stood far over it. I could not have possibly guessed accurately from this distance, but they must have been at least nine feet tall. Their heads hung low like that of the ravens, but the shape of these heads were bizarre. They were flat, as if they were the faces of sunflowers drooping at night time. I could not make out any details of their faces from here while they were looking down. They almost seemed asleep, but I was still filled with a sense of immense fear while looking at them, the most primal form of fight or flight was overwhelming me and I did not know what to do. Should I run? Should I call out?  
That decision ended up being made for me. Slowly, one of the creatures lifted their head and looked directly at me. Their flat, oblong face was almost entirely featureless except for two massive eyes. They were both slightly off center, as if drifting up and down across the surface of their flat face.Their eyes were stark white, almond shaped with two pinprick black pupils pointing directly at me. We locked eyes for a moment, horror filling me as their gaze pierced my own. Then the creature lifted its dangling arm and pointed at me, which was when a mouth appeared on the surface of its face, lopsided and asymmetrical, full of jagged, imperfect fangs, and it screamed. The scream was painfully loud, high pitched enough to cut through the heavy air and reach me perfectly on the shore. In an instant, the other figures in the water snapped their necks up and looked directly at me, making eye contact with me just as the first one had. I didn’t wait around to see what they did, I turned around and began sprinting away, adrenaline filling every muscle of my body as I willed myself to flee. I did not need to turn around to know they were pursuing me, It was not long after I fled that I heard the splashing of water behind me, them giving chase.   
I kept running, daring not to scream lest I waste precious air in my lungs that could otherwise be used to get away. I could hear them behind me, large feet stomping into the ground and growing closer with every stride. I willed myself to wake up, but to no avail. I kept running until one of them had come close enough to me to be within reaching distance. It grabbed my shoulder and pulled me back hard, slamming me into the dusty ground below. This is when I started screaming, thrashing around as it pinned me to the ground and kneeled over me. It was soon joined by the others, each grabbing onto me and pushing me into the dirt. They were all staring at me, their contorted flat faces so much more horrible up close. They all had mouths visible now, hanging open as if panting, revealing the rows upon rows of uneven teeth. So many teeth, far too many for any one creature to ever have. Far more than made any sense. Their wide eyes all stared at me, into my soul, their tiny pupils made that much smaller by the indescribable whiteness of their eyes, as if made of the finest porcelain. I screamed and tried to fight back, but could notl. In unison they opened their mouths wide and leaned close to me, all emitting that piercing cry I had heard over the water. The sound was beyond defining, I could feel my eardrums being ripped apart by their noise.   
Finally, I woke up from the dream turned nightmare. I woke up screaming, thrashing around on my bed. I cried for some time after that. When I pulled myself together I took a long shower and got ready for the day ahead. It was when I left the house that I realized it had not just been a nightmare. I was standing alone at the subway station, and I could not shake the feeling of being watched. I looked around frantically, desperate to find who or what was watching me. The feeling made the hairs stand up on the back of my neck, and then I saw it. There was a figure in the shadows on the other side of the track. It was tall and lanky, and then its eyes opened, almost glowing in the dark shadow it was hiding in. Those almond shaped eyes, those pinprick pupils, they were here, and they were still watching me. I bolted from the subway station just as the train arrived, creating a moving wall of metal between the two of us.  
I sprinted all the way home, terrified that the others had followed it. I got to my house and ran inside, bolting the door and shoving a nearby dresser against it. I have boxed myself into my room, curtains drawn and all of my furniture against my doors. I managed to lose my phone when running from the subway station, so I have been emailing everyone I know trying to find help. I emailed Jamie, but I am afraid I will never hear a response from him. I can feel them watching me, giant haunting eyes observing me. I am certain at any moment they will be at my door, outside my window, and eventually in my room, on top of me, screaming. I don’t know if this will do any good, honestly. It might not be today, or tomorrow, but I am certain they will find me. The faces saw me, and now they will not stop. I do not know what sort of evil I have stumbled upon, but I do know that it watches me through those haunting, empty eyes.
