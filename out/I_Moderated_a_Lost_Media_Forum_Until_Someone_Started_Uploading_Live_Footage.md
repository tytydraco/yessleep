I used to mod a small subreddit dedicated to lost media—old TV broadcasts, obscure commercials, stuff that vanished before the internet could archive it. Nothing weird. Just nerds chasing nostalgia.

At least, that’s what it was supposed to be.

About 8 months ago, a new user popped up. No karma, no history. Username was just a string of numbers. They made a post titled:

“Found footage that shouldn’t exist. Need help identifying origin.”

The video link wasn’t YouTube or anything normal. It was some onion link. I shouldn’t have clicked it, but curiosity gets you.

At first, it looked like a low-quality camcorder recording. A dim room. Yellowish light. A guy sitting in a chair, hands zip-tied behind his back. His head was down, like he was asleep.

There was no sound. Just this faint buzzing.

I assumed it was fake—like an ARG or some edgy horror project. We get those sometimes. I was about to remove it for violating rules, but then I noticed something that made my stomach drop.

The timestamp.

It wasn’t embedded into the video—it was live. Seconds ticking in real time.

And then the guy in the chair lifted his head.

His eyes were swollen, like he’d been crying or worse. He looked straight into the camera and mouthed something. No audio, but I swear I could read it:

“Help me.”

I closed the tab immediately.

I told myself it was staged. Had to be. I removed the post and banned the account.

But that wasn’t the end.

⸻

The next day, the same user messaged me directly.

No greeting. Just another link.

And a sentence:

“You shouldn’t have deleted that.”

I didn’t click it right away. I sat there for like 20 minutes, debating. Eventually, curiosity won again. I wish it hadn’t.

This time, the stream opened faster. Same room. Same guy.

But he wasn’t alone anymore.

There was someone else in the background. Tall. Standing just outside the light. You couldn’t make out a face—just a silhouette. But they were moving slightly, like pacing.

The man in the chair was shaking now. His head darting around like he could hear something I couldn’t.

Then the figure stepped forward just enough for the light to catch… something.

A mask.

Not a Halloween mask. It looked handmade. Pale, stretched, almost like skin. The eyes were too wide, too empty.

The figure leaned down toward the man and whispered something.

And this time… there was audio.

Static at first. Then a voice. Distorted, but clear enough:

“They’re watching you now.”

The stream cut instantly.

⸻

I didn’t sleep that night.

The next morning, I checked the subreddit.

There were three new posts.

All from different accounts. All with zero history.

All linking to similar streams.

Different rooms. Different people.

Same mask.

Same voice.

⸻

That’s when I realized this wasn’t targeted at me. Or at least, not just me.

Anyone who clicked… was being pulled into it.

I tried reporting it. Admins removed the posts, but new ones kept appearing faster than we could delete them.

Then users started commenting.

At first, it was the usual:

“This is fake, right?”

“ARG?”

“Creepy but cool.”

But after a few days, the tone changed.

People started recognizing things.

A tattoo. A piece of furniture. A voice.

One comment stuck with me:

“That’s my cousin.”

No follow-up. Account deleted an hour later.

⸻

I finally decided to stop engaging completely. I stepped down as a mod and left the subreddit.

For a while, things went quiet.

No messages. No links.

I almost convinced myself it was over.

⸻

Then, two nights ago, I got an email.

Not through Reddit. My personal email.

No subject. Just a single line in the body:

“You’re still watching, right?”

And a link.

I stared at it for a long time.

I shouldn’t have clicked it.

But I did.

⸻

It loaded instantly this time.

No buffering. No delay.

Same room.

Same yellow light.

Same chair.

Empty.

I felt this wave of relief at first. Like maybe it was over.

Then I noticed the camera angle.

It was different.

Higher. Wider.

Like it was mounted on a wall.

Facing the chair.

And then… slowly… the camera started to pan.

Not digitally. Physically.

Turning.

Scanning the room.

Until it landed on a dark corner.

And just as my eyes adjusted to the shadows…

I saw it.

The mask.

Already there.

Already watching.

And then the voice came through, clearer than ever before:

“We’ve been looking for someone new to sit.”

The stream cut.

⸻

I haven’t told anyone this part.

But since that night… my webcam light turns on sometimes.

Just for a second.

No apps open. No reason.

And yesterday, I found something in my browser history.

A link I don’t remember clicking.

An onion link.

Same format as before.

Same pattern.

I haven’t opened it.

I don’t plan to.

But it keeps refreshing itself.

Every few minutes.

Like it’s waiting.

⸻

If you ever see something like this—some “lost footage,” some weird live stream—

Don’t click it.

It’s not content.

It’s not a game.

And I don’t think they’re recording anymore.

I think they’re recruiting.
