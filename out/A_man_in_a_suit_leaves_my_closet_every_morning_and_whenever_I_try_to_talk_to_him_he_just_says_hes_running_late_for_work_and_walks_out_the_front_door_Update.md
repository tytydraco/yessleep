I wasn’t able to get the post up this morning because trying to get the man in the suit on video as he was leaving my closet and my house took up more time than I anticipated. That said, I’ve been writing this post on a handful of scattered post-it notes that I’ve stuck to the edge of my work computer so I look like I’m being productive whenever my boss walks by. And right now, I’m on my lunch break typing everything out so I can get it posted for you all, so I apologize if this is a bit scattered. 

I’ve got some good news and bad news. 

The good news is that I was able to reach out to one of my friends, I’ll call him Jamie and he said he could stay over Sunday night since he requested this upcoming Monday off like two months ago so he could play in a regional disc golf tournament that afternoon. We’re going to check if he can see the man too when the man leaves my hallway closet on Monday morning. So, I’ll get back to you all about that sometime Monday. Thankfully, Jamie’s just as interested—if not possibly a little more—in anything supernatural as I am, so it wasn’t a crazy awkward thing to ask. 

Our first conversation with one another was actually about The Left Right Game, which I’m sure a ton of you probably are familiar with. I was standing at the copier with one of my earbuds in and I had kinda zoned out listening to part in the story when Bristol and Rob had first come across the quiet city when Jamie jabbed me in the side with his elbow and asked me why I was just staring at the copier, which made me just about jump out of my skin. I apologized and he asked me what I was listening too because it had to be good for me to have zoned out as hard as I had. I didn’t expect him to know what I was talking about when I told him but he immediately  pulled out his phone to show me that he’d just finished listening to The Left Right Game the other day, right when the new podcast episode had just come out.

The bad news is that when I was looking through the photos and videos I was able to get of the man in the suit this morning as he left my closet to see if I could try to post them for you all, I couldn’t find him. The photos are all still here in my photo library, but he’s just not in them anymore. He was right after I took the photos because I double checked as I was walking out my door to get to work, but he’s just not there anymore. The weirdest part is that there’s not even any misaligned pixels or anything that would suggest some sort of ex post facto editing. Obviously, I know that editing can be done without leaving those kinds of traces, but it just seems really odd to me. I’m pretty sure that even if I did post photos of the man right after I took them in the morning, I have a feeling that he wouldn’t be in them by the time most people see the post. So, trying to get hard, lasting, photographic evidence looks like a dead-end as of right now, but I’ll let you know if I come up with any possible solutions for that. And of course, as always, if any of you have ideas about things I could try that might fix the issue of the man in the suit disappearing out of any photos I take of him, please let me know.

Okay, there’s just one last thing I want to include before my break ends in like five minutes. It could totally be nothing, but in case it turns out to be something, I want to get it down here, so I can keep track of it. I could swear the man’s tie was looser today. Not by much, but I’m like ninety percent sure I could see his top button—the tiny one that’s supposed to be hidden behind the tie. I’ll let you all know if I notice any other changes on Monday morning when hopefully both Jamie and I see the man in a suit leaving my hallway closet to get to work.

Once again, I want to thank you all for your suggestions! I really do appreciate all of your guys’ help!
