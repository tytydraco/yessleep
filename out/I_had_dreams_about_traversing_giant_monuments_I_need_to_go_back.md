Hello people of Reddit! I have started to write down my dreams and I have decided to share them openly because I have to know if someone else is experiencing them. I have always had vivid dreams, but these dreams about those monuments are something entirely different. I have never before experienced anything like them, and they have touched me in ways nothing else ever has. But then they just stopped, and now I don’t know what to do. I hope any of you can make sense of what I have experienced.



Entry 1

The sky hangs dark and brown over me, full of geometric cloud formations. The sun has been hanging in its zenith for what must have been days by now, illuminating the world in its soft glow. I am lying on the ground after having walked for days trying to escape the marble platform I have found myself on. I try to breathe evenly as I lie there. I must find the ending of this plain soon, surely. And if not I must find at least something else. But even after days of walking the dark horizon still doesn’t show any signs of anything and the unmoving sun's light will not show me anything new either. Not in the sky or on the ground. Even the strange, geometric clouds hang there unmoving, mercilessly refusing to change the scenery even a little bit. It is then, staring at the sky in its wholeness that I realize that this is no sky at all. The brown clouds are geometrically carved stone. The static sun is actually just a giant oculus. I am inside of a dome.



Enry 2

My feet hurt. My hands hurt. My back hurts. My legs and arms hurt. But I have been scaling this wall for God knows how long, so that is expected. I just wish there was some protection against the cold, harsh wind that is threatening to peel my skin off if this continues. The wall seems to be made out of stone bricks, which have developed deep ridges and wide cracks. I can’t see the ground. It is so far below that I don’t see anything but a blue hue where the ground should be. Basic science says that the sky is blue because of how sunlight scatters in the atmosphere. Is that what’s happening here? Am I so high above everything that I just see the scattered light in the atmosphere instead of the ground? Is that even possible on Earth? I stop the thought that I’m not even home anymore before I just let go and let myself drop. My upper arm flails and makes impact with some sort of platform. I pull myself onto it, finally relieved that the climb is over. I lie on the ground for a long time before I stand up to observe my surroundings. On the left side of me is the blue, open sky. To my right is a plain of stone bricks leading to a darkness on the horizon. The plain in front of me leads to a wall that stretches impossibly high. I think I might be on a small window.



Entry 3

The water is still as I swim. I am glad that the water is still. The body of water is gigantic, and I am glad not to see how big its waves could be on a bad day. I wish I had a boat. Being wet and swimming for such a long time is tiring. Surprisingly, I don’t need to fear drowning. The water is very salty and very buoyant; I was even able to sleep relaxing on the water's surface. Still, I am longing for land. The water is deep and dark, and I swear I see something big move in the periphery of total nautical darkness. I can see the wall on the horizon. Or is it a wall? I can actually see it cut off at the very top of it. This might be a step of a stair descending down to this … well? I think this is a well. I wonder what I will do once I actually reach the "shore".



Entry 4

The wind is howling past me at dangerous speeds as I cling to the ruined marble floor for dear life. My hands are bleeding and my arms are burning. When I strain my eyes against the wind I can see a titanic hand reaching across the horizon, its open palm turned upwards. When I turn one way all I see is open sky. Turning the other way I see a giant marble wall I can’t see past. I think I am on the small piece of nail not attached to the finger. Before this realization can sink in I lose my grip and start flying past the floor. I try frantically to re-establish any grip on the ground but to no avail. I get dragged across the floor for a very long time until finally, I lose the ground entirely. The wind is so strong I don’t even fall; it carries me. First I just start to make out the finger. Then the hand. Then the arm. Then the entire statue. And the statue gets smaller and further away until I lose sight of it completely. Then I realize something. The Monuments might have been Titanic, yes, but ultimately even they are dwarfed by and made nothing in the vast spaces they exist in. It scares me so much that I want to weep, yet that fear of that all-dwarfing vastness gives me such absolution that I can’t help but feel fulfilled as the wind takes me.



Did you feel what I felt? Do you know what these dreams mean? Do you know how to go back? I need to be afraid again in the way I was in my last dream. I just need to be there again. I need to go back to the space that dwarfs even giants. Or I need to bring that space to me.
