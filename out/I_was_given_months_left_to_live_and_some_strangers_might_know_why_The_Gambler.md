I found out I'm dying today. 

I'm not a good man, and I'll be the first to tell you that myself. People still have hope for me, for some reason. I've given up on all of that a while ago. Life has a way of breaking you down in subtle ways. Everything's great on the surface for me, honestly. Loving mother, serious girlfriend, decent job. They just don't know what's underneath. What goes on behind closed doors, where I'm really at when I tell my girl I've got overtime or business meetings. Only type of business happening after 9 p.m. is sex, drugs, and gambling. Fortunately, I'm addicted to all three. Hard to say when it started, and even harder is to answer 'why?', but there's nothing I can say there. I was bored. Always was, always will be. The thrill, the adrenaline, the rush - whether it's a whore wrapped around me in a cheap motel, a needle entering my veins in an alley behind a bar, or betting my girlfriend's share of the rent on red - I was going to chase it. It's the only feeling that makes life worth living. 

What they know won't hurt them anyways, right? I wonder sometimes, if she notices the hickeys on my neck, or the smell of cheap perfume on my shirts. I wonder if she ever reads through the credit card statements or the angry letters from the landlord asking for late rent. I wonder if my parents know how many times I've been in and out of rehab. Man, it's just so easy to lie. Hell is where I'm headed, I know that already. 

I've started seeing him more lately. I'm not sure who or what he is, but it's hard to say. This guy is legitimately one of my formative memories, my mom says the first time I saw him I was so excited running into the house talking about how I just saw Abraham Lincoln. Nah, he didn't look anything like Abraham Lincoln, but I mistook his bowler hat for a top hat, as I think many six year olds would. Other than that, he was largely unremarkable - bowler hat, goatee, and an unremarkable dress shirt and pants, all of which I thought made him Good Ol' Abe Lincoln. I still remember that day, I'd gotten in trouble at school for roughing up a boy that was bullying my friend. 

Since then, he's shown up here and there. It took at least a half dozen times and until I was about 18 years old to turn around and say wait a minute, but even then, it was hard to believe. All he did was stand and watch, and no, nothing crazy like him being emotionless or smiling like a freak with 'too many teeth' or anything, he was just... there. Sometimes he looked happy, other times he was pensive at best. Hard to say, really, I never wanted to talk to him. It did creep me out. It's even hard to say if he's really aged, he may have been 30 when I first saw him and I guess he could pass for being in his 40's now, but who knows.

Now, I wish he was the only weird person in my life that stands across the street and stares at me, but there's another one, and she's slightly more concerning. This disheveled woman showed up at our house one day when I was twelve - that was an awful day, dad had been drinking again and mom tried to stop him from smoking a cigarette in the house in front of me. I tried telling her it was okay but my mom ended up answering the door with a black eye, just for this lady to walk right in like she had any right to be there, gunning right for me. Dad was out cold from drinking, and I started to scream but mom grabbed that lady and dragged her out of the house by her hair. I didn't know what they talked about, but mom walked right in, went to her room, and slammed the door shut. I cried myself to sleep that night, thinking she was mad at me, and no matter how many times I asked her about it, she didn't even acknowledge that I was there.

I've seen that crazy lady here and there as well since then, but she unsettles me. I can tell she's scared to talk to me - maybe mom did threaten her back then - but she watches, from across the street, or elsewhere. She looks at me like she could eat me alive, it's just pure intent and it's suffocating, sometimes I've felt her there before I've actually seen her, and it's hard to get me startled with all the shit I've been into.

Speaking of which, that takes me to today. I've been sick lately. Well, I've been sick for a while. Annie caught me coughing up blood and I just damn near rolled down my sleeve with the needle palmed in my other hand in time before she opened the door. She booked the doctor's appointment, and drove me there. It's hard to look her in the eye sometimes, but I think today for the first time I noticed how beautiful she really is. She seemed so genuinely concerned for my health, and the night before I was balls deep in my usual hooker, down over $1200 for the night. I just looked out the window, letting the city blow by me. 

Over the next few weeks, they ran a few tests, nothing crazy. When the doctor called me in for results, Annie came along, but he asked me if I'd prefer to talk alone. I asked her to wait in the lobby, but she seemed shocked that I'd ask her to leave, and said she'd just wait in the car.While she stayed home struggling to sleep every night, I was drowning myself even deeper in my vices. I stopped bothering to hide the tracks. I wonder if she knows what I'm up to, but is too scared to leave me. That's the kind of thought I got lost in, and I didn't hear what Dr. Marvin said, as he repeated himself after clearing his throat.

"I'm what?"

Dying, he repeated. From what, cancer? AIDS? By some insane miracle of God, my STD test last month came back clean, and I don't sleep with anyone new without a condom, but could it be latent? No, it wasn't an STD. It wasn't cancer either, at least not anything he could detect. Just widespread, acute organ failure. Little by little, my body was eroding. My heart was tightening up, lungs shriveling up, stomach acid leaking, liver enzymes out of wack... That sort of thing. He said it's the type of thing he'd see in Stage 4 cancer patients except I didn't exhibit a single trace of cancer in any of my tests. He said he's never seen anything like this in someone as young as me. 

I walked out of that room without a thought in my head or a treatment plan prescribed. I was given two months at the current rate of acceleration. In a few moments, I had to tell Annie this, and break her heart more than I already have. Life could be so much easier for her if she just left me, and I thought about finally setting her free, but before I reached the pavement from the staircase, that old lady was there again, right in front of me. I nearly fell back, being shocked that she'd gotten so close again after all of these years. I looked around, and people were watching, staring at the old lady, so at least I knew she was real. She just looked down at me, and she was crying. I crawled back up the stairs before getting up to my feet and now I was concerned. Was this an old woman with dementia who thought she knew me? 

I asked her what her name was, what she wanted from me, why she was here, but she just kept sobbing, saying "sorry" over and over again. I asked what she was sorry for.

"He bet your soul before you were born."

What? I thought, and then I asked. What? She just said it again, and it confused me even more this time. She handed me a book, well, more like a stack of looseleaf paper tied together with strings. I tried to hand it back, but she told me to read it, and walked away still crying, and I was left with a stack of papers in my hand. I got in the car with Annie and she asked if those were my discharge papers, and who that lady was who I was talking to. 

I told Annie I'm cheating on her. About the drugs. And the gambling. I told her how many women I've slept with since we've been dating. I told her I'm addicted to heroin. I told her I've lost over $20,000 in the last year from our savings.

And she just told me that she knew. Annie thanked me for finally telling her, then asked me to get out of her car, and told me to find somewhere else to stay.

Karma catches up with you quick. I got away with my sins for too long, and I was finally starting to pay for them. It made me happy though. Annie’s heart was broken, but her soul was freed. On the way home, some loan sharks I owed money to caught up to me. They asked me for the interest I had to pay them and I just laughed in their faces. I kept laughing, even as they kicked me until I couldn’t breathe without a crushing pain in my right ribcage. Tears streaming down my face, covered in my own blood, but for the first time in forever I was genuinely happy. 

Well, that brings me here. The roof of my office building, twenty floors high overlooking the Columbus skyline. I think it's been a good life. I had everything I could ask for and threw it all away for cheap fun. Crazy thing is, I don't even regret it. I enjoyed it. I loved fucking hookers, I loved snorting cocaine in the bathroom of an underground gambling den while my life savings were on the line. I felt alive and I won't feel bad about that. Like I said, I already knew I was going to Hell. This was also how it was meant to end for me. Finding out I'm dying? I wasn't sad, I wasn't scared. It was time for me to right my final wrong - and end this meaningful existence on my own terms.

I felt like I was ready to jump and I looked down.

There was no way.

Abraham Lincoln was standing on the street looking up at me, and I felt like six year old me seeing him again for the first time. This time it pissed me off. How dare he pull me out of my perfect moment? Suddenly the memories rushed in. Every time he was there, in good or bad. I remembered mom, I remembered Annie, I remembered my friends. I remembered my vices, I remembered my depression, I remembered that I was going to die. The tears came streaming down my face as I ran down the stairs, determined to catch him. I was finally about to feel the sweetest release of them all, but he ruined it. I was going to find out who this bowl hat wearing son of a bitch was but as I made it to the ground floor and limped my way out of the office building, he was still there, across the street. 

I got up in his face and he barely flinched, he just squinted his eyes and chuckled. We were under the moonlight, and in the dark, he seemed even less ominous, even more normal. He just seemed curious, as if he wanted to see what I’d say next.

I was lost for words. I wanted to ask who he was, what he wanted, why he was always here, but nothing came out. He just shook his head and handed me a stack of papers - the same ones that crazy old bat gave me. I knew I threw these out on the way home, was this guy following me? Do he and the lady know each other? I was lost in a thought loop once again and that’s when I snapped out of my trance. He’d finally spoken. I expected to hear some demonic voice but he just sounded like any other guy from Chicago did. 

“You really ought to read that.”

That was it? Twenty years of being stalked and I need to read 20 pages of scribbles on loose leaf to get any answers? I marched after the guy and grabbed him by the shoulder but suddenly a sharp pain in my ribs was the reaction to my sudden arm movement, and I fell to the floor crying out in pain. 

Man, I really was dying. Bowler hat guy walked away, and all that was left before me were these papers. I held the papers from the batshit lady in my hands, 

What was my life? Was there actually more meaning to it than I had thought, for these two strangers to be so involved in it? What did she mean when the lady told me that my soul was wagered? By who? Could these papers really hold the answer? Does my mysterious illness have anything to do with all of this?

I could’ve been dead right now, but after a life of escapism - the drugs and the hookers, gambling thousands of dollars at once to feel even an ounce of excitement - was that it? Annie would’ve blamed herself. My mom would’ve found out from the police showing up at her door. My entire life would’ve amounted to nothing more than a detached hedonistic sprint to the end of it, but no. Not like this. I don’t know what it was. A will to live, maybe? A subconscious desire to find answers? Or maybe this was another thrill - another bet, another hit, one more way to feel the rush one last time.

I read the papers the lady gave me. I was shocked. I still can hardly believe a word of what I’d read. But that’s not what has me scared tonight.

“I’m pregnant, and I’m keeping it.”

I thought Annie left something at my house and wanted to come get it. I didn’t realize she’d be giving me something that I haven’t felt in years.

At that moment, I look outside the window and see him, clear as day under the lamp post. That bowler hat. That look of curiosity. And the slightest smile on his face. 

I head outside. I know what he is now. Just like me, just like the man who conceived me… He’s a gambler.

And I’m going to make him tell me everything I want to know.
