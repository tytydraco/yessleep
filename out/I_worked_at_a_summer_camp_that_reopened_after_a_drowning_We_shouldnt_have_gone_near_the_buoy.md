I don’t know if I’m allowed to post this, but I don’t work there anymore and I don’t think they can stop me.

Last summer I took a job as a counselor at a small private camp in upstate New York. It had been closed for years. According to the owner, it was just “insurance issues.” According to literally everyone else in town, it was because of a drowning.

The story was that a kid swam out to the old buoy one night and never came back.

No body.

The lake isn’t huge. That’s what made it worse. You can see from one end to the other. There’s a dock with a security gate now. They said that was for “liability reasons.”

On our first night there, before the campers arrived, four of us hiked up to a lookout point above the lake. From up there, it looks perfect. Still. Black. Quiet in a way that feels… padded. Like the sound doesn’t travel right.

When we came back down, we saw something hanging from a tree near the trail.

At first I thought it was a scarecrow.

It was a couple of old lake buoys tied together with rope. Someone had put a kid’s shirt and jeans on them. There was a noose around the “neck.”

The face was painted on in something dark and flaking.

One of the guys laughed and said, “Guess we found the ghost.”

No one else did.

That night, two of the counselors went down to the dock. I didn’t go. I was tired and honestly a little freaked out by the thing in the tree.

I was in my cabin when I heard shouting from the lake.

Not screaming. Not at first. Just raised voices.

Then one of them yelled something like, “Okay, okay, very funny.”

Then silence.

I remember thinking how strange it was that there were no insect sounds. No frogs. Nothing.

About thirty seconds later I heard splashing.

This time it was panic.

I ran outside barefoot. The dock lights were on, but no one was there. The lake looked completely calm. No ripples.

I called their names.

Nothing answered.

I was about to run back to get help when I noticed something on the dock.

Wet footprints.

They started near the edge and led back toward the gate.

Bare footprints.

But both of them had been wearing shoes.

I told myself I was misremembering. I told myself I was in shock. I told myself maybe one of them fell in and climbed out and I just didn’t see it.

The next morning, one of the beds was empty.

They searched the lake for two days.

No body.

The owner kept saying this was “exactly what happened last time.”

She said it like it was a scheduling inconvenience.

They closed the camp again.

Here’s the part I haven’t told anyone.

On the second night, after the search crews left, I stood alone on the dock.

I wanted to prove to myself it was just an accident.

I walked to the very end and looked down into the water.

It was so still I could see my reflection perfectly.

And underneath it—

Not clearly. Not fully.

But just below my reflection, there was another face.

Not moving.

Just watching.

Then I heard it.

My name.

Not from behind me.

From under the water.

I left that night. Packed my car and drove out before sunrise.

They say the property is reopening again next year.

If you ever get a job offer at a place called Whisper Lake, don’t take it.

And if you see a buoy out on the water—

Don’t swim toward it.
