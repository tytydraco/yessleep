This happened when I was around 7-8 years old, so some small details might be fuzzy, but the main events are burned into my memory.

My family of four i.e. me, my older sister, and my parents, had rented the top portion of a house on the outskirts of a busy city. I vaguely remember the house number being 674. The area itself was quiet, almost too quiet compared to the chaos of the city nearby.

The owners lived downstairs: a man, his wife, their daughter, and their dog (I’ll call him Kevin). The daughter was kind and mature, but the father… not so much. He was a heavy drinker. Almost every night, he’d come home drunk and fight with his wife. Sometimes it got physical. The daughter usually had to step in and calm him down.

Doctors told him to quit drinking because it was killing his health. He didn't listen.

Eventually, the drinking killed him.

After his death, the family moved away. For some reason, we were allowed to stay in the house as long as we paid rent and took care of Kevin because they couldn’t take him with them. We still lived in upstairs and didn't budge to shift downstairs as the space was more than enough for us.

The very next night after he died, we heard knocking on the exit door.

It wasn’t regular knocking. It sounded just like how the old man used to pound on the door when he came home drunk: irregular, heavy, half-conscious thuds.

We brushed it off, assuming Kevin must’ve been jumping at the door or messing around.

But it didn’t stop.

The knocking continued. And every night, it got louder.

From here on, I'll split the story into four pieces, so bear with me, please.

***1. The Night My Sister and I Were Alone:***

One day my parents had to go out for work, leaving just me and my sister at home. I was in the living room playing with toy cars while she was studying in her room.

Then the knocking began again.

But this time, it wasn’t knocking.

It was banging.

Loud and aggressive and pounding on the exit door.

My sister rushed out of her room, grabbed me immediately, and pulled me into her bedroom. She locked the door, shut the windows, and told me to stay quiet.

We had a wind chime hanging in her room.

Even though the windows were closed, it suddenly started spinning and clinking like strong wind was blowing through the room.

Then the banging stopped.

We heard a door downstairs slam open.

And then footsteps.

Slow, deliberate footsteps walking inside the house, room by room, as if searching for something... or someone.

I remember both of us sitting frozen, barely breathing, listening as the steps came closer… until they stopped right outside her door.

Then the banging started again. Harder than before.

We were both crying at this point.

My sister texted our parents to come home quickly.

And then we heard a voice outside the door.

A man’s voice.

Mumbling over and over:

**“Open the door.”**

Not shouting. Not whispering. Just repeating it in this dull, flat tone.

We didn’t move.

As soon as we heard our parents’ car pull into the garage, everything stopped instantly.

No footsteps.

No voice.

No banging.

Just complete silence.

My parents thought it might have been an attempted break-in.

I didn’t think so.

Something about it felt… wrong.

***2. What Happened Downstairs:***

A few days later, my dad and I had to go out for some work. My mom and sister stayed home.

For reasons I still don’t understand, they decided to sleep in the downstairs portion of the house that night, i.e., the same place where the old man had lived and died.

They chose the daughter’s old room because it was spacious and comfortable. They locked the doors and windows and went to sleep.

That night, it started again.

But this time, the voice wasn’t saying “open the door.”

It just kept repeating:

**“Get out of my house.”**

Over.  
And over.  
And over again.

He banged on the door constantly.

My mom eventually started praying out loud, and after some time… it stopped. Completely.

Neither of them slept much that night.

***3. The Final Incident - The Terrace Encounter:***

We started looking for a new place immediately after that.

The night before we moved out, my sister and I decided to take Kevin up to the terrace to get some fresh air.

Out of nowhere, the temperature seemed to drop. The air just felt… colder.

Kevin suddenly started barking like crazy at one corner of the wall, not even playfully, but the kind where a dog is genuinely alarmed, like when a burglar tries to enter n the middle of the night.

Then, right in front of us, a glass bottle lying near the wall lifted by itself and flew across the terrace, smashing against the opposite side.

The problem was... it was late at night, and there was no one else except my sister and me.

***4. After We Left:***

We made sure that Kevin was handed back to the owner’s wife because we didn’t feel safe leaving him alone in that house.

We moved out soon after.

And from that day onwards, we never had anything like that happen to us again.

Even to this day, I still wonder what would have happened if my parents had come home a little later that day… or if we had stayed in that house any longer.

I don’t know what we experienced in that house.

But whatever it was… it really didn’t want us in that house.
