My wife Quinn had been telling me that she had been experiencing a recurring dream for the past 2 weeks. The first week, I honestly brushed it off. I still thought it was a bit strange, but nothing more. Her running theory for what caused this dream was honestly a pretty crappy string of events. She had found 2 spiders on her person before she made it into the office. She’s no slouch when it comes to stuff, but everyone freaks out a bit when they get caught off guard like that.

Week two came, and she stopped mentioning the dreams as much. However, the bed was wet, not like that, but like from sweat. I’m told I sleep warm, but this wasn’t from my side. She mentioned that she was still having the dream, but the dream wasn’t one continuous story like how it was the week prior. It was like the dream skipped around, like a scratch on a CD. After she told me that, I grabbed a notepad and wrote everything I remembered about her dream and what she had just told me. The dream doesn’t scare me, but I’m becoming increasingly worried about her sweating at night now.

I haven't shared with you her dream. I’ll just type what I remember for now so I don’t have to search for the paper. Her dream begins with her running through a poorly lit neighborhood street at night into an even darker house. She is unsure who the owner of the home is or if it’s even a real place. She just knows it’s unlocked. She dashes in and closes the door in hopes she wasn’t seen. A giant huntsman spider is chasing her. She runs upstairs and hides in one of the back rooms with attic access. Hearing the spider bang on the front door, then break through it, it begins searching the house for her. She cracks the door she is hiding behind to peek through to the hallway. A large shadow can be seen cast from the bottom of the stairs, accompanied by small thumps, almost like the creature knew it was getting warmer. Seeing this, she doesn’t close the door but backs into the room, hoping to hide in the darkness. As the creature approaches the door, she holds her breath, nearly passing out from the length of time. Once the shadows disappear from the creature, she lets out a small but quiet, exasperated breath, thinking she’s escaped. And when she does, the creature breaks down the door, and she wakes up.

The reason I’m writing this is partly for sanity, but also because I’m scared. Two nights ago, I was struggling to sleep and thought I would give in and catch up on the novel I would read before bed. I really justified it so I could watch and take care of Quinn as she slept. Around 2 a.m. is when it happened. I was reading and heard a faint noise from the hallway, not enough to get up and check, but enough to sit and listen. Everything was still; my home was quiet except for the low hum from the AC. I looked up from my book to the bedroom door and saw four dark, jagged arachnid legs. The creature slowly and silently opened my normally noisy door with its body until I locked eyes with it.

And what I saw is what I see every time I close my eyes now.

It was a poor imitation of myself; it was like someone had taken the flesh from my face and poorly draped it over the spider’s. My eyes were poorly hollowed, like something had ripped them out, leaving bloody rips. The creature looked at me through them. We sat there for what felt like hours, but it was probably seconds. The spider began to back away, and as soon as it was hidden behind the door, I woke up gasping for air. I was sweating like crazy, and my wife was worried. I was so confused. When had I fallen asleep? Was it real? Why did it have my face? Why is it happening to me and not her? That last one had been keeping me up.

I think it’s important to let you know that I did mean it when I said this happened two nights ago; I just left out that I have not slept since then. I spoke to her about it, and I guess I deserve this, but she’s doing what I did to her the first week. I’m just so scared to fall asleep. I think her nightmare is coming for me. Demanding to be seen.
