This story took place 4–5 years ago. Let me preface this by saying I am not a good writer and have barely a high school education.

This is by far the craziest story that has ever happened to me.

One night, I snuck out of my house to smoke weed. I was 14 at the time and had just started smoking. Afterward, I came back inside and went to my room. I was lying in bed watching football highlights with over-ear headphones on when I suddenly heard my door creak open behind me.

I looked over and saw a guy with a mustache so long that it extended past both sides of his face. He started mumbling and asking me for directions. I didn't really process what was happening at first, but then I started freaking out. I began yelling and telling him I was going to kill him. I chased him past my kitchen and grabbed a knife. I probably could have caught up to him, but I didn't want to.

To this day, I do not know how he got into my house because my front door was right next to my bedroom at the time. I can provide a photo of the room and front door layout.

By then, my parents had woken up, and I started crying. About five minutes later, we saw him talking to my neighbor and asking where the nearest train station was. This happened in the middle of the night, so I was surprised my neighbor was even outside.

I wanted to call the police, but my parents didn't want to. I didn't get a good night's sleep for probably a long time after that.

Fast forward a year later. I had started high school in person after COVID. Something my best friend and I used to do back then was get drunk and mess with homeless people. I know it was messed up.

We live in San Francisco, California, so there are a lot of homeless people, and after COVID there was a huge increase. One day, we ate at a restaurant, bought some liquor, and were heading home. We were waiting for the train at Powell Street BART when my friend dared me to throw a milkshake at a homeless guy who was standing there with a bike.

I did it.

We both ran behind a divider in the station. We were laughing hard while the guy was freaking out. Some random person started scolding us for what we had done, and then another guy came over.

At first, this guy looked familiar. Then I realized who it was.

He had the same mustache, the same build, and even the same shoes.

I was shocked and just stood there frozen.

The guy who had broken into my house saw how scared I was and really started ramping things up. He kept saying that he knew how to fight. After a while, instead of talking normally, he started making strange noises and was repeatedly moving his arms up and down.

Eventually, we just walked away.

To this day, it is by far the creepiest thing that has ever happened to me.
