Dear Internet

I think there is a man in my room and I don't know what to do. 

So it's, My husband (Danny) our two boys, Jacob who is 4 and Jason who is 9, 5 year age difference makes things difficult at times. Our two cats, Sysco and Nova. 

Sysco was rescued from my old job where she had chosen to hide underneath a Sysco truck during drop off. We got nova literally two days before from a nice family whose cat had kittens. I originally firmly said no animals but my boy's, husband included, begged me for basically anything pet wise. Cats are assholes but usually self reliant so that was a no brainer to me. I didn't want to add litter box clean up to my list of running chores but in the end I did what I always do and gave in. 

We live in a two bedroom, single and a half wide trailer (don't ask, I don't know either). It is cramped. The kids share a room covered in toys top to bottom. It's never clean, by the time the last thing is put away the rest has been destroyed by one of the two lovable terrorists I spend hours negotiating with until Dad speaks and then it's Sir yes sir! 

Same goes for the rest of the house though. It gets clean but never stays that way. Laundry piled up, someone eats six different meals and leaves the dishes, boxes magically appear full of discarded items nobody ever needs again until that one special time and then of course it's not there. 

Pillow forts, shopping mall, paw patrol race tracks, kitchen emulator, the works of kids and imagination makes the house a home. Even when the owner (me) becomes agitated by these things and just wants a clean peaceful place some days. 

Enough about that. Now to the topic at hand.

My husband and I each have a desktop PC for gaming. He games more than me but I still like to on rare occasions. He set our desks up together putting himself against the wall in the living room and my desk pokes out slightly past the kitchen door frame. 

Due to this placement I can turn around and see through the entire kitchen, down the tiny hallway that leads to the bathroom and our bedroom. I can also see my husband's side of the bedroom when the door is open. 

I have never had an issue with this. Comes in handy for catching sneaky children. Just a quick look back over my shoulder or a slow silent turn around and stare for a moment to see why it's suddenly silent. 

This is where my problem comes in. On a few occasions now I have glanced that way and been startled to see a man sitting on my bedroom floor on my husband's side. The lights are usually off to save our power bill as much as possible so of course my brain is like, man-shaped object in the dark, creepy. 

I literally ignored it and chalked it up to overactive imagination. Later, I checked my bedroom to find the tall laundry basket sitting around the same area. So I moved it because I don't need to have the creeps about doing laundry, that would be disastrous. 

Then it happened again, this time it was the shorter basket of laundry which made no real sense because the man was tall even while sitting. Plus the clothes were colorful and he was a bland, brown shirt kinda boring. Whatever though, imagination I suppose. 

It's just glimpses mostly. I've locked on a time or two but then it becomes blurry and by the time I've walked back to check there's nothing in sight. It's even happened with the lights on, though his area was a bit darker for whatever reason. 

I mean I've had other weird things happen so I'm not jumping to say it's something paranormal or whatever. 

Other stuff being:

\-Knocks on my door, softly like one of the kids

\-Saw my door move on its own, just slightly

\-My ceiling fan turns into a saw blade every now and then, spinning out of control until the strings fly up into it then calms back down

\-Stuff moved

\-Stuff missing

\-Things randomly falling.

Also my cats are pretty decent little rascals. They stay out of most things and knock less stuff over on purpose than other cats I've seen. They are lazy, lazy, lazy. They sleep 95% of the time, beg for food for 3%, ask for pets for 2% and then the overtime 2% is the willful asshole time. 

Some stuff can easily be blamed on the cats. Something fell in the kitchen? Saw Nova there earlier. Toy missing from the track? Saw Sysco staring it down earlier. Box was by the back door and is now beside the fridge, look in it, “if I fits I sits” is a real life motto.

Some stuff can't be explained like my glasses going from my bedside to the TV stand in the living room. Or the paint can I was using going from the kitchen to the bedroom while only I am home. That one was definitely freaky. 

I don't know. Maybe I'm crazy. But I swear I keep seeing him and it's creeping me out. I loved sitting in my bedroom for a moment of peace and quiet but now I'm just paranoid. 

What if a man lives in my walls? What if it is some benevolent ghost out to get me? Worst case scenario I'm going nuts. If I'm not and it is a ghost how the hell do I prove that WITHOUT seeming like a crazy person first?

I told my husband and he laughed it off the way I did, overactive imagination. Maybe it is, but the more it happens and the more I think about it the more creeped out I get. 

I don't know what to do. Ugh help!
