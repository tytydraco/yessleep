It’s always had that name.  

It doesn’t leave anything up to the imagination, but at least you know what you’re in for.

For generations this plot of land, this field across from a small suburb that houses farm animals, pylons, woodland animals and small bodies of water, has always been referred to as “The Ghosties.”

The oldest story I heard was that a World War 2 plane crash-landed there, and bits of the wreck were found by kids, so they decided it was haunted.  

Another story was that a bus crashed into the nearby lake, the driver went insane and drove a bus full of people into the water, drowning everyone.

I think everyone’s heard different stories about the place, and some are true, or at least bits of each story are true.  

There have been plane parts found in there – does that mean a plane crashed there specifically?  

There is a skeleton of an old bus at the bottom of the lake you can see in the summer when some of the water dries up – does that mean a bus driver went crazy and killed himself and all the passengers?

I personally believe people find these things and make up stories round them.  

One thing no one has talked about enough though is the random car carcass in the middle of the woods.  

Graffitied and burnt to a crisp by kids bored out there while drinking and camping.  

The thing is a death trap, with glass shards poking out and springs from the seats reaching out, rusted for decades.

The old stories were always just that to me: old stories.  

None of them solidly explained why it had the name The Ghosties.  

Until I found out.

The field at the front entrance to The Ghosties is a frequented dog-walking spot.  

It’s not made to be a dog field, but enough teens on dirt bikes have carved a loop into it and it’s coincidentally perfect to take your dog through.

Anyway, I was out with my dog Phred on a cold winter night.  

It was around 4 or 5 p.m., just on the cusp of becoming night, the sun setting.  

Since it was cold enough to freeze the dirt, and quiet at that time, I took Phred over to The Ghosties for one last run around before our dinner.

Me and Phred were no strangers to The Ghosties.  

We had our small route, and Phred knew he could run around and do whatever he wanted as long as I still had a view of him.

This night, though, our routine was knocked.  

A power cut in the houses around us suddenly blanketed The Ghosties in darkness.  

Even though it was still technically light outside, it was right on the edge of sunset, and I didn’t realise how much light all the houses provided until it was gone.

The power cut happens, and suddenly the hum of the pylons stops.  

That’s when I realised you’re never truly in silence in The Ghosties.  

The hum of the pylons and the tiny vibrations of the cables meant you were always listening to some noise, but you must get used to it real quick, because I’d never noticed it until now.  

Dead silence, in the middle of a field.

Once all the lights in the houses went out, I called Phred back so we could go home.  

The silence suddenly becomes incredibly loud.

I shout Phred, and I hear him running, but I cannot see him.  

I hear him running towards me, then suddenly stop, then run the other way.  

I hear his paws go through the grass and the noise he makes as he thumps over the frozen dirt.

He’s heading towards the woods.

I take out my phone, bring up the torch, and start to run after him.  

I can still hear him running, but it’s as if he’s purposely leading me toward something.  

I hear him sprinting towards me, then back away from me, back and forth, deeper and deeper into the woods.

I realise we must be near the old quarry, which now just fills with water throughout the year (the one with the supposed bus body at the bottom of it), as I hear splashes from something nearby.  

I know it’s not Phred, because he’s still running circles, but he’s running around the quarry.

He leads me away from it and, at this point, the sun has well and truly set.  

All the trees cast long, branched shadows that are getting darker and darker, and I’m essentially running blind into a dark forest, surrounded by trees.

No matter who you are and what you believe in, being in the woods in pitch black, with nothing but old stories to fuel you, is terrifying.  

All the old stories start to haunt me there and then, all at once.

Once the fear settles in, I start to shout for Phred more frantically, try and coax him with the bag of treats I brought, but I hear nothing now.  

I stand still and try to listen, since I’m in nothing but silence.

No paws running anymore.  

No panting breath.  

I start to hear my own breathing and begin to panic.  

Why did I come here so close to it being dark?  

Even with the lights from the nearby houses, it’s still fucking dark.

At this point I hear a hum.  

Similar to what the pylons would sound like, but it’s coming from further in front of me.  

A hum and a buzz, then silence.  

A hum, a buzz, then silence.

I see a slight glow pushing through the trees, and I immediately think: Phred has somehow come across people who have a light.  

Or maybe people are camping and have a weird lantern.  

Or kids fucking about while drinking up here.  

Whatever it is, it’s my only hope to see anything in this place.

I get closer and closer, and I realise the light and noise are coming from the old 70s car that’s been abandoned here.  

The one that’s wrecked, burnt, destroyed, windows smashed out.  

But as I get close, it’s as if it has blacked-out windows.  

It’s still the beat-up, no-wheels, rusted wreck it’s always been, but it looks like someone has completely painted the windows black.  

The light is coming from inside the car.  

I can see it spilling out between the gaps in the doors and from the cracks in the body.

Every time the light pushes through, I see Phred standing next to it, mesmerised.  

“Thank God,” I think when I finally see him.  

I walk towards him to put him back on the lead and find our way out of here for good.

Every time the light stops, I’m swallowed in darkness. I can’t see two feet in front of me without light so anytime the light goes out, I stop. 

The light buzzes back on, and I realise there seems to be more trees appearing around us.

The light buzzes off.  

Then back on.

Those aren’t trees.

I start to run towards Phred, regardless if it’s light or not, but the light starts flickering, and I realise the trees are moving closer and closer. 

As I’m getting closer, I start to smell that smell of rain you get just before a storm. The smell of rain and the taste of copper inside my mouth and it only gets worse and worse as I get closer. 

I get to Phred, quickly clip his lead onto his collar—  

Then the pylons ignite back on, and the car door suddenly swings open.

I look down to pull Phred along and realise he’s gone. 

I look up and I find out why it’s called The Ghosties.
