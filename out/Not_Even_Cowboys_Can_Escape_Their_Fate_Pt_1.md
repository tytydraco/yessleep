Gunshots continue to fire at us from the sheriff and his team. Boone is ducked down shooting back with no fear. “How are we gonna get outta here, Jed?!” Boone yelled over to me. “I don’t know, its gonna have to be a miracle!” We continue to fire as we try to look for any exit to get out of the situation we were in. I look into the reflection of the window in front of me as I’m sitting behind an old wagon. They surrounded us. The sheriff in his blue jeans, a shirt that used to be white but now brown from all the dirt and commotion, and a path that is not blocked by the other law men. I crawl over to Boone and tell him, “Theres a spot just to the left of us that no one is covering, “I say we take as much of the money as we can and just run! Go tell Eli, I’ll wait for y’all here!”

 Boone gets up and runs over to the crate in the streets Eli was hiding behind. I see them talking and how Eli’s face has nothing, but fear painted on it. Eli’s brown hair covered in sweat, with his blue eyes brighter than ever against the blood of the bankers on his face. You wouldn’t even know he had freckles. Boone starts yelling at Eli, but I can’t make out what they’re saying over the gunfire. Boone runs back over to me and says, “Eli wants to take a shot on the sheriff!” I yell at Eli and do everything I can to have him understand I’m telling him “NO”. His fear grows as I gesture for him to come to us. He runs as fast as he can and almost gets hit by a flying bullet. “Why not I have the shot!?”, he yells. “Because we would have even more heat on us and you’re just a kid, you don’t need to be killin’ no one else today! Leave that to me and Boone.” I say before a shot goes through the wagon we’re up against. “We need to go, Jed!”, Boone yells to me as he starts grabbing as much money as he can hold. He takes off for the opening. I grabbed Eli’s hand, and we ran with whatever we could pick up.

As I’m following right behind Boone, he trips over his dusty black boots and slams to the ground. “Boone! Get up and go!”, I yell as we keep running. We’re almost in the clear, but then I heard a louder gunshot go off. I turn to see Boone still lying on the ground, covering his head with his blood-stained hands. He’s not hit. He’s in shock as he looks up to me and Eli. Another shot from the same gun goes off and goes right between me and Eli. A man with a repeater sits at the top of the saloon in front of the bank. We weren’t hit either. It’s quiet, too quiet. The sheriff starts to yell at us from the front of the bank.
