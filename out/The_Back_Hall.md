I'm nineteen, and I've been living in my current house for about seventeen years with my mom, and my dad up until their divorce when I was sixteen.

I loved that house, but there were three places that made me uneasy, and always had. The gutted bathroom and the guest room on the second floor, and the attic.

There's not really any reason for the unease, lingering around those areas just made me super anxious.

I guess part of it was how when I was little, I'd see a big dog that looked like it was surrounded by swirling smoke in those places. I thought that if I didn't do things in very specific ways, then the dog would come out and eat me, or hurt my family.

We later learned that I have OCD, and that kind of explains it, but even after I got medicated I was still always really anxious about being in those places.

My aunt actually has a video of me hysterically crying when I was like, maybe four, because when she was helping me put on my socks, she did the left one first, and that meant the dog would take my legs.

She thinks it was funny.

I think it was kind of a dick move for her to record a toddler having a meltdown.

Even when I was a fully grown adult, I still sometimes worried about the dog. I was a lot less anxious, and I could usually go in and even linger without too much discomfort, but it still was t pleasant.

Every once in a while, our cat would get into the attic, or into the guest room. The guest room was off the hallway I don't like.

Obviously, I was always the one who had to go find her, despite freaking out a bit whenever I had to go retrieve her.

This one time, I remember I couldn't find the cat, so I checked the gutted bathroom. She loved hiding in the spots I hate. I heard her scratching, so I was expecting to find her quickly.

I was, sadly, wrong.

Sort of, anyway. The scratching was coming from the closet, but when I opened it, the cat wasn't in there. I squeezed inside the small space. It was only like a foot wide, maybe a little less, and almost four feet deep.

Sawdust smell. The whole bathroom did, honestly, and a little moldy.

Only mildly deterred, I shoved myself into the small space.

"Mooey?" I called out, wriggling further into the closet. "Where the hell are you, stupid cat?"

With only about a minute of slow movement, I made it to the back of the closet.

No cat.

I could still hear her scratching, though, and it was closer than when I was in the bathroom.

Maybe there was a hole in the wall or something, and she managed to get through it and was stuck?

I kicked the wall lightly, looking for the hole. Nothing happened except the wall shifting backward slightly. Shoddy work. That was pretty par for the course, though. My dad was always leaving projects shittily half finished. Believe it or not, he started the bathroom when we first moved into that place, and he never finished it in the almost twenty years we lived there.

I sighed, and started wiggling back out of the closet. I was starting to get claustrophobic. Once I was out of the bathroom, I exhaled loudly. That was unpleasant.

My head twitched slightly to the side. I hated when that happened. Whenever it did, I felt like a bolt of lightning shot down my spine and made my head jerk over to the side. It was so weird, and I always felt uncomfortable when it happened.

I shook it off, rolling my shoulders and walking out into the hallway. I jumped when I heard something fall in the room the cat must've been scratching in. The guest room.

"You'd better not have broken anything," I called. "I swear to god, cat." I continued vaguely grumbling as I opened the door to the room.

Something by the closet slammed, and the cat zoomed past me, out the room. I jumped and let out a very childish shriek.

"Fucking hell!" I shouted, slamming the door shut. I grabbed the cat, holding her like a baby. "You're an asshole, you know that? Scared the shit outta me, Jesus fuck…"

Mooey just blinked at me and yowled.

 

A couple days later, I was looking for some yarn. My cousin was having a kid soon, and my mom and I wanted to make some clothes for it. My mom is a pretty great seamstress, but I'm much better at crochet.

She made me store my yarn in the attic, because if I didn't, the dog would get into it and it would end up all over the house.

So, I had to go into the attic.

Before I continue, I feel like I should give a rundown of what this area of the house looks like, just to keep things from getting confusing.

On the second floor of the house, there are six rooms off of the hallway. In my mind it's separated into the front and back hallway. On the front half, there was the sewing room, my mom's room, and my room.

The orientation of those rooms aren't very important to the story.

In the back hall, there was the gutted bathroom, the guest room, and the attic door. When you're coming down the hall from the front of the house, the bathroom is directly ahead, with the guest room door on the left, and the attic door on the right. There was also another staircase next to the attic door, it was really dilapidated and gross looking, especially compared to the almost ornate front stairs.

I'm hoping this will be enough information for all of the locations to make sense, but if it's not just let me know and I can clarify further.

The attic door had one of those weird metal latch things, hook and eye, I think. The doorknob was also only a knob from the outside, so if you accidentally shut yourself in up there, you were stuck until someone came to get you. Unless you had a screwdriver or something to shove in the doorknob hole.

I remember I was up there in the summer once, I think I was around six, where we had all the windows open, and the wind must have blown the door shut. I was stuck up there for almost an hour.

There were only two windows up there, and the singular light bulb was barely functional. I cried the whole time.

I don't think I can overstate how much I hated that area of the house, especially as a kid.

*Especially* alone.

I was a lot better about it the older I got, like I said, I was able to handle it much better at that point. No more tears, but the unease remained.

As I walked up the stairs, I told myself that the feeling of being watched was just residual from when I was little and thought a monster lived here.

*Monsters aren't real,* I reminded myself, feeling my head twitch to the side again. I took a deep breath and began searching through containers for the yarn bin. *This is all just your brain being a dick.*

I almost fell over when I felt something brush against my legs.

Once again, it was my fucking cat.

"Are you just trying to scare me?" I asked her. She ignored me completely. She meandered aimlessly around the attic, undeterred by my fear and the darkness. I wished I could have been that calm. I followed her for a moment. She seemed weirdly comfortable up here for the door always being locked and no way for her to get in.

I shrugged it off and went back to searching through the boxes.

I jumped once again at the sound of creaking from directly below me. I was starting to feel the panicky feeling I did as a kid whenever I was forced to be here.

*Just find the yarn*, I told myself. *Nothing out of the ordinary is going on, this house is old, it's gonna creak.*

I felt another twitch coming, which was not helping with my feeling of panic. I began searching faster, and finally, *finally*, I found the yarn bin.

I practically sprinted downstairs, shaking all over. I was breathing heavily, too.

*I'm a grown ass adult*, I thought to myself, eyes feeling teary. *I should not be getting worked up about monsters in the attic!*

I leaned back against the wall and took several deep breaths.

In, hold, out, hold, repeat.

On the third round of this, Mooey came down the stairs, still seeming perfectly calm. She trotted over to me, and sat down, facing the wall of the guest room and just… kind of staring at it.

"You're not helping," I told her. Her eyes then tracked up the wall, over the ceiling, and then to the attic door.

That set me off again.

I lunged forward and shut and latched the door. I think I was crying at this point.

I was scared, I should probably have gone downstairs to take an Ativan and lay down for a bit, but instead, I continued to watch my cat, almost frozen with fear.

It was illogical, irrational, but for some insane reason, I thought she could see the spirit I no longer could.

I was sending myself into near psychosis at that point, and was vaguely aware of that, but wasn't really able to snap back to reality until my mom started yelling at me from the living room.

"Jack, this is taking much longer than it should!" she called. I could hear her voice getting closer. "You okay?"

I cleared my throat, but it broke anyway when I called back to her. "Yeah! I'm fine, just… uh, you know. This hallway."

She nodded sympathetically when she reached me. "You wanna hug?"

I nodded, wiping my face and slumping into her arms. "Thanks."

"Of course," she smiled. "Honestly, sometimes I feel uncomfortable here too. I think all your stories got in my head." She laughed, and I did too, but what she said wasn't all that reassuring.

Something in the guest room creaked, and I shrieked.

"Sorry," I told my mom, trying to take deep breaths. "I'm just on edge. I'm sorry. Can we please just get out of the damn hallway now?"
