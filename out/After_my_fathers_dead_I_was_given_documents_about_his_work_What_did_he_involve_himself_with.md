I received the news two weeks ago, I was at the local market buying groceries when my phone ringed and the shaky voice of my mother telling me my dad was no longer with us. It's hard for me to recall even now, I dropped everything at that moment and rushed back home, couldn't even begin to cry because my brain was still processing the words "father" and "dead" together.

But I saw this coming. In truth he was chronically sick all his life, even since he started that government job that I remember him having since I was little. No one ever explained to me what exactly his condition was and always kept it vague, it frustrated me but I didn't to act insensitive towards my dad and his health, so I kept my mouth shut most of the time.

I was inconsolable, the man that was my role model and the coolest person on earth to me was no longer going to be with us. I sat at the side of his bed weeping, an ugly cry, but it wasn't comparable to how ugly everything surrounding death was. Before I knew it his body was sent to a mortuary in preparation for his funeral.

The funeral happened a week ago, and with it he left us his will. He left me with a few things, but the thing that caught my eye was the many folders of documents relating to his job. He wrote in it that he wanted only **me specifically** to have access to them and to read them. This wish of his reminded me of the last words he ever said to me.

"I know you think I'm perfect, but no one is. Know that I love you even with my flaws."

I had a bit of a habit of idolizing him, it mellowed out as I aged but I couldn't help but let myself slip sometimes. I could hardly blame my younger self, to little me it felt so cool that my old man worked at a government facility, even if he did say that what he did there wasn't something a kid like me would be interested in. It did bring me pain at the same time, his work was apparently the cause of his illness and the reason he had to be absent in my life for months at a time, due to the facility being in Alaska while my family lived in Oregon.

But his final words to me and the secrecy that obscured his job. What did my dad do there, what happened to him?

It worried me, the way everything in my life started becoming questionable, death wasn't satisfied with just taking my dad away physically it seems. It wasn't until today that I finally gathered the courage to look through the big box of folders, knowing well that the contents will definitely end up changing my opinion of him for the worst.

I won't detail everything I found in those things, so I will only summarize the ones I found most important.

Most of the documents were about a new research site being built on Nestle Peak Island in Alaska, it was built around April 4th 1986 by a "Director Ferguson". The purposes of this site was to conduct "Project Amber", what that project entailed was a mystery to me at the time. But the name was familiar, Amber, I have vague memories of my dad saying that word.

As I slowly reached the bottom of the box the documents began to become more wrinkled up and dirty, like they were pulled out from rubble or something. It also made it a bit more difficult to make out the words, the first one that caught my eye was one titled "Specimen Reports" dated January 13th 1987. From what I could skim it was about the results of studying parasites found in various animal carcasses, cats, dogs, deer. I thought my eyes were tricking me, but it also said the parasites were found in crystals inside of the carcasses.

This made me pause. **What the hell was my dad up to?** I started burrowing through the piles of documents faster, reading reports about a meteorite being found on the coast of Alaska with the dead animals being found near it. **Was this what he was referring to?** The next one that caught my attention was titled "Prototype A-Ruinous", it was by far the most unintelligible and damaged out of the documents and I could only make out certain phrases like "first successful human host", "...instinct to spread", "needs strict containment procedures". **What sins did he commit.**

Despite the fact that he claimed the box only contained documents, at the bottom of it was a VHS tape with the label "Newcomer Initiation".

My dad always kept a VHS player deep in his closet. "You never know when you need something" would say, he knew I would need it. I wasted no time setting it up, adrenaline pumping through my veins as I racked my brain on what this could mean. This isn't what I needed, not so soon after I had lost him, not so soon after his funeral, not so soon after he said those last words to me.

I popped it in, hoping it would give me answers.

"With the world engulfed in total war, we Americans need to do our best to defend our nation!" The unfamiliar voice boldly stated.

"During the first few months of battle, strange lights were spotted in the sky on the West Coast. We initially thought these to be enemy planes but their origins remained unclear. Years later, an investigation lead to the discovery of a meteorite on this big Island on the coast of Alaska, the sight contained things that we've never seen before on this planet. The carcasses of many animals were found scattered around from the impact of the meteor, crystal fragments that were recovered from the bodies contained an alien organism that could manipulate the genes of animals. And even humans."

My heart stopped, this couldn't be true, surely this is some sort of elaborate posthumous- I stopped that delusion prematurely.

"Director Ferguson ordered the construction of the Nestle Peaks site with military protection so that further research can be conducted. With the ever growing threat from the east, we believe this gift from cosmos can be used to enhance our men." 

I knew what this implied. 

"Bright minds from all over the country were brought to the site where our leading researchers have examined the effects of the alien organism on local wildlife. It's been able to replicate and distribute the genes of infected animals to a human host."

**No.**

"Unfortunately, our cross-species experiments of animal to man have been largely unsuccessful, but that's why you are here! To make a change."

**Him.**

"If we are successful, our research will create a new kind of soldier."

This is all I have for now, I need time to recuperate everything. I'll update when I feel like it.
