Every hostel has one or two ghost stories with it.
- My Roommate.

When I came to this hostel, I was extremely sceptical about how safe it would be. I would walk past the buildings and feel someone was watching me from the terrace. 

Anyways, this story is about that one day I met her, the burnt ghost.

  
I was watching a very intriguing series on my laptop one day, late at night, around midnight to 1 am in the morning. It was your daily brainrot youtube short reel drama about a time-travelled villainess who wakes up in a crucial time plot and rewrites her fate. My roommate had left for her hometown and was returning the next day, so I had the entire room to myself.

  
Suddenly, I felt an intense urge to drink water and warm myself up. It felt like the room temperature reduced by 5 degrees. I checked my water bottles and my kettle, only to find water good enough to wet my chapped lips. So, I paused the drama, got out of the bed and took the water bottles to fill with water. 

  
The water dispenser was in the dining hall, which was located at the end of the corridor, and the hallway had a light bulb that never lit properly. It fluttered so much that it hurt my eyes. The corridor felt like someone or something was roaming around, but I couldn't see a single soul. Even the housekeeping staff were sleeping in their assigned rooms. 

Somehow, unlike usual nights, that day felt restless. The energy was too restless. Anyways, I opened the door and filled my water bottle successfully. I then turned around and headed back to my room. Everything seemed fine until, strangely, I smelt burned flesh behind me. Out of curiousity, I slowly turned back and saw HER. A woman in a polka-dotted nightdress had her hands burnt and her face covered with drool, as if she had been purposefully burnt when she was asleep. Her eyes were pitch black and her lips were crooked, as if she were electrocuted. 

"Who?" I heard the wind say. The cold wind churned around me as if it were sizing me up. The wind was so chilly that I started to shiver. 

"Not you. It's not you. Where is she? Where is she?" Her burnt, skinny hands reached for my neck. I could feel she wanted to kill me.

"I don't know you; I am new here. I don't know," I cried out.

"You're new? So you don't know who did this to me. That's possible." She reasoned. "Go then; go back." 

"Thank you. Th-thank you," I said.

I turned back, and as calmly as possible, I went back to my room and locked the door. I drank some water and prayed to God, and, out of fatigue, I fell asleep with an intense fever. The next day, still weak from last night's fever, I went to the temple to pray for good luck and protection and found that my roommate had returned. I told her the entire event in detail. 

"You met her? Wow, I thought she had left this hostel a long time back. It looks like she was interested in you." She said, teasingly. 

"Who is she? Or to be precise, what is she?" I asked, anxiously. 

"She is a senior who died mysteriously in this hostel. "My roommate replied.

"What happened?" I asked back. 

"This event happened when I was in my first year. I was sitting on this very cot when I smelt something burning for the first time. Back then, I thought someone was making roast chicken, so I didn't pay attention to it. Three days later, the room you see over there, the one at the end of the corridor, yeah, that room was forcefully opened, only to find the senior's hands burnt, drool all over her face and electrocuted to death. No one found out that she had been missing for the past three days. Not even her own parents." My roommate said this while unpacking her suitcase. "Ever since then, she roams the corridor on the 14th of each month. Yesterday was the 14th; that's why you saw her." 

"But why does she roam around? Is she looking for someone?" I asked. 

"There is a rumor—just a rumor—that she was murdered, and she is looking for her." My roommate said, conspiratorially.

"How so?" I asked again.

"You see, the room was locked from outside, not inside, and her roommate, who went home just the day previous to her death, mysteriously jumped from the terrace on the 14th of the next month." My roommate said.  

My body suddenly got goosebumps, like a warning sign that something was about to happen.
