After we decided to go back to shore once it got dark, I kept thinking about Jörmangandr's Shadow, what it fully looked like, and where it even came from. It clearly wasn't a normal deep sea animal, and certainly not after it partially froze that whale. I didn't sleep particularly well that night.

Once the day came, all of us came together to see if we could get the thing higher up, so we could see its full body. We deployed the ROV to the same place as last time, to search for it again.

We looked for 3-4 hours, and couldn't find a trace of it. Unfortunate, but since it was a free roaming animal, it wasn't surprising. After that minor disappointment, we decided to pull the ROV back up.

That was, until the temperature gauge on it suddenly plummeted to around -80 degrees, and it stopped responding to controls. As the ROV sunk, the last thing the camera sent us was the serpent, glaring at the camera, before looking up and leaving.

"There it goes... that's a few thousand bucks down the drain", a researcher said. "Well, at least we found it again. But where could it be going next? It definitely looked at the camera, and then it went up..."

That's when it hit us.

We had the ROV essentially right under the boat. And if it was going up, that could only mean one thing. We might be in danger. We all realized that pretty quickly.

"Alright, listen up." the captain said. "We have an idea for what that thing's capable of. We should try to keep our distance from it. If anything, we will at least get to see what it looks like."

Right after that, there was an unnerving roar from below. The air slightly chilled, and the beast came out of the sea.

It was at least 80 feet long, with extremely dark blue armor-like skin, light blue crystalline structures lining its body, and a hammerhead shark shaped head, with two golden eyes glaring at us with clear murderous intent.

It dove right back into the sea, and the boat instantly rocked, knocking a few people off their feet and sending things overboard. The people with the best aim got harpoons, and got ready for when it would next appear.

The surface water on the starboard side of the boat suddenly froze over, and the beast rammed it, sending it flying into the air, aimed to fall back on the boat. Fortunately, the captain managed to shoot and land a harpoon shot. The ice block, taken by gravity, smashed into the ship, causing it to start sinking.

There was a lot of back and forth, and a few were killed by the serpent, but eventually a lucky harpoon shot into an already damaged part of its body caused it to roar in agony, and attempt to flee. However, I managed to grab on, and force it into clear shot of one of my crewmates, finally taking down the damn thing.

After that, we brought both it and the bodies of our fallen to the shore, and we gave them proper burials.

We gave the body of the serpent to some scientists, who would research it and figure out how it did the things it did.
