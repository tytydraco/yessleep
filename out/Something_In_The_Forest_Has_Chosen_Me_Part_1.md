Two years ago today, something reached out to me from the woods near my house. My brother and I disturbed something ancient that day and now the forest is riddled with rumours of missing persons and strange occurrences. Even entering the place is now considered taboo. I won’t bother with much preamble. It’s easier if I just get it over with. I must leave a record of what happened back then. I can’t keep hiding what I did.

My older brother, Isaac, and I relaxed in the living room alone, watching TV, when it called upon me. We lived on the edge of a large forest, and the immense trees shook in anticipation as the clock hit midnight. The rage of the wind, the thunder and the rain covered up the beginning. The house’s creaks and groans, as it fought the elements, evolved gradually into powerful vibrations as the house shook. We looked at each other in shock. Never before had the two of us witnessed a real earthquake. It simply wasn’t a worry where we lived. We knew no protocol to follow in a situation like this, so we curled up and waited for an end, covering our heads, as the building trembled. Bulbs burst, glasses shattered, furniture collapsed. The large windows of the back door, which looked out into the woodland, exploded inwards. As they did, the winds chucked objects around maliciously and the floor was drenched. Chaos reigned as we huddled together and waited for the house to give way to the elements and crush us.  
The tremors proved ephemeral because, as quickly as it came, the shakes stopped. Utter silence hung in the air. As the fear abated, I noticed that in the same instant the quake ended, the storm outside vanished. Isaac’s face mirrored mine, filled with a mixture of horror and confusion. Then he pointed at the seemingly unharmed, back door.  
“I watched the windows shatter. I heard the bulbs break. I felt the wind and rain on my skin. Why is nothing broken?” He said.  
“What?”  
I turned to survey the room, focussing my attention on the room’s condition. I was certain that I too had both heard and seen the room around me ripping itself apart. Yet nothing was an inch out of place and there was no damage. The room was even bone dry. An impossibility. I stood staring vacantly as my brain attempted to process the enigma before me, but the quake was the herald, not the main event.  
Suddenly, I was hit by a shockwave of energy. The room lit up as bright as a summer’s day, and an unholy cacophony of noise began to fill the room. It was like the tortured shrieks and screeches of damnation flooded into the house, nearly rupturing my ear drums. None of that mattered though. In fact, I only noticed the light and noise in retrospect. All I knew in that moment was the Call. Something had connected with my body, mind, and soul. It felt vast, it felt intelligent, it fed me ecstasy. I had never felt such an exquisite phenomenon and I haven’t since. Pure euphoria. The radiance occupied every part of me as something, beyond what a human mind can experience, attempted to communicate with me. It succeeded.  
The voice it resonated with cannot be heard. The words it spoke cannot be written. The images it revealed cannot be seen. I was exposed to a force beyond comprehension and yet, for an instant it was not unknowable. I still couldn’t decipher the intricacies, but I understood its significance. It was calling me to its side. I had been chosen. I had been set a task and was determined to accomplish it. It was the only thing that mattered. But I could not bring myself to move. The overwhelming satisfaction the connection granted was all encompassing. My mind sank into the endless depths of perfection until an icy cold hand gripped me by the shoulder.

I was thrown back into reality as Isaac shook me. The violent passage from complete bliss to the dullness of the real world stunned me for a few short moments. The angelic warmth was replaced by the pitiless cold of the room. The unending joy that had both coated and coveted the essence of my being was supplanted by the now unbearably uncomfortable, rough texture of clothes upon my skin. Previously normal sensations felt foreign and unreal. I sank to my knees in despair and began to inconsolably sob as grief consumed me. To know infinite distilled exhilaration and then be thrust back into a pathetic, limited body, was a relentless agony. The pleasure had suffused my soul but, in its wake, it left only a vacuum behind.  
“For fuck’s sake, Max! Calm down. Everything’s fine. It’s over.” Isaac shouted in a startled reply to my sudden outburst. I clung to him, there on the floor, for almost ten minutes as I struggled to piece the shredded remnants of my psyche back together. It took me a long while, but I eventually managed to steady myself as Isaac asked,  
“What the hell happened to you Max? You look like a corpse.”  
I lazily looked down at myself, somewhat disinterested by the uncanny falseness of my surroundings. Through the foggy haze that clouded my mind I understood what he meant. My skin was pale and wet. It hung off my body like a damp coat on a rack. It appeared that the entity had taken more from me than simply a part of my soul. More than just my sanity. I had lost significant muscle mass in what couldn’t have been more than a couple minutes. My body had deflated as if I had been drained of life. But in the end, it didn’t matter. Nothing else mattered. I retained only a miniscule thread of connection to the being that had spoken to me. It wasn’t enough. I had to fulfil my purpose. I had to answer the call. The weakness of my flesh would not prevent me from finding it.  
“Did it speak to you too?” I croaked in a dry rasp.  
“Did who speak to me? The storm went insane, but I didn’t hear any voices. Was there someone here? Did someone break in?” He looked around cautiously.  
“Something, Isaac. Not someone.”  
*I had to know what it was.*  
“What was it then?” He looked at me with concern in his eyes.  
*I had to understand.*  
“It called to me.”  
*I had to find it.*  
“What did? Are you fucking with me?”  
*I had to feel that again.*  
“The Mother…”  
*I needed Her…*
