I've always considered cats peculiar creatures. They eat, sleep, and mess with their owners as much as possible. Sometimes they'd push something over or perhaps scratch some furniture, other times they'd get scared and run around like maniacs in the middle of the night for no reason.

I have a story to tell.

It was late october and my parents were away for their annual trailer trip. I had the house to myself and as a teenager it was a blessing reocurring every year that I considered a perfect opportunity to deeply immerse myself in the online world with no parents around to nag me about chores.

Our house was located in a secluded area on a hill in the forest of an island, a few hundered meters from the sea. The house was of typical scandinavian character; the kitchen and the living room were built in the same room creating an opened plan living area. It had big windows facing in all directions so that when you stood in the living room and faced south, you could see down the hill towards the sea, to the west you'd see the gravel road and driveway and to the east you'd see our backyard, the garage and sauna. Our house was originally built as a cottage/vacation house with the only neighbouring houses scattered several hundered meters away with no one living there during late october. It didn't bother me being all alone since I'd be playing videogames all week anyway.

In preparation for the weeks activities, I had bought snacks, soda and a bunch of energy drinks -- it was going to be amazing.

I played non-stop from around 08:00 am to 11:30 pm except for short bathroom breaks and the occational re-stocking of my drink supply. As night beckoned I became weary from all the gaming so I decided to enter the kitchen for yet another energy drink. Once I wandered into the living room I was faced with pitch black darkness, except for the faint glow behind me emitting from my computer screen. I had all but forgotten how dark it would get once the sun crept under the horizon.

\-No matter, that's why we invented lightbulbs.

When I was little I always feared big windows, especially when it was dark outside. I think it was the dreading sensation of not being able to see outside, yet the outside could see inside. That fear came over me once more when I wandered into the now lit kitchen/living room. The windows were numerous and huge, and the only thing I could see was my own reflection painted on a black canvas of pure darkness. The forest had a daunting way of absorbing all light. For obvious reasons I didn't want to linger so I quickly grabbed my energy drink from the fridge and made my way back to the hallway next to my room.

That's when I noticed something odd:

Our cat, Alfred, was sitting perfectly still almost paralyzed, staring towards the windows. I laughed and asked, jokingly; "What are you doing?" waving my hand in front of his face as to get his attention. He didn't flinch. Instead he kept staring towards the same direction as before. I tried desperately to follow his gaze and see what exactly was so interesting, but all I saw was the windows reflecting my own image back at me. Suddenly Alfred jolted, followed by a terrified sprint in the opposite direction. Usually I'd brush it off as yet another episode of cats being cats, however, something felt different.

\-It felt like I was being watched.

The wind roared forcefully, tearing through the forest, followed by an almost complete silence.

Shivers went down my spine as I focused my eyes trying to penetrate my own reflection for a glimse of what lied beyond. Out of the darkness emerged white, grinning teeth with a set of eyes wide open, staring. The face abnormaly pale. It stood perfectly still, as if it had waited for this moment. Waited for me to see.

\-That's when I understood; Cats gets scared for a reason. The reason is just not apparent to us.
