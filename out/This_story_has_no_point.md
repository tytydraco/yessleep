Writing this has no point. It doesn't have a beginning, middle or end. There isn't a chain of events that lead to a climax and resolution. This is simply a documentation, though, I'm not sure of what. 

I keep seeing this face. I see it in the woods and across the road. I see it behind me when I drive at night. I see it just outside my door. It peers into the car's windows when I park, and hangs out at the very edge of my headlights. Sometimes I can see it slamming against my car with a heavy thud, fists pounding against glass it simply cannot break. I ignore this easily enough. When I exit my car, it starts to move slowly but surely towards me. It's hard to tell from where but it's pointless to wonder, because whether it came from miles away, or mere meters, the result is the same. I keep calm and simply walk to my porch. I don't run. I never, ever run, because I know the moment I do, is the moment I solidify its existence. That's the thing about it, whatever it is, it doesn't exist. I can't tell you when or how this started because it didn't, after all how could something start if it never was? I cannot tell you what its face looks like because it does not have one. I cannot describe the ear-piercing pain of its scream or the nausea that comes from a laugh that has no lungs to form it. It can not linger just behind me as I fumble to put the keys in my lock. It doesn't hang right outside my bedroom window waiting for me to pull back the curtain. It doesn't watch me and observe every detail with its huge unblinking eyes. It doesn't do anything because it doesn't exist and never has. But it wants to, I can feel it, a pure desperate desire to be solid enough to place its feet at the ground. It begs me to look at it, to run from it. It begs me to be afraid of it so that I may release it from the agony that is not being. 

It doesn't let me sleep. The taps at my window are silent yet keep me awake anyway. If I dare to open my door at night, the lack of substance and shape will begin to lumber towards me and I am helpless because if I slam the door, that's all it will need to know it is now real. 

I believe now is the time to tell you that I lied. This story may not have a beginning, but it does have an end, as horrible and painful as that end will be. The truth is, that the being that isn't, is no longer be as fictional as it once was. The never ending tapping that has not been occurring every night has begun to, on occasion, land a real tap. Not often, but just enough to tell me what I feared most. The branches from the woods and grass from my yard shift have begun to bend and shift under a newly forming weight. The floorboards at the end of the hall let out a creak. The creak is quiet and unsure of its cause but it is real. Each day the assurance of its nonexistence gets weaker and the certainty of my demise gets stronger. 

 I owe you an apology, though it may be meaningless in the end. The things I have not experienced, are also things that you have not experienced. By writing it down and giving it form, I have made it real for both myself and you. I'm sorry. I'm so deeply sorry for this. What I have done to you is selfish and cruel.  My actions won't save me, I know that. It rattles at the knob of my door, and laughs a deep sort of liquid wail that can only come from a newborn set of lungs. When you begin to see its absence in the hall, or feel the lack of presence just behind your shoulder, you will do your best not to notice, but it's too late. It was too late for me too. I can hear the door starting to splinter and crack. I am going to die. I am going to die and there is nothing I can do to stop it, so here is my final confession. The point of this story is to not die alone.
