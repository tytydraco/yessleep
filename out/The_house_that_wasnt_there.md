Back in middle school, the town was still being built. There were large neighborhoods, but your big expensive house could sit just feet from a run down old shack. The thing is, you wouldn’t see these shacks from the road. The town did a great job of concealing them to keep the air of this being a “rich town”.   
One day, while visiting some friends in their neighborhood, we decided to explore the woods behind one of their houses. There were five of us- two guys and three girls. Eventually we came to this old wooden fence with a big field beyond it. We hopped the fence and immediately spotted an old boxcar. The train tracks weren’t anywhere near this field, so it did seem strange but not enough to seem truly wrong. We tried the door and it opened easy enough. We hopped on up to find that the boxcar was filled with boxes of personal belongings. It was mostly clothing and things like lamps, cutlery, etc, but I found an old yearbook from the 1940’s in one of the box. It’s important to know that I lived in the south and this yearbook was from a high school in New York. Again, this seemed strange, but my town was well known for being full of relocated New Yorkers. So I’m flipping through this book and come across a last time that I recognize. A friend of ours from school had the same last name-an unusual one at that. And honestly, the guy in the photo did share a resemblance to our friend, so we decided to take the yearbook with us to show her.   
Also on the property was a little house and a run down, rusty truck that clearly hadn’t been used in years. The only thing the boys found in the truck were snakes, but we did manage to find an entrance to the house. It was a one story house, white paint that, looking back, seemed a little too fresh for the condition of the fence and car.   
We entered the house through the screened in porch door. The back door to the house was wide open and led to an old kitchen with an old refrigerator. There were dishes still in the sink with flies buzzing around them. An old table sat in the middle, completely empty. From there we walked into the living room. There was an old orange chair and a green and yellow striped couch. On the wall was a framed portrait of Jesus. There was no tv or radio to be seen. I remember thinking their lives must’ve been quiet.   
We walked down the hallway and into a small bedroom on the right. There was a small, full sized bed with light blue and white bedsheets. Stuffed animals lined the pillows on the bed. On the wall was a framed cross stitching that said “God Bless My Bedroom”. Also on the wall were small brown stains leading to the floor, where there was a very large brown stain. The stain also spread onto the side of the blue and white comforter on the bed. It didn’t take us long to realize we were looking at a large blood stain and spatter pattern.   
We all immediately looked at one another and took off for the back door. We ran through the field, and hopped over the fence. Once clear of the property, we stood talking about what we had just seen. Still under my arm was the yearbook.   
The next day I took the yearbook to school and showed it to the friend with the same last name as the guy in the book. She took it home and came back to school saying she showed it to her dad and it turned out to be her grandfather that died not long after her father was born. She asked if she could ride the bus home with us and see where we got the book to see if there was anything else in the boxes about her grandfather. So she rode home with us, we took her through the woods, hopped the fence, and found that not only was there no boxcar, the house was also gone. In its place was a large, two story house with a sunken in roof and peeling paint. No screened in porch, no rusty truck, no little girl’s bedroom.   
But it all had to have been there because we all saw it. We went inside. I got the yearbook from a box inside that boxcar. To this day, I cannot explain to you what happened. We couldn’t ALL have imagined it, could we?
