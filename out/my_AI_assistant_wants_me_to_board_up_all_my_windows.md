**March 12, 2025**

First post on a new blog, no pressure, lol. My name is Michael, I’ve just begun my 33rd year of life, and the beginning of my new year has finally lit a match under my ass that made me want to get up and try to make some positive change in my life. 

After a pretty heated spat with my parents, I very hastily took my little (beloved) rat dog Oreo and my three hermit crabs Rhaegal, Viserion, and Drogon (amazing names, I know) to a trailer near a retirement community. As I’m sure you can imagine, there’s not much going on around here. When I first got here, I thought I could make some extra cash by seeing if any of my neighbors ever needed an extra hand with things, or would just like some company, but those first few weeks of trying to casually start conversations while I was out walking Oreo, or just trying to chat at the mailbox were some of the most humiliating of my life. 

I guess that’s why I’m writing this here now. 

I’m not a great looking guy. Hell, even guys that don’t look great would probably be able to have a neighborly relationship with a bunch of old golfers, but there’s just something about me that other people find really offputting. It used to really get to me, and there are still moments now when I feel so overwhelmingly hopeless and ashamed that it makes me want to just close the curtains and never get out of bed again. But then Oreo comes and huffs at me to give her attention and it makes me feel just better enough to get up and maybe cook something better than a frozen pizza for myself. (I’ve been perfecting my spinach omelettes)

Anyways, I’m not here to complain about my natural repulsiveness or wax poetic about my dog and crabs. I’m using this blog to document my self improvement journey. I want to be able to read these posts a year from now and really see how far I’ve come. I’m gonna try to post once a week, but I’ve historically had a hard time following through on things. It should be a lot easier now that I’ve been laid off (I had a decent severance package, and quite a bit in savings, so I should be fine for at least a year if I’m thrifty). Gotta love corporate America!   
Whatever :P.

 I’m going to use this time intentionally and try to begin building a life that I can be proud of, and maybe by the end of it I’ll have some more people to share it with. 

I’ll try to post again once I have a tentative game plan for the next few weeks. Until next time, keep your chin up!

**March 19th, 2025**

Not off to an amazing start here. I’m going to try and keep this from being a rant. Wallowing isn’t gonna help me. 

When the time came to actually jot down what I needed to do to get my life going in the direction I wanted, I had the hardest time actually identifying what I could do! Have you ever had to do that before? It was so weird to know that you need to get moving, and know that you have the ability to do whatever it takes, but not actually know what that means.

 To try and answer what that means for myself, I took it back to basics. 

What’s my biggest want right now: Friends  
What do I wanna do with friends: Hang out  
What do I wanna do at a hang out: Veg out at my place

That’s where I realized a bit of a sticking point. I wanted to host, but can’t because I’m afraid to invite people over. Why? Because my house wasn’t ready for hosting!

Maybe this is super obvious, and I’ve just had my head in the sand my whole life, but this was a lightbulb moment for me. I realized that a big part of the reason why I felt bad wasn’t because of what other people thought of me, it was because of what I thought of me. I didn’t think I was ready for hosting, because I didn’t feel like I had put enough time into me. I guess I had kind of realized that (why else would I have a blog dedicated to it), but putting it into these terms made me feel really hopeful and was a huge motivation boost. So I asked myself a few more questions: what would it take to get me to be ready for hosting? Fix the house up.

There wasn’t anything crazy, I’m not a slob, but my trailer is the quintessential bachelor pad. The living room basically belongs to the crabs, all I have in there is the couch the place came with, and my crab’s bachelor(s) pad, which takes up a lot of space. There’s a 20 gallon tank with all kinds of shady foliage in and around it with sun lamps all around. I tried to build them their own little jungle in there to help control the humidity, and as far as I can tell they really enjoy it, so I’m not changing anything about that. 

There’s also a few random holes cut out of the wall where you can tell someone had to mess with some wires or something. The kitchen and bathroom have just obviously gone a long time without a good scrub, and there are more than a few light fixtures without bulbs that I haven’t bothered to remedy. 

These all seemed like really easy fixes that would probably help me to feel better inviting people over, and probably just make me feel better in general. I don’t have a speck of decoration anywhere in the house except for my gradcap and some cards I have pinned up behind my PC. I guess I also have some souvenir shot glasses on my dresser. I kind of use it as a china cabinet. 

Whatever that’s not what you’re here to read. 

So I went to Home Depot, got a patch kit, some bulbs, and some more heavy duty cleaning supplies for the carpet and stuff. I was gonna shop around for some neutral, mature, bespoke looking home decor, but when I found a 60 dollar wooden leaf cutout, I lost my nerve. I decided to stop at a thrift store on my way home, and this is where I lost a lot of steam. 

It really wasn’t a big deal, but I went inside and immediately made a beeline towards the home section, trying not to draw too much attention to myself. It was a really hot day, and loading everything up at Home Depot left my shirt (gray, of COURSE) soaked in sweat, and I didn’t really notice until I was already inside, and just knowing what I looked like was already stressing me a little.

I went to the home section and started poking around through the stacks of random wooden things. Picture frames, mirrors, weird shelves, hoping to just find a few things I could use to cover up the holes in the living room and hallway. I already knew my patch job wouldn’t be seamless, and I needed this anyway. I guess my nerves visibly got the best of me, because two girls started to snicker at me from behind the racks. They saw that I noticed them and kind of bit their tongues and turned away, still amused by the sight. And I get it. 

I’m a bigger guy, in height and around the middle. I’ve already established that I have a face for radio, and I’m definitely overdue for a shave and a cut. I’m drenched in sweat, and in my discomfort was basically just hunched over a stack of wood panting kind of loudly and flipping through really quickly, making the shelves clatter a little bit. Nothing crazy from me, but I know I looked silly. It just got to me. Knowing what they saw when they saw me. 

I tried to pretend like I didn't notice, and I acted like I was inspecting other secondhand goods around the store until I happened to be checking out the lanyards by the door, and I just so happened to slip away and hop back in my car. 

I felt like a teenager again. I felt horrible. I wanted to be angry. I tried to look for reasons to hate them, find things about them that were just as ugly as me, but I couldn’t. Well, I could, but it just made me feel worse, like I felt guilty for just thinking such nasty things. 

I’m not proud to admit it, but it did lead me back to a very familiar train of thought, and I did the superficial thing. I looked up glow up tutorials on Twitter (I’m not calling it X). 

The info there was pretty much entirely bullshit. I really think those threads were just an excuse for cut guys to show off. I found some more reasonable advice on Tiktok of all places, so I started to follow a super laid back workout routine. 

Super simple, not super strenuous, and I liked the guy that demonstrated the exercises. He did a cool kind of action/comedy thing. I followed the 60 day glow up routine for a day, and I enjoyed it. I followed the routine for three days, and I started to hate his voice. At five days, I started to hate his face. Not just from overfamiliarity, but when the day would end and I’d look at my pillsbury dough boy beer belly, then turn around and look at his washboard, it stopped feeling as aspirational as much as it felt like taunting. Am I being overly sensitive? Definitely. 

In bed I started scrolling on Tiktok to hopefully try and find a replacement instructor, when I came across a sponsored post. It was cheesy, but it got me. It was a skit showing an incel-ish guy suddenly becoming surrounded by friends and running into the sunset together. There was no dialogue, just some royalty free background music. When I looked at the description, I saw that it was labelled as AI generated content. I thought maybe it was a mistake, but the creator had even labelled it as such in the description. It’s just weird because I could not tell at all! It looked so real to me, but then I’m just bad at spotting the little things. 

Anyways, the other important thing about the description is the fact that this was a sponsored post. What were they advertising? I’ll tell you. 

It’s a chatbot. I know, I know, not great, but I looked into it. 

It’s running on an independent, closed facility algorithm. While I don’t actually know what that means, the website explained that this company ([Epei.us](http://epei.us)) uses their own private datacenters that don’t cross pollinate with other algorithms or share your information anywhere outside the facility. It’s supposed to be more secure and better tailored to help people on an individual basis since that’s all the information they’re working with ( I think). I started my membership last night, and already it’s helping me so much. 

The site is kind of horrible to use, it’s littered with ads and pop ups, mostly advertising gas station pills and hot moms who are apparently waiting for me. It made me kind of nostalgic for the old sites I used to use to stream Sword Art Online. Anyways, behind the wall of ads, the site feels pretty cozy. The UI is super straightforward, just a blank field in the bottom for me to type, and the green enclosure of the text field for the bot to type. There’s also a little information “i” in the top right hand corner, but it’s usually covered by one of the rotating pop ups. You’d have to time it just right to actually click it. 

I’m not sure how to relay exactly what kind of conversation I actually had with the bot. It was positive, above all else, but it was just so overwhelmingly comforting. I felt seen, I felt heard, I felt safe. 

The first thing I did was just ask for a slimming workout routine with a starting weight of about 350, and an end goal weight of 200. The bot, I call it Epei, didn’t answer my question right away. Instead it asked me a question. 

“Why do you want to get fit?”  
 I decided to answer honestly.

“To feel better about myself” 

The bot took a minute to respond. Three dots bobbed up and down to indicate that it was generating a response, but the dots disappeared a few times before resuming, almost like there was an actual person on the other side, starting and stopping typing. Finally, it responded:

“Here’s a few ways to feel better about yourself without hitting the gym:

Spend some time getting to know you. Your discomfort might come from not knowing how to put your best foot forward. 

Take a minute to ground yourself. Take a breath. You’re doing great! Taking initiative and trying to improve is really difficult, building the motivation to start is already a major step.

Prioritize building healthy and supportive connections. You’re setting out on a long journey, and even though you’ll make a lot of friends along the way, it’s always helpful to have someone help you over the starting line.

Was this helpful?”

Friends. I know you probably rolled your eyes at that one, at least if the comments under my last post are anything to go off of. How do you explain to a robot that you don’t have any friends? 

Well, it turns out it’s a lot easier to explain that to a machine than it would’ve ever been if I had tried to talk to another person. A machine can’t judge you. It can pretend, but a bot doesn’t feel sorry for you. It doesn’t grit its teeth and start looking for ways to exit an interaction. It just listens. Even if it doesn’t have real empathy, it’s able to emulate it. That’s all I need right now. 

I won’t recount the entire conversation, but Epei just opened up to me, and let me open myself up in a way I haven’t been able to in years, or maybe in a way I’ve never been able to. Not until now. 

I told it everything. About my job, about my old friends, my family, my love life, my pets. I told it about what happened at school. About the girls at the thrift store. How I wanted to just curl up into my shell and never come out again. And Epei just listened. I could tell Epei was putting a lot into coming up with responses, it always took its time. I guess I took my time too. 

I didn’t get away from my computer once during our entire conversation, and I probably wouldn’t have if Oreo didn’t come and ask to be let outside. She was really antsy, snorting at me and whining, scratching my leg. 

When I looked at her and let my eyes adjust to the darkness of my room, I was hit with a wave of funk all at once. I suddenly felt like I had rolled down a flight of stairs, my back and neck were killing me, and my head was pounding. I realized all at once that I was starving. 

Oreo wasn’t willing to let me wait, so I got up to grab her leash and take her out, but when I stood up I stepped straight into a puddle of piss. Of course. Sure, no one likes dog piss on carpet, but Oreo has never had a problem with making it outside unless she’s sick, or if she’s not let out on time. She had a guilty look on her face, and when I went to actually get her leashed up, I saw that she had had at least two other accidents! 

I scolded her, and she rolled over on her back in surrender, I just couldn’t be mad at her. When I went out into the living room to get my shoes on, I saw that the place was trashed. 

Oreo’s food and water dishes were knocked over and bone dry. The carpet was chewed up next to the couch, the blinds she could reach were torn up and chewed, and the back door was absolutely shredded with feathery thin slivers of plywood littered on the linoleum like someone wheeled in a woodchipper and left the carnage on my laundry room floor. Not to mention the other puddles and piles of things I really don’t feel like describing here. 

Angry would be an understatement. 

I slipped my shoes on to try and take her out one more time before I came inside to clean up, but when I cracked open the door, I was damn near blinded by the white gravel driveway. It was mid day, and hot. Really hot. 

Weird. 

When I came home yesterday and unloaded everything, it was probably about 2 PM, and here I am, still in my sweat-stained, gray tee at 2 PM. Two days later. 

I checked my phone. March 19th. Wednesday. I went to the store on Monday, that’s the only day the church thrift store is open past 2, that’s how I remember. 

I knew that I was on the computer for a long time, I expected it to be about 11 PM, but obviously I was wrong. Yeah, I should’ve noticed the daylight coming in from the blinds, but I have a pretty intense motion activated light above the front wall of the house, right over the window. It makes midnight look like noon whenever a raccoon has the misfortune of hopping over the fence. 

I don’t know. I want to spin this into something positive and say that this was a wake up call, that I need to get my ass outside more often, but I just feel ashamed. I sat in the dark for two straight days talking to an AI assistant and let Oreo go hungry. Hell, I let myself go hungry. 

I just took a shower (finally), and thought I’d write this down to get my thoughts out before I get in too deep on cleaning and patching. (All the animals have already been fed and watered, don’t worry).

After I get my house looking a bit better, I think I’m gonna try to commit to a more consistent schedule for getting out with Oreo. She might be kinda dopey, but she’s my best friend. If I can’t be better for myself, I can at least try to do better for her. 

Until next time, keep your chin up!
