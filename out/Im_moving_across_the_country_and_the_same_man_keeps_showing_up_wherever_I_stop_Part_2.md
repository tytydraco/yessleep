He followed me to my motel.

I got a place outside San Antonio for the night. It's a little roach motel, but it was just twenty bucks for the night. Can't complain I guess. 

When I went to check in the guy in the office seemed pretty standoffish. It was midnight so I guess I got it. I probably look pretty rough. 

He got me a room on the upper level and handed me my key.

"Thanks." I took the key and held it for a moment. I figured it wouldn't hurt to ask him about the guy.

"Have you seen a man about six foot or so, brown hair, and bright yellow eyes around tonight?" I hadn't seen him here yet so I kinda hoped I'd be safe.

The guy just stared at me like I was crazy before he spoke up, "Road hallucination kid, hear that kinda stuff all the time. You'll be better in the morning."

I didn't say anything for a while before nodding, "Yeah, yeah sure. Okay. Thanks anyways." Before leaving the office.

I walked to my car, but my eyes kept darting around the parking lot. Even if he didn't show up this definitely didn't feel like the kind of place I should be lingering outside for long by myself.

I quickly dug through the back of my car and grabbed my backpack and suitcase. I wanted to make sure I had the essentials.

The metal staircase echoed with each step, bouncing across the parking lot. As I reached the landing of the second floor I was quietly thankful that I'd been given the room all the way at the end.

I turned the key and pushed open the door. Any joy I felt in that moment was quickly squashed.

The room smelled sour and musty, as if they hadn't even bothered to spray air freshener in the last 3 years. The curtains appeared to have stains on them and the bedding looked as if it had been pulled straight from the 90s. The carpet didn't fare much better.

I shut the door and locked the deadbolt behind me. I just had to sleep for a few hours and then I'd be back on the road in no time. 

That's what I told myself at least.

I gave the bed a quick once over for any bugs before crawling in. The blankets felt like stiff sandpaper against my skin. This was the worst bed I'd ever laid in but I was exhausted.

Surprisingly, it didn't take long for me to drift off to sleep. I'd been awake for twenty one or so hours now and I was exhausted. 

Unfortunately, I only managed an hour or two before an itch in my fingers woke me up. 

Smoke.

God I did not want to go out there. I did not trust that he hadn't somehow caught up to me. 

No. No, I would just have to wait and do it in the morning. I needed to sleep. I closed my eyes tight and tried to get myself to sleep again.

It lasted about thirty minutes.

I leaned up and walked over to the window, pushing open the curtains slowly to try and peer out into the parking lot. The place appeared empty, there only seemed to be four or five cars in the parking lot including my own.

I looked from left to right, scanning the lot for the gold of his eyes. The coast seemed clear.

I grabbed my pack of Marlboros, my lighter, and the keys before slipping on my shoes.  I pulled open the door and took one final peek to either side before walking out. I locked the door and walked over to the edge and lit up my smoke.

The peace only lasted a few moments before I heard someone coming up the stairs. The footsteps were heavy and I turned my head. First, I saw the top of his head, thunk, his eyes, piercing into my soul, thunk, his smile, just a slight grin. He stopped there for a moment, just staring at me.

I could feel my heart throbbing in my chest and my hands began to shake. I dropped my cigarette and turned. I fumbled to pull my keys from my pocket. 

Thunk.

He reached the top of the stairs, I could see him out of the corner of my eye. I whimpered as the keys struggled to meet the lock. They slipped from my hands and hit the ground. I felt tears pricking at my eyes as I fell to my knees to grab the keys from the ground. I barely managed to make the keys match the lock before I turned the door and crawled in. I pulled the key out and slammed the door, sitting with my back against the wood. 

Thunk.

I covered my face as he walked closer and closer to my door. I flinched as I heard a soft tapping on my window. I barely looked over, staring at the glass. I could not see much of him but I could see the bright yellow of his eyes. They reflected against the window.

My muscles felt like stone. I didn't dare move. If I didn't move, perhaps he wouldn't see me. Maybe he would just leave.

Suddenly, a car pulled into the motel and their headlights lit up my room. I peered out from behind my hands to look at the light shining on my wall. There was no shadow from the man.

My eyes snapped back to the window. He was gone. 

I scrambled to my feet and basically ran to the window. I closed the curtains tight and moved the dresser in front of the door before I laid down. My heart was racing but I desperately needed sleep. I still had twenty or so more hours of driving.

God I feel so sick.
