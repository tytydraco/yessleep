I work as a dishwasher at the New Hiem Steakhouse. It seems to be a pretty relaxed place. Though, when I first started, my bosses, ( the sous chef, manager, and owner ) were so content on being on point. Wash dishes, get pans, and cutlery out as soon as possible. Not taking breaks unless you had to piss, if you have nothing left to do, then scrub the walls. If there's grease, even if there’s not grease, then go ahead and scrub them anyway. I didn’t mind it, this is my first job so I assume most places are like this.

Recently they’ve been letting customers in the back. I’m assuming they are friends of the bosses because they don’t all seem to talk about normal things that someone would talk about the first time they’ve seen the back of the restaurant. That’s why I think they’re pretty relaxed. 

Usually, they know exactly what I like and they talk about those subjects.

“Hey Andrew, what’s cracking on your side of the shell?” He said to me with his normal toothy smile.

“Jack shit my man, didn’t even see you walk in. Almost startled me there. You should really knock.” I gave a small smile. 

I was honestly drowning in dishes from a 13+ party that insisted on getting 2nds and maybe even 3rds. 

It was Ray, I only met him about 6 days ago whenever the bosses started letting people in. He was a cool guy, but he had a slightly odd look to him. His smile was a little too big but I’m not one to judge. Hell, I don’t even know how many teeth a person really has.

“You know Andrew, I’ve been thinking. Maybe you should come work for me?” He looked at me intently.

Usually, we talked about Warhammer or manga that we were both interested in, that was  before he would tell me, “Peace, I’ll see you later.” Then he’d leave. 

I didn’t even know he had a business.

I kept washing the plate that was already in my hand and put it into the washer tray. I went to grab another plate and when my hand made contact with it he was standing behind me with his hand already on my forearm.

“You can feel me can’t you?” He asked, but his voice sounded like it was multiple people, sexes, talking all at once.

“Of-of course?” It was so quick and off putting, I even didn’t see him walk over.

“Why are you ask-…” I turned around while asking the question. It wasn’t Ray. 

His face was contorted, he looked like he was trying to smile but couldn’t figure it out, With his eyes closed.

“My job is simple!” He told me, in what I could make out as a chuckle. 

“I find people like you.” He or.. it  raised my arm above my head.  
     
“Andrew!” Daniel shouted at me. God, the sous chef that had always been so strict was glaring at me holding my arm. I had a butter knife in my hand?

I looked at him with haste feeling a cold flush of relief as I heard his angry voice. “What the fuck are you doing? You look crazy standing there like that. Are you on fucking drugs?”

“N-no sir can’t you-didn’t you..” I tried to reason but as I looked for the culprit there was nothing. Nothing at all. 

“Can’t you see that I’m stretching?” Lying. I asked even though my breathing was heavy. I know I looked stupid.

“You concern me sometimes dude. Wash the damn dishes please, we’re running out of pans so make a run to the front.” He walked away.

“Asshole.” I muttered.
