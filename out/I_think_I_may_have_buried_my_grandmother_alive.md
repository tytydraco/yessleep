Before anyone judges me: I have no history of mental health issues, and neither does my family. I grew up in a healthy environment, full of love and support, and I am completing a master's degree next semester. I have never believed in any religion or conspiracy theory. Please read carefully what I am about to say before telling me I have gone crazy.

Recently, I read a research article conducted at King's College London where their team collected post-mortem data on over 3,400 people after they died. They measured brain activity specifically and found that the brain can maintain a kind of 'state of consciousness' (as they called it) that is observable through electrical activity. The scientists involved were careful not to speculate too much. They stated that this activity does not necessarily imply being 100% conscious or "awake" in any way. But here is the thing: the same lab has been studying the neural networks of consciousness, and they found an overlap between the two studies. In other words, the same neurons involved in conscious activity are still active or 'lighting' during the weeks when the brain is degrading. For anyone with zero background in neuroscience, let me say what that means:

**Dead people may be aware that they are buried.** Experiencing, without moving, without seeing, breathing, or smelling. And (I hope) without feeling any pain. This could last for days, even WEEKS, before the brain begins its natural process of melting.

Think about this: everyone we have ever buried. People have been rotting since 1800, since before that. Everyone who ever died there alone. Maybe screaming inside their own heads. Irreligious people suddenly praying for heaven to be real just to be pulled out of that misery. And nobody came because nobody knew. We just kept burying them.

Why is nobody talking about this yet?

I do not want to make the scientists into villains. But someone needs to say something more convincing than the vague responses I have seen in the media. I searched everywhere, and all the lead investigator offered was this:

*"We are not saying the dead are aware in the way the living are aware. We are saying that the boundary we have long drawn between life and death, at the neurological level, is not where we believed it to be. The implications of that are something we are still working to understand."*

I also found this:

*"The team first observed the phenomenon in 2023 during a study on traumatic brain injury recovery. Post-mortem scans of two subjects showed activity in the prefrontal cortex that should not have been present. Assuming an equipment error, the team recalibrated and repeated. The activity remained. Over the following eighteen months, they expanded the study, tightening controls, bringing in independent reviewers from University College London and the Karolinska Institute in Stockholm. The results held."*

Professor David Hartley of the University of Edinburgh said *"If this is replicated, and I mean properly replicated across institutions and cultures, then I am not sure we have words yet for what it means."*

My grandmother was my best friend. She passed away last week, 93 years old, completely lucid until the end. A complication from hip fracture surgery. I wish it had been me before her. She was the most generous person I have ever known. She had a gift for making even people who had lost everything feel loved again. She was always addicted to telling stories, and I refuse to let this be her last one; she deserves better, and I will do everything I can to make sure of it.

She may not feel the wood against her fingers, or the absence of oxygen inside a coffin. But she may be conscious right now, actively thinking, while I am sitting here writing this. 

She may know she was beloved until her last breath, lying in a hospital bed. Maybe she is holding onto that feeling, the only thing she has left, because she knows that nobody is coming. Just infinite silence. 

It has been 48 hours since the funeral. It's killing me, and I have no one to talk to about this.

I have not slept or eaten since. I have been tracking my sleep with a smartwatch for a long time, and I could share the data if anyone needs proof that I am not making this up.

I am sitting on my sofa right now, thinking about what would happen if I just dug her up.

Has anyone heard about this? And more importantly: what would you do?
