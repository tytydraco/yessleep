I honestly haven't really talked about this since it happened. I mean, I don't know what I'd say. Nobody would believe me anyway. But it's been weighing on me lately, and who else to share it with but the internet? 

This all happened when I was about 14 or 15. As cliche as it sounds, I grew up in a small town where everyone knew each other, you barely needed to lock your doors at night, blah blah blah. Well, that's until this all went down. 

At the time, I had a small but tight-knit group of friends. There was Marshall, who was my next-door neighbour (and also happened to be my cousin), our other friends Sarah and Jeffrey, and Jeffrey's little sister Tina, who was a year or two younger than us and sometimes tagged along on our adventures. I've changed a few details to hide my identity, but for some additional information this was the early 80s somewhere in North America. 

It all started at the beginning of the school year. Marshall, Sarah, Jeffrey and I were all starting ninth grade. We had gotten news that there was a new school teacher, which was exciting to us since we hadn't had anyone move here in our entire lives. His name was Mr. Abaddon, but we could all call him Mr. A, if it was too hard to pronounce. We were all sitting in the grade 9/10 classroom (our town was so small we had to combine a lot of classes and grades), discussing who he was, or what he would be like. 

"Good morning, kids!" We heard an unfamiliar voice bellow. 

The first thing I noticed about Mr. A was how he was dressed. Our town was predominantly blue collar-most folks here lived a pretty simple life. Even on fancy occasions, we mainly dressed down. Not him, though. He had a three-piece suit on, freshly ironed, with shiny shoes to match. He was incredibly handsome, and had the most contagious smile I've ever seen. 

"I'm Mr. A, it's so nice to meet all of you!", He said, clasping his hands together. "I'm mainly here to teach science and some history, but I'll be happy to help you with anything else you need. Just let me know." 

We all immediately fell in love with him. Maybe it was just something new for us to look at, maybe he was the first person in expensive clothes we had ever seen, but something about him just...drew us all towards him. In the beginning, at least.

He taught us science, mainly, as he said. But he'd also check in on us at other times of the day. He even always had his classroom door open in case we ever just needed someone to talk to. He was like our teacher, counsellor, and best friend all in one. He knew all of our names, which, granted there were only a couple hundred kids from kindergarten to grade 12, and most of us were either named Lisa or Mike, but still. 

But as time wore on, my friends and I grew a bit more wary of him. He was nice and all, but something about him was just...off. He just felt fake. Have you ever been to an amusement park and met a mascot? They're all friendly and hug you and all, they pose for a picture, but when you leave, you turn around and the facade is off. They just look dead. That's what Mr. A was like. 

One day, it must've been around November, Marshall and I had stayed a little after class to get some help with chemistry. Even though at this point, my friends and I had grown a little wary of him, even still, he was so charming it was hard not to be drawn to him when he talked to you. I don't even know how to explain it, but he made you feel like the only person in the world. 

"Alright, thanks, Mr. A," Marshall said, collecting his work and shoving it into his bag. I stood up and waited for him by the door. 

"No problem, bud. It's what I'm here for." He winked and smiled, that magnetic smile of his that lit up an entire room. Marshall and I left the room, but I peered through the window after we shut the door. And his smile was just gone. In its place was the most terrifying face I'd ever seen. It was a hideous snarl, not in an, "I just smelled something disgusting," way, but in a way that just said he wanted to hurt someone. And that's when I reflected on that damn smile. In all of the times I had seen it, I realized it had just never reached his eyes. It was the most beautiful smile I'd ever seen, but above his mouth there was just nothing. No joy, no warmth. He was faking his charisma. 

That day, we had met up with our friends at Jeffrey and Tina's house, since they had the biggest basement. I explained to them what I had seen after leaving Mr. A's room half expecting them to call it bullshit, since everyone else in town seemed so in love with him, but to my shock, they agreed with me. 

"Yeah, he's kind of weird." Jeffrey said, as his mom brought us a plate of cookies. "Thanks, ma." 

"My friend Lisa and I saw him watching us through a window when we went home for lunch.  And also he smells really bad if you've ever been close enough to him. Like roadkill." Tina added. "I don't even know why he was in our classroom, he doesn't even teach our grade." 

Sarah had stayed quiet up until then, but I could tell she had something she wanted to say. "I've seen him do some weird things. He lives close to my house, I think. I see him walking past my house a lot. And he's not like how he is at school. I don't know, just something about him looks off. If I say what I saw, will you guys believe me no matter what?" We all nodded. "It was really late one night, and I woke up to get some water. I looked out the window, and he was just there in the middle of our street. But he wasn't walking, he was crawling. Like some sort of animal. And he looked at me, so I ran back to my room. But when I looked at him, his eyes were different. Not his regular eyes. Just not human. When I got upstairs, I swear I heard scratching at my door the rest of the night." 

We were all silent for a while. We didn't know what to do with that information. After a while Marshall spoke up.

"Sarah, you said you're neighbours, right?" She nodded. 

We came up with a plan. One day, we'd all go to Sarah's house. When we saw Mr. A walking down her street, we'd follow him to see where he lives. Then, the next day, we'd skip school to break into his house and snoop through his stuff. Truthfully, I don't know what we thought would happen. I don't know if we thought demons or monsters or whatever we thought Mr. A was carried around monster identification cards or something, but whatever we expected, it definitely wasn't what we found. 

We successfully enacted the first part of the plan the next Monday after that conversation in Jeffrey's basement. As quietly as we could muster, we followed him on our bikes. Well, that was until he walked straight into the woods. 

Sarah lived on the outskirts of our town. Past the final house on her block, was thick woods. It was near impossible to navigate it, we had tried for many summers. But he just walked into it like he didn't even see it. 

We ditched our bikes and tried to follow him through the woods. It was hard, but we managed to do it. He eventually led us to what looked like an abandoned cabin. Was that his house? It was barely standing. We watched in horror as he opened the door, but his arm phased right through the wood. He opened the door from the inside. Before he stepped into the house, he looked back and made direct eye contact with us. 

He smiled at us. Not that charming, ingenuine smile he always gave at school. This one was sinister. I've never seen any human give that expression before. It was pure evil. 

It was a quiet bike ride home. That night, we had decided that Tina wouldn't be allowed to come with us the next day, much to her dismay.

"But I wanna help!" She protested. "I think he's creepy and weird and I want to come with you guys." 

"Tina, no. I know you just want to help us but I'm not letting you. Best case scenario, Mr. A is a magician who's creeping on girls, worst case scenario we're dealing with demonic shit. I promise you'll be allowed to hang out with us as much as you want after this, but just this once, stay home with mom." Jeffrey said roughly. Sarah, Marshall, and I exchanged glances. Obviously we were no strangers to Tina and Jeffrey's bickering, but this seemed different. 

Tina sighed. "Okay." 

The night came and went, and we had all successfully lied to our parents about being sick. Once they had all gone to work, we snuck out. We somehow navigated through the thick woods and found that stupid cabin again, and crept our way in.

We all stood there in silence. Whatever this was, there's no way any human was living in here. 

It wasn't what was in there, it was the lack of what was in there. It was just...empty. No furniture, no photos, not even a toilet or kitchen. In my lifetime since this day, I've encountered squatters. I know it's possible to live with no electricity or running water, but there was just a complete lack of ANYTHING here. No garbage on the ground, no milk jugs full of piss, nothing. How did he come to work so sharply dressed every day if this is how he was living? 

"I knew you guys would be coming around." We heard, and all four of us jumped. We had thought he was at work.  

He came out of the shadows. I was horrified. He looked even less human than usual, but he was still keeping up that stupid charming voice. 

"W-what are you?" Marshall asked, backing up. 

Mr. A laughed. "That's not important," As he spoke, his voice starting warping, almost like a melting record. It got less and less human as he continued. "What's important is that none of you ever tell anyone what you saw." 

"Then we won't. I promise. We'll leave and I swear we'll never tell anyone. We'll take it to our graves." Sarah said, and Jeffrey nodded frantically. As they pleaded, I paid attention to his face. It was moving-his skin looked like it was morphing into something else. 

"That's not what I meant. What I meant was that none of you will leave here, again. Do you see what's happening to me? It's been so long since I've fed." 

At this point his voice was completely different. It was so atonal. People have a speech pattern; you may not notice it, but they do. When someone's speaking with no rhythm, it sounds truly, so disturbing. His voice was deeper than I thought a person could go-but then again, whatever he was, he wasn't a human. 

His face had melted away. In it's place was...I don't even know how to describe it. His eyes were completely black, his skin was grey and deformed. I heard a cracking sound, which I soon realized was his spine. He was growing taller. 

Suddenly, he lunged at us. 

We all jumped away, but Sarah had been scratched by his nails, which were now monster-like claws. I rushed to grab her and we both ran up the rickety stairs. I heard in the distance Marshall's screams, and Sarah had to cover my mouth to keep sound from coming out of it. Marshall, my neighbour. My cousin. My best friend. I heard the sickening sound of flesh and bones being pierced through, and thought back to all of the birthday parties and slumber parties we'd have, and how many I'd have without him in attendance. We held our breaths as we heard footsteps approaching us. We both let out a sigh of relief when we saw Jeffrey. 

He hid with us in an empty closet. We whispered as quietly as we could, discussing an escape. 

"I think I saw a window in this room before I found you guys. If we jumped out, we'd probably break a few bones, but we'd survive." Jeffrey said. Sarah and I nodded as we slowly opened the door.

It was eerily silent. Not even footsteps. I couldn't even hear Marshall's groans of pain anymore. At least he wasn't afraid anymore. We almost made it to the window when I felt Sarah grip my arm. I looked up and froze in fear when I saw what she was looking at.

There was a reason why we couldn't hear Mr. A looking for us anymore. He had already found us. There he was, on the ceiling, grinning at us with a haunting smile. He crawled down the wall slowly. He was teasing us. Sarah still had a scratch that was bleeding heavily, I was frozen in fear. There was really no hope for us as he slowly crept towards us. We knew it was over. That is, until Jeffrey lunged towards him. 

"Jeff, stop!" Sarah shrieked. I tried to tell him to stop but I was still frozen. 

Jeffrey was tall for his age, and he played football, but we knew it was a losing battle. There was no way Jeff was beating whatever that thing was. He looked at us, terror in his eyes, although I could tell he was trying to hide it.

"Just go, go, through the window! Now!" 

I felt a rush of adrenaline as I suddenly had control over my body. I approached the window as fast as I could, Sarah close behind me. But she wasn't fast enough. Mr. A reached one arm forward, and even though Jeffrey tried to stop him, it was no use. His razor-sharp claw pierced through her skin as she collapsed on the ground. Jeff and I shared a look, tears in our eyes, but there was no time to waste. I grabbed him as fast as I could and jumped backwards through the window. 

We hit the ground with a thump. I could immediately tell my arm was broken, but Jeff clearly had it worse than me. 

"Go." He managed to say. 

"Come on, let's get up, we have time." I winced as I heard Sarah's screams in the house. "He's busy." I grabbed his arm, but he slapped it away.

He shook his head. "I'm not getting out of here, man." 

"No, come on please, I'll carry you-"

"Too slow. You're getting out of here." 

Maybe I was just in denial, but Jeffrey wasn't. Looking back, logistically there was no way he'd survive this, even with the best healthcare in the world. I've never seen so many injuries on one person. He knew it was his time.

He gave me a shaky smile, tears in his eyes. "Will you tell her what I did?"

I nodded. 

"Make sure nobody bullies her, K?" 

I began to cry as I began to leave. I didn't want to leave him, but I could hear Mr. A approaching. 

Then, I ran as fast as I could. 

My family moved pretty soon after that. I tried to tell the police what happened, what really happened, but they didn't believe me. They said Mr. A was a monster, but not the kind I was thinking about, and that I was safe now. I knew it wasn't true.

As for Tina, we didn't really keep in touch. I tried to tell her what happened, but her mom wouldn't let me near her. Maybe I'll try to find her on Facebook. I did promise Jeffrey, after all. 

As for Mr. A, I haven't seen him since. Well at least, that's what I tell everyone.

The night after the cops found me, broken arm, covered in blood that wasn't mine, stumbling around the streets, I was being treated in the hospital. Not the shitty, tiny healthcare facility in our town, I had been air lifted to one miles away in the nearest big city. I had many things wrong with me, but my parents were told I'd make a full recovery. 

That night, I laid in my hospital bed, in complete shock. I could barely move anything but my head. 

I turned over to the window, and saw someone staring at me. 

It wasn't Mr. A. He looked completely different, and this was so far away. But I knew that smile, and those stupid fucking eyes anywhere. It was him, even though nobody would believe me. 

He knew I recognized him, and his smile widened. Before I knew it, he was gone.

It's been years, and I still don't trust anyone. As far as I know, he's still out there. So please, if you ever meet someone who just seems a little off, even if everyone loves them, listen to your gut and run far away while you still can.
