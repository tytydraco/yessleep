Lately, whenever I go down to our basement to get something- whether that be any of my fishing gear I keep down there or something out of the deep freezer, I'll look up the stairs when I get to the bottom and I turn the light on and just, for a split second, see something at the top of the stairs. 

We've lived in this house for quite a while, me and my wife, so most noises and things that go bump in the night can be explained by creaking floorboards or wind whistling against the window panes- but this is different. It keeps me up at night thinking what it is, if it's my eyes playing up or even a tumour pressing against my skull (what Diane, my wife, suggested). I've tried to stay down there and squint into the dark, trying to parse where the figure would be- even repeatedly flicking the lights on and off, on and off, on and off. For hours. Just trying to see the outline of the figure for even a second. 

Diane thinks I've had a breakdown. She asked my job for 'emergency medical leave' for the forseeable future and has sat me down with every doctor and therapist she can find to try and diagnose me and drug me up so I can't feel anymore. Drugging me up won't make the fear leave. Trust me- I've drank enough over the years to know how to feel completely numb to the world but this is on a different level. I can feel it in the deepest pit of my stomach that whatever I see in that split second is real.

I'd been sat in a crumpled heap on the floor of the basement, my hand reaching up to continuously click the cord of the light and weakly turn my head up to the top of the stairs. I didn't know what hour or day it was or how long I'd been down there at this point- my eyes burned from the staring and my arm was throbbing but nothing else mattered.

In my trance I heard the steady padding of Diane's footsteps as she approached the door to the basement. She sighed and ran a hand through her hair. I can hear her start to walk down but in my focus I completely lose her. It's like she doesn't even exist. All I can focus on is the click of the light and my wavering heartbeat. On, off, on, off, on. Maybe if I squint I can see it better. On, off, on, off. I'm sure if I keep trying them whatever I've been seeing will finally stay there and let me see what it is and finally let me rest. On, off, on, off, on...

"Please, won't you come to bed? I keep telling you there's nothing there sweetheart. It's probably stress from work."

Diane squints against the strobe of me repeatedly pulling the cord of the light, a hand reaching to caress my face. I want to recoil from the touch. She was ruining my focus.

"You've been down here for hours, you're really starting to worry me."

I stop the clicking to look at her. Really look at her. A worried frown pulls the edges of her lips into a tight line. She doesn't believe me in the slightest.

"You don't understand... I.. I know something is there, whenever I turn the light on. I swear I can see it. Why don't you believe me?" I can feel the desperation bubbling up my chest.

She sighs again and embraces me in a tight hug.

"Why don't in the morning we make another appointment with the doctors and see what we can do about this insomnia of yours? You know, I was reading about how staying up too long can cause hallucinations and..."

She trails off, the words turning to static in my ears. I feel all the hairs on my body stand up simultaneously. In the light of the basement, in a fuzzy shape, something was standing at the top of the stairs. It looked tall and almost misshapen, but was so blurry and faint I couldn't make out much else other than a sad expression on it's 'face'. An almost comedically exaggerated sad face that gazed at me emptily. I ripped myself out of Diane's grip and screamed as loud as I could, tears rolling down my face. My body shook violently as I scrambled to hide in the corner of the room, trying to shield myself from whatever was there. Diane was horrified, trying to shush me like a child and demanding to know what happened.

"I SAW IT! WHY CAN'T YOU SEE IT! I SAW THE LOOK ON ITS FACE!", I take a deep, shuddering breath, "PLEASE TELL ME YOU CAN SEE IT!" 

I cover my eyes with my hands and sob pathetically like a child.

She sighs, exasperated, rapidly looking from my terrified form to the top of the stairs-

"Honey, I keep telling you, there's nothing there! Now stop being silly and come to bed with me." 

She rips me up off the floor and drags me up the stairs and into our bedroom. I don't remember much of the days after- it was a blur of Diane basically force feeding me and making me get up and walk but as much as she made me I wouldn't step foot anywhere near the basement. She made me promise that I wouldn't go back down there or we would go to the doctors and finally get some medication.

There was one night, however, where I suddenly sat bolt upright in our bed and felt an overwhelming urge to go to the basement. In an effort not to wake up Diane, I crept around the house as quietly as I could. I finally made it to the basement door. 

My heart beating out of my chest, I reach out a shaking hand to the doorknob and...

The light turns on.

"Honey. You promised me that you wouldn't go down there anymore! I've tried to be as tolerant as I can to this behaviour, as I love you and I'm always on your side, but you're taking this too far now. You're mentally ill and we need to do something about it." 

She chides me like I'm the dirt beneath her boot. She never understood me. Ever the patronising Diane.

"Diane, I am telling you, in my heart of hearts that there is something there. I saw it the last time I was down there. It's making me feel crazy and the only way to solve this is to try and see it for long enough, I know-"

"Feel crazy? YOU ARE CRAZY! Do you know what I've had to tell people? Tell our friends? I've had to make up stories about how you're so busy with work that you can't leave the house anymore to see them. You haven't been to work for MONTHS!"

Anger bubbles up my chest and I feel my fists clench. Diane takes a step towards me, towards the door.

"And you know what? I'll open the door and show you that there's nothing there, and this fantasy of yours can finally go away!"

She yanks the door open, to see the darkness of the basement below.

"See? I told you that-"

My body moves on its own. I shove Diane with all of my force down the stairs. She looks at me in horror for a split second before I watch her body fall down each step, landing on the cold concrete with a loud thud.

My chest heaves. All I can hear is buzzing in my ears.

It takes a while of me frozen there before I can realise what I've done. The sensation comes back to my body as my legs carry me down the stairs and I cradle Diane in my arms, crying pitifully over her.

I chance a look up the stairs a final time.

It's there. Staring at me. This time, with a cartoonish smile on its face.
