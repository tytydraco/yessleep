When I think about how my 20s would go, I didn’t think a world-altering contagion would be a part of it, but, here we are. 



My name is Reggie, and I’ve been working at this CVS for around 7 years now. The entire world basically ended around 2030, ever since the first strain of Parasitica Propagaris was found. It was frozen in some arctic ice cap for millennia, and then, apparently, it found its way into some poor polar bear or seal or whatever, and then into an unlucky researcher. He lived for another five years, until, suddenly, his entire body exploded. Like, fully detonated; his skin and blood and bone becoming vapor, and being inhaled by passerby on the street. Naturally, this sent the media into a spiral, especially when 10 more people in the coming days had the same thing happen to them. It turned out that, because of the asymptomatic and rapidly spreading nature of the plague, around 99.9% of the human population had been infected by the spores. Top scientists determined that, within the next ten years, the entire human race would go extinct.



And that was when the corporations got involved. Some sponsored drug trials to cure the disease, others tried to find humans immune to the disease, or simply those who hadn’t been infected, but a Silicon Valley start-up company, SoulCorp, had a new approach- acceptance. They said they had found a way to fully digitize a human being’s consciousness after they Ruptured; that, if all of the blood, viscera, DNA, and what they called a “soul” could be trapped together, they could live in the digital world forever.



This caused a moral and philosophical dilemma for much of the human race; some insisted they’d stay behind, others immediately went to a SoulCorp center to have themselves implanted in the cloud. In the end, from what I’ve heard, they couldn’t find a cure, and all humans had Ruptured around 2045. The “survivors”, if you want to call us that, have been in this digital world for around eight to twelve years now.



At first, it was assumed it’d be a digital paradise, but I think SoulCorp was able to get all the money they did for the conversions from… other corporations. Everything was ad space in the digital world; houses were branded with Mountain Dew logos, sidewalks spoke of curing your nicotine addiction, playgrounds had advertisements for the newest toy. And then, the worst news came-



“Salutations, Ruptured!” a voice rang through the digital atmosphere. “My name is Mr. Soul, CEO, President, and adorable digital mascot of SoulCorp! Now, as you’re aware, you paid exactly zero dollars and zero cents for the conversion of your squishy human exterior into the sleek digital interior, all thanks to your friends at SoulCorp! However, we need to make that money back. Soooo….” Pieces of digital paper drifted down from the sky, landing on lawns, homes, and people…. They were applications…. For jobs.



“Time for y’all to get to work!”



\---------------------



So I’ve been working for this CVS. It’s fine, really. It’s enough to do so that I can go back to my shitty apartment and watch the news or some lame sitcom and feel somewhat accomplished. But it’s… boring. Just mundane. That was, until, my most recent shift.



I was doing what I normally do; helping customers, stocking shelves, and staring out into space out of total boredom. I heard the familiar chime of the automatic doors, and saw a man my age walk in.



“Hey, you guys got chicken soup? My girlfriend has been sick with something.”



“Yeah, should be Aisle 3.”



“Appreciate it.”



You wouldn’t think that a fully digital world would have diseases, but, maybe in the interest of “keeping reality feeling real”, SoulCorp made a lot of decisions that don’t exactly help the common man. I just think it’s to keep people at work, keep earning money, but what do I know?



The man came back up to the register. “Oh, by the way, for when she gets better, you guys got Cockpro Maxxx for Men?”



“Yeah.” I grabbed it from the back shelf.



“Thanks, appreciate it. And hey, what’s that guy on back there?”



“What guy?”



“There was some guy with long hair, like, tweaking out. I didn’t know if it was a product you guys sold.”



“No, why would we sell something like that?”



“Well, you’re a drugstore, right? Maybe you guys sell something harder than Tylenol.”



“What do you think we sell here? Meth?”



“Listen man, I don’t even know, but I do know that guy was bugging.”



“Alright, alright. Thanks.”



I rang him up, and after he left, I went to check on the man he was talking about. However, besides a woman with her baby, and one of our regulars, I didn’t see anyone. I went back to the register.



After a few more minutes, the regular, Mildred, came to check out. “Oh, hello, dearie,” she said.



“Hey, Mildred, how are things with Allen?” Allen was her husband; they had gotten married in the virtual space after both of their spouses refused to be converted. 



“Wonderful, dearie, just wonderful,” she sighed contently. “You know, Baxter, he never liked my music when we were alive, he was like ‘Mildred, turn off your hoodlum music, you’re going to give me ear cancer.’ And now, Allen, bless his heart, he actually quite likes it! Have you heard of the young man, MF Doom?”



“Yeah, I haven’t listened to him much, but he’s good.”



“Yes, yes he is.” Mildred sighed. “You know, I feel so young when I listen to it. Gets me riled! When Allen put it on the other night, oh, I just got funky, and then, both of us got funky.” Mildred giggled.



“.....cool. You need anything else, Mildred?”



“Cockpro Maxxx for Men.”



I grabbed it, and she walked out, the exit door beeping, with a little bit of pep in her step. Honestly, good for her. I’ve wanted to date in here, but, of the few dates I had, everyone just felt… off. Nothing romantically feels right. Or maybe I’m just a prude. I sigh quietly as I help the mother with the kid, and then the store was empty.



And then he came up to the register. He had long, greasy hair, was unshaven and ragged, and had a strange way of walking. I was sure this was the guy that Chicken Soup was talking about. 



“Hey, how can I help you?”



“I… I lost them….”



“....what?”



“Mother always said I was a bad boy, I wouldn’t amount to anything… do you think I’ll amount to something?” He looked down ashamedly.



I don’t know what this guy was on, but I just needed him out of the store. “Y-yeah, man, sure.”



“So you’ll help me find them?”



“Find what?”



“I lost them… I lost them in here. Or maybe outside. Where am I?”



“...CVS?”



“Oh. You guys got Cockpro Maxxx for Men?”



I grabbed it from the back shelf.



“Thanks,” he said as he paid. His hands were… off. Like his fingers looked unusually smooth. And then, he walked out without a sound. Weirdo.



After an hour or so, it was time for me to get off my shift. I relinquished register control to my coworker, and then I started to clean before leaving. I swept the aisles, did a bit of vacuuming near the back of the pharmacy, and while I was looking at what needed to be stocked, I saw something poking out of the wall. Like, it was fused with the wall. It was small, around an inch or so in length, and it was an opaque yellow. I touched it… it was hard. Harder than cement. I got in close to see what it was…



It was a fingernail. More precisely, four of them; each one protruding more than the last, except for one of them, which floated out in space…. I tapped it lightly, and it didn’t move at all. Like it was glued to the air. I don’t even know how.



But I don’t get paid enough for that. So I headed out for the night.
