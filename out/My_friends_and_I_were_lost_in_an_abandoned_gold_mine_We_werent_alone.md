Those two had left me waiting once again.

I scrolled an urban explorer forum I frequented and found nothing I hadn't seen before.

One post did catch my eye though.

It was a post about Wiseman Mine. A haunted gold mine said be located somewhere on the edge of town.

George Wiseman had died a rich man sometime in the eighteen hundreds.

On his death bed he is said to have sworn to return and collect the rest of his gold from the earth.

If anyone was to try and steal it from him he further swore to strike them down and they would serve the mine in their death.

Its said if you're lost in the forest around Westbrook you might hear him still digging beneath your feet.

Finally I saw headlights approaching through the trees and put my phone down.

A red suv pulled up beside me and a bearded man called out from the passenger seat.

"Hey Skinny, we had to stop for gas!"

I'd given up trying to make them stick to a schedule years ago. I got out of the car and grabbed my bag. The other two did the same.

We walked towards a gated fence surrounding an abandoned automotive factory.

The bearded man, Hunter, bent down as we approached the fence with wire cutters in hand.

The power was already off.

Dave Hardwell died of a heart attack thirty or so years ago. No family came forward to claim his assets or his business.

People stopped showing up for work. Nobody paid the bills and Dave's Auto shop was left to be reclaimed by the forest.

It only took a few minutes and we were soon walking through a courtyard overgrown with weeds and moss.

The door to the factory itself was unlocked.

Disassembled remains and skeletal frames of cars littered the factory floor.

There was a hole in the ceiling.

We slowly made our way through the room and onwards up a set of stairs that were once an emergency exit. We climbed them upwards and found the door to the roof locked.

Thea held up her torch as Toolbox began unbolting the door.

It didn't take long to get the door open. We stepped out onto the roof and beheld our prize.

A beautiful view overlooking the whole town of Westbrook.

We went back and forth pointing out sights we recognised in the light of street lamps below.

We had planned this trip for a night without clouds.

The stars lit up the night sky above us.

We could see them better on the far side of the roof facing away from town.

I pulled a few beers and some snacks from my bag.

We all sat there looking up at the stars. We Talked for hours. During a lull in conversation I asked if either of them had heard of Wiseman Mine.

Hunter told me he had no idea but Thea gave me a knowing nod.

She asked if I meant the haunted gold mine and Hunter had a concerned look on his face at the word haunted.

Naturally I asked if he was scared of ghosts and he said yes.

Then I asked if he wanted to hear the story of Wiseman Mine.

He hesitated but nodded.

After the story he had forgotten about the ghost and asked instead how much I thought the gold would be worth.

We went back and forth for a while arguing over how much it would be or how you would sell it in the first place.

Thea had been quiet until now but spoke up now. She said she wanted to look for the mine.

The conversation came to a halt and I looked at her. I laughed.

Wiseman Mine was just a scary story.

I told her this much after I had finished and she told me I was wrong. She said a trusted source mentioned rumours it was a few miles west of Barbers Ave.

I didn't believe her.

Hunter said he was in.

I expected her to laugh at him for falling for the joke. She just kept looking at me.

When I didn't say anything she said that she wasn't interested in the gold or the ghost story.

She told me that she just wanted to find out if it was real.

I realised so did I.

I told her I was in.

We planned to meet up three days from then and make our way out to look for the mine.

The next day a storm blew in and didn't give up for the better part of the week.

I used this time to research what I could find about Wiseman Mine. The supposedly real Wiseman Mine, not the ghost story people told.

I found nothing helpful except for one article in a newspaper from 1902.

A local man, Joshua Higgs, had died from falling into a collapsed mine shaft. The odd thing was, in the fall he had impaled something sharp straught through his skull.

They said he was unrecognisable.

Only the ID in his wallet identified him. It was ruled an accidental death and the mineshaft tunnel was filled over. 

At the end of the week the rain had passed. We started our search early at six AM.

The night before I had a strange dream. I woke up in my bedroom.

There was no furniture inside except for my bed. I could hear something faintly. A repetative banging. It was coming from downstairs.

I woke up the next morning and shrugged it off as a strange dream.

I got ready for the search and drove out to the start of Barbers Ave.

The other two were early.

We drove for an hour. About halfway through the paved road gives way to packed dirt.

Barbers Ave led north in the opposite direction of the automotive factory. It ended in an interstate highway headed towards the coast.

It was mostly woods and hills between us and there.

I pulled out a map with a few locations circled that I thought looked good to search.

Thea added a few more and we set off searching for George Wisemans Gold Mine.

Later that day we left the woods tired and bruised. I got bit by ants. Hunter got stung by a bee. We had covered half the ground we expected too and found nothing.

Three days of searching later we found it.

It was late afternoon on the fourth day.

We were heading back and a fallen tree trunk blocked our path. The dirt around its roots was sunken several feet into the ground in an odd pattern.

I remembered the article I read a few days prior and pointed out the small sinkhole to the others.

We all started to dig.

The soil was still damp from the rain and it made the digging easier.

Twenty minutes later our shovels burst through into open space. Dirt piled into a mineshaft tunnel about four feet below us.

The gap we could see through was small.

The sun was starting to set and so we all prepared to return the following morning.

That night I had the same strange dream.

My bedroom empty. A faint banging noise from below. I went to go downstairs and investigate only to find the door to my room was locked.

I put my ear to the floor. I listened to the noise. It sounded like metal being struck serveral stories beneath me. I could hear something crack under the weight of each blow.

I woke up the next morning unnerved but soon forgot my strange dream as I prepared to explore Wiseman Mine.

We never planned to go far.

We just wanted a souvenir. A rusty pickaxe. A miners helmet. Something like that.

We got to the mine in the morning and began to dig a large enough gap for us to get in and out.

The deeper we dug the more stones we have to dig around and carry out ourselves.

By afternoon we had cleared a space slanting downward into the mineshaft.

We made it inside.

The walls were jagged stones. Carved at odd angles often slanted towards the ceiling.

It was wide enough for the three of us to walk side by side.

We only had one way to go and so followed the tunnel further down carefully. As we walked there were often side passages that bent and twist at odd angles.

We came to a dead end.

We made our way back towards the exit taking our time to inspect the side passages more throughly.

They must be the space left behind once the ore was mined.

A few rocks sparkled in the torch light. We coud see markings of where a tool scratched the rock.

Hunter found a nugget of gold ore.

It was still embedded inside of one of these side passages walls. It was the size of an apple.

He began to chip away at the rock.

Small chips of stone fell to the ground around him, some larger chunks snapped off.

He finally pulled it free and a section of large stones underneath the ore cracked and each one hit the floor with a heavy thud.

The ground shook. There was a rumbling and great cracks of stone snapping beneath our feet.

We fell ten feet into another tunnel below.

We survived without any major injury however our flashlight did break in the fall.

We landed in the dark.

Thankfully Hunter had a spare flashlight and after a few minutes of scrambling around in the dark we found it.

The stones were cold and sharp. The air was damp and moisture clung to the walls. We were in a deeper part of the mineshaft now.       

We tried to climb out but our hands and feet found no purchase on the wet stones.

The tunnel wall was more narrow where we had fallen.

We walked single file towards a section of tunnel that opened up to our left. Hunter held the flashlight now and led the way.

We came across a sudden, steep drop.

A deep cavern had been mined into the earth itself with wooden supports and ladders crossing the gap in several places.

Even with our torch light we couldnt see the bottom. Wooden supports and ladders could be seen crossing the empty pit bellow.

Hunter threw a stone and we leaned in to listen. We never heard it hit the ground.

I did keep listening and I thought I heard a familar sound.

A repetative striking of metal on stone.

Hunter must've noticed it too for he called out in a shaky voice. Echoing off the walls of the pit.

"Hello?"

No reply ever came. We didn't hear the noise any more either.

We soon decided to explore the tunnel in the opposite direction. As we walked Thea asked a question.

She asked if either of us had any strange dreams recently.

She described having a dream similar to my own the last few nights. She recognised the sound from the cavern as the same she could hear from under her bedroom floor.

Hunter said he had a strange dream too however it wss different from ours.

He told us he would find himself very tired. His arms and legs were moving without his control. He was holding something heavy. He couldn't see in his dream.

He was exhausted. He wanted to rest. His muscles burned with exertion and his throat was so dry it felt like sand paper.

He told us he woke up crying each night.

The conversation faded.. We listened to the echo of footsteps and the sound of our own breathing.

We came to an intersection of our tunnel and another. One section had caved in leaving only one direction for us to go

We went left. The tunnel twisted and turned in winding paths so we could never see the path very far ahead of us.

Hunter turned a corner ahead of us and froze. Thea almost walked into him and staggered backwards.

Shaking torchlight revealed a tall figure standing just beyond the bend in the tunnel.

He was pale. Long black hair covered his face. His shoulders were broad and in his hands he held an old pickaxe. He took one step forward and raised it high above his head.

Hunter didn't move.

I can still hear the disgusting crunch as his skull gave way under the blow. I saw the tip of the pickaxe protruding from the back of his skul.

Thea began to scream. Maybe I was the one screaming. I don't remember.

The flashlight fell to the floor and turned off. Darkness consumed the tunnel and a wet thud followed as hunters body landed beside it.

I didn't move. It had all been over in a span of ten seconds. I must have stood there for hours before a single word was uttered from the dark in front of me.

The voice was low and guttural. The tunnel around us trembled as his command hung in the air.

"Leave"

I ran.

I couldn't see. My friend was dead and I ran. I crashed agasint the sharp stones in the wall and felt them cut into me. I didn't care. I had to get away.

I reached the intersection. I bagan clawing at the dirt blocking the other path.

It was only then I noticed Thea had followed and was digging next to me.

After a few minutes, maybe an hour of digging I broke through and felt a breeze. Widening the gap slightly the air whistled through the tunnel.

There was an exit through there.

My arms ached. Each breath hurt my chest. I could feel the cuts on my arms and legs. I didn't dare stop.

We broke through to the otherside. I got through first and didn't wait for Thea. I felt my way along the walls trying to find the exit.

After what felt like hours of searching I heard Thea's voice call out from somewhere in the dark

"I can see light over here"

I followed the sound of her voice and soon saw it. We escaped Wiseman mine. Me and Thea.

That was two months ago now.

Me and Thea didn't leave one anothers side for the first week. We sat in silence often. I liked having the company.

Eventually we had to get back to our lives.

Thea told me she was going to stay with family. She asked what I was going to do.

I told her I was doing to the same to make her feel better. She didn't smile but her shoulders relaxed.

I haven't heard from her in weeks.

I found her mom's phone number online and after passing her interogation to prove I knew her daughter she told me Thea left a few days ago in a hurry.

I asked where she was going. She told me when Thea had left she mentioned getting a call.

I asked her who it was from. She said she didn't know. Thea had only said,

"He called out to me. I have to go help him."

She asked me if Thea was okay.

I told her she was but I don't think she belived me.

At night I have that same dream. I wake up in my empty bedroom but now I hear a second person digging far below. Some nights I can hear a distant sobbing.

Last night I heard a third. She was sobbing too.

I have to go save my friends.
