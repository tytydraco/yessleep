October 8th 5070 - Captain’s Log - Captain Icarus Ludwig Campbell

It has been four years to the day since we set off from Terra 01. We have travelled roughly 11 billion light-years every year since we left. I know that sounds slow, but remember this ship is a research vessel, and once we reach void space, we can simply drift and then turn around again later.

I miss my husband, my Hyacinthus. I haven’t seen him face-to-face in so long, and I really miss him.

But he and I have set up a private channel on the Voyager Company communication hub network; the rest of the crew uses the communication hub for important work throughout most of the day, and so I have to message him early in the morning and late at night, whenever the others are asleep. Some days, we chat shit about nothing in particular, and sometimes we can’t help but burst into tears from the sound of each other's voices. In a roundabout way, it's good because I know he misses me just as much as I miss him; our relationship was a little rocky when I left, but we still love each other.

I didn’t tell the others I was doing this when I started. I’m sure that everyone, other than maybe Echo, knows now. I don’t really know why I didn’t just ask for permission to use the communication hub, but it is a personal weakness of mine to ask for forgiveness later when I could have just asked in the first place.

This morning, I woke up, got out of my unkempt single bed, got dressed in my uniform, grabbed my cane in case my leg acted up again, and went to the communications hub. I started a private communication with Hyacinthus. It took him a few minutes to join, but I remember that when I woke up, I was tense, like I was carrying the weight of this whole ship, and when I heard him say “Hello?” I suddenly realised I was just tired, I hadn’t slept well because I never really do, I’m more of a night person. I guess the only way I could tell it was night or day was that the lights automatically dimmed for 12 hours when it was ‘nighttime’. His voice let me just be tired, instead of feeling the burden of having to be stressed first thing in the morning because of yesterday's stress.

“Hi, can you hear me, love?” I said. “Yeah, I can hear you, morning.”

“What day is it down there? I know the date, but I can’t remember what day of the week it works out as being,” I continued.

“It’s Thursday today, I’m at work right now, so I’ll have to be quick, baby”

“Okay, that’s fine, I just really miss you, seen that it’s the anniversary of when I left”

“I miss you so much, but Brutus just stepped out his office, so I'm going to have to talk to you later, okay baby? love you”

“Oh, okay, well, I’ll try get in touch with you later, send a message to the computer whenever you're done with work, I’ll try keep track of the time as best I can myself. Love you, honey, bye”

He hung up at that point. I had been standing still for a few minutes and could feel the nerves in my leg beginning to seize, it was more like pins and needles then a total shut down of mobility in my leg, I like to call this level of seizing ‘itchy bones’ because that’s what it feels like, the itchy feeling is below the skin and all around the bones that make my knee able to move on my right leg in particular, despite this though I was still mobile enough to make my way to the storage room, I grabbed a box off a low shelf and took a small plastic container out of the box, inside the container was a greyish browny sludge. “Yummy”, I said as Echo came into the storage room after me, her bright and warm expression and her delightful smile betrayed by the bags under her eyes. If she had learned anything from me, it was her terrible sleep schedule, and if she had learned anything from Athena, it was ‘work work work’.

She laughed like how one laughs when a co-worker makes a joke that is actually vaguely humorous, but you're too tired, and you haven’t had enough to eat, and you haven’t had any coffee. “I know, right? Just what I dreamed of having this morning”, she said in her smiley, bright and kind of awkward way. She, being only 5 foot 1, struggled to reach a box on the higher shelf. While she did so, I had a glance at the packaging on the plastic container - ‘eat this so you don’t starve in space’.

I had a little chuckle to myself like a weirdo and then pointed at a box right in front of Echo, “You do realise that you could just grab the box right in front of you on the lower shelf if you can’t reach the higher one, the high one also isn’t even opened” I said, trying to sound like I’m giving professional advice rather than sounding like I’m in forming a child that they could do something more easily, even though I felt like I was doing the latter.

“Oh fuck yeah, you’re right”, she said, clearly trying to hide her embarrassment as she looked inside the box and pulled out a plastic container that was the same as the one in my hand. I was just glad it wasn’t Athena who saw Echo in this situation because she would have made some shitty remark about her intelligence.

“Are you wanting a coffee?” I asked, seeing the tiredness on her face, as it was clear that neither of us was fully awake, and we knew that we had almost to fuck all to do today.

“Yeah, I’ll heat up the food”, she replied. We made our way to the common room; it was like a normal canteen you might find in a factory, but with exactly enough La-Z-Boy chairs in a semi-circle around a single CRT TV. It was a shithole, but it served its purpose. Eventually, the coffee machine spat out the coffee, and the microwave turned the cold sludge into hotter sludge.

“Thanks, Captain”, Echo said as I handed her the coffee mug. I just nodded at her and sat down on the La-Z-Boy, but didn’t turn on the TV. No one has ever watched anything on the TV.

“How’s your leg doing today, Captain?” Echo asked, her true empathy and care desperately trying to push through, but offset by her attempting to come across as casual and not too pushy when talking about it. However, before I could answer, Hermes walked in and said, “Honestly, are you going to ask him that every day? His leg has only stopped working twice in 4 years”.

“It’s still very nice of you to ask about it, Echo, but Hermes is right, I’m fine, promise,” I replied as the caffeine finally hit my system. “Have you had your meds yet this morning, Icarus?” Echo questioned with the most authority she could muster, which wasn’t much, but on that subject in particular, she would always sound more demanding. “No… I’ll go get them… now,” I responded, quickly drinking half my coffee and then fully downing the rest, burning the roof of my mouth. “You're supposed to have most of them first thing in the morning!” I heard Echo yell faintly through the wall as I left the room, and the door automatically closed behind me.

I was speed-walking down the hallway, quickly arriving at my room. The plaque on my door reads ‘captain’s quarters’ even though the inside of the room was exactly the same as everyone else’s, minus our personal effects. I grabbed bottles of pills, and once I had a handful of antidepressants, I threw them into my mouth, and then I made my way over to the sink, which, due to the size of the rooms, stood no more than 3 feet away from my bed. I leaned down to drink from the tap to wash them all down. I then grabbed a cream-like medical solution from the sink counter top and applied it to my radiation scars that cover my hands, some that run across my arms, and then the smaller ones that mark my face.

I looked at myself. The ME thing. My skin is pale in most places except for my scars, which are like large red boils. Looking at the small bubbles of skin on my face makes the larger ones on my hands feel like they’re burning all over again. I must have been staring at my own blue eyes for a few minutes. I threw water in my face, and then said to myself, “You look like shit”.

I went back to the common room. At this point, the rest of the crew sat round a table, all five of them eating the same sludge from the boxes in the storage room, except for Echo, who had seemingly finished her food and now had her glasses on and was reading the ingredients on the plastic container, not for any particular reason other than perhaps to stave of the boredom. “How are you feeling now, Captain?” Echo asked, looking past her glasses at me, but before I could speak, Hermes said, “Yeah, Captain, you're finally fit to do your job” he began to mumble then and continued by saying, “Some leader you’ve been”. “Hermes, lay off, will you? The ship is on course, and nothing is broken, and the ship isn’t on fire. So, as far as I can tell, we’re on the right course, and Icarus is doing his job perfectly fine” Sisyphus spoke up in my defence as I sat down at the table and took a plastic spork from the pile someone had thrown on the table and then took a bite of my now lukewarm sludge, it tasted like wet bread that had just slightly began to mold over. It was shit.

“This isn’t a question I ask to be rude or mean, BUT, Icarus, do you really think I couldn’t do your job?” Hermes said. I put my spork down, if I had to be honest, really honest in the deepest part of my soul. I wanted to put my spork through his eye, but then I realised I still hadn’t addressed Echo’s question. She was still looking at me as if she wanted an answer, or at least that she was waiting for me to react to the conversation as a whole.

“I’m feeling much better, thank you, Echo”.

I haven’t felt ‘much better’ in 10 years, but I didn’t want to stick my head in the oven, and that’s what she was really asking me. She didn’t really respond. I had given her the correct answer, I didn’t burst into tears or flames, and so she didn’t really have to say anything more; at least it was one step more than people asking me ‘how are you doing?’ and expecting nothing in return other than ‘fine thanks’ because they couldn’t REALLY care less if they tried. She at least could care less.

“And Herm, I think you could give it your best shot, you're the smartest man I know, no offence Sisyphus, but what that means is you're a doctor, and you're a genius, but you also sometimes forget how the fucking coffee maker works, it’s just not something you think about because you’ve got two billion other thoughts and that’s fine but I don’t have to think so much, I just do shit” I continued and Hermes and Athena looked at me like I was a freak.

“Are you saying you're important because you're an idiot?” Athena asked, not getting the point of my argument. “No, my point is you guys have amazing ideas, but I’m a more practical person”

“As much as I enjoy you guys bickering, are we gonna get any work done today, or…?” Artemis interrupted, and no one really said anything. I finished my food and stood up from the table. I got an energy drink called Voyager Fuel soda from the fridge, sat down on the La-Z-Boy and said, “You guys enjoy making your reports all day”, and waved my hand dismissively. They all shuffled through to the communications hub and spent the rest of the day in there, reporting to Brutus about their ‘great experience on the voyage’ and ‘all the amazing research about the edges of the known universe’. When they weren’t doing that, they would send emails back and forth, write reports and logs, and make diagrams and presentations. Mostly about other crew members' attitudes or perceived mental health, or just about how none of us really like, or dislike, each other all that much, but we all just like talking shit because it gives us something to be interested in, even if that means causing drama on purpose.

I woke up after hours of napping, sometime after the rest of the crew had shuffled off to bed. I made my way down the hall to the communications hub, found the private channel on the computer, and after a few minutes…

“Hello?” I could tell he was almost asleep. “Hey babe, how was your day at work?” I asked in response as I sat on the floor in the small room filled with old computers and thousands of wires that weaved over each other all around me. “It was fine, I’m just really tired…” he said before yawning, which made me yawn too. “What did you do today?” he continued, and I paused for a moment. I almost didn’t want to tell him that, as he had been busy working all day, I had spent all day sitting asleep in front of a TV that wasn’t even on. “Oh yeah, I mean, I wasn’t really up too much, I was asleep in the common room most of the day”. I gave an embarrassed laugh before hearing him say, “Did you take your meds? Did you eat?” “Yeah, babe, don’t worry, Echo reminded me to take my meds”, I responded, ignoring the second half of his question. “And did you eat?” he asked again. “I had breakfast this morning, yeah”, I tried to say as if he wouldn’t notice that I didn’t mention lunch or dinner. He took a deep breath and said, “yeah fine, just please try and eat more tomorrow, and those fucking energy drinks don’t count, dummy.”

“Hey you don’t have to be mean about it,” I said with a smile before we both gave a breathy giggle. After a moment of silence, that almost became an awkward silence, he broke the silence by saying, “Right well, I’m going to sleep now, don’t stay up too long”. “Yeah okay, I’m gonna get something to eat, if only to appease you, and then I’m gonna work out in the gym, and hopefully not wake up Hermes by dropping the bar again, hahaha… then go to bed… good night, love”

“Night”, he responded before he ended the communication.
