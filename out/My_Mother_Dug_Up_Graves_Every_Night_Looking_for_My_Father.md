“I am Maria. I watched my mother lose her mind, slowly — but to this day, I still don’t know if it was her who was going mad… or me.”

My name is Maria Brown. I am fifteen years old. I live with my mother, Catherine Brown. My story is probably one of the most terrifying experiences any human being could ever go through.

About two years ago, my father, David Brown, was kidnapped — and he never came back. The police closed the case, claiming there was no evidence of a kidnapping. His camp was discovered deep in the Arizona wilderness, and it was assumed that wild animals had killed him. The investigation was officially shut down.

For two years, everyone believed my father had fallen victim to wild animals.

Everyone… except my mother.

My mother knew something that perhaps no one else did. She told the police that my father hated forests. He was terrified of darkness and wilderness. So how could his camp possibly have been found deep inside the Arizona woods? The police had no answer for that. But they didn’t investigate further. The case was already closed.

That broke my mother.

The woman whose laughter once filled our home with warmth now carried a permanent sadness on her face. I watched her slowly drift from light into darkness. She cut herself off completely from friends and relatives. She would spend hours staring at my father’s photograph, waiting endlessly for a phone call that never came.

But the waiting only grew longer.

She knew sitting still would change nothing, so she chased every lead, knocked on every door, searched for every possible clue. But she found nothing.

My mother became like a living corpse. She stopped talking to people. Eventually, she even stopped talking to me.

And yet, I was still happy that she was with me.

What I didn’t know was that this was only the beginning. The real storm had not arrived yet.

Then came the dreams.

A terrible series of nights that would destroy everything.

One night, while I was asleep in my room, I heard crying coming from my mother’s room. I slowly walked toward her door. As I got closer, I realized she was talking to someone between her sobs.

The door was locked.

I tried peeking through the window.

And what I saw froze the blood inside my body.

My mother was sitting on the floor in the middle of the room. My father’s old shirts and clothes were scattered around her in a strange circle. She moved her hands through the air as if she were touching someone’s face.

But nobody was there.

There was only an empty corner of the room.

Yet my mother’s voice sounded as though she were begging a real person.

“David… why are you wet? Why is dirt falling from your clothes? What happened to you, David? Where are you, David?”

She kept muttering with her eyes closed.

And then, right there on the floor… she fell asleep.

That was the first incident.

But it was far from the last.

It was only the beginning.

Every third night, my father began appearing in her dreams. My mother became convinced that he was trying to tell her something.

One night she dreamed that he spoke to her directly. He told her that human beings never truly die. The bodies buried in graves only appear dead because evil forces imprison them there, trapping them in an endless sleep.

The same thing, he claimed, had happened to him.

A spirit had trapped him inside some grave somewhere, and even he didn’t know where he was. He told her that if she performed exorcisms on buried corpses, the evil spirits would flee — and as they escaped, they would reveal where he was hidden.

After that, she started muttering those things even during the day. She pretended everything was normal, but the truth was something far darker.

I still remember that horrifying night.

It was around two in the morning when I heard the front door open. I stepped outside my room and saw my mother walking toward the forest with a flashlight in her hand.

I wanted to call out to her.

But I couldn’t.

I couldn’t bear seeing my mother in that condition anymore. She had never gone out alone at night before. Never.

For the first time, I followed her.

She disappeared far ahead of me into the darkness. Soon, I couldn’t see her anywhere. I called out to her several times, but there was no answer.

Then suddenly…

I heard digging.

I followed the sound.

And there she was.

My mother was digging up an old grave with her bare hands. Her fingernails were torn apart. Blood ran down her fingers, but she didn’t seem to feel any pain. She kept whispering something under her breath.

I slowly stepped behind her.

My shadow fell across her body. The sound of my breathing echoed through the silent graveyard.

But my mother…

It was as if she no longer belonged to this world.

The pupils of her eyes had rolled upward.

I had never seen anything like it before.

I panicked and ran all the way back to my room.

Sleep never came to me that night.

After that night, everything changed.

My mother began digging up graves almost every month. She performed exorcisms, hoping that one day she would finally get answers.

I knew it was madness.

But I had already lost my father. I couldn’t lose my mother too.

If I reported her, if I called her insane, they would take her away from me. So I stayed beside her. And every day I kept asking myself the same question:

Was she truly losing her mind…

or was she seeing something the rest of us simply couldn’t?

I never told her that I knew what she did at night. I was afraid that if she found out, things would become even worse.

And honestly…

her madness terrified me too.

Our house stood at the edge of town. A few miles away was a church where her friends and relatives gathered every Sunday. I didn’t want her to disappear completely into darkness. I wanted to pull her back toward the light.

But she never spoke to anyone there.

She would simply stand behind the church for hours, staring at the graves.

Eventually, even our relatives distanced themselves from her.

And then I stopped taking her there at all.

Slowly, the thing I feared most finally happened.

At school, people whispered that I was the daughter of a mad woman.

I decided I would stop going to school.

But my mother didn’t seem to care anymore. She was lost inside her own world.

And then things became worse.

Rumors spread through the town that someone was digging up graves at night. During the day, graves were found destroyed. Corpses were left exposed. People became terrified.

No one knew who was responsible.

Eventually, the entire town agreed that every grave would remain locked until the culprit was found.

That only made my mother’s obsession worse.

The dreams never stopped. But now she could no longer reach the graves.

One night, she went out again.

That night, I didn’t follow her.

I thought the graves were locked, so she would return home after a few hours.

But she never came back.

Two days passed.

I called the police. They began searching for her.

And then something strange happened.

Deep in the Arizona wilderness, they found another camp.

My mother Catherine’s camp.

Her name was added to the missing persons list.

Just like my father… she was gone.

I was left alone.

Both my parents had disappeared from my life.

Soon after, I was sent to an orphanage, where I began living with several other girls.

Eventually, I made friends there. I started laughing again. Playing again. For a while, I believed my childhood had finally returned.

But I was wrong.

The storm I thought had passed was coming back.

The same dreams that had consumed my mother began coming to me.

The madness started pulling me toward it too.

I began feeling the urge to dig up graves. Sometimes I would sit beside them for hours, staring silently.

But I told no one.

I refused to let people believe I was losing my mind too.

But then…

one day, something happened that changed everything.
