My neighbors smile too much. Like, way too much. And not in the normal “you have to be friendly to your neighbor” type smile, just, weird. I moved in a couple weeks ago and ever since the first day, things have been really, really odd.

Before I moved, my life was honestly perfect. I had a girlfriend, a good paying job, and a decent apartment. My life was honestly going a lot better than I think anyone in my life expected it to go. Unfortunately, I’ve never been able to keep a job (or girlfriend for that matter) for longer than six months, I’ve always done everything at the last minute and just never cared about anything important. It’s truly a miracle I had that job for as long as I did. 

So, I don’t think anyone was surprised when I got that email saying I was being let go. It was also the last straw for my girlfriend. I mean, I knew the breakup was coming sooner or later but I guess I just wasn’t expecting that to be the straw that broke the camel's back. I wasn’t a good boyfriend. Never have been. Always lying about where I was going or where I was, brushing her off, constantly putting every burden I may have had onto her. I know that’s messed up so please, you don’t have to tell me. I couldn’t outrun karma for too long I suppose, so she kicked me out.

Luckily my buddy from work took pity on me and decided to let me crash on his couch until I could find a new job. The poor guy never liked me so I don’t know why he decided to be nice now. But nonetheless, I'm grateful.

After endless nights of searching through linkedin and other crappy job finding sites, I found an offering for a shitty position at a mall clearly on its last legs. The pay wasn’t good and it’d be a hell of a drop from my previous office job, but I didn’t have that much of a choice. So, I sent in my resume and scheduled an interview.

There was no wonder why the mall had seemed like it was barely holding on. It was clear there weren’t many customers and the budget was probably less than half of what it should’ve been. It sat on the outskirts of town, situated on a road that wasn’t exactly known for its stellar traffic and the closest place to it was a gas station equally as troubled with customers. 

The parking lot had more cars than I thought it would’ve, maybe ¼ of the way full. Though if I had to guess it was probably mostly the workers. I parked near the front and made my way inside, glancing at the email to make sure I was on time and went to the right place.

The store was a dingy little vintage record shop located in the very back corner of the mall. It looked more run down than the shops that were indefinitely closed, but the flickering sign that read “Open” was enough for me to know I *was* in fact in the right place.

 The store smelled of old dusty books and cigarette smoke and the place looked like it had been coming apart since the 90’s. Standing at the counter was a guy that looked to be around my age, maybe mid 20s if that. Not quite the type of person I'd expect to be working here but honestly I was in no place to judge.

The interview went fine, it seemed like he was just asking questions for the routine of it all and less because he cared. He told me I was pretty much hired and I could start soon which I was super happy about, there’s no way I could keep crashing on my friends couch for much longer. I told him that. He asked why I would want this job and I was honest. That I got fired, kicked out, needed a place to stay, etc. That’s when he went from the laid back, happy guy to being anxious and almost restless, looking around and shrugging a lot. He told me about an apartment complex not too far from the mall but also close enough to town that you’re not in the middle of nowhere. Honestly, with how he described it, it seemed like the ideal place. Cheap, close to everything, and supposedly the people living there seemed content. He also mentioned that he’d pay for my rent until I get stable in this job as long as I work when he needed me too. It seemed like an absolute no brainer at the time.

So I did something stupid. I took him up on the offer and next thing I knew I was moving in. 

The apartment was pretty spacious. A lot more spacious than what was listed. But, I wasn’t gonna argue. It was a pretty easy move considering I didn’t have much to move in the first place. The only issue I had was my next door neighbor. He was normal at first, asked my name and why I moved here, the normal stuff. Then he just got… weird. I don’t really know how to explain it exactly but he just seemed off. For starters he was always smiling, and not in the way you would just to be polite and tell the other person you’re listening. His smile was off, I still can’t place why but it made my blood run cold and my nervous system go into fight or flight. The first night I spiraled about it, I didn’t even know why I was so shaky and hyperventilating, like my body was aware of something my brain wasn’t.

The next day I was outside my apartment doing a piss poor job at trying to make my door a little less depressing and I saw that some of my other neighbors had stepped out and it looked like they were watching me. I gave them that tight lipped smile you do when you have to acknowledge you see someone.

The second I did that I felt my stomach twisting again and my paranoia heightening, i felt like everyone in the building was looking at me. And those creepy smiles they all had stuck to their faces. They had them too. 

There was one person who seemed to be normal in the whole building, though. It was some 30 year old guy I had met while doing laundry. He never once gave me that odd feeling and he had even mentioned himself about how odd everyone was. Which sent a wave of relief over me. Finally someone normal.

While we were talking he had mentioned how he was planning on inviting his neighbors over for dinner so he could get a better read on what was happening. I don’t really know why but I felt in my gut that wasn’t a good idea, but he was having none of it. So, he did exactly that.

I was pacing the hallway. 9pm. He was supposed to be done by 7. He wasn’t answering his phone and his door hadn’t opened in hours, not even when the neighbors were supposed to leave. I knew I should’ve listened to my gut. But, for some reason, that twisting feeling was gone. All I felt was an odd serenity I haven’t felt since I moved in. I couldn’t wait anymore, something was going on and I just couldn’t wait. 

I opened the door, which I didn’t even expect to open. The apartment looked still, like there hadn't been any movement for a long, long time. It was eerily empty too, no couch, no tv, no pictures or car keys to signify anyone lived here. It truly looked like no one lived here. My mind spun, none of this made any sense. Where was he? Why wasn't he home and why did it look like no one even lives here.

I left after I looked through the entire apartment. This had to have been some horribly sick prank or something. Nothing made sense and for some reason I didn’t like how I couldn’t feel that horrible feeling anymore. I thought about staying up and trying to find him or an answer but for some reason, I just got into bed and fell asleep.

The next morning was worse. Everything was fine and that made me sick. Nothing out of the normal, nothing out of place. Like no one noticed the absence of the most sociable resident. What made my stomach churn though, was that everyone was normal. Too normal. No more weird smiles, no bad feeling. Just, normal. But it wasn’t. Not really. I’m not sure it ever will be.

Honestly, I only wrote this so I could tell someone and tell myself I’m not crazy.
