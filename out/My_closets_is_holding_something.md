I didn’t think anything of it at the time.

It was a normal day. School was boring, the bus ride home smelled like old fries, and I remember being annoyed about a math test I probably failed. My mom was working late, so the house was empty when I got back. That wasn’t unusual. I microwaved something, watched TV for a bit, then went upstairs earlier than I usually do because I was more tired than I should have been.

I got into bed and fell asleep almost immediately.

I woke up because something felt wrong.

Not loud or sudden. Just wrong. My room was dark except for the faint orange light coming through the blinds from the streetlight outside. Everything looked normal at first. My desk, my chair, my posters. Nothing out of place.

Then I noticed my closet door was open.

I always keep it closed. I am completely sure about that.

I didn’t move. I just stared at it, trying to tell myself I must have forgotten. Maybe I left it open earlier. Maybe it just drifted open. I was still half asleep, so I tried to brush it off.

Then I saw him.

There was someone standing inside my closet.

At first it didn’t register. It just looked like a darker shape inside the darkness. But the longer I stared, the clearer it got. A person. Too tall to be one of my coats or anything hanging there. Completely still.

Facing me.

Watching me.

I felt this drop in my stomach that didn’t go away. My whole body went stiff without me even deciding to do it. The only thought in my head was not to react.

If I didn’t move, maybe he would think I was still asleep.

Very slowly, I closed my eyes. I tried to keep my breathing steady, but it wasn’t. It sounded too loud to me, uneven and shaky. I was sure he could hear it.

I stayed like that, just lying there, pretending.

Then I heard something shift.

It was soft, like fabric brushing against wood.

He moved.

Every instinct I had was telling me to open my eyes or get up and run, but I couldn’t. I felt completely stuck, like moving would make things worse.

Then I heard a step.

Slow. Careful.

Closer.

The floorboard near my closet creaked. I know that sound. I step on that exact spot every day.

He was out of the closet.

I could feel it. That feeling when someone is too close to you, even if you can’t see them. It was that, but worse. Heavy. Wrong.

Another step.

Closer to my bed.

I tried to stay completely still, but my fingers twitched just a little. It felt like I had just made the loudest noise possible.

The footsteps stopped.

Right next to my bed.

I stopped breathing.

The room went completely silent for a second, and then I heard it.

Breathing.

Not mine.

It was uneven and wet, right beside me.

Then I felt the mattress shift slightly. The edge of my bed dipped down like someone had just sat on it.

He was sitting next to me.

I didn’t move. I didn’t open my eyes. I just stayed there, hoping that if I kept pretending, it would stop.

The breathing got closer. I could feel it on my face.

Then I heard a voice right next to my ear.

“You weren’t supposed to see me.”

I almost reacted. I don’t know how I didn’t. I just kept my eyes shut as tight as I could.

There was a pause.

“That makes it harder.”

Something touched my blanket and slowly dragged upward toward my chest.

I couldn’t take it anymore.

I opened my eyes.

There was nothing there.

My room looked completely normal. The closet door was closed. No one was in the room. I sat up and checked everywhere, even inside the closet. Nothing.

I didn’t sleep for the rest of the night.

I tried to convince myself it was a nightmare or sleep paralysis or something like that. It didn’t feel like one, but I didn’t have a better explanation.

This morning, when I got up, my closet door was open again.

And on the inside of it, scratched into the wood deep enough that I could feel it with my fingers, were words that were not there before.

*Next time, keep your eyes closed*
