I know I'm gonna sound crazy but trust me, this is true.

Everything started a week ago. It was a Monday, the day after my birthday.

I woke up late. That should've been expected when you had been out partying till midnight. I could still smell the booze on my breath.

With bleary eyes, I walked into the bathroom, already thinking of what excuse to give my boss for showing up late.

I began brushing my teeth and my eyes  wandered up to the bathroom mirror. Blonde shaggy hair, tired blue eyes, a subtle hint of a beard. I probably should shave soon. 

My reflection was the same as ever but something about it felt off. Something didn't quite add up. Maybe I'm still drunk.

I jumped in the shower, shaved my stubble, threw on my work clothes and was out by 9:15am. I had to clock in at 9.00am. Oh, well.

___________________________________________

After getting a mild scolding from my boss, i collapsed onto my desk.

"Party boy's late", my best friend and coworker, Luke, chimed from the opposite desk.

"Why are you so happy today? You don't even look like you spent the weekend partying"

"Hush hush, that's a trade secret. Now go do your work, you slacker", he winked.

"I'm late just one day and now I'm a slacker? Pray tell, what should I call you then?"

"I can't see you, I can't hear you", his voice came from his desk.

With a sigh, I began my work. I really do hate Mondays.

The day rolled out monotonously. At lunch, Luke asked me when we were going for the suit fitting.

Luke's older brother was getting married in a few weeks and we were both groomsmen.

"What about Wednesday? We have the afternoon off that day", I said.

"Perfect", Luke grinned.
_________________________________________

It happened for the first time on Wednesday at the fitting.

I was trying on my custom-made navy blue suit. I was looking at my reflection when it rippled? Like when you throw a stone into still water.

"What the?"

That wasn't normal. I blinked hard, hoping I imagined it. The mirror was back to normal.

"Noah? You done trying out the suit?" Luke called out.

"I'll be right out", I yelled back.

I didn't want to be here anymore.

Weekend came around fast. Saturday was my designated lazy day. So when I was woken up around 3am on Saturday, my mood was already sour. 

Something had fallen off in my bathroom. I assumed it was my dog, Max.

"Max! Come here, boy!", I called out.

Suddenly it dawned on me that Max was at the vet. Had been there since Thursday. So then, who was in my bathroom ? A burglar?

I took out the baseball bat from under my bed and slowly made my way to the bathroom.

The lights were off. Tightening the hold on my bat, I switched on the lights. The bathroom was empty. I couldn't even figure out where the noise was from as nothing was out of place.

"Look at the mirror", a voice whispered in my head.

I had been kind of avoiding looking at mirrors since the fitting room incident. Something made me uneasy around them now.

I slowly raised my eyes to the mirror. It wasn't rippling. Still, I got the feeling that something was off.

I leaned a little bit closer, though something in the back of my mind was telling me to stop and run far away.

I ran my eyes over my reflection. There, the tiny scar that I have on my right jaw was on the wrong side.

I slowly raised my hand to my right jaw running my finger over the scar. 

The thing inside the mirror realised its mistake. It stopped mimicking me.

"I'm coming for you, Noah", the thing said.

The rest is a blur. I remember smashing the mirror and running out of the house with my car keys and phone. 

Currently I'm in a Walmart parking lot, hiding in the backseat of my car. I broke my sideview mirrors when it also started rippling.

I can see a slight reflection of mine in the phone screen but it's not a mirror, right? Any advice on dealing with this?
