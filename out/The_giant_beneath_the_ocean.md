Before laying on this unknown little island with nothing to eat but the clothes on my back, the sand touching my chest, and a coconut, I was sailing near an archipelago around South-East Asia. My boat was named the S.S. Demetri. After yours truly of course. It was a white beauty, with a mass that can rival the titanic. A significantly smaller version of the titanic with golden crests at the sides of the exterior.

 It was also a bargain, a working ship that only costs fifteen hundred million dollars is a steal in my book. This ship will soon be irrelevant and destroyed completely, but I would like to boast of its grandiose nature before I pass through the gate.

 I will tell you how I ended up here but to understand this fully we must first speak of the crazed obsession I had developed in my younger years. I am, or was, quite a wealthy fellow. I came from a highly established line of businessmen that made monopolies every where they went. The screwdriver, the screwdriver cocktail, the chair, iced tea, the hit board game monopoly, slavery, and more inventions that no number can quantify.

 All the works of my family’s numerous escapades dating back to the origin of money, though, you will never know it was us at all. Back in the 18^(th) century Sir Isaac Carigon, my great, great, great, grandfather, decided to divide the estate into different fake names and puppet individuals to keep our family’s power hidden.

He was so thorough with this process that even his name was changed to the name you see here, his relatives soon followed in his practice. Thus began our secretive nature, though no one in my family knew why he had done this for Sir Isaac disappeared without a trace.

Yes, my family was never poor, never once struggled. This consumed me, the mystery of it. It led me to places and individuals that could have held the answer though none had succeeded. The private records were of no use, my oldest relatives held no answers, the libraries were empty. This journey of mine was not fruitful in my early days, so I retreated to my solace and occupied myself in passionless passions.

However, the point on which my family acquired this vast fortune, the mystery that has plagued me for years, will soon be known to me in the form of a painting from the damned.

After my grandfather had passed, he left me a ten-foot by ten-foot canvas with the portrait of a hooded figure sailing in the river Styx. What surrounded him was a bout of light, fading green ghouls whose skin was of the thinnest fabric. If I leaned closer, I could hear the souls of the damned yelling and screaming with their raspy voices. Release me, they said, release me. That was what I did in a way.

I hired restorers to rejuvenate the piece to it’s former glory, reviving the colors and cleaning the dust. I had had a buyer, someone that would show it off in an art gallery somewhere in Paris. I wanted to share the ghost with others. Then, in the light of the full moon while I was lighting my cigar, I heard whispers. Release me, release me, they said. Quietly creeping to the painting, I stood in front of it. It was as if the wretched things were priming to eat me. I came closer and closer until the end of my cigar was touching the surface. My burning, smoking cigar.

Light came instantly as if a lightning bolt struck beneath my feet. I jumped back and fell with me on the floor, slowly smoking away, like the painting. Though it wasn’t smoking away but burning away. Orange-yellow flames climbed up the portrait inch by inch. Leaving blackened patches. It climbed and climbed and climbed. Until it reached the ceiling.

The water sprinklers activated but it was no use. The flames spread and the ghouls set free, but they would not leave me. A blackened square was left from the canvas, and it fell to my feet making a quiet thumping sound. The fires raged and the smoke filled the room, but my eyes were locked in the black square.

I grasped it ignoring the burns on my fingertips and I dashed to the exit. Running across doors and hallways that served me no more, the ghost still followed me with the same fervor as death is to a bleeding solider in the battle of Verdun. I came out of my mansion, tripping onto the ground, again it was ignored as I crawled to the black square. It was an envelope, blackened by the flames. The contents of it were not harmed, though now, I wished it burned away just like the hooded man in the painting.

The first object was a marked map, barely holding on to it’s structure. Maddened ink drawings on the ocean and arrows pointing at a single island in the east. The second object was a cracked compass. It was not pointing to the North; however, it was pointing to something. I spun it, shacked it, and even tossed it across the captains quarters in my absolutely marvelous boat. It was only pointing to something. The final item, apart from dead cockroaches and dust bunnies, was a notebook, in it was the name Isaac Carigon. I now had the answer to my long-asked questions. All I needed now was a beautiful boat to sail in style and a crew to do all the seafaring work for me.

I precured all the things I needed for the journey. The boat, men, supplies, the usual. I was accompanied by three people. I am embarrassed and ashamed to admit it, but I didn’t bother remembering their names. So, I shall make some for them, my fallen men. There was Leo, the captain. He had a white snow beard and a gruff accent. He was riddled with wrinkles all across his face, especially on his forehead and at the sides of his eyes. He was also a pain in the ass.

He would always scream and yell around, throwing insults around like they were candy if a single mistake were to be made, but I’d rather take a pain in the ass than a complete moron. Which is exactly what Arnold is, a total dunce. Arnold was the first mate. He looked exactly like Santa Claus in the commercials but with brown hair instead of white. Now he did have the expertise but the basic common sense on this one was abysmal. I remember distinctly the time when Arnold tried to talk to me while I was reading Sir Isaac’s notes. He was waving both of his arms yelling.

“Watch out!”

I was not a big fan of motion pictures, especially one with the title “out”, so I shooed him away. Then immediately after, I was hit with a wooden plank. The idiot should’ve warned me. After him was the second mate. He was a jolly fellow. He was a bit gaunt with bags that stretched to his cheekbones but don’t let his ghastly appearance fool you, he is a sweetheart.

 One time he gave me a sweet little doughnut with black icing on top and eccentric pill shaped gummies with skull marks on them. I ate the whole thing without a second thought, and he went off stomping on the floor, eager to get me another one. What a jolly lad, let’s call him Mikey. Yes, yes, it was all quaint for the few days with this crew. Sunshine, cool breezes, the scenery, all very nice. It was a shame, the things that happened to each of them, I could only imagine.

We followed the compass as per Isaac’s instructions, following the same exact course annotated by the map. What came from this path was a tall order of waves, waves that rose to the size of mountains and plummeted to the depths of caves. It was one of many trials that would slowly, inch by inch, reveal the leviathan that we would soon meet.

Let me get more thorough with the storm that we faced in our journey. What I believe to be the catalyst for my own debacle. The ship rocked back and forth in a rather unrhythmic fashion. The boat would tilt just slightly to the right before careening to the left, almost flipping the entire vessel. Chairs would be on the ceiling before smashing onto the floor breaking a leg piece or two, tables would collide onto the wall leaving an indent that would grow deeper and deeper, and people would get hurt believe it or not.

I got stabbed by a wayward fork. Thankfully it was only on my shin instead of my other, more essential body parts, though the limp didn’t help me at all. Arnold and Leo were in peak condition, without any scratches or marks on them, the lucky bastards. But poor Mikey got the shortest straw. He was throwing nets around the boat for more food before the storm started and he didn’t make it to the cabin on time. Flung like a half-eaten apple he was.

When the storm calmed itself, we found him floating on the ocean. Though his body wasn’t half sunken in the water. It almost looked like he was on the water like he was laying on a tiled floor that moved up and down in random intervals. We picked him up, pale as a white stallion and just as heavy, veins popping out and blackened like jellyfish tentacles were inside him. Eyes glazed and wide open. He couldn’t talk or move but his heart was beating.

After settling on him to his quarters we decided to discuss our next move. We went on up to the captains quarters, silent to the thick atmosphere that had developed. I could hear the faint moaning of the ship. It’s rustic scratches sounded like a whale call.

“We have to turn around” Arnold said with his arms crossed and his mouth frowning.

“This whole trip is just a bad thing waiting to happen, I can feel it. Mickey doesn’t look like he can hold on much longer and I am not willing to sail with a dead man.”

“We came all this way Arnold! I am not going to cancel this entire expedition just because of a little accident! He will be fine I can assure you that.” I replied.

“Little? This little accident gave Mickey a damn coma, heck maybe something worse than a coma! And you’re telling me that we should just sweep this under a rug and carry on. The man can’t even move his own toes let alone blink. I say we radio the mainland for an emergency rescue and come back more prepared. You know, with more food, equipment, and a damn doctor. And maybe some more information to go off of.”

“How much am I paying you again? Oh right! 120,000 dollars for your tight lips, your expertise, and your unquestioning obedience, not a bloody lecture. I’ve spent my entire life on this, and I will not let a moronic sailor like you stop me! Why don’t you ask your bedridden daughter to lie down and die, hmm. Why don’t you tell her that her pathetic dunce of a father couldn’t risk a few scratches to nurture her to good health- “

A thud came to my face as Arnold punched me on the tip of my nose. I went flying off of my chair and the back of my head slammed onto the floor with a big thud. Blood made a little streak on the side of my cheeks making them soaked with red.

“ENOUGH!” Leo slid his chair back and stood as tall as he could. His eyes glaring wide with red strings pointing at his pupil.

“Fightin about it ain’t gonna change nothin. Now Arnold why don’t ya be more useful fer once and stop it with the fist fightin. And you, Mr. Demitri Sir, why don’t ya fuck off and let the real professionals handle this situation. You’ll get your damn treasure well enough so sit tight and stop being a pain.”

“Were going with Arnold’s idea.”

“We will most certainly not!”

“I’d rather have an alive crew mate than a dead one Mr. Demitri. Now, unless Mickey gets up and about again, we are going straight to the mainland. Then you can do whatever you want from there. Now I don’t know what your playin at Mr. Demitri, but that storm was one hell of a surprise, and I hope there aren’t any more of those out and about”

There was a pause. Leo was still glaring at me, staring into my eyes while I squeezed my nose to stop the bleeding. Arnold stood there glaring at me. His fists are clenching red.

I stood up and walked all the way into my room. Fuming at the time, pacing around thinking of the nastiest thing I could do to these gentlemen. Buy them out or set up a competitor that could overshadow them completely or something ridiculous like that. I am embarrassed to say but it is true, I acted like a child. My ego blinded me. I see that now. Tapping these keys with my dry tongue on this even drier island really gives you a sense of humility.

I went to sleep after that skirmish. With two large tissues shoved inside my nostrils, they were soaked red by the time I woke up. It was 4:00 A.M. in the morning. No matter how hard I tried I just couldn't sleep. The rocking of the waves and the sweet song of the boat had given me this sense of rhythm. Like a humming that floated into my eardrums. It became louder and louder as the minutes passed. So, I got up and investigated the cold, rust filled hallways of the ship with only the yellow, white light that shined in these oblong shaped light bulbs.

When I passed Mickeys room I felt a wetness on my bare feet, deep enough to make a splash. Mickey’s door was the rustiest out of all the doors in the ship. It was leaking black, viscous liquid in small little droplets, like a sauna. It had an oily texture, the liquid. Staining my palms with an inky black, though it had a salty taste to it, a sort of umami flavor more akin to soy sauce but it was also quite bitter, enough to make a grown man crinkle his face.

A little malfunction I thought, just a ship thing that happens a lot.

Something to do with the engine or the exhaust pipe or the motor. I decided to walk into the canteen, that was before the creak of the door hinges. The door swung in front of me inch by inch, completely hiding the creature that owned that room. Sweat dripped on my temples, shaking grew on my forearms. The hallway grew longer and the door just there, existing in it’s putrid manner. Then just like the door, a monster showed it’s head, inch by inch.

First it was the black hair that looked like seaweed on a stick. Then it was the white, featureless forehead, no wrinkles or indentions of any kind. Then it was the eyes. Deeper, blacker, and more sunken than any ocean. It was as if he had stood sideways to greet me in that manner. It was then that I felt an overwhelming collage of spectacle that I had once observed but was now more clear in my mind. The little bumps and cracks of the metallic, dirty orange walls, the wet black substance that went up to the soles of my feet, the door crying it’s midnight tears, and the man that peaked it’s weary head on this full moon night. 

“Hello there Demitri. How are you in this fine evening?”

The voice was both deep and high pitched at the same time. Two voices layered on top of each other.

“It appears you have made a terrible, almost comically terrible mistake.”

“In this infinite canvas of blue I implore you to even once think of the consequences.”

“Many have died seeking me and yet you chose to tread on my domain like your forefathers.”

“Had the dreams been too much? Those visions of gold coins that stretched far as the eye could see.”

“Were they too enticing for you? They were for your folks.”

“All of your folks.”

“Though, now it seems that they are not interested in anything else.”

“You will have to pay the price now.”

“Pay the tab for the thousands that haven’t.”

“Or maybe you will grow the sense to turn back.”

“Only Malahet knows of your fate now.”

“But not to threat for I shall grant you the water that will quench your thirst.”

“Just know that you will never be thirsty again.”

Ever again, no matter the time nor the place nor the people. There will be no end to your suffocation. The thought wriggled inside of my brain forcing everything else to push to the sides to make room.

Then the door swung even more. Revealing the proportions of the being that had talked to me. It’s nose stretched itself to the door frame, so did the ears. Leaving a stretched contour. Though the lines that may have been his mouth reached the corners of his eyes. He was smiling. Then he showed his teeth to me, almost brandishing the thought. Millions of prickly spikes crammed together, some were poking outward and inward, stabbing the man’s face, the thing’s face. It looked painful.

“The bell tolls, it tolls for thee.”

Inch by inch just like how it entered. Slamming the door back to its closed position. Then it swung inward into the room. Out came Mickey, dressed up looking less pale than the state we found him in. He looked at me and I looked at him. I stared at him to be more specific. My eyes widened by the encounter I had. He gave me a toothy smile before walking away.

Business as usual. Everyone was at their workstations doing what they should. Mickey on the sails, Leo steering the ship, Arnold inspecting the engines from our previous excursion, and me plotting the course. Although the tension in the air thickened a lot more. Glances from both Leo and Arnold have been burning on my back. Every time I passed by them, they would lock onto me with these sunken eyes and these furrowed expressions, their eyebrows compressed in some sort of focus.

All of this was soon buried under the rug within a few weeks of time. The sea was grueling after all; it demanded all their attention. This was when we heard the sound. Like a thousand flutes shouting at the sky. I was in my room looking at the map and it’s contents. These crude illustrations of hurricanes were here exactly where the storm was. Two more remained, and we were heading towards one, a fish. We were headed towards a lone fish. Pathetic looking on paper but looks can be deceiving. My bed began to shake and so was I. To the point where if I looked at the map the continents and islands would merge into a single blur.

As soon as the rattling stopped, I walked to the deck, each step with careful consideration. It was pitching black outside. No stars showed their face. The only light available came from the ship like a single orange ember in a cold charcoal pit. Arnold and Leo came out of their places and looked around, eyebrows raised and eyes darting, whispers of damn and what the hell came out of their mouths. Except for Mickey, he seemed to be all peachy. A light toothless smile was slapped on his face, but it didn’t reach his eyes.

The water was still. No amount of rocking from the boat and splashing from the nets made a difference. It was as if we were frozen in place. Bubbles emerged from the water. Out came a fish head with all white eyes and opened mouth. Then another, and another, and another. A school of fishes all dropped dead and rotting in this dark, cool place. Despite these conditions the wind was strong enough to blow the sails. We were moving, at least we hoped so.

Leo came to me, feet stomping at the wooden floor with a thud. He grabbed my shirt and pulled me to his face.

“YOU FUCKING MANIAC, WHAT HAVE YOU GOT US INTO!” He said holding a harpoon gun in his other hand. He was absolutely ready to gouge my organs out of me. It was a good thing he was a sensible man.

“You’re going to tell me everything you know about this expedition of yours or I’ll turn you into chum you lousy posh fuck.”

We huddled up in the captains room, and I explained everything without a single drop of information excluded. From my younger notes to the journal of Isaac Arigon or whatever his real name is.

Their faces grew tense and slumped. Never did they expect something like this to occur in their entire lives and yet they have been granted passage to it. All their life choices lead to this very job. They may not have agreed with that statement but subconsciously they knew.

A pause in silence, taking up all the cold and wetness of this place, we have found ourselves in. There was no sight of Mikey anywhere on the deck.

“That book” Leo said.

“That book you have, does it say anything about any of this, any way to get out of here?”

“No, most of the note’s excerpts were on the storm not whatever this is”

“M-maybe there’s more stuff in there that you skimmed over. A piece that’ll give us a clue.” Arnold said.

“I’m sorry but I’m pretty sure there’s nothing.”

“WELL BE MORE FUCKING SURE! I have a goddamn family you asshole, and I am not going to die in this shit hole you put us in.”

A screech rocked and vibrated the whole ship. Moving it side to side. Leo looked at the darkness squinting at the little pieces of light that surrounded us a moment later. It was as if the stars were hurtling towards us. Shambling would be the right word though. Those things were shambling towards us almost as if this was the first time they ever walked on two feet. Some three feet.

These things, creatures, myths, whatever you may call them. Slime like fluids made their skin glisten in the light of their bulbous antennas. White as snow but not as concrete. Webbed appendages gave them uneven strides but that didn’t affect their speed. Sickly but starving, that is the most accurate depiction of these trolls, these abominations to the very definition of beauty. We were to face these creatures.

They came smashing the undercarriage of the ship, pounding their massive tendrils. Every smack was a mini earthquake. My mind panicked and I turned towards the others for any sort of assistance, a hint as to what to do. They were just as clueless as I am. Then came an idea. These fish men aren’t invincible, are they?

There was no time for doubts, or mishaps, or anything of that nature. The harpoon guns will have to do. I ran towards them with the balance and grace of a polar bear on a small melting ice cap. I fell to the ground and that caught the attention of the others. They knew exactly what I was going for. Soon Leo manned the harpoon gun and launched it at the biggest one. Maybe killing the boss would quell the horde but alas it was of no use. The spearhead was absorbed by the creature immediately and was soon part of it’s mass, slowly melting into the beast. It’s arms morphed into a sharp crude stick and soon the vessel was being punctured with holes that sank the ship by inches and inches. Slowly but surely.

“Now you listen here and you listen good.” Arnold said, slamming me into a wall, a small pocketknife pointed at my esophagus.

“You’re going down to your room and your getting your notebook. Understand me.”

“If I don’t find you with it, I’m going to decapitate you and I’m going to feed your head to those things.”

That was that. I went on descending the steps into the lower levels of the ship with Arnold looking over me. Then he was gone and so was the light. There were no emergency lights in the lower parts of the ship, someone must have tampered with the generator. It was like stepping into a block of black that absorbed any and all light. Thankfully Arnold tossed me a torch.

Soon I walked along the rusty corridors of the labyrinth. Shaking and pulsating all around me. To hell with the notebook, I thought. I had poured hours into deciphering it and Arnold had the gull to question my expertise. I know damn well there was no getting out of this. So, I simply waited in the nearest room I could find. Which was the generator room. A long room that stretched to the end of the ship that encompassed ten machine-like structures that now stood still. The light from my torch reflecting the griminess back at me. This was the place that I would die in. My grave. I welcomed it with open arms. At least I’ll die pursuing my lifelong goal. My family mystery.

Water flooded the ships interior now. I’ll be the first to die. That was what I thought. A noise emanated at the other end of the room. I pointed my torch at it and low and behold, there was Mikey.

Black like tentacles underneath the skin writhed and wiggled like large muscle fibers. They stretched the skin leaving reddish marks beside the black gooey lines. His pupils were dilated. Almost covering all of the white except the small little corners.

“Hello there Demitri!” With the same echoing voice. Only this time it was as if a symphony of people were talking at the same time.

“The time is due now.”

“P-please, I’m begging you. Spare me!”

“Your time is nigh; it has been for generations.”

Water began to fill the room. Rushing in large thrashes like a river after a tsunami. It was at the level of my knee. Then my pelvis, and then my temple. It then reached the ceiling of the room. Small bubbles were escaping my mouth in small groups. My body floated upwards, but Mikey wasn’t affected at all. He was still standing on the floor without any show of struggle. Merely staring at me with those large eyes of his. I wonder if he was still conscious. That the entity that clung to him like a parasite only took control of the body moving parts of his brain and not the thinking parts. Maybe he felt the pain of a thousand ropes slithering in his muscles making him say and do things he’d rather not.

I lost consciousness for a while. When I awoke, I came face to face with an army of those fishmen. All surrounding a giant. An obese, naked giant. I was facing the many folds of his back while the fire beneath his cooking pot provided orange light across this cavern. It was as if I was facing a mountain.

“Why hello there Demitri.” Shaking came around making stalactites fall into the massive crowd and even stabbing the back of the giant, though they were like small water droplets to him.

“Oh, drat I forgot my inside voice, pardon me.” He was still focused on his cooking pot.

“What do you want from me?”

“It’s not what I want from you, you silly little gremlin you, I am simply taking what had already promised me long ago “

“THAT WAS ISAAC-”

“Now, now, no need to fuss. Your uncle was a tricky fellow; he covered his tracks well but know that he did make a deal.”

“To give me the feed for my broods, my children. Oh, they’ve grown quite hungry since then. I’m getting quite hungry myself” He stopped stirring his pot and grabbed a few creatures from the crowd and tossing into the stew. He then scooped a spoonful and gave it a little taste.

“Needs a little bit more meat. Well, help yourselves boys, unlike those two scamps we found, this one won’t go bad for a while.”

Now it puts us here. To this very moment. After the skirmish I was carried to an island with a singular coconut tree for comfort. It took a while for my head to regenerate itself, so did my shoulders. Then my legs. The cycle continued for what felt like centuries, millennia. I forgot my name all together for a while before remembering it after the twenty trillionth seventy-five millionth five hundredth seventy fifth time. Then I lost it for a while after the next cycle, and the next, and the next. A circle of remembering and forgetting.

Now it is the year seventy-five thousand ac, after a coconut fell from the coconut tree, and now the big giant thing has given me a gift. A thin machine that folds like a book but has a mirror that tells you everything. The people in the machine call it a laptop but I much prefer the book of infinite knowledge. Now I spend my centuries typing and searching away. I tried calling for help, but none arrived. So that’s that I suppose. Do you have any questions?
