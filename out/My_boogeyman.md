Hola!

you'll have to excuse my English, fue mucho años aprender inglés. But I will try my best.

If you ever heard of American stories in the mountains, you will hear alot about rules of what not to do. Leaving your windows open at night, don't whistle as you hike, never follow voices when you are alone. All of them gain a reputation for a reason. Being ignorant, especially in your home will find yourself in despair. What my mother will always say to me. ""En esta casa no entra nada que no sea amor y luz"

During my time in Guatemala, I lived in a small village that doesn't compare to city life in America. Hot, humid, desert, and Tropical. I would play in my grandmother's yard as the chickens stampede across the open land. my Uncle playing his guitar next to empty bottles of beer the desolate sun beaming down his tan skin as he looks zombiefied from his last Boracho moment. Next to him were a wide field pigs laying in there own filth by our outhouse. The cows lining by the pasture as my dog bites their ankles to make formation.

I would fill the trough as well as the rest of the animals. 

My chores seem endless from sweeping, bring fresh water from the creek, espically making sure the hamacas, and chairs were tied and folded. I was yelled at by my grandmother to  make sure they were always done when no one is sitting or laying. As I finish my chores,  I set myself out to play with my friends and my dog Chispa. 

As I darted to the fence making a trail of dirt rise, my Uncle yelled out "slow down!" making the deck dirty again. My grandmother stopped me.

"Mijo! stop!" 

I paused at the gate.

"Abeula I finish my chores and I ready to go play" 

"I am telling you this because you have been reckless on putting things away. the broom was on the floor last night. you need to place it behind the door!"

"Why? its just a broom. why do we have to do any of these things. we sit on chairs and lay on the hamacas everyday. why not just leave them there."

"you don't know who you invite into this home Mijo. whether its people with bad intentions or worse."

My grandmother always had something when it came to superstitions. she would tell me any story about a man riding his horse in the dead of night. checking houses, seeing if the lights are on, taking things or placing themselves like home. dawning a a large hat. you can hear the horse clopping but leaving no trail. 

I nodded and rolled my eyes as I focus more on playing with my friends and my dog. I related the stories to my friends. we all laugh about as we just find it as reasons to clean up the house. Trying to make ourselves presentable in the middle of nowhere.

As night draw near I stayed a little to late outside as the sun was already peaked on the mountain side. I was a couple kilometers away from home. passing roads and bridges. I finally stop at a intersection of the road. There was a viewpoint on a hill i saw from below. 

A short man riding his horse stopping to see the villiage.

I try to close my eyes for a better view but his silhouette stayed the same oddly enough his sombrero was larger than any other typical ones usually the larger ones are for traveling for more shading but now and days people drive old pickups and bikes.. At first I thought it was just another man just finding new places to explorer. I've never seen. anyone up on that hill. from what everyone says, its better to stay near the village than going forward towards the mountain jungle.

As I make my way back home, I couldn't stop but notice he was looking at me. I wonder if I was just the only one outside besides other people at home getting ready to head inside. I walk faster as my Chispa kept up with my pace finally making it home. 

As the stars and the moon illuminate this desert. I laid by the yard watching the wind pushing the palm tree, hear a ripen coconut cracked like an egg spewing its juice on to the dirt. The hot air surrounding me starts to dwindle as a shiver begins to erupt in me. I spotted it.

along a tall wall that shields my grandmother yard shows a tall figure with a large sombrero slowly guiding along. from the it seems, the figure was shrouded in a cloak that seems to reveal a dark cloud keeping its shape as it sways within itself. 

a sudden bark can be heard from behind me, I scramble out of my comfort falling a foot off it. Chispa darting towards the wall following the shape as it reaches the yard. I ran inside as I scream. "Chispa! Vengar aqui!" Her dedication was more dominant than her flight response.

I immediately ran inside hoping she would scare him off slamming the door. I can feel the strong winds fighting againts my own strength, keeping the door shut. it finally stopped, i listen as the wind slowly calmed itself. A eery guitar can be played. so peaceful, yet so fiendish, it played for while as it muffled the sound of Chispa. it was long after I heard a sudden yelp. 

I stayed behind the door, a tear running down my cheek as I hear sound of the devilish guitar slowly fading. 

As I awoke from my frantic episode last night, I was laying by my bedside. I felt like a dream until I look on my bedside. Chispa wasn't there. 

I walked out of my room as I see my grandmother sweeping the deck. she looks at me with great disappointment. The Hamaca was taken down, I see my Uncle taking it with him to a small burn pit near the pig farm. 

I watch as he sets it ablaze, both of us staring at the flame. I can hear my Uncle finally saying one weird that I finally understand what came to visit.
