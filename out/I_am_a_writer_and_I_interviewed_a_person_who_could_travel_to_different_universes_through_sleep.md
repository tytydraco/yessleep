It was a hot summer day. The sun had been shining since morning. There wasn't a single cloud, so the apartment was stuffy. I was sitting in the kitchen at a large table. The kitchen was spacious. Two journalists and a psychologist were also sitting at the kitchen table. We were all waiting for the guy who had invited us; the housekeeper had let us into the apartment.

"Are you a writer?" one of the journalists suddenly asked me.
"Yes," I replied. "Just a beginner so far."
"My name is Nikolai Olegovich. But you can just call me Nikolai," Nikolai extended his hand. We shook hands. Then the second journalist offered his hand.
"Marat Alexandrovich. And what's your name?" he asked, shaking my hand.
"Alexander Yuryevich," I answered.
"What do you write about?" Nikolai asked me.
"I'm trying my hand at science fiction for now. And you?"
"I write articles for the newspaper 'White Truth'," replied Nikolai.
"And I write articles for the magazine 'Fantastika'," said Marat.
"How soon is the guy supposed to come? What's his name?" asked Nikolai. The psychologist looked at the time.
"In about 5 minutes. I think his name is Maxim Mikhailovich," answered the psychologist.

We began to wait for Maxim Mikhailovich. A few minutes later, the front door opened and a tall guy, about 18-19 years old, entered the apartment. We couldn't see his face. He took off his sneakers, put on slippers, and came into the kitchen. When I saw his face, I was very surprised. He looked like he hadn't slept for a week. He had dark blue bags under his eyes. His hair was disheveled.

"Greetings," he said to us. We all said hello. "Give me a minute."

After these words, he walked over to the kitchen cabinet and took out a pack of some pills. Then he swallowed about 5 or 6 of them. After that, the guy made himself some coffee and asked if we wanted any, but we declined. The guy sat down opposite us.

"So, let's begin," he said. He raised his mug and took a sip. "My name is Maxim, you can just call me Max. I'm 18 years old."

I took a notebook from my backpack and started writing everything down; the journalists and psychologist also began taking notes.

"I don't know how to explain this. But please, listen to my story. It all started a week ago and hasn't happened again since. I live alone in a rented apartment. So, no one could stop this madness," Max began to tell. "It was an ordinary day, and when night came, I went to sleep. That evening, I just went to bed, and the next morning I woke up. At first, it seemed like something had changed, but I didn't pay it much mind. I lived a simple life, soon I met a girl and married her. A few years later, we had our first child..."

"Are you telling us about a dream?" the Psychologist interrupted Max.

"Let me finish, then you'll all understand," the guy replied to the question.
"Alright," said the Psychologist.

"We had a son, then about two years later, we had a daughter. I lived a wonderful life with my family. The years flew by quickly. I remember my daughter graduating from college, and my son and his wife giving me grandchildren. I became a grandfather. I turned 70, my health deteriorated, and soon I was bedridden. I kept getting worse and worse. I realized I was dying. I died surrounded by my relatives: my wife, children, grandchildren, cousins, sisters, and nephews. I remember closing my eyes and hearing a woman's voice: 'He's awake! He's awake! Get the IV ready!'"

"I opened my eyes. I was lying on a bed in a medical ward, nurses were running around setting up an IV for me. Most importantly, I noticed I was young again. I mumbled incoherently."

"'Where am I? Where is my family? What happened?'"

"'You were in a coma for 10 years, and you've just now woken up,' a nurse answered my question."

"I was in shock. I couldn't believe that my entire life was 10 years in a coma. None of it was real—no wife, no children, nothing. I had lost 10 years of my life. That's what I thought at that moment. Because of all this, I became extremely agitated; to calm me down, the nurses gave me a sedative, and I fell asleep. Asleep again."

"When I woke up, I felt different. I opened my eyes and saw rough, gray walls and a window with bars in front of me. I was lying on a bed without a mattress. I looked around and realized I was in a prison. At first, I thought it was a dream, but everything felt so real that I started to go crazy. I tried to wake myself up, but it was useless; I remained in prison. I couldn't understand what this was or how it happened. There was a small mirror hanging on the wall. I walked over to it and saw myself. Myself, but looking about forty years old."

"I spent the whole day in that cell thinking about what was happening. I noticed that no one checked on me. That was strange too. So, in complete solitude, I sat there until nightfall, and then I fell asleep."

"I woke up, or rather came to, on some kind of flying thing. A guy was sitting next to me, apparently piloting it. We were flying between skyscrapers that looked like they were from a cyberpunk world. We were being chased; some people were following us and shooting lasers."

"'Oh, you're awake?' the guy addressed me. 'How are you?'"

"I didn't answer him. Suddenly, a laser hit us, and we crashed into a skyscraper. I came to in a forest—no, they were jungles. I was bare-chested and covered in various tattoos. I was wearing pants made from the skin of some animal, and there were human scalps hanging from the pants! And a bloody tomahawk. I was walking next to some Indian who looked like me. He also had a few scalps. We were walking. But I didn't understand where, and I also didn't understand what this was? What is happening?"

"Soon, we reached an Indian settlement. He went into his wigwam, but I just stood there. All of this was new to me. I stood like that for a few minutes, and people started looking at me strangely."

"'Swift Wolf, what's wrong with you?' one of the Indians came up to me. I understood him."
"'E-everything's fine,' I replied, stuttering."
"'Why isn't Swift Wolf going to his wigwam?' he asked. My nerves couldn't take it anymore, and I dashed into the jungle."

"I ran through thick undergrowth, just running. The Indians didn't seem to be chasing me, but I kept running. I tripped, fell, ran. Suddenly, I unexpectedly ran out onto a large cliff and couldn't stop. The drop was high, and I fell down. When I landed, I didn't feel any pain, I just lost consciousness."

"I came to in a small apartment, in a bed. I got up and saw, instead of my usual stomach, a huge belly. There was a mirror in the apartment. I walked over to it and looked into it. A man who looked like me, about 30 years old, was staring back. I was very fat, obese. I sat down and began to think about what was happening. In the end, I constructed this version: that most likely, when I go to sleep or die, I move between different universes where I am different people, and that my family was also just another universe. I assumed that in reality, I am 18 years old and I am still asleep. I began to wonder how to escape this anomaly, but I couldn't think of anything. And besides, in such a large body, I could hardly do anything. So, I waited until night. The time was 23:00. I put the phone in the pants (it was already there) that I was wearing when I went to sleep."

"I woke up. And the very first thing I noticed was long hair falling into my face. I was lying in a bed with flower patterns. There was a pink computer in the room, stuffed animals, and photos of Korean celebrities hanging on the walls. There was also a mirror in the room. I got up and looked into it. I had awakened in the body of a girl who resembled me. I was wearing pink pants and a t-shirt. In the pants, I found a phone—not a pink one, but the one I had put in my pocket when I was in the fat guy's body."

"I drew a conclusion: that some objects can also move with me. I decided to experiment and went to sleep with this phone. I woke up in some strange place, but I didn't pay attention to that. I started rummaging through my pockets and found that phone. I realized I definitely wasn't going crazy. I started taking photos and videos with this phone. So, I traveled to many different universes and recorded everything on this phone," Max took a small touchscreen phone out of his pocket. "And then one day, I woke up as myself, in my own body. Since then, I haven't gone to sleep, because I'm afraid it might happen again."

"Why are you so sure it wasn't a dream?" asked the psychologist.
"Because I felt everything. You can't feel that in a dream," answered Max. He handed us his phone. "Here. Look, here are all the videos and photos from different universes."

We took the phone and started scrolling through the photos. Each photo showed a person resembling Max, but it wasn't Max. There were many photos.
"This is photoshop," the Psychologist said sharply. The journalists and I were silent.
"No, it all really happened!" Max tried to prove.
"It's the age of technology, neural networks are everywhere. I can generate similar pictures with my own face," said the psychologist.
"But it's the truth!"
"Hmm, you said it all started a week ago. Please tell me, how much time passed in this universe from when you started your journey?" asked Marat.
"One night. In the evening when I went to sleep, I started moving, and it all ended in the morning," answered Max.
"So, if you fall asleep now, will you start traveling through universes again?" I asked.
"I think so," replied Max.
"Then let's conduct an experiment so you can prove it. We'll take your phone now, and return it to you this evening. You'll put it in your pocket and go to sleep, and we'll stay with you all night. If there are new photos on it in the morning, then we'll check it for you," suggested the psychologist.
"Alright," answered Max.

Everything was done as planned. The day passed quickly, and evening came. As agreed, we returned the phone to Max. He put it in his pocket and lay down on the bed. He fell asleep within a few minutes. We decided to stay awake all night.

"If we wake him up, will he stop traveling through universes?" asked Nikolai.
"I think this is all nonsense. The guy is clearly sick, that much is obvious," said the Psychologist.
"I don't know, but it's an interesting plot for a story," I said, checking what I had written down.
"And for an article too," said Marat.

We spent the night in quiet conversation like that, not even noticing when dawn began to break. When it was 10:00 AM, we ourselves were starting to fall asleep. Suddenly, Max woke up. He was drenched in sweat. Sleep immediately vanished for me, the psychologist, and the journalists.

"So?!" asked Nikolai.
"W-wait," Max said, breathing heavily. "Let me catch my breath."

After a couple of minutes, he spoke.
"I won't tell you anything. You'd better see for yourselves. And I realized it's all over." Saying this, Max took out the phone and handed it to us.

We turned it on and opened the gallery. There were thousands of different photos! The photos showed various people resembling Max. We were amazed; even the psychologist was surprised.

Now, I don't know what happened to Max, because I returned home to share this story with you.
