I’m a regular high school student, btw I’m from France.

I don’t really care much about school—just my thoughts and my sketches.

If I had to pick only one subject for the rest of my life, I’d choose Fine Arts.

But unfortunately, I can’t.

Art gives you solace, but it doesn’t really pay much.

My teacher, Ms. Elato, says I’m one of the best students in art.

She’s the only one who genuinely respects my work.

She’s also the only person I can talk to about my problems—she’s chill and she actually understands me.

Speaking of problems… I’m basically a problem for my parents.

My father is an engineer, my mother is an architect, so of course they want me to be one of their high-status prodigies.

But honestly? I think their jobs are soulless.

Sure, they pay well… whatever.

I don’t really know what I want to become anyway.

Who even knows that at 16?

I just know I have hobbies that give me peace—exploring museums, spending time in the library, sketching, making coffee, talking to my two only friends, Anne and Georgi…

and quietly admiring my crush, Liya.

She’s the only girl I really like, even though she doesn’t know I exist. But at least I get to see her every day.

So yeah—

This was actually a moment I could be proud of.

There was an art fest in our town, and I got selected in the top 10 students representing our school.

Ms. Elato had a lot of expectations from me.

The names were announced in the whole school.

Then we were called to the art department. We gathered there, and Ms. Elato, our team head, gave us cards with themes and emotions written on them.

We had to make sketches based on those.

I prayed for something interesting… and my card said “Introspection and Desire.”

I had no idea what the hell to make on that.

The only good part was—Liya was also representing our school.

And we got special permission to use the art lab and library 24/7 with no academic classes for the whole semester.

Dream come true.

We got two days off before the start.

I told Anne and Georgi about it.

Georgi got overly excited and said if he could help me, he’d be my “sub-partner”, which means he could come to school at night.

I asked why that was exciting and he said he’s really into horror, and our school area is near the Pic de Bugarach mountains.

He wanted to explore them at night—he’d heard all kinds of stories about the place.

Anne and I said no—it could be dangerous, and if we got caught, we might be suspended.

We said we’d discuss it later in the library.

Evening came—we all went home.

I told my mother about the fest.

She didn’t care at all, unless I clear a science bachelor entrance exam someday.

I slept like a rock.

I woke up at 2 AM.

I really love being awake at night—it's peaceful.

I lit a cigarette, sat by my window, and looked outside.

I even climbed out and sat on the ledge.

I could see the whole town—my favourite spot.

One wrong move and I could fall, but honestly… I didn’t really care.

There are very few people I live for.

I don’t believe anything is forever, and maybe that’s the blessing.

I went back to sleep.

Next day, I went to the library—it was closed.

That rarely happens.

Whatever. I didn’t need inspiration.

I walked to La Pareartoó, the old book market.

Books are cheap there, but I’m low-key allergic to dust.

I went into this old bookstore.

I wanted to give my sketches a thriller/psychological-horror touch, so I went to that section.

A man in front of me was returning a notebook.

He looked scared.

He said he got nightmares whenever he slept beside the book.

Kinda interesting.

I thought Georgi would love the story behind it.

So I decided to buy it.

The old lady packed my books, and when I asked for the price of the notebook, she said:

“It’s free. But please don’t return it.”

Weird as hell, but I took it.

I don’t believe in that stuff, but Georgi would.

His house is near mine, so I went to give it to him, but his mom said he was at school.

Right—he’s not part of the fest, so he had regular classes.

I wasn’t going to hand the notebook to his mom—what would I even say?

“This book gives nightmares”?

So I just took it home, kept it on my shelf, and went for lunch.

My mom asked why I was home; I told her about the break.

Later, I remembered I needed cat food for Bella.

I went to the pet shop, bought her treats, food, and a new red collar because it was on sale.

Came back home—exhausted.

Read some manga.

No clue when it became night.

Before sleeping, I set up my bookshelf and kept the notebook ready to give Georgi the next day.

I slept.

No nightmares.

Next day, I went to the Léoduv Museum.

I love the art there.

Every time I look at a painting, I find something new.

By sunset, I headed home and ran into Georgi and Anne.

We talked about school, and Georgi begged again to be my sub-partner so he could explore the mountain at night.

That’s when I remembered the notebook.

I told him about it and he got super excited.

He asked me to bring it tomorrow since we start fest preparations then.

I went home, packed my bag—art books, brushes, tools… and the notebook.

When I picked it up, I noticed something on the first page.

A sentence.

“How was your day?”

I froze.

Because I know that page was blank before.

No one in my house cares about my stuff, so who wrote it?

I ignored it and slept.

Next day I went to school, pretty sure Georgi just wanted the book for the thrill of exploring.

So yeah… I thought maybe I should make him my sub-partner.
