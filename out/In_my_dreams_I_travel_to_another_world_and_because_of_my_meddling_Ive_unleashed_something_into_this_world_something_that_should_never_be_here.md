No, its not "just a nightmare", and no, I'm not "being anxious and paranoid for no reason". I've been told these phrases before, and they do NOT describe what I've seen.

Every night for the past week, in my dreams, there is a place. A place I liked to call my "dream world". It scenery was breathtaking, more beautiful than I've ever seen in waking, the "real world". I'd find myself in a meadow. Clear sunny skies, slow breeze that carried the fresh scent of spring, grass soft enough to sleep in, with flowers in bloom as far as the eye can see. It was tranquil in every sense of the word.

There was a building in the distance, a brooding ominous one, something that stuck out of the tranquility like a sore thumb. I tried to ignore it, believe me, I did. Yet as the days passed it kept gnawing at my conscious, even when I was awake. What was in the building? I had to see. So, on the sixth night of these consecutive dreams, I went there. Was it out of boredom? I had nothing else to do in the meadow. Maybe it was curiosity. As they say curiosity killed the cat. 

And kill the cat it did.

That was the single worst decision I have ever taken my entire life.

It took me maybe a couple minutes of walking to reach the building, I felt conflicted the entire journey, I felt I was going somewhere I wasn't meant to be. As I got closer, the building came into focus. It was a shed, something looking like a stereotypical red farm barn. Yet something was terribly wrong with it. I cannot accurately describe what I felt when I arrived at its door, but I will try.

Even though it was made of wood, touching it would leave rust on my fingertips. At the bottom oozed a red liquid I did not want to fathom the origin of. The grass near it was all yellow. All dead. The fragrant breeze now blew with the scent of what I can only describe as death. The skies turned black.

There was a small door to the side, a size perfectly designed to fit me. Made sense, it was my dream to begin with. Yet the stench emanating from the door was repulsive. It smelled of decay and rot, as if a thousand animals had died inside and left to be reclaimed by nature. How could something like this exist within the same tranquility outside? I had to know. I had to see what was inside and put a rest to this. Nothing was going to ruin this place.

I turned the doorknob, and without a single sound or any resistance the door opened, as if it was beckoning me in. And so enter I did. The air inside felt as heavy as if a thousand oceans were pressing on my lungs, so cold I felt as if I would freeze if I stayed a second longer. In front of me was a dim hallway. I could barely see a couple feet from where I was standing. Beneath me were black and white checkered tiles. To my left and right, stone walls that felt as if they were going to crush me. They extended too far for me to see the ceiling, which was consumed by the blackness surrounding me.

I trudged onwards, my breath catching at even my own footsteps. Then I heard it. It wasn't a bloodcurdling screech, or a voice that knew me, but a whimper. The hallway went on for what seemed forever, far beyond the reaches of the barn that contained it. Each step, the whimpering grew louder, until I finally reached a window. The window was slightly ajar, with even colder air seeping through it. But what horrified me wasn't the window itself, but what I saw in it.

It was me.

No, It couldn't have been me, I was right here, not over there. That thing that looked like me, was tied to a metal chair, gagged and with some sort of contraption over the ears. It was breathing ragged breaths, the air from its nose being visible as it would on a snowy day. The rope looked as though it dug all the way to the bone, colored a deep red. I locked eyes with it, and suddenly its dead eyes filled with life. The worst kind life. Terror filled its eyes, as it violently convulsed in its constraints, its head vigorously shaking no telling me to leave. Fear glued my feet to where I stood. I couldn't move, not even a muscle. my eyes stayed locked with the thing, unable to look at anything else.

From the darkness, a hand emerged. A white dusty hand, with long slender fingers, which looked so brittle even the slightest force would snap them. It grabbed the thing. The convulsions from the lookalike became more violent, I could hear it trying to scream, yet the gag somehow prevented it. A sort of white powder poured from the hand, each movement from the thing causing the dust to pile even faster.

When everything finally went silent, the thing which looked like me, covered in white powder went limp. The hand retracted into the darkness and from the darkness emerged a face. A white, dusty face, similar to the hand. Its eyes with impossibly tiny pupils, abnormally large eyes, and a frown which seemed to contort its face to let it reach down much farther than it should be humanly possible.

Except it wasn't human. It began to move again, yet this time, I broke free from my paralysis.

I ran.

I ran faster than my legs could carry me. It felt as though I used every ounce of speed I would ever gain in my entire life channeled into my legs. I ran towards the door, without ever looking behind me and swung it open.

I woke up, covered in sweat. And white powder. My wrists, ankles, and neck had rope marks on them. Blistered in the same spots where the rope dug into the lookalike's flesh in the nightmare world.

It stalks me, it waits outside my house every night, and in the day, where it stood, there was an impossibly large pile of white powder where it stood. Nobody believes me. They can't see it, only the powder it leaves behind. They think I'm going insane. I'm not. Everyday it grows closer. Watching, waiting to see if I make a move. I don't know if it can be harmed, or even killed. I not alone anymore. I haven't been since I decided to venture into the building in the nightmare world.  
  
Somebody, please save me.
