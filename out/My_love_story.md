The oldest memory I can remember is of my mother holding me in her arms after my father left. The smell of that dingy apartment, the pattern of mold on the walls. She held me tightly, hiding her face away. I could hear her sobs, but I couldn’t do much to comfort her. I still remember her words, the way she cradled me as I wondered where Dad had gone. “Your father got bored of us, sweetheart.” Her voice never wavered. “But that’s what men do, honey. Only us women really show love.” She said it quickly before I could ask why. Her words lingered in my mind. “Do you hate Daddy?” I asked softly. “No, dear. I still love your father.” she said. I saw the truth in her eyes, the deep longing that had replaced her joy. “Then I’ll love him too, Mommy.” I said, trying my best to reassure her. She laughed softly.

As we grew up, we traveled a lot. My mother would disappear at night and come back with food or money in the morning. I was too young to ever realise what she was doing. She starved herself thin back then, wanting to feed me and clothe me the best she could. One night she never came back. I assumed Daddy had come back for her. The police found me wandering the streets. I was hungry. They put me into a care home, but no one ever wanted to adopt me.

I grew up there, going to school. I found it hard to make friends. They’d ask about me, but then they wouldn’t want to hear the answers. So I decided to watch instead. No one ever noticed me. But there was one boy, Roy. He was nice to me. He would sit with me at lunch and talk to me through classes. He had glasses and always seemed a bit nerdy. He would ramble on about Pokémon, but I was always happy to listen. We took the same bus together, and I couldn’t help but feel close to him. I wanted to know more about him. I wanted to know why he was so kind to me.

That day the teacher told us about Valentine’s Day. An entire day spent celebrating love. It seemed wonderful to me, and I had a particular boy in mind. I even baked cookies in preparation for the day. I put on my best clothes and looked for Roy. This was my chance. My opportunity to get to know him better. To be closer to him.

I gave him my cookies with a smile. I told him I had baked them myself, taking care to account for his nut allergy. At first he seemed overjoyed, and it made my heart feel fuzzy and warm. A bright blush filled my cheeks as I looked away. But that was all it took. Lucy had gotten chocolates for Roy too. I was sure Roy wouldn’t take them, but he did. Lucy pranced about, handing chocolates to all the boys, even flashing me a cheeky grin. I felt my hands ball into fists. Why would he do that to me?

I stormed off, and he stood there confused, as if he had no idea what he had done. Later that day I saw him dancing with Lucy in the gym. The music blared loudly as everyone seemingly had someone to dance with. I tried to hold back my tears. I decided I’d walk home from that day onwards

I remained isolated in school from that point on. Rumours spread about me, like I was some kind of psycho. I decided it wasn’t worth trying after that. I was too afraid to get hurt. I stayed to myself watching the way others seemed to turn on each other. It made me oddly happy when Roy began to fail his tests. Almost made it feel right. Like he got what he deserved.

Time has passed since then. I’m seventeen now. I’m still living in the care home. They said once I turn eighteen I’ll be free to leave. Honestly, I can’t wait. I’m tired of never having a place of my own. High school is boring. The teachers act like they understand us, but they don’t know anything. I normally hang around the library or the lunch hall. I have friends I eat with, but no one ever really pays me much attention.

The library is full of kids who think they’re edgy or unique. They make me laugh, if I’m being honest. Disrespect their games and they explode at you. But they don’t do anything about the constant bullying they seem to attract. The lunch halls are always jam-packed with people, no matter the time of day. Jocks, bullies, emos, nerds. You see it all. Each group inhabiting a table like it’s an island. Then there’s Alex. He’s unlike any other guy I’ve seen. The way he manages to saunter across all the tables, flashing a smile to almost everyone. His laugh is soft and joyous, the kind that’s hard to fake. Everyone likes Alex. I mean, what’s not to like?

One day I was running late for class. They change the locations almost every week. I ran through the empty hallways, they were littered with the new art departments posters. It’s hard to keep track of, and the teachers do nothing about it. They only care if you’re late. As I turned the corner quickly, I bumped right into Alex. The papers in my hands flew everywhere. I was about to curse the person who got in my way. Then I realised it was Alex and my face began to burn. “I’m—I’m so sorry.” I quickly dropped to my knees, frantically picking up my papers. I was so stupid. He must hate me. My thoughts ran wild. I felt my hands tremble.

But as I looked up, there he was, picking up the rest of the papers. My breath caught in my throat as he handed them to me with a smile. “Be more careful next time, yeah?” he said softly. “Y..yeah..of course..” I muttered, looking down at the ground as he walked away. A goofy grin filled my face, my face burned bright red. I stood there for a long while, the image of his smile seared into my mind. I knew I was going to be late, but I didn’t care anymore. I got to talk to Alex.

As I got to class, Mr Hendricks was quick to berate me. But my mind was too focused on the way Alex had helped me. No other boy had ever treated me so nicely. And his smile… oh his— “I take it you won’t be late again, Amy?” Mr Hendricks cut off my thoughts as I nodded quickly. I sat down at my desk, flipping through the pages as I continued to think about Alex. I couldn’t get him out of my head.

After class I began to ask around. I couldn’t help but be curious about Alex, and apparently his reputation was well known. A basketball player who seemingly gets along with everyone he meets, kind and empathetic. Everyone talked so highly about him, it fascinated me. I continued through the day thinking about him, running scenarios through my mind. How would he talk? Does he smile often? Would he remember someone as quiet as me?

The school days continued. My thoughts didn’t stop. At lunch I would sit with my friends, my gaze trailing to where Alex was. The noise of the cafeteria filled the air. My eyes almost naturally sought him out. At first no one really noticed. They were all too busy chatting away or eating too loudly to care. I doubt even Alex did. He seemed so confident, so brave. The way he would hold his fork in his left hand and the knife in his right. He was left handed. His smile never seemed to falter, and part of me wanted to see more of it. It felt harmless. I was just trying to get to know him. I didn’t want to make a bigger fool of myself than I already had.

I had heard from a friend that Alex would be going to the park after school that day. I went there early wanting to introduce myself as he passed. It was silly but the idea made my heart skip a beat. I had imagined it a thousand times, just a small hello. Just so that he knew I existed. As I got to the park I couldn’t help but feel a pit of nervousness in my stomach. What if he found me creepy? I was the schools loner. I got scared as I heard his friends chatter coming closer, I quickly jotted some words onto a note and placed it on the bench. A red ribbon attached to it. Just to hold it in place.

I watched as he came closer, I ran and hid wanting to watch his reaction. I just wanted him to know that I existed. He slowed and sat down on the bench, an involuntary smile filled my face. The breeze of the wind messed with my hair as the note shot up catching his eye. He picked it up slowly, he was actually reading it. He was holding onto something I had wrote. He began to look around and I quickly ducked my head away. It would be too embarrassing to be caught now. I could feel my face burning up as the birds began to chirp. It was a beautiful day and I had the best view.

The next day I had prepared more notes to give to Alex. I wanted him to guess who I was. A bit of a mystery admirer. Every note revealed another detail about me. At first it was small things, a note in his locker before lunch. I’d watch his expressions closely, the way he would crease the paper, the way he hid it from others. He seemed to find them special. I couldn’t help but fawn over the way he seemed to enjoy this mystery as much as I did.

As the days went on my desire to be closer to Alex grew. I had given him plenty of details already. My black hair, my height, the colour of my eyes… yet I didn’t know much about him. I mustered all the courage I could and decided to sit near his table at lunch, silently listening in on his conversations. His sweet voice practically carried across the lunch hall anyway. Was it so bad that I wanted to be a bit closer?

The more time that passed the more I felt my crush blossom. I was head over heels for Alex, I would blush at the thought of him. I’m sure he had a clear idea of me by now, how couldn’t he? Not after all the notes I left him.

I decided that night I would write him a letter filled with my feelings. I wanted him to find me. I wanted him to confront me. I needed him to be the one to do it. The ink smudged against my fingers. I was writing too fast. I had too much I wanted to jot down. The way I felt my blush rise at the thought of his smile. I crossed lines out, thinking they were too cringey. I sat there for a long while, wanting the letter to be perfect.

I put the letter on his desk, tied with a red ribbon. Just like my first ever note. I sat down at my desk as class started. I watched the way he carefully opened it. A smile filled his face and I knew it in my heart. He knew it was me. It was only a matter of time before he would approach me. After class I rushed to the toilet, fixing my hair. I was so nervous. The smell of lemon filled my nose. My mind raced with ideas about how he would approach me. Would he do it in front of others? What would people think?

But he didn’t do anything. He tucked the letter away into his bag and continued on with his day. Most people would get disheartened, even annoyed. But not me. I understood Alex better than anyone. I knew what made him smile, how he really felt. He must’ve gotten used to my notes, or maybe he didn’t have a chance to properly read it. I’m sure that if he had read it all he would’ve already rushed to me. But that’s okay, Alex. You can take as long as you need…

I continued to write him notes, though now they carried a bit more of an edge. “Why’d you not come to me?” I wrote on one. I stood to the side as I watched his expressions, a small frown filling his face. So he did want to see me. Perhaps he needed more clues. Silly Alex… just how clueless can you be?

That’s when it happened. I wrote another note like usual, only to see Alex crumple it up and throw it away. He didn’t even bother to glance at what I had written. I felt a pang deep in my chest. The bell rang loudly in my ears, but I didn’t move. Slowly I bent down and picked up the discarded note, tears brimming in my eyes. I had done something wrong. Did I come off as too demanding? Too pushy? Or maybe he didn’t—no… he wouldn’t have smiled if that was the case. He has to like me. He has to.

I couldn’t sleep that night. I continued to toss and turn, wondering where I had gone wrong. It had to be my fault. Alex was so kind. He would never purposely hurt me. I know him. Better than his so-called friends. All he really needs is me. Maybe they said something. Perhaps they saw something. Of course, it had to be them.

I made up my mind. I was going to win Alex back. I was going to fix this.

I began to watch his friends at lunch. The kind of jokes they would make, the way Alex would awkwardly laugh them off. I felt my grip tighten on my fork. He shouldn’t have to deal with that. Not someone like him. He deserves better. He deserves someone like me.

I decided to bake Alex some cookies. Mother always said the way to a man’s heart is through his stomach. I wrapped them up nicely and even wrote an apology letter. I wanted to explain to Alex that I wanted to be there for him. He could take as long as he wanted. I would wait for him. I left them both on his desk, a flutter in my heart as I imagined his reaction. He would probably have to hold himself back from thanking me.

I waited and watched. He eventually came, and this time he read the letter. I watched his expression carefully. He almost seemed lost in thought, but he didn’t throw it away. He was giving me another chance… this time I wouldn’t let him down. He took a bite of the cookie, a warm smile filling his face. He liked them. I felt a wave of relief rush through me. My shoulders relaxed as I let out a soft sigh. It wasn’t over.

Nothing could prepare me for the next day. There Alex stood, just in-front of his locker. But this time he was holding Sofia’s hand. Sofia always had been the queen bee, she had it all. Looks and a great personality. At least that’s what people saw, I knew her for who she really was. Just another two faced lying bitch. The worst part was she had her grubby hands all over my Alex. How could he do me like this? I thought he read my apology. Did he not like my cookies? 

I watched as they walked down the halls like some perfect couple laughing happily. It felt like I didn’t even exist. Why was Alex so quick to forget all the moments we shared together, all the notes I had given to him. The emotions I had poured into my letters. I felt my tears threatening to spill. My hands balled up on my sides.

He just needed to see me. To actually talk to me in real life. No more stupid notes. I’m sure he would realise how stupid he’s being once he actually talks to me. Yeah…he knows deep down I’m the better option. After all. I’m the one who knows him the most. I’ll prove it to him.

There was no more hiding for me. No more games I could play. Not if I wanted to lose the only thing that ever mattered to me. Alex. I didn’t want to end up alone like my mother unable to cope. That would be a fate worse than death. I needed him. And he needed me. We were perfect for each other. I’d make him see that.

Under the cover of night I went to Alex’s house. He kept a spare key under the flower pot. I bet Sofia didn’t know that. I slowly unlocked his front door. It creaked as I pushed it open. I closed it gently behind me, climbing up the stairs. I made my way into his room silently. I watched the soft rise and fall of his chest. His quiet breaths filling the air. He looked so peaceful, my sweet Alex. I made my way over to his bedside. I gently moved his hair to the side, my touch feather-light. “I’m here now,” I muttered quietly as I gently caressed his face.

No one would ever take Alex from me…

I’d make sure of it
