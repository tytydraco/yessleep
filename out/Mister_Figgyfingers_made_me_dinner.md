I'm sorry for leaving you waiting for so long. It's just... I've been busy. 

Well, its more like I've been putting it off to tell you the truth. This is where the worst of it happened. The parts that get a little muddier. From what I can remember.

It’s an odd thing. Staring death in the face and wondering how it will be in the end. Will it hurt?

The man looked like death. His grin was so full of giddy joy I could only believe that he’d crawled out from a black cloak and was sharpening his scythe for the kill.

Cody finally spoke, “What the fuck are you *talking about*?!” he screamed. “Take us back!”

Cody cried through his words. His leg was so swollen. So ruined.

*“Well yur in no fosition to ve goin anywhere! You are home, lil man! That leg’s gonna needa ve attended to sometime I reckin, dontcha think?”* He reached a big hand towards Cody.

“Don’t touch me! FUCK OFF, you pervert!” he spat weakly at the man and scrambled back, hitting his leg in the process and-

*(squealing)*

\-crying out again in pain, grabbing at his twisted and swollen leg.

The man’s expression changed at this and he recoiled. He lifted himself up to standing again and became very grim and serious, his voice lower now and commanding.

*“Now, I am no fervert. Don’tchou even sujest such thangs. That is no way to be talkin to Mister Figgyfingers when he tryna help yew now is it?!”*

“Why are we here?!” I yelled at him. “What do you want from us?”

His terrible eyes were on me now, dark and predatory. His mouth hanging open and his mangled jaw dripping steadily.

*“It ain’t what I want, son.”* He licked his gnarled lips and turned to the door.

I couldn’t tell what he was doing at first until I heard a crackle and a hum and yellow light began to pop on above the stained floor at the far end of the room.

One by one, those tube lights fastened to the ceiling began to turn on, illuminating what we’d been missing in the darkness.

Where the stains began, they branched off into heavier, darker splotches all the way to the concrete wall and a large wooden door cut into it. The door was blocked off by metal fencing a few feet out which went all the way from the ground to the ceiling on that side of the room, creating a sort of cage separating our section from whatever was past that door. It looked like a makeshift jail cell made with welded together rebar.

I heard a heavy cranking noise as the door began to lift upwards and stop. Revealing the black darkness beyond and the source of the smell. It poured out of the hole like hot breath and both Cody and I were overpowered by the stench of rot and shit.

And a noise.

*“It’s what the Figgies want…”*

When they finally emerged I thought I’d gone insane with fear. They were so enormous I thought I was seeing some sort of deformed monsters and only after a couple seconds did I realize what I was looking at.

Out of that cavernous door walked two gigantic pigs. My heart sank to the floor. The sheer size was unbelievable. They had to be four feet tall from the hooves to the top of the head and around seven feet long. They probably weighed as much as seven hundred pounds each from the look of them.

Their stomachs were these wide, bloated barrels sprouted with tough red hairs that were caked in mud and shit. Each step they took was like dropping a bowling ball on the floor. Their mouths had jagged tusks jutting out of the top and bottom jaws.

One was a little skinnier than the other and the bigger one nipped at it, making it squeal with surprise. They lifted their huge heads and sniffed the air, grunting and oinking. They snapped and rubbed their teeth together making a-

*Ccccccrrrrkkkk cccrrrkkkk*

\-and they smelled absolutely awful. Cody began to scream. I began to scream. The pigs squealed with hungry excitement when they finally noticed us. They began to pace heavily back and forth in the space of the cage and grunt loud and needily. The man began to laugh. 

“Wha– what the hell do you think you’re gonna do…?” I could hardly speak.

The man hunched down and made his way to the cage, stopping a few feet from the metal rebar.

*“Boyth, this is Hank and Jurgen. They my Figgies.”*

The bigger one began to ram into the rebar of the cage, hard. Making its nose bleed, but it just kept going, trying to get him. He pointed at the skinnier hog. 

*“As you see here, Jurgen aint doin so hot. Fig’s gotta eat, and mvy fingers can only do so much…”*

He stuck his last intact thumb out like a hitchhiker. He did it so fast I barely had time to gasp.

Once his thumb was past the bars the bigger pig latched on. The man screamed and screamed. The pig’s jaws working, chewing heavily at his thumb until he pulled hard enough that the joint gave away with a loud pop and he pulled his hand free. It was spilling blood like a faucet and as it hit the floor in gushes I realized where the other stains had come from. He held up what was left of the torn thumb, the ragged end of it nubbed off at the joint, and stuck it in his mouth like a kid who’s touched the hot stove.

He slurped and slobbered over his finger and spoke while he did in a garbled drooling splash of hardly intelligible words.

*“...Like fork chopsshh…”*

The giant hog behind him crunching and cracking down his severed thumb while the smaller one next to it settled to the floor and let out a long, hot breath of air like it was sighing in hunger pains.

My eyes were so blurry with tears and my chest was heaving with sobs as he made his way to our level again and crawled towards us.

He whispered grimly, *“I don’t think Jurgen’s gon be with ush too much longer at thish rate. Which one o’ yas is gonna be a good sshport and thake one for the team?”*

He glanced at me, then turned to Cody. The man made a sharp intake of breath and gestured towards Cody’s leg.

*“It’s sthwolled. Don’t know if I can save it. Maybe gotta cut it off.”*

He got up and looked both at us and past us to where I didn't know if he was talking to us or the pigs.

*"Fawell, Figgies. Hope you's eat good. Real good."*

He left without another word, slamming the door behind him. Leaving us with these horrible thoughts and his pigs. The big one just stared right at us, grunting slightly. The skinny one sat in that same position, breathing softly and ragged.

I thought he was going to feed us to his pigs.

But he didn’t.

He didn’t because days must’ve gone by. I don’t have a clue how long. The yellow lights were always on and I drifted in and out of consciousness. Delirium. I felt so weak, so thirsty and so hungry…

It felt like forever. Hours or weeks, I didn’t know. I feel like I tried to calm Cody but he was in hysterics after that. He wouldn’t talk to me. Crying and hyperventilating. Screaming looking at the pigs. The squealing and grunting felt like it was taking over our minds.

I tried to console him. I was so dizzy and my vision was so blurred. So much time went by that I don’t even remember. The man didn’t come back.

I wanted to get the door open but I was too weak. Everything was blurry. Everything stank. Everything burned in my lungs and I gagged without any food in my stomach to throw up.

I remember peeing in my hands and trying to drink it.

I remember-

*(squealing)*

\-screaming and crying and missing Cody.

Because Cody was gone. Cody was gone and so was his leg.

He stared at me with glazed over eyes and I could just hear his voice screaming at me to wake up and do something but…

But the door was locked. 

Cody stared at me and his leg was gone at the knee.

It wasn’t swollen anymore. A long red streak like a fountain painting the concrete floor and the pig growling. Grumbling.

The larger pig.

Hank was growling and grumbling because he was full.

Because the smaller one Jurgen was dead and Hank had eaten every last bit of him off his bones and then the bones too.

And I was crying and growling and grumbling too because I was full too.

I drifted in and out and someone was feeding me pork chops…right off the bone. And it was so good I ate all the bones too like a hungry little-

*(pig)*

*(Figgy)*

\-dog and it was so good it didn’t even need ketchup.

*…Cccrrruukkkkk ccrrrukkkkkk…*

And I was so full I knew just how to unlock the door’s silly little lock with its jagged keyhole cause old Jurgen’s tusk would fit right into that hole or maybe even this bone.

So I tried the knob.

And it wasn’t locked.

The door swung right open like it weighed nothing at all when it was so heavy before… I thought he…locked it…?

I looked up the flights and flights of stairs and back at Hank who stared, grunting. Fat as a tick.

“Was it unlocked the whole time?” I asked him.

“Always,” he grumbled back. “Will you please let me out? I’ve never seen the sky. I don’t think he’s coming back.”

I turned away. “Pigs can’t look at the sky,” I said and began to crawl my way up each step.

When I reached the top and pushed open another door I was in the woods again.

 So I kept crawling. Crawling on all fours through the woods like a pig. Until they found me rooting in the dirt by a tree and took me home. 

I am not the same.

To this day I don’t know where I was all that time, or where the man who kidnapped us went after leaving us there.

I don’t even know where all my fingers went.
