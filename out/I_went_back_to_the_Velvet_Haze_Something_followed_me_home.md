I posted [here ](https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/comments/1sq81dl/i_think_i_met_a_drag_queen_who_died_decades_ago/)a few days ago about what happened at the Velvet Haze.

I said I wouldn’t go back. I meant it when I said it. I turned down two more bookings there. Told myself it wasn’t worth it, that I didn’t need whatever that place had going on.

But I couldn’t stop thinking about her. About Marla. About the photo. About the way she talked to me like she knew me. Like she’d been waiting. I tried to leave it alone. I really did.

But a few days ago, I went back. No makeup, no heels. I figured that made it less... real somehow. Not at night. During the day. I told myself that made it different. Safer, somehow. Like whatever I’d seen before was just… atmosphere. Late-night nerves. Too much adrenaline.

The Velvet Haze looks different in daylight. Almost wrong. The alley out back was empty. No music, no voices, no crowd spilling out onto the pavement. Just bins, damp concrete, and that same smell I couldn’t quite place before.

Inside, it was even worse. The lights were on, but dim, like they hadn’t bothered turning everything up. No music. No laughter. Just this low hum, like the building itself was still holding onto something from the night before.

There was someone behind the bar, cleaning glasses. Older guy. He barely looked at me.

“Closed,” he said.

“I know,” I told him. “I just… I performed here a few weeks ago. I think I left something backstage.”

He didn’t argue. Just shrugged like he couldn’t care less and went back to what he was doing.

The hallway to the dressing room felt longer than I remembered. Quieter. My footsteps sounded too loud, like I was disturbing something.

The green room door was open a crack.

Inside, everything looked the same. Same cracked mirrors. Same lights. Same wigs on those foam heads, staring at nothing. But it felt empty in a way it hadn’t before. Like something had been there. Recently.

I told myself I was being stupid. I didn’t even know what I was looking for. I just… started checking around. Under the counter. On the shelves. Anywhere I might’ve left something behind.

That’s when I saw it. A cigarette, resting on the counter. Just sitting [there.No](http://there.No) ashtray. No pack. Just the cigarette. There was a faint smear of lipstick on the filter. Dark.

I stared at it for a long time.

Then I picked it up. It felt warm.

Not hot. Just… like someone had been holding it not long before I got there.

That’s when I heard something behind me. Not a voice. Just the faintest sound, like fabric shifting.

I turned around. Nobody there. The room was empty.

I left after that. Didn’t run. Didn’t panic. I just… walked out, like if I acted normal, everything would stay normal.

The guy behind the bar didn’t even look up when I passed him.

I threw the cigarette away outside. Straight into one of the bins in the alley. I remember that clearly.

I went home. Tried to forget about it. That was two days ago.

Last night, I was getting ready at a completely different venue. Different part of the city. Different people. Different everything.

And I smelled it again. That perfume.

Powdery. Old-fashioned. Strong enough that I actually turned around, expecting someone to be right behind me.

There was no one there.

I told myself it was in my head. Then I checked my bag.

The cigarette was inside. Same one. Same lipstick mark.

I know I threw it away. I know I did.

I didn’t say anything to anyone. I just left it there and finished getting ready. Tried to act like nothing was wrong.

But it keeps getting worse.

This morning, I woke up and the smell was in my flat. Not strong. Just… there. Like it had soaked into the air overnight.

I opened all the windows. Thought maybe it was something from outside. Someone smoking nearby. It didn’t go away.

And about an hour ago, I went into the bathroom.

The mirror was fogged up. I hadn’t had a shower. I hadnt even been in the bathroom for hours. There was something written in it. Not clearly. Just like someone had dragged a finger through the condensation. I wiped it away before I really thought about it. Instinct, I guess.

But I saw enough. Just one word.

“Finally.”

I don’t think she was lonely. I think she was waiting. And I think I brought her back with me.

The smell is stronger now. I don’t smoke. I never have. But there’s ash on my kitchen counter.

I don’t know what to do.
