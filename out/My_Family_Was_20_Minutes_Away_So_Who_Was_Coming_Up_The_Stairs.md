In 2004, I moved in with my girlfriend.  
Quick backstory: we’d actually met years earlier in high school, but after graduation we lost touch. Then one night my brother, my roommate, and I threw a small house party. She came with a mutual friend, we reconnected, and the rest was history.  
She had a six-year-old daughter named Majesty, and the two of them quickly became my family.  
About six months after moving in together, something happened that I still can’t explain.  
I worked nights while my girlfriend worked days. Most afternoons I was asleep when she and Majesty got home, but I’d usually wake up long enough to say hello before drifting back off.  
That day started no differently.  
I was upstairs in bed, sleeping with the blankets pulled over my head like I always did during the day. It blocked out the light and helped me sleep.  
Then I heard the front door downstairs.  
I could’ve sworn it was them.  
I pulled the covers back and called out their names.  
“Hey, babe? Majesty?”  
Silence.  
No footsteps. No voices. Nothing.  
It struck me as strange because there was no way both of them could’ve missed me yelling. I waited another minute, listening.  
Still nothing.  
I convinced myself I’d imagined it. Maybe it had been the neighbors. Maybe the wind. Whatever I’d heard, I let the blanket fall back over my face and closed my eyes.  
Looking back, I think that’s exactly what *it* wanted.  
The instant my eyes shut, I heard footsteps sprinting up the stairs.  
Fast.  
The kind of light, excited footsteps only a child makes.  
I smiled to myself.  
Majesty always came bursting into the room after school to show me drawings or tell me about her day. I figured she was racing upstairs to jump on the bed.  
Then…  
The footsteps stopped.  
Right outside the bedroom door.  
The door was already open, but from where I lay, I couldn’t see anyone standing there.  
I called out again.  
“Majesty? Is that you? You and Mommy home?”  
No answer.  
I tried one more time.  
Except this time…  
Nothing came out.  
My mouth opened, but no sound escaped my throat.  
At that exact moment, every muscle in my body went completely numb.  
I couldn’t move.  
Not even an inch.  
That’s when I saw it.  
Through the thin floral bedsheet covering my face, a shape drifted into the doorway.  
It wasn’t a person.  
It stood maybe three feet tall with a bulky frame, completely black from head to toe. No eyes. No face. Just a solid shadow.  
Admittedly, I nearly lost control of my bowels.  
I knew—deep down—that this wasn’t a child.  
It wasn’t human.  
As it slowly approached the bed, I fought with everything I had just to move a finger.  
Nothing.  
I was completely frozen.  
And somehow I knew…  
It was doing this to me.  
I could hear every step it took.  
It wasn’t a normal footstep.  
It sounded heavy.  
Almost like something dense dragging itself across the hardwood floor.  
This thing had weight.  
It was real.  
I was twenty-four years old, worked out every day, and considered myself pretty fearless.  
None of that mattered.  
I’ve never been so scared in my life.  
The closer it got, the harder my heart pounded.  
Sweat poured down my face beneath the blanket.  
I didn’t know what it wanted.  
I only knew that every instinct in my body screamed that whatever stood inches away from me was evil.  
It stopped with its face only inches from mine.  
The bedsheet was the only thing separating us.  
To this day, I’m grateful for that.  
Because if I’d actually seen its face…  
I honestly don’t know if my heart would’ve handled it.  
I kept trying to scream.  
Nothing.  
The figure simply stood there, swaying ever so slightly, twitching in small, unnatural movements.  
Then, just as suddenly as it had appeared…  
It collapsed inward on itself.  
Almost like a shadow folding into nothing.  
And it was gone.  
The instant it disappeared, I could move again.  
I shot upright, ready to run downstairs.  
Then another thought hit me.  
*What if it’s standing outside the bedroom door?*  
I stood frozen for a few seconds before deciding I’d rather take my chances than stay in that room another second.  
I bolted downstairs wearing nothing but a pair of pajama pants.  
No shirt.  
No shoes.  
No socks.  
I refused to go back inside.  
I must’ve smoked five cigarettes waiting for my girlfriend and Majesty to come home.  
I wouldn’t even sit in my car because I couldn’t shake the thought that whatever I’d seen might be waiting in the back seat.  
About twenty minutes later, they finally pulled into the driveway.  
The second my girlfriend saw me standing outside, she knew something was wrong.  
Not wanting to scare Majesty, I pulled her aside and told her everything.  
She believed me immediately.  
She gave me her pastor’s phone number, and he prayed with us over speakerphone.  
Afterward, he asked to speak with me privately.  
He told me he believed something dark had crossed my path and warned me to be careful about the people I surrounded myself with.  
“Spirits have a way of following people,” he said quietly.  
Whether he was right or not, I don’t know.  
What I *do* know is that I’ve never experienced fear like that before or since.  
I’ve tried for over twenty years to convince myself there was a logical explanation.  
Sleep paralysis.  
A vivid dream.  
Stress.  
Maybe.  
But none of those explanations account for the footsteps running up the stairs… or the heavy scraping sound of something walking across my bedroom floor.  
Whatever stood inches from my face that afternoon…  
I hope I never see it again.
