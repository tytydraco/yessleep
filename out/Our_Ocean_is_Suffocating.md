Hi there, I’m here writing to document a discovery that if proven to be true, could mean terrible consequences for our society. I am Harvey Westfield, a rescue diver for the coast guard, and recently a cargo ship(The S.S Raphael) had been voyaging during an awful storm that capsized the vessel. We received the S.O.S and immediately got to work on looking for the survivors.

The ship had reached levels beyond standard levels, in this case we were deployed in a dive bell to saturate. This would make the operation a whole lot lengthier than any survivor would like, but we do what we must to ensure the safety of our men as well. The storm had not yet dissipated which made the operation a lot riskier, under normal circumstances we would cut our losses, but this vessel was harboring over 35 men, and we couldn’t chance letting such a sizable crew be devoured by the depths. We made sure to deploy sonar in case anything would be coming close to interrupt(whales, sharks, etc.), it is best to make sure we have heads up when it comes to ocean danger.

After acclimation to pressure, we exited the bell and began to search wreckage. “I’ll take staterooms, you boys check the bottom deck.”, me and my team have done these operations dozens of times, uncanny livable areas beginning to be consumed by the ocean, a sight that many never get to witness.

“We’re picking something up on the sonar.” called our reconnaissance team. “What are you hearing?” I exited the wreckage to survey our surroundings. “It sounds like a choking noise, you know when you gargle a drink in your mouth before you spit? It sounds oddly similar.” I was puzzled by the description, what would cause a noise like that? “You reckon whale calling?” I offered puzzled, “No ... .it's too loud and too our radars are picking up heavy motion, whatever it is would have to be right on top of you.”, “Well I am surveying and I see nothing, just rumbling from the storm up top.” 

In that moment a sensation unlike any other befell me, sudden constricting pressure was grabbing me, breathing was difficult. “What the hell” the ocean proceeded to rock the ship from the ocean floor, I saw a countless ton vessel begin to get brushed across the sea floor, my team was inside. “Matthews, Wilson, are you guys ok!?” All I could hear on the radio were the sounds of panic and agony, the rapid increase of pressure along with the inertia of the ship guaranteed that my men were not making it out, as much as I regret my actions, I fearfully reentered the bell. “Pull me up! The others are gone! Please god!” I erratically screamed into the comms microphone. As the bell returned upwards I watched as the pressure began to crush the metal, warping from inside the bell. Had I not been rapidly returning to the surface, the bell would have exploded and killed me. 

I entered the intel room to see puzzled shock on each of the recon teams faces. “Westfield, come look at this” I sped walk over to the sonar monitor to see what had everyone else so uneasy. “The readings are showing rapid increase of pressure and sporadic motion activity. Do you know what this means?” I shake my head. “The ocean is fucking breathing, not just that, it’s choking, those noises are the ocean.” My face lost all color, how could that even be possible? I left my room with dread worn all over my face. That is where I sit now writing this entry. My team would like to keep quiet and study this, but if the ocean truly is suffocating, I can only begin to worry about the sort of outcomes this could have for mankind.
