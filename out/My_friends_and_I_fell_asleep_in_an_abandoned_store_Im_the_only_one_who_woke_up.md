I am writing this here because I have nowhere else to go. I know some of you might think this is just another internet story, but I swear on my life — every single word of this is real. This actually happened to me. It is a nightmare I will never be able to escape, and I never, ever want to go through it again. If anyone ever proposes you to do what we did... run. Do not make the same mistake.



I was 14 years old back then. It was just a regular, boring day, and my friends and I were sitting around, trying to figure out what to do. Eventually, we decided to check out an abandoned building on the outskirts of town. It was supposed to be a grocery store, but it was never finished because the owner died under mysterious circumstances and the project was completely shut down. 



We grabbed some chips and popcorn from a store, hopped on a bus, and headed a bit out of town. After a short ride and a five-minute walk from the bus stop, we finally reached that half-built store. We sneaked inside and started eating our snacks.



By the time we realized it, it was already very late. We were all supposed to go home, but we decided to pull off a "genius" plan to trick our parents. We found an old phone booth nearby. I called my mom and said I was staying at my friend's house, and my friends did the same. We decided to spend the night in that abandoned store. 



But then we faced a problem: we had nothing to sleep on. We seriously considered gathering some dry leaves from outside to make a makeshift bed. One of my friends got uncomfortable and said he wanted to just go home because sleeping on concrete was a terrible idea. 



But then, something weird happened. Right there, in the corner of the dark, empty concrete room, we noticed blankets. Not just one, but exactly three blankets. Clean, dry, and perfectly laid out. It felt like a stroke of luck, so we ignored the creepiness of it. We covered ourselves, lay down, and started telling each other stories.



After a while, the friend who initially wanted to leave decided to go outside and check the area one last time. He walked out... and 10 minutes passed. Then 15. He wasn’t coming back. We thought he was just pulling a stupid prank on us to scare us, so we decided to go look for him.



I told my other friend, "You go out first." 



He stepped toward the exit, and suddenly, a heavy door somewhere slammed shut with a deafening crash. I rushed to open it, but it wouldn't budge. It was completely locked. Right at that moment, the friend who was with me in the room started screaming at the top of his lungs. 



Suddenly, I smelled gasoline. I heard the distinct, terrifying sound of a roaring fire right next to me. I could see the flames. My friend collapsed to the ground, his body violently twitching in horrible seizures. And then, out of the shadows, the first friend — the one who went outside — stepped in. But it wasn't him. His eyes were blank, and he effortlessly picked up my seizing friend. He lifted an 80kg guy like he was a feather. 



The smoke became unbearable. I began to suffocate, coughing violently. Just before everything went black, I felt something cold and unnatural touch me. 



I woke up in a pitch-black room. There were no walls, no light, just endless darkness. And then the voices started. They were screaming, echoing from every direction, piercing my ears: "I WILL KILL YOU! I WILL KILL YOU!" I was in excruciating pain, completely paralyzed. I couldn't even stand up. I just sat there in that dark void for what felt like two agonizing hours.



When it finally ended, I opened my eyes. But I wasn't in the store. 



I was in a hospital bed. My parents were sitting next to me, crying. They told me that my friends and I had lost consciousness on the bus on our way out of town. The paramedics found us there. They said nobody knows what happened to us... but my two friends never woke up. They died that night. 



I am the only one who survived. And to this day, I still don't understand what really happened to us in that bus... or in that store.
