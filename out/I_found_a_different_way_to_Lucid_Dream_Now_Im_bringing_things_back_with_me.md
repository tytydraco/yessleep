​My name is Alan. Like many of you, I was just an ordinary man living in a rented apartment I could barely afford, working a soul-crushing job, and coming home to an empty life. I had ambitions, but I was too small, too insignificant to ever reach them. So, I turned to dreams to numb the reality I had to face. Sitting here now, writing this with trembling hands, I wish I had never tried it.

​It all started on the darkest day of my life. I had just watched a major promotion—one I had bled for—get handed to someone else. To twist the knife, I found out the girl I had loved in silence for years was engaged. I wanted to vanish. I wanted to forget everything.

​I ended up at a bar. I had never been a drinker, but the atmosphere felt like a sanctuary. As I sat at the counter, a man approached me. He was gaunt, dressed in disheveled clothes with skin so pale it was almost translucent. His eyes were sharp—unnervingly sharp. But when he spoke, his voice was warm, melodic, and impossibly inviting.

​"Rough day, isn't it?" he asked.

​"Everyone has rough days, friend," I muttered.

​"True," he smiled, "but yours is different. Today is a crossroads. If I hadn't stepped in, I fear you’d have done something... reckless... when you got home."

​"How could you possibly know that?"

​The bartender placed a drink down for the man, his movements stiff and fearful. He wouldn't even look the stranger in the eye.

​"Because God sent me here to save you," the man said. "The world our bodies inhabit isn't the only one, Alan."

​He reached out and handed me a folded scrap of parchment. It wasn't paper; it felt like cured leather. Scrawled on it in chaotic, jagged ink was a title: "LUCID DREAM." Underneath were instructions on how to enter "the other side." I won’t reveal the exact methods here for your own safety. At the time, I thought he was just a lunatic or a scammer looking for a few bucks. But when I looked up from the parchment, he was gone. I asked the bartender where he went, but the man just shook his head, looking pale. He claimed he hadn't seen anyone talking to me at all.

​I woke up the next morning at 12:29 PM with no memory of how I got home. I quit my job that day, hoping for a fresh start. A week passed, and life began to stabilize.

​Then came Saturday. While scrolling through YouTube, a video about lucid dreaming popped up. The memory of the bar flooded back. A strange mix of curiosity and dread washed over me, compelling me to find that parchment.

​I found it in my closet, neatly folded on a shelf where I’m certain I hadn't put it.

​I compared the "mainstream" lucid dreaming techniques to the parchment. They weren't even remotely related. A fire of curiosity—and a sliver of fear—ignited in my chest. Was that man even human? I decided to try the parchment’s way. I was desperate for the "salvation" he promised.

​I prepared the materials:

​6 white candles.

​Red chalk and specific dried herbs.

​9 pieces of Labradorite and 9 pieces of Moldavite.

​I drew a hexagram on the floor around my bed, placing the candles at each point. I lay on my back, stuffed the herbs into my mouth—their bitter taste coating my tongue—and placed the stones on my chest, forehead, and shoulders. I began the incantation. The words felt heavy, like lead in my throat.

​The rule was absolute: Do not leave the circle while the candles burn.

​Then, I felt it. My limbs turned to stone. A crushing weight pressed down on my chest as if two invisible entities were standing on me. Then came the sound—the sound of long, sharp fingernails dragging across the hardwood floor just outside my circle. Scritch... scritch... scritch.

​Suddenly, I felt myself being pulled through the floor. I was falling through a pitch-black void. I tried to open my eyes, but I could only see an infinite, suffocating darkness.

​After what felt like an eternity, the falling stopped. I stood up in a world of absolute shadows. I ran for miles, or maybe years—time had no meaning there. Just as I was about to give up, I saw a light.

​I stepped into a prehistoric forest. Towering trees, vibrant plants... and birds. Dozens of birds hung in the sky, but none of them were flapping their wings. They were just... hovering. Motionless. There was no wind. The air was thick and stagnant. And most terrifyingly of all, I could no longer feel my own heartbeat.

​"Welcome to the Lucid Dream," a voice echoed from all directions. It was him. The man from the bar.

​I shouted for him, but there was no reply. I kept moving. In this place, gravity was a suggestion. I could leap over canopy tops without effort. Eventually, a ravenous hunger hit me. The moment the thought crossed my mind, a tree appeared before me, laden with glossy, blood-red fruit.

​I plucked one and bit into it. It was the most divine thing I had ever tasted—a mix of wild honey and a cooling sensation that eased my soul. But it was sticky. It felt thick and viscous as I chewed, like swallowing sweet, wet silk. I gorged myself until the tree was bare.

​Sated, I leaned against a trunk and noticed a strange flower. It looked like a sunflower but was a deep, neon blue. It emitted a scent so powerful it felt like a physical pull. I plucked it, inhaled the aroma, and the world shattered.

​I woke up drenched in sweat, my body aching as if I’d run a marathon. It was 3:40 AM. The candles were burnt out. The Labradorite and Moldavite stones on my body had been crushed into fine, grey powder.

​I reached up to wipe my forehead, and my blood ran cold.

​My hands still smelled like that fruit. The scent of the blue flower hung heavy in the air of my bedroom. And when I pulled back the covers, my feet were caked in thick, wet, black mud.

​That was the moment I knew. My life would never be "normal" again.
