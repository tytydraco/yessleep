Hoia Forest, deep in the heart of Romania, is not a place that one would choose to visit after dark. It is shrouded in mysterious tales, with rumors of eerie phenomena, restless spirits, and shadowy figures that never leave. Despite numerous expeditions into the forest, no one dares to stay overnight. It is a place where no one truly understands the dark forces that hide in its depths.

On the edge of the forest lies a small village called Hoia. The people live in relative peace, though that peace is far from permanent. In the early 20th century, a family of farmers vanished mysteriously. Their house remained, but no one knew where they had gone, or if they were still alive. The villagers avoided the house, knowing that it was a place of danger, an evil that could never be undone.

One day, a group of paranormal investigators from Cluj-Napoca decided to travel to Hoia to investigate the family’s disappearance. The group consisted of three people: Andrei, an expert on supernatural phenomena; Laura, a psychologist who had studied fear and paranormal occurrences; and Mihai, a photographer with a passion for capturing eerie images.

They arrived in Hoia just before dusk, as the sun began to set and a thick mist began to envelop the forest. They were greeted by Gheorghe, an elderly man who had lived in the village his entire life. His eyes were clouded with fear as he gazed at them.

“Going into that forest... you will regret it,” Gheorghe warned, his voice trembling. “There are things there that cannot be explained.”

“Follow me,” Andrei said, though even he felt uneasy. “We’re heading to the first house of the missing family.”

Laura and Mihai followed closely behind, though both felt a nagging sensation of being watched. Laura couldn’t explain why, but she could feel an invisible force, as though something—or someone—was following them in the shadows.

After an hour of walking, they finally reached the house. It was a decrepit wooden structure, its walls faded by time, windows cracked and broken. The door stood slightly ajar, and there were no signs of life. The forest had swallowed the house whole, and there was an overwhelming sense of abandonment.

“We should go inside,” Andrei said, not waiting for a response. He stepped forward and pushed the door open.

The silence was oppressive, and yet they could hear strange noises—distant sounds like something moving in the upper floor of the house.

Suddenly, there was a loud noise from above. Footsteps—heavy, deliberate, as though someone were walking upstairs. Mihai, though terrified, held up his camera, taking a photo of the staircase.
