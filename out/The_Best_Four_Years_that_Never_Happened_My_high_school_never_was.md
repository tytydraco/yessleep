I guess I’ll just get into it: I’m the only person who seems to remember going to my high school.

My school district started building a new high school the summer before I was in seventh grade. They completed it early – a few weeks before Spring Break when I was in eighth grade. Looking back, what they did next didn’t make a ton of sense, but they threw all the gifted and talented kids in the district into the school for the last 10 weeks of the school year.  
  
We had a blast. But it was wild and it was kind of stupid. We had access to computers and robotics, and a brand new shop, and an advanced theatre. We had some very different gymnasium equipment and some unusually good science equipment. All the art supplies were brand new. We had a kiln! I met my best friend in what was passing as an English Class but was actually six people writing a group novel about a sentient locomotive. It was a time to be alive and I’ve always looked back on those 10 weeks with insane nostalgia.

And for a long time, so did my old friend group.  
  
I go back to my hometown about two or three times a year. The family still lives there, as does my high school best friend. I went back last weekend, and made sure to have enough time to visit my best friend. This time of year used to be pretty cold, but it was surprisingly warm, and I suggested we go out to “Stormwater Lake” – the drainage pond behind the high school that was nearly always flooded into a small lake.  
  
My best friend seemed confused, but agreed. As we got closer to campus, I noticed that the houses were in unusually poor shape. Dead grass in the lawns, broken down cars, just looking rough. This had always been a nice neighborhood in the past – the kind of neighborhood that put up the fancy holiday decorations. But these houses looked like they hadn’t been painted in years. Some had the look of way too many people crammed inside.

The school building and campus were wrecked. The campus had been very ‘manicured’ in my memories but the only things left were the big football field goals and the absolutely decaying remains of a tennis court. The school building was gone except for the ‘tower’ which had been a central ‘core’ that contained the gym, the main auditorium, the library, the cafeteria, and what we called the commons.  


I looked on in horror, and my friend excitedly told me that she’d forgotten to tell me that “the carcass” was finally going to be converted into community center. The carcass? The school looked like it had never been completed! She pulled out a toke and lit it. “Just like old times.”  
  
Reddit, I’ve got nothing against the giggleweed, but she and I never toked up in the old times. We’d never toked up period. She asked me if I wanted to ‘break in’, see if our ‘Art Trophies” were still in there.  
  
I decided I did, even though I am damn near 50. Breaking in involved using a bump key that was literally hanging from the door. Inside, the building was practically empty except for a large glass case that still contained a silly exhibit that our eighth grade art class had done at the end of the year, called “Visions of the Future” where we tried to imagine what the high school would look like in ten, twenty, and fifty years later. As I remembered, my friend and I’s art had ‘won’ – we’d done a silly panorama of the parking lot from the library windows, showing hover cars.

 would have been a neat idea to keep the exhibit over time, but there was nothing else on that board that I didn’t remember. Memos and bulletins from dot-matrix printers. Some hand-drawn and hand-lettered posters. There wasn’t a hint of time marching on, and there was no sign that my high school years had even happened. It didn’t appear that anything other than those ten weeks of gifted kid camp had ever happened at this building.

When I got home, I did some googling.

After the stint of G&T mayhem, an irrigation culvert started leaking into and slowly flooding the basement mechanical area. Unfortunately, no one was in the school for the next 8 weeks, and no one realized that the place was being damaged. By the time they returned, the damage was considered quite severe. Over the next year, they tried to clean up the building, but they were thwarted by one of the rainiest years ever recorded. It never reopened, even though apparently there was a Hail Mary attempt two years later that saved the central tower. The classrooms were torn down, and then the community center idea came to life. But it never came to fruition either.

It seemed that the building was designated as storage for the district.  
  
How do I remember going to high school in a building that never opened as a high school, and was partially torn down my junior year?  
  
How do I remember attending a concert here on a summer break from college?  
  
How do I remember our fight song?

I know, it sounds dumb, but I imagine this is how people feel when they find the smoking gun that their spouse is cheating, or they were adopted, or have staggered into some life-changing secret that they never even went looking for. I halfway expect to open the front door and notice some strange, unfamiliar car lurking in my neighborhood. I expect people to show up on my doorstop, flash some badges and tell me to come quietly. Of course not, nothing has happened like that, but it feels imminent at this point.

Obviously, after I went home to my own house I started digging through my box of mementos and yearbooks. I found two yearbooks – eighth grade and senior year. I remember having a year book for my freshman and junior years too, but it’s completely plausible they’ve gone missing in some of my moves. Beyond just the high school being wrong, other stuff is wrong.  
  
I’m on the pep squad. I have never been pep squad material. But there I am, in a little nylon tracksuit with ribbons in my hair, a mascot painted on my cheek.  
  
I lettered in Drama. Apparently I was the lead in our big blowout school musical my senior year. I was certainly in Theatre as a teen, but I was usually a stagehand. I think I had two speaking roles?  
  
This was a great high school career and I remember none of it. Absolutely nothing in there seemed right. There was a picture of me as a Prom Princess (one of the nominees for Queen) wedged into the yearbook and it was the sort of dress my mother would have never let me buy. In fact, I had the prom dress I remembered in the bottom of the box. I took it out, shook it out, and yep, it was exactly what I remembered. Other things in the box didn’t match the year book either. I found a pendant for the high school I remembered attending, and t-shirt that had been made for the theatre kids who didn’t have stage roles. The joke only made sense if I’d gone to the highschool I remember, because it was a play on the name. So we certainly wouldn’t have used that joke at a different school.  
  
Of course, to keep things interesting, some of the stuff I found supported the yearbook. I found an old Kodak envelope and there pictures and pictures and pictures of me at a school I’ve never seen. It’s me. It’s my friends. We’re doing things that we would do, and looking cooler than I remember, but it’s us and my best friend still basically wore purple everyday and I still had this weird poodle haircut that I thought was so cool.  
  
But I also found a manila envelope of pictures I remember taking at the high school I remember attending. And we’re wearing what I remember wearing. The picture my friend took of me squirting milk out my nose after telling me the dirtiest jokes. The picture of all of us hiding in the “crows nest” above the auditorium. I know schools look like schools, so I got on the internet and looked up the other school in town to see if maybe these pictures were really taken thenre. But that school was built in 1930 and it’s just entirely different architecture and dimensions. I also found nothing about the school familiar. Not one thing. I would swear up and down and sideways that I’d never been in the building before.  
  
Right now, I’m alone with my own thoughts. It’s not good for me. I keep looking for little other things that are wrong or different in my life. I feel like I’m in possession of a truth I shouldn’t know, and that somehow, whomever has been successfully hiding this truth knows that I know, and that’s not good. That’s not what they want. They don’t want anyone to remember.  
  
But I do remember.

And that’s a problem. The world feels so sinister now. And untrustworthy. 

I’ll forget a few minutes here and there. I’ll get wrapped up in the Olympics, or in making dinner, or a show. But the minute I’m not distracted my heart starts pounding. I feel like I shouldn’t go outside. Or pick up the phone. I don’t think I could force myself to answer the door. I wonder if I shouldn’t ever go back to that town. Shouldn’t call my best friend. I feel like I need to just go far away from all of this and maybe they won’t get me.  
  
But who is “they” anyway? It makes no sense, absolutely none. There’s no possible benefit to faking a high school career for a random woman of no interest or importance. I’ve never done anything related to the government or their enemies. I’m boring. I work part time as a car detailer for a little veedub place. It’s the least sketchy business I’ve ever encountered. I can’t think that they’ve mistaken me for someone else. And I can’t think that this is being done to someone else and I’m just caught up in it – I don’t know shady people. You know that adage about “ask the shadiest person you know to introduce you to the shadiest person they know…” We’d have to go a few more rounds of that to get to an honest-to-betsy shady person. There’s no way I’m collateral damage in some bigger plot.

I think I’m even afraid that there’s a rational explanation for this. Am I losing it? Is this the first step in tripping down a staircase into the hell that is dementia? Is my brain just imagining these backgrounds that don’t match the other high school? Can a brain edit a picture in real time so it looks like what you remember, and you can’t see the real details? Can you develop paranoid schizophrenia later in life? Should I call a doctor? Or should I just burn this box and have a loud conversation about things I don’t remember with people I guess I don’t really know to convince the people I don’t know are out there that I’m going to behave?
