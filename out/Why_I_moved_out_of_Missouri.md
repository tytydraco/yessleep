I had lived in this shitty town in Missouri for most of my life. Quiet, dull, full of the sort of people who think tater tot casserole is the peak of cuisine. But despite that normality my mom always thought there was a dark sort of aura to the town and these days since I moved away I'm inclined to agree.

It was roughly the first month of my freshman year of highschool, or at least it was the first trimester. At that point in time I was a member of the school's orchestra, played cello, wasn't very good at it but whatever, around that time this kid who I'll just call Evan, joined orchestra. I couldn't really put my finger on it but something was just off with him. I hadn't ever seen him before that year, some of my classmates claimed he was there in 8th grade but its been too long for me to really care. But After that day I noticed him, weird incidents started occurring around him.

Strangely though, nobody really noticed those incidents and people really liked him for whatever reason.

He got invited to hangout with the upperclassmen, he got to run for homecoming king despite that not being allowed for freshmen, he got to do fucking everything.  Evan always seemed happy, of course theres nothing wrong with being happy, but he'd be happy even at really weird times like those incidents I mentioned.

Like when one of the goals on the football field fell down and crushed a kids leg, he was just sincerely smiling. I remember giving him a' wtf they just got their leg crushed' look and he looked back at me with that same smile. It was almost as if he had planned it, or took pleasure in the distress.

I think he started following me around after that, I'd always see him in the hallways, I'd see him at my bus stop, when I went on walks with my friends, he'd always pass by. 

It got really damn creepy. Or maybe I was just being driven insane by being a freshman. Either way incidents kept occurring.

The elevator broke at my school with people in it before crashing down to the first floor, Evan just so happened to be waiting for the elevator.

His neighbor's house burnt down, the next week a tree in my back yard caught on fire. When I got to school the next day he sat down at my lunch table across from me, smiled at me, and talked with my friend for the entire period. That one might be a stretch but there were many more incidents than just those three.

Realistically I know a lot of these things could have been just chocked up to the fact that my 14 year old self wasn't medicated for anything he needed to be, but still, there was one incident that happened and drove me to move in with my aunt on the other side of the country. 

It was late april and all three of my friends were busy, no problem, I was completely content to just watch youtube all day. So thats what I did until I decided to go downstairs and get a glass of lemonade, nobody else was in the house so I have nice absolute peace. Until there was a knock at the door. I go to see who it is, Lo and behold, its Evan.

I probably asked hims something along the lines of, “How the fuck do you know where I live?” 

Because I remember him saying: “Your friends told me to get you so you don’t spend the entire day rotting in your room.” I was extremely reluctant to hangout with him of all people but  I probably figured that this would be a way to figure out what his deal was.

So we went on a walk, heading towards the edge of town and train tracks. It was a really quiet day, in fact I didn't see anyone walking or driving or any kids playing outside.

He talked a lot but it was never anything of substance, how the orchestra was doing with our end of year pieces, how there was some discourse going on with the prom planning committee, how his friends were very excited to see some film in theaters. It was the most boring mundane shit possible for how much anxiety his presence filled me with.

At one point I stopped walking but he continued on talking about whatever. It was at that moment I realized I never knew how he looked, and somehow still didn’t know despite the fact that he was walking right in front of me.

It was like he was nobody but a vessel for someone to input a preferred personality. Maybe thats why I felt so creeped out? Regardless I started walking along side him again before we made it to the train tracks and its crossroad. 

Evan sat down on the tracks and looked up at me with his usual smile before patting the rail next to him. Thats when the cross road light and dinging noise started to go off. 

“Dude get the fuck off the tracks!” I said. But there was no response, no reaction, nothing but that disgusting smile on his face.

I tried to pull him off the tracks, I really did, but it was like he was glued on. I kept yelling at him, begging him to get off when the train finally came and I jumped back.

There was a lot of blood. A LOT of blood. I can still remember the way it splattered onto my shirt and the fast moving air from the train just two feet away from my face. I can remember the way it glistened on my hands and the concrete. I can remember that when the train was finally gone his corpse laid there mangled, still with that smile.

Though I don't remember the walk home whatsoever or the rest of that day or the rest of that weekend, everything was just blank until that Monday. I didn't tell anyone.

I walked into school, into the orchestra room, and there he was, Evan just rosining his bow per usual. For whatever reason, I promptly vomited. The vomit felt too much like the blood and according to my mom I passed out.

Something must've broken in my head after seeing him alive after seeing his body crushed on the train tracks. The next few weeks were a whole lot of staying home sick and begging my parents to let me move in with my aunt all the way on the east coast.

There was no one to talk to about it. I still have the shirt from that day, still stained with blood. But every time I got dragged to school he was there, smiling. What could I have done? Tell my friends it was a dream? Dreams don't stain your shirts with blood.

It was all mostly a blur of being on my guard and trying to go to sleep with an unhealthy amount of NyQuil, besides one night where I just couldn't sleep. 

Maybe it was cause the fact my house was too warm, maybe it was cause I'd spent 75% of the past week sleeping. But I couldn't. It was like I was being mind controlled to stay awake if that makes any sense. At one point I made my way downstairs to the kitchen and grabbed a glass of water when I heard a knocking at the floor.

From what I remember I dropped the glass and it shattered on the floor.

For whatever reason I grabbed a knife from the knife block on the counter. I'm not sure why. Maybe to kill him? To kill myself?

I closed my eyes, slowly opened the door, stepped onto the porch, I opened my eyes and saw him. I can still remember the sound of the midnight train approaching town.

He was standing on the sidewalk, under the streetlight, 10 meters away from me. In a way he looked almost ghostly. HE SHOULD HAVE JUST BEEN A GHOST!!!! And It was like every time I blinked in shock he got closer and closer until he was right in front of me, staring into me with that damn smile.

In that moment myself somehow became transparent, like a ghost. That's the only feeling I can recall as the horn of the train sounded, I blinked, and he was gone.

Anyways that morning apparently Dad found me passed out on the sidewalk with the knife in hand and the edge of the sleeve bloody, but I didn't have any wounds on me. I think that's what convinced them to let me leave but there were probably some other details that he omitted when talking to me later that morning.

These days I still don't get what exactly happened to him or me. Just that I'm glad to be out of the town. And I get that it might not be very scary to y'all but it's something that's just kept me from getting sleep for years. And I get that you probably don't believe me but this happened.
