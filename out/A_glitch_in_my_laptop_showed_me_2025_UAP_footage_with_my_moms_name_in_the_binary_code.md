Alex Reed's fingers trembled as they hovered over the keyboard, the dim glow of his London flat's single bulb casting jagged shadows across the cluttered desk.  
Rain hammered the window like a relentless code breaker, January 6, 2026, ticking past midnight in his cramped Camden space.  

He rubbed his thumb against his index finger—a nervous habit from childhood, one his mom always teased him about during their late-night talks.  
Those talks... back when she was healthy, sharing stories of her RAF days near Suffolk, laughing off old tales of strange lights in the woods.  

Now, her neurological disorder had left her frail, the medical bills piling up like unchecked errors in his audits.  
Alex pushed through the fatigue, eyes burning from hours scanning data logs for his cybersecurity gig.  
Anything to cover those debts.

Tonight, though, the screen betrayed him.  
A glitch ripped through the feed—in medias res, an unauthorized stream burst in: raw 2025 UAP footage from over Suffolk, echoing that infamous Rendlesham Forest Incident.  

He'd skimmed the declassified MoD files, fresh from last week's NDAA 2026 updates, whispering about intercepts and hidden labs at Woodbridge.  
But this video?  
Unfiltered, descending triangular shape, silent against the night sky.  
No drone buzz, just charged air.

Alex leaned closer, the ozone scent sharpening, metallic and invasive, as if the room itself was downloading something forbidden.  
He paused on a frame where symbols glowed faintly on the craft's belly—pulsing binary, like a corrupted signal.  

His old data-scientist instincts fired up; he ran a quick decode script, fingers slipping on the keys from the shake in his hands.  
The output scrolled erratically: fragments of coordinates, garbled dates, noise cluttering the lines.  
Then, amid the chaos, a partial string clarified—"Elean... Reed."  
His mom's name, twisted but unmistakable.

Alex's breath hitched, a cold sweat breaking on his forehead.  
He remembered her voice from last week's call, weak but warning: "Some shadows from the past, Alex... they follow you."  
Was this tied to her illness, that sudden onset after he'd dug into old files for a freelance job?

The room felt smaller, the rain's rhythm syncing with his pounding heart.  
He minimized the window, but his phone buzzed—a notification from an unknown app.  
"Link established. Proceed to site."  

He swiped it open, hands still unsteady.  
A map flickered: Woodbridge pinpointed, overlays hinting at underground structures, whispers of recovered tech from those congressional hearings.  

Alex's mind swirled with fragments—Reddit threads on r/UFOs buzzing about Non-Human Intelligence, podcasters like Rogan debating ancient origins without naming them outright.  
But this hit too close.

He grabbed Signal, messaging his Seattle contact Mia: "Suffolk feed glitch—personal. Check NDAA intercepts?"  
No immediate reply.  
The ozone thickened, his laptop fan whining like a distant hum.

For a moment, Alex sat there, the weight of it all pressing down—the debts, her fading health, this impossible intrusion.  
He thought of her smile in old photos, how she'd point at the stars and say, "There's more out there, but don't chase it alone."  
Now, isolation gripped him, the glitch pulling strings he couldn't see.

Then, a sharp knock echoed at the door.  
Insistent, timed with the rain's pause.  
Alex froze, rubbing his thumb harder.  

Through the peephole: two hooded figures, one scanning the frame with a device that hummed faintly.  
A breath—shallow, almost inaudible—seeped from under the door.  
His laptop, even closed, pinged once more: "They know."

Heart racing, Alex snatched his backpack.  
The handle rattled.  
Woodbridge called from the map.  
But who—or what—was waiting?

The glitch had just begun.
