The rain fell steadily against the ancient stone walls of the Vatican, turning the narrow streets into rivers of black glass. Midnight bells echoed across St. Peter’s Square, long after tourists and pilgrims had vanished. Only a handful of windows still glowed within the immense complex.

One of them belonged to **Archivum Prohibitum** — the Forbidden Archive.

Father Matteo Rossi hesitated before the iron door.

He had served the Church for twelve years. He had studied theology, Latin, ancient manuscripts, and the rituals of exorcism. Yet the letter he held in his hand made his fingers tremble.

**AUTHORIZED ACCESS — LEVEL TEN.**

Below it was a seal he had never seen before.

Inside the archive, dust hung thick in the air. Endless shelves of locked cases stretched into shadow. Books older than entire countries rested behind glass. Some were chained.

Waiting for him was an elderly priest in black robes.

“Father Matteo,” the man said quietly. “I am **Cardinal Valenti**.”

Matteo bowed his head. “Your Eminence.”

The cardinal’s eyes were sharp, almost too sharp for a man of his age.

“You were recommended because you have performed three successful exorcisms.”

“Four,” Matteo corrected softly.

Valenti gave a faint smile. “Let us hope that confidence serves you well.”

He turned and walked deeper into the archive.

Matteo followed.

They stopped before a heavy table where a **thick leather manuscript** rested. Its cover was scarred and burned, as if it had survived fire.

Matteo recognized the symbols immediately.

“Aramaic… and older markings beneath it,” he whispered.

Valenti nodded.

“This text was sealed here in **1683**. It records the interrogation of something the Church never managed to destroy.”

Matteo looked up slowly.

“Something?”

Valenti’s voice dropped.

“A demon that claims to remember the **first exorcism ever performed by mankind**.”

The candles flickered.

Matteo felt a chill crawl across his spine.

“Where is it now?” he asked.

The cardinal closed the manuscript.

“In Rome.”

# The Possession

Two hours later, Matteo stood inside a hidden chamber beneath a small Vatican chapel.

The room was bare stone. Thick iron rings were bolted into the walls.

At the center sat a young man, chained to a wooden chair.

His head hung low.

Four Swiss Guards stood silently nearby.

Valenti spoke quietly.

“The boy’s name is **Luca Bianchi. Seventeen years old.** The possession began three weeks ago.”

Matteo approached.

“Has the entity identified itself?”

Valenti hesitated.

“Yes.”

“What name?”

The cardinal’s expression tightened.

“It calls itself **Azravel**.”

The moment the name was spoken, the boy’s head snapped upward.

His eyes were completely black.

Luca smiled.

“Ah,” the voice rasped, though it sounded far older than the boy. “The Vatican finally sends someone interesting.”

Matteo felt the temperature drop.

He opened his ritual book.

“In the name of the Father—”

The demon laughed.

“Do you know what lies beneath this city, Father?”

Matteo ignored it.

“I command you—”

“You walk over **bones and secrets** every day.”

The chains rattled as Luca leaned forward.

“I was here when your church was only whispers in caves.”

Matteo held up a crucifix.

“I command you to reveal your name!”

The boy’s smile widened.

“I already did.”

The candles suddenly blew out.

Darkness swallowed the chamber.

Only Luca’s voice remained.

“You think the Vatican hunts demons,” Azravel whispered.

A long pause followed.

Then the creature chuckled softly.

“But Father… the Vatican **also keeps them**.”

A deep rumble moved through the stone floor.

Somewhere below them, something enormous shifted.

The guards looked around nervously.

Matteo slowly turned to Cardinal Valenti.

“What… is beneath us?”

The cardinal’s face was pale.

“There are levels under the Vatican that no priest is allowed to enter.”

Luca began laughing harder.

The sound echoed through the chamber like something crawling through the walls.

Azravel whispered:

“And tonight… one of them **woke up**.”
