Although each of the students were eager to pounce on me with a million spiked questions, they stayed at the seats, bound by the members of staff assigned to the room. The dark floorboards were stained a near white where the varnish had been scraped away by dragged chairs. In some places, where the chairs had followed the same tracks of their ancestors, the floor had uneven trenches. Faded posters and timelines adorned the room, and Brother David beamed a genuine smile as we entered the room. The blue-painted walls, usually dull and uninspiring, seemed for the first time, to me, like a sanctuary.

"So boys" Brother David clapped his hands to wake us up "during this time European security was based off various alliances"

Michael rose his hand "what does that mean sir?" 

Michael asked this question not out of interest, but rather to ignite Brother David's infamous tangents. I always suspected that Brother David knew what we were up to.

"Excellent question young Michael" he smiled. While Brother David began to slowly execute his students via vaguely linked historical trivia, I found myself looking out the window.  It was that sort of beautiful day where the sun shined and the distance became a mauve blur. I wondered what the people in that blur were doing,  how many were doing their nine to five? How many felt lonely? How many stared back at me,  wondering the same? 

Despite my best effort,  I found my stare falling back onto the hawthorn tree that could be seen from the window.  Beneath it, you could make out disturbed mudden clumps. I shuddered at the memory.
