I’ve just about given up.



Nobody will listen. I get replies like “Interesting concept” or “Stop using AI slop.”



Somebody even asked me if they could write something based off the idea.



Nobody believes me when I tell them it’s not supposed to be funny or some kind of stupid new meme.



The first video was a short clip, just ten seconds of something large moving through the little bit of brush behind my house.



I don’t live somewhere remote. I live in a little cul-de-sac that borders a large stretch of woodland. Cookie cutter houses in neat rows like you’d see in some brochure.



The thought that a large animal was anywhere near us was exciting. I probably should have been more concerned but I was literally recording from my back door with a chain link 

fence separating me from... whatever it was.



I tried to upload the video directly from my phone, didn’t bother reviewing it or trying to add any effects.



It was taken down one day later.



The reason?



Copyright infringement.



Apparently I had some copyrighted music in the background.



Bull.



It was a clip straight from my phone, outside on a school day. It was as dead as it gets.



So I played the video back, thinking maybe a neighbor had something on and I’d just tuned it out.



And there it was.



Soft. Tinny. Distorted.



A song I’d heard hundreds of times growing up watching old VHS tapes.



I won’t name the tune because a pretty large company owns it, but it’s something we’d all recognize.



It was drawn out, a little out of tune, but definitely the same melody playing in the background.



And something else stood out.



There was a little black patch behind the brush. Something I hadn’t actually seen while I was recording.



It blinked open but stopped halfway. The image blurred and pixelated before turning into a smooth green mesh.



Like poorly rendered CGI.



What the actual hell?



Had I put on some stupid filter? Why was the video coming out like something from a low budget late night TV skit?



I don’t have that footage anymore. I deleted it after watching it.



Sadly that wasn’t the last video I’d take of the thing.



Two days later, kids were out and about. Screams of joy, ringing bike bells, the distant sound of somebody mowing their lawn.



I was out on the back patio, beer in hand, pointlessly scrolling through short videos on my phone when something made a loud crack.



I’ll admit, I jumped.



Didn’t fly out of my seat or spill my beer, but it definitely gave me a jolt.



And there it was again.



Something large.



Clearly furred, because I caught sight of a patch of brown catching the sunlight through the leaves.



I brought my phone up, flicked the control panel down, and tapped the camera app.



This time I checked first. Made sure I wasn’t somehow in some filter mode.



Then I pointed the camera right at the break in the tree line.



And for a minute I watched it move.



The fur.



The shape of it.



Something slowly lumbering through the brush, shaking small trees and large weeds without a care in the world.



It wandered lazily away, deeper into the trees and out of sight.



This time I watched the footage immediately.



I felt more than a little smug as I pulled up the video, the first few shaky seconds after I tapped record giving me a sense of satisfaction.



I took a long pull off my beer and then choked on it.



More music.



Not even the same song. This one was something by a popular pop star, played way too fast and pitched higher like a recording on fast-forward.



And the fur didn’t look right.



It was spiky. Too sharp in some places and blurry in others.



It didn’t interact with the light correctly.



The sun would hit one patch and the color would flatten completely, like somebody had brushed over it with brown paint.



It looked fake.



Worse than fake.



It looked generated.



And poorly generated at that.



I overlaid the audio with some royalty free sounds and tried posting the video as “Camera Acting Weird Near Bear.”



It got copyright struck again.



This time it wasn’t the music.



There, in the sky, in the background where I hadn’t even thought to look...



A face.



Poorly overlaid, low opacity, but still visible.



I don’t know why that was enough to get me flagged, but at that point I had two strikes.



This time though, it stayed up long enough for me to read the comments.



“Dude, stop trying to make analog horror.”



“Scary mascots are so overdone.”



“Cheap AI slop.”



They thought I was faking it.



That this was some poorly thought out ARG or some stupid advertisement for a new low budget indie game.



The third time I didn’t even bother trying to record it.



I just sat in my chair, beer in hand, waiting to flip my pork chops on the cheap little charcoal grill I kept on the slab of concrete that counted as my “patio.”



Whatever was going on, it didn’t seem interested in me.



It always felt like it was just passing through. Maybe sniffing around. Maybe checking things out.



It hadn’t bothered me.



Yet.



This time it got closer.



I could make out the outline of a large animal moving between the trees.



Slow. Heavy.



I didn’t stick around long enough for it to step into the weak white glow from the back door light.



Chico did.



He was at the fence barking like crazy. Tail stiff. Little body rigid.



The thing stopped.



I heard a deep inhale.



Then a huff.



Chico went quiet.



Not all at once. He sort of trailed off into these low, confused little growls.



The screen door slammed shut behind me and Chico was suddenly back at my feet, barking up a storm like the little rat dog was actually going to fight whatever the hell that was.



Thankfully the little guy was smart enough to follow me in through the doggy door when I shouted for him during my scramble to the door.



Not smart enough though.



He kept growling at the back door.



Low. Constant. Ears pinned back.



Then he poked his head out through the doggy door.



He always thought he was ten feet tall for such a little guy. 



I remember him picking fights with dogs twice his size.



But more than that I remember the sound. 



Can still hear it.



Can still hear the yelp.



The crunch.



The quiet.



I bought a shotgun. My neighbors have called the cops on me more than once for just sitting out back with the thing.



But I'm still waiting for that bastard to come back.



I'm not going to record it this time.

  
Not until it's stopped moving. 

  
Then if there's anything left I'll let everyone see.
