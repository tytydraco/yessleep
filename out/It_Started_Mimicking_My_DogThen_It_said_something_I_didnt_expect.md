Hi, I'm Jack. I'm 22 years old and to be honest I have no one in my life except my dog Tommy. He's very clingy to me and I love that nature of his. Well Tommy is a very energetic and an active dog but nowadays he's been real quite. I knew something was wrong with him but was not quite sure about it until last night.

It started around 2:13 AM.

I woke up to the sound of scratching.

Not unusual. Tommy sometimes scratches at my door when he wants to come in. But this...… this was different. It was slower. More….... deliberate. Like whatever was on the other side was trying to be careful.

“Tommy?” I called out, still half asleep.

The scratching stopped immediately.

Then came the whine.

Soft. Broken. Exactly like his.

I got up and opened the door.

The hallway was empty.

And Tommy…....was already inside my room.

He was sitting in the corner. Completely still. Just staring at the door I had just opened.

I felt this weird chill run down my spine, but I tried to brush it off. I called him again.

“Tommy, come here.”

Nothing.

He didn’t move. Didn’t blink. Just…....watched.

Then his ears twitched.

Not toward me.

Toward the hallway.

And then I heard it again.

The same whine.

Coming from outside.

At that point, I shut the door slowly. Locked it. I don’t even know why I just did.

Tommy let out a low growl.

I had never heard him growl like that before. It wasn’t aggressive. It sounded….....afraid.

The sound outside changed.

The whining stopped.

For a few seconds, there was nothing.

And then

Scratching again.

But faster this time.

Impatient.

“Go away,” I said, trying to sound firm.

Silence.

Then something happened that I still can’t explain.

From the other side of the door…......

I heard my own voice.

“Tommy, come here.”

My heart dropped.

I didn’t say that.

I hadn’t moved.

I slowly turned to look at Tommy.

He was still in the corner.

But now….....he was shaking.

Actually shaking.

I grabbed my phone and just sat there on my bed, staring at the door.

Minutes passed.

Or maybe hours. I don’t know.

At some point, I must have dozed off.

Because the next thing I remember

Was waking up to silence.

Morning light coming through the window.

Everything felt….... normal again.

Too normal.

Tommy was lying next to my bed like nothing had happened.

I told myself it was a dream. It had to be.

Until I noticed the door.

Deep scratch marks.

Long. Uneven. Like something had dragged its nails across it over and over again.

I didn’t open the door immediately.

I just stood there, staring at it.

Then I heard it.

Behind me.

A soft whine.

Tommy’s whine.

I turned around.

He was sitting on the bed now.

Looking straight at me.

Tail wagging slowly.

And then

He opened his mouth.

And in a voice that was almost right….....

Almost human…......

He said:.......
