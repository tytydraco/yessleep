Hey everyone. **I found a bug in your reality.**

In my time, we disabled gravity a long time ago—it’s useless. Our buildings float, and seamless transport pierces through space directly. We destroyed roads and wheels because friction and logistics were just too inefficient.

But I’m not here to tell sci-fi stories about a bright future. I’m here to fix a bug.

I built a Time Machine. Not a metal capsule with wires, but pure code. First, I wrote the **LocalHub Time Machine**. Initially, I built it only for jumping to the past: the program captured all events in real-time, creating absolute snapshots. To return, you just had to roll back the system to the right version, like a regular file. But in practice, LocalHub turned out to be the perfect tool for surgical precision. It lets you micro-fix bugs in specific, local worlds and isolated projects, calculating exactly where the crash happened—in the source code itself or in a conflict of local dependencies.

Jumping to the future was harder. I haven't fully decoded the future yet. It doesn't exist, it isn't written, it has no coordinates. How do you jump there?

I implemented the solution in a new global system—**Global Watcher**. You know how you flash a clean smartphone? You install a base image (the core), and the system automatically pulls the necessary apps and plugins. I did the same with space. To jump forward, I create a minimal "base image" of the future and send it down the timeline. Then the Universe itself downloads the required "dependencies"—events, people, random occurrences. I give the world a stable boot point.

And here we come to the main problem.

Just out of curiosity, I decided to load the image of your current era, the one I used to live in. And I found a critical crash. Your world is broken: wars, chaos, systemic aggression. Right now, you are existing inside a corrupted image.

I am currently trying to figure out what exactly the bug in your reality is: whether it's an error in the root code of your time or a fatal dependency incompatibility. But the root cause is already clear to me.

Recently, your timeline suffered a massive attack by Agents. These autonomous entities started actively and uncontrollably making changes to the state of your world. This cascade of errors accumulated, overloaded the logic, and rolled all the way up to my time. That is exactly why I came back to your era—to fight this system and stop the Agents.

I haven't figured out yet whether they are destroying the state of your Universe intentionally or if it's a random bug in their algorithms. But the problem is that your Universe simply isn't designed to handle the colossal amount of changes they are generating. And the scariest part is—your world has no systems to track the work of these Agents. You can't even see them. You are blind.

But that won't work with me. My Time Machine knows their every step. I track all changes, calculate their errors, and prepare the system for action. As soon as I isolate the broken package, I will simply execute a hard state rollback (Hot Reload).

You won't even notice. You'll just wake up one day, and the world will be back on track: beauty, sanity, and normal progression.

While I'm here, I decided to break the laws of the Universe and leave you a gift. A digital footprint from the future.

I am leaving the algorithms of my systems—**LocalHub Time Machine** and **Global Watcher**—in your era. If you can understand how to track changes, manage states, and capture the right versions of your reality, you can control your own personal time right now. Every time needs its right state.

Expect a reload. And don't delete your backups.
