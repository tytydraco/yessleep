So I was walking around campus with my friend who lives in the dorm across the hall from me. We were doing something *other than* drugs (look, say what you will but the rest of this happens stone cold sober). We went to these caves because there’s zero light inside them, and when you’re *not* doing drugs like we were, that’s when fun things really start happening. 

While we were there, we kept kicking around something that was obviously plastic, and so we picked it up—mostly just to clean whatever garbage it was out of the cave. 

When we got it out, it was VHS tape. We were mind blown because this thing must’ve been there since at least the early 2000s, but maybe even since the late 90s. My friend is the only person I know more passionate about physical media than your average millennial gaming YouTuber, so luckily for us he had a VCR.

We were a bit too paranoid to play it immediately, the next day (when we really were sober) we gave it a shot. We were hoping it would be old, taped episodes of Rocket Power or something, complete with ads for questionable kids toys like Mr. Bucket, but disappointingly the whole thing seemed blank. When we first starting playing it, however, his… *vase…* fell off his desk. We stopped the tape, put it back, and then restarted the tape. We were watching the screen intently once again, but then… \*CLUNK\* \*glugluglug\* it fell at the exact same time. 

After long time of experimenting and yelling back and forth at each other over what could or could not be possible, we concluded that the tape didn’t play anything on the TV, but instead “played” itself on the room around us. Again, I swear on my future child, we were actually sober at this point.

So this is what we’ve found so far after messing with it for a while. 

1) About 3 seconds in, our *vase* fell. We found this can be replaced by anything fragile as long as it’s placed on a shelf. 

2) About 30 seconds in, the TV will turn on and off rapidly, and then our Xbox will turn on and stay on. We’ve found this works even if the TV is unplugged, but this does not work for the Xbox.

3) At about 45 seconds, my A/C will turn on, but it do this weird sort of annoying sputter if the A/C is already on, it will just start sputtering.

4) At about 1m 15 sec, there will be a knock on my door. When we look through the peep hole, nothing’s there. 

5) Around 1m 20 sec, my door handle will jiggle somewhat aggressively

6) At about 1m 23 sec, there will be the sound of a sliding key, and then my door will unlock.

We haven’t done farther than this. If anyone knows what this is or if anyone has any more ideas about what we should do with it, I would greatly appreciate it.
