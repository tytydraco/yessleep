There is an urban legend, a niche one, that speaks about a sort of game you can partake in under very specific circumstances. Most people don’t know of it, because almost every single person who has played the game died trying to get to the end of it. In fact, most of this information was received from the one person who I know of that has ever survived it, my best friend, Richard. He recounted his experience to me a few days after he had reportedly played the game.

This game goes by many names, but the one most commonly used is “The Cadaver Maze.” Just sounds so cheerful, right? Now, don’t think you can just play this game whenever you want, wherever you want. The criteria to even simply just activate the game are extremely strict. For starters, it must be night, specifically sometime around 1:00-4:00 AM. To add onto that, it must be a Wednesday. Any other day, and nothing will happen. Lastly, it has to take place on the specific month and day of June the 6th. This is mainly why the legend is so unknown, the window of time to play the game is so unbelievably short that you only get one chance to do so, usually having to wait multiple years to try again.

If all of the previously mentioned factors are true, you want to enter either a cemetery or church graveyard. Since a lot of them will be closed during this time, you’re probably going to have to break in illegally. The cemetery must be at least 5 acres in size. If you attempt to initiate the game in a cemetery smaller than this, one of two things will happen. A. The game will simply not activate, or B. If you are intentionally trying to activate the game in a smaller cemetery thinking it will make it easier to win, he will see this as an act of arrogance, and will kill you. 

Next, you’re gonna want to enter the cemetery with three things on hand. The first thing you’ll need is a piece of paper with your own blood on it. This is the metaphorical liability waver, letting him know you are willing to give up your life upon failing to complete the game. The second thing you have to have is a small, sharp object. It can be anything, a pair of scissors, a makeshift blade, and anything else of the sort. The only limit is that it cannot be an ACTUAL knife. That would make the game less difficult. If you try to activate the game with any other weapon, it won’t work. The final thing you will need is a red, black, or purple candle, as well as something to light the candle. After you’ve assembled all of these items, you can finally start the game.

To activate the game, hold the candle in your left hand, and light the candle with your right. Once it is lit, it will begin to levitate a few feet in the air, and a deep, raspy, booming voice, coming from no discernable direction, will announce the beginning of the games. The ground in which you stand on will warp and twist, and the landscape of the cemetery will transform into a giant labyrinth, with the tombstones transforming into giant walls to keep you from escaping. The game has already begun, and you cannot go back once you have reached this point. A detail Richard pointed out was that the walls looked like they were made of human flesh, covered from top to bottom in eyes, teeth, and nostrils. The eyes would follow your every movement, blinking once every few seconds. The nostrils were also leaking thick, chunky, purple mucus, that stung to simply touch.

The floating candle will still be in front of you for a few more moments before vanishing, disappearing into the maze. Afterwards, the same voice from earlier will say this exact phrase:

“THE CANDLE WHICH YOU HAVE PROVIDED HAS BEEN TRANSPORTED TO THE EPICENTER OF THE LABYRINTH. HIDDEN WITHIN ITS MANY PATHS IS A CANDLE SNUFFER. YOU MUST FIND YOUR WAY TO THE CENTER OF THE LABYRINTH WITH IT AND EXTINQUISH THE CANDLE’S FLAME. YOU HAVE 30 MINUTES. IF THE TIME RUNS OUT, YOU SHALL DIE. NOW, PROCEED.”

Now, despite the voice mentioning a time limit, do not, and I cannot stress this enough, DO NOT RUN. It will create more noise, attracting them to you quicker. You’re going to want to take soft yet quick steps. Also, don’t even think of putting your sharp object anywhere. Keep it in your dominant hand at ALL TIMES. They will be relentless in ambushing you, so be on guard every second of the way if you value your life.

A few minutes into the labyrinth, they will begin to rise from the ground, usually ten at once. The moment they emerge from their graves, you will hear their distorted, gurgly cries a distance away, or, if you have bad luck, mere feet away from you. Although in some cases you can avoid them entirely, there’s a good chance you will have to encounter at least one of them. Depending on how long they’ve been buried, they might be actively rotting and decaying, their flesh shades of greenish, purplish, or black. Despite their sluggish movements, do not underestimate their speed when they lock eyes on you. There’s a good chance that if you do not sprint at your fastest possible speed, they will catch you. They are stronger than they look, and will most likely be able to keep your violently thrashing body pinned to the ground while they devour your innards like starving piranhas. If you see one, immediately back yourself up against the walls, and do NOT make eye contact. The mucus oozing from the walls may sting, but it’s the only way to live. Chances are if you do this, they will pass you without changing their snailish movements, not even recognizing your presence. If you aren’t fast enough to evade being spotted, as I said before, DO NOT RUN AWAY. Instead, hold your weapon up in front of you defensively. They will attempt multiple times to find an opening to attack, but do not let up. Eventually, they will back off, stepping away backwards slowly. Once you can no longer hear their deep mumbles, it is safe to continue forward.

Now, some of the corpses may have decayed for so long that they are just bone, with no flesh or organs. They are smarter, and more strategic than the rotting ones. You have to be smart to catch them. While you are walking, make sure to occasionally listen for the sound of low breathing. It may be very quiet, but not inaudible. If this is the case, stop dead in your tracks. Chances are it has been following you for quite a bit now. Once you have stopped, keep listening for breathing. If there is no longer any audible breathing, it has disappeared, and you can continue walking safely. If you have stopped walking yet you still hear something breathing, do not stay still for any longer. Forget what I said earlier about not running, GET OUT OF THERE AS FAST AS POSSIBLE. If you don’t, it will bite you in the back of the head, and kill you. They are not fast enough to pursue you for any longer than a minute. Once you no longer hear its footsteps, immediately start walking slowly again. Chances are that your prior sprinting has alerted more rotting ones, so continuing to run would not be a wise decision.

After around 15-25 minutes of walking through the maze, if you have good luck, you will find the candle snuffler, lying on the ground in front of a dead end. Upon picking it up, the voice will appear again, and say this:

“GOOD. YOU FOUND THE SNUFFLER IN (how many minutes it took you to get there) MINUTES. NOW, YOU HAVE 5 MINUTES TO PUT OUT THE CANDLE. GET TO THE CENTER BEFORE TIME RUNS OUT.”

After this, you will hear the cries of the wandering corpses, sinking back into the earth below them. However, you now have a completely new threat to worry about. From the ground where the candle snuffler laid, it will emerge from the ground, slowly, clawing itself to the surface with the desperation of a rabbit scrambling to escape the jaws of a wolf. Its body, its putrid, black, rotting, foul smelling body, will tower feet above you, its tentacles writhing around mindlessly. Its eyes, all 16 of them, will lock on you with a look of immense hatred and gluttony. The beast is starved, and you are its target.

Do not wait for it to start moving after you. Immediately drop your weapon on the ground where you stood, and SPRINT. Despite being massive, it is also extremely agile. One wrong move, one tiny falter in your speed, and you will be consumed faster than you can even blink. At this point, a massive, blood red beacon will light up the sky, directing you to the general direction of the end of the Labyrinth. You only have exactly 300 seconds to make it there, or else the devil awaits you on the other side of Hell, and he’s watching expectantly on his throne, waiting for you to be devoured.

If by an unexplainable fluke you run fast enough and through a certain path without even thinking, there’s a chance you may reach the long awaited center of the Labyrinth. You will see the candle, positioned in the middle of the ground, with a circle of crimson light surrounding it. It’s still pursuing you with haste, so you must use all of your strength to make one last dive to grab the candle and extinguish it with the snuffler. If you do not dive far enough or put the candle out quick enough, well, you can probably already guess what that thing will do to you.

If this is not the case however, and you actually extinguish the candle, the game is over. The monstrous entity that had been previously pursuing you will begin to decay, melting away until it becomes a fleshy mass of eyes and teeth, nothing more than a lifeless corpse that reeks of rotting tissue.

The candle you just extinguished will levitate once more, flashing various rapidly changing colors, shapes, and patterns. The world around you will seemingly be sucked into it, as if it is consuming all of reality. Space and time will cease to exist, as nothing but you, the candle, and the infinite void of darkness continue to be. Everything and nothing, the beginning and the end, all at once. 

Suddenly, before your very eyes, the candle will vanish, and he will appear.

From the darkness of the void that lay before you, his black robe will embrace him, his red eyes glowing from beneath his hood, staring upon you from above with an unreadable expression. He wields a snaith that will grow in his right hand until it is longer than his entire body, and equipped with a crescent shaped blade that will drip the dark, crimson blood of someone, or yet, something.

He will then begin to speak, his voice the one you heard before in the labyrinth. He will speak in an incomprehensible, older than ancient tongue that contains various sounds that us mortals from the 3rd Dimension cannot even begin to wrap our heads around. However, despite this, somehow, you will understand everything it says from start to finish. He will congratulate you for completing the game, and for your effort will grant you one wish, no matter how small or large, within the limits of his own near limitless power.

Once you have made your wish, he will disappear, and you will begin to fall. Into the endless, all encompassing void you will plummet for over a minute until you lose consciousness completely. When you regain it, you will be laying on the ground completely unharmed, exactly where you were when you originally lit the candle, and it will be the exact same time as it, too. However, all of the supplies you brought with you: the candle, the bloody paper, the sharp object, they will all be gone, nowhere to be seen. 

After Richard told me all of this information, I curiously asked him what he had wished for. He refused to answer
