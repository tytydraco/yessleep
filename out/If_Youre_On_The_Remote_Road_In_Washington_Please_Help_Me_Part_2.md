After a few fruitless attempts to drive out of the ditch, I reluctantly resined myself to spending the remainder of the night there. With whatever the heck that thing was I refused to step foot outside while it was still dark and raining.

I didn’t get much sleep, but when I slept I was kept in a torturous half awake half asleep dreamlike state. Towards sunrise, I gave into my exhaustion and fell into a restful sleep. I heard murmurs of strange voices speaking in tongues, raising my head I beheld a set of keen eyes studying me through the rear view window. The scent of mud and rotting flesh assaulted my nostrils as cold delicate hands hesitantly stroked the nape of my neck.

I awoke with a gasp, drenched in sweat. My eyes shot to the rear view mirror, empty. I twisted around to search the rest of the car to find it just as I had left it. There was some mud on the seat behind me, but I figured I drug it in from my last hike.

The rain finally stopped but it was replaced with a thick fog. I don’t know which was worse.

I stayed in the car as long as I could, gathering supplies for my hike out. In a sudden burst of courage and stupidity, I flung open the car door and timidly stepped out to survey the damage. The back wheels were firmly grounded in the mud, she would need a tow to get out of the ditch. I cursed my stupidity and chose to believe what I saw was due to an overactive imagination.

I grabbed my gear, made a last ditch effort to find a cell signal, and set off into the fog. It wasn’t long before I found the main road and began making my way towards Elbert. The air was still and the fog was dense, it swirled around my ankles and gave me a false sense of security. The silence was deafening and my footsteps seemed far louder than they ought to have been.

I made good time, or so I thought but the fog disoriented my sense of time. After some time, I ran across several downened trees and washed out spots in the road, and took comfort. Just up ahead, I saw what looked to be a mailbox and hurried towards it in hopes of being rescued from this hellish dream-like  world. In my hurriedness, threw caution to the wind and sprinted towards my salvation. That is, until I wedged my foot under a tree root and took a hard fall. I felt something in my knee give out and twist in an unnatural way, I gasped and clutched my knee in the fetal position, fighting off waves of pain until I heard the distinctive pitter patter of feet slamming cement.

I bit my tongue and stood, balancing on my good leg. When I did, the noise came to a sudden stop no more than a few yards from me. After a hesitant moment, it took a step closer and scurried off into the woods. I ran as fast as my leg would let me towards the mailbox and to my delight saw the flag was raised as though someone had just put a letter out to be mailed. I again heard the scurrying behind me, but this time it sounded as though multiple creatures were running towards me. I screamed and yelled trying to get the attention of the homeowners to the backtrack of nails scraping gravel.

I slammed into the side of the house and limped onto the front porch. I banged on the door. Silence.

I saw movement out of the corner of my eye and tried the handle, to my utter relief the door swung open and I quickly stepped in and slammed it shut. I heard something bound onto the porch and try the handle as I secured the door. Whatever they were it was chanting in rough ancient tongues.

“Hello?” I softly called out.

I squinted as my eyes struggled to adjust to the darkness.

“Anyone home?”

I jumped a little when my eyes adjusted, I saw the outline of a person sitting in a chair facing away from me.

“Pardon me, I’m so sorry about bursting in like that, but-“

When I came up to the chair, the man had been dead for quite sometime. I closed my eyes and looked away before taking a breath and examining his body. I stepped forward and the stench hit me, wet mud and rotting flesh. He didn’t have any obvious wounds, but then again I didn’t look too closely.

I found a landline and was finally able to call for help but they said it would likely be several days before they were able to send a truck out to pick me up. Since then, I’ve been sitting near the door with my back to him, I can’t stand to face him but I don’t have the courage to leave. The sounds outside have died away but everything once in a while I’ll hear a shuffling behind me. Please, I beg of you, if you’re anywhere near the remote road, please, please help me.
