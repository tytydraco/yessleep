I find it odd, how language changes over time. An example being the fact that German and English evolved from the same language, but a difference in spelling made drastically different languages. Another, and my personal favorite, being that what we now call ‘mirrors’ used to be called ‘looking glasses’. I’ve always been curious about these things. Why did they change? Who changed them? How did they change? Why did so many people pick it up? My parents used to encourage these questions. No matter what I wanted to learn, they helped. No question was a stupid one. All learning was done hands-on.

I’ve found that children tend to receive interactive learning a lot better. Every time I lecture, I always have them stand and interact with the material. Works every time.

My point is, I have an innate interest in learning, and I love encouraging others to do the same. Love of learning is how societies evolve. Learning and constantly asking “why?” is how we have the most important sciences in human history. It’s why we know so much about space, about gravity, about any science you can think of, it starts with one attribute: curiosity.

Which is why, when one of the students from a lecture asked me to entertain his curiosity, I complied. God, I wish I hadn’t. Curiosity can be good for many things, but too much curiosity is where bad things start to happen. That’s what happened this time.

It started with a simple mirror. That’s all. A mirror. One of those stupid funhouse mirrors that makes you look all weird. He showed it to me, and, chalking up the odd appearance to it being a stupid goddamn mirror that tricks your eyes, I told him how those mirrors work and started to leave. But he wasn’t satisfied with that explanation. His next question is the reason I will never, EVER, look at a mirror again. “If that’s how they work, then why does it look at me like that?”. Childish question, it seems to be, but I’d seen it, too. It wasn’t a stupid question, no matter how much damn logic I applied. What did I see in that mirror that made everything so awful, you ask? Let me answer your question with a question.

What do you see when you look in a funhouse mirror? Misshapen reflections. The mirror is warped in a way that makes everything elongated in some spots and squished in others. It doesn’t, however, look back at you. No matter how damn much you look at it, it never stares back. Right? Tell me, why are you afraid of the dark? Why do you feel as though something is watching you? Why is it, that when you turn off the lights, you sprint to the sanctuary a lit room or your covers create for you? Are you afraid of something reaching out of the inky blackness to grab you and drag you into its depths? It’s the same feeling, mirrors. They’re predictable because they show you what is there. What you can see.

But what happens when it shows you something you… can’t see?

What happens when something, evil or not, I’m not a cliché, the concepts of good and evil don’t apply to entities beyond my understanding. They are not tied to the rules of our plane while living in another, so why would they be tied to our societal concepts? Concepts like good and evil are for us, not them. They’re to regulate our behavior, not the behavior of entities outside our world, outside our societies, outside our very plane of existence itself.

But I do question… if an entity is visible in something on our own plane, is it bound to the same rules? Is it only bound to them when being observed?

 

 

I don’t look in mirrors anymore. It stares back. It isn’t even in that stupid fucking funhouse mirror anymore. It’s everything.

I used to dismiss the idea that mirrors are a gateway for spirits and other entities. I used to do, say, and be a lot of things.

Don’t look in your reflection. It has eyes. Any reflection will do. I’m writing this on my computer. I can see it in the screen. It’s been staring the entire time. Not getting closer, not exactly. It’s eyes are wider, though. Its eyes, oh God, its eyes. They used to be small dots. I thought it was a speck of dirt under a screen protector or something.

They’re almost the size of its face, now. I don’t wanna look. I don’t wanna see. I never heard from that student again after that day, and I don’t want to know why he introduced me to it, but I think it’s because it’s like a damn disease. I need to spread it.
