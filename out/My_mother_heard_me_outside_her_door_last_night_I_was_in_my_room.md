I didn’t plan on writing an update.

Honestly, I was trying to pretend none of it happened.

The last two days felt… strangely normal.

My mom acted the same, the town felt the same, and I avoided that road completely. I even started convincing myself that maybe I had panicked over nothing. Stress. Darkness. My imagination filling in gaps that weren’t really there.

That’s what I kept telling myself.

Then the messages started.

The first one came the night after I posted about what happened.

Unknown number.

Just one line:

“Why didn’t you stop?”

I stared at it for a long time.

Didn’t reply. Didn’t block it. Just put my phone down and tried to ignore it.

The next night, same time.

Another message.

“We waited.”

We.

That word bothered me more than anything else.

The next morning, I asked my mom about it as casually as I could.

“Did you text me from another number yesterday?”

She looked confused.

“No… why would I?”

I dropped it.

That should’ve been the end of it.

But that same night, I heard it again.

Not outside.

Inside the house.

A soft sound coming from the hallway. Like someone shifting their weight on the floorboards.

I froze in bed, staring at my bedroom door.

Then—

“Lucas…”

My mom’s voice.

Clear this time.

Not distorted. Not dragged out.

Normal.

Coming from the hallway.

My heart started racing immediately, because something about it felt wrong.

My mom’s bedroom is on the other side of the house.

And there was absolutely no reason for her to be standing in the hallway whispering my name in the middle of the night.

“Lucas… come here for a second.”

I didn’t move.

I didn’t answer.

Every instinct in my body told me not to.

Then silence.

A few seconds later, I heard footsteps.

Slow.

Dragging slightly against the floor.

Moving away from my room.

Toward the back door.

I stayed awake until sunrise.

The next morning, I checked the house.

Everything was locked.

Nothing looked out of place.

Except for one thing.

There were faint marks on the floor.

Dirt.

Dark. Fine. Almost like dust.

A trail starting near my bedroom door…

and continuing all the way down the hallway.

Toward the back of the house.

I followed it carefully.

It stopped at the back door.

Right where the wood meets the floor.

Like something had been standing there for a long time.

Waiting.

I cleaned it up. I don’t even know why. Instinct, maybe.

I tried to move on after that.

But then I started noticing something else.

Time.

Minutes stopped feeling normal.

Sometimes I’d sit down for what felt like a few seconds and realize an hour had passed.

Other times it felt like I’d been awake forever… only to look at the clock and see that barely any time had moved at all.

I remembered something I wrote in my first post.

“It doesn’t kill. It consumes.”

I wish I hadn’t written that.

Because now I can’t stop thinking about it.

Last night, I got another message.

This time… it came from my mom’s number.

“Why are you ignoring me?”

I immediately walked to her room.

She was asleep.

Her phone was on the nightstand beside her bed.

Screen off.

I checked it.

No sent messages.

Nothing.

When I turned around to leave, I noticed something I hadn’t seen before.

Her window.

Slightly open.

Just enough for the curtain to move.

And then I smelled it again.

Faint.

But unmistakable.

Wet earth.

Rot.

Something old.

I shut the window immediately.

Locked it.

Didn’t sleep at all after that.

I’m writing this because I genuinely don’t know what’s worse anymore.

The idea that it followed me home…

or the idea that it never needed to.

Because last night—

I heard my own voice.

From the hallway.

Soft.

Trying to sound calm.

“Mom… open the door.”

My mom answered half asleep.

“Lucas? Why are you awake?”

I was in my room.

Door locked.

I didn’t move.

I didn’t breathe.

And then—

I heard her bedroom door open.

I don’t know what she saw.

She hasn’t mentioned anything about it.

But this morning, before I left the house, she asked me something that’s been stuck in my head all day.

She looked genuinely confused and said:

“Lucas… why were you outside my room last night?”

I told her I wasn’t.

She nodded slowly.

Like she wanted to believe me.

But didn’t.

And now I can’t stop thinking about something.

About the crossroads.

About the messages.

About the voice.

It doesn’t just imitate people.

It learns them.

And if that’s true…

then maybe it’s not trying to get inside the house anymore.

Maybe—

it already did.
