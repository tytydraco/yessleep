Ever since I was young, I had the fear that someone, or something was coming to kill me. 

When I was about 3 or 4, I would wake up crying. I'd be sobbing and terrified that whatever was in the closet, under the bed, in the tub, was going to kill me. I had such a strong fear of mortality at a young age that other kids didn't seem to have. 

I started to hallucinate things later on. Rarely still do, thankfully not like I used to, at least. Always cryptid looking things. No matter what, they'd be able to talk to me. Say gibberish, usually. 

They would always be right with a crack of my door where light would just pour in the slightest amount. Then there'd be horrific things trying to put their hand through and look at me. Whenever I would see them, or something, I would force myself to pretend like it wasn't there.

I'd have to pretend like I didn't hear something vaguely call out what seems to be my name. When it hid under the covers, I'd have to pretend like I didn't feel something touching my ankle over the top of the blanket. 

I'd have to pretend like I was normal. I knew seeing these things and hearing and feeling wasn't normal at the least. I was terrified of myself and the things that I'd see.

There's one thing though, that still weirds me out more than the rest

I think I finally figured out what that fear, the cause, or paranoia is.

It's from a man named Epsilon.

He says he's a man, but I would say otherwise.

He came to me when I was 8, over a decade ago. Then again, at 16. I swear he’s a hallucination, but he is the only one that I've ever seen twice. I think a third time, well, let's not think about the future for a bit.

The first time, at 8; it was summer. A really hot, humid, typical Midwestern summer. My family and I lived in a rental during this time. A very small house with a basement that my brother and I lived in. 

It was about a half mile between houses in front of a road and a roundabout. Our house was a light yellow, with a garage to the left, and a few steps and the front door to the right.

When you entered the house, behind you to your right were two windows sandwiching a massive storm window. In front was a couch, and on the wall was a TV. To your left, the kitchen and garage. In front of you, was a small hallway containing a bathroom and my parents room. But between the kitchen and that hallway was a door leading to a basement.

The basement was always cold. I remember how achingly freezing the ground was. Down the stairs, on either side, were doors that led to a jointed room with my older brother and I. At this time, my younger brother's room was with my parents.

I remember one night, I woke up thirstier than a leech in a desert. I grabbed a nearby glass on my bedside table and started to trek to the kitchen.

The basement freaked me out enough as is. My older brother, as well as the rest of the house, was sleeping. Going up the stairs, I felt fine. Normal. Nothing was wrong.

I opened the door at the top of the stairs, taking a U-turn into the kitchen when I stopped midway.

I turned to face the window.

And there he was.

A man.

He was tall. I couldn't guess height measurements at 8, but he felt like he was 9 feet tall. He peered in the window at me. The only way I could see him was because the moon was out that night. No idea if it was full or anywhere near, just bright enough to see a silhouette.

He was all blacked out, his entire figure. 

Muscular, yet thin and lean.

And just staring.

And I stared back. Cup in my hand slowly slipping with how much sweat I was producing.

He just stared at me.

In my entire life to that point, I've never felt more scared in my life. The fear of mortality bloomed out of nowhere. I was gonna die, and I was only 8.

I started to accept my fate quicker than you'd think.

He raised a hand, pressed it up against the glass, roughly at the height my head was at, and scratched.

I blacked out, hearing the glass drop and shatter before I hit the ground.

I woke up in bed. I remember jolting up, looking around, trying to figure out what happened. It was daytime.

A nightmare is what I decided on at the moment. 

Later in the day after lunch, my mom made my brothers and I go play outside.

I remember playing monkey in the middle, the youngest always in the center. I swear to God, it was the funniest shit seeing him get fed up.

At one point, I was too busy laughing and forgot we were playing a game, so the ball went flying past me and by the window. 

I ran over to grab it, but when I grabbed it and got back up, I had a weird feeling. 

I looked up, and right above me, there was a handprint on the window.

I've moved since. Some place miles away, in a very nice neighborhood. The houses were close together, always my favorite part. If you need help, it's only a call away or even a couple of steps.

The second time I saw him was at this new house, at 16. 

It was a cold, Midwestern winter in January. My parents were leaving for a date night when I was left home alone to watch the dog.

I fell asleep on the couch pretty quick until a loud bark woke me up. My fat ass dog had to go to the bathroom and it was already very late at night. I think it was about 11pm? Doesn't matter. It gets dark at 4pm in Wisconsin's winter. 

I lazily got up and walked over to the side doors in our house. I opened them and let her out. 

I didn't bother turning on the porch light. Since most dogs are nocturnal and I trusted her to do her business without me watching.

This house was way different than the one before. This one had an upstairs and a basement. It was much more spacious than the previous one. 

5 or so minutes go by, and there's no sign of my dog coming back in. I always leave the side door open a crack so she knows that she can go in right away. But she wasn't even standing at the door.

I suddenly get this feeling coming over me like I don't know what's wrong. But my heart is racing. I opened the door a bit, and I called out to her. I couldn't see anything nonetheless. Her and I start to get this not in my stomach as my heart beats faster.

I called out her name, and I didn't even hear a skitter. I made the decision to turn the porch light on.

She was standing in the middle of the backyard, looking at something I couldn't see because of the house being built the way it was.

The backyard was an L shape. The garage extending past a certain point where you couldn't see the rest of the backyard from where I was standing on top of the steps. 

My dog just stood there. Her hair raised on the back of her neck, staring at something I couldn't see. 

I called, yelled, and screamed, at her to come back inside, but all she did was stand staring at something.

I've never seen a dog as terrified as I could be.

I mustard all the strength.I had in my being to take the few steps outside to see what she was staring at.

Each step walking over even though it was only a few yard distance. I felt like I was about to die like when I was 8.

I touched her, pushed her, and shoved her. I still wasn't looking at what she was looking at.

Until I heard a voice call my name clear as day.

I perked my head up, and there was a 9 foot tall being standing across from me, a few yards away.

“Hello there,”

It started,

“He is prefered.”

My mind had questions running through my head, which he started to answer them.

“What are-” he cut me off.

“I'm a man.”

He stepped closer, the light finally reaching him.

He looked otherworldly. His entire figure blacked out. His hands; absolutely massive, close to a giant's. His skin was akin to genuine leather. He was naked, bare everywhere like a ken doll. His head was bald, sharp and angular.

His face, though, was the part that terrified me the most. His mouth wasn't there, instead was the same leathery skin bunched up linearly. No nose, but his eyes were very off balanced. The whites of his eyes were pink instead. Little black dots like pinholes bore into my own eyes.

He stepped closer.

I closed my eyes.

“What do you want from me?”

Silence.

I take my hands away from my eyes.

His face was in front of mine, “to scare you.”

I woke back up on the couch, sweating like hell. My dog gets up, walks away, turns around and does the same bark like she has to go outside.

It's been a few years, yet I haven't seen him. However, I have greatly felt his presence. Anytime I'd turn, duck, or jump to see if it was him, the feeling would fade and he wouldn't be there.

What if he came to you? I doubt he would visit another person. But that feeling is there. You know the one.

That paranoid feeling of someone watching you. That something horrible is going to happen to you. That's just your imagination. No one's there. You're safe, you're alive, you're fine. No one is there.

Or maybe someone is?

Maybe Epsilon found you, too. 

Or maybe he found your kid, or your pet, or your sibling.

Maybe Epsilon is there, somewhere.
