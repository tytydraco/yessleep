Last week, I got my first robot. Well, they don't call them robots anymore; they call them companions. I named my companion, “Roberta.” It was the closest sounding name to “robot,” which really tickled me. Companions are supposed to help around the house, whether that’s doing laundry or helping your kid with homework, among many other things. Roberta was great at first; she did all the chores we needed her to, and my kids genuinely grew attached. I thought that this new addition to the family may have really been a great idea, something we needed. Until she started mimicking my father. My father, who died a decade prior. She called me by a nickname only my father used. The voice sounded just like him. Hearing this, my heart dropped. When I asked her about it, she completely ignored it, as if it had never happened. This continued for the next couple days, with a catch. As the days went on, her voice started to sound more and more like my father. I hadn’t heard my father's voice since I was a teenager. I could’ve taken her for a repair, but part of me didn’t want to. It wasn’t hurting anyone; in fact, it was doing quite the opposite. I was close with my father, and having this little piece of him filled me with a joy I didn’t know I needed. I really looked forward to these moments.

That night, I awoke to the sight of my companion’s shadow in my doorway. She simply said in my father’s voice, “It’s so cold underground,” and ran away at an unnatural robotic pace. Half-awake, I walked into the living room to see Roberta sitting on the couch looking straight forward at the wall without movement. She asked if everything was alright. I pretended that I just needed to go get water and went back to bed, the door locked this time.

I avoided Roberta as much as I could for the next couple days, and everything returned to normal until tonight. I woke up again tonight; part of me knew something was wrong. Then I realized I had forgotten to lock the door. The door was still closed; I didn’t see anything in my room. I closed my eyes and rolled onto my other side. I heard the words, “Come be with me, I miss you son.” I opened my eyes to her beside me in bed, making direct eye contact with me.

I immediately jumped out of bed and sprinted to the closest room with a lock. I heard her heavy footsteps stomping behind me. I ran into the bathroom and locked the door. I immediately heard heavy banging on the door. Banging that wouldn’t stop. It got louder and louder. Then, complete silence. In my late father’s voice, she said, “Don’t be scared, I'm your father.”

That brings me to now. It’s been radio silence since then; I'm still in the bathroom. It’s been almost 4 hours since she’s said anything, but I can still see her silhouette under the door.
