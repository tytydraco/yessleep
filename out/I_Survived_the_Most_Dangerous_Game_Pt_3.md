Hey everyone, I'm back. As I've been writing more about what happened to me, things are becoming a little more clear. Reading what I wrote before I'm confused about parts of the story myself. Anyway, back to what happened. I saw smoke in the distance.

What sort of idiot would light a fire in this situation? I almost catch myself saying this out loud. But now isn’t the time. I need to hide and fast. 

  
I make my way into a thicker portion of trees. I keep stalking my way through, away from the stream, when I notice I’ve actually made my way closer to the source of the smoke. Through the trees I begin to make out the fire itself.   


I hear a voice. It stops, then begins talking again. Then suddenly another voice starts, cutting off the first. As I get closer, this second voice is familiar but feels off.   


“I can tell you’re scared but this isn’t the time. I’m not gonna kill you whether you’re the original or a clone.” The second voice explains. “My job is only to find my original and take his place.”   


I finally found a view through the trees. Sitting around a fire, about 15 meters away, is a woman I vaguely remember from the night before and none other than myself standing above her. I creep a little closer to hear more clearly.   


“I know but you’re scaring me.”  


“Get over yourself. You have five minutes to leave or else I give our position away.” My clone towers over the woman. His eyes are bulging with anger. It seems like this woman has thrown a wrench into his plans. “I can’t promise you that any of the others will be as kind–”  


A branch snaps and I crouch low. I look towards the source of the sound but no one is there. I look back to the fire and my clone is once again looking towards me. I can see that his eyes are focused above and behind me. I turn to see what he’s focused on when a huge figure suddenly towers over me. A sharp pain in my jaw and the air knocked out of me from the landing and suddenly everything went black.   


I awake sitting at the edge of a small clearing in the trees. I see a large man sitting, facing away from me but looking at a small fire. I make to stand up but I’m bound to the tree and my ankles are tied. I still myself and stop just before calling for help. I’d prefer he didn’t know I was awake just yet.  


“Which one are you?” The large man has a surprisingly smooth voice. After a moment of my silence he continues, “I know you’re awake, no need to be scared. I just want to know which one you are.”  


“Easy for you to say, you’re not tied to a tree! And if you were, you could probably break out without any help.”  


“Now this may be true, but you didn’t answer my question. Which one are you?”  


I take my time to think and he doesn’t cut into my silence this time. “What difference does it make?”  


He finally turns to look at me. His face is a mess of scars and ink. The scars appear to be too precise to be from a wild attack, while the ink looks like an ink well must’ve spilt on his skin. One eye has gone grey with blindness. He’s holding the hatchet from my survival kit in one hand, cleaning his nails with one of the points. “The difference it makes is everything.”  


“Who even are you? There was no one even near your size at the mansion.”  


He took the hatchet, stuck the blade into the ground and stood. He came close and sat before me. “I’m the real trial of these woods.”  


I press myself into the tree as best I can. “What does that even mean?”  


He chuckles. “You can call me the Judge, or Executioner, your choice. Basically, this whole hunting game you’ve been dragged into, I’m the one who decides what happens here. This island is a domain I’ve created and I have control over everything that occurs here.” He looks down and starts to draw aimlessly in the dirt. “Let me ask you a few questions and maybe you’ll start to understand. First, what’s the name of the woman who gave your little presentation last night?”

“Julia.”  


“Wrong it was Gwen. When you were bombarded by screams in the woods, you saw a face in the tree, whose face did you see?”  


“It was my girlfriend’s face.”   


“Correct.”  


I don’t understand the point of his questions. Was I not supposed to see her face? I could have sworn her name was Julia, but maybe it was Gwen?  


“When your clone came to your room last night, he entertained the idea of not killing you, right?”  


“He did.”  


“And when you awoke this morning, you didn’t have an alarm and weren’t given a choice to stay or leave?”  


I pondered over this question. “I had the option to leave?”  


“Oh no, you never truly did, but Charles originally told you that you would.” At this time he stood and came closer to me. I shied away as he reached towards me. Suddenly the rope loosened around me. “Come to the fire, let’s chat a little more.”  


I wearily made my way over and sat on a stump near the fire. The Judge picked up the axe and sat back down where had before. As he sat with me it seemed like everything else in the forest melted away and it was just us and the fire.   


“So, as I hope you realize by now, I control everything here. And I mean everything. How do you think you got so lucky as to find this?” He held up the hatchet.   


“What do you mean lucky?”  


“Well no one else had the survival kit in their armory options. The ability to leave your clone essentially defenseless seems a bit of an oversight for such a prestigious and intelligent group as this one pretends to be.”  


“Pretends to be? Aren’t they all crazy rich and successful?”   


“Sure, but that is of no doing of their own. As I said, I control everything on this island. My influence becomes limited when they leave, but they are ultimately under my control, still.”  


“Under your control?” I stared incredulously at him.   


“Well, maybe control is a bit of a stretch. But I can influence things around them.” He began picking at his nails again with the hatchet.   


“Why make them rich and successful? Why make this sham of a hunting club or whatever the hell this is?”   


“Why? So I can consume them, of course.” At that moment the world seemed to come back. Suddenly the Judge’s face turned into that of the man Julia, or I guess Gwen, had said was the founder of this club. His voice changed, too. “I need to feed. Human lives sustain me.”  


“Then what’s all the shit with the clones? Why do we have to hunt ourselves if you’re what we really need to stay away from to survive?”  


“Well the fear factor surely helps.” His face had returned to its scared and inked state. “But ultimately that was an independent decision I let the club make on their own. I find the clones add a sense of desperation to this whole ordeal that far surpasses that of an unknown predator in the woods.”  


“Why didn’t you kill me?”   


The Judge sits upright at my question. “Well, I had to be sure you were the real one.”  


“Then why am I still alive? I didn’t say it directly but surely at this point you know I’m the original”   


“Most of the club doesn’t remember I exist, or my role in this game of theirs. For those who do, we came to an agreement that I only kill in the last twelve hours of the game.”  


“So you just hang out with me until morning and then you’ll eat me?”  


“Perhaps, but perhaps not. You’re interesting.”  


A shiver goes down my spine as he says this. I want to ask what he means but I’m scared of what he’ll say. Will he just manipulate the outcome so I survive, but to what ends? If he feeds on the participants then the majority of people in this club are clones from previous hunts. Why are my memories a mess? Even as I write this I can’t figure out the Judge’s motives. Why were the names different? Why did I see my girlfriend’s face in the tree? Why am I alive?   
The Judge lets out a loud sigh and pulls me from my thoughts. I must have been thinking for a long time as I see the sky starting to lighten.   


“Regardless of what happens, I’ll see you again. Just try not to die in the meantime.” He tosses the axe to the ground in front of me, stands from his seat and walks into the woods.
