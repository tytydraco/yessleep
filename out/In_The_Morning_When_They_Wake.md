I've lived in this neighborhood for a few years now. Moving from a small condo in the bustling financial district of New York to this quiet, peaceful place. 10 years of that damned city, any sane person would wanna leave. I'm 17 now and everyday, I wake up to a view of this beautiful lake in the middle of town. I'm kinda glad that mom convinced dad to pick this house rather than some high-rise apartment in New Hampshire. I’ve never been a fan of busy areas, mostly because I can’t get any peace of mind. There’s just too many things to worry about in the city. The town is really just this small rural area beside a lake. Everyone here knows everyone, having been a tight knit community since before I got here. When we first arrived, someone from the  welcoming committee told me a story of how this place came to be. Apparently, it was a common ground for animals to elope in, a place where calmness is absolute. People didn't start coming over till around the 1900s but even since then, it has retained the peace. What a wonder to find a place that isn't stained by the desolate chaos of humanity. 

My best friend Jeremy lives just across the street. I met him during one of the welcoming parties they hold every time new people move in. He’s a shy guy, we didn’t really hit it off the first time but instantly clicked after finding out we both played Mario Kart. I usually invite him over to play during the weekends after homeschool. Jeremy and I got to know so much about each other through the years, mostly because it really is just me and him in our age group in the town. A bunch of old people live around here, so much so that you'd think it was someplace in rural Japan. No, really. Usually the people that move to this town are seniors looking for a place to settle down and retire or just regular people looking to get away from the world. There isn't any way a family of four could fit in such a calm area. No one else here is as young as me and Jeremy, it's kinda why he and I are so close. There aren’t any schools here either, although it is fun to just be homeschooled with your best friend. We follow regular school schedules and we do homework, quizzes, stuff like that. I mean, there isn't really an actual school here so why bother. I find myself asking that a lot. Stupid if you ask me.

Today went on as usual, subjects, assignments, all the boring bull they make you do in school. It was after the last class that Jeremy told me about this huge treehouse he discovered just across the lake beside town. 

“Come on Bobby! It was huge! I bet you could fit a couple of rooms inside that place. Can't you imagine what we’d find?” He said, elated. 

I was intrigued but I held back knowing my parents hated me being out after dark.

“You know how strict my parents are, they would kill me if they found out we stayed out! This late into the night! Let alone outside the damn town  Jeremy!” I warned him. 

Jeremy looked at me with distaste, a face I saw way too often. Having such strict parents meant that I myself had to follow rules, but I know that they’re for the better. Ever since the incident with my brother.

“Fine, I guess I’ll go there myself. I wouldn’t wanna mess up the obedient son huh? What a good boy you are to mommy and daddy.” He said, teasing me. 

The damn guy had always been like this. I knew Jeremy meant no harm but I can’t say he wasn’t annoying whenever he did this. I notion for him to just go on ahead without me. I smirk as I accompany him to the door.

“Don’t hit your ass on the way out buddy”  I tell him as I flick his head. 

“If you get caught, it's on you man. I wouldn't want my only friend to be on house arrest for a week.” I told him jokingly.

Jeremy was notorious for sneaking out at night, having been grounded several times beforehand for doing so. My parents already knew how much of a bad influence he was, for that reason only. They never had a choice though, he really is the only person as old as me here. I mean they can’t just deprive me of my only source of company right?

Walking away from the patio, I couldn't help but tell him to be safe. A weird feeling hit me that said that I needed to say as much as I could before he left, anything to make him stay here. Before today I’d never felt that exact sting of dread in my mind. Words alone can't describe exactly how I felt in that moment as it was all just so bizarre to me, the sensation, so new…  Even so, I did nothing. Just a couple of words to say goodbye. 

“Hey, idiot! Don't do anything stupid okay? I don’t want to be covering for you if you’re gone for too long man.” I say as all my thoughts pound my head, over and over, screaming at me to make him stop, to make him stay. 

He smiles at me and looks off into the trail beside the road. Jeremy lifts his hand forming a peace sign with his two fingers, as he walks off into the distance. 

It was dark that day, quite darker than when it should be. 

I shut the door behind me and walked over to the couch, sighing as the cushions pushed against my body. It was a tiring day, even a boring one at that, but I still could not shake that damned feeling. What the hell was it?

As if all of my fear and dread crumpled itself into one gigantic mess of a feeling, just mushed itself into my brain. That alone, I will never be able to handle feeling that again.

My vision goes bleak as my world starts to go black. Next thing I know it's early in the morning, and my parents are back home from the business trip they went on about a week ago. I never really knew much about what their work was. It was always something important like meetings or gatherings, but they didn’t once mention to me what kind of jobs they both had. I mean, it brings in good money for the family so I don't really ask those kinds of questions anymore. 

I went on with my usual routine. Took a bath, brushed my teeth, wore comfy clothes and set up the table downstairs for school with Jeremy. Our parents have this system wherein every other day, school takes place at the other person's house. They say it's good for us, a way of being mobile or something? I just remember my dad saying that it was good that we weren't just in one place all the time for school, he always wanted me to try out different things. My dad really tries to teach me about “flexibility” and how it'll be useful to me soon.

It took about thirty minutes for my teacher Ms. Diana to arrive. She was a good teacher, but a better person at that. Everyday after class, she would always give me a little pep talk, often saying that I was going to achieve great things in life, with the usual “if you put your mind to it” kinda stuff. Although Ms. Diana did put a twist on things, saying that I was an exception, that I was a special kid. I always felt really good after speaking with her. She was sort of this gigantic reassuring pillow that would always be able to brighten up a gloomy day. 

Ms. Diana always came into the house with a big greeting as if we were her long lost relatives, never once dropping that beautiful smile on her face. 

“Good morning Bobby!” Ms. Diana said, glowing with joy as she did so. 

“Good morning it is! Hello Ms. Diana!” I said, running over with a smile on my face. 

She opened up her arms for a hug and I of course ran straight into them. A few seconds pass and we walk over to the table to go over our schedule for the day. Today I had English, Math, Science and….

Wait. Something is missing. There seems to be some part of my day that isn’t right at all. I know something was missing. Someone. But, who? Why is it so hard to remember? I couldn’t have forgotten anything too important right? It's always the same, isn't it? Wake up, brush my teeth, get ready, go to school. There is something I'm forgetting. Jeez, what's happening to me? 

As I start realizing that something is really off, I get that same, unforgettable feeling. That pounding in my head. I could hear everything, everyone, all at once. Just all mushed up into my mind. All the fear, all the dread. I felt absolutely everything. From the smallest creak in the floor to the slightest buzzing of the light in the kitchen. Why won’t it stop? What can’t I remember?   
Out of nowhere, it clicks. Jeremy. He wasn’t here with me. How could I forget? My best friend, my only friend! The one who goes to school with me everyday since he got here! How could I have forgotten about that? 

I gained back consciousness from my trance and looked at Ms. Diana, sweat pouring down my face.

“Dear boy, what’s happened to you? Do you feel sick Bobby?” Her eyes widened as she spoke. 

I couldn’t do anything else but shake my head. It's as if the realization had halted all my senses at once. Somehow, I knew that it was not supposed to happen. Remembering my only friend, it felt so wrong to do so, like I was committing sin against something. I was yet to understand.

“How is that even possible? He isn’t here, and I didn’t notice!” I shouted at Ms. Diana.

“Bobby, what do you mean? Who isn’t here?” She asked me, her head tilting in confusion. 

I gave her a puzzled look as I stood up from my chair to try and get any kind of sense from what was going on. 

“Jeremy! Jeremy isn’t here Ms. Diana! It’s like I forgot that he even existed? How is that even possible?” I flail my hands around trying to tell Ms. Diana.

This was by far the only time Ms. Diana looked at me like I made absolutely no sense in what I was saying. I knew by the expression on her face that she had forgotten too. The way she looked at me was way different from any other time I was acting out in the past. I bet she actually thought that I had lost my mind. I mean from my trance-like state and my frantic screaming, while running along the house, anyone would think I went mad. Ms. Diana always tried to look for the good in things. I knew that somehow she was still trying to think of ways to see my actions as justified, saying that I must have been triggered by something. 

What the hell is going on?
