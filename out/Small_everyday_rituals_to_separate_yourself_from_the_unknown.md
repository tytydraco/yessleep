They say that people who are drawn towards the unknown attract the unknown. If you’ve been lurking here and find yourself enthralled and curious about the unknown, the spiritual, the urban legends, you’re susceptible to letting them into your lives. So I thought I’ll pass on some caution to the wind for those of you who have more than what you can handle lurking about.

My grandmother taught me these small rituals, which were then cemented by seeing my aunts and uncles and other elders in the village practice them. And if you find yourselves needing that bit of protection, or sense that uncertain unsettling feeling come over you you could try these. I’ll also add an anecdote to help you understand how it works.

One. 

If you return back home late at ungodly hours, always spit out your doorway. No spirit or evil should feel welcome in your home, spitting is generally seen as an unwelcome gesture and you need to signal that.

When I was studying in a nearby town I had stayed at a dormitory where a senior would encounter a strange thing. It was late at night when she after spending a restless night decided to take a walk to relieve herself, in our dormitory back then the washrooms were located outside the building and she went there. When returning she felt a strange presence following her, she tried to shrug it off and went back to her bed. As soon as she laid back down she could feel someone staring at her, she looked around and found nothing. She closed her eyes and pulled the blanket over her and pretended to sleep when she felt the weight of the bed shift like something had sat by her legs. She got up in panic and in the dark scampered to find her bible first. She turned on her lights, prayed and then went out the door and spit out, locking the door behind her. She was then able to go to sleep. Although this encounter left her with raging fever for days as is common when you make contact with the non living.

Two.

When you visit unknown places, large open spaces, forests or areas which are inhabited, abandoned or isolated, always check your shadow and make sure you leave with it. To ensure it, call your name and ask your shadow to follow you.

The tribe believe in spirits that still inhabit the untouched lands and spaces, and oftentimes our own shadow and spirits are susceptible to be swayed away, stolen or sometimes if your spirit is unhappy it decides to stay back. While leaving call out your name and say “lets go” so that you return with your spirit.

My uncle in his youth went to the woods with his friends for a swim and when he returned he went straight back to bed. He remained unresponsive, dazed and a shell of who he was. Since he was often up to no good, it was alluded to him taking substances but my grandmother discovered that he had been casting no shadow as the daylight broke in the next day. Alarmed the whole village came together to help retrieve his shadow, while my young uncle dazed in bed laid without eating or drinking, not reacting. The way to do so was to open all the doors and windows and then throw a feast with what my uncle liked. His favourite food, drink and items were kept out on the table and everyone started calling his name to lure him back home. After three days, as twilight set in they heard my uncle’s voice respond to my grandfather’s. When my grandfather asked him to come back the spirit refused, his voice was clear and came from the woods while my uncle’s body still laid in bed dazed, his lips unmoving. Finally my grandfather was able to persuade him to come back after which my uncle’s body broke out in fever for a few days and his shadow had returned. They say that it was luck that they were able to lure him home instead of having to go to the woods to fetch him. My uncle was never the same after that, he left the village, lived a life away from the city, as far away from the woods as he could.

To this day it is a practice, for the farmers after a long day of working in isolated fields call their own names beckoning their shadows to follow along as they return home. Also do not use your real name, use your nickname that your loved ones call you. No good comes from giving away your real name.

Three.

Burn old items. You need to let the spirits know the mark of the living.

If you live in cities with the noise, the hustle and the bustle you will not need to do this as spirits tend to stay away from densely populated living areas. However if you ever end up staying in a remote place or somewhere further from the population, find some old items and burn them. Or start a fire and burn an old sock or string.

A farmer was once out in the fields, after working through the evening it was too late to return to his home. He decided to sleep the night away at the small farm hut, usually built to store tools and keep overnight watch. Since he was unprepared he wasn't able to bring any match. He collected the tinder and two sticks and started rubbing away to start a fire. As he busied himself he felt a cold chill run through him, outside the hut he could hear chatter of a man and a woman with a child. He was already very far out from the village settlement and it made no sense to have people there, he thought maybe they were just people passing by and he should greet them. But his guts told him to keep making the fire. As he started to vigorously increase the rubbing he felt the voices come closer to his hut. He panicked and rubbed harder till the tinder started smoking, and as he blew into the tinder and fanned the first flame the cold dissipated and the voices disappeared. He stayed inside, afraid to look out, tending to the fire. It was in the morning when the first light hit that he remembered the warnings by the villagers about the spirit of a man, woman and child that would walk through the village every evening and those who had encountered them with their eyes would always fall sick or sometimes die. 

So there you go, these are stories passed down from my family. Things we still practice to keep ourselves separated from the non living and the unknown. Even if you may think spirits are non malevolent, they are never meant to mingle with the living. And you’d be wise to maintain that boundary.
