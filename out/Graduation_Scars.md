Before high school graduation, my friends JC, Daniel, Evan, Valentino and I wanted to explore one more abandoned building. Urbex was a hobby all of us did, and we were visiting new spots all over Cali nearly every two months. This time was no different.



It was around 7:00 PM, and we’d all had dinner beforehand so none of us would go hungry during the exploration… except Daniel “forgot” to eat, so he came with us on an empty stomach. 



He’d also picked the location; it was this old psych ward hidden in some corner of LA. The whole area was deserted, and the nearest building was over a five minute walk away. This fog also clouded the area whenever you’d get near it, which we all thought was cool, to be honest. 



Like always, we’d brought our own gear: a flashlight, first-aid kits, a knife (JC came with a wrench and Valentino brought a screwdriver), and a phone to take videos.



As soon as we climbed inside through a broken window, the vibe completely changed within the group. We were all excited beforehand, but upon entering, we collectively KNEW something was wrong. During nearly every exploration, at least one of us felt this way. Rarely did we ever all feel this sense of looming dread that felt like the building was going to collapse on us at any moment.



The interior was disgusting: all of the cells smelled, unknown liquids continuously dripped from the ceiling, and debris littered all over the building. I guess I wasn’t so wrong to feel that it’d collapse, but the dread encapsulated so much more than that. 



By this time, Daniel skipping dinner must’ve caught up to him, because he proposed that we’d split up to get more photos and videos much faster. I guess everyone had brushed off their anxieties, because eventually, we did split up, and I went with Daniel and JC. Evan and Valentino paired up and went off into the dark psych ward together.



After discovering a few needles and getting scared by spiders, my group made our way to the second floor where most of the cells were. We were able to get a good view of Evan and Valentino from across the building, so we didn’t worry about them much. In fact, by this point, we weren’t worried at all. All of our paranoia had completely disappeared, even when a random tile fell in front of us; we all had a hearty chuckle afterwards. After tiptoeing through the psych ward even further, we’d found this corridor that led to a part of the building we hadn’t even discovered yet. We quietly walked through the corridor, but by the time we made it to the end, I froze. I told my friends that I was scared, but because it was our last building before graduation, we just kept going. The looming dread finally returned like a bucket of ice being dumped over us, or maybe that was because the room grew cold and there were goosebumps all over my body. Then we saw him. A random man in a torn up business outfit. He somehow looked more worn out than it. 



Daniel, JC, and I moved back carefully. We didn’t want to disturb him. His back was turned, and he just limped around and breathed heavily like he’d just completed a marathon. Either way, the vibe was clear: this guy was DANGEROUS and it wasn’t worth getting any closer to him. 



As we stepped back into the corridor, I saw the man turn from the corner of my eye. With zero hesitation, I started to run, and I guess my friends noticed him turn, too, because they sprinted with me. That’s when we heard metallic scraping echo through the corridor. Out of morbid curiosity, I turned around for only a second…



I don’t know what terrified me more: his blank, possessed expression, or the fact that he was dragging a large machete on the wall while chasing us. I swear I saw sparks flying. 



We screamed for help, hoping to get Evan and Valentino’s attention. But we got no response. What were we thinking? They were most likely on the other side of the building anyway. And just when things couldn’t get worse, JC tripped by the stairs and broke his ankle falling down. Daniel and I tried picking him up, but he didn’t want to slow us down. We carried him anyway, but the man in the suit was still behind us. In a matter of seconds, I felt a sharp, burning pain in my left rib. I looked over my shoulder, and that’s when I saw him. The man in the suit just stared at me, completely expressionless. When I looked down, I realized I’d been stabbed, but he quickly pulled out the machete, pushing me back. Daniel punched him so hard, he fell back, nearly tripping. But it only looked like he gripped his machete tighter. 



The man in the suit swung at Daniel, but he dodged and punched him in the face again. JC and I screamed for help, in hopes Evan and Valentino could hear us. Suddenly, Valentino appeared behind the man and kicked him. Furious, the man turned to him, about to swing. Then, Valentino went, “WAIT, WAIT!” and was able to convince him to move slowly before a large tile dropped from the second floor, “knocking” the man out. When I looked up, I saw Evan with his arms still out. He dropped the tile, and he looked horrified. He didn’t have the face of someone who saved his whole friend group, he KNEW he’d just killed someone.



I remembered he said something like, “Are you guys okay?” Before we all walked out quietly. Only Valentino saw how the man looked after the tile dropped. When we asked how he looked, he just said, “Dead.”



It was awkward telling our parents what had happened, especially because they were always afraid of squatters and someone getting injured. Thankfully, we were at the hospital already, so we didn’t get any spankings. To honor their wishes, however, we promised to never explore an abandoned building again. How could we anyway when some of us were going to college out of state? 



Even as a Sophomore in college, I still vividly remember that night more than any other exploration. The doctors said that if the man stabbed any further to the right, he would’ve pierced my intestine.
