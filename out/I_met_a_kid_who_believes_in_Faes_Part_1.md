The woods near my house are dense. Packed tightly in a cluster that if one was to go in blind, one might never leave. The woods were also ancient. Older than my father and his father and his father. They surrounded my house and they trapped me. I could leave anytime but I always found myself coming back. It was my home, although it was my fathers. But to respect my old man, I stayed. The house itself was a normal wooden cabin that my grandfather built when he was a teen. My father worked as a park ranger and I did too. It is like what some folk say, “I did not choose the park ranger life. The park ranger life chose me”

The woods around my house are technically part of a national forest but due to land claims, they belong to me. Thus, whoever stepped into the woods was considered trespassing and I had every right to shoot at them. 

It was Sunday Evening when a knock came at my door. Usually visitors would not go near my property as big yellow “GET-OUT” signs were tapped all around trees. This had to be a teen, a prankster, or my boss who was the only one I dared not shoot. Opening the door, I was met with an unfamiliar face. He did not look like any of the teens that I knew but he was a teen.  His eyes darted around suspiciously and he spoke, “Mister, do you know where the main trail is? I was camping with my friends and I needed to leak. But I could not find my way back”.

“Alright, I’ll help you”, but under my breath, I cursed at him and his friends for not picking a designated camping site. At the national forest, there were many off-grid campers that got lost easily. It just so happened that my house was near one of the off-grid camps. The teen looked 15 and possibly a boy scout, which made me even more pissed.

If I was going to have to take a kid back to his camp and miss my dinner, I would need to get a good reward. In the past, I had gotten free food, supplies, and more. I never got any money because I didn’t need it. Everything in my house was built by myself and I had a stable income. 

When I came out with my supplies, the kid was in the front area of my house. His figure looked taller than when I first met him.  But, it was getting dark and human shapes would distort in various shapes. 

I held out my flashlight and illuminated the forest ahead of me. At this region, the sun set pretty fast and when the kid had arrived, it was already starting to get dark. Now, everything was pitch black and I could only see in front of me in the flash light’s glow. 

“Alright, we are moving. If you dare to leave or pull anything, I will abandon you” I said as I marched into the forest. 

The kid did not say anything and only followed. Unlike the kid, I knew these woods like the back of my hand. Contrary to popular belief, even though the woods were dense, they eventually spread out. On this day, the moon was at a peak meaning that once I left the woods in front of my house, I could use the moon to navigate. 

The first few minutes were uneventful and the only sounds I heard were birds chirping. But when an hour passed, the boy finally asked, “Do you believe in anything? I heard stories about the woods” 

But before he could continue, I cut him off and said, “At this moment, ghost stories are not your biggest concern. You have friends to get to” 

But he did not move. His eyes darted around again before sighing. Then he started walking. 

Now, I was really angry. This fool must have gone online and watched some video about skinwalkers and now he’s asking me. 

I had been in these woods for 40 years and only left it twice for college and my first job. There was no such thing as skinwalkers. 

The rest of the journey was uneventful as expected. But soon we reached the clearing that had a creek next to it. The creek ran parallel between two banks and on the other side, the woods were a little more sparse.

They still were dense but it was enough for the moonlight to penetrate through. 

At the start of the river, the kid stopped again and this time he looked at me. When we were walking, I was in front of him but now he was in front of me. 

“How did you-?”

“Let me ask you again Mister,  what do you believe in”? The kid said but now his voice started to lack emotions.

“For the second time, I don’t. Look here, do you want to return or not”? 

The kid looked at me for a minute before saying, “Then how can you help me find my friends? If you don’t believe”

I stood there mouth agape as the kid repeated the same phrase again. But this time, his cadence was off. There was something that sounded monotone about how he spoke. Like it was a recording that was played back. 

“What are your friends”? I asked, still keeping a calm composure.

The boy looked at the moon and at the water, then at me. He knelt down and started drawing three figures. 

Three figures that I had not seen in 40 years. Three distinct figures that my grandfather wrote tales about. 

In front of me were a rabbit, a fox, and a deer. The fae as my father had said. But to me, they were just illustrations. As a child, I believed in them. But now I am an adult.

This was getting overtly stupid. A fantasy kid gets lost from his family and now is talking about his friends being faes. I had enough. 

But just as I was going to return to my house, I heard a second voice. It was not the kid’s as it was deeper, more primal. It only said one word,

“Micheal” before something white and fast rushed at me.

“How do you know my name?” I gasped as the world began to spin. 

Then everything grew quiet, too quiet.
