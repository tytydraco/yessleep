Each crack I made to the rusted steel lying shattered in front of me got me closer to leaving the east wing of the shop.  Knick and I had been commandeered to work on a "special task" led by Dan (resident shop-dad) and we were shaking to get back to the main area for an average workday.  My arms burned as much as my ego every time I saw the old man watching us as the smoke dripped from the cigarette lazily hanging from his greasy soot-stained lips.

"I think I'm done!"  I yelled.

Dan hobbled over one wheeze at a time.  "Hmm.  No!  Look at the marks!"  Dan points as if his arm were asleep while smoke slowly spread around my face like the beginning of a storm.

"That's from the hammer!"

"Go ova it again!  Make sure you can't see any marks."

"The hammer creates those marks!”

Dan picks up a shard to inspect it.  “Look!  Ya see that?!”

I look to see a white mark that could have been dust.  “The mark?”

“Yes sir!”  Dan looked at me like he taught me a skill.

Knick pops his head over my shoulder.  "Why don't we just use the torch and buzz saw?"

Dan turned his attention away from me and glared at Knick as if a rat were found chewing on his food.  "Whataya stupid?!  Ya gonna have shards everywhere!"  Dan stood up straight and coughed.  "You need to shape up, dude!"  Dan put his cigarette out on one of the metal shards and turned to walk to a dark corner while holding a different cigarette.

Knick took a deep breath and turned his head back to his own shard pile.  I tried to focus but I could feel his eyes glued to the back of our heads despite him not being in eye sight.  The sound of his breathing cut through the sound of our tools.

\*\*\*\*

With the east wing being filled with junk from old cars, rotted building parts, and chemical scum from our cleaning supply that we refused to discard despite this branch having enough violations to shut us down if anyone cared enough to follow up on an inspection, Greg had a good reason for his rule barring people from entering.

"Dan's making us work in the east wing!"

"No one's allowed in the east wing....."

"That's what we told Dan!"

"Hmm.  I have to have a talk with him.  I'm going to pick up some parts.  Do your best to stay out of trouble and don't get hurt."

Greg didn’t seem to have a clear sense for enforcing these rules.

\*\*\*\*

Knick sat on a bucket near one of the dusty corners.  I was banging the metal as if it would actually accomplish something while glancing in the corner of my eye to see Dan breathing down Knick’s neck.

”Ya doin it wrong!”

Knick seemed too exhausted to flinch.  “I’m….making progress.“  His voice naturally became light and dainty when confronting Dan’s remarks.

Dan grabs the hammer.  “Ya do it like this!!“ Dan hits the corner of the shard causing a tiny piece to fly and slice at Knick’s cheek.  ”Whoops!  Sorry dude!”

Knick took a step back to grab his cheek.  “what the hell!“

Dan looked as if a small dog were barking at him.  “Relax.  you’re okey.”  He sounded, almost, bore.

Dan continued to hit the metal like he was trying to find the most efficient way to cause damage.“Whataya waiting for!!??  Grab a tool and help!!“

Knick looked around for a moment.  “I need a hammer!”

”Knick!!“  Dan shook his head slowly.  “Grab something else!  Use ya brain!”  He went back to hitting the shards.

Knick was gaining less ground while using the wrench to break the sharp metal in front of him while I continued my pursuit hoping that constant scraping and smashing had lolled Dan into a state of neurotic passivity that would cause him to project onto something other than us.

Without warning, Dan stood up and dropped the hammer.  Mouth agape, he coughed up a gush of grey phlegm.  “That sucked!”.  Dan left our field of vision.  Knick and I gave it a few more minutes till we felt like we could take a break.

"God!!  Why does it still feel like he's still next to us!?

"Fuck him!......."  Knick took a swig of his energy drink.  "It should have been him that blew up!”  Knick spit an orange glob of phlegm on a piece of glass.  "Why did it have to be Jake?!"

\*\*\*\*  
Dan’s old partner Jake got caught in the explosion of the old septic truck after it over heated for the hundredth time in a couple months.  Greg rented a new septic truck the next week so Dan could get back to work asap.

Dan didn‘t get a day off, wasn’t given time to grieve, and didn’t even get a chance to take it easy in the office as an ’interim period.  He was just given the keys to the new truck and expected to go on as if no explosion happened and Jake went on vacation.

Dan spent most of his workdays at the dump pumping off, but it was rumored he spent most of his time in his truck across the street smoking his cigarettes.  He stopped socializing with the rest of the shop and became a self appointed manager.  He was very short with people and would yell as if he were an angry father.

Dan began to project all his insecurities on Topher, a guy who was a bit lazy, but a Joy to have around.  Dan decided to take him “under his wing”.  This began to make Topher act weird and forgetful.  He seemed like he never got enough rest.  Everyone assumed he quit due to how he stormed out of a shift.  We never saw him again after that.

\*\*\*\*

After not hearing from the old man for almost thirty minutes, Knick and I took to wandering the dilapidated rusty maze in search of anything interesting.  Aside from us, this place hadn't been touched in years, yet the accumulation was constant.   We found an old forklift next to a mountain of pipes that had been ripped out of the tin walls and decided to sit for a bit and try to ignore the occasional sound of the loose metal being shaken by the howling wind.

Every clink, bang, echo, and distant footstep made us flinch and reach for our tools.  I took, almost, inaudible deep breaths and counted to five in my head repeatedly until I could match the energy Knick seemed to have; always aloof.  

My final breath came out as large exhale like I sat down on a comfortable couch.  
“What ya doing this weekend?”

” Probably gamble.”  Knick spit next to the table.  “Weekends are always too short.”

” I get it."  I pretended not to notice the spit.  "I hate how fast the month is going!  I feel like it was just Valentines Day!"  I kept glancing at his spit, knowing it was there to stay.  I would glance and hope that it would be dry, but it was always wet.

"I can't believe it's been a month since Max disappeared."  Knick turned his head to me as if he heard a dog bark.  "How has no one been looking for him?!"

\*\*\*\*

Max vanished a month ago after having an argument with Dan near the septic truck.  The only thing we know for sure is that he never left the shop as his car was still in the lot.  Due to Greg waiting for room to clear out of the lot, he waited a week before calling the police.

\*\*\*\*

The sound of moist slippers slapping on concrete mixed with a cacophony of labored wheezing brought us back to the present.

Despite the constant whirling of the old greasy vents, we could easily tell where Dan's chorus of bodily breakdown was coming from.  The smell was also a dead giveaway.  Since working in the east wing, I became aware of a smell I never directly noticed before.  The smell made me think of old houses I used to play in as a kid and stores that no longer existed.  The smell was never something I thought about before coming to this side of the shop, and Dan had begun to smell like it all the time.

Knick and I had been behind a beam watching pops stand by an old septic tuck inhaling stick after stick while he stared at a charred pile of equipment that looked like it was freshly burned.

“Ffangule!” Dan spoke sternly as he doused the charred pile with gasoline.  We watched while Dan used the flame to light his cigarette and watch the fire as if he were watching Knick and I smack the metal shards.  We watched, frozen, in our hiding spot until the sound of a leaf blower could be heard amongst the series of other sounds.  We decided, in unison, to follow the sound of the leaf blower and hope we didn't hit any dead ends.

\*\*\*\*

”Greg!!   You need to do something about Dan!!”

Greg had a look of reserve as if he knew what we were going to say.  “What’s wrong?”

”Dan's lighting shit on fire in the east wing!!”

”Hmmm.”

”This was just in the middle of the floor next to charred metal!  I think he's been doing this for a bit!”

”Hmmm.  I’ll talk about to him in a bit.“  He started to write something down as if we were giving him inventory on gloves.  “Dan‘s been emotional lately.  The old man can barely finish a job without crying about his back or some shit.”  Greg looked up and started to laugh as if we Were in on some joke.  He never stopped laughing and his knuckles got tighter as his voice began to get hoarse.

\*\*\*\*

"I'm not going back!" Knick just turned and headed back to the main shop ignoring what could have been a death stare fifty feet behind us.  I followed only glancing back to see an apathetic faced Dan Glaring like he gave up trying to convince us of something.  I swiftly turned to Knick.  "The dude's staring!"  Knick just kept walking as if I didn't say a word.

We spent the next thirty minutes cleaning units when we heard a growl echo through the yard like the beginning of an attack.  We turned to find Dan driving the broken septic truck into the valley of the lot leading to an exit that hadn't been used in years.
