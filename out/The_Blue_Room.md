It appeared one day. An extra white door occupying the usually empty wall.

I noticed it when I got home from work, at around 11pm.

And it was begging me to open it.

So I obliged, turning the silver knob and pushing the door open, revealing a big blue room.

My first thought was that it was similar to those “liminal spaces” that I had seen online, but limited to one room. Imagine something like the backrooms, but instead of the wallpaper being yellow, it was blue.

The sky blue color was inviting and pleasing to look at. I stepped in, closing the door behind me. I don’t know if it was the effect of the room, but it felt… calming. Like it was reassuring me, telling me everything was going to be alright.

A wave of tranquility overcame my mind. Tears started streaming from my eyes.

I really needed this emotional release at the time because nothing was alright. 

I lived a life of abuse. My parents weren’t very well off. Once I graduated from high school, I was kicked out to of the house. I don’t remember how long I spent sleeping on the streets.

Eventually, I found some jobs and saved up enough to rent a small apartment.

I saved what I could. Being poor teaches you things about money, but the system makes the poor become poorer. Never buy takeout, I told myself. Only buy groceries.

Now here I am. Here, in my sorry excuse of a house, crying in a blue room that came from nowhere. 

Experiences like mine make you strong, but often at the expense of suppressing your emotions.

I cried there for what seemed like five minutes, and then used my sleeve to wipe tears from my eyes. I turned back into the house. The moment the door clicked closed, it vanished completely.

I looked forward to and went into the room daily without fail. After work, I invited myself in, sitting in the center while staring into the blue, thinking about nothing.

It was about a week in when objects started appearing. The room summoned objects that matched my wants in that moment.

One day I came home with an urge to watch a new show. But first, the room. 

The door waiting for me in the living room like always.

But this time, in the center of the space was a tv with a couch sitting in front of it.

I was taken aback. How could it read me?

Nonetheless, I settled onto the couch and started watching a new show on Netflix. The seemingly new tv was already logged into my account.

I watched for about two hours before I decided I needed to make dinner. I turned the tv off and came back into the living room.

But… why was it still light outside?

I checked the clock. It was around the same time I went into the room.

And that was how I discovered time didn’t pass there. I just didn’t stay long enough to notice it before.

I depended on that room. It helped keep life going, to give me something to look forward to everyday. A break from reality.

Sometimes I spent hours on end in that room exploring new hobbies. Drawing, shooting basketball hoops, pottery, you name it. The room gave me everything I needed. I finally had time to do things outside work and survival. I had time to be happy.

Then came today.

I was excited to go into the room like always. I set down my things and ate something small, humming a tune. 

After eating, I walked straight to the door. Excitedly, I turned the handle and into the sky blue hue.

The door vanished once I stepped in, replaced by wall.

And before me stood that sorry, sorry excuse for a house.

My house.

Because that’s what I really wanted. To live inside here forever.

I don’t know if I will age. I don’t know if I can ever go back to the real world.

So here I am, trapped in this moment of time. I have been living in this exact second of this exact day for a very, very long time.

But it doesn’t bother me.

At least I am happy.
