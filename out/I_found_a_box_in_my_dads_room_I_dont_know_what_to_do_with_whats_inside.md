So I just got back from burying my dad three days ago and I haven't really been sleeping. Tonight I went into his room to start going through his stuff. Not because I was ready, I'm not, but because standing in the hallway outside his door was starting to feel worse than just going in.

He kept his room pretty sparse. My dad was not a stuff guy. A dresser, a bed, a nightstand with a lamp and a book he'd been reading for probably two years. I don't think he ever finished it. I don't know why that's the thing getting to me right now but it is.

Anyway. Under the bed, pushed all the way to the back, you had to really reach for it. There was a box. Metal, like a document box, the kind you'd get at an office supply store. Locked. I didn't even know my dad owned anything with a lock on it.

I found the key in his nightstand drawer, taped to the inside back panel. Like he'd hidden it but not very hard. Like maybe part of him wanted it to be found eventually.

Inside the box there were folders. Documents. I haven't gone through all of them yet but I got through the first layer and then I had to stop and I've been sitting on his bedroom floor for about an hour trying to decide if I should keep going.

The first thing I pulled out was a letter. Handwritten, my dad's handwriting, dated March 1968. Addressed to a "Director Harlan" at what appears to be a CIA department I've never heard of. My dad worked for the government his whole career — I knew that, it was never a secret — but he told me he was an analyst. Boring desk stuff, he always said. Budget reports.

This letter is not about budget reports.

I'm going to post it here because I genuinely don't know what else to do with it and I haven't slept in three days and my brain isn't working right. If anyone has any idea what they're looking at please tell me.

>*Director Harlan*,  
*I am writing to formally raise concerns regarding the trajectory of Project GREENFIELD following the conclusions drawn from our Q4 1967 assessment.*   
  
*As you are aware, the cellular response observed in the Phase 3 specimens has exceeded all projected parameters by a significant margin. What was initially documented as accelerated growth has presented in the most recent samples as something our current models cannot adequately explain.*

*I want to be precise here because I think precision matters: we are not observing mutation in the traditional sense. The specimens are not simply changing. They are responding. The distinction may seem semantic but I do not believe it is.*

*I am requesting a formal independent review of the Phase 3 data before we proceed to the next stage. I think we owe it to the integrity of the project to be certain we understand what we are looking at before we continue.*

*I recognize this request may be seen as overcautious. I would rather be overcautious than the alternative.*

*Respectfully*,  
*M. Dune*  
*Project GREENFIELD — Biological Research Division*  
*March 14, 1968*

I don't know what Phase 3 is. I don't know what the specimens are. I don't know who Director Harlan is.

My dad was a budget analyst.

I'm going to try to sleep. I'll probably go through more of the box tomorrow. I just needed to put this somewhere.
