"What?"

I grabbed the flask from the back of my satchel, a drop of whiskey hit my shirt and many more followed on the ground. The wilting grass curled and wisped around us.



“You heard me. Look around you. All of it.”

“Sir you’re not making sense.”

“Neither does a damn thing we do to change things. Same outcomes, every single time.”



Nobody can remember when, it just started. The sky bleeds out into the night. Beautiful colors sure, still blood. Reds and oranges and yellows. Violets and subtle pinks meet at the canopy, the exit wound. 



“Outcomes?”

“Yes. The same story at the end of a pointless book, pointless people and pointless lessons.”

“I wouldn’t call the things we’ve brought to the world pointless.”

“Do you remember that small tribe we met a few weeks back? The one with the weird kid, crazy birthmark?”

“Yes I do remem….”

“I checked the holomap. Dead. All of ‘em. It’s nothing but a smear of dried red on the rocks now.”

“Well I wouldn’t say that is the outcome of our average encounter.”

“How could you know that? How could you know for certain that no matter how many things we’ve done, lives we improved, that it all ends with a stain on the ground and another one of those abominations fed?”



After the exit wound was made, not long after did it begin to open. Small at first, a strange plant here or an even stranger frog there. No one could have known. The scholars were the first who sought this as divine, that God himself began to spill out into creation. This was the first mistake. Many more came to follow after.



“But the weapons sir. Were they not sufficient?”

“For a time sure, but what happens when the energy in them runs out, when they have no idea what its made of or how to make more of it? When they finally stop waiting and their screams are all that fills the air? I’ll tell you what happens. One more tally mark on a list of bodies, that’s what.



The nights have always been the hardest here. The wailing. God the wailing. Something so convincingly human yet so unnatural. That was likely what brought the first wave of bodies. Even as I write this the retching seeps out of me. I looked over to my partner as he turned his head towards the forest.



“What’s your problem?”

“Nothing, it’s just… I hear them. All of them. They want me to be with them.”

“God damn… Get a grip on yourself man! We’ve been over this. Those aren’t people!”



He turned his head back to me and what I saw, I would not wish to describe even for the reader now. I will only say that I have never seen someone's eyes do that before or since. He was on me like a rabid dog and no matter what I said to him it couldn’t break through. I pushed him off and as he raised to meet me, he darted back into the black abyss of the night. I haven’t seem him since. The idea that I could go on without him left my mind many days ago, I have not resigned myself to death yet. However the thought looms ever closer. Most days I walk, I sit and I write. I don’t know why I started it just seemed like a good place to get down my thoughts. 



I found another holomap today. This one had an old web-browser attached to it. I have been lurking on this site for some time now and this place seems as good as any. Many of you people seem to share the worst encounters of your life. I commend all of you. I don’t even know if this post will reach out or simply be denied. In any case, I plead with you, all of you. If the wound has not opened in your worlds yet, PLEASE, DO NOT LET IT. I may not know what causes it but I hope the knowledge of it’s existence along with a glimpse into what horrors it brings can help you see the signs. Please, for my sake.
