The soft yellow lights illuminated the main station. There was no one to be found around me. It was a peculiar feeling to be all alone here, with silence only being broken by random announcements, whereas you had to wrestle with the crowds to make your way just a few hours ago.

I had a few drinks too many at the company party. Not enough to get drunk, enough to regret it tomorrow morning. I was waiting to catch the late night train to my hometown.

The announcement rang out. "The late night train bound for..."

I phased it out. I liked being organized in terms of transport and had checked the departure timetable on my phone beforehand. Plus, my head was killing me and that loud, monotone female voice accompanied by static buzzing was not helping at all.

Still, just to be sure, I glanced at the giant digital departure board hanging from the ceiling in the distance, double checking, as was my habit.

"Great", I mumbled, "30 minutes to go, I'm thirsty as hell and the store here closed half an hour ago."

Thirty minutes of waiting and an hour of travel with a dry throat was not something I was looking forward to.

Even though it was futile, I decided to take a walk back to the store, if for nothing else then to make the time go by.

My phone buzzed in my pocket as my footsteps echoed on the tiled patio. I opened the message.

"I'll be parked across the street, next to the pet shop. Are you on your way?" Joseph was a good friend of mine, when we spoke earlier that day he offered to come pick me up. An offer I graciously accepted.

"It leaves in about half an hour. I'll text you when I board, okay?" I typed out quickly, still walking.

"Ughhh 30 mins, you couldn't have licked your Supervizor's boots faster and got on an earlier train? Sucker. You owe me a drink." A reply shot back.

I smirked and put my phone away. Now facing a closed storefront, I pondered where else I could get water. The station bathrooms were locked after 10 PM, and it was after midnight, so it was no use.

Instinctively I reached for a cigarette but decided not to. Drying my throat out even more was not a wise move.

I decided to walk back and just wait it out.

"It's closed, isn't it?", a voice rang out behind me.

Almost mechanically, I swirled around.

A young woman stood in front of me.

"Yeah. Yes." I proclaimed, startled. I heard no one walking yet she was standing there, a few feet from me.

"Awh, damn it." She pouted. One look at her face and figure revealed a portrait of almost decadent beauty. The kind where you feel unworthy of staring. Long, straight black hair perfectly complimented her shapely eyes, aquiline nose and full bodied, wine red lips.

She wore a black long coat with a burgundy red blouse and trousers underneath. The coat was swaying in the breeze.

An unpleasant silence descended. She looked directly at me, as if expecting me to continue this brief conversation.

"You...You're also traveling? I inquired.

"Oh, not at all. A late night at the office." She replied, adjusting her hair.

"Right. Yea. So... What brings you here?" I asked.

"Hunger. I'm hungry." She briskly walked towards me, then raised her hand and pointed at the store window. "They have my favourite pretzels here. Can't find them anywhere else."

"You really came here just for the pretzels?" I asked.

"I'm really craving them. The ones you find here are really tasty. Perfect late night snack." She said.

I took a step back. "It seems that we are both out of luck then. I am thirsty as hell. Still have some time until departure so I decided to try my luck." I said.

Her lips formed a courteous but balanced smile. "I think I can help." She said before producing a medium sized metallic bottle from her inner pocket.

"It's your lucky day. Or night, I guess. I usually just leave it at the office. Not much in there but it's better then nothing, right?" She chirped as she handed me the bottle.

I gratefully accepted. Our hands brushed as I took the offered bottle. Her skin was soft and silky.

The container was about half full. It felt weird drinking out of a metallic bottle though. I remember thinking it was like the water itself had a weird, metallic aftertaste. I did not like it one bit. I decided a gulp or two was enough.

"Thank you." I said, closing the bottle and handing it back.

"Oh, don't mention it." She stored the bottle before extending her hand, expecting a handshake.

"Melrose." She said.

"Michael." I replied.

"Nice to meet you, Michael." She smiled. "Mind if I stick around while you wait?"

"Not at all."

I glanced that the departure board in passing as we returned. A migraine was starting to form in my right temple and pulse through my head. I sat on the bench and pointed for Melrose to take a seat as well. She remained standing.

"Sooo, what do you do?" She inquired as she stepped forward, her shadow eclipsing me.

"I'm an accountant."

She laughed. "No offense, but I figured as much."

I looked at her and feigned offense. "What's that supposed to mean?"

"You do look like a typical office drone." She teased.

"Ugh. It pays the bills." I cut the conversation short.

A few uncomfortable seconds passed between us.

"Sorry, I'm just teasing. I didn't want to insult you." She said apologetically, placing her hand on my shoulder.

"It's not that." I stammered. "It's just, I'm kinda tired and I just want to board that damn train and go home. I'm really sorry if I'm boring you to death." I concluded, chuckling.

"The train is not coming."

"What? Yes it is. It should be here in ten minutes."

"The train is not coming. The last train left about an hour ago. You must have had your wires crossed or something, mister accountant." She said.

I stood up and glanced at the timetable. At least, where it used to be. There was nothing there. "I don't understand." I muttered to myself. "What the fuck?"

"That is no way to speak in the presence of a lady." She teased. "I know a hostel where you can spend the night. I'll help you get there. If you act nice I might even visit you in the morning".

"No. It should be here. I know it should be here." My head was starting to hurt like hell. The pain spread out from my temple and soon enough developed into a full blown migraine.

"The train is not coming."

She grabbed my hand and started walking away. My legs were at odds with the rest of my body. My vision was starting to blur and I was lightheaded.

As we left the station I could have sworn I heard the announcement once again. 

"You can wait all you want, Michael. The train is not coming." 

The scenery just switched. Soon, she was leading me away from the station. The late night yellow hue was replaced by a barely illuminated alleyway I did not recognize.

"Where...We?" My lips were practically scraping against my teeth.

"It's just a few streets away. Don't you worry". Melrose replied. Somehow, her voice sounded different. I couldn't help but notice how deserted this alleyway seemed. We were on a narrow path, like a single lane road , surrounded by old, red brick buildings on both sides.

A vibrating sensation jolted me awake, if only for a moment.

My phone.

I was dragging behind and she was looking straight ahead, so I quietly slipped it out of my pocket and glanced at the screen.

"Come on, it arrived five mins ago. Hurry the fuck up man."

I could not type. My fingers were disjointed and clammy. It was not the wisest of decisions, but somehow I hit the call button and placed the phone against my ear.

As the beeping started, she stopped dead in her tracks and looked at me.

"What are you doing?"

Some primal instinct overtook me. I pushed her away. Barely. She was heavy. Heavier than you would expect for a woman of that size. Her body seemed almost dense.

"Mick? Mick? Where the fuck are you?" Joseph's voice rang out.

"The train. There was no train. I don't know. Help me."

"The fuck you mean there was no train? Are you fucked up or something? Did you not make..."

She came at me. Her movements were janky and unrefined now, almost beastly.

"Help me. Please." I murmured as I backed away from this woman.

"Joseph can't help you. Only I can help you." She hissed, parting her lips. She had too many teeth. Bubbly, yellow saliva was coating the lower part of her face and dripping down.

I stumbled and fell down. "What the fuck are you?" I cried.

"Imhungry" She wailed.

"Imhungryimhungryimhungry".

She got down on all fours and launched herself at me. I tried to back away but she latched herself to my right leg and jammed her teeth into my outer thigh.

Crying out in pain, an adrenaline rush surged through me. I became aware of the phone still in my hand. Desperately. I began hitting her on the head. I was hammering that phone into her skull, yet she did not subside her feast. I used my other free hand to try and pry her away from my leg.

I could feel the metallic object bend in my grasp. She still did not yield, her slurps and moans were interjected only by my screams.

She went to reposition herself and continue her feast, and I managed to fold my other leg and jam it between her shoulder blade and head. I pushed and heaved as she let out ear scraping screeching sounds from her bloodied mouth.

She slid downwards, around my knee and I felt the grip loosen. She janked her head up and gazed at me.

I seized the moment. I flung the phone. A sickening cracking sound rang out as it connected with the bridge of her nose.

She seemed dazed and I seized the moment. With my good leg I hit her in the head and pushed. Finally she subsided and her grip loosened.

I crawled backwards and stumbled onto my feet before breaking into a crooked unsteady jog. I heard her labored breathing as I attempted to make as much distance as possible.

Footsteps sprung up behind me. I picked up the speed as I saw what seemed to be an exit out of an alleyway.

Out of the blue I became all too aware of a deep gash in my leg. Of the creature at my heels. I gave my all for that final stretch before I made it into the street.

Crying out, I broke into a sprint and rushed out onto the street.

Then something smashed into me. A sudden screech. Breaking of glass. Muddled, panicked voices.

"Holy fuck. You fucking kill..."

"Out of nowhe..."

Sirens. 

Bright lights.

 

Warmth.

I woke up three weeks later in a hospital bed.

Joseph was there. So was the poor bastard who hit me with his car, crying his heart out, his girlfriend consoling him. I wanted to tell that fucker that he saved my life, but I could not speak.

My leg was amputated. Later, when I came to, the medical staff showed me the pictures. My leg was black as coal. The gash was grivously infected. The doctors that operated on me were amazed I pulled through.

No one believed me, except for Joseph. They chalked it up to a rabid dog attack.

I work from home now. I stay away from train stations.
