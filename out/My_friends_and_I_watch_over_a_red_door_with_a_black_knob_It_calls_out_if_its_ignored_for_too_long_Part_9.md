On the outside, Kait’s apartments looked like any other small city block of housing. Plain slatted buildings with water-stained windows and rusted AC units hanging off the sides. They certainly weren’t appealing—not much was out in our area, even an hour out from Stillwater—but they were a far step up from the ones in our small town, and they did what they were supposed to do. Provide a home for those who could hardly afford one.

Tenants smoked on the front porches and balconies of their units, children ran around the courtyard giggling and screaming without a care in the world, and on the main road a few yards away, cars bustled back home from work after a long day. Like I said, on the outside, Kait’s apartments looked like any others.

It only made me all the more nauseous that while everyone moved about, so blissfully unaware, the corpse of a girl’s memory was rotting into obscurity on the second floor.

As we traveled through, I wondered how many of the neighbors had known Mindy at one point. How many she’d say hi to in passing, or perhaps even stop to talk with from time to time. It was a question I’d never be able to get an answer to now.

Our steps felt heavy as we moved up the concrete stairs of Kait’s wing. They thumped like a steady heartbeat, and I hoped they were somehow helping to pump our own because it felt like they were unbeating. My throat got tight and choked, and my body was washed with numbness.

Kaitlynn’s hand had found its way back into mine once we’d left the car, and for a beat there, I felt like we were kids again. Not young teens on a rooftop—*actual* kids. Young children afraid of the dark with only each other for comfort.

This wasn’t the Red Manor, and compared to the others we’d left back there, we probably didn’t have much to fear. Still, this was all part of the unknown, and that thought was almost more horrifying given what we knew.

The sound of clocks chiming rang out over the city just as we reached my friend's front door, and our eyes drew back out over the horizon. I said a silent prayer that back in Stillwater, Lacey, Bryce, and Carly were all going to be okay.

I could feel Kait’s hand grip mine a little tighter as she faced the front door again, working up the courage to pry it from mine and grab her keys. She faced me at one point, and I worked at my best smile to reassure her. I don’t think it did, but she tugged them out and unlocked the door anyway.

Inside, the air was cold and deathly still. Kait had only been away for two days, but it felt as if the place hadn’t been stirred for as long as the Red Manor. Not even the dust dared to settle on anything; time was frozen stiff here. Maybe it was a side effect of its other occupant being erased from time.

The dull light of the setting sun sifting through the crooked blinds set the space in a dismal air, and the shadowy corners of the room that seemed normally shallow looked like bottomless abysses that anything could be lurking inside of. It made it harder to step forward, but together we did.

Kait’s apartment was clean. A few stray messes were left here and there from moving in a hurry, but for the most part, she and Mindy had decorated it up well, adding much needed charm to the simple, rundown interior. It was cozy, a luxury most people in our area often couldn’t afford.

Even so, there were spaces among the comfort that, in harsh contrast, felt like a gory image. Like an ugly stain on a spotless rug, Mindy had remained behind, not only in the form of her room.

There were pictures on a corkboard panel near the kitchen wall that both girls had hung, some from Kait that were old polaroids she’d taken of us, others were faces I didn’t recognize. Mindy with people that must have been her friends and family.

There were shoes by the door that were a size too small for Kait, jackets hanging on a rack that didn’t quite look her style, and objects that had been left on the floor but never picked up, seemingly by sheer neglect. They were a menagerie of items left behind that must have been too intrinsically tied to Mindy for Kait to interact with them, lest she break the grand illusion that her roommate might have existed at one time.

The sight was unsettling for me, to say the least, but it was nothing compared to what Kaitlynn was feeling. She looked around the area with entirely new eyes, tears pooling in her lids as she vacantly stumbled forward. I couldn't imagine what she must have been going through, knowing that she had inhabited the apartment for months after Mindy’s passing, only to now have the veil lifted.

It must have been like what Lacey said about Casey. If the girl’s corpse had been here buzzing with flies, Kait would have just smiled right past her. I suspected even just the belongings made her feel such a way.

Despite the tragedy, there was nothing else of note in the room; at least, not that gave us answers. We already knew what to expect coming in here, and there was a far more grueling place to search still.

Kait and I moved toward the back hall where Mindy’s room would be.

My friend immediately knew where it was as we peered down the dark corridor. The hall only had three doors; a bathroom and two bedrooms, and given that one of them was Kait’s, her eyes immediately locked the only other place that didn’t belong to her.

In her mind, a door had simply appeared in her own home.

It was strangely haunting the way the door was ajar, letting in the pale blue light from the dulling sky beyond the windows. We moved closer slowly, the floor creaking beneath the carpet the way the boards at the Red Manor did. It seemed no matter how far we ran from the place, its very being was haunting us.

Kait and I peered into the room beyond to find it uncharacteristically dirty compared to the rest of the house, but this didn’t seem to be its natural state. There was a hamper that could be used to hold the clothes scattering the floor, and the glasses on the side table filled halfway with water looked like the drinker kept forgetting they already had a cup in the first place. A vanity on the far wall beneath the window looked to have not been touched in quite some time, a fine layer of dust coating the collection of knickknacks.

It wasn’t the space of someone who was a slob, rather the space of a person who was too tired, or rather, too in distress, to keep it clean.

Kait and I looked at it with all the respect one might toward a murder scene.

We had been slow going so far as we moved through the apartment, but I think the intensity of the room finally broke us from our nerves, and we became eager to unravel what happened to Mindy.

Together, we entered and spread about the room.

It was clear by Mindy’s room that urban exploring wasn’t the extent of the girl's hobby; she clearly was into the paranormal aspect of things too and had an affinity for the spiritual. A large, witchy tapestry hung above her bed, and crystals rested on her nightstand alongside antique artifacts that fit her goth aesthetic.

Already, there was a connection between her room and the Red Manor, and while her collection was much more contemporary than the ones back at the house, they followed the same beats. Studies of astrology and a clear interest in the occult. Was it Mindy that triggered something in the Red Manor, or had the place drawn her to itself because she was already listening to the calls of things like it?

It felt wrong going through the stranger's stuff, especially when I knew deep in my chest that nobody ever would. No police to come investigate her disappearance, and no family to collect keepsakes. In that sense, it took the weight off my shoulders. We were doing this to bring Mindy’s killer to justice, in a sense, and nobody would ever know we desecrated her remnants in the first place…

I perused a bookshelf near her bed while Kaitlyn rifled through her dresser and vanity, looking for any clues that might help us learn how the girl got tangled up in the manor’s mess to begin with. Other than the identical interests, there wasn’t much so far, that was, until I reached her side table.

Beside a set of tarot cards and incense sticks, I found a small, leather-bound journal. My fingers hesitated to pick it up, feeling even at this point that reading a diary might be too much of a violation toward Mindy. The problem was, if there were answers anywhere, they were going to be inside, so I slowly undid the journal’s strap and flipped it open.

What was inside wasn’t what I expected—it wasn’t a diary in the traditional sense. Each entry was dated to keep track like the information was important, but there were no recaps of her day and no deep dark secrets recorded down. Instead, there seemed to be stories, and as I skimmed through the first couple, they were very weird and abstract. A lot of details that didn’t make sense, or points that jumped from one plot to the next.

It took me a few moments to realize that what I was reading was Mindy’s dreams. It was a dream journal—maybe to take down visions in case they were important, or maybe an attempt to achieve lucid dreams. At that, the passages began to make more sense.

Some were nightmares, some were just the usual odd run of images that most dreams are. I was about to give up as I continued skimming, resigning that there wasn’t going to be anything of use within the pages, but then I got to an entry about halfway through the journal.

*I’m standing alone on a long driveway before a red house, the woods behind me and the Appalachians on the horizon.*

“Kait,” I called immediately, the words bursting from my lips like a grunt of pain.

She turned over her shoulder to see what I was calling her for, but when she saw the book in my hands, she jumped to her feet and scurried over.

“What? What is it?”

I didn’t respond, only met her eyes with a tragic expression as I scooted the book between us. She looked down at it, then understood as together we began reading.

*The house is magnificent and grand, several stories tall with a tower and a circular window near the top floor. Beside it, before the cliffs, is a large field, and in it, I can see people playing. There’s two women sitting on a blanket watching a little girl sway on a swing that dangles from a tree. I can’t make out their features, but I know that it’s Mom and Grandma. The little girl is me. I feel sad because Dad isn’t there, even when he should be since I’m so young.*

*My vision is brought away from them as the ornate, carved door of the house swings open, but nobody stands on the other side. Something calls me forward, and my feet begin to move. Wind chimes sing in the breeze as I climb the porch, playing a melody that sounds familiar, like my favorite song.*

*Once I’m inside, there’s nobody there, but I can feel a presence. I’m not afraid, though; I feel calm and at peace. The interior is extravagant and marvelous, with expensive furniture and beautiful floral walls. I want to explore it, but I feel that presence call again, and this time, I realize its Dad.*

*I feel that sadness again, and I want to go to him, but his voice seems to be echoing down the halls and balconies of the house, and I can’t quite place it. I decide to try and move up the stairs, but before I can, I wake up.*

Kait and I stare at the page until both of us are sure that the other read it in its entirety, at which point she speaks, “She was dreaming of it…”

“She said she was there with her family as a kid,” I note, “Was it a childhood home of hers?”

Kait chewed my words for a moment before spitting them back out, “I don’t think so. She doesn’t describe it as a place she knows; she talked about it like it was all new to her. Plus, she said her dad wasn’t there even though she was at an age when he was still around.”

“She mentioned him in her video to her mother. Said that she thought she could fix things. I wonder if she lost him somehow…”

Kait nods in agreement, then turns gravely to me, “But he wasn’t gone. He was in the house…”

“Just like it used her to lure us when we came,” I finished, “Do you think the manor somehow made her dream the vision to lure her in?”

Kait flipped the page of the journal, revealing more entries beyond. I could see on her face she didn’t want to follow the threads to the end of its tragedy, but we had no other choice, “There’s only one way to tell.”

The next entry actually wasn’t about the red house again, to our surprise. We scoured through it, looking for any links to the last dream, but ultimately it seemed to be a one-off thing. That was, until a few days later when Mindy wrote another entry.

This one started different than the others, not just jumping straight into the dream and instead having a preamble:

*I dreamed of the big red house again. I hadn’t been able to get the last one out of my head since the other night. It was all just so vivid. If I’m being honest, each time I sleep now, I’m hoping that I’m there in my dreams again.*

*I just want to hear Dad’s voice. I had forgotten it for so long—just small fragments of his tone and his laugh remained, but in that dream, it was really him. *I was back at that house, and this time, I was upstairs in that tower near the back. I was looking off over the cliffs as the sun set on the horizon, and I felt that warm, comforting peace again, like I was perfectly in the right place.*

*The surrounding room was filled with toys and all sorts of lavish things; the kind of room I would have dreamed of having as a kid, but that we never could have afforded. There I sat on the bed a few feet away, however, playing with dolls that I had laid out before me.*

*I moved close to my younger self, but she didn’t seem to see me, and at that moment, there was a knock near the door. I turned to see mom standing there smiling. Smiling like I hadn’t seen her do in so long…*

*She told me dinner was ready soon and to come downstairs to wash up. I followed her and my younger self as they began to head down the steps, passing through the warmly lit floors as they went. It was around halfway down that I heard what I’d been hoping for again; dad’s voice.*

*“Come on, M&M’s!” he said just like he used to, “I’m waiting for you down here!”*

*He said ‘down’ but again, his voice sounded like it was coming from everywhere. I looked off towards one of the hallways, trying so desperately to trace where he could be, but something distracted me from him.*

*There was another girl there, staring at me with surprise. I didn’t recognize her, but she looked around the same age, and she was dressed up in an outfit like a princess.*

The room felt cold, and numbness washed through my veins. My eyes flickered to Kait to see if she’d read that part yet or if she was looking at me, but she seemed too engrossed. I continued on.

*Unlike Mom and myself, she actually seemed to see me, and when I turned my body to face her, she shied away like she was scared. I told her it was okay and asked what her name was, but  when she opened her mouth to speak, there was no sound.*

*Dad calling out for me again made me look back down the gaps in the balcony, wondering if he might be below, but before I could do anything else, I woke up. *I heard dad’s voice still. For the slightest moment, like it followed me up from the dream, I heard his voice still.*

*‘Come find me, M’s! I need to see you again!’*

I was hoping that it was just her wrestling with the weight of what had happened to Mindy, but unfortunately, she had picked up on the same thing I had.

“Jessie, that little girl she mentioned… That was exactly what you said you saw in the basement.”

I bit my cheek and nodded.

“And you had a vision. The ones like Mindy was having in these dreams…”

“Mine was different. It wasn’t a full dream like this, it felt like something else. It happened in a flash, too—you know; you were there.”

My reassurance didn’t work, and Kait looked at me with a tremble, “Jessie… whatever infected Mindy—”

“It doesn’t have me,” I told her quickly. “And even if it did, these dreams had to have been the Manor’s way of luring her in. It already succeeded in that, and we know that it’s evil, so it’s not like it can sway me in any meaningful way.”

“We don’t know *what* it’s doing.” Kait shook her head desperately, concern boiling over, “We don’t know *anything*, Jess. It already got Mindy, then it wanted *me* to open the door. If you pay the price for that, I’ll never—”

“Kait, *it’s okay.”* I told her, “It doesn’t have me. All this means is that there’s more to that little girl than I thought.”

Kait sniffled and nodded, looking back to the journal, “Do you think she’s malevolent?”

I thought for a moment, then shook my head, “No. I still stand by what I said before. She seemed afraid—Mindy even thought so too. I don’t know what she is, but if she was showing up in the attempts to lure Mindy, she must be important somehow.”

“If she was a soul trapped at the Manor like you thought, why would she be showing up in Mindy’s dream, though?”

I studied the diary carefully, unable to give an answer. We were slowly unraveling more of the mystery, but it seemed like with each stone unturned, we’d find a million more little questions scurrying around. The Red Manor ran off laws that humans couldn’t fathom—what hope did we have of solving them?

I turned the page to the next log and held my breath, “Let’s see if she knows.”

The next entry didn’t skip like last time. Mindy’s next dream only two nights later once again featured the scarlet house.

*Something is trying to talk to me through my dreams, and I just can’t figure out what it’s saying. It can’t really be Dad, right? I always hoped in my superstitious little heart that there might be ways to contact people once they passed to the other side, but if I’m being honest, I don’t know if I believed it. I at least thought it would be more abstract. Not just his actual voice calling out for me.*

I’m scared, but what scares me more is that part of me is actually hopeful. Hopeful that what my father just told me tonight might actually be true.*

*I miss you so much, Dad, and I hope I really can have a second chance.*

*I was just going to wait for one more dream, but now I just want them to stop. Ever since the last time I saw my father, I can barely sleep, and when I do, all I see is that red door. I stood with her, “Kaitlynn, you did help her. She wrote that down—you were trying to take care of her. I know you—you would never turn your back on someone without good reason.”

Kait shook her head, “It doesn’t matter—I should have been there. No matter what, I should have gone with her. Fuck! This is all my fault!”

“Kaitlynn! Listen to me!” I said sternly, grabbing her by the shoulders and fixing her with my gaze. Her breathing slowed, and she tensed up, not expecting my sudden increase in volume. To be honest, I hadn’t either. I felt a pang of sickness, knowing who I owed it to.

Still, I pushed on, “This is not on you. Did you just read all of that? We are in over our heads, and there was nothing you could have done to stop Mindy from going. And if you had gone? Things might have ended differently, and the door could have stayed open with no one to stop it, and then we’d *really* be deep in it, okay?”

 Kait’s panic had quickly simmered off beneath my steely gaze, but her opinion certainly hadn’t changed, and she made sure to let me know this in blunt terms: “If I had gone, Jess? Maybe we both would have died after Mindy shut that door, and then we never would have come back to fuck things up a second time.”

My mouth fell open to respond to her, and it took a second to recuperate from the mental blow of imagining Kait in Mindy’s shoes before I could speak. The thought of forgetting her forever was physically too much to bear.

“Kait, that’s not… I don’t think—”

A flash of grey in the reflection of the vanity mirror behind Kait caught my eye, cutting my words short as I whirled on my heels. Behind me on the wall, and clambering down from the ceiling, I had seen a pale, gangly human figure thudding down the wall, long, black, tangled locks thrashing about wildly.

I jumped back with my arms out, guarding Kaitlynn, but as I took in the room with my heart now in my throat, I didn’t see anything.

My head snapped back to the mirror in sheer panic, but with where the body had just scurried from, it would have landed behind the far side of the bed, making it hidden.

Kait quickly lost her grief in favor of fear at my sudden switch, “Jess? Jess, what’s happening?”

“Something is in here with us…” I told her breathlessly.

I felt her reach out and grip my arm, squeezing so hard her nails dug into my flesh. We both stood perfectly still, staring at the open space between us and the door. Kait’s eyes were glued to where mine were behind the bed, and before we could decide what to do, there was a harsh thud of the side table rattling, then the edge of the rug beneath the bedframe tossed ever so slightly.

Steps moving towards us without the sound.

Kait caught on quickly and lunged forward, still holding my arm as she yanked me along. We banked sideways toward the head of the bed opposite to where our unseen guest would be, and she nearly threw my massive form onto the top of it with pure adrenaline alone.

We held onto each other tightly to keep balance as the mattress wobbled unsteadily beneath our feet, then Kait yelped as a hard *SMACK* rang out from the frame, like something had just banged against it.

Beneath the bed, we heard belongings that Mindy had stashed there begin to shift and skitter.

Kait and I stood as centered as we could on the mattress, scanning its edges and praying we didn’t see a pale, inhuman arm crawl up over the edge, clutching at our ankles. In the brief moment of stillness, I finally had time to process what was happening.

What was this thing? There was no way a monster would have escaped the red door and made it this far already, and if it had been here the whole time, why hadn’t it taken Kait when she was here alone?

Was this the demon that had been haunting Mindy?

*“Sssss… huuuu…”* a raspy breath squeaked from below us, so quiet that had it not been for our perfect stillness, we may not have heard it. *“Sssss… huuuu…”*

Kait looked at me, petrified, then leaned in close to my ear, trying to be quieter than even the voice, “We have to jump for the door and run.”

I eyed the opening in the dull light of the room, thinking that the trip down the black hallway didn’t feel much safer. This creature had been wicked fast from my brief glimpse of it, and I wasn’t sure we could make the front door in time. Still, we had no other option other than to wait like sitting ducks.

*“Isssss… y-yuuuu”*

With a thundering chest and Kait’s hand in mine, I softly slipped my boot across the mattress and hooked my toe on one of the pillows. Giving a nod to Kait to make sure she was ready, I kicked it off the side opposite the door.

The second it hit the ground, I saw a grey, bony, gnarled hand shoot out, slamming the pillow and yanking it back. That was the only time I spared.

Kait pulled me hard, and together, we launched off the other side toward the door.

We made it all the way in one bound, and I risked a look over my shoulder as the labored, cracking voice that could barely whisper a moment ago unleashed a bloodcurdling wail. It was the same kind I’d imagine a mother might make if she were to find her child unmoving.

The sound mirrored the same noises it had been making so far, but at full volume now, I realized that it hadn’t just been gasping. It was speaking words.

“*IT’S YOU! I-IT’S YOU!”*

Grey hands shot out from the darkness beneath the frame, grasping and clutching at the air like a dog being choked by a leash. We didn’t wait around to see if they would stay that way. Kait turned and continued to barrel off down the hall, making my arm go taut before I started getting yanked too.

In the small window before that happened, however, my vision stayed fixed on the hands, and I noticed something odd about them. Like the little girl back in the dark Victorian hallways, they weren’t entirely solid. Look hard enough, and you could see a ghostly, intangible sheen over its ghastly flesh.

I wasn’t going to take that as a sign that it might be gentle like the princess.

Kait and I flew out of her apartment door and down her steps, not looking back again until we reached my car. I fumbled for the keys to get my door unlocked, and once we were in, the time between me starting the vehicle and gassing it backward was only a fraction of a second.

Neither Kait or I said a word to one another as we whirled back onward down the highway toward Stillwater; just panted and gasped like dogs. The whir of my engine was suddenly broken, however, by the vibration of Kait’s phone that had fallen from her pocket to the floor.

She snatched it up, and put it to speaker immediately, smoothing her hair and asking, “Hello? Lace? What’s going on?”

“Shit—Carly! Keep pressure! It’s okay—I-It doesn’t look that bad…”

My mouth felt horribly dry, “Lacey? What’s wrong, is everyone okay?”

The girl spoke again as if she’d just remembered she’d called, “Guys? Guys, we need you back here right now—something else came out, and now Bryce is hurt.”

“Shit!” I muttered, pressing the gas harder.

“I-I’m fine,” I faintly heard my friend whimper, “Seriously, I’m okay.”

“Just sit down and let Carly look at it,” Lacey demanded before returning to us again, “He’s okay, right now, but if something else comes on the next hour, I don’t know if we’re going to have the manpower to stop it without all of us. That last monster it was… it was the worst so far.”

“Hang tight,” I told her, “We’ll be back as fast as we can.”

“Please tell me whatever you guys found out there was worth it?” I heard Carly plea from a distance, panting as hard as Kait and I.

Kait and I finally met each other’s eyes, but the road stole mine back rather quickly. We couldn’t die to something so trivial as a car crash when there was undoubtedly a worse fate waiting for us.

As for Carly’s question? I had no idea. We’d learned a lot, but whether any of it was actually worth it?

Well, the whole theme of this tragedy had started and would most likely end with ‘ignorance is bliss’.
