It all started in July. My girlfriend(Jenna), Chris, his girlfriend, and I decided to rent a two story house in the Sierra Mountains. It had all we needed. Three bedrooms, two bathrooms, a nice backyard that led out to the woods, a paved driveway that was just long enough for us to fit two cars. The garage? We converted it to another room. 

Perfect...

Until the lockdown. Unfortunately, everyone else had jobs that cut our hours. My own job as security at a factory kept up but I could only do so much. What once was a great idea to save money became a hindrance. We moved all of our gaming set ups from the garage to the attic. We hardly had time to game anyway and we needed another person to pick up rent. 

Chris's girlfriend, Linzie, put up a post on Craigslist and immediately got a hit. It was like fate or something. We met him at a Mountain Mike's in town. It was the only joint in the area that was always packed. 

He was an old WW2 veteran. Seemed he just wanted to rent our garage to store some things. 

"I don't trust those damn storage companies," he grumbled. His oxygen machine whirred and his breathing sounded labored. "I've seen those shows. They just wanna sell your crap." 

I knew that was a dumb excuse but, beggars can't be choosers. We took a vote, or what we considered to be one. Chris seemed to take charge on this one. 

"It's either this or we find some rando to share a house with," he shrugged. Honestly, it was kind of a steal. 

The next few days had seen us moving boxes from an old four door pick-up into the garage. It took all day the first day and part of the next. We gave him a copy of the key. Honestly, we would've just left the whole thing to him but the washer, dryer, and water heater were in there. 

Years of history and war paraphernalia and the only thing he said before leaving was, "Whatever you do, don't open the footlocker."

It struck me as odd but the others seemed to not care. His assistant, some young lady around our age, probably 25, let us know we'd only see him every few months. She left Chris her number, drawing a scoff from Linzie, before leaving. 

Weeks went by. You would assume everything was normal from the outside. But, everyone was acting different. 

Chris became a drunk asshole with a tendency to disappear for entire days with no calls. Linzie was always on edge, her nails bitten so low, she had to cover them with bandages. Even Jenna had started sleeping on the other side of our bed. She didn't even want to go on dates. 

Worst of all was me. I had taken to smoking cigarettes again. My anxiety was at an all time high. Every night I would wake up in cold sweats from a nightmare I couldn't remember. The only thing that kept ringing in my mind was "Don't open the footlocker." 

Another week went by and the lockdown had finally been lifted. Say what you want about it, but I was finally glad it was over. Everyone went back to working full time and it seemed to calm things down slightly. Chris was still a jerk sometimes and his nightly escapes began to thin. Linzie's nails recovered. Jenna even began to cuddle with me. I kept smoking but my anxiety was little more than an annoyance again. 

Jenna had an idea to hold a party over the weekend. A kind of "You survived the virus!" party. Her and Linzie put it together. She invited old friends and neighbors. 

That night was fun. Games of table pong and idiots riding an old dented radio flyer down the hill. 

Everything seemed normal again. The next day was chipper and bright. Then Chris told us he opened it. 

At first, we had no clue what he was talking about. 

"Opened what?" I chuckled as I took a sip of coffee. I burned my tongue. "Dammit." I hated doing that. It always felt like I had a mouth of sandpaper for the day. 

"I opened the footlocker."

Everyone went silent. It seemed that I wasn't the only one worried about it. 

"Why?" Linzie groaned. 

"I don't know, I just... I thought I'd find something cool." Chris shrugged. He always did that. Shrugged off some dumb decision he made. 

"Christ, Chris." I shook my head, "He had one friggin rule, dude." I walked out to the garage. 

Sure enough, the old iron lock had been broken and a pry bar lay to the side, forgotten, like a tomb laying bare. 

Everyone joined me. I sighed. "May as well check it out." 

I wish Jenna stopped me. I wish Chris never broke it open. I wish Linzie never put up the post. I wish I never touched that box. 

What lay inside? A couple folded uniforms, some letters, a book, and photos. One was a US Army patrol uniform and the other... I recognize the German uniform immediately. The black and silver with the bright red band. But this uniform had some holes. I guess the old man saw action. 

"Damn," Chris breathed. It smelled strongly of vodka. "The guy was a badass." He pointed at a picture of soldiers. They were all decked out in their gear with a structure crumbling behind them. 

I nodded, but I wasn't really paying much attention to the rest of it anymore. The book kept me. It was wrapped in something too light to be leather but it wasn't plastic. I couldn't make out the material. It looked horribly sewn together. Then there was the lid of the footlocker itself. There, as plain as day, was Latin written out in what I hoped to be red paint. A few crosses were burned into it as well. 

I swallowed and stood up, bumping Chris as I did.

"Well... I don't think we should look anymore. I'll call his assistant and we'll just explain that someone," I shot a look at Chris, " opened it and we'll need a new lock." 

Too late. Jenna was already looking through the book. 

"Jenna, what're you...."

She pushed me as she read a passage. 

"Surgant qui infimi habitant et ultionem in dilectos Dei sumat.

Qui cum igne et dolo ambulat, iterum surgat."

My skin crawled and the lights flickered. I swear I could hear... No... I couldn't hear anything. No crickets. No birds. Nothing. It was just like that for at least five minutes. Then Linzie took the book and put it back in the locker before closing the lid. 

Jenna seemed a little perturbed but understood. Everyone was a little creeped out. I shook my head and walked back into the house. I was angry. Who knew what she just read. Hadn't she paid any attention to Evil Dead? I swore and marched to our room, laying down. 

Nothing happened. 

Days went by. Nothing. I felt bad being disappointed. I almost wanted to be vindicated. Toxic, I know. 

I almost forgot about it, but dreams began to surface. I could remember this one vividly. 

I walked through the house in a haze of smoke and silence. Not even the boards gave way to the moans of age. It was dark but there seemed to be some light at my feet coming from an unknown source. As if the fog itself was light. 

I began to explore, calling for anyone to hear. The only sound, a faint whisper. I couldn't make it out, but I followed it. I didn't want to, yet my legs seemed to have a mind of their own.

They led me to the garage. My hand reached out and slowly opened the door. Even this had no sound. What I found will forever be scarred to my mind. 

It was Jenna, but her skin was paper thin and ripped open in areas like old, oily shrinkwrap. Her eyes were bloodshot with yellow rings around her irises. Her nails were purple and bloodied. Some were even hanging from the pits. Her hair seemed thin and patched. Her lips chapped as if she hadn't had a sip of water in months. 

She just stared at me, unmoving. Not even breathing. 

This ended when her chest began to rise and fall, slowly. Then faster and faster, until she screamed and launched at me with unnatural speed and agility. Her teeth sank in and I didn't wake. I was in agony. I felt as though I wouldn't get out. I was dying. 

Then my eyes shot open and Jenna was looking down at me. Her face full and eyes the same beautiful green I remembered. 

"Are you okay?" She looked worried. From what I gathered, she had been shaking me. "You were screaming and thrashing around."

"I'm... I'm fine." I tried to swallow but I was parched. Jenna was nice enough to give me some water. 

She turned the light on and looked to me, freezing.

"What?" I looked at her. 

"Your shoulder. It- it's bleeding." She quickly took off her shirt and held down on it. I winced as the pain finally took hold. 

"My dream." My eyes widened. 

"Who care-"

"I was bitten in my dream." 

We looked at each other for what felt like eternity.
