"Jamie!" The sound shot through the abandoned town as I looked across the small town square, surrounded by dilapidated buildings, some which nature itself was trying to reclaim. I could see Rich running toward me, his eyes the size of saucers and a frightened expression on his face. I didn't understand. The three of us were scouting a location, another once-thriving town beaten down by many circumstances, whether it was the plants closing, the farms no longer needing steady hands due to machinery, or just the general feeling that the town was boring, so the young fled. All I knew was it was basically left behind, dust settling on window frames, the glass busted years ago, most likely from bored teenagers, ivy sprouting, gently climbing the walls.

"What the hell is going on?" I shouted back.

"Find Dylan, man, we have to get the fuck out of here!"

"Dude, we haven't even finished scouting the location yet!"

"I don't care!" he screamed. He was about ten yards away from me. He turned his head to look back, then turned back, still wearing that scared look as he rushed toward me and grabbed my arm. "Come on, dude!"

It was instinct, perhaps me feeding into his fear myself, as I followed him to a derelict building and watched Rich open the door. His light brown hair was drenched in sweat, his hazel eyes bugged out, his cheeks flushed red from physical exhaustion. He had to have been running awhile.

"What the hell is going on?" I questioned, as we walked over to a broken window, staring outside. A small breeze pushed remnant papers, dust, and other things around the empty town square. I turned to look at Rich, who was still freaking out, wiping the sweat from his forehead.

"Why were you running?"

"They were chasing me!" he grunted. "They were on a bike too!"

"You got chased by a motorcycle?"

"No, no," he said as he waved off that notion. "It was like one of those BMX bikes."

"Alright, I feel like I am even more confused now," I replied, as Rich slouched against the wall, sliding down as he rested his head in his hands. I could see a small blur in the distance, on one of the roads that led into the town.

"Where's Dylan at?" Rich asked, a hint of desperation in his voice, as I was trying to make out what I was seeing in the distance. I was still confused by what was going on, why Rich had been left in such a panic. This was a normal routine. We explored long-abandoned towns. We ran into people before, usually folks who lived on property outside the town, mostly farmers who didn't pay these old towns any mind.

"He went to the old post office, to see if there was anything interesting there."

"We need to go get him and get the fuck out of this place, dude. Something is off with this place, like for real."

"Like what?" I asked, catching a light reflected in the room, like a reflector being hit by the sun. I locked eyes with it, transfixed, curious, and hoping for answers about what had scared the hell out of Rich so bad.

"The town, it's cursed."

"Every damn town we go to is abandoned and cursed. It's the entire gimmick for the channel."

"No, this town, that weird preacher story," he replied. I looked outside to see a boy casually riding his bike. He looked to be barely thirteen, but then again everyone under the age of twenty looked like babies to me.

"That was just a wild rumor posted online, an unnamed preacher saying this town was cursed," I said. "How did the town just suddenly become cursed when the door factory left? Also, I think I am seeing the person that was chasing you."

"What? Fucking hide then!"

"Why? It's a kid riding a bike, dude," I snarked.

He started running his fingers through his hair. His eyes told the tale; he was legitimately scared. I really couldn't understand why as I looked at the boy. He had sandy blond hair, wearing an anime shirt, his lanky legs without a sign of any sort of hair on them, making me think he was even younger. In a pair of denim shorts, he just kept circling the square as if he was going for a casual bike ride after school.

"You know, I think I can take him," I joked. "You just wait right here, Rich."

"No, seriously, don't leave me here."

"You want to leave, right?" I asked.

"God, yes. This place has to be cursed. That preacher really meant no sinners were to enter this place."

He was referring to another online post. It had been posted on some old forum when we had been doing our research on the town, but the legend went that anyone who disturbed this cursed town and who was a great sinner would never be allowed to leave. It was frankly as stupid as watching a grown man be scared of a kid on a bike.

"Will you shut up!" I hissed. "I am going to get rid of him, then we can go find Dylan and leave, because I am tired of dealing with this."

"How?"

"He's a kid. I am going to tell him that the adults are doing some work around the town and he needs to leave for the day."

I stepped outside. The kid just kept circling the square, coming back around toward me. His face was blank, as if he didn't even see me, his lanky legs just kept pedaling away. "Hey, kid, we are doing scouting for our YouTube channel," I called out. "Do you mind just riding your bike elsewhere for a little bit?"

He did not say a word. He just remained with his blank stare as he rode past me. I watched him circle around again, still not even paying me any mind. "Kid, seriously, we are trying to work here, so get out of here," I called out, but he still ignored me. Something about it annoyed me.

But he continued to circle around. Ignoring me. "Kid, seriously!" I yelled.

As he passed me again, monotonously following the same path, the wheels spinning, I stepped in front of him. He lifted his feet off the pedals, dragging his feet on the cracked asphalt of the road as he stopped completely and just gave me a stare that left me with an unease.

"Kid, me and my friends are trying to do some work," I stated. "Maybe you've seen us on YouTube?"

He remained silent, just a blank, emotionless stare.

"Alright," I mumbled. "So you haven't seen us."

He started to turn his head, as if he was scanning the buildings, searching. I just stood there awkwardly for a moment before he stared straight back at me. "So, are you bored being silent and circling around yet?"

The gaze didn't change, but he slightly lifted his bike and turned the handlebars. He shot one last look over to me before he started cycling away, this time not circling but taking the road he had previously ridden down to get to the square.

As I watched him disappear, I walked back to where Rich had been camped out and hiding. As I stepped back inside, Rich was still sitting on the floor, this time holding his knees. "Alright, let's find Dylan and get out of here. That kid was creepy as fuck."

"This place is cursed, dude."

"Maybe. That was some Children of the Corn shit."

"Let's just get out of here," Rich said, as he extended his hand for me to lift him up. I grabbed on to it and pulled him up. I looked outside again to make sure the creepy kid had left. We exited the building.

"Where is the post office at this place?" I asked.

"It's down there," Rich pointed down a road that passed the other side of the square. "Let's just make it quick and get him."

We quickly jogged over to an older building. It looked like the oldest one in town. Vines were growing all around it, weeds pushing through the pavement that led to the entrance. As we stepped inside what was perhaps the darkest building in the town so far, old tile floors were stained with mud, empty beer cans everywhere.

"Get away from me!" I heard a familiar voice yell. It was Dylan. I raced off, leaving Rich behind, running down a corridor. It had several doors, and I heard Dylan cry out again, "You're dead, I know you're dead!"

I ran to the first one and pulled on it, but it refused to budge. I darted to one beside it and banged on it while pulling, while I yelled, "Dylan, are you in there? I'm coming for you!"

I arrived at one with a marker so dirty it said sorting room. I barged in to see a familiar red-headed man with a bushy beard squirming on the floor, trying to escape from what looked to be a college-age student who had blond hair and fair skin. His blond hair was plastered to his face, and he was strangely soaked, wearing only swim trunks.

"Dylan, what the hell is going on?" I shouted.

The person in swim trunks didn't even bother to turn around, his focus solely on Dylan, who was crawling on the floor toward me. "He's dead. I know that guy is dead!"

"What?"

"He's dead, Jamie. He's been dead for almost ten years."

"That doesn't make sense, Dylan!" I yelled as I tried to reach for the stranger, who walked slowly toward Dylan. He still wasn't even acknowledging me, so I moved myself in front of Dylan, who stood up quickly behind me.

"I didn't mean to do it," he cried out. "I'm sorry, I was young and stupid."

"Do what?" I asked.

"He died at a pool party. Me and my friends kept giving him drinks, then dared him to jump into the pool."

"Wait, did he die?"

"He didn't come up. We thought he was playing a joke."

I felt a bit of disgust as I stood between him and whatever was in front of me, my mind spinning at the very idea that a dead person was in front of the two of us, and I had not known about this. But I was a late addition to this trio. We had gone to different colleges, and I had joined up with them because of my video editing skills, which they had lacked when wanting to start this venture.

"We are just going to have to make a break for it," I instructed. I started to back up to the door, nudging Dylan to back up as well as we made our way out. Whatever was standing in this room with us took a step forward, its eyes locked on Dylan as we made our way into the hall. "Alright, run!"

The two of us bolted. I could see Rich standing at the end. He had not bothered to help, still full of terror that rendered him nothing more than a deadweight coward at this point. "Come on, guys!" he cried out. "Let's get out of here!"

We burst out of the post office and started to run toward the edge of the square where we had parked our car. Dylan looked back and said, "I don't see him. I think he isn't following us anymore."

We slowed down our pace, started to just walk. "So what happened after the pool party?" I asked.

"What do you mean?"

"Why didn't you and your friends get in trouble for what happened?"

"The police ruled it an accident."

"Did you tell them about how you fed him drinks?"

He shook his head, showing some signs of guilt as we kept walking toward the edge of the town square. We had parked the car down there. I felt some disgust toward Dylan at that moment. "Let's just get to the car," I said, with a judgmental tone.

"Jamie, it was an accident. It's not even as bad as what Rich di—"

"Don't you bring that up, Dylan!" Rich snapped. "We don't talk about it!"

"I didn't see you getting chased around this shithole."

I could see the car a short distance away, but now I was curious. "Actually, he had some weird kid on a bike that scared the hell out of him."

Dylan stopped and asked, "So you saw him?"

"I don't want to talk about it, Dylan!" Rich screamed out, as they both paused and we reached the edge of the square. The car was parked somewhat crooked in a parking space next to another ruined building.

"Let's just get out of here and we can talk about everything once we leave."

"No, my longtime friend didn't even bother to help me, and now he wants to act like he didn't do anything fucked up in his life."

"Will you two please shut up and get to the car!"

Dylan charged at Rich. They were standing nose to nose as he shouted, "Why don't you tell Jamie about how you used to day drink all the time and how your daddy always covered for you!"

"Back off, asshole!"

"You saw him, didn't you?" Dylan growled. "You saw the kid you hit while drinking!"

Rich shoved Dylan away as I walked to the car. I turned to see the two were about to fight, but that wasn't all that was happening. The guy in the swim trunks and the kid on the bike were heading toward the two of them, coming toward us.

"Guys, look behind you!" I shouted out. The two turned to see what was coming toward them. They turned around and tried to head to the car, but when they reached the edge of the square, they stopped. "Come get in the car!"

They tried to walk, but something stopped them. Dylan put his hands up as if he was touching something. "What the hell is going on?"

The sins of their past were getting closer, fixated on the two. I watched both of them try to cross some invisible line. Rich charged toward it, but he couldn't get past it. I was bewildered at the sight. "Stop fucking around!" I yelled. "Get over here."

"We can't."
