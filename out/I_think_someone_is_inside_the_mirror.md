
When I was a teenager in Lisbon, I used to be scared of Mirrors. Not because of the stories that my parents or friends told me during our camp days or those tales that were explicitly meant to keep us away from the dark, but because of the person who glared back at me.

A lot of my friends found it silly and made fun of me. They called me childish and elevated me to the highest rank of idiocy. I tried my best to narrate my phobia to them but they laughed it out like I was a circus clown.

My elder brother, Samuel, understood my character without raising questions. He would render an open ear whenever I wanted to narrate one of my stories to him. He was the best. But, a time came when he shifted and attempted to talk me out of the imaginary world I locked myself in... but at a cost.

He held my hand tightly and dragged me to a mirror that was nailed to the wall. It was shiny and quite expensive. He made me stand in front of it. My heart almost jumped out of my ribcage, goosebumps clothed my skin, my legs shook as though I was walking on thin ice.

My voice hissed when I tried to talk to him about the mirror but he didn't listen to me. He cared about me and my social life. He wasn't going to be by my side all the time so he wanted to make sure that no one took me for a joke while he wasn't around. He wanted me to literally face my fear.

I gazed into the mirror. I saw myself trembling like a toddler. My eyes were drowsy and feared what hid behind the mirror.

Samuel's voice bounced in my ear like a melody I wanted to forget because it felt as though a part of me was being ripped out. He told me to extend my hand. That I should freely move my body without fear. I had an assumption that something would jump out from the mirror and attack me mercilessly. That it would squeeze my throat until my eyes flipped or until my veins were blocked. 

He assured me that there wasn't anything to worry about. He explained that the mirror was not real but only a portal between my imagination and the real world. Everything that was in the mirror was a reflection of the real world. I was relieved after that session that day. I felt as though a heavy load was lifted from my shoulders. 

The whole day I was in disbelief of the knowledge I had about the mirrors. I thought they were gateways of evil that awaited a chance to attack those who gazed into it or anyone who tried to expose their secrets. 

Most of my fiends jubilated after I told them about the therapy session I had with my brother. Teasingly, they explained to me how brave I was to finally face my fear. I found it pathetic after looking at it from a different perspective. I was just being a foolish little boy who couldn't get those horror movies out of his head.

Everything went well until Friday the 13th of June 2025 when I woke up to darkness. I switched on the light and sauntered to the bathroom. I merrily danced my way past a mirror until I noticed something strange... my reflection had stopped mid-way.

Confused, I took a step back, I couldn't feel my tongue anymore. My stomach was hollow, my palms sweated. I turned slowly to face the mirror, immediately a shiver shot down my spine when I saw my reflection glaring at me with hostile eyes. My lips became dry and brittle. Saliva flowed down my throat without realizing it. 

I couldn't scream because my voice was locked down in my body. I was only granted visual access to what was unfolding before me. My pulse increased, I would have collapsed if a force wasn't rooting me to the floor. 

My reflection walked forward slowly but carefully. Its footsteps echoed in my ear. It was too great and irritating for my ear drums to ignore. For every step it took, I felt closer to it than my own skin. It horrified me but I couldn't run because something caught my legs. 

I heard my own voice speaking to me but I wasn't talking and neither was my reflection. Maybe it was in my head, but adrenaline oozed through my veins. Before I could blink again, I saw my reflection crawling out from the mirror. It stepped into my world, dropped on the floor and moved like water until it became my shadow. 

Suddenly, I could move my body again. I could speak and scream again. But no one heard my scream. I couldn't run past the mirror or see my reflection on it. 

It took a long time before I saw the glimpse of the sun rising over the horizon. I heard footsteps coming from my room.

My body cracked upon seeing myself on the mirror once again. The reflection had a frown on its face, like the one I always had in the morning. It stopped and looked at me. Suddenly, I was mimicking my reflection: I touched my hair and showed my teeth before walking away with a wink.

It was then I realized that I had become a reflection of my own body and was stuck in the mirror.
