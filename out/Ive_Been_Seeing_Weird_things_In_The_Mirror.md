I know this is a strange thing to claim, and puts what I’m about to write into question, but I wish I was just losing my sanity. That would be so much simpler. If it had an easy explanation like that, there wouldn’t be this gnawing feeling in me, further unmanning me day by day. But this unease, it just continues to grow, and I just hope to God that I’m really insane, and none of this is real. 

It all started a month ago, or at least, that’s when I started to notice it. After shaving my face in the bathroom one morning, I was just washing off any residual shaving cream, and went to look in the mirror to make sure I had gotten it all off. 

Yep, all of it, except I noticed there was a nick on my Adam's apple. I remember muting something like, “That’s definitely a bad omen.” and right as I said that, I noticed my reflection was closing its eyes. 

Its eyes instantly opened after that, and it behaved like my reflection had always behaved, which is to say normally, but that was all I could think about on my drive to work. I remember wondering if I had somehow observed some rare law of physics at work or if I was just hallucinating because I was so tired, but I ended up scribbling that episode onto a post-it note when I got to work, and bringing that back home with me for a record of the event. 

  
No need though, because it was going to happen again, and again, and again after that. The next morning, just like last morning, I found my reflection shutting its eyes tightly, but this time it also smiled in such an amused mischievous way, that really made me nervous and caused me to laugh. This only lasted a few seconds, and then it went back to my normal reflection. 

The next day, while closing its eyes, it did a shushing motion at me with its hand and then began making silly faces at me. I laughed less nervously that time, but it really got me thinking. I began trying to inspect my reflection whenever I passed a mirror or a window. On my ride to work in my car’s mirror, in my office’s bathroom mirror, and I would keep looking without any interesting results, but every morning, when I was at my bathroom mirror to shave and brush my teeth, my reflection would go into these crazy antics, like it would do this funny dance, or try to juggle the things I had out on my sink, like my comb, toothbrush, toothpaste, and razor. Some mornings, it even seemed to want to play charades.

   
At this point, I wasn’t that alarmed anymore. My thinking was, that if it only happened in my bathroom mirror in the morning, and didn’t really affect my life, besides amusing me for a few minutes, what harm was there? Sure, maybe I was crazy, but not that crazy, right? I was ok with things as they were. It didn’t bother me–until it did.

 One morning, my reflection simply left out of my bathroom’s door, and didn’t return. I checked my car’s mirror when I went to drive to my office, and it was there, and my reflection in the bathroom mirror at my office was there, so I didn’t worry about it until I got home, and realized I couldn’t find my reflection in my bathroom’s mirror. This was strange, and slightly inconvenient, but I didn’t really care much beyond some innocent curiosity, until I found a note on my coffee table. Scribbled in red crayon, in this crabbed handwriting that looked erratic and large, were the words “I’m out now!” 

  
The morning after that, my reflection was still missing, and strange things started happening. I started losing things that I had just put down, or finding things where they shouldn’t have been. This escalated when people I knew started telling me I had just talked with them, but that I was doing the strangest thing, closing my eyes the whole time. People would actually start to act surprised when they saw me, because apparently I had just told them I was going home, or I had just gone in a different direction, and came back impossibly quick from the opposite direction.

 The girl I’m currently seeing messaged me while I was at work about how I was so sweet to go visit her and bring her flowers. I was so disturbed after reading that, that I couldn’t help but tacklessly ask, was I closing my eyes? She messaged back, yeah, you told me you got them dilated, right? 

  
At this point the weirdness was too much for me, and I was thinking about seeing some kind of psychiatrist. Everyone was telling me all these weird things that couldn’t be true, and I was finding these notes in my house scrawled in aggressive red crayon that read increasingly threatening messages, which was just too much for me. So, it came to the point where I had made the resolve to talk with a psychiatrist the next morning. The appointment was already made, and I just had to go.

 That night, I decided to invite my girlfriend over for dinner and to watch a movie. As we were in the middle of watching 2005 Stay, which probably wasn’t the best choice given my mental state, I got a text from a co-worker, telling me that it might sound weird, but today they saw the strangest thing and couldn't explain it. While they were passing my cubicle, they swore they saw another me standing right behind me, but this me was closing his eyes and grinning inhumanly wide. This other me directed his smile at him my co-worker while he passed, and because the sight was so bizarre, he had to do a double take and look again, but when he quickly went back to see the two mes again, the other one was gone. I guess I’ve been drinking a little too much coffee, he ended the message with.

   
As I was reading this on my phone, my doorbell rang, and my girlfriend offered to answer the door and jumped up quickly, because I guess she didn’t like the movie. When I finished reading the message on my phone, my girlfriend came back to me and asked how I did that. Did what? I asked back. How I had gotten up from the couch, ran to the door, rung the bell, and then stood there, handed her a note, and then reappeared on my couch. She thought the whole thing was a magic trick I had done just for her. She wanted to talk more about that, but I was impatient and just wanted to see the note, so I demanded it.

 When she handed it over to me, I felt my blood run cold. In that same red crayon, in the same chicken scratch hand writing, was the message: I’m the real one.
