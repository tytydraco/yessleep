There isn't much to say so I'll keep this one short, I moved to North Carolina to take care of my grandfather back in 2025. He lives just outside of Greensboro on about a 50 acre plot of land he inherited from his own father. It’s a small house attached to a failing farm, but he’s always called it home. I received the call back in November. Grandpa had completely stopped talking on a random night and needed someone around the house to help him. I had just gone through a bad breakup myself, so the idea of getting away for a while sounded right. A change of scenery was exactly what I needed. For months everything was quiet and routine out there. That was true until last week.

I had just finished the nightly chores around the house when suddenly a loud **bang** cracked through the air from somewhere outside. My first thought was that the pigs had somehow forced their gate open again, so I sprinted out the door. I came to a sudden stop when the crisp evening air hit my face. I stood there for a moment, taking in the field. From what I could see, everything looked normal. No animals wandering the property. The pig pen was still shut. It wasn’t until my eyes drifted toward the barn that I noticed something strange. The large red barn doors, usually left propped open during the warmer nights, were completely shut. 

I turned on my phone’s flashlight and started walking toward the barn, hoping I could just reopen the doors and head back inside to the sleep I had been dreaming about all day. But as I got closer, something began to feel wrong. It was quiet. Too quiet, even for rural North Carolina. My heartbeat started to pick up as I realized the constant drone of cicadas that normally filled the night had vanished. The wind wasn’t blowing either; the metal chimes on the porch hung perfectly still, not making a sound, like the air itself had been smothered. I forced myself to keep walking, telling myself I was just being paranoid. But with every step my shoes felt heavier, like the ground itself was trying to hold me back. When I finally reached the barn, I grabbed the doors and pulled them open with a long groan of old wood. I braced myself for something inside, but when I shined my light across the floor, everything looked normal. Empty stalls, tools hanging on the walls, nothing out of place. I propped the doors open and let out a long breath of relief. I won’t lie—I started jogging a little on my way back to the house. A few minutes later I was in bed and drifting off to sleep.

It wasn’t until later that night that I woke up again. At first I thought I was dreaming, but then I heard it clearly: a faint scratching sound coming from my bedroom window. There’s a massive oak tree outside my room, and its branches sometimes scrape the glass, so I wasn’t too worried. That is, until I heard my grandfather’s voice come from outside. “Come outside, champ.” The words caught in my throat. No one has called me *champ* since I was a kid except my grandfather. The voice came again, raspier this time and more insistent, but something about it sounded wrong, like a recording that had been played and copied too many times. “Come outside.” I shot upright in bed. Every instinct in my body told me not to look, but I couldn’t stop myself. I turned toward the window and my eyes immediately locked on the barn. The interior light was on now, casting a pale glow across the yard. Just outside that light stood something tall and twisted, barely visible in the dark. As my eyes adjusted, I saw long, narrow claws hanging from its hands, easily ten inches long. Patches of gray, matted fur clung to its body. The creature leaned slightly into the light and opened its jaws, and what came out still terrifies me to this day. “Champ. I need some help.” The voice was a perfect imitation of my grandfather’s, but hollow and mocking. I dove back under my blankets, heart pounding, hoping sleep would somehow take me again. Eventually it did, though the slow scratching on my window continued the entire night.

The next day my grandfather passed away in his sleep. The first responders said it was a heart attack sometime around **3 a.m.** I’m in charge of the farm now, which honestly hasn’t been too bad. But every night, once everything goes quiet, I hear his voice outside calling for me again…
