This happened earlier and I’m still a little shaken thinking about it.

I fell asleep on my side, curled up facing the wall. At some point I suddenly became aware that I was looking at the wall in front of me. Everything looked slightly blurry, like when you wake up too fast.

So naturally, I tried to move.

Nothing.

My body wouldn’t respond at all.
I didn’t panic right away, but I knew something was wrong. Then I remembered something my brother once told me: if you can’t move, try moving your fingers or toes first.

So I focused everything on that. I tried to move my fingers.

It barely worked. It felt like my whole body weighed a thousand pounds. But after struggling for a bit, I finally managed to move my arm and pushed against the wall beside me.

Suddenly my body shifted and I was lying flat on my back.

I could see my bedroom door, which was open.

But outside the door… wasn’t my house.
It was a mall.

Like a full mall corridor. Lights, open space, everything. That’s when the realization hit me:

Wait… this isn’t real.

Before I could even process it, I was suddenly back in the exact same position as before — curled up facing the wall, unable to move.
It started all over again.

Trying to move. Fighting the heaviness. Slowly getting control of my arm. Pushing the wall. Thinking I had finally woken up.

But every time something was wrong.

Another time when I “woke up,” the door opened into what looked like some kind of palengke-style neighborhood, the kind with tightly packed houses and narrow pathways everywhere. I could only see it from my bed through the door, but it definitely wasn’t my house.

Every single time I thought I had escaped the dream, I realized I was still inside it.

This loop happened around six to eight times.
The weirdest part? I could hear my sister the entire time. She was right next to me in real life watching reels on her phone. The sound never stopped. It was like my brain was mixing real life with the dream.

I could also hear my own thoughts incredibly loudly while I was trying to figure out what was happening.

By the last loop, I was getting frustrated. When I finally managed to move my arm again, I tried something different.

Instead of pushing the wall immediately, I used my hand to force my eyes open.

And this is the part that freaked me out the most.

It felt like something was pushing them closed again. My eyelids were insanely heavy, like some invisible force was trying to shut them. I had to physically hold them open with my fingers while using my other arm to push the wall.

Then suddenly everything went completely black.

And I woke up.

Actually woke up this time.

My room was normal again. My sister was still beside me scrolling through reels like nothing had happened.

But for a few seconds I just laid there staring at the ceiling because after “waking up” so many times in the dream… I wasn’t even sure if I was really awake yet.

Has anyone else ever been stuck in a dream loop like this?
