*Hilltop Museum is not responsible for any physical and/or mental injuries resulting from transporting assigned cargo and/or personnel. You may not go to a hospital without first notifying staff.*

*-Clause 3.42, fine print, of the Hilltop Contract for Michael F., cargo/escort driver.*

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[Previous](https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep/comments/1sbecrz/im_a_contract_cargoescort_driver_i_drove_the_most/)

I do not know how long I stared at my mangled face in the rear-view mirror.

After consideration, I realized the man who destroyed me was a specific agent of the museum: the Hunter. I was told they would send him if I broke a rule while transporting. Guilt flooded into my body and placed a suffocating weight on my conscience. I was too comfortable talking to the now-missing woman. I craved her attention too much.

I wasn't too sure if the money from this job was worth it anymore. I built enough resolve to commit to driving this odd car back to the museum and quitting.

The problems started when I began driving. I felt the muscles in my throat and chest mimic the motions of laughter. The corners of my lips were, despite the injuries, satisfied with their recent experience. The memories of listening to her talking about her odd, strict parents were all I could think about.

I found myself trembling at the thought of being fired.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Representative was already outside waiting for me. How did it know I broke a rule? How did it know when I would return?

I parked and steeled myself to meet the Representative. Was it even possible for this thing to yell?

"I see you have met the Hunter. He is effective." I nodded, feeling the loose bits of cartilage move with me. "While I believe it was more Sherry's mistake than yours, you did nonetheless request the information she so foolishly gave, which is a breach of your contract. I assume you will not make such mistakes again." It turned on one heel and walked back into the museum.

Normally, a single breach would end the contract. Maybe they like me here.

Two floors below ground level of the museum was some form of miniature hospital. I did not understand why a museum needed such a department.

After being healed far too quickly, I returned to the exports department. My heart thumped every time I looked at passersby. Whenever I waved or greeted strangers, I could feel my legs ready to jump while my stomach jittered. Excitement and anxiety live together.

I approached the desk of Sam C., the department secretary, and asked for my next assignment.

"How did you already finish your last one?" I recalled that Sherry's assignment should have taken sixteen hours.

"The Representative would be a better person to ask." She glared at me in a familiar way.

"It's an object. I was told to present you with this file." She pulled a metal packet with a sizeable lock with two keyholes. Two keys rested in another drawer that unlocked the packet. Sam carefully pulled out a lone manila folder and handed it to me.

\~\~\~\~

**Object:** Pearl of Orpheus

**Class:** Gani

**Value:** 3

**Appearance:** A white, lustrous, ideal, and metallic sphere 30 cm in diameter.

*Staff note: the Rule Writer requested that this object not to be put on display. The Director has formally disagreed.*

*Security note: See protocol OP1C for suppression guidance and PPE. It is not to be attached to this object file by any means.*

**RULES:**

**1:** You must ignore your reflection.

*RB-1.1:* Subject 1 stared at their reflection in the object. After 5 minutes had passed, the Rule Writer asked them to look away. Subject 1 said "I've been trying to." Containment room cameras noted significant differences in the reflection when compared to the real Subject 1. Specifically, the reflection was colored such that it was entirely inverted from Subject 1. The reflection also had much more scarring on its face. Its skin was drooping.

After 15 minutes of staring, Subject 1 was pulled into the object and exchanged with their reflection. In other words, they switched places. The new Subject 1 screeched as a volume greater than 100 dB (the recorder was shattered by the screech). Security attempted to neutralize it via external means to no avail.

Four security officers entered containment. Subject 1 appeared excited. However, soon after it became quiet and stared at Officer 3. Subject 1's hands distorted to appear as they would in a distorted reflection tile. They grabbed Officer 3 by the throat and broke their neck.

*Staff note: Officer 3 was the only married officer in containment at that time.*

Subject 1 was neutralized.

*Rule Writer's note: It was not, and still is not, clear where the original Subject 1 went or what their fate may be. It is not logical to assume they will turn into the distorted Subject 1, as that reflection existed before Subject 1 had gazed on the object. Yet, the possibility cannot be excluded.*

**2:** If you hear music, evacuate.

*RB-2.1:* Subject 2 obeyed Rule 1. Within 5 minutes of entering containment, they reported hearing a "beautiful harp" playing. Subject 2 displayed signs of possession soon afterwards. They proceeded to pick up the object (while not directly looking at it) and began to distort and invert colors as described for Subject 1.

***CB-1***: Rather than waiting for security like Subject 1, Subject 2 continued to hold the object in one hand and used the other to break the wall separating containment and the waiting area. They proceeded to display the object, which resulted in multiple instances of breaching Rule 1, leading to many cases near-identical to Subject 1. All who were married had their necks broken.

Security was aware of Rule 1 before Subject 2 was called to containment. However, when they refused to look at their reflections, the army of distorted people forced their heads and eyelids to break Rule 1. Suppression was achieved by \[REDACTED, OP1C\].

\~\~\~\~

There was more to this file, but it was blacked out.

Sam noticed my bewildered expression. "You will be transporting the Pearl of Orpheus to these coordinates, approximately five hours away. Good luck."

I kept rereading the file. Why did they want me to know this?

I walked up to the already-attached trailer. The imposing lead walls felt cold to the touch, even though it was hot outside. I looked at the door and counted the many chains, deadbolts, combination locks it had.

Yet, the Representative wanted me to know these rules.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

As soon as I got into the car, a nervous pulse propagated throughout my chest.

Cars have mirrors.

I scribbled with a black marker over the rear-view mirror. Even though I was sure the glass was completely covered, I resolved to not look in this mirror ever again. A calming exhale was immediately taken by a gasp. I caught my reflection in the window. The marker would not be enough.

I walked to my housing and brought a roll of aluminum foil to the car. I covered every single window, except the windshield. I checked every lock on the trailer multiple times. I banged on the walls and heard no echo.

I put another layer of foil on all of the windows and added two layers to the rear-view mirror. I checked every lock on the trailer. I banged on the walls.

I turned off the radio and set my phone to airplane mode. I finally felt ready to start driving.

As soon as I shifted to drive, I noticed my hand was different. Was it always this long? This curved? I slapped myself. The object could not possibly influence me.

Ten minutes after leaving the museum, a car I was next to at a red light was listening to classical music at a high enough volume for me to hear. I swore that I kept hearing harps. Though, I was no expert.

I stopped at a mart to buy earplugs.

The distance between me and the destination shown by my GPS was decreasing all too slowly. Was one of the redacted rules relevant? Could the object distort distances? I began sweating; saltwater mixed with dirt pooled on the sides of my nose and lips. Each droplet carried worry.

I pulled over in a panic. Each breath felt like a weight was being dropped rather than lifted. I slowly crept to the back of the trailer to check the locks. All of them were intact. I counted them many times to be sure. I even wrote a list on the back of my left hand to keep myself sane while driving.

I returned to the driver's seat and resumed the job. I was finally on the highway.

Not long after taking an entrance ramp, I was pulled over. This looked like a real police car, in contrast to my recent visit from the Hunter. A normal officer approached my window.

"What's with all of the foil? You looked like you were swerving a lot. Have you smoked or drank anything?" I shook my head and looked at the list. He made me take a breathalyzer and various other tests for sobriety. The foil fell out of his focus.

"That's a pretty serious trailer you have. What's in there?" I started to pinch the skin of my arm repeatedly.

"Cargo." The officer was not pleased.

"Could you be more specific?" I was pinching harder.

"That's all I know. My boss doesn't let me know what the cargo is." Tears welled in my eyes.

"I'd like to take a look." No.

"You need a search warrant." He grumbled.

"I'll give you a ticket for reckless driving." I felt my entire body relax as he walked away.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Each exit sign I passed appeared more and more distorted. The object was toying with me.

I kept imagining how it would feel to have my neck broken by one of the object's minions. I was cracking my neck often and scratched my skin enough to make it bleed. I periodically pulled over and checked every lock on the trailer. The list on my hand was the only way I could remember all of them.

According to my GPS, I was only one hour away from the destination when I noticed that a neon green car had been behind me for at least thirty minutes. I didn't breach my contract. Why would he chase me again?

I took a detour that only added ten minutes to the ride. The car did not follow.

This was a mistake─I was now much more likely to be stopped with other cars. Other cars that played music.

I was not convinced by the distance shown by my GPS. One hour felt more like twenty. I had grown certain that this object had breached containment.

That is, until I arrived at the destination.

A small number of Hilltop employees worked to detach the trailer. One came up to my window. I removed the foil.

"Why the foil?" The look of confusion and concern on his face annoyed me.

"The object." My voice was monotone.

"Huh? The cargo? It looks untouched. We can tell if something happens." The list on my hand had never lied. Once the employee left, I registered the location I was at.

It was a mansion of sorts. A couple, presumably married as shown by the woman's ring, stood outside. They were excited. I overheard a part of their conversation.

"Dan, it's finally here! That art I wanted from Hilltop!" The husband was calmer. A line from the object's file surfaced from my memory:

*All who were married had their necks broken.*
