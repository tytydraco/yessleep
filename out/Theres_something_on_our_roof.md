The mind wanders to various places as I try to sleep. I tend to think about my plans for tomorrow, keeping me occupied. It keeps me restless, but I can’t help it. My thoughts bubble up much faster in the dead of night. I envy my father because of this. I could only hope to be able to sleep as easily as he does. The moment he plops down his head on a pillow, he would doze off immediately. My mom and I had a laugh about it one time. My dad was lying down on their bed while my mother was talking to him. She had asked him question- although her eyes were on her phone- but to no reply. She looked up and saw my dad sleeping, just a few seconds after he had replied to her previous question. This made her laugh, which caused him to wake up and ask what’s going on. It made my mom laugh even more.

Mundane memories like these resurfacing is not that uncommon for me. I get so caught up in remembering things that I could easily go an hour before I even get tired. It doesn’t help when the night isn’t as silent as you would think. My bed is positioned in the corner of the room, with my right side facing the wall and the window just two feet higher than the frame. My neighborhood is not the spacious kind. Our house is hugged by buildings usually shorter than ours. I could look outside the window and see the roofs of other homes and imagine I could walk on them. These roofs are littered with rusty metals, damp tires, and dried-up feces. It wasn’t the best place to put your nose in, especially during hot summers.

Stray cats would always be the closest source of noise. It seems like at least one cat has to be in heat every night. They would scream their lungs out at each other, and the fights would make the roofs clang like rocks banging against steel. But tonight, they were silent. The only thing I could hear was the faint sound of a karaoke night a few blocks away. It felt strange, but peaceful. The night marched on as I started to calm down and fall asleep. Just then, my eyes opened as I heard a couple of quick, but tiny sounds coming from the roofs. A sense of familiarity came as I listened to feline footsteps come closer to my window. I waited for a while, before the creaking came. The growling of a singular cat followed. Whenever they fought, it always felt like a competition to see who’s the loudest. This constant growling would go on for a minute or two until the fighting inevitably starts. The cat near my window was growling in a way I didn’t recognize. It seemed to be edging closer and closer to the gap between our house and the house it’s on, as the creaking got stronger.

The cat’s final growl was interrupted by a thud. Silence came immediately after. It seemed to have fallen in the gap, or at least that’s what I thought. Something emerged from where the sound originated. My mother always told me to close the curtains at night. She said that it was to hide ourselves from thieves that would use the roofs to go in and out of houses easily. If they pass by our windows, we’d be able to see their silhouettes and act accordingly. The figure beside me was no thief, nor did it seem like it was even man in the first place. Its head was protruding forward, as if it was hunched over. Its breathing looked heavy but it was silent. And as the creaking came to be again, it started turning towards me. Ten seconds passed, and then twenty-five, and then thirty-nine. It stayed there until it turned around, and its shadow eventually got smaller and smaller until the creaking couldn’t be heard anymore.

The next day arrived and I was still thinking about what I had just witnessed. My mother was never really the superstitious type so I mentioned creaking and that there could be someone prowling around our roofs at night. She told me that she’ll call the local station later. I can tell that she didn’t want to look spooked but it was clear it had some effect on her, as she reminded me to lock the windows and keep the curtains shut. She told me that our neighbors were saying the same thing. Loud sounds akin to walking would be heard above their heads. Although, again, the stray cats seem to be the most likely culprit of these sounds. Nighttime came, and this time, I had more things on my mind that prevented me from sleeping. Aside from the shadow’s presence itself, I wondered what would happen if it came while I was asleep. As I pondered these new thoughts, another cat came by my window. It hissed the same way the last one did. Its sounds felt more “painful”, with a longer cry and a raspier tone. An uneven rhythm to the metal clues me in on a leg injury, possibly from one of many fights. It kept crying and crying while rustling on scrap metal can be heard relatively close.

After a few minutes, the rustling stopped. Faint, tiny steps were gone just like that. Though the pained meowing never stopped, and once again edged closer to the gap. But a bit of relief came over me, as the cat’s crying was met with a deeper one. It’s a usual fight, I thought. I certainly preferred this over the shadow but I had to brace myself for the clanging that would come soon after. A loud thud and an even louder cry came, as expected.

But then nothing.

Just then, the hunched shadow loomed over my window, making me jitter. It was closer than before. My instinct was to shut my eyes just enough so that it looks like I’m sleeping. Despite the curtain blocking the shadow’s view, I still acted like how a kid would act when his parents caught him still awake during nap times. It was an uncomfortable moment. I can hear my blood pumping, as I keep my faux-closed eyes and my posture intact. The minute felt strangely long until I glanced towards my wall clock and saw that it was now 2 o’clock. The silence throughout was interrupted by the occasional deep groan, which I thought belonged to a stray. The figure stood still by my window, as the slight gust from my electric fan tempted me in seeing what this thing looked like. Another hour went by without any movement, though the noises started getting higher in pitch. I was starting to doze off. The idea of this thing watching me as I sleep was creepy, but the lack of rest was much stronger. As my body started to relax, the wind from the fan finally blew the curtains up for a brief moment. The silhouette was still dark, masked by the neighbor’s lights illuminating from behind. Its eyes betrayed this path of light, as it shined like marbles despite everything around it looking like the void. It was focused at its front view, but its eyes snuck a glance at mine while the curtains came down. Again, I faked my slumber. I was terrified. After a few minutes, the creature slowly faded into the lights. I wasn’t able to sleep that night.

I told my mom about this “thief” again. Only this time, I said I was surer than ever before. She told me that our neighbors were contacting the station today. One of our neighbors had their gates broken into, and their dog was nowhere to be found. Another had their rooftops filled with various items and scrap metal all scattered around, like someone went in there and tripped. With this bulk of complaints, the local authorities would have to do something about it, she said. I wanted to say more but I reserved my grievances and waited for one more night. With the way the houses are built here, officers are given permission to do checks while on top of some of these easier-to-climb roofs. I thought it was the perfect night to get this thing away from my window for good.

But just in case, I took out extra pillows and made it look like a body was sleeping on my bed. As night approached, I sat just outside the window’s field of view, in front of my bedroom door. If the shadow comes and finally does something, I’m at least a few meters away from it. As I sit, hunched down, leaning on my door, I waited until the creature looms once more. I slowed my breathing in order to hear much more clearly. Aside from my air conditioner, the usual sounds of the graveyard shift would be heard. The occasional motorcycle passing by, the random barking from stray dogs, and the cats on top of these rusty roofs. Although, I noticed there was little fanfare from where I would usually hear them. It’s either they all ran off or the season of heat has passed. Regardless, some of them were still as rowdy as they were two nights before. Hissing would soon come, followed by footsteps. I thought this was the creature but these footsteps were heavy, heavier than that of a cat. It sounded almost… human.

I hear more scurrying as the footsteps came with slight murmurs. Somehow, I felt more scared, as the thought of the midnight thieves filled my head. I reached for the doorknob as I maintained my crouching posture. But before I unlocked the door, I hear a high-pitched meow coming from the other side. It was quick, but it was as clear as it could be. My window started rustling, like someone was trying to silently pry it open. I was breathing harder but I was composed enough to wait for another sound outside my door. Another groaning meow came, and I knew exactly what it was. Not knowing what to do, I tried sneaking under my bed. Just then, a couple of men screamed “Stop!”, as they shined a light towards my room, revealing a silhouette of a man just by my window. The man ran, with a loud rattling as what seemed like officers following suit. My mother knocked on my door because she wanted to see what was happening, scolding me over the window just outside my room being open while there was a thief on the run.

The morning came and footage of the man has been spread throughout the neighborhood. My mother didn’t want me to see, but I caught a glimpse while she was talking about it with her friends. The man was half-naked, with blood over his mouth. On his right hand was a small white, black, and yellow-colored blur. The dread can be felt as she was telling the story, but there was an immediate sign of relief as they realize that all this was over. The night after was quiet, and I was finally able to sleep. Though, I still wonder as to who did that shadow belong to…
