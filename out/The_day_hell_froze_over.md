*Burn forever or freeze forever?* Pondered Davey.

*Uhh, I'll answer this question when hell freezes over.* I joked.

  
In the dead of my living room, our laughter cut through the silence like an airhorn.

The little blue couch we lay on sat in pristine condition against the boring brick wall in the back of the room. While the other walls were painted in various Star Wars murals. 

"Live long and prosper" Davey muttered as he arose from the soft cushions, slicking back his hair to mimic Spock from *Star Trek*.

"Peace and long life... dork." I said, rolling my eyes as he walked out the front door on the far side of the room.

Davey and I met on a cold winters day back in 74'. He sat still as a shipwreck on the little red swingset outside the high school, staring blankly at the sea of woodchips beneath our feet. As if desperate to join the world of inanimate objects. To become so boring the world would forget he ever existed.

"Hey, cool if I join you?" I offered.

"Um- yeah. Oh- ok. Sure- g- go ahead" His voice quivered.

We swung in a heavy silence for what felt like an eternity when a gruff male voice permeated the air.

"Hey! It's Davey. Man, you really are hard to find... *Davey Jones*. Argh, argh, argh."

The owner of the voice was a six foot tall teenager built like a brick, dressed in a torn leather jacket with blue denim jeans. 

He loomed over the playground on the concrete at its borders, like death coming to claim its next victim.

"Davey, Davey, Davey." He mocked, shaking his head in disapproval.

"*You're not supposed to have friends*, except me of course. Remember our little agreement?"

"You shoved me into my locker. How was it an agreement?" Davey shot back.

"It isn't now since you broke it. But actions have consequences, don't they Davey?"

He took one step onto the playground. Then another. And another. Suddenly he was standing on woodchips.

I tried to speak, to get up, to do something but I was superglued to the swing. Only my heart pounded against my chest like a guest knocking to see if anybody's home.

He froze in front of Davey's swing. Balled his hand into a fist, and then...

*Wham!*

I pushed his swing into the bully full force. He went flying like superman before he even landed first punch.

*Thud.*

I felt the ground physically shake as he landed *hard* on his back. Like thunder in a snowstorm.

"Oh my God!!!" Davey shouted. "Is he okay?"

His skin was pale as the snow around us. His eyes lay open and he wasn't moving.

"Let me check his pulse," I offered.

I pressed two fingers against his wrist where his arteries should be only to feel his cold exterior. Not the familiar rhyhmic beating of veins commonly called a "pulse".

"Check again," Davey commanded.

I did. No matter how many times I checked his wrist and neck for a pulse or any sign of life, there was only a cold, fleshy exterior.

"I th-th- th-ink he's d-d- d-ead." I hesitated.

"What did you do? *WHAT DID YOU DO?!?*"

"Oh God. I'm so sorry. I didn't mean to hit him that hard."

My words seemed to calm him down a bit.

"Ok, ok." He breathed a sigh of acceptance.

"You were just standing up for me, right? You acted in self defense."

"Of course. Self defense. Right."

"We must move quickly now." He ordered. "I know a spot deep in the woods where no one will ever think to look."

I grabbed the bully by the shoulders and hoisted him up for Davey to grab the legs.

We walked off the woodchips and into the dense, snow-packed Aspen forest.

The trees were loomed over us like a jury of wooden statues and the wind howled like an angry courtroom.

  "Are we lost? The forest looks the same in every direction." I observed.

"No, we are getting close."

We inched up a small incline, dredging through murderously thick sheets of snow.

After what felt like hours of gruelling legwork, a point came into view.

"I think that's-" I paused to take a breath.

"The summit?" Davey finished.

Soon the forest down below became grass and suddenly we were at the top.

"Ok." He said wiping sweat from his forehead.

"Dump the body in *the wound*. At least that's what I call it."

On the summit there existed a circular depression to the far right side. It looked to be at least 30 feet deep. The walls and floor were comprised of coarse dirt and granite like the rest of the mountain had been.

After throwing it in, we made our way down the mountain through the path we had carved to get up through the snow.

"How do you even know the spot is safe" I protested.

"I used to come up here all the time with my dad as a kid. He was obsessed with everything abandoned mineshaft related. Anyhow, this was the only spot he found with no origin, no recorded history, and no hikers or bikers that ever come near it. You know how hard it was to get up here in the winter. Now imagine how impossible it is in summer."

I nodded.

The next few days were a blur. Davey and I safely made it home, but barely spoke after the incident.

Then came the inevitable news report of a missing 16 year old kid from our school. 

Seeing his face a second time on the news only doubled the guilt I already felt for what felt like ages.

I felt both relieved that our plan succeeded and sad for the victim and their family.

He may have been a bully but that doesn't mean he deserved to die.

 *Wham.* 

The front door flew open with a frosty fury.

It crashed into my side, sending me flying across the room.

The brick wall drew ever nearer.

*Bam. Grsh.*

Then the world went black.

When I came to, I was seeing double of the world in front of me.

I noticed the walls that circled me were made of *dirt*.

The hair on the back of my neck stood on end. Something about this felt... off.

I willed myself up, turned around and...

There lay the body of the bully. Now a skeleton in a leather jacket and denim jeans.

Then came a voice from above, "Thank you for sticking up for me all those years ago. But some people *shouldn't be saved*. Neither of us meant to kill him, but *one of us* must pay."

"Let me out of here you freak!" I screamed

"Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed, for God made man in his own image." Davey asserted.

*Thud*

A shovel peeped through the cylinderical opening up above, flinging suffocating globs of mud onto my face and body. *Am I to be buried alive?* I thought.

"Then shall he also say unto them on the left hand. Depart from me, ye Cursed, into everlasting fire, prepared for the devil and his angels."

Reality may be cold but the afterlife will be much, much warmer.
