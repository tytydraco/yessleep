It was a random Tuesday night. I was a broke man sitting in my cheap small apartment scrolling through job forums looking for some hope.

I was getting married in two months. I was desperately looking for a job. If I wanted to start a family I had to. She was beautiful. A trustworthy woman. Blonde hair, blue eyes, the works. If I didn’t get a job soon she would probably divorce me less than two years after marriage. And that’s when I came across it.

It was a website advertising a delivery job. The pay was good. I clicked on the sign-up form link that the website provided.

It looked simple. So I signed up. I was doubtful I was going to get that job.

But two weeks later when I was checking my mail I found the letter.

“Dear Mr. Roberts. We reviewed your information on our delivery company website. We have decided that you are eligible to be one of our delivery drivers.”

I was overjoyed. There was an address attached to the letter.

Shortly after, I went to the interview spot. I got the job. They made me download their app. I signed up through the app.

There was only one rule. You could never open the packages that you were delivering. I brushed it off. What else was I supposed to do?

The first delivery request came a day later. I accepted it. Of course I would. The delivery spot was at a run down apartment building. Room 403.

The first thing I saw when I pulled up was a lightbulb hanging on a pole for dear life. It was just swinging in the wind. I was glad that wasn’t me. I took the package off my passenger seat and got out. I walked inside and went to the front desk.

“Delivery for room 403,” I said confidently.

“Take the elevator to the 4th floor. It’s the second door on your left,” the woman sitting at the desk said flatly.

I walked up to the elevator and found the button. Or what was left of it. Looks like I was going to have to force the elevator doors open myself. I shoved my fingers through the tiny gap between the doors and pushed in separate directions as hard as I possibly could. When they finally did open, there was only half of a button lying on the floor. I pressed the button for the 4th floor and the button literally came off. The doors finally closed and the button clattered to the floor. When I arrived, the room door looked like it had been through an apocalypse. There was half a door knob. Most of the bottom part of the door was just gone. The room number had something brown on it. I knocked on the door and placed the package in front of it and just walked away. If the door looked like that, I wouldn’t want to imagine what the interior looked like.

I heard the door open behind me with a squeak so loud I thought it was coming from a base boosted speaker.

The next few deliveries were fine. I made 200 bucks in just three deliveries. I started to pay attention to see what was in these boxes. That’s when I found out some strange things.

One package said it contained a PS5. The box was not heavy at all. It was lighter than a penny. So I opened it. And what I found inside made me scream.

It was a human finger. It looked like it had been cut off cleanly.

I decided I was going to deliver the package normally. I didn’t want to get involved in whatever the fuck these people were doing.

The address lead me to a house on the outskirts of town. An old lady opened the door. An old woman that looked like she wouldn’t hurt a fly. I handed her the package and quickly speed walked to my car.

I checked the next package. It contained an entire arm.

I kept opening more and more packages and they all contained human body parts.

I kept delivering. One package even contained a single eye.

I didn’t know what to do. That’s until it happened.

I opened a package. It was her. My fiance. Her decapitated head was lying in the box and next to it was a note.

“Your next.” I screamed. I fell backwards. I cried. I reported it to the police. There was no evidence. No clues. I kept seeing that message over and over in my head. I fell into depression. I stopped eating completely.

It’s midnight now. I heard a knock at my door two hours ago. I didn’t open it. I looked through the peep hole and saw a man holding a machete. I was scared out of my mind.

I’m sitting in my room typing this. I don’t know how long I have. I’m writing this to all of you about what’s really in those big packages your neighbors keep getting.
