I have always had trouble sleeping ever since I was little. I wasn’t the type to get nightmares or even insomnia, but I was sensitive to just about everything. Noise, temperature, light, etc, would always make me stir or completely wake me up in some cases. I hardly remember dreaming at all.   
Once I began living on my own, my minor sleep annoyance was becoming major, as it would for anyone. Feeling restless despite laying in bed for 8+ hours was absurd, and it was affecting work, school, etc. 

I asked my girlfriend recently if I snored in bed, perhaps thinking it was my air flow keeping me up, maybe a deviated septum. She says she doesn’t recall, though she’s a heavy sleeper and may not even be able to tell. She concluded that I’m too sensitive overall, to sound and light, movement, etc. She suggested trying to fix these things one at a time.   
The algorithm likes to taunt you with your problems, so I was getting ads for all kinds of items. 

Slowly I began to incorporate different elements into my routine. White noise app, ear plugs, a blowing fan, softer sheets, memory foam pillow, the whole nine. I thought maybe I needed medication after all these years. Maybe even meditation.

I gave things some time, and actually saw a bit of improvement with my ear plugs, but I found myself snapping awake at the first sliver of light that hit the bedroom. Blackout curtains be damned, it’s like my body *knew* when the sun was up. I also didn’t want my girlfriend to have to wake in total darkness when she had to get ready for work earlier than I woke up sometimes. 

I wasn’t a full insomniac, so I didn’t think a doctor’s visit would do me well, but right as I was about to consider it, I saw an ad while scrolling on social media. The “99% Satisfaction” sleep mask jumped out at me. I’d seen sleep masks before, but dismissed it, thinking I would be sensitive to something placed around my head or eyes as I slept. The ad made me think I was just being lazy if I wasn’t going to exhaust all my options. 

Once the box arrived, I was unsurprised to see that it featured mostly the same description from the ad: 

*“The 99% Satisfaction Sleep Mask is designed to transform sleep from night one, and make the disruption of a full night’s rest the least of worries for every user. Trouble keeping yourself immersed through the night? Your lack of sleep will disappear, or your money back!”*

The translation wasn’t great, but the mask felt cushioned and soft, with gel that seemed temperature controlled. I was prepared to get my money back. In this case, I was likely the unlucky 1%.

“Looks comfy, my sweet,” my girlfriend had said once we prepared for bed that night. “Is this the golden ticket?”

“Probably not.” 

“Have some hope. If you worry about how much you’re gonna sleep because of this thing, then you’ll be so excited that you won’t get any rest at all.”

I appreciated the sentiment, but it felt a little naive. We both reached to click the lights, and I slipped the mask down onto my eyes. 

Then it was 10:13am.

I stared at the clock, my thumb tilting the sleep mask up as one eye peered at the analog. “No way. I’m late!”  
Despite rushing so much I had coffee on my shirt, I smiled the whole time on my commute. I couldn’t remember a blink, and considered it the biggest success of my life. My mind raced. It wasn’t a sketchy looking product, but had they put some kind of drug in the damn mask? I was beyond knocked out. 

After a shocked look from my boss at my never-before-seen tardiness, I was able to check my phone to see a text from my girlfriend:

*Scared the crap outta me this morning, but would we call this a success? Is my man well rested? lol*

The attached image caught me off guard. Me in bed… completely face down. Hands by my sides as if I was a stiff corpse atop the bedcovers. My head wasn’t even turned to the side, just my face in the pillow. I responded:

*WTF?? How long was I like that?*

The text came back fast.

*The morning at least. I thought u were pranking me, babe! I shook ur shoulders nd everything nd checked your pulse, haha.* 

Hardly a prank. Something about the photo was equal parts funny and unsettling in a way. A sleep mask knocked me out this much? Where I emerged from my covers and cut off a chunk of my air supply without a stir?

Either way, my afternoon slump was absent for the first time in forever, and I didn’t need a second cup of coffee. 

I was almost excited to get home and get to sleep. I didn’t want to seem totally lame, but when I skipped the nightly video games in favor of getting an early rest, my girlfriend didn’t seem to question it, smirking to herself as if she knew what was going through my mind.

“Tucking in early?” she asked. I nodded, blushing. “Can’t wait to spend a day with my well-rested boyfriend soon.”

“I know, right?” I scoffed. “I’ll be able to watch a whole movie with you without passing out.”

And that was the last thing I remembered. I woke up at 10:48 a.m.

My boss was less than pleased, but I assured him I’d be setting a few alarms from here on out, and made sure to finish my whole day of work. 

I checked my phone as I was about to drive home, surprised to see no text back from my earlier message to my girlfriend:

*I slept the hell in again! Boss thinks I’m having a rough time, haha.* 

It was unusual not to hear a thing from her. My mind rationalized a few things, including a conversation about her being busier than usual at her own job. 

As I got home, her car was there, so my more frantic mind-wanderings were put to rest for the moment. I went inside as usual, seeing her sorting through mail in the kitchen. As I hung up my coat nearby, I saw her nearly jump out of her skin, like a cat that had been frightened. 

“Geez! What the hell,” she scoffed. 

“Uh… what the hell to you?”

“Did you sneak in or something?” she asked, hand over her heart. I shook my head. “Don’t scare me like that.”

I chuckled, a little confused. “I didn’t mean to, I promise. You alright?”

Tension flooded the air, and I felt goosebumps creep along my skin at her lack of response. As I was about to open my mouth to speak again, she put her hand up. 

“You… sleep talk. I’m finding that out.” Her voice was… eerie. Like she was holding back, even as she let out a little chuckle. “The you in deep sleep is nearly unshakeable. I can’t wake you up.”

“Damn babe, I… did something happen?” *Sleep talking? Unheard of!*

The video she showed me sent the creeps up my entire spine. 

I was sitting up, 90 degrees, mouth moving. My girlfriend’s face comes into view. 

*“I can’t figure out what he’s saying,”* she giggled. *“Whispering something.”*

As she got closer, I seemed to get louder. 

*“...you think. It’s always closer than you think. It’s always closer than you think. It’s always closer than you think—”*  
As she realized what I was saying, her face changed. She reached for my sleep mask, pulling it up. 

My eyes were wide open.

*“IT’S ALWAYS CLOSER THAN YOU THINK! IT’S ALWAYS CLOSER THAN YOU THINK!”*

I screamed, bulging eyes, unwavering as I seemed to stare directly into her. She jumped back, scrambling from me, and the video ended. 

She said she tried shaking me awake for a minute before I completely crashed like nothing happened. 

“I’m so sorry, Babe,” I felt my hands shake a little. 

“I’m glad you’re getting sleep. Seriously. I think it’s taking some getting used to. But if this is some prank, I hate it.”

I shook my head. “I would never—”

“And if it’s your body naturally reacting like this to your nightmares… I hate it more?” She wore an anxious smile, biting her lip, tapping her fingers. “If something’s bothering you, you can tell me, you know?”

The only thing bothering me at that point was my body acting like I was possessed by sleep, and the obvious nightmare that I couldn’t remember. I had not a clue what I was referring to in that video, and I felt beyond conflicted. On one hand, the last thing I wanted to do was scare my girlfriend with my nightmares. On the other hand, the last 48 hours had me so well rested and productive that I was eager for night time just to get a good sleep. 

We both decided that a two-minute scare in the morning shouldn’t outweigh my resting time. After going basically my entire life on the verge of restlessness, I wasn’t sure what the norm was for me yet. 

Aside from sleeping through my alarms, which happened again. 

After waking around 11:28am, I was going to be a full two hours late for work. I called my boss and lied, saying that my new sleep treatment was actually causing some oversleep and health issues (just to get him off my back) and he agreed, saying that he’ll let me have the day to consult with my “doctor” so that this can stop happening. 

Somehow, this just didn’t feel normal. With no text from my girlfriend, I was already feeling suspicious that something had happened this morning. 

Before I even got out of bed, I decided to look up the reviews to see if anyone was as speculative as I was. 

Firstly, it took a while to find the correct sleep mask, given the lack of what seemed like an official brand name. I basically had to type the entire description on the box to find anything associated with the mask aside from the initial ad and storefront from a third party. No official website of its own, but something that looked like a review page. 

What I found… raised more questions. 

**\* \* \* \* \* Five stars :). I have been asleep for such a long time.** 

**\* \* \* \* \* Five stars. Everything has changed. :) Amazing satisfaction.**

**\* \* \* \* \* Five stars. Unimaginable and amazing experience. :) I cannot imagine my old life. It is gone.**

**\* \* \* \* \* Five stars. My w a r n i n g will only get posted if I rate five stars and use a smiley face. :) Please do n o t use this wonderful treatment! :) It’s a T R A P. :D** 

**\* \* \* \* \* Five stars. DO N O T USE MORE THAN THREE TIMES :D Fantastic! :) YOU WILL N O T BE ABLE TO E S C A P E THeeeeeeEEEE—-----------**

**\* \* \* \* \* Five stars. My dream product. If it is too late, you will find out soon. :)** 

Then the most peculiar thing. My wifi went out for a moment. When I refreshed the page, the last three reviews were gone. Wiped. My heart pounded in my chest, and my brain kept circling around the idea of a prank, like my girlfriend brought up before. It was too specific, though, and a prank this elaborate… was a safety issue. No more than three times? 

“Not fuckin’ funny,” I said to myself, looking at the mask on my pillow. I closed out of the website. It had to be drugs or something in the mask to explain MY behavior. Psychedelics? I had never taken any. I barely smoked pot in my late teens. 

I remembered what was stated about the money back guarantee, and decided that I indeed wanted my $18.99 back. I looked at the box again, and tried to find any number to contact, or an email confirmation for some store. I’d complain about this little creepy review website, too. 

After looking over the whole box and finding nothing, all of a sudden, it was like a number appeared out of nowhere. I swear I checked every face, even inside, but clearly not enough. At the appearance of the digits, I whipped out my phone to call. 

*“Press 1 for customer service. Press 2 for headquarters.”*  
Weird prompt. 

After some wandering around, I finally ended up with a real human on the other side, though it sounded like talking through an old TV screen. The static was driving me nuts.  

*“Hello. May I have your order number?”*

“993857.”

*“Confirmed. Thank you for being a customer. May I ask your comment or concern?”*

“Y-yeah. This mask… I need my money back.”

*“I am sorry you feel this way. What is the issue, sir?”*

I didn’t know what the hell to say. I surely wasn’t going to explain the weird happenings. “I think there’s just something wrong with the mask I got.”

*“I understand. You are not satisfied with the treatment?”* Her voice through the crackle made this seem like a sidelined issue.

“I’m not, no.”

*“I understand. You are not able to sleep?”*

That was hard to answer. I decided to lie. “Correct. It hasn’t helped.”  
*“...”*

I waited a moment. “Hello?”

The static cut out, and I heard a very clear, icy response.   
*“I do not believe you, good customer.”*

My heart sank into my stomach. The voice nearly sounded like it came from inside my room, not the damn phone. 

“L-Look! I’m unsatisfied with the mask. So the money-back guarantee—”

*“Good customer, you are promised your money back if you lack sleep whilst using our product. Do you lack sleep?”*

I was afraid to lie this time. “I… have slept almost too much.”  
Another pause, but it was longer. I clenched the phone in my hand, feeling my heartbeat in my palm.

*“You are welcome to stop using the treatment, good customer.”*

No money back, then. Not like I really cared about that. I was talking about the wrong thing with this person anyway.  
“Wha—y-you guys are scamming people or what? I saw the review site. The creepy stuff posted, and a warning.”

*“We hope you leave a five-star review, sir.”*

“Why would I do that?”

*“...”*

She didn’t have to respond. I had indeed gotten what I was promised through the ad. 

“If this creepy shit has something to do with drugs or chemicals in your mask, you guys will get sued, just so you know. I’ll join any lawsuit—”

“*You. Are welcome. To stop using the treatment.”*

I was getting mad. “I will! I probably will.”

*“However. You should probably find it first.”*

What? Was this a damn riddle? “Find it first?”

*“Yes, sir.”*

“Find what?”

*“...”*

“Find what?! What are you talking about?”

*“Find it before it finds you, of course. It’s always closer than you think.”*

*\*\*CLICK\*\**

My blood ran cold. I actually haven’t quite recovered yet. That was hours ago.  

I’m officially stumped. Those six words. This is no coincidence, and to my intuition, no prank. Something is happening. Surveillance?

Has anyone dealt with anything this strange before? I don’t know what to do. I think this sleep mask somehow got hacked? I didn’t even know there was enough tech in the mask to hack it. I’m contemplating throwing it away, but I can’t fully let the idea of it go for some reason. Evidence? Peace of mind?

Worst of all… I can’t shake the feeling of “something.”

Something unfamiliar, restless, lurking nearby… but it’s only when I stop paying attention. When my eyes are closed for a bit too long in thought, or when they shut while I stretch…the tiny implication that I’m not alone eats at me. I’m afraid to keep looking around. But I’m equally afraid to stop looking.

Worst of all, the anticipation eats at me. I feel like I should go to sleep…
