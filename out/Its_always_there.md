There's always something there, and sometimes I can see it, but sometimes I can't. I don't know if saying "something" is even the right way to communicate it to you; it isn't even really anything at all, but in some ways it is. It's just this heaviness that's always hanging around, and sometimes it's more- you know, "there" than other times.



Most of the time, I can just feel it around my body, barely more than a light pressure applied to my neck or limbs. Recently, however, I've found that it's got this one spot along my lower spine that it tugs at. It usually just feels like something's pressed up against my back, despite there being no obvious external cause. Aside from a bit of discomfort, it's not so bad. But less often, it pulls with so much force, and it just hurts so damn bad. It's fairly rare that the latter happens, but it's been doing that more frequently over the past few months. I've considered going to a doctor for the back pain, but I'm honestly kind of scared that they'll either say it's nothing, or actually find something wrong. Plus, I feel like I'm taking advantage of the healthcare system if I keep getting checked out for things that don't turn out to be problems. I'll probably end up changing my stance eventually though: this pain is seriously killing me.



When it gets really bad, everything just gets so heavy, like the whole atmosphere starts pressing down with insane force. Sometimes, when I walk into a dark room, my vision becomes narrow; I can't see anything that isn't directly ahead. But there's this thing that I *know* is there in my peripheral vision that my brain is just blocking out. It's like I can see it in the way that I know it's there, but I just am unable to fully comprehend it or something. It's really hard to put this into words, so I'm sorry about that. I'm hoping that maybe trying to explain this stuff might help me process what's going on; my brain's pretty foggy though, so there's that.



Oh yeah, and the other day, I had to keep twisting my head around to keep it from doing anything to me while I wasn't looking. When my neck grew sore, I had to relocate to the bathroom to spin naked in front of the mirror. It lasted for hours, but I couldn't stop. I knew it would hurt me in the places I wasn't able to see. The mirror was placed too high though, and I couldn't see below my knees, so I had to remove it from the wall and set it on the floor at an angle that fit my entire body in its frame. I cut my hair down to my scalp too; I had to so I could see the skin under it. Otherwise, it might hurt me. I shaved my scalp too; all while continuing to spin and look down at the mirror. Luckily, I only managed to get a few nicks, but I couldn't use a tissue or anything to wipe the blood - I feared when I did, it would take the opportunity to hurt me while I couldn't see.



I had to get more mirrors too; near the end of the little episode. It sounds kind of funny to think about now, but I ended up having three fairly-sized mirrors surrounding me by the time it was over: one from the bathroom, one from my room, and another from my roommate's (thank god he wasn't home at the time to witness this). Thinking back, I'm not too sure why I was so damn scared of not seeing myself. But at the time, it truly felt like if I was unable to witness all of myself, I'd be at the mercy of that thing; like the act of seeing was the only thing keeping it at bay.



However, something to note, I did find some pretty nasty bruising the next morning running up and down my calves, and especially around my ankles. I knew I should have moved that mirror sooner, but it doesn't seem too detrimental. They're almost healed now anyway.



Writing this was kind of a spur of the moment sort of a thing; I just wanted to do something to ease my mind. I was hoping writing it down here might help. That's really all I have the energy to write at the moment; I haven't been sleeping so great lately. If anyone has had something similar happening to them, please let me know, I really need to know I'm not hallucinating or something. I swear, I just have this god-awful pit in my stomach thinking about it.
