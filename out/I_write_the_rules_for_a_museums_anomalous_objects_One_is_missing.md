A paintbrush dipped into a full inkwell made of meat. The ink was the fluid I was fermented in; the one I see the ceiling drool and the one I vomit. The handle was bone, the bristles hair. My hair. The brush, defying its purpose, began writing in big, swooping letters on a dried square of grey, broken skin. My skin.

It was writing my name.

The brush painted a cracked, bronze spearhead. It somehow animated the spear piercing the center of my name. The letters burned away.

I jolted awake. A knock on my door.

The skin on my ankles now drooped low enough to drag along the floor—a chain fastened to me by the peach wine of Foxglove Ridge Winery. The haunt that started this hell.

I trembled as I opened the door. It was the Representative.

"The Director knows that you want to warn Foxglove Ridge." It still seems to be practicing its tone.

It shuffled away. Ominous.

My pager rang with a completely normal sound, dredged somehow from the deepest fear.

There was nobody else—yet I was forced to my knees as soon as I entered my office. The weight was almost as heavy as my guilt. It forced me down to my elbows. Soon, I was smashed into prone.

A pitiful puddle of sore skin, folded around a frail captive brain.

The pressure eased away. I was allowed to stand again. I walked to my desk and pushed the cold, apathetic button to remove the shutter on the window. The shutter was an underappreciated guardian—protecting my office from possession by these damned objects.

It took multiple, long presses to coax the shutter into obedience.

The window, the shutter, my office, and I—we all shivered. The spearhead on display was too familiar.

**Object:** The Spear of Caesar

**Class:** Ani

**Value:** 4

*T+00:00*—*Rule Writer's note: Requesting Ani classification and value assignment.*

*T+00:34*—*Director's note: Approved Ani classification. Assigned value of 4 for strategic necessity. Further rationale restricted. Named as The Spear of Caesar.*

*Security note: Ani classification granted by the Director. Follow rules and other directions strictly.*

*Staff note: WARNING. OBJECT CLASSIFIED AS ANI. PRACTICE EXTREME ADHERENCE TO RULES AND REMEMBER WHERE YOU ARE, WHO YOU ARE, AND WHAT YOU ARE WORKING WITH.*

*Security addendum: Refer to documented protocol SCJ34 before considering interacting with the object. A point to emphasize is to wear proper personal protective equipment, as detailed in Rules 2 and 3.*

**RULES:**

**1.** You must enter containment exactly as follows: Open containment door over the course of 30 seconds—use a timer synchronized with a quantum clock. Walk 50 cm past the threshold, turn around, and begin closing the door immediately—also over the course of 30 seconds. Turn around to face the object within 20 seconds of closing the door.

*RB-1.1:* Subject 1 attempted to enter containment via the usual hidden automatic door. Its average opening time is 10 seconds. They immediately exhibited severe psychosis and required neutralization.

*RBs-1.2–1.9:* Attempts to refine door timing produced identical psychotic outcomes or immediate fatality.

*RBs-1.10–1.13:* Attempts to refine threshold distance produced identical psychotic outcomes or immediate fatality.

*RB-1.14:* Subject 14 closed the containment door over the course of 30 seconds. Nothing occurred. They were then asked to wait 30 seconds to turn around. After 20 seconds had passed, they began frothing from their eyes and mouth and expired.

*RB-1.15:* Subject 15 obeyed all constraints currently listed as Rule 1. No effect observed.

*Rule Writer's note: Turnaround latency appears fixed at 20 seconds; no variable threshold was observed.*

**2.** Do not enter the object’s variable radius without Hilltop-issued shaded lenses. No substitute eyewear is acceptable.

*RB-2.1:* Subject 15 began approaching the object. Once they were within 50 cm of the object, their eyes began melting with the consistency of white chocolate. The molten eyes caused third-degree burns as they dripped down their body. They expired shortly after.

*RBs-2.2*–*2.4:* Attempted to verify the 50 cm radius. However, the specific radius varied from subject to subject. Subjects 16–18 had the same symptoms as Subject 15.

A security member was asked to loan their Hilltop-issue shaded lenses (sunglasses) to Subject 19. They were able to approach the object without adverse effects. To minimize risk, the Rule Writer is specifying *these* sunglasses.

*Rule Writer's note: Radius varies by unknown factor(s); distance should not be trusted even between consecutive subjects.*

**3.** Touch only the handle, and only while wearing black rubber gloves issued by Hilltop Security. “Handle” refers only to the black cylindrical end beneath the bronze spearhead.

*RB-3.1:* Subject 19 was asked to pick up the object by the handle. The hand that touched the handle immediately underwent total necrosis—nerves included. This forced them to drop the object back onto its support column. Exactly 11 minutes after Subject 19's hand died, sudden brain death occurred.

*RB-3.2*–*3.6:* Verifying the definition of the handle; all fatalities matched Subject 19's.

*RB-3.7:* Subject 25 was given Hilltop Security rubber gloves. Subject 25 was unharmed by touching the handle. Upon touching the sharp bronze end, however, Subject 25 exhibited the same effect as Subject 19.

*Rule Writer's note: To clarify, the object had still not breached containment despite 25 confirmed rule breaches. There is no missed documentation.*

**4.** Do not believe the statements the object whispers.

*RB-4.1,* ***CB-1***: Subject 26—following rules 1, 2, and 3—reported hearing whispers after holding the object for longer than 30 seconds. This caused significant distress. Subject 26 did not comply when asked to relay them. They did eventually say:

"I believe you. I need to go. I think you're right, and I need to go check."

*Rule Writer's note: Subject 26 behaved as though the object had revealed an urgent threat outside containment. Subjects exposed to whisper-phase appear resistant or unable to provide accurate relay.*

While still holding the object, Subject 26 began banging on the containment window—begging to be let out. They thrust the object through the wall leading to the waiting room. The wall separating containment from the waiting room underwent rapid material loss within seconds. Subject 26 ran out and was neutralized by security within the waiting room.

A new containment room was prepared, and the object was moved safely.

Subject 27 experienced the same whispers but exhibited a much stronger resolve than Subject 26. They were, however, unable to relay the whispers.

**5.** Do not hold the object for longer than 5.2 minutes.

*RB-5.1,* ***CB-2***: Subject 27, following all of the previous rules, was told to simply hold the object. Once 5.2 minutes had passed, the object changed its appearance to polished silver. Subject 27's whole nervous system signal turned red. They stabbed the wall to the waiting room. It underwent the same rapid material loss observed in RB-4.1.

Subject 27 pierced a waiting room subject. The victim's nervous system signal turned red, and two copies of the object manifested in the victim’s gut. The victim proceeded to rip them out and throw them at two other waiting room subjects. The two objects reappeared in the original victim's gut.

This cascaded rapidly. Within 20 minutes of the object breaching containment, over 60 people (staff and security included) were manifesting spears and spreading the effect. Spread ceased only when no further exposed individuals remained mobile.

*Staff note: Rules 2 and 3 did not appear to apply to silver-form manifestations.*

*Rule Writer's note: Silver-form manifestations appear self-renewing and do not require permanent transfer from the original host. Compliance with Rule 4 does not prevent silver-form transition at T+5.2 minutes.*

Ani protocol began. The Museum and auxiliary facilities underwent maximum lockdown. I could hear alarms and radios exclaiming for all residents to evacuate Foxglove Hill due to an incoming missile strike.

They had no idea that a missile strike would have been preferable.

The guests locked in the Museum panicked, of course, and eventually succumbed to the Spear of Caesar. It was horrible. The blood reached the ceiling. The spear’s point vaporized walls, doors, and ceilings. The victims were pierced over and over by regenerating spears. I tried not to imagine what they understood while it was happening.

I saw kin murder one another while crying. A mother piercing her toddler. The worst was seeing the toddler spread the spear; piercing many others before expiry.

The remaining security and staff were equipped with the Hilltop Security sunglasses and gloves. We were locked in our respective rooms, as per protocol. Essentially, we were forced to sit and listen to the chaos until the spear exsanguinated its last victim.

Security went out first. Then the staff. Then me.

The entire building was soaked in every bodily fluid imaginable. All the corpses were crying.

The most disturbing of all? Its silver forms were nowhere to be found. Its original form was missing.

Countless sweeps by every living person were performed to no avail.

The object was found 1 week later on another continent. By sheer luck, it resurfaced in a city with Museum contacts. Multiple events trailing from the Museum to its final location could all be traced to the Spear of Caesar. It was all covered as a contagious disease.

It whispered in my dreams the night it was returned. Per rule 4, I tried not to believe its claims.

The next day, the Spear of Caesar breached containment again. It has not been found.

A dark and furry spider of a thought has nested in my mind. The Director said I had already evaluated all objects they had previously attempted to classify.
