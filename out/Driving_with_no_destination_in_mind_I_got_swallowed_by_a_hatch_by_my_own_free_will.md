I just drove, and drove. Where would I go? I don’t know. I don’t care. My family stopped responding. For what? Don’t ask me. I’m just so fucking confused. I beat my hand on the steering wheel and accidentally honk the car, making me jump in my seat. I swear under my breath out of embarrassment, even though I’m the only one here. My headlights lit up the straight asphalt road in front of me. Branches hanging lower than I feel like they should on both sides of the road. The white stripes came one after another under my car. Yes I don’t care, I just drove in the middle of both lanes. Who would be here at this hour anyway. 

I drove, until a patch of gravel showed itself abruptly in front of me and I hit the breaks. My chest leaned heavily against the seatbelt and I could feel the car skid on the gravel. Trees in front of me crept closer and closer and the car stopped with branches kissing my front window. 

“What the fuck…?” Who in their right mind would just end the road like that without any signs? Or did I miss one while staring blankly out the window? I unbuckle my seatbelt and exit the car to check for any potential damage. It's not a fancy car or anything, but it's one of the things that keeps me going. I pull a cigarette and a lighter out of my pocket and light it up under the clear night sky after I see no damage. The area around me was illuminated from the headlights of my car. Unfortunately, the lights were dimmed thanks to the car basically hugging the edge of the forest. 

I look around with the humming of the car in the background. It was peaceful out here. Should I be scared? Maybe. But being away from it all in the middle of nowhere was what I needed. I inhale for the last time and flick my cigarette on the ground and stomp on it. *Clang*. 

“Huh?” I dust off the ground close to the cigarette and a metal plate took place. I glance to my side and  I see a dusty handle sticking out of the ground, announcing its presence. The neck on my hair raised and I shiver. I can’t tell why. My hand swiped from left to right to find a hatch hidden under the dust. Why would it be here, creepy. My intuition told me to not open it. But for some reason I really wanted to. But I trusted my gut feeling and just entered the car again, reversed and left the area. But the hatch for some reason is still attached to my mind. But after only a few seconds of going straight, I see the same gravel lot and I hit the brakes again, this time I made it before going straight into the forest. 

“What the fuck. I drove straight, why am I here again?” I glance to my side and see the hatch again. My eyes widen and I hit the reverse once again and accelerate faster this time. But what do you know, I have to hit the breaks again. I could feel my body tense up and my forehead getting sweaty. This was seriously fucked up. I glance at my side mirror and see the hatch again. It’s open. What, how? I stare at it for a few seconds, and instead of staying in the safety of my car, I reverse it slowly. I drive next to the hatch, and make sure I don't roll into it. On my left, there it was. I was scared to take a peek. But I have to. My curiosity is killing me. I turn my head slowly to look down. I don’t see anything. It’s pitch dark with the beginning of a ladder at the top. 

I check the surroundings around the hatch. I don’t see any footprints, or any other prints or signs of anything exiting the hatch itself. Is it safe to go out? Why does my body want to leave the safety of my car? Why do I turn off the car? Why does my hand reach for the handle? Why do I go outside? I stare down at the hatch, fully agape. It was just void. Not a single flicker of light. As I look intensely down the hatch, I swear whispers filled my mind. I gulp, and begin shaking my body and stretching my neck. I’m going down.

My body in the mouth of the hatch, my hand on the first step of the ladder, and the light of the moon was still grasping my face. I could not see my own feet below me. I take a step, and another one. One more, two more. It's as if something is calling me, pulling me down. 

I keep going, seeing the faint light that shone down the open hatch disappearing. It's soothing down here, I feel calm. I would guess I'm in the throat, seeing how it just keeps going. I have a feeling it will continue. 72 steps, 73 steps. I keep going. Total darkness. I take another step deeper into the void, I’m inside the belly now. My own breath being my only comfort. The air down here is humid. It's neither warm nor cold. Just right. But a weird smell started taking place, but I didn't put much thought into it.

Something touched my neck, I froze. It didn't feel like a solid, hard object. It felt gooey. It felt alive. My whole body shivers as I reach my neck and touch it. It was slimy, or wet. Like spit. I stay in place, not knowing what to do. I can’t see around me. My breath quickens and begins hyperventilating. The whispers in my mind amplify and my eyes begin looking around into nothing. My palms get sweaty and I brush them off on my shirt, not wanting to slip into the void.

I gathered my composure. I take a deep breath. And in that split second of pause where it should be just me and the whispers, everything turned quiet. Except a raspy, quiet growl, coming from below. I hold my breath. I keep hearing it. It's as if something is breathing under me. I reach for my pocket, my arm shaking. I try my best to engulf my lighter in a flickering flame. But my shaking hands are not doing a good job. A split second of light, then it disappears. But in that split second I saw that the wall in front of me was not concrete or anything you’d assume to be the wall of a hatch. It was pink, was I seeing things? Was it a trick of the light? Again a flicker. I swear the wall was moving. I take a deep breath and try to steady my body. The flame appears and stays there. The walls were moving and pulsating around me, drenched in some mucus. It was fleshy, like I was actually inside the belly of something. 

“What the, where am I!” I scream as I look around me baffled. Why am I down here? What has happened to me?! I glanced down beneath me, and just a few steps underneath there were teeth. A maelstrom of teeth spinning. I could see its breath coming out from the middle. As I looked into its mouth it howled and everything around me began moving faster. I scream back and begin climbing frantically up the ladder. I did not look back. I feel something touching my leg. It is wrapped around it. I look down and can barely see a tentacle of some sort trying to pull me down. I kick it with the leg that is free and it loosens the grip. I climb and climb. Until the night sky begins illuminating the area around me.

I fly out from the hatch and launch myself towards the ground, crawling towards the car and dragging myself inside. I’m crying and swearing underneath my breath non stop. I’m dreading the thought of that thing coming after me. I reach for the keys and turn the car on, until I look outside. I was in the middle of the road. There was no gravel lot. There was no hatch. My car was parked in between the lines of the road. My quick breath slows down and I furrow my browns in confusion. What is going on? I checked my leg that had just been wrapped by something unknown and I could still feel the goo. It was real.

Up ahead of me headlights were crawling towards me. Closer and closer. I hastily steer my car and enter my own lane. But the car slows down when it reaches me. The person parked right next to me, windows facing each other. An old man, he rolls down his window. I follow, still shaken up about everything. I didn’t even have the chance to warn or say anything before he spoke. 

“Did you see it?” I stare at him confused. I’m not in the mood to talk to anyone right now. My breath is still fast and my mind is still blank. 

“See what?” 

“Did you see it?” he says again hastily. My eyes open wide as I hear the desperation in his voice. I glance at him and nods. “Where, WHERE!? I need to find it, the hatch, I need to hear her voice again! Why did I run? WHY! I’ve been looking for so long! The old man starts shaking in his car and I can see he was full of anger. He turns his head towards me and almost reaches his head into my car.

“What are you-!”

“DID YOU NOT FOLLOW THE VOICES?! DID YOU NOT HEAR WHAT YOU WANTED TO HEAR!? WHERE IS IT!? WHERE IS MY DAUGHTER'S VOICE!? GET OUT OF HERE, ITS MY TIME TO HAVE AN AUDIENCE!” The side of the face I had seen had shown no signs of anything from. But now when I see it in full, staring at me. I could see the other side of his face had an abundance of small holes, and his eye had been replaced with an empty socket. The veins had turned black around the holes of his face and the mouth had been cut almost up to his hairline. I hit the gas and drove away from the man. I did not look back and just went on. Fear crawled up my spine and the hair on my arm raised. Where the fuck had I ended up?! Quite soon I hit an intersection with a sign that took me to the highway, away from the forest. 

What did he say? The voices, here that voice again? I had not focused on the voices previously. They had just slightly echoed into nothing in my head. But now when I think back. They were words of comfort. My mother. It was soothing. It was telling me everything was okay, that everything would be fine. Was that why I climbed down that god forsaken hatch? Like a lullaby which put me in a trance. After a few minutes of thoughts that did not really lead anywhere, I grab my phone on the other seat and begin calling my mom.

They had not stopped responding, I had. They were just not saying what I wanted to hear. But they don't know what is happening in my life. I take a deep breath. And prepare to face reality. But what I do know is that I’m never going back to wherever that was, and I’m never going down a hatch again, no matter how soothing it sounds. I can't explain what happened to me in anyway, but I do know that it did happen. And I'm typing this to warn anyone of they stumble across something alike. Something otherworldly and cosmic is trying to lure you down into its own maelstrom. Don't let it get to you, like it go to me.
