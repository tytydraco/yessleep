My sister has always talked in her sleep.

Full conversations sometimes. Laughing, whispering, arguing. It used to freak me out when we were younger, but eventually it just became background noise.

You get used to it.

At least… I did.

Last night was different.

It started around 2 AM.

I woke up because I heard her talking again through the wall. Our rooms are right next to each other, so if the house is quiet, you can hear everything.

At first, it sounded normal.

Soft mumbling. Pauses. Like she was dreaming.

Then I realized something.

She wasn’t talking randomly.

She was answering something.

There were gaps between her sentences.

Long ones.

Like she was listening.

I sat up in bed, trying to hear better.

Her voice was clearer now.

“…I didn’t mean to,” she said.

Pause.

“…no, I won’t tell them.”

Another pause.

Longer this time.

And then—

I heard it.

A second voice.

Not loud.

Not clear.

But not her.

It didn’t sound like it came through the wall.

It sounded like it was inside my room.

Right next to my bed.

I froze.

It said something.

I couldn’t make out the words, just the shape of them. Wet. Slow. Like it was learning how to speak while it was doing it.

Then my sister responded immediately.

“No, she’s awake.”

I stopped breathing.

I hadn’t made a sound.

I hadn’t moved.

There was no way she could’ve known.

The voice in my room made a soft noise.

Not a word this time.

More like… a reaction.

Then I felt it.

The side of my mattress dipped.

Just slightly.

Like someone had placed a hand on it.

Right behind me.

I didn’t turn around.

I couldn’t.

Every instinct I had was screaming at me to stay still.

My sister spoke again.

Quieter now.

“She can hear you.”

The thing behind me shifted.

The mattress dipped more.

Closer.

I squeezed my eyes shut.

I don’t even remember deciding to do it.

And then—

right next to my ear—

I heard it try to whisper.

It didn’t get the words right.

It sounded like it was copying something.

Testing it.

It tried again.

Closer this time.

And this time—

it sounded exactly like my sister.

“…don’t turn around.”

I don’t know how long I stayed like that.

Minutes. Hours. I have no idea.

At some point, I must’ve passed out.

When I woke up, it was morning.

Everything felt normal.

Too normal.

I went into my sister’s room.

She was sitting on her bed, on her phone like nothing happened.

I asked her if she remembered anything from last night.

She looked at me weird.

“Remember what?”

I told her she was talking in her sleep.

That she was having a conversation.

She shrugged.

“I always do that.”

I started to tell her about the other voice.

But she interrupted me.

“Yeah,” she said.

“I heard you.”

I felt my stomach drop.

“I wasn’t talking to you,” I said.

She smiled.

Just a little.

“Yeah,” she said again.

“I know.”

Then she tilted her head slightly.

The exact same way the thing in my room had.

And in a voice that was almost hers—

but not quite—

she said:

“It’s easier when you’re both awake.”
