I shared this in  r/AskReddit  and felt it deserved its own post here. This came as a response to “what was the most terrifying dream you have ever had?”

Know that this is 100% real. Thankfully I have not had these nightmares since I discovered them buried deep within my mind. But the dreams -were- real.

All I have to do is to close my eyes and I can see my trees.

——-

I had a whole series of nightmares as a kid that were so bad I repressed them. They surfaced again when I was about 30. I’ll try to explain.

I have consistently been good at drawing a very specific kind of tree. The whole thing looks like it is made of a menacing razor-sharp tangle of thorns. I doodle them all the time. Otherwise, I’m not a talented artist but people kept commenting that my trees looked creepy if not outright terrifying. I remember drawing them as early as mid-high school. Whenever someone would comment on my doodles, they were always a bit scared of them, saying things like “what the hell are THOSE?” or “dude are you OK those are creepy as hell!” I always shrugged and said “yeah I guess they’re pretty bizarre”. But I never thought anything else about them. They were my trees, and no one else can draw them.

Something happened a few years ago that jarred my memory in a sudden terrifying fit of clarity. I think maybe a friend asked where the trees came from because they certainly didn’t represent anything REAL. Someone called them out asking what they were from, as if they could have been part of some other artists’ demented portfolio. It’s entirely possible that I saw something that jogged that repressed memory - or possibly both things happened. Regardless I suddenly had a deeply terrifying memory of where those Hell trees DID come from.

They were part of a recurring nightmare from childhood, one that repeated itself with gusto, that maybe occurred from the ages of 6-12. A nightmare that I had DOZENS of times.

In this dream the sky is always crimson as clotted blood and it is always overcast with black clouds. To say the sky was a semblance of blood red is so accurate that I remember the sky actually did bleed - it didn’t just rain blood but if you were to think of the sky as a gigantic canvas with a tangible surface… maybe 200-300 feet up… that was what bled. Blood ran down the sides of the sky. Old. Rotting. Menacing. There is no sun, no source of light. The overcast sky always has a consistent putrid glow to it. The space itself usually has some rolling hills, some large plot of land only dreamt of within the inner spheres of hell, but I am mainly concerned with the gentle valley that is before me. It slopes downward and winds through an endless open space. The ground is pitch black, not of just the color but of decay and filth and terror. It is nearly barren with the exception of tufts of thin black dead grass that held a sheen as if dipped in old motor oil. The only other feature in this hellish landscape? You guessed it, THOSE TREES.

Thousands of them. Both sparse and menacingly dense at the same time. Craggly. Dead. Decaying. Made of pure black evil. Swaying in a breeze smelling of rotten flesh and decay but one invisible to the eye, not felt on the skin. And the SOUNDS. The sounds were so loud that it was as if the sky itself was rending in two. A low resonant rumble as if some monstrous demonic noise generator was left on by a malicious deity - but it surrounded me. It encompassed me, was trying to destroy me, consume my soul.

There was always - ALWAYS - a feeling of sheer terror of an unseen presence, some evil malevolent entity that wanted nothing more than my utter destruction. It only served to CONSUME. I could never see it - but I knew its voice was that of rending metal, shattered glass, the sound of a million souls screaming in agony. It had black skin as if it was made of Cthulhu’s portal itself. It had an immeasurable, inconceivable black maw capable of swallowing entire worlds and an even bigger appetite for children. I could FEEL it. I knew it for what it was - pure unadulterated evil. Words cannot describe the feeling of terror I experienced while in its’ presence.

I would scream. And I would run. Endlessly run. Trying desperately to escape, to survive, to not be devoured by evil itself. There was a sense that I could possibly rend the sky in two where it met the hillside, and I always tried to escape but there was never any escape to be had. I would realize that I was -inside- the evil with no way out. I would endlessly run, rotting, roiling smoke filling my lungs as if some gentle fog that was… not fog… more like the smoking remnants of a thousand tormented victims already consumed had filled the air, trying to serve some purpose but too thin and too vile to accomplish anything but create further insanity.

And I would pass these trees trying to escape. Endless numbers of them. I would try to look at them, to find something tangible in Hell itself. But they would always twist and distort and writhe with hatred for the living, for ME because I was alive. I could only look at the ground, or look at the trees. I knew that if I looked up, the unspeakable horrors just out of sight in the fiendish sky would descend upon me as one, not as a group but as a single coalesced entity of pure utterly terrifying EVIL.

I would always look up at the sky at some point, as I inevitably would. I would see it as an immense vault, as if the entirety of the world was contained under that tortured clotted cloud cover. The hellish landscape would consume me, close in on me like some pestulant bubble that had burst, would scream in at me with the pressure and force of a thousand black suns. I would begin to feel my body be consumed, ripped apart at some fiendish molecular level.

And then I would wake up screaming.

I told myself dozens of times that it was just a dream, it wasn’t real.

Those mornings I woke up with fog outside in the twilight moments just before dawn were the most terrifying. I would look out and see the sky glowing deeply purplish-orange and for a split second I thought it was REAL, that it had come for me, that I was going to die.

I still dislike fog to this day and it makes me cringe. And I don’t know why I remembered this so suddenly after years of repression.

But at least I know the connection to my nightmare trees.

And yes. I still draw them.
