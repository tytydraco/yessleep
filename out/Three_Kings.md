I sat staring at the burning village in front of me, the blood red moon hovering over it like an omen over my lineage as it leaves this realm. Three kings exited the flames, each of their crowns made of bones, flesh, and flies. Others walked out behind them, dancing in their bareness, screaming, shouting, and praising these three men as they marched forward. As I fell to my knees and watched them approach, the one leading looked down at me.  
“Poor boy.” The cheering and chanting continued, some faces I recognized before they were lost to mania. “Tell me child, what do you see before you?” He grabbed my face and pointed my gaze towards the dancing crowd. They sang pieces from a time long before and danced until they’re feet blistered, until their blisters popped, until their popped blisters infected. They sang and danced like children. “They are happy, are they not?” His voice boomed in my head as he showed me their unfettered joy. I watched as the joy consumed them. And when the joy consumed them, next came the rage.   
One after one I watched as those manic animals tore into each other’s flesh, the other kings laughing and laughing as glee melted from their mouths and high-pitched screams and shouts flooded the air. I heard a man’s voice change as his vocal cords were stretched from his throat. I heard a woman cry in a language that I could not understand, and most recognizable; my friend shout as his tattoo that, I only saw once, was torn from his flesh with a sour note. By the time the screaming had stopped my eyes had run dry, and a woman hummed a hymn from an old religion that no longer exists. She stood amongst the mass of bodies nude and heavenly, while the other lie dead and hellish. She swayed in her hymn, until her leg gave out and she fell to the ground.  
The king that held my head let go and I slouched, my knees aching unlike anything I had felt before. The flames of the village silhouetted the pile of once thoughts that sat in front of me. The kings stood in front of me, one kneeled down to meet my face. “See what we have done with only our presence, my son?” He got close to my face, close enough to see the lines in his iris. He smelled of rot, his hands, different from the ones that guided my gaze, felt like leather, gripping my skin like a bug. His nostrils flared and drank in whatever scent was left in the air, like a dog searching for the past. The air escaped through his mouth and he looked to the sky. “You fear us? Do not fear us, join us Noel, become one of us. Roam the lands that were promised to us.” His hands cup my head and I feel him lift, my legs slowly rise until I  am on my feet, him towering over me. “Noel, we are love. They were loved until their last breaths. Come with us. Be a scholar. Be a dreamer.”   
   
For the next few days, I followed them through the wastes of what was once Wisconsin. They gave me leather bound pages to record all their acts of animosity and brutality against other villages like mine. What I could not comprehend was why their aura of mania did not affect me like the others. Why these three kings held me not as a hostage, but as a scholar, a record keeper. One man from a village would join us for two days, and on the third, the largest of the three kings came trudging back to us, dragging his body by the back of his neck. Dropping him like a sack of spuds, his chest was gouged out, ribs splayed outwards like a budding rose.  
The kings would use him as a crude bowl for the feasts they held among themselves around a large fire, as I sat off to the side, my own bowl, filled with “food” that they made for me. I would watch them eat from the distance, their mandibles mashing the food, spilling from their dry mouths. I often stayed quiet, until one day, I decided to ask them a simple question: where had they come from? The one who held my head still that fateful night, the one that I began to call Remick, spoke.  
“We were born from the Book of the Grotesque. We harbor thoughts, truths, and falsehoods. We are not a single person; look anywhere, and we will be there, in pre and post life, in the now, the past, and future.” He looked at me, his eyes now glazed with a primordial hunger. The others have since stopped as he stood up and approached me. “Look to the fire, my pupil. Tell me what you see.” I watched the flames dance, memories flashing through my head as I felt his hand rest on my shoulder. I told him I see everyone they killed, and he sighs. I feel him release my shoulder as he moves to be directly in front of me. He kneels as the others stand. They walk to the fire and fall into it. Remick removes his crown from his head, skin tearing and being ripped from his muscles and skull. He places it upon my head, and I feel an immediate weight as the crown digs into my scalp, finding its place. I look into his milky eyes, and he gives me a grotesque  smile. He stands and lifts me with him. He walks to the fire and stares into it.  
“You were born from the Book of the Grotesque, you will harbor thoughts, truths, and falsehoods. You are not a single person, you will see yourself everywhere, pre and post life, the now, past, and future.” He fell forward, engulfed in flames. I watched them burn, their ashes float into the air, and the popping of their flesh.   
I am a king. I stand at the gates of a survivor’s village, and I carry with me the book that had birthed me. I carry with it a thought, a truth, a falsehood. A child approaches me, and I smile, as I whisper to them, such beautiful truths.
