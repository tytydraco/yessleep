I woke up somewhere I wasn’t meant to be.

I woke up from what felt like the deepest sleep of my life.

I couldn’t feel my fingers.

At first, I thought they were just numb—like I’d slept on my arms for too long. But when I tried to move them, they didn’t respond properly. 

Stiff. Slow. 

Like they weren’t mine anymore.

Then I noticed my breath.

Each exhale came out as a faint white cloud, hanging in the air before disappearing into the darkness.

That’s when I realized two things at once.

It was freezing.

And it was completely, unnaturally dark.

I tried to sit up.

My head slammed into something hard. Metallic. Hollow. The sound echoed painfully in the tight space. 

Pain shot through my skull.

I gasped, sucking in freezing air that burned my throat.

My hands shot up instinctively.

Metal above me.

Metal below me.

Metal all around me.

I froze.

Somewhere in my mind, a thought was forming that I didn’t want to finish.

I swallowed.

“Hello?” I called.

My voice bounced back immediately—small, trapped.

No answer. Just a low mechanical hum.

I pushed. Nothing.

I pushed again, harder. My head struck the lid again, pain flaring through my neck—but I kept going.

My breathing got faster. Louder.

I kicked.

Something shifted. A dull scrape echoed overhead.

I twisted, forcing my body sideways, panic driving me more than strength.

Then—

A crack.

Light spilled in. Cold air rushed out like something had been sealed away too long.

I forced myself through the opening, scraping my shoulder, and collapsed on the floor. 

I lay there, gasping.

Then silence.

No hum. No echo. Just my breathing.

I slowly sat up.

Bright light hit my eyes.

Rows of stainless steel drawers. Cold walls. 

Clinical silence.

A morgue.

I stared at the drawer I had just climbed out of. My hands were shaking.

How…?

How was I inside that?

A sound broke behind me.

Footsteps. Heavy. Controlled.

I turned.

A man stood there in scrubs. Tall. Built. Calm in a way that didn’t feel human.

He stared at me like I was doing something wrong. 

Then he moved. Fast. Too fast.

“Hey—wait!” I yelled.

He didn’t stop.

He grabbed me before I could fully stand and threw me back down with trained force.

“Stop!” I shouted. “I’m alive!”

He didn’t listen.

We hit the floor hard.

His knee pressed into my chest. I swung—he blocked it easily. Then he pinned my arm.

His hand went to my throat.

The air vanished.

Cold fingers. Tight grip.

My vision started to fade.

I clawed at his hands, kicking, but he didn’t budge.

Then—

Footsteps again. A door opened.

“Stop it!”

A distant voice.

Two more people entered.

Another employee and my mother. 

Behind them were my father and older sister. 

All frozen at the door. Staring.

For a second, everything stopped.

The man slowly loosened his grip. 

My mother stepped forward.

“What are you doing?” she said. “That’s our son.”

The pressure on my neck loosened even more. 

My father rushed towards us, pulling the man off me with sudden force.

I collapsed onto the floor, coughing violently, air rushing back into my lungs in painful bursts.

The cold room spun. 

Voices blurred.

But I was breathing.

I was almost choked back to death just for the sake of some small profit.
