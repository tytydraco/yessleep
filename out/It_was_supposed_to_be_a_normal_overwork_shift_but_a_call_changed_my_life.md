I didn't post this online just for people to be absurd about it. Please forgive my storytelling and narration; I still feel traumatized while sharing this. 

The date is June 18, 2013. I work at a call center. I usually take night shifts because I believe working overtime at night makes me more focused, unlike day shifts. But this time, it was different.

I received a call around 10:09 PM. My colleagues had already left, and the lights were off. I was still there, trying to earn an extra paycheck. This call was more disturbing than I expected. I thought it was a prank, but it wasn't. It was like calling 911 but on the wrong line. 

I could hear a man breathing shallowly, as if he were having an asthma attack. I asked if he had an inhaler, but he didn’t respond. I connected the line to 911 to see if I could help him. Thankfully, they located him. It wasn't in a house or remote area; *it was in the call center building.*

I texted my parents that I’d be staying later due to unexpected events, reassuring them I was fine. Once that was done, the 911 operator told me to look for the person while the police and ambulance were en route. I was nervous initially. I’d never experienced this before. Sure, I’d seen horror movies like this, but I never thought it could happen in real life. It all seemed too absurd. I grabbed a staple gun, the only defense I had, showing how unprepared I was since I’d never been in this situation before. 

As I headed to the stairwell, I heard strange, short breaths echoing through the stairs. It felt so close. I was on the 4th floor, and the sound came from the 2nd. As I stepped on the dusty concrete stairs, I saw a hand on the other side of the stairwell. It had blood splatter as I descended. I saw only half of a body, clearly a sign of a struggle. The blood was fresh. Someone was inside the building. I was stunned. I felt like it was my time to die, like life had given up on me, and I was destined to die this way. 

I ran upstairs, locked the door, and blocked it with chairs. I told this to 911, and they said I could go look. I felt furious at the person. I was offended—they were trying to coax me into dying needlessly! No ambulance or police arrived. I was still stuck in the office. The breathing grew louder. I thought it was one floor below, but it wasn't. I left the room in the elevator, grabbed my things, and exited the building. I cried in the street, feeling like I’d been freed from prison. I thought I was haunted. 

I never looked into that case again. 

I resigned from my job and decided to work as a freelancer from home. To this day, in 2021, I still feel no happiness with what I did. I still harbor anger toward the 911 agent, who made me act in ways that worsened the situation. It seems like everything was planned. That experience left me with deep trauma. I will never work night shifts or a job that requires physical presence. Everything about it still feels weird. No public cases or bodies were ever found, and my colleagues never saw anything. I'm not diagnosed with hallucinations; I knew what I saw.
