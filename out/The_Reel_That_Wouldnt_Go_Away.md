I couldn’t sleep that night. My mind just wouldn’t stop. Same thoughts over and over, like I was stuck on something I couldn’t finish.



So I grabbed my phone and opened Facebook. I started scrolling through Reels, just to distract myself. Normal stuff at first. Random videos, people dancing, dogs, memes. Nothing unusual.



Then I came across this one video that felt off.



It looked really low quality, like it had been compressed too many times. There was this faint reddish tint over everything.



A kid was sitting on the floor. Not doing anything, just sitting there.



In the background, there was a dog barking. But it sounded wrong. It was like the same short bark was repeating over and over. No variation, just a loop.



It made me uncomfortable right away, so I scrolled past it.



A few videos later, it showed up again.



Same video. Same kid. Same weird barking.



I thought maybe it was just the algorithm glitching or something, so I kept scrolling.



But it kept coming back.



No matter how many times I swiped, it would show up again after a few videos.



At that point I started feeling uneasy, so I closed Facebook.



I sat on my home screen for a bit, then opened Instagram instead.



I went to Reels again, thinking it would be normal.



It wasn’t.



After a couple of regular videos, that same clip showed up again.



Same kid. Same red tint.



But this time, the barking sounded louder.



Not just louder, but closer. Like it wasn’t just coming from the video anymore.



I swiped up immediately.



Next video… then again, it was back.



And something had changed.



The camera was closer now. The kid took up more of the frame.



They were still just sitting there, but their head was angled down, like they were looking at the floor.



I kept scrolling, faster now.



Every time it came back, it was closer.



Closer.



Closer.



The barking didn’t stop. It just kept looping, getting more distorted each time.



I tried to close Instagram, but it wouldn’t respond.



I swiped up again and again. Nothing.



The video came back.



Closer.



The kid’s head moved a little.



Not smoothly. It kind of twitched, like frames were missing.



Then it slowly started turning toward me.



My chest felt tight at that point.



I pressed the power button, but the screen stayed on.



The barking was loud now. Way too loud.



I couldn’t look away.



The next time it showed up, the kid’s face was almost filling the screen. Still hard to make out, just shapes and shadows under that red tint.



Then their head snapped up.



Right at me.



The barking stopped.



Everything went completely silent for a second.



Then my phone froze.



The screen was stuck on that frame.



I couldn’t move. I couldn’t even blink.



And right before the screen went black, I realized something.



The kid wasn’t looking at the camera.



They were looking straight at me.



I woke up right after that.



My room was quiet. My phone was next to me, completely normal.



No weird video. No sound.



Just my reflection on the screen.



I didn’t touch it for a while after that.



The part that still bothers me is this:



I don’t remember ever seeing that video before.



So I don’t know why my brain knew exactly what it looked like.



Or why it knew how it would end.
