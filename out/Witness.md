Her body was limp and motionless on the floor; the TV glow illuminated the silhouette and revealed my hiding place on the couch. I peeked out from my tent of blankets to see daddy picking my sister up. Her arms hung like a ragdoll from his arms. He held my sister close and cradled her before turning away and walking towards the back hall.

I slid out of my tent on the couch and tiptoed down the hallway after him. The light was on in my sister's bedroom, shining into the hallway through the crack left by the partially closed door. He was getting her ready for bed. He had her leaned up against his chest as he was combing her long, dark hair. I watched as he undressed her and put her in her favorite nightie; the Barbie one with pink laces around the cuffs and hem. Daddy then picked her up again to pull back the covers.

"Down comes baby," Daddy whispered.

He pulled the covers up around her neck and tucked in Jorgie, her teddy bear snug around her arm. He glanced up and saw me watching through the crack in the door.

"Go back to bed," he told me in a hard, smooth voice.

I obeyed immediately. I didn't want to be tucked in like my sister. Stumbling down the dark half of the hall I entered my bedroom. I quickly turned around and in one move, turned on the lights and closed and locked the door behind me.

All I wanted was to be in my tent, breathing in the hot, heavy air. The blackness of the covers was my shell; they were impenetrable. In there I was strong. In there I was her hero. In there I was invincible.

Breathing fast, I made my tent as fast as I could. My face felt like sunburn and hot tears squeezed through my eyes. I couldn't cry; I had to be strong for her. I wiped the tears away with the back of my hand. I took a deep breath and turned out the light before crawling into my tent.

Daddy knocked on the door. I heard the doorknob jiggle; I held my breath as he left my door. I listened hard in the heavy darkness for him to be walking back down the hall towards the living room. My ears were buzzing from the black silence. Instead of hearing him go back to the living room, I heard him go in the opposite direction, towards his room.

My breathing quickened, I grew panicky, and I listened for any sign of him going to bed. I could feel my exhaled breath bounce against the blankets; my heart was beating hard against my ribs. My eyes were wide searching my shell for holes and weaknesses.

I jumped at the sound of Daddy unlocking the door. I closed my eyes hard, trying to wish him away. He opened the door; light from the hall ran into my room, collecting in front of the door like a puddle. He walked over to my bed, pulled back my tent and come within inches of my face. The cold air hit my face; his hot breath smelled of alcohol and cigarettes.

"Your sister fell asleep," he drilled, the words punching holes in the air.

"She fell asleep," I repeated shakily.

I flinched as he smoothed my hair back. He smiled and then turned and walked towards the door.

He stopped short of the hall, turned and repeated, "She fell asleep." He accented the word 'asleep' with two quick finger jabs.

I swallowed hard and nodded in forced agreement. Daddy turned and went out to the living room, clicking the hallway light off after him.

I pulled my tent back over my head. I let the darkness smother my body and waited for my shell to return. I felt around my cocoon, checking and rechecking for holes, feeling for cold air coming in from the outside. The air inside became hot yet again, relaxing me and bring my breathing back to normal.

I heard Daddy fumble around in the kitchen, glasses chinked together and the ice machine whirred to life. He was making himself another drink.
