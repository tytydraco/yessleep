I shouldn’t be writing this.

I know that every time I put this into words, they get stronger.

But I’m not fully one of them.

Not yet.

It started three weeks ago at 6:02 AM.

My alarm was set for 6:15.

But at exactly 6:02, my eyes opened.

The room was quieter than it should have been. No cars outside. No pipes from upstairs. Not even the faint hum of the refrigerator.

It felt like the world had paused.

In my dream, there were two white figures.

One tall. One smaller.

They weren’t standing next to each other. They were inside each other. No clear edges. No boundary where one ended and the other began.

They had no faces.

But they were turned toward me.

And in the dream, I knew what they were called.

The Peaceful Visitors.

When I woke up, the calm vanished.

And the way it vanished… hurt.

\---

The first day, I told three people about it.

That same day, I started seeing them in reflections.

The second day, in the corner of my room.

The third day, beside my bed.

Every time they appeared, something inside me decreased.

First, my anxiety faded.

Then my anger.

Then my restlessness.

You think you’re becoming calmer.

But you’re actually becoming less.

\---

When my girlfriend told me she had the same dream, something inside me cracked.

I hadn’t told her.

I swear I hadn’t.

But she woke up at 6:02 too.

“When they’re there, my head goes quiet,” she said. “For the first time ever.”

That sentence terrified me.

Because that silence is addictive.

\---

The last night we were together, the air thickened a few seconds before 6:02.

She looked at the corner.

I didn’t want to.

But my eyes moved on their own.

This time they were clear.

The tall one almost reached the ceiling. The smaller one separated from its chest and spilled downward like liquid light.

They weren’t white anymore.

They were absence.

They didn’t reflect light.

They reduced it.

“They’re coming,” she said softly.

Too softly.

The smaller one stretched toward her.

She didn’t move away.

I pulled her back and stepped in front of her.

The moment it touched me, everything inside me went silent.

No anxiety.

No fear.

No anger.

For a second…

It was perfect.

That’s the danger.

Because you want it again.

\---

I still wake up at 6:02.

They’re here.

But there aren’t just two figures anymore.

One tall.

One small.

And me.

In between.

Not fully merging.

Resisting.

When I resist, my head pounds. My ears ring. Time feels slow after 6:02. I avoid mirrors, because sometimes I see a third density forming.

Me.

But thinner.

Less defined.

\---

They keep whispering the same thing:

“You’re tired.”

They’re not wrong.

But not like this.

I won’t stop speaking.

I won’t let myself dissolve.

Because that calm isn’t human.

\---

If you’ve been waking up at 6:02 lately…

If you’ve seen two pale shapes in reflections…

Don’t trust the peace they offer.

And if one morning you wake up and your mind is completely silent—

That isn’t relief.

That’s subtraction.

I’m still resisting.

But every morning, there’s less of me.

And one day, at 6:02, something might stand at the edge of your room very gently.

One tall.

One small.

And something that used to be me.
