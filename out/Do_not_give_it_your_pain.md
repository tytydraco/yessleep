It's always hard to figure out where you start to explain something, but the best place is the start.

I have not lived a comfortable life. I won't get into it, but when I was kicked out at 16, it was a relief. Freedom, of a sort, from my family.

The drugs came later. Years of sketchy needles and risky deals, just to cope with homelessness and trauma. You name it, I probably tried it. Much as the drugs took from me, they led me to an opportunity, as roundabout as it was.

Fentanyl-landed coke, the doctors told me. Overdose, cardiac arrest, the whole thing. I never felt it myself, but I was dead in the ambulance for 30 seconds or more. Out of everything I've survived, that scared me the most. So I made a change, got clean. The hospital let me shower and wash my clothes, so I was able to look presentable enough to get a job, and then a cheap apartment.

That was ten years ago, now. I'm still in a cheap apartment, but it's nicer than my first one. I still give thanks to whoever called me an ambulance, because it let me turn my life around and turn over a new leaf. It's a miracle, one of the only I've ever experienced.

And then the murder happened. Slit throats in their sleep, not even a fingerprint left behind. I found out on the news, and I felt my heart skip. My parents, dead. I'd imagined it thousands of times, of course, but I could only feel...hollow? It's hard to describe, the kind of conflict you feel when your abuser dies.

That's the night that my nightmares returned. Memories of the abuse made fresh by their deaths, again and again in torturous detail. It would be months before I found out they were called night terrors, but the sleep paralysis was the worst.

Half-in, half-out of sleep, dreams leaking into vision as hallucination. Unable to move in my own bed, in my own room, as one of them bears down towards me, evil in their eyes. I tried everything I could over-the-counter, but they just made them more vivid. My doctor suggested ambien, but I didn't want to risk it with my history.

With the night terrors and paralysis, I got precious little sleep. My numbers at work started slipping, and after a month or two I was laid off. Between unemployment assistance and similar programs I was able to keep afloat long enough to get on disability assistance, thankfully. Doctor took one look at me and signed off without hesitation, so I must look bad.

I haven't looked in the mirror in a couple weeks now. Not since the shadow man started following me.

I thought it was a hallucination at first, given my sleep deprivation. A sleep paralysis hallucination persisting past the end of the episode, perhaps. It wasn't until I accidentally caught it on camera that I realised I wasn't alone.

That night, it took the place of my hallucinations. Instead of torture and horror, it simply stood and stared at me, its eyeless face boring into my soul with each passing minute. Its jaw twitching and moving as if talking, soundlessly into the dark.

It's taken the place of my parents in my dreams, too. Instead of torture, it simply stares and mouths something unseen at me. I see it in reflections, behind my eyelids, in my dreams...

Last night I heard it's voice. I think it's been trying to speak to me the whole time, maybe it just couldn't figure it out? It's hard to say, but in my dream, it asked me a question.

"I can ease your mind and soul. Will you give me your pain?"

When you live the kind of life I have, there's a tremendous amount of pain in it. Interwoven into a million different moments, inseperable and incredibly difficult to heal from. When you add that on top of sleep deprivation, depression, and poor impulse control...

"Please take my pain from me..."

All I remember after that point is a snap, and I woke up. It wasn't until a few minutes later that I realised I wasn't under sleep paralysis, and I'm not ashamed to admit that I cried in relief.

Here's the problem:

As I've been writing this, each of the memories has faded from my mind. I don't remember anything of the events detailed above, but if I've written them then they must have happened. That's not the biggest problem though.

I don't remember my name. All my ID and documentation has been blanked or redacted somehow. I... don't remember anything. No birthday, no age, nothing. It's all gone.

So please, if you start seeing the shadow man, don't give him your pain.
