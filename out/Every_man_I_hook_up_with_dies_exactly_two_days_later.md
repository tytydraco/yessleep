I didn't download Grindr because I was lonely.

That’s the answer people expect, and it’s not totally wrong, but it's not the whole thing either. Mostly, I was just tired. Tired of dates that went nowhere. Tired of trying to read signals that never seemed clear until it was already too late. Tired of putting in a lot of effort for conversations that fizzled out like they were always going to.

I wasn't angry about it. I don’t think I was, anyway. I wasn't angry at women or blaming them or spiraling into some incel online cliché. I'd gone on dates. Some were fine, others awkward. Most just… ended.

What wore me down was how much work it all took.

With women, everything felt indirect. Like there were rules everyone else understood that I kept missing. Don't text too soon but don't wait too long either. Be interested but not *too* interested.

With men, it had never really been like that.

I'd hooked up with guys before. Nothing secretive or tortured about it. It just happened sometimes. It was straightforward. You wanted someone, you said it. If it worked, great. If it didn’t, you moved on. No one pretended it was deeper than it was.

So after one especially dull Tinder date that ended with a polite hug and a "text me when you get home" that we both knew meant nothing, I came back to my apartment, kicked my shoes off, sat on the edge of my bed, and downloaded Grindr.

I told myself I was just curious.

I didn’t overthink the profile. I picked a couple photos where I looked like myself on a decent day. No jokes, no clever bio. I figured if nothing happened, nothing happened.

I went to the kitchen to pour a drink.

When I came back, my phone was already buzzing.

I actually laughed. Out loud. Not because it was shocking, but because I'd forgotten how immediate it could be. Messages stacked up fast. Compliments that didn’t dance around what they were. Guys who knew exactly what they were looking for and weren't embarrassed to ask.

I chatted with a few people. Kept it light. One of them, we'll call him Matt, stood out mostly because he didn’t push. He asked what I was into. I answered. He said that worked for him.

He was walking distance close. I was bored and horny so I told him to come over.

I took a quick shower and he arrived twenty minutes later.

He looked exactly like his pictures. That mattered more than I realized at the time. Shallow, I know.

We didn't do a lot of talking. There didn’t seem to be a reason to. He stepped inside, I closed the door, and things unfolded the way they tend to when neither person is pretending they want something else.

I won't go into details, but I'll just say it was good.

Afterwards, we stood in the kitchen drinking water from two glasses that didn’t match. He leaned against the counter like he'd been there before. Like he fit.

Eventually he checked his phone and said he should head out.

I walked him to the door. He smiled again—different this time, quieter—and stepped into the hallway. I closed the door and locked it out of habit.

I stood there for a second longer than necessary, just breathing.

That's when I noticed the breathing.

At first, I assumed it was mine. That made sense. My heart was still racing a little. My apartment was quiet enough that every sound felt louder than usual.

But when I actually paid attention, it didn’t line up.

My breathing was quick and uneven.

The other one was slow. Deep. Calm. Like whoever it belonged to was completely relaxed.

I pushed myself off the door and listened.

It sounded close. Not echoing. Not like it was coming from the vents or the walls. Just… close.

I turned around.

The apartment was empty.

I stood there, staring at my couch, my kitchen counter, the doorway to my bedroom. Nothing out of place. Nothing that should’ve made me nervous.

The breathing didn't stop.

I checked anyway. Bathroom first. Empty. I pulled back the shower curtain, even though I felt stupid doing it. Bedroom next. Closet. Under the bed.

Nothing.

The breathing stayed steady, like it wasn’t impressed by my effort.

I remember thinking how dumb I felt standing alone in my own apartment trying to figure out if there was someone else breathing with me.

I took a deep breath on purpose.

The other breathing didn't change.

I grabbed my phone off the counter and checked the time. A little after midnight. Without really thinking about it, I opened Instagram, Reddit, then Grindr  again. I clicked on Matt's profile.

He was already offline.

I set the phone down and rubbed my face. Adrenaline. That had to be it. A stranger in my place, late at night, my brain filling in gaps.

By the time I went to bed, the breathing was gone.

Sleep didn't come easily anyway. Every time I started to drift, I had the feeling someone had entered the bedroom.

At some point before morning, I woke up with the absolute certainty that someone was standing next to my bed.

I didn't open my eyes right away, but I felt it close. Close enough that I could feel it on my face.

I sat up too fast, heart slamming in my chest, and opened my eyes.

The space beside the bed was empty.

But the mattress wasn't.

The sheets were pressed down, like someone had just stood up from them. The fabric slowly pulled itself flat again while I watched.

I didn't sleep after that.

Morning made everything feel smaller. Safer. My apartment looked normal in the daylight. Familiar. Mine.

I told myself it was stress. Leftover adrenaline. The brain doing weird things when it didn’t fully shut off.

I went to work. I drank too much coffee, and forgot all about my hook up.

Two days later, Matt's face showed up on my phone.

It was a local news article. Just a suggested headline I would’ve scrolled past if the photo hadn’t stopped me cold.

The picture was older than the one he'd used on his profile, but I knew it was him.

I sat down hard on the couch and read the article. Then I read it again.

No signs of foul play. I sat there with my phone in my hand and felt something settle in my chest that didn’t feel like fear yet.

That night, the breathing came back.
