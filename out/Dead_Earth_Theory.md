A blue sphere – a planet brimming with life.

Lights shined on the surface, outlining countries and continents. At some points it seemed almost like a mirror to the stars above.

A world of wonder above and below.

That’s how I see it here on the station, I’m kind of in between. Some kind of limbo, falling endlessly yet never touching down. Physics is fascinating, I gotta say. That’s what inspired me to work my ass off to get into this metal coffin of mine… Well, coffin, I used to call it my vessel. My Santa Maria and I Columbus, striving to reach new horizons.

I learned in life things change quick. As you grow old you get new perspectives, and the more perspectives you gain the closer to the real truth you are.

And the perspective I gained from being on this station, now three months too long...

Is how the world of light turned to darkness… It gives you perspective how fragile all this is.

I always imagined the apocalypse to be quick, violent. And I always imagined the lights going out on the surface, one by one, as the power grid slowly fails.

But that was one perspective, one lens, inspired by fiction.

But I saw lights, physically, I still see them. The cities still shine. The internet still works. From my eagle eyed view the world continued as if nothing happened.

It took some time until we realized that everything… Everyone, was gone. In an instant, poof.

How… I don’t know, let alone why. One of my crew-mates, a guy who originally grew up in the Bible belt said it was Rapture. He never was religious, I knew him for years. He turned into a paranoid wreck in a matter of days.

Another just kept trying to get into contact… Getting his hopes up when a reply came through, only to realize it was an AI. Another managed to connect to the web, choppy as the connection was, and create an artificial ChatBot of his wife and daughter.

He acted as if nothing happened. Everything was normal. He showed me conversations with his daughter… How “she” made him drawings. AI generated images, I could spot them instantly.

I… I had nothing down there – that is why, I theorize, I am the last one standing. The rest… Took matters into their own hands.

I had my fair share of burials at sea in the last few months.

Isolation tends to play with your mind – thoughts keep on coming to you. I even theorized that nothing happened, that the people were not gone but that this was some kind of elaborate social experiment… A sick one.

But that would not explain our inability to contact absolutely *anyone* on the surface. Nothing, nada.

I remember staring out the window, down to there. I saw some places lost their lights, mostly undeveloped regions I think. North America, Europe and Asia we’re still shining bright as if nothing happened, months after.

But what I found most fascinating was social media. There were still posts for weeks on end. Many of the videos posted were probably just scheduled in advance… While others I couldn’t explain. No people though, just voice overs or AI slop.

The messages on boards where “people” debated and bickered, AI, all of them. I saw entire comment sections of bots debating and fighting with each other over political matters that no longer exist. Hell I saw an entire damn terrorist attack faked in some country – videos and all. They said it was the latest news, they had news broadcasts with fake news crews and deep faked politicians saying their opinions – you even had reactions, thousands of them. Comments, likes, dislikes. But all artificial.

I even tried to get into a conversation with some of the bots, but you know how that goes. Most of them react to keywords and respond with pre-written messages.

The AI chatbots on the other hand, were a bit more productive. I even asked one to confirm whether or not the world below is gone. It could not either confirm or deny, it took the prompt as a hypothetical. It reassured me I was just going insane from the isolation and that help was on the way…

What a fucking joke… I managed to access live CCTV on some parts of the world.

Broad daylight on Times Square, empty. Tokyo, empty. L.A, empty. Berlin, empty – every major fucking city I checked, every single camera I could… Not a single soul. I doubt I even saw a bird, a pigeon…

Now that I think about it I never saw any animals either. I watched for hours, if there were no people I am sure wildlife would come to urban centers? They’d escape from Zoo’s…

Plants are still alive, that I am pretty sure. I can see greenery from space. The Amazon seems have even grown over the past few months.

Maybe… Maybe all of this is some sort of sick experiment.

The whole internet is dead, yet there is still activity. Ghosts, AI, bots… How the fuck is the power grid still on? I can’t sleep anymore, I can’t do anything. I can only observe, try to reason how all of this fucking happened. How did 8 billion people just vanish into thin air?! Why was I… And the rest of on the station spared?

Nothing is real I see. Everything is artificial. Not a living thing… Phantoms. An entire planet of phantoms.

The only thing left for me is...

No… No I will not be going down that path.

I have to get down there, I know a way. It is insane… But it’s worth a try.

Even if I die, which is highly likely, at least I’ll die trying *something.*

I’ll crash the station into the Earth – we have an escape pod which is only a temporary solution. I’ll have to do some calculations… But I think there is a slim chance I can do it.

I have to see for myself. I’ll go insane this way, looking through a goddamn screen.

This – This is my last post, probably. It’ll be lost in a sea of slop and artificial bickering, silenced by the loudness of those phantoms.

But I will rest easy knowing that one last message from a living, sentient being of this planet was sent out to the ether.

Mankind will either persist with me, or go in a blaze of glory.

My name is Isaac Arthur Smith.

Fuck you, whatever you are that made this happen.

Fuck you.
