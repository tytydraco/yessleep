I started a new job recently and my life has gotten much busier. I just graduated from college and took the first opportunity I could find to find work that would pay enough for me to move into my own apartment. The company I work for owns a couple of apartments near the office, and they've graciously let me rent one at a heavily discounted price. Despite having to move far away from home, the opportunity seemed too good to pass up.

The first week of my new job was going really well. I liked my coworkers, my boss seemed to know what they were doing, and I was picking up the work itself pretty easily. I thought I had found my footing until some weird things started happening. One of the downsides of my job is that we work alongside people in different time zones across the world, so I have to be in the office at some untraditional times. I have never been a deep sleeper, so this wasn’t a problem for me, until my alarm stopped going off.

At first, I thought I had figured out the issue. It was either that my phone had died during the night or I had seemingly forgotten to set an alarm at all, but as my alarm kept failing, I got more suspicious. I bought an old-fashioned alarm clock, thinking, for whatever reason, the alarm on my phone must be broken, but that too failed to work. I asked one of my coworkers if they had had a similar problem. 

“Haha,” they exclaimed, “you sound a lot like Dave, the guy you replaced. He was a stickler for punctuality, too. Right up until the end.”

“What happened to him?” I replied,  “Did he move on to a better firm?”

My coworker’s face dropped. “Oh. You didn't know? Dave didn't quit. He had a heart attack. Right there in that company apartment they gave you. Stress, they said. They found him on a Tuesday morning, three hours after his shift was supposed to start. His alarm was still ringing when the landlord opened the door.”

I went home and sat on the edge of my bed, Dave’s bed. I looked at the digital clock on the nightstand.

That night, I didn't go to sleep. I set the alarm for 3:00 AM, placed the clock on the floor where I could see it from under my eyelids, and I waited in the dark.

At 2:59 AM, the room felt colder. The air grew thick with the smell of old people. I kept my breathing shallow, my eyes slivered.

The red digits flickered to 3:00 AM.

The alarm didn't beep. Instead, a hand, grey, translucent, and flickering in the moonlight, emerged from the shadows beneath my bed. It didn't just press the snooze button. It slammed its palm down on the clock with a silent slam, and I heard a chilling laugh fill the room.

Then, the head followed. He leaned over the side of the mattress, exposing his ghoulish face. He pressed his cold, blue lips against my ear, his breath smelling like decomposition and general nastiness.

“My shift isn't over,” he hissed, “And you’re sleeping in my bed.”

I screamed, got out of bed, ran out of the house, and am now sitting in my car. Should I tell my coworkers what happened, or should I flee this cursed apartment and never come back? What do I do?
