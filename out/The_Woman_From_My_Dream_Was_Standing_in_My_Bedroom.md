I was standing in a field when I spotted a woman with her back turned to me.

I called out to her, but she didn't respond. An unpleasant feeling crept up on me.

Finally, the woman turned around. She was smiling, but I didn't smile back.

I didn't like her. I might have even hated her. I didn't know why. Was it envy? Did I feel slighted? I couldn't tell.

With the bitchiest tone I could muster, I told her to stop smiling.

And she did.

For a moment, wiping the smile from her face felt good. Really good.

But the feeling didn't last.

Other than the fact that she stopped smiling, my words seemed to have no effect on her. She turned around again and went back to ignoring me.

The unpleasant feeling returned. This time it felt worse.

I'm standing in a small square now.

People surrounded me. The woman was there too.

She was tied up, standing motionless while everyone stared at her. They didn't seem to like her.

I didn't like her either.

None of us did.

The woman looked like she had been through hell, but I didn't feel sorry for her. All I felt was disgust.

Maybe even satisfaction.

I could choose to be kind to her, or I could choose to be cruel. Either way, she would have to take it. There was nothing she could do.

Looking at her, I realized I didn't know what she had done.

I didn't really care either.

As I watched her, the woman slowly looked back at me.

Her expression was difficult to read.

I didn't like it.

That unpleasant feeling was creeping up on me again. It was different this time. There was anger mixed in with it.

Nobody liked her, and yet she still managed to make me feel this way.

It was infuriating.

And she knew it.

She had to know it.

It was her fault.

My body started moving on its own.

I bent down and picked up a stone from the ground.

Before I knew it, it was flying through the air toward her.

I don't know if it hit her.

Because I woke up.

I woke up in my room, staring at the ceiling, trying to process what had just happened.

All the feelings from the dream had vanished.

All except one.

Guilt.

The room was dark. I was lying on top of my blankets when I noticed the silhouette of a woman standing in the corner.

Long hair.

Motionless.

At first, I thought I was imagining it.

Then she started moving toward my bed.

I tried to turn my head to follow her, but no matter how hard I tried, I couldn't move.

It felt like I was paralyzed.

My heart started pounding at the thought of her feeling the same way I had made her feel in the dream.

Or worse.

What if she hated me too?

I wanted to tell her I was sorry.

No words came out.

As panic set in, I felt a hand resting on my upper leg.

It wasn't warm.

It wasn't cold.

I couldn't feel any fingernails.

Still, I dreaded the possibility of her digging them into my skin.

Then I felt her hair brushing against my leg.

First my right leg.

Then my left.

I finally managed to move my arms, though I still couldn't turn my head.

I reached out with my right hand and touched her head.

I grabbed her hair and tried to pull her away.

I had no strength.

I reached out with my left hand and pushed with both arms.

She didn't move an inch.

For the last time, I tried to look at her.

This time, I succeeded.

Her hair was the same color as in my dream.

But she was much paler.

I could see the bones beneath her skin.

I felt both her hands now.

And then I saw her crawling on top of me.

She moved inhumanly.

Like a spider.

Never in my life had I felt fear like this.

I clung desperately to the possibility that I was still dreaming.

I closed my eyes.

With every muscle and every fiber of my being, I tried to wake up.

I woke up in my bedroom again.

Morning light was coming through the windows, and I was lying under the covers this time.

My heart was still pounding.

I was drenched in sweat.

The woman was gone.

But the guilt wasn't.
