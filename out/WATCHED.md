My name is Christopher. One day, I was going camping with my friend Nick, my brother Gabriel, who was three years older than me, and his friend Marcus. We had found a forest near us where we wanted to camp. 

It was a very pleasant August day, and Gabriel had driven us to the forest because he had a driver's license. We planned to grill hot dogs for dinner once we had set up camp. All four of us had backpacks with the necessary items. To be on the safe side, Gabriel and Marcus had also brought two hunting rifles, as there could be bears in the forest. It was a fairly dense forest that could get quite dark at night, but that was probably one of the reasons why we decided to camp there. 

When we were near the opening by the forest, I saw something between the trees. At first, I thought it was a deer, but then I saw that it was too big to be either of those, and it seemed to be looking at me. I wondered what kind of animal it was, and after I had looked at it for a few seconds, it suddenly ran away.

"Did you see that? I think it was a deer or something," said Nick, pointing in that direction.

He obviously hadn't noticed that it was too big to be either of those. I was still a little puzzled, but decided it was probably a moose.

"No, I must have missed it," I replied. 

We walked into the forest, where there was an icy and uncomfortable silence, not a soothing one where you can hear the wind and birds chirping, but complete silence, and I had a constant gut feeling that we were not alone on our hike. We followed a path until our legs started to burn. Finally, we found a clearing. We decided to set up camp there, as it was only a few kilometers from the car and it was getting late. We grilled our sausages and began telling each other campfire stories. But the uncomfortable silence in the forest was still there, and the feeling that we were being watched did not go away either. 

A few hours, the sun began to disappear and the clouds began to darken.

"It didn't say it was going to rain today," said Marcus.

"Let's just hope it doesn't start thundering," I said.

We went into our tents just before the clouds opened up. Nick and I were going to share a tent, while Gabriel and Marcus shared theirs. We sat in Gabriel's tent eating chips and drinking soda. After a while, I felt my bladder starting to fill up.

"Don't go too far," said Gabriel, "and take a flashlight with you."

I grabbed my flashlight and went outside. Without the flashlight, I could barely see my own hand. The rain pelted down on me as I shivered and searched for a good tree. I found one and did my business. As I was about to go back, I heard a noise to my left. My heart leapt in my chest and I swept the flashlight in that direction. There was a dense bush behind two trees, one of which had a strangely shaped root that almost looked like fingers. It went up a bit on the trunk and seemed to have two glowing flies on it. I swept the light in another direction and then shone it back at the two trees.

The finger-shaped root with the flies was gone. In front of me were two ordinary trees with a bush behind them. It took my brain a moment to process it. Was I hallucinating? I decided to go a little closer and check. 

I stood between the trees and shone my flashlight on the bush. There were a number of broken branches on the ground and a hole among the bushes. Something had been standing there. Something had been standing there watching me. 

Puzzled, I hurried back to camp with my heart pounding.

Nicklas, Gabriel, and Markus were getting their sleeping bags ready in the tents.

"What happened, Chris? You look like you've seen a ghost," Gabriel asked.

I didn't feel like telling him what I saw, because I figured he would just laugh at me.

"What, am I?" I said, trying to sound surprised.

"Yes, you're as white as a sheet," said Gabriel. "Are you okay? You're not sick or anything?"

"No, no, I'm fine," I lied, because at that moment my blood ran cold. 

Behind our tents, I saw two glowing dots, just like the ones I thought were glow-in-the-dark flies in the bushes. It was here.

"You're not fine, Chris," Gabriel said in a slightly louder voice. "You're shaking like hell. Go into the tent before you catch a cold."

But I stood there, still frozen in terror. The dots were gone. Where did it go? Was it hiding behind the tent...?

I didn't have time to finish my thought because I suddenly felt a stinging sensation on my right cheek. Nicklas was standing in front of me.

"Get into the tent now, damn it!" he yelled. "You'll catch a terrible cold if you stand there."

He pushed me into the tent and I lay down in my sleeping bag without undressing. I tried not to think about what I had seen during the evening, but it was difficult. Eventually, my eyelids grew heavy and closed.

I woke up with a start; I could have sworn I heard something outside the tent. I lay perfectly still and listened. The water was still pounding against the ground, but through the rumbling of the storm, I heard twigs snapping and the sound of feet or paws on wet ground. And it was close, too close. That's when I heard It. It sounded like a bear trying to sound like a human, but it still had that animalistic bass tone. My heart started beating faster. Is this just a nightmare? I pinched myself on the arm to check, but to my horror, I felt a stinging sensation where I pinched myself. I looked through the small plastic window in the tent, and all the warmth in my veins disappeared. 

In the middle of the window, I saw two glowing dots. It was outside the tent.

Suddenly, everything lit up and a loud roar was heard. It was gone. No glowing dots were looking at me through the window. I listened for footsteps. Nothing. The water was still pounding hard against the tent, but there was no rustling. It must have disappeared. But where? My eyelids grew heavy again and closed.

The next morning, the clouds were white and the rumbling had disappeared. As I was packing up my sleeping bag, Marcus called out to us.

"Hey, guys! Check this out!" he shouted.

We went outside to see what he wanted to show us.

"Look, footprints," said Marcus, pointing to the ground outside my tent.

My blood ran cold. So it really had happened during the night.

"It was right outside our tent," said Nick. "Strange we didn't hear anything."

"I wonder what animal it was," said Gabriel. "It doesn't look like a bear."

We packed up the rest of the camp and headed out of the forest. The icy silence remained, and the feeling of being constantly watched did not go away. When we got to the car, we packed everything in and got ready to go home. I looked out the window, into the forest we had come from. In the middle of the path stood something long and narrow. It looked rotten and had glowing dots where the eyes should be. It looked at me and smiled with a smile that was far too wide to be human. My whole body shivered. When we started to drive away, It seemed to follow me with its gaze until we were out of sight. 

A few days later, I was home alone, sitting in my room reading a book. I felt my stomach start to rumble and went down to the kitchen to get something to eat. After making a sandwich, I glanced out the window at our yard. I wish I hadn't. In the middle of the yard, It stood. My blood ran cold. Its glowing dots for eyes were fixed on me, and It seemed to be miming something with its horrible, wide mouth.

"I'm waiting for you."

Three weeks have passed. I see It every day, and every time I see it, it's closer. Let it end. I can't bear to live in constant fear. Let it end. My time has come. It's standing outside my room. The noose is tightening. The pain is unbearable. Why did I tip over the chair? Let it end...
