I had an exam from 2 to 4 PM.  
I’d slept maybe four hours in the last sixteen. My head felt hollow by the time it ended, not tired, just *empty*, like my thoughts were arriving late to everything.

After the exam, I went to the canteen with friends, ate something I don’t remember , went to my room and laid on bed, scrolled through reels for a while, called home, and told my parents the exam was “fine.” I don’t know why I said that. I didn’t have the energy to say anything else.

Back in my room, I turned off the main light. Left only the warm bulb on. Turned the fan on full speed. It was already getting dark outside.

I lay down.

I don’t remember falling asleep.

And suddenly i was in different place, My parents called me and my brother to my mausi’s (mom's sister) house.

There was no urgency. No reason. We just went, me and my brother.

The house was quiet in a way that didn’t feel natural ,like sound itself had been asked to behave. No TV. No background noise. Even footsteps felt too loud. An awkward silence and sad vibe was filled in entire house, lights were dim.

They took us to a room and told us to sleep there. There wereThree beds.

On one bed on corner, my parents lay down immediately.  
On the second, my brother and I lay down. The third bed was already occupied.

Someone was sitting there.

Not lying down.  
Not adjusting itself.

Just sitting.

Its feet were hanging off the bed, touching the floor, toes bent slightly inward. Its body was extremely thin, wrapped completely in white bandages , not clean white, but old, worn, yellowed white. The kind that has been changed too many times.

Its head was bald. Not shaved.  
Just… absent.

Large blisters bulged beneath the bandages on its scalp, uneven and wrong. A single dim bulb hung directly above its head, but the light didn’t fully reach its face. It stopped just short, like the room itself was hesitant.

It wasn’t looking at us.

It was looking down at the floor.

I don’t know how long it had been sitting like that.

Before I could ask anything, my mother said sharply,  
“Don’t shout.”

Not *don’t disturb her*.  
Not *be careful*.

Just don’t shout.

My mausi added, without looking at it,  
“she's very ill.”

That was all.

No name.  
No explanation.

No one acknowledged its presence beyond that.

My brother was happy. I’d come home after a long time.

Out of nowhere , suddenly, He turned the speaker on.

The volume was not too high but yeah, high, The sound exploded into the room.

Too loud. Too sudden.

My chest tightened instantly. I rushed to turn it off, whispering urgently, trying to pull it away from him. He laughed and resisted, not understanding why I was suddenly panicking.

I looked at it.

It hadn’t moved.

But the bandages around its chest shifted just slightly as if something underneath had tightened.

The air felt heavier.

Then my mother shouted at us in a very worried and scared tone.

Not loud.

Sharp.

The speaker went silent immediately.

The silence afterward felt worse than the noise.

Me and my brother covered ourselves under blanket.

Inside, the darkness changed.

The blanket didn’t feel like cloth anymore ,it felt like a boundary. The space inside felt larger than the room. Colored lights appeared , red, blue, purple , pulsing slowly, wrongfully, like a celebration that shouldn’t exist.

I heard laughter.

Not from outside.

From everywhere.

It stretched unnaturally, slowed, cracked ,

and suddenly turned into crying.

Not dramatic crying.  
Not loud crying.

The kind that comes after something is already over.

I knew it was coming from it.

I forced myself to look.

The third bed was empty.

No bandages.  
No imprint.  
No sign anything had ever been sitting there.

The bulb flickered once, twice, then steadied.

No one reacted.

No one asked where it went.

As if acknowledging its absence would be worse than acknowledging its presence.

The room felt colder, and no one inside exept me, everyone was vanished with him.

I woke up suddenly, gasping.

I was in my room.

Same warm light.  
Same fan.  
Dark outside.

My friends were laughing somewhere in the corridor.

But for a few seconds, I didn’t move.

Because I wasn’t sure if I had woken up alone, mind was confused and scared.

I didn’t turn my head immediately. I listened. I waited.

Only when the room stayed ordinary did my body finally accept that I was awake.

It was 8 PM. I’d slept for two hours.

I felt disturbingly fresh.

And even now, writing this, what scares me isn’t only what it looked like but also the way it was sitting.

Like it was regretting something.  
Like it knew it couldn’t undo it.  
Like it had already accepted whatever came next.

Everything felt so real that i was confused about reality after waking up. It felt more real than reality itself.

And the worst part?

No one in the dream thought its presence was strange.

Only me.

 And thinking about that sudden crying and everyone suddenly vainishing still giving me chills.
