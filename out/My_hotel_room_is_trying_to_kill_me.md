Summer came around after a hard year of work. A few months ago, I was excitedly planning a two week trip to New York, looking for rooms that weren’t too expensive and stalking broadway shows for the best seats.

The plane ride was nothing special. I put on *Crazy Rich Asians*, my to go plane movie, and then watched what I could of *Sinners* before landing.

My hotel room was actually pretty nice for what it was. The room wasn’t big but felt spacious. To the left of the front door was a small bathroom with a shower, toilet and sink. Behind that was bed with drawers on either side complete with lamps and outlets, and a desk on the opposite side where I could put my things. 

I jumped onto the bed and scrolled for a few minutes before getting ready to hit the streets. I wanted to go check out some stores nearby before finding a decent restaurant to eat dinner at.

For the first few days, nothing seemed out of the ordinary. I was having the time of my life watching broadway shows during the day and watching Netflix shows by night.

It wasn’t until after the first week when I noticed my room felt… smaller. Like it had enclosed in on all sides by a few inches.

I waved it off. It might’ve just been my imagination. I just needed to get more sleep and stop watching shows until 4am.

And I did end up going to sleep earlier that night. It didn’t help my case.

The next day, I came back after some late night activities to find the room had shrunk even further. I was certain that it wasn’t this small before.

The bed was pushed closer to the desk, leaving barely any walking space for me. The front door which used to swing wide open now hits the bathroom door when I enter.

I decided I would report this to the front desk, though I was unsure of how they would respond to this issue.

I turned around and tried to unlock the deadbolt, the extra safety measure that the hotel rooms had and I happily used.

It didn’t budge.

I tried again, using all of my strength to get it to unlock, but it was like someone had welded it to the door.

Panic started to set in.

I tried calling the hotel desk.

There was no service.

I squeezed between the bed and the desk to reach the window, throwing the curtains open and banging on the glass, screaming for help.

Nobody seemed to notice me, and the busy streets continued on without so much as a single glance towards my direction.

I was trapped in this shrinking room with no service and no way out. Not even a way to call for help. I tried knocking the door down with no success.

It’s been 5 days since I was trapped. The room has shrunk to a size that can barely contain me and its contents.

The bed, desk, lamps and bathroom contents have all been crushed under me. Pieces wood and porcelain bite into my legs and arms as I type. The room has gotten so small that the only way for me to fit is to curl up into a little helpless ball, my knees against my chest and neck craned to the side. 

The wall is pressing against my ear. It feels uncomfortable. I don’t remember how long it’s been since I have been able to hold my head straight. To have the freedom of space.

Now that I’m in this state, the room finally let the phone service in again. Maybe it’s showing mercy. To let me say my goodbyes. I’ve consolidated my phone battery, praying for this moment.

I’ve already called the police. I don’t think they will be able to save me.

Please help me.

The room is closing in.

It’s going to crush me.

I’m not ready to die.
