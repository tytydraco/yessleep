The sun was setting, and I was walking alone through a particularly run-down part of town. Windows were broken and garbage rolled past like tumbleweed. There weren't even the roaming drug addicts my brain expected to see in a place like this, as if they, too, couldn't stand being here. Needless to say it wasn't a place you'd expect to run into a street performer. Let alone a magician.

But, the one person I saw, startling me, indeed wore a tall black top hat and a flowing red cape. He towered on the edge of normal human height, and leaned forward a bit, reminding me of a stick bug.

I pretended not to notice.

He said, "Would you like to see a magic trick?"

His voice was like an old-timey radio voice. I kept walking.

Then he said, a bit more stern, "Would you like to see a magic trick?"

I must have accidentally walked a little faster.

Because then he said, "I know you like magic tricks."

"Fine," I said. I tried to stop myself but couldn't. I turned to face the Magician.

Obviously satisfied, he said, "I am going to pull a rabbit out of my hat."

"Go on," I said. Openly attentive now.

He took off that tall black top hat, revealing his perfect hair, reached his white-gloved hand deep inside the hat, and pulled out a white rabbit. The white rabbit was alive, and jumped out of his hand, and hopped off down some alley.

That's not what interested me though. I could not help but notice: to reach the rabbit, the Magician had reached his arm into his top hat *shoulder deep*.

"How did you do that?" I blurted.

"A magician never reveals his secrets," he said.

"No, I mean, where did your arm go? Your hat is long but your arm is more than twice as long. Where did your arm go? How?"

He smiled. "A magician never reveals his secrets."

"Can you do another magic trick?" I caught myself asking. I thought maybe if I could get him to reach his arm into the hat again, I could figure out the secret.

He frowned. "No."

"No?"

"Only one trick," he said. He shook his head.

"Why only one?"

"If you see only one magic trick, then you can convince yourself it is fake. But if you saw more than one..."

"What? If I saw more than one what?"

"You would understand, permanently, that nothing is real."

With that the Magician put his tall black top hat back on his head, and started to walk away.

I looked around wildly for if anyone else saw this.

Then I looked back, and the Magician was gone.

I was alone.

Do you know that feeling you get, when you just barely survived an intense near-death experience, like a car accident or skiing mishap, and you can't stop thinking about it, and you feel like you're living on borrowed time, almost, and nothing quite feels real?

It felt like that.

It happened years ago and it feels like that.

Right now I'm alone in my shitty apartment and someone is banging on the door, saying they're the FBI. I don't know what I did wrong, but I have a strong feeling that once I let them in, or they let themselves in, I won't be back for a long time. I know this is connected to that business with the Magician. I don't know how, but it is.

I guess I haven't gone out yet, because I'm wondering whether they're real.
