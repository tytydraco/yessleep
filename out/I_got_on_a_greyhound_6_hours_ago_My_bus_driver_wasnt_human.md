**You know when you were a kid and you used to act like you would run away?** How you would pack a backpack with a blanket, pillow, stuffed animals and some animal crackers. You would get it all ready and walk right by your mom. Remembering to be as dramatic as humanly possible. You would walk to the end of your street and hold up your thumb as if to find a ride. And then you would ultimately grow bored after five minutes and head back to your house acting like nothing happened. Do you remember that? I do.

I remember doing it countless times as a kid. I even got picked up once. (It wasn’t weird. It was the coach of whatever sports team I was on at the time and he just took a big loop all the way back to my house) I remember wanting to run away from my house, from my parents, from the shitty town that I lived in. I needed to get away. I didn’t realize back then that I would ever actually follow through on running away. I didn’t realize for 23 years. At 4 AM this morning I pulled the trigger. I ran away. Like I was a child. Like the child I am.

See, even as the grown ass man I am I still am the most childish person I know. And I have three brothers in elementary school. I don’t know if I should use past tense there. *Had* doesn’t really fit. They still are healthy and living, as far as I know. 

I have had severe ADHD my entire life. From the time I was a little kid I can remember throwing severe tantrums just for something to do. I never could focus (Especially in school) and because of that my parents were forced to pull me from public to homeschooling when I was a junior. I didn’t ever really mind. I never had any friends in school anyway. I was the nerd who ate lunch on the table in the corner. I was the *kid who doesn’t fit in with all the stereotypical school stereotypes* trope, living and breathing. 

Anyway, back to running away like a little kid.

Living with your parents until you are 23 sounds as bad as it is. I always felt like my parents resented me for some unseen reason and that just seemed to escalate when I decided to skip out on college (I didn’t even apply) and keep living with my parents in my childhood home. They both acted like they supported me but I knew it wasn’t true. 

  
  


So after 5 years of sitting at home. Just straight up left. In the middle of the night.

I went to the greyhound bus-stop at the edge of town. No one was there (as I expected) so I sat down alone and waited for the bus. Come to think of it, I didn’t even know if a bus was coming. I guess it was sheer luck that one did. ….wait….Come to think of it #2, I can’t remember literally any time in my life seeing that bus-stop. Not EVER. Yet I went right there when I wanted to leave. I guess that makes sense now that I have seen what I have seen. 

I think we have gotten to know each other enough now. We should probably talk about what happened today. 

I got on the bus at 4:46 AM. There was no one on the bus except for the driver. He looked middle aged, maybe like late 30s early 40s, but he had little streaks of grey in his hair. The way he looked sorta reminded me of the saying ‘*wise beyond his years*’.  He was wearing a blank black t-shirt with a black jacket that was only zipped up halfway. He had on black jeans. They weren’t tight by any means. They also weren’t baggy at all. They looked like, uhhh… how do I say this? It’s like they fit *perfectly.* I know it seems strange to dwell on the fit of someone's pants, but there was something unnatural about the way his pants looked. The way *HE* looked. Just. Strange.

I didn’t get a look at his shoes but I deduce they must have been black as well. He had a gaping smile. Not really incredibly gaping, like to the point of creepiness, but just kind of a warm gaping look. Not completely unnatural, but it still didn’t sit right in my book. 

He greeted me with a nod and a quick “Enjoy your ride sir!” His voice was scratchy and also, somehow, high-energy. I took a seat around the middle of the bus and powered on my phone. I checked my snap (no friends) My reddit (no followers) and I doomscrolled Instagram for a while (nothing good). After a while of mind-numbing shortform content I put my phone down and sat in silence. That’s when my bus driver spoke.

I almost jumped when he started talking. I don’t know why. “I was waiting for you to get off that damn thing so we could talk! How is your ride going sir? I pride myself in having the best possible service for my passengers! They always get off with my bus with big smiles on their faces!” I shuddered again as he finished speaking. “Is that so?..” I said, not really looking for a reply. “Yep! It's truer than night!” He replied. (Not a saying I have heard before) 

It was another about thirty minutes before he spoke again. “Where are you from, sir?” He asked. “Just \_\_\_\_\_\_, Where you picked me up”  I said. I don’t know why I chose to, but I also said this “Where are you from?”

There was a pause like I had done something stupid and uncalled for. I debated whether I should have said that or not. He interrupted my self doubt with a chuckle. “Not from around here” he said in a quieter voice than usual. “I come from a place you ain’t never been” He continued. “Oh, yeah? Try me” I said sort of playfully.

He went completely serious. “The underworld,” he said. Now it was my turn to chuckle. “No, I'm serious,” he said. “Y’know those things you people call demons? I’m one of ‘em” He finished.

His voice had gone from highly-energetic to serious and dark in the matter of a minute. I was completely sure he wasn’t serious and I believed him at the same time. It was one of the weirdest feelings I have ever experienced. 

“Nah, I’m just kidding kid!” He said. His voice sounded so shrill compared to his previously darker tone. I laughed nervously. I still somehow didn’t believe him. 

That was about when I went limp and my bus driver started laughing maniacally. He rose from his seat and the bus started driving on its own. That was when my vision started flickering in and out. I lost consciousness pretty fast.

I woke up in my bed. The bed in my house, screaming bloody-murder. Both my parents came running in “What’s wrong James?” my mom asked. 

Sorry for leading you on but for the sake of clickbait I had to do this. It never really happened. It was all a dream. Sorry again.
