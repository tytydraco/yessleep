I wrote this over 20 years ago. Never expected it to be relevant again. Or for it to get scarier.


The 2nd Halloween Massacre

By the time the Navy Captain who ran the White House Situation Room finished reading
the flash traffic on her screen, she already knew that it was going to be a long time
before she saw daylight again.

She picked up the phone and started up the complex, hidden machinery of the SIT
Room. POTUS was on AF1 in transit back to Andrews, and she and her staff had a million
things to do in the 30 minutes before he arrived back at the White House. There wasn't
nearly enough time. There never was.

The researchers and graphics people were still frantically assembling a set of briefing
slides when the advance team of President's security detail came in. They moved
efficiently around the room confirming the identity of the dozen people already present,
and spoke softly into their hands. The senior military people in the room were standing 
at one end of the table clustered around the Chairman of the Joint Chief of Staff having
a low, animated discussion. The graying admiral jotted down a few notes and looked
disturbingly calm. Halfway down the table, the Deputy Director of Central Intelligence
and someone new from the FBI that he didn't know sat stonily while the National
Security Advisor finished a phone call.

As one, the Secret Service detail suddenly moved to stations around the room, which
told everyone else that it was game time. POTUS walked in.

He looked tired as he walked to the head of the table, and sat down heavily. He had
been at yet another campaign stop in Western Pennsylvania only 40 minutes earlier
passing out Halloween candy, when the world had changed again. He rubbed his face
with his hands and said, "Okay. Somebody give me an update."

The National Security Advisor began her summary:
"Mr. President, an hour ago at about 7PM Baghdad time, several barrages of what we
have tentatively identified as MLRS rockets began landing in central Baghdad. The
intended target area was clearly centered around the Coalition administrative district
known as the Green Zone. The true scale of the strike is still being assessed but, our
current estimates are of between 30 and 90 Katyusha rockets being fired. Many
contained conventional high explosives and caused considerable damage." She stopped
and took a sip of water, then looked around the room before continuing.

"It quickly became apparent that some of the missiles also contained a chemical agent
of some type. We are still trying to identify the specific agent, and the affected areas.
Many of the CBW sensors located in the area were damaged in the attack, so we are still
not exactly sure of the full scale of this attack, However," she paused, seeing the look on
the President's darkening face, "it is already clear that casualties have been very heavy.
Certainly hundreds are dead. Probably many, many more."

The President made a fist. "Where were the missiles fired from. Anybody know?" 
The Army four-star general near the end responded:

"Mr. President, by sheer luck, a unit from 82nd Artillery was testing their counter-battery
radar systems after some maintenance, and were able to get a target solution within a
few minutes. The missiles were fired from at least two locations. He nodded his head at
the SIT room director. She pressed a button on her keyboard, and prayed to god
something would happen.

Her staff had come through, and a map appeared on the wall screen.



"The launchers were located here, about 20 kilometers northwest of Baghdad, and…"

"How far is that from the Iranian border?" The President asked in a gritty voice. The
general forced himself to not look at the notes in his hand, but to maintain eye contact.
"About 120 kilometers, Mr. President.” The President gave a brief nod, and the general
continued.

“From the data we have so far on the missiles path and speed, we have tentatively
identified them as Katyusha-type 122mm multiple rocket launcher." he nodded to the
SIT room director again, and she pressed her space bar, bringing up the next slide:


"It fires a salvo of thirty rockets with conventional or chemical warheads, with a range of
roughly 20 kilometers. Originally a Russian design, it has been produced in several
countries and seen action in many conflicts, including the Iran Iraq war."

"On which side?” POTUS said softly.
"Both", the General answered. He immediately got a bad feeling in his gut. 
The feeling you get just after you say something that didn't seem significant, but suddenly is. 

"They were fired from what was probably close to their maximum range, but the relatively
small area of impacts may imply these were a fairly advanced version.

"Or just a lucky shot." All heads swiveled to the uncomfortable looking man from the
State Department down toward the end. He looked like an English teacher who had just
heard the words "stick 'em up" in a dark alley. After a few awkward beats, all eyes
returned to the President. He stood up, ran his fingers through his hair, and began
walking around the room.

"So it's definitely an attack with nerve gas?" the President asked. The Deputy DCI spoke
up. "From what we have so far..., yes sir. We had a Kiowa recon helicopter over the area
moments after the attack, and the pilot reported many individuals on the ground in what
appeared to be seizures, and a television crew filming a Halloween country music
concert apparently captured footage of large numbers of people suddenly overcome..."
He paused and took a breath. He had seen some of the footage from the concert
promoter's satellite feed, and was still shaken over the nightmarish scene. "... overcome
by what appears to be a nerve agent. The Kiowa crew were apparently also affected, and
their aircraft crashed in Sadr City shortly afterward."

Papers were shuffled, and a few of the people at the table spoke over their shoulders to
their aides. 

The President then spoke to the Army Four Star: "So most of the missiles landed
inside the Green Zone?”
"Yes, sir, Mr. President. The rest landed in residential areas nearby.”
"Dammit." The president whispered as he studied the image. "Must be a helluva lot of
civilian casualties too, right?"

"Yes sir", the DDCI said. "Anyone caught outside and downwind within a kilometer is
probably affected." Probably dead, he thought to himself. My god, they've used WMD's
on their own people in a Muslim country. That was absolutely crazy, even for Jihadis.
Even for Baathists. What the hell just happened?

"Sir", the Army four star spoke up, "our Paladin artillery unit that detected the inbound
missiles managed to get off some counter-battery fire against the missile firing
positions. They didn't have much ordnance stowed at the time, and their gear wasn't
fully calibrated, but they may have stopped another salvo of missiles. Apaches are
searching the suspected launching area now, and we should have more information
about what and who did this very shortly. The launchers must have been disguised as
civilian trucks to have been able to get so close to Bagdhad. It’s night there now, so it
may take us a few hours to locate them."

The President nodded, grateful for even a small piece of luck that night. He returned to
his seat and rubbed his eyes. The grueling campaign schedule had really worn him
down, wound him up, and pissed him off. The SIT room Director had seen POTUS here
before when the world changed, and quietly typed a message to her staff in the booth to
get POTUS some coffee.

An aide with a cell phone emerged from the shadows behind the FBI Deputy Director,
and they exchanged a few whispered words. The aide came around to the SIT Room
director and whispered in her ear.

"We have video from the attack scene. The country music concert for the troops was
being recorded for a television special, and the satellite feed is still on." 

She felt her stomach twist. God no, she thought, not in here. Not now. Not while he's like this. 

The aide passed her a scribbled note. "Here is the satellite and the channel. Get it ready to
bring up on the big screen. Bring up al Jazeera, too." She nodded unenthusiastically, and
got to work.

"Mr. President, we have some video feeds from the area." The FBI man looked a little too
pleased about it.

The President nodded and returned to his seat. "When you're ready, Captain." She
started the satellite feed, and it changed her forever.

The video was apparently shot earlier, and was being replayed by the production
company for their benefit. A large outdoor stage in a park in the early evening, cheesy
Halloween decorations and even some attempts at Halloween costumes by the audience.
She didn't really like country music much, but even she recognized the superstar who
was onstage when the first missiles hit. Several exploded off camera almost
simultaneously, flickering like lightning across the scene. Then one on hit at the edge of
the stage, with horrifying results. Chaos reigned as the hundreds of concertgoers tried to
escape or just find cover.

After an endless minute, the horrendous wave of explosions had stopped. But above the
shouting, new sounds could be heard. Loud pops that sounded for all the world like
fireworks. The flashes of light that came with the new sounds seemed to be coming from
overhead. And now the scene on the monitor, already more horrible than most of the
stunned people in the SIT room had ever seen, got worse.

The smaller people, mostly women, were affected first. They stopped and swayed
unsteadily, and began coughing. A few vomited. The soldiers among them began
screaming, because they knew what was happening, and they knew it was too late. They
all watched in silence for another five minutes as hundreds of soldiers and coalition staff
fell, convulsed and died.

"My god." someone said hoarsely. No one could move. It seemed as if they were all
suddenly underwater.

Over the next hour, more information came in and more video came down the satellite
link. Al Jazeera, CNN, BBC. They all had crews on the scene, and many died while telling
the story, unaware of the true nature of the attack until it had killed them, too. Clearly a
large number of civilians in the surrounding neighborhoods had also been consumed by
the chemical cloud. It was a horror unseen since the 1980's, when Saddam and the
Mullahs killed their millions while the world waited.

The President sat writing on a notepad. He was subdued now, almost calm. Murmured
conversations flowed around the table as the assembled group struggled to anticipate
new threats, and come up with options. The SIT room director watched and waited for
some sign as to what they would need next. The president caught her eye, and crooked
his finger at her to come to him. She stood and walked to the end of the table hoping
she didn't appear as wobbly as she felt. "Yes, sir, Mr. President?"

He looked at her and smiled. He actually smiled, which shook her deeply for some
reason. "Captain, can you put a map of the area around Iraq up on that screen, and give
me that light pen gadget so I can draw on it?

"Yes, sir, Mr. President. Two minutes." She returned to her station, got it ready in one,
then enabled the light pen. She handed it to POTUS as he walked by her on his way to
the briefing screen. He didn't need to ask for their attention. Christ himself would have
had a hard time getting noticed in that room.

The president walked up to the briefing screen, and began to study it. He stood there
with his back to the confused and angry group. Then he stepped to the screen, and drew
a few quick shapes:



He stepped to one side, and stood looking at it sideways with an odd, placid smile on his
face. A few in the room realized immediately what the President had drawn, and what he
expected to happen next. Most, still reeling from the hellish scene they had witnessed
minutes earlier, waited anxiously for their leader to explain his decisions and give them
instructions. The room felt like a clenched fist.

"Our people have been slaughtered horribly. Someone has used these horrific weapons
of mass destruction upon citizens and soldiers of the United States. That someone, is
Iran." Everyone in the room now realized that those marks on the map were places they
wouldn't want to be. The DDCI had round eyes, and he whispered to himself, "no..no..."
The president continued: "Our national doctrine for many years has been to respond to
an attack with WMD's by using weapons of equal destructiveness. This is justice. And
this we will do."

No one moved. They were all underwater again, only it seemed to be much deeper this
time.

"I am instructing the military forces to destroy the targets I have shown here as soon as
possible. I also want to destroy any other known or suspected nuclear development sites
in Iran.

"I am authorizing the immediate use of nuclear weapons."

The President looked around the room at the faces there. Some were blank with shock;
others with clenched jaws and closed eyes. Several nodded, with determined looks. He
walked back to his seat, and sat down. He used the light pen to point at the Chairman of
the Joint Chiefs. "Admiral do you have any suggestions about how this vengeance should
be carried out?"

He was an experienced submariner, this Admiral, but he sat there stunned, wondering if
he had ever been this scared before. No, he thought- he didn't think he had.

"Mr. President, sir; shouldn't we wait until we can confirm who exactly committed this
atrocity? We don't have any evidence that the Iranian government had anything to do
with it, do we?" He turned to the DDCI, who was visibly shaken and was desperately
wishing his boss wasn't in the midst of knee surgery. Anesthesia seemed like a very
attractive option at the moment. "That's, that's correct Admiral." he stammered. "We
haven’t seen anything suggesting the Iranians were planning a move like this."

The president glowered back at him. "Well now there's a shocker. You know one of these
days you people are gonna tell me about something before it happens, and I'll probably
fall out of my chair."

The professorial type from State spoke up. He began hesitant and deferential, but
quickly grew more strident. "Mr. president, this is extremely unwise. There are 6 million
people in Tehran. 6 million. A nuclear attack will kill millions of innocents and make the
US a pariah nation. Plus the entire Muslim world will rise against us and our allies. Who
knows what the Russians and Syrians will do!

The president blinked a few times, then turned to the CJCS. "Admiral, I also want to
destroy any major Iranian troop concentrations, as well. Add that to your battle plan. I
want it ready for my signature and execution within 30 minutes."

The CJCS looked and studied the president’s face, searching for any sign this nightmare
was not what it seemed. A joke maybe, or a test of some kind. But no, the president had
that look of relaxed confidence again. Expectant. Certain. Of what?

The CJCS looked at his hands on the desk. He still wore his ring, even though Mary had
been gone for three years now. His daughter understood why he still wore it. She knew
he was still married. Their granddaughter was seventeen, and looked a lot like his Mary
when he first saw her in Annapolis. A thousand years ago. He could suddenly hear his
heartbeat very clearly. He slowly stood. A Marine corporal stood by the door of the SIT
Room.

"Come here, Corporal." The young marine had already seen more than he ever expected,
was understandably confused that he would be drawn into the picture. He took a
hesitant step, then strode over to the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs and snapped a salute.

"Corporal, I want you to place me under arrest and escort me from here to an area
where I can be confined pending a court martial. Do you understand?"
"Yes, sir! uh, on what charge, sir!?"
"Refusing to obey a direct order from my commander in chief."

The room erupted in confused chatter. The president looked at the CJCS with an
expression of baffled rage. "What do you mean you're refusing to obey my order!? He
was turning red now, and coming around the table toward the CJCS. The admiral turned
away from him toward the Corporal, and held out his wrists.

"SOP, right?"

The young marine found himself in the most absurd situation of his life, but he knew just
what to do: he fell back on his training. He pulled the cuffs from his belt and quickly
snapped them on the Chairman's wrists. The admiral glanced at the raging president,
looked around once, then walked through the silence toward the exit of the SIT room,
followed by his young guard.

"Hey Marine!"
The corporal turned to the source of the voice. It was the Army Four Star.
"You better call your CO and tell him to get down here with some more people. And tell
him he's probably going to need a whole lot of handcuffs. One for everybody in this
building who wears a uniform."

The CJCS turned to his Army counterpart, and gave him a quick nod and the faintest of
winks. Then he turned and led his baffled guard out of the room.

One by one, the other military men and women in the room stood to attention, and held
out their wrists before them. The Director of the SIT Room, a pro to the last, paused to
save the file she was working on, and then stood tall and held out her wrists.

The water was very deep here.
