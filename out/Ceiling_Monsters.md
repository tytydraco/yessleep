There’s a part of my childhood I remember quite vividly, despite the rest being quite blurry and unassuming. It was something that happened during a singular night as I was trying to sleep.

It felt ethereal, like a dream I simply can’t seem to forget.

I was eight. My mom had put me to bed, following the usual routine of tucking me in tightly and kissing my forehead good night. I had grown braver, so each night I was left in darkness. It usually didn’t take long for me to fall asleep after that. After all, there was nothing to do at that hour for an eight-year-old, and I didn’t like to keep my eyes open and stare into the abyss of my ceiling. That always made me imagine scary monsters coming out of it to gobble me up, and no matter how many times my mother reassured me that something like that would never happen, my mind always defaulted to that thought in the right conditions.

That night, I couldn’t fall asleep right away. As I faded into Morpheus’ realm, amidst that pre-slumber daze, I began hearing voices. Two voices, one male and one female, having some sort of discussion in my head, or at least that’s how it felt. I didn’t really understand what it meant back then, but as I grew older and the memory stayed so intact that I could vividly recall it every single time, I realized how dangerously close I was to a terrible fate.

Or perhaps what happened after was simply a terrible coincidence. The conversation I heard was short. Had it been heard—or imagined—by a developed mind unlike my own at that time, the swiftness of the event wouldn’t have made it any less alarming.

The female spoke first, “Do not be hasty. This one’s too young.”

The male replied, “But I’m hungry, love. Y’know this craving can only be sated by thems like this one!”

“I know, but it would be a waste to eat one so young.” She spoke sweetly to the man, her voice always calm and soothing. The man, on the other hand, spoke crudely and voraciously.

“Don’t get it, just lemme at ‘im!”

“It shall not be touched. If you do, it won’t have a chance to develop and make others.”

“But I’m hungry!”

She sighed, but kept talking sweetly to him. “If this one’s here, maybe there are others. Let’s look some more.”

“But that’ll take ages! I can’t handle one more minute without my sweet ol’ nectar, love!”

“Don’t be dramatic and let’s go, before we wake it up.”

After some sort of growl from the man, their presence (or what I thought to be their presence) faded quickly. I think I fell asleep right after. Nothing happened after, my mom brushing it off as a nightmare.

The next day, my mom passed away. It was ruled as natural cause, she died in her sleep. She was my only parent, my father walked away from us before I was born. And so I was the one to find her, her expression so peaceful, so undisturbed… That I didn’t realize she was gone until way after.

I miss my mother dearly, life has been hard without her. During my developmental years, I was incredibly lonely, always seeking that warmth and comfort I could no longer have.

Even so, the memory of that night haunted me the moment my brain was able to understand it better. In my teenage years, I avoided having any sort of romantic or sexual relationship. As soon as I turned eighteen, I got a vasectomy just to be sure I wouldn’t bring any other “special” people like me into this world, just in case. After all, I figured that if push comes to shove, adoption is not a bad idea.

Now I’m in my early thirties, married to my beautiful wife and two wonderful children we adopted. My life hasn’t been easy, but I’ve made the best I can out of it. Her and I are practically joint at the hip. We both work from home, we always go out together for everything with the kids, and we have a very active sex life. I know a vasectomy isn’t a hundred percent safe, so I make sure to use protection anyway.

Last week, my wife told me she’s pregnant. I watched with hidden despair as she told me the news, a light in her eyes like I’ve never seen before.
