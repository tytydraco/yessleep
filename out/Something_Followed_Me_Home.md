The sky is a mess of mellow blue and swirls of rose gold by the time I reach the treeline. It’s pointedly quiet; the air carries an almost sacred quality. *Step in,* the trees seem to say. ***You won’t be the same person when you leave.*** And so I do, small, hesitant steps crunching in the foliage. 

I am instantly engulfed by the thick, heavy stench of the lush woods. A sense of calm seems to overtake me as I breathe in the cleaner air, a relief after a lifetime in the city. My watch reads 6.30 pm; the ticking of the seconds hand seems louder than usual, the dial almost vibrating. I chalk it up to my imagination, and simply keep walking. I am here for a reason, after all. 

I don’t think much about where my feet are directing me, focusing instead on absorbing every small detail about the woods. Although appearing healthy from a distance, the wide trees are bleached bone-white; despite being tall and overarching, they seem oddly malnourished. The spindly branches remind me of an enclosure, one which, if broken, would let loose a monstrosity. I shudder, force my imagination to settle. A slow breeze cards its coolly gentle fingers through my hair, ruffling the short strands my mother seems to dislike so much. I’d never understood how someone could hate over something so trivial.

\[*“You’re a girl, Claire. Bloody act like one. Keep this up and you’ll have everyone thinking you’re a -”*\]

Seconds trickle into minutes, and minutes blend into each other the longer I walk. The ticking of my watch seems to get louder with each step, incessant against my wristbone. 7.43 pm. I look around and realise I am thoroughly lost. Blurry, my eyes struggle to focus; through the haze, I feel no sense of panic, the odd blanket of dangerous calm suffocating me still.  Distantly, I know I should focus, that this is what they’d warned me about. But I’m struggling to remember why I came here in the first place. I want to stop, to gather myself, but my feet keep moving, dragging unnaturally against the moist forest floor. 

***You won’t be the same person when you leave.***

8.17. I feel a frigid rush of air next to my ear. I abruptly stop walking, try to spin around, but my movements are sluggish, as if drugged. My eyes focus on a dense shadow, darker than those surrounding it, slick like an oil spill. Panic curls its clawed fingers around my heart, threatening to squeeze, and the beginnings of a scream crawls up my throat, licking against my teeth. But I blink, and it’s gone, and all that’s left is the shifting shadows of the dark woods. Movements jerky, I turn my head, attempting to focus again on the deer trail I seem to be following.

The sight I am greeted with freezes me anew, ice creeping into my veins, rendering my limbs useless.

It’s a hare, common in a forest such as this, greyish-white fur stark against the monotone shadows. I am not immediately sure what startled me so violently, but the longer I stare, the more apparent it becomes. The creature doesn’t move, unfazed by the out-of-place girl in the woods; instead it simply *looks* at me, as if truly seeing me, something of human intelligence in its narrow pink eyes. Its head is tilted unnaturally, and it twitches, periodically, as if in some great deal of pain, yet unable to express it. I watch, paralyzed, as its mouth hangs open, tongue lolling, to reveal too-sharp teeth, almost fangs. I notice the dirty crimson crusted in the scruff of its neck. 

A heavy shifting snaps me out of my trance, yet I am unable to take my gaze off the animal before me, unable to turn and pinpoint the origin of the sudden noise. The hare’s ears snap upwards, and it twists itself at a vicious angle, head swivelling wildly. I can see the impression of its ribs through the sparse fur. I watch, transfixed, as it turns itself around, painfully slow, and begins to move.

My stomach drops, sickening vertigo, gaze still glued to the animal as it drags itself away, underbelly scratching against the forest floor, limbs bending at jarring angles. There is none of the usual grace you’d expect from a hare; only a writhing, jerky, agonised movement, sparking a visceral sort of horror deep in my bones. I watch as it disappears into the murky woods, distantly noting that the shifting noise had stopped. 

Slowly but surely, the panic I’d felt subsides, and my legs begin to carry me forwards once more.

***You won’t be the same person when you leave.***

  
9.23. My watch buzzes steadily against my wrist. I look up from the clockface just as my feet jerk to a stop. Reflexively, I glance down; the movement takes effort, as if I am moving through treacle. My stomach twists with the sight that meets my eyes.

It’s a crow… no, a raven - \[*“Ravens are bigger, kiddo. That’s how you can tell.”*\] - lying forlorn on the muddy earth. One sleek black wing is bent at a sharp angle; a thin bone has shredded its way through the skin. I am certain the bird must have bled, but the claret blends in perfectly with the onyx feathers, indistinguishable. One milky eye stares up at me, unseeing; the other is missing, leaving only a gaping socket. Lethally sharp, its beak glimmers even in the darkness, a knife’s edge. The stomach had been ripped open, pink insides spilling out like an offering. I squint, realise the beak is coated in viscous red, as if the bird had eviscerated itself. I am transfixed once more. 

As I stare, the bundle of guts seems to move, squirming like earthworms on a rainy day. Gasping, I stumble backwards, realising I had been standing closer to the corpse without consciously meaning to. Slowly, I inch around the disheveled bird, giving it as wide a berth as possible without colliding with a tree. Somehow, the thought of touching those skeletal behemoths fills me with impossibly more terror than the corpse in front of me.

The idea that these animals might be a warning crosses my mind, but I dismiss it. 

\[*“Stop being ridiculous, Claire. I’m tired of this. I’m tired of you.”*\]

***You won’t be the same person when you leave.***

9.54. I become aware of a thick, translucent fog, licking at my ankles. There seems to be a greenish tint to it. Strangely, I am thankful for it; it would hide any further surprises the forest floor has in stock. Distantly, I wonder what my mother would think, if she knew I was wasting my night traipsing through the woods. It isn’t hard to imagine her response. 

\[*“...useless… what’s next, you gonna watch birds? Gonna take after your dad, are you?... drugs, alcohol… waste away just like he did…”*\]

Sudden, vicious, the ticking of my watch intensifies further. It pulses against my wristbone, sharply painful. I can feel it throbbing behind my eyes. My fingers move almost of their own accord, fumbling with the clasp, ripping it off my wrist to drop it into the milky sea below. I keep on walking.

The ache in my feet is what snaps me back to reality again. I no longer know what time it is, just that I’d been moving for too long. My footsteps are echoing strangely. A cramp in my thigh causes me to pause, slow my stride, but I freeze when the echo stutters and shifts before falling back into place.

It’s not an echo. Something is tailing me. 

Pain forgotten, I run.

It’s impossibly later when I stumble into a small clearing. I double over, clutch at my stomach, try to remember why I’d been running. The wind slams into me suddenly, a wall of ice. I stumble, back hitting a tree trunk, frigid even through my shirt. I pant, breath forming clouds in the air.

It’s not so dark here. I look up, slow, creaky movements, to see the crescent moon hanging in a blanket of pitch black, no stars in sight. It seems to tremble and shiver, uncertain. Something compels me to look back down at my hands, and I am met with the sight of split fingernails, fingers slick with blood, shimmering like liquid obsidian in the moonlight. 

The haze that had gripped my entire being since I’d stepped into the woods clears abruptly, clarity crashing in, a burgeoning tide. There is no trace of the panic I’d felt just moments before. I know now that running is futile. I should simply accept the gift *it* will give me. My movements are easy, graceful, as I drop to my knees. A devout priest. A willing sacrifice. 

***You won’t be the same person when you leave.***

The monstrosity emerges from the dark woods, all unnatural twists and sharp angles, glimmering like an oil spill in the unsure moonlight as it drags closer. It is focused solely on me, an intense, sharp glare. I see something of myself reflected in those gleaming, glassy eyes. 

Relief floods me, burning like holy fire, washing me clean. I am pure again, salvaged. 

I won’t be the same person when I leave.
