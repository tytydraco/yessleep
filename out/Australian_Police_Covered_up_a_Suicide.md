**Report prepared by:** Coroner’s Office, Tasmania

**1. Brief summary of circumstances**

On 12 June 1982 Tasmania Police (TASPOL) attended a residential address in Devonport to execute a search warrant and conduct an arrest of Victor Ian Bostock.  This was approximately 12 hours after Mr. Bostock’s spouse and daughter were found deceased in the Derwent River.  Upon TASPOL arrival to the residence, Mr. Bostock was observed to be in possession of a firearm and engaged with police officers.  Mr. Bostock was pronounced deceased at the scene.

**2. Evidence considered**

The Coroner considered the following material (non-exhaustive):

* TASPOL incident report and scene photographs;
* Witness statements from attending police officers and first responders;
* Forensic pathology examination and toxicology results (post-mortem);
* Ballistics report relating to the firearm recovered at scene;
* Medical records and any available psychiatric/GP records;
* Statements from next of kin and other witnesses.

**3. Findings as to identity**

The Coroner is satisfied the person found deceased at the residence was Victor Ian Bostock, aged 48.

**4. Findings as to cause of death**

* Amelia Bostock (aged 39)**:** Cause of death; drowning.  Body recovered from Derwent River.  The circumstances leading to her presence in the river are the subject of this investigation (see below).
* Lily Bostock (aged 9): Cause of death; drowning.
* **Victor Ian Bostock:** Cause of death — gunshot wound to the torso consistent with police firearm discharge.   (Pathology report cites a single fatal bullet wound to the chest as the immediate cause of death.)

**5. Circumstances and findings of fact**

·       Evidence indicates that the bodies of Amelia and Lily were located deceased in the Derwent River prior to the fatal police encounter at the residence.

·       Forensic and scene evidence indicates that a firearm was discharged by Mr. Bostock during the attendance of Tasmania Police.  Ballistics and medical evidence support that the firearm recovered at scene was discharged.

·       Following discharge by Mr. Bostock, police returned fire.   Medical and ballistic evidence indicate that Mr. Bostock sustained a single fatal gunshot wound and was pronounced deceased at scene by paramedics.

·       At the time of the incident, there were no eyewitness accounts present contradicting the sequence above.  The Coroner notes there is insufficient admissible evidence before the Court to establish motive for the events that preceded the police attendance.

**6. Mental health and medical history**

* Medical records obtained indicate Mr. Bostock had exhibited a decline in mental health in the period preceding the deaths.  General Practitioners’ notes and statements from acquaintances describe symptoms consistent with depression and reported visual and auditory phenomena.
* The Coroner notes that Mr. Bostock had recently inherited a motor vehicle (described as a Ford XB Falcon, matte black in colour) from a distant deceased relative.  Some witnesses reported a change in behaviour following this event; however, there is no direct forensic evidence linking the vehicle with the deaths of Amelia and Lily.  Vehicle in question remains unrecovered and is assumed stolen.

**7. Conclusion (Coroner’s determination)**

* On the balance of probabilities and having considered the available evidence, the Coroner finds that Amelia and Lily were deceased prior to the fatal shooting of Mr. Bostock.  The cause of death for Mr. Bostock was a gunshot wound sustained during an exchange of fire with Tasmania Police.
* The Coroner is unable, on the evidence available, to form a definitive finding as to the motive which led to the deaths of Amelia and Lily.  The Coroner makes no determination as to criminal responsibility; that is a matter for the Director of Public Prosecutions if relevant.

**8. Recommendations / Comments**

* The Coroner recommends that Tasmania Police ensure that all available medical, psychiatric and social history be provided in similar future investigations to assist the Coroner in forming a complete picture.
* No further recommendations are made at this time beyond provision of this finding to next of kin in accordance with Coroners’ rules.

**9. Distribution**

A copy of these findings will be provided to the senior next of kin and to relevant agencies.
