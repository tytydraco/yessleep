I’m posting this from a burner phone outside a library in Cluj-Napoca. My hands are shaking so hard I can barely hit the right keys, and the screen is cracked, but I need someone—anyone—to know the truth before the 24 hours are up.

They say the streets of Romania are paved with history, but for me, they were paved with freezing slush and the smell of cheap tobacco. My name is Radu. I lost my job at the IT firm during the 2025 layoffs when the AI-restructuring hit the EU like a freight train. By the winter of 2026, I was sleeping in the shadow of St. Michael’s Church, huddled in a sleeping bag that smelled of damp wool and despair.

That’s where I met Bogdan.

It was two weeks ago. The temperature had plummeted, a lingering effect of the "Siberian Gasp" weather pattern everyone’s been talking about on the news. I was freezing to death.

A man approached me. He didn’t look like a social worker. He wore a coat of wool so thick it looked like pelt, and his eyes were the color of the Olt river after a storm—murky and deep.

"You look like a man who remembers what it’s like to have a ceiling, Radu," he said. He knew my name. I hadn't told him.

"Who are you?" I croaked.

"A curator of legacies," he replied, sitting beside me on the frozen stone. "I have a property in the Hoia Baciu. A manor owned by a family that no longer... requires it. I need a caretaker. In exchange, the deed becomes yours after seven days. You’ll be the richest man in the district. No catch, other than a single rule of hospitality."

"What rule?" I asked, my heart hammering against my ribs.

"You must never stop the clock in the foyer. It is the heartbeat of the house. Keep it wound, and the house will keep you."

I took the deal. I was a fool, but hunger makes a ghost of your common sense. He drove me to the edge of the forest. The manor was a jagged silhouette against the moon—old, timber-framed, and smelling of ancient pine and something sweet, like rotting plums.

The interior was... impossible. The heating worked, the larder was filled with venison and fine wines, and the furniture was carved from wood so dark it seemed to swallow the light. But the clock—the Ceasul Satului—was a monstrosity. It stood seven feet tall, carved with figures of screaming saints and silent wolves. Its tick wasn’t a click; it was a thud.

Thud. Thud. Thud.

On the second night, the "cost" revealed itself.
I was eating a steak that tasted like ash when I heard it: a scratching inside the walls. Not rats. Voices. 

They were whispering in an archaic dialect of Romanian I could barely understand.
"Radu... the marrow is cold. Give us the heat. Give us the time."

I looked in the mirror and screamed. My reflection was... lagging. When I moved my hand, the man in the glass waited a full second before following. My skin looked translucent, like parchment. I realized then that the "wealth" wasn't being given to me. I was being exchanged for it.

Bogdan returned this morning. He looked younger. His eyes were no longer murky; they were bright, piercing. My own eyes were turning the color of fog.

"The clock is slowing, Radu," he whispered, his voice smooth as silk. "The spirits of the land—the Moroi—they are hungry for a pulse. You have a choice. You can leave now and go back to the slush, back to the hunger, and you will die by morning in this frost. Or, you can stay. You will have the gold, the land, the power... but you will eventually become the 'Heartbeat.' You will live forever, but only as the rhythm of the house."

"I want to go," I sobbed.

"Then find a replacement," he smiled, showing teeth that were far too sharp. "That is the law of the Folk. A soul for a soul. Post your story. Find someone desperate. Someone who wants to be rich."

That brings me to now. I’m sitting here, writing this, feeling my heart slow down to match the thud of that cursed clock. I told myself I was writing this as a warning. I told myself I was a good man.

But as I type this, I realize the "wealth" is already hitting my bank account. I just checked my banking app—the balance is growing by thousands of Euros every minute. My skin is becoming solid again. I feel... powerful.

I didn't post this to warn you.

Bogdan told me that the contract is sealed by witnessing. The moment you finished reading this story—the moment you visualized the clock, the manor, and the "Ceasul Satului"—you accepted the terms of the hospitality.

Look at your hands. Are they a little paler? Is the room a little colder? Do you hear a faint thud in the distance?

I’m sorry. I really am. But the Siberian Gasp is coming, and I finally have a warm bed. Thank you for taking my place in the foyer.
