I almost didn't post this.

I've been sitting here for twenty minutes staring at the blank text box trying to figure out where to even start. So I'll just start from the beginning and hope it makes sense by the end.

A few years ago I was going through some dark corners of the internet. You know how it is, 3am, can't sleep, you just keep clicking deeper and deeper until you're somewhere you probably shouldn't be. That's where I found the story. I saved it in a folder I barely open. Told myself I'd do something with it eventually and then kind of forgot about it.

Recently I started a YouTube channel. DarkLoreASMR. It's small, embarrassingly small honestly. Horror narration, dark atmospheric stuff, that's my thing. I don't have many subscribers, barely anyone watches. I'm not saying this for sympathy, I genuinely mean it because I need you to understand, whatever started happening after has nothing to do with views or attention or any of that. This thing, whatever it is, clearly doesn't care about my subscriber count.

I remembered that old story. I made a simple video about it, nothing fancy, just narrated it the way I do, posted it, and went to sleep.

That night something woke me up. Not a dream. Not gradually. Something physically shook me awake and when I opened my eyes there was nothing there.

Then it started happening every night.

I keep hearing my grandmother's voice. She passed away years ago. But I hear her, clear as anything, calling my name from somewhere far away, like she's in another room, or outside, or somewhere I can't reach. It stops the moment I try to move toward it.

Two nights ago was the worst. I woke up at maybe 3am and there were dark figures standing around my bed. Just standing there. Whispering to each other, not to me, just among themselves like I wasn't supposed to be awake. I couldn't move. Couldn't make a sound. My whole body just refused. I don't know how long it lasted. I don't know how it ended. One second they were there and then I was just, awake, alone, heart going insane, blanket on the floor.

I know exactly what you're going to say. Sleep paralysis. And maybe. But I've never had it before in my life and it started the night I posted that video and I'm sorry, that's too clean a coincidence for me to just file away and move on.

I don't know what I woke up. I don't know if it followed the story or if it was always attached to it, waiting for someone to put it back out into the world.

I'm posting it in parts. I need people to know what's in it before anything else happens.

Part 1: The Call

I almost didn't publish it.

I still think about that. How close I came to filing it away, telling myself I didn't have enough. How easy that would have been.

I was covering local politics. Small beat, small city. New journalist, nobody important talking to me yet. I was patient. Building something.

Then a man called my office at 11 PM on a Wednesday.

He wouldn't give his name. He said he used to drive for someone. A minister. Wouldn't say which one. He said he had seen things and they were sitting inside him like something rotting and he couldn't carry it alone anymore.

I said I was listening.

He talked for forty minutes.

He said it started months before any election. The minister would get quiet. Focused in a way that had nothing to do with campaigning. Then the calls would start, always late at night, always to the same number. Then the fixer would appear.

Nobody ever introduced the fixer. He was just suddenly there, riding in the front seat, speaking in a low voice, and the driver learned quickly not to look in the mirror.

The children came after that.

Always young. Always from poor families in areas where people didn't ask too many questions. The fixer would find them first, spend weeks around the family, bringing gifts, cash, groceries, whatever they needed. By the time anyone might have thought to be suspicious, the family already trusted him. Already depended on him.

The driver said the children always looked happy when they left. That was the part he couldn't stop thinking about. How happy they looked. How they had no idea.

\---

The forest trips happened just before major elections.

Always at night. Always the same route. The minister, the fixer, two other men the driver didn't know, and the child in the back seat asking questions about where they were going and being told it was an adventure.

The road would end. Everyone would get out.

The driver was told to stay with the car. Every single time. Stay with the car, face forward, don't follow, don't come looking.

He stayed. The first time because he was told to. After that because of what came back through the trees.

He said the screaming started maybe twenty minutes in. He said it didn't sound like a child who was lost or hurt. It sounded like a child who had seen something. Something that had no business existing. He said there is a difference and once you hear it you never forget it.

He said he sat in that car with the engine off and his hands over his ears and he prayed. He hadn't prayed since he was a boy. He prayed then.

They came back without the child. Every time, without the child. He never asked. They never explained. The minister would get in the back seat and straighten his jacket and say, let's go, and the driver would drive.

Three weeks later the election results would come in.

The minister always won.

He said he left the job after the third time. That he told himself he had no choice the first two times, that he didn't know, that he couldn't have stopped it anyway. He said he ran out of ways to lie to himself after the third.

He called me four years later because the screaming still woke him up at night. Because he needed someone outside of it to know. Because he said silence felt like being one of them.

I said I understood.

I didn't, not yet.

That came later, in the forest, when I found the clearing myself.
