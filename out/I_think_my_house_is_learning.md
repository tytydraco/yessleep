I’m posting this because I need someone else to tell me I'm not losing my mind. 

Three months ago, my parents left for a week-long trip. I'm 17. I’ve stayed home alone before—no big deal. We live in a regular suburban house. two floors. finished basement. nothing creepy. no tragic backstory. No old cemetery in the backyard. just beige walls and bad wifi. 

The first night, something small happened. 

2:13 am, my bedroom door clicked shut. 

I always sleep with it open. I hate the feeling of being sealed in, but I figured the air pressure might change when the AC is turned on. I got up, opened it again, and went back to bed. 

At 2:13 am the next night, it closed again. 

That’s when I started checking the hinges. the latch. the frame. nothing wrong. I even wedged a shoe in the doorway. 

2:13 am. 

The shoe was pushed aside. 

The door closed. 

I didn’t hear footsteps. no creaking. just the quiet click of the latch settling in place. 

I opened it and stood there for a while, staring into the dark hallway. nothing moved. nothing breathed. the house was silent in that heavy pressurized way it gets at night. 

The third night, I stayed awake. 

I sat in bed, lights off, phone recording, eyes fixed on the door. 

2:13 am. 

The knob didn’t turn. 

The door didn’t move. 

Instead, my closet door creaked open. 

slowly. 

not dramatic. not slammed. just… easing open like someone careful not to be noticed. 

My closet doesn’t do that. It sticks. You have to yank it. 

I couldn’t move. I just watched the darkness inside shift slightly as the door reached its stopping point. 

nothing came out. 

nothing needed to. 

I slept with the lights on after that. 

———————————————————————————

Things escalated in small, reasonable ways. That’s what makes it worse. 

The house started adjusting itself. 

Chairs would be angled slightly differently than I left them. kitchen cabinets open an inch. The TV volume lowered by one single notch. 

It never did anything dramatic. just… corrections. 

like it was fine-tuning. 

One morning, I came downstairs to find the family photo on the wall had changed. 

same frame. same placement. 

different picture. 

It was still my family. same clothes. same backyard. 

But we weren’t smiling. 

And we were standing closer together. too close. our arms pressed tight against our sides. 

like we were making room for something just outside the frame. 

I checked my phone for the original photo. 

gone. 

deleted. 

from cloud backup too. 

———————————————————————————

By day five, I stopped sleeping. 

The house didn’t like that. 

I know how that sounds. I don’t care. 

When I stayed awake past 2:13 am, nothing happened. 

The house waited. 

But the second I drifted off— even for a minute— something changed. 

Once, I woke up downstairs on the couch with no memory of going there. 

Once I woke up in my parents' bed, lying perfectly flat on my back, hands folded over my stomach. 

I don't sleep like that. 

I never sleep like that. 

The scariest part? 

the thermostat. 

We don’t have a smart thermostat. It’s manual. You slide it. 

One afternoon, I walked past it and froze. 

It had been set to 72. 

But someone— something had scratched tiny tick marks into the plastic beside it. 

not random. 

tallies.

There were dozens. 

They stopped at 2:13. 

———————————————————————————

The last night my parents were gone, I tried something. 

I left. 

I grabbed my keys and drove to a 24-hour diner across town. I stayed there until sunrise. I kept the receipt. time-stamped. 

When I got home, the front door was locked as I had left it. 

inside the house was… different. 

not messy. not trashed. 

Just arranged wrong. 

The couch faced the staircase instead of the TV. 

The dining chairs were lined up in a single row, all facing the front door. 

And on the wall in the exact center of the living room hung a new photo. 

It was taken inside the house. 

at night. 

The camera angle was from the top of the staircase. 

looking down. 

The picture showed the living room exactly as it was now. 

Except I was in it. 

standing in the center. 

facing the staircase. 

looking up. 

The timestamp in the corner of the photo read 2:13 am. 

I was wearing the clothes I had on at the diner. 

I rechecked the receipt. 

2:07 am. paid.

2:09 am. left. 

It takes at least fifteen minutes to drive home.

In the photo, my face looked wrong. 

not distorted. not monstrous. 

just… blank.

Like I was waiting for instructions.

———————————————————————————

My parents came home the next morning. 

Everything was back to normal. 

furniture reset. The strange photo is gone. The thermostat is smooth and unmarked. 

I almost convinced myself that it had all been stress. 

until last night. 

We were eating dinner. 

My mom laughed at something my dad said. 

And I noticed something that made my stomach drop. 

Their chairs were angled slightly inward. 

just a few degrees. 

facing me.

both of them. 

perfectly symmetrical. 

Like the dining chairs last night. 

I glanced at the wall behind them. 

The family photo was back to “normal” again. 

We were smiling again. 

But this time, I looked closer.

There, just at the very edge of the frame, barely visible behind my shoulder—

It was a hand.

Resting lightly on me.

Long fingers.

Too many joints.

And in the reflection of the glass, faint but unmistakable—

The staircase.

With something standing at the top.

Watching us.

Waiting for 2:13.
