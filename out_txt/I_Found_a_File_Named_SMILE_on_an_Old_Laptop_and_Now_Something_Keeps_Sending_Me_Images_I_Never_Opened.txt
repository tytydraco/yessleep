I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding insane, but I
need to write it down while I still remember everything clearly.

About a week ago, I bought a second-hand laptop from a flea market. It
was cheap, scratched up, and honestly I just needed something for
schoolwork. The seller didn’t say much—just told me it “still had some
files inside,” but I didn’t think anything of it.

When I got home and turned it on, I noticed there were already folders
on the desktop. Most of them were empty or corrupted, just random broken
files with no meaning.

But one folder stood out.

It was named:

/SMILE/

Inside that folder was only one file. No name, no extension, just a
blank icon. I assumed it was corrupted, but I still opened it out of
curiosity.

It was an image.

A dog. Or something that looked like one.

At first it seemed normal, like a golden retriever. But the longer I
looked at it, the more wrong it felt. Its mouth was stretched into an
unnatural human-like smile. Too wide. Too symmetrical. Like it wasn’t a
mistake in the image—it was intentional.

The eyes were what disturbed me the most. They didn’t look like an
animal’s eyes. They looked aware. Focused.

Under the image was a single line of text:

I immediately closed the file. Then I deleted the folder.

At least I thought I did.

That night, when I went to sleep, I left the laptop shut and unplugged.

Around 2 or 3 AM, I woke up to the sound of my laptop turning on.

The screen was glowing.

It was sitting on my desk, open by itself.

On the desktop, there was a single file again.

smile.jpg

I froze. I didn’t touch it at first. I just watched it.

Then it opened on its own.

The same image was there again. The same distorted smiling dog. But
something was different this time. The image looked… closer. Like the
camera had zoomed in slightly overnight.

I shut the laptop and unplugged it completely. I didn’t want anything to
do with it after that.

The next day, I tried to forget about it.

But that night, my phone buzzed.

Unknown number.

One image received.

Against my better judgment, I opened it.

It was the same picture.

But now it looked like it was taken from a different angle. Like whoever
took it was standing behind me while I was looking at the laptop.

Under it was a message:

I deleted the message and turned my phone off immediately.

For a few days, nothing happened. I thought maybe it was some kind of
virus or prank file. I even considered wiping the laptop completely.

Then my friend Emre messaged me.

I hadn’t sent anything.

He sent a screenshot.

It was the image.

But this time, there was something behind it.

Something dark. Like a figure standing in the background, barely
visible.

He sent one more message after that:

Then he went offline.

His account stopped responding. A few hours later, it was gone
completely.

Deleted.

That’s when I checked the laptop again.

Even though I had left it shut, it was on.

And there was a new folder.

/faces/

Inside were multiple images now. Dozens of them.

All different rooms. Different people. Different angles.

But every single image had the same thing in common.

The smile.

That same unnatural, stretched expression.

At the very bottom of the folder was a new file.

you.jpg

I haven’t opened it.

But the weirdest part is this:

I don’t remember copying anything into that laptop.

And I don’t remember leaving it plugged in either.

But it keeps turning on at night.

And sometimes, I hear the notification sound… even when it’s completely
off.
