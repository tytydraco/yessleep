“Grizzly Bear In West Virginia?!”

The big letters in bold of the newspaper told it all. It was of a local
story, of a report that in this very town, in the forest that encircled
where we lived, that there roamed a giant grizzly bear.

I drank from my cup of coffee as I continued to read. But it was quickly
interrupted by loud banging on the front door. Knowing fully well on who
it was, I groaned as I got up to open it.

Before even opening it fully, a middle-aged woman who wore too much
makeup wasted no time in berating me.

“Do you know just how behind you are?!” She yelled.

I stared at her. “Kind of.”

She thrust her hands in, trying to get a hand on me. “I’ve given you
weeks out of the goodness in my heart, but no longer must I wait! I have
paying tenants who pay on time, and many more potential tenants who’d
love to live here!”

I looked around at the worn-down studio apartment. Even if all I had was
a sleeping bag, a singular chair and table, and a large duffel bag with
all of the equipment I need for my job, it’s not like I had room for
much else. Besides, there were always these black spots that I’d find
throughout the space. I’d try to clean them, but they never go away.

“Are you listening to me?!”

I sighed. “I am.”

The landlady was going to continue, but I stopped her by holding up the
newspaper to her.

“What’s this…” Her voice trailed off.

I moved it back down.

Her face was shocked. “Are you-”

“I am. I’ll have the money ready soon.” And with that, I closed the
door.

Hunting isn’t the first option that comes to mind when you want to get
rich. At best, it’s a weekend hobby you dabble in with your friends
after a long week typing away in that small office. But it’s my job as a
22-year-old hunting guide. Aside from my hunting gear, a sleeping bag on
the floor, a flip phone, and an old car that should have been scrapped
several years ago, I had nothing that was worth speaking about.

I sighed as I went over to my duffel bag.

But, come on, a grizzly bear? There’s no way it’s actually real…though,
I kind of need it to be. I really need the money…

If only he didn’t drag me into this.

-—–

This is strange.

You see, one of the perks to hunting, or more specifically solo hunting,
is that I rarely have to interact with people. Beside buying supplies,
there’s really no opportunities to talk to people. And I like that. I
don’t really like talking to people, or rather, having to talk to them.
And as such, I’m typically ignored or easy to miss when I go out. It’s
all fine with me anyway.

But this is just plain weird.

I stopped by the local outdoorsman store for some bear spray. Like
usual, I had planned to get in quickly, grab the product, pay for it
while looking away, and leave like nothing happened.

But as soon as I stepped in, everyone looked at me. I froze instantly,
wondering what just happened. But in just a few seconds, they then
looked away and returned to normal. The old man with his grandson were
just looking at me like I had killed someone, but now they were chatting
away about what fishing rod to buy. The man over there stared at me with
bulging eyes, but now he was holding up two pairs of boots and scanning
them carefully. The fat, bald owner who I’ve only shared a simple hello
with glared at me like I had burned down his entire store, but now he
was waving hello with a small smile.

Was it just a coincidence?

I pushed the thought away, while also pushing off a small bug that
somehow managed to fly onto my shoulder.

I had planned, but with a noticeably slower speed, I picked the bear
spray and made my way to the counter. I also noticed dark spots on the
walls. This town was dirty.

“Is what I heard true? You’re really going?”

I didn’t look up to answer. “I am.”

I heard the owner sigh while he rang up my bear spray. “I know you’re
good at it, but it’s a grizzly bear! And you’re going alone too!”

I handed over a few bills. “I’ll be fine. It’s just another hunt.” I
then turned to leave with the bag of bear spray in my hand.

Just another simple exchange.

“Just be safe!” The owner called out.

I looked back.

The owner was pleasantly surprised to see me respond. He then smiled
brightly and waved, expecting a word of thanks or such.

He was shocked to see me glare.

-—-

I parked my car in front of a worn-down shack. Cussing as this damned
car refused to let go of the key in ignition, I finally pulled it out
and exited the car, slamming the door in annoyance. I then saw that my
headlights were glowing red, so I opened it again to turn them off, but
I saw that they were off. I kicked the car angrily.

This shack belonged to Ms. Wendell, the old woman that had first
reported the bear. Since this place was out on the border of the forest,
it made sense that she’d be the first to notice such a creature roaming
around.

I was here to get her personal account. Was it truly a grizzly bear, or
was her old age acting up and making her see stuff?

I banged on the door. “It’s the hunter!” I waited for a bit, slapping my
neck as I felt another bug land on it. I should’ve sprayed my bug spray
earlier.

The door then creaked open, to reveal the small, frightened figure of
Ms. Wendell.

This was new. Even I knew that Ms. Wendell was never seen without a
smile. She was always volunteering for the community, whether it was
picking some crops, running a small family-owned business, or just
watching over a small child while their parents were out.

But here, her small eyes were beady and twitching. Her neck squirmed as
her mouth opened to let out small, strained exhales, and her body itself
trembled.

“Ms. Wendell?” I asked.

She had opened the door in such a cautious way that it made me think if
she thought I was someone else.

“Hunter?” She asked warily.

I nodded.

She then pursed her lip and opened the door fully. “Come in.”

I was a bit taken aback by her appearance, but I still went in.

The inside unsettled me even more. From the small conversations I
overheard while shopping, usually from small children that were babysat
by her, it seemed like Ms. Wendell was a very tidy person who expected
everyone else to follow her sense of cleanliness.

But this was different. It was like a…bear had run amok in here. Dirty
clothes were on the floor, on opened and closed shelves, and on flipped
tables and chairs that were in the wrong rooms. There were traces of a
liquid, water probably, all over the floor and the walls. I had to be
careful too, as I saw the broken bits of chairs and glass on the floor.

“I-I’m so-sorry for the state of my ho-home.” Ms. Wendell stuttered as
she led me to a sofa that was still intact. “Ever si-since seeing the
be-bear, it’s been hard to-to keep calm and-”

I tried to let in more light considering how dark it was, but most of
the light was blocked by more dark spots on the windows. This place
NEEDED to be cleaned soon.

“It’s fine.” I muttered while taking a seat, making sure there wasn’t
anything on it.

She also sat down. Then, she let out a long sigh. “…The bear.”

I brought out a small notepad. “You said that it was a grizzly bear,
correct?”

She didn’t answer for a bit.

I raised an eyebrow. “Ms. Wendell?”

She kept her eyes cast downward.

Just as I was about to poke her on her shoulder, she suddenly looked up
and right at me.

I noticed that she stopped trembling.

“I remember it well, the night that I had gotten up to check what this
loud sound was. It was as if something was scraping its giant claws on
my bedroom wall, trying desperately to get inside. And I was right.
Right outside my bedroom window, I saw it. The beast. A giant grizzly
bear, easily capable of breaking through and devouring me.”

I stayed quiet, shocked at the sudden change in her demeanor. It was for
a while, before I realized that she had stopped talking. I then cleared
my throat to get her attention.

Interestingly, she had begun trembling again and her eyes darted around
once more. “…So?”

What was going on?

“Uh, I-” I thought quickly. “From your testimony, it seems that this
grizzly bear is starving. The forest around here is known to only have
small prey, so it makes sense that a giant bear like this would be
desperate for anything. Bears don’t usually get so close to humans or
buildings anyway.”

She nodded, but for some reason, it felt like she had no clue what I was
talking about.

“Are you okay?” I then asked.

She flinched. “…What do you mean?”

“This change in your…being. Why were you so calm earlier? And why are
you shaking still?”

Her small eyes bore right into me. “What…change?”

I stood up, my hunter’s instinct sensing that something was wrong. “What
happened after the bear appeared?”

Her mouth quivered as she began to hyperventilate.

“Ms. Wendell?” I repeated louder.

“…I….I….It’s-”

“It’s?” I demanded.

“It’s…a…d-d-…dis-”

“Dis? What are you talking about? What happened? Why are you so scared?”
I pressed.

It was then that her eyes slowly made their way to my shoulder. Her face
became pale.

I looked at my shoulder and found another small black bug. I flicked it
away.

“Was that-” She trailed off.

“What? That bug?” I asked while still looking at my shoulder. The bug
felt hairy.

But that was the least of my concerns. When I looked back at
Ms. Wendell, she had that same eerily calm look from earlier.

I backed up.

She got up and stepped over to me.

“What…what are you-”

She walked closer.

For some reason, I was frozen.

Then, with a straight face, but with eyes that never left mine, she
leaned in and spoke three words.

“Just be safe.”

-—-

I chopped away at the tall brush with my small hacking blade, making a
flat clearing to set up camp.

I was…bothered for a bit when Ms. Wendell said those words to me, but
almost instantly, she went back into that frightened state. Then,
I…I…grabbed her. I shook her, demanding to know why she said those
words.

But she was surprised. She didn’t remember saying them.

I set up my tent, preparing for a potential long hunt for this beast.

I didn’t believe her at first, but her eyes, those eyes, they weren’t
the same as before. She truly didn’t remember it. But if that was the
case, then who was I speaking to?

After setting up camp, I put on my camo, tightly securing my hunting
belt that held ammo and other necessities. I readied my binoculars on my
neck, my night vision goggles resting on the top of my head.

The owner back then. I had thought about confronting him, to interrogate
him on what he meant by that. But his were kind. They didn’t hold
anything beneath. That’s why I only glared at him.

I picked up my Mossberg and my 12-gauge shotgun, the latter I was hoping
that I wouldn’t have to use. I also hoisted on my backpack, carrying
food, water, bear and bug spray, and other necessities.

It had to have been a coincidence. To use the same words that…he did, it
had to have been…But what about Ms. Wendell?

Using my small knife to leave behind marks on the trees, I started
hiking to spots I thought would have a great chance at getting a good
shot in on this bear.

I quickly swiped at my neck as I felt another hairy bug. I already
sprayed some bug spray. Did I need more?

I had a good mental image of this forest. I’ve been hunting for years,
after all, so I knew where you could find caves of black bears, caves
that a certain grizzly bear could have possibly taken for itself. And
after trekking for a bit, I found it. Right there, next to that small
stream, you can see it. A pretty big cave.

I took off my backpack and chose a nearby boulder to rest upon, using
the nearby foliage to hide myself. It looked like I was underneath an
apple tree. I think I remember an old storm that blew in seeds from
faraway, so this is probably a result of that. I thought about picking
one to eat, but the ones that were right above looked bulgy and
unappealing, so I decided not to.

Using my binoculars, I looked at the cave, zooming in on the inside.
Making sure that it was empty, and that there was no returning bear
anywhere, I grabbed the raw beef I had carried in my backpack and headed
over to the cave.

Dumb idea, you may think.

But if my theory was right, and this bear was starving, there’s no way
it’d pass up on this free meal.

And after I placed it right at the spot where I’d have a clear shot at
sniping it, I rushed back to my spot. It’d be a bit tricky to get a good
headshot in, due to how my spot was pretty low, but that was important
if I wanted to use the wind to mask my scent.

And after making sure I was all set, it was time. Time to wait.

This is what either makes or breaks an aspiring hunter. Many don’t take
well to the idea of having to wait potentially hours for a successful
hunt. They tend to believe that hunting is the act of going out and
shooting at the first little critter you see.

Idiots.

Analyzing the prey you’re trying to hunt, thinking of countermeasures
and traps to lure it, scoping out the area and deciding what spots it’ll
most likely be in, and camping out with a gun, ready to fire as soon as
it appears…

This is hunting.

And besides, I like the quietness of it more. That’s why I hunt solo.
Something about being here, with only the leaves falling on you, and the
sound of chirps and squeaks of small little animals around, and the
knowledge that only you’re here, and that you’re able to feel it all,
it’s a wonderful feeling.

And even if you could somehow find it boring, it’s not like you could
possibly fall asleep.

After all, your life is in your own hands here. No one’s here to wake
you up when it appears. No one can possibly warn you when it’s sneaking
up behind you. It’s just you.

How could you possibly sleep?

-—-

“And that’s the last one.” I muttered to myself.

I sighed as I got up to pick up all of my belongings.

This was the last spot I thought of. It’s been hours, and the sun’s
already going to be gone soon. It’ll be nightfall by the time I return
to camp, so I’ll have to get going.

It’s confusing. How has it possibly not appeared yet? I’ve got the bait
and the locations…Was she lying? Maybe, but there has to be something
here. Throughout all of this, in some moments, the entire forest goes
silent. That only happens when something big is nearby. But still
nothing.

As I retrace my steps with the marks on the trees, I pause as I look up
at a tree cavity. I’ve been seeing a lot of them recently, but
surprisingly, I’ve not seen any squirrels. I haven’t even heard any.
Since the forest has gone quiet numerous times, it makes sense to think
that they’ve all been hiding in there.

But why haven’t I heard any when the forest comes back to life?

That thought, along with many others of this hunt itself, continue to
creep through my mind, as I slowly make my way back to camp.

This is the bad part of hunting, when you fail after thinking you got it
all figured out. You can plan sure, but this is the wild. Everything
doesn’t always go the way you planned….

In that sense, it’s kind of similar back home.

I stop as I notice an overgrown tree root. If I wasn’t paying attention,
I could have tripped and gotten stuck underneath it…

….

Hah, stupid right? There’s no way that could possibly happen….

But if I did, I’d be stuck for a while. A very long time. Maybe forever.
Or rather, however long until it takes for me to get free. Or die.

I’d die if that happened. And no one would even know.

Does that sound fun?

-—-

I’m in my sleeping bag, staring up at the top of the tent as I listen to
the crickets singing their usual, annoying tune.

It reminds me of an orchestra, but not a happy one. It’s a sad one.
Maybe it’s because things are looking quite bleak here.

Let’s assume there’s no grizzly bear. I’m screwed then. But let’s say
there is one and I can’t find it. I’m also screwed. And let’s say, that
while I’m asleep, it makes its way here and kills me in my sleep. Again,
I’m definitely screwed.

I roll over in my bag.

What am I supposed to do here?

…

He would know. If he was here, he’d tell me.

Are you wondering who I’m talking about?…

…..

Fine. I’ll tell you.

His name was Caden. We used to be friends when I was younger. He was
actually my only friend. And…we hunted. He was the one who got me into
it. It’s not like I had any hobbies back then, and since I wanted to
spend more time with him, I went along with it. We couldn’t stay out for
too long back then, so it’s not like we did anything too crazy. It was
probably boring.

….

Hah….

Heh….hehheh…Actually,

One time, I still remember this, there was this branch that was drooped
down enough where it looked like a slide. Caden wanted to slide down it.
It obviously wouldn’t work, but I still watched.

Yeah, it didn’t work, and Caden just got his pants ripped by it.

But it was funny to see.

……

Oh, and there was that time we found a wild boar. We had to climb up a
tree to escape it. That was…scary…but actually pretty funny. Now that I
think about it, we weren’t scared. We were laughing.

Days like those were the days where we came up empty. We went out to
hunt and came back with nothing, but I think…we were still smiling. We
had…fun.

I…I…

…..

Caden-…he…asked me to go on a hunt with him one time. It was just
another normal day. It’d probably be another empty day….I hoped it was.
I still hope that it was.

But while on the way, he slipped into an overgrown tree root and got his
foot stuck inside it. The knives that we brought were too weak to cut
into it. I still tried to even without the knife. I still remember the
pain when my fingernails almost got pulled off as I dug into the roots.

But Caden stopped me. He told me to go back and ask for help. Since he
had a bottle of water, and I was a pretty fast runner, he said that it’d
be better this way.

I wasn’t sure about it. Leaving a kid like him in a giant forest like
this didn’t sound great.

But he smiled. He insisted that he’d be fine. He just said to be back
fast. He didn’t want to be alone for too long.

…

I nodded and turned to leave, but then he told me something else.

…….

“Just be safe!”

…

When I came back with help, he was gone. The roots had been pulled out,
as if someone, or something, had forcefully pulled out Caden.

We yelled out to him, hoping that maybe he had somehow gotten himself
out. Maybe he was nearby, tired and drained from it, but still fine,
nonetheless.

I believed it.

Even when hours had past, and we had decided to return home for the day,
I went to bed that night, believing that Caden was still there, just
waiting for us to find him.

…………..

It’s been 10 years.

He’s not waiting.

-—-

I woke up the next day with a strange feeling. I rubbed my eyes,
noticing a slight wet texture. Shaking it off, I recollected my
memories.

Okay, no bear. I’m still alive.

I exited the tent gingerly, expecting to see something waiting for me.
But there was nothing. Still feeling strange, I got out and was about to
ready myself for another day of hunting, but I stopped.

The tree that I had set up next to now had a tree cavity.

It wasn’t there yesterday. I was sure of it. Even the mark I had left on
it was there. And I knew for a fact that when I left it, that cavity was
not there.

Could a cavity form so quickly? There was no way…was there? Was I
somehow misremembering? That had to be, since I-

I almost vomited.

That putrid stench. It’s unmistakable. There’s a nearby carcass.

Very quickly, I equipped my guns and headed over. There was no time to
get everything. Bears are territorial, and they rarely leave behind a
carcass. It was somewhere here.

Still making sure to mark the trees, I rushed past, through the brush,
through the bushes, through the open fields, over a small river, until
finally, when the smell became almost unbearable, I-

……..

It’s a bear carcass. It’s a grizzly bear. My breaking became shaky.

There were possibly hundreds of small, black bugs burrowing into the
carcass, devouring it bit by bit. They had wings and were hairy. I
realized that they were the same bugs that had been bothering me
recently.

They’re moths.

I felt my legs buckle.

How is this possible? How is the bear…dead? This injury, it’s by
something bigger. But what could have…and these moths, they’re not the
type to-

*creak*

There’s something in the trees behind me.

I quickly turn around, my hands holding my rifle, ready to shoot at
whatever it was. But I saw nothing. I took a step back.

What was actually going on? I know I heard something. No, am I actually
going crazy? I’m seeing moths eat a carcass. It has to be me. Maybe I
ate something bad. That-

*snap*

I looked back at the branch that I had believed was holding something.

The tree cavity moved. No, it continued to move. Matter of fact, the
ones on nearby trees moved as well. They moved in such a way, such a
disturbing way, that I felt myself fall to the ground.

And very slowly, I saw it.

The tree cavities became eye like designs, designs that revealed
themselves to be embedded into brown, torn wings. And as the wings came
into view, so did the beast that they belonged to.

Hairy.

The beast was hairy. It was like a fully grown male, but he was covered
entirely in brown hair. Up, in what I assumed was his face, was a long,
elongated mouth that had sharp fanglike teeth going down on its sides.

And its eyes. They were red. Bright, bloody red. Bulgy.

It stared at me.

I had been in similar situations before. Even in my first time against a
black bear, I had been rational and calm enough to scare it off. I was
fully prepared to face off against this grizzly bear as well. I really
was.

But this, this-

I was too scared to move.

The monster hopped down, its tattered wings slowing the fall. And at
once, all of the moths eating the carcass swooped over and swarmed the
monster. But it wasn’t an aggressive one. It was as if they all were
here to worship it.

“What-what are you?!” I managed to yell.

It cocked its head. Then, it raised its right arm, revealing its human
like hands, but with sharp talons that pointed right at me.

And then, it felt like my head was being squeezed into itself. It wasn’t
necessarily a painful experience, but it was very uncomfortable. And
then it was gone, just like that.

Then I ran.

I didn’t even care about the guns I left. I didn’t care about how much I
spent on them. I just wanted to leave.

But it was fast too.

As I ran, I heard it soaring above, and the buzzing of the hundreds of
moths as they followed as well.

I still ran, briefly checking the trees to make sure I was following the
right path, but also dismayed when I realized all previous tree cavities
were now gone.

It was following me the entire time.

I jumped over fallen logs, doing my best to keep up right and not to
trip.

Its eyes, those disgusting, red eyes. They were the apples right above
me. They were the red lights I saw on my car. It was right there.

I made it back to my camp, but I didn’t even give it a second look. I
continued to run, the idea of escaping being the only thing on my mind.

Back at the store, at her house, those dark spots…

I tripped over a root, but I quickly recovered and sprinted on, cursing
under my breath as the pain in my foot made me realize it was sprained.
But that wasn’t going to make me stop.

It was in my home. It was watching me even then.

I had to run. That…mind thing, that messing with my head, it gave me
thoughts. It made me realize everything.

This being had the power to control minds via fear. As long as it knew
what you feared the most, it could use the eyes on its wings to
hypnotize you. That’s why the people at the store looked at me and acted
like nothing happened afterwards. It’s why Ms. Wendell acted like a
different person and forgot about it.

I almost cried when I saw my car in the distance.

What Ms. Wendell had tried to say, before the being had stopped her, was
this.

It’s a distraction.

The grizzly bear was just an excuse to get me in here. It was an excuse
to explain the state Ms. Wendell’s home was in. It was used to explain
her fearful state.

It was studying me that entire time.

I heard Ms. Wendell yell when I exited the brush, but I ignored her. I
rushed to the side of my door, thankful that I never lock the door
nowadays. This car was too shit for anyone to steal.

Opening it and grabbing the key tucked in the passenger seat, I started
the car.

But it failed.

I heard her scream.

I looked to see the being in the trees above, staring right at me.

I tried to start it again.

The car wheezed.

Again.

A quiet wheeze.

I slammed the wheel, starting it again.

A faint hiss.

I screamed in a fusion of frustration and fear as I tried to start it
again.

It started.

With vision that began to go blurry, I quickly went into drive and was
about to leave.

“Ju……”

It spoke in a gargled tone. It was inhuman, but it was able to be
understood.

“Ju…e….e…”

I looked in the rearview mirror.

It stayed in the tree.

“Just….”

I prepared to speed off.

“Just be safe.”

With eyes that could barely see in front of me, I sped off, leaving
behind whatever it was back there.

-—-

I’m filling up my car with gas right now. I’m far away from that town. I
drove as far as I could.

I don’t know why I decided to do this, but as I arrived here,
that…monster, it’s brain twist thing. It gave me one final thought, one
that appeared as I pulled up.

“Post this story online.”

I don’t know why it wanted me to do that, but I don’t care anymore. I’m
too tired for this. I don’t know why it did all of that. I don’t know
why it said that. My biggest fear, whatever it was, I just don’t care.
I’m done.

I’m done hunting.
