Sorry if this is the wrong place for this, I just don’t really know who
else to ask and I’ve been sitting on it for a few hours not sure if I’m
overreacting.

I took a ride home late last night. The app quoted ~40 minutes. It took
over an hour. On my end the trip is still sitting there like it’s still
happening. It doesn’t have a fare breakdown, no rate-your-driver screen,
nothing. I’ve forced-closed the app multiple times. 

For context, I live out past the edge of town, off a windy highway
through a redwood forest, and my exit is one of those that sneaks up on
you right after a curve. Miss it and there’s no shoulder to turn around
on, you have to go to the next exit and loop back, which adds a good
25–30 minutes. I always mention it up front now because I’ve had drivers
miss it before, and I get a little anxious about it every single time.

This driver mentioned early in the ride that he was deaf in one ear, I
don’t remember which side it was exactly. I was in the back on the right
passenger side, so I made a point of leaning forward when I told him
about the exit a second time, loud, like super loud. He still missed the
exit. (To be fair, I have a very soft voice so it may have been partly
my fault.)

I said “Ack, that was my exit,” probably more sharply than I meant to, I
think I was already a little on edge about the late-night drive in
general. He asked me to repeat it. I repeated it and he apologized, said
the app was rerouting. Okay, it happens, I told myself I wasn’t going to
make a big deal out of it, even though it’d truly put me a little on
edge.

Then we got back around to the correct exit and the road into my
neighborhood had police cars blocking it. Several of them were lined up
at the mouth of the road. My stomach kind of dropped. Tthat’s not a
normal thing to come home to.

The driver rolled the window down. The officer said the road was closed,
they were looking for someone in the area, and told us to stay off the
side roads. The driver didn’t catch most of that so he turned around and
asked me what was said. I told him it was closed and we needed to go
around, which, technically, was true, I just didn’t say all of it.

There is another way to my home. It’s a backroad through the woods, half
gravel, the GPS drops out on it constantly, but it connects if you know
the right ways to turn. I almost didn’t suggest it, honestly. It’s not
exactly the kind of road I love being on with a complete stranger in the
middle of the night, but it was either that or return to my pick-up
spot. So I told him there was another way and started directing him to
it.

A couple minutes onto the backroad his phone went off with an emergency
alert, played through the car speaker, low volume, road noise stepping
all over it. I caught “dark hair,” “beige sweater,” “last seen near
Ridgeview.” It might’ve said more, but I was kind of frozen for a
second.

He asked what it said. I told him it was the road closure thing again. I
don’t know why I said that instead of just telling him, I think I
freaked out a little at the circumstances and it came out before I’d
thought about it.

It repeated a few minutes later. I caught “do not approach” that time.
He asked again. I told him same alert. My hands were doing this thing
where I kept pressing them flat against my legs to keep them still.

The GPS froze twice on that road, and he kept slowing down, kept asking
if I was sure this was the right way. I kept saying yes, almost there.
We were only a few minutes away.

At some point he looked at me in the rearview a beat too long. He
could’ve been looking at the road behind us, hard to say from where I
was sitting, but it scared me. He asked, “you said this is your house?”
I said the road goes to my house. He asked if I lived alone. I said yes,
and immediately wished I hadn’t. I really hate when people ask that
question.

Then he slowed down again, said he didn’t think he could keep going, the
app wasn’t showing a road there anymore. I told him it does that out
here, and that we were super close. He said he might have to end the
ride right where we were in the middle of the woods, and my whole chest
went tight.

This is the part where I’m asking for your advice, because I don’t know
how Uber treats the audio if a ride gets flagged for an unusual route,
and I think this one’s going to get flagged.

I had a knife in my bag. It was from the house I’d just come from, I
didn’t want to leave it behind, so I grabbed it on my way out.

His eyes caught on my sweater in the mirror, just a half second, maybe
less. I don’t actually know if he saw what I thought he saw. My
sweater’s cream. Supposed to be cream. Some stains. From the back seat,
in the dark, I couldn’t tell whether he was looking at the road or my
tainted sweater. But I wasn’t going to sit there and find out which one
it was. I don’t even fully remember making that decision, my heart was
just suddenly going so fast I could feel it in my ears.

I panicked. I don’t remember deciding anything, I just remember the
knife wasn’t in my bag anymore and then it wasn’t really in my hand
anymore either, and his throat was the part I went for, mostly because
it was the part closest to me leaning forward like that, and I was
shaking so badly I don’t think I could have aimed for anything more
specific even if I’d tried.

The car kept rolling another few seconds, then drifted onto the
shoulder, into the brush a little.

His phone was still up on the dash, app still open, some kind of safety
screen on it with a little red bar moving, I’m assuming that means it
was recording audio? I genuinely don’t know how that feature works. I
tried to swipe it off, but my fingers kept sliding wrong on the screen,
and besides, they were sticky and wet.

It’s worse the second time. You’d think familiarity would dull it. It
doesn’t. If anything you notice more of it, because you’re not busy
being shocked, you’re just listening. People call it a death rattle, but
a rattle is dry, something loose shaking around in a box. This isn’t
that. This is wetter and a lot less rhythmic. Air losing a battle with
blood, over and over, in a throat that’s no longer built to let either
one through. There’s a bubbling underneath that rises and falls with no
pattern, and threaded through that a thinner clicking, like a tongue
trying to manage something it doesn’t have the coordination left for.
Every so often it would catch and go quiet a second too long, and I’d
think, there, that’s it, and then it wasn’t it, and it would start back
up again. It went on longer than I expected, which I think about every
time, and I don’t know why I keep expecting it to be shorter.

I left his phone where it was and walked the rest of the way, and I was
shaking so hard by the time I got inside that I had to sit down on my
own kitchen floor for a while before I could even think straight.

The trip is still showing as in progress on my end. It says the route
was flagged as unusual and that Uber may review safety audio if anything
gets reported. I also got charged more than the original quote because
he missed the exit and had to loop back, and he didn’t even drop me at
my actual pin.

So do I open a fare dispute now, or wait for the trip to close out on
its own first? Also, does anyone know if the safety recording thing gets
reviewed automatically, or only if the rider or driver flags it?
