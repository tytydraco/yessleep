call it was simple. Always quiet and always working.The first exo planet
in which roots took hold. It became the obvious choice that it would
become a food world. A place to fuel earth’s citizens. Full of livestock
and crops it was only sprinkled with living communities. With a human
population of only 1.3 million and a livestock count at 60 billion and
counting, its easy to say that it was quite lonely here.

Most things were automated, such as plowing fields and harvesting crops.
Even the livestock was nearly self sufficient. The massive bio domes
built here would filter the methane from the cattle into storage tanks.
The tanks powered the complexes along with solar energy. With nothing
but workers and livestock it was necessary to have so much automation.

The family’s that lived here were more engineers than they were farmers.

My alarm clock chimed for six o clock and I slapped it heavily on its
shut off button. Letting out a deep breath in stood and made my way
around the bed, stopping to look back at my wife Miriam. She was
unphased by the alarm and was still peacefully asleep. I stared as her
chest would rise and fall with each breath. The best part about living
on Kepler-B is the amount of time you get with your partner.

I made my way to the kitchen and started breakfast. Miriam loved bacon
in the mornings so I always made sure to prepare extra. Eventually the
smell must have stirred her and she appeared in the doorway. Hair messy
and eyes groggy she smiled and rubbed the sleep from her eyes. I smiled
and nodded at her breast that was almost fully exposed from her blouse.

“Little early for desert dont you think?” I said.

She let out a laugh and walked over to me while I cooked. She wrapped
her arms around me and squeezed me below my waist.

“Is there such a thing as too early?” she asked.

She then kissed down my neck and pulled me away from the cooking food.

“I’m in the mood for a sweet breakfast.”

She then turned off the stove and moved the skillet with half cooked
bacon over to a cold burner. We made love, slow and passionate, as if
she knew what events were on the horizon and this was our final chance.

After returning to a now cold breakfast, Miriam turnes on the satellite
television. This was reserved strictly for news and information rather
than television dramas and such. High powered satellites that linked all
colonized planets so that information stayed relevant to everyone.

screen was just the company icon and a voice played over top.

“Hm, what do you make of that Pete?”

“I honestly dont know, maybe its another space storm causing blackouts

The sun was hot like it always was, unlike earth Kepler had a thicker
atmosphere. It was just thick enough to trap excess heat. Because of
this the domes also had a light filtration system to prevent the
livestock from over heating. With most systems running autonomously my
job is to do rounds and make sure everything is runny smoothly.

The most common issue was drones. Old drones that just ultimately run
out of juice or no longer hold a good charge. That and methane tanks.
They needed changed daily, if they were all full and went left unchanged
then the methane would build up in the domes and eventually suffocate
the livestock.

I got on my solar bike and made my way towards the eastern domes. As I
made my way to them I found myself day dreaming about the planet itself.
How for so long humanity was bound to a single planet and in the blink
of an eye here we were. A plague that was once bound to a single rock in
the void of space now has the capability of spreading deeper into the
unknown.

I wasn’t alive when space travel first became possible. Miriam and
myself were both born here. Raised in the small communities that
outlined the dome farms and fields of crops. I still remember when I
first saw her twenty years ago. She had her face pushed against the
glass of one of the domes trying to see the cattle inside. I found it
strange how a woman as beautiful as her wouod be on an agricultural
planet, but then again she had no choice in the location of her birth.

“Hey Pete.” a voice crackled over my radio.

“Go ahead Miriam.”

Static filled the radio for a moment.

“Can you check the field by the eastern domes. My panel says we have
three drones down over there.”

“Yeah im already heading that way, which field is it?”

More static.

“Its the soy beans, should be easy to spot.”

“Alright ill take a look.”

I knew the methane tanks would be okay for another full day so I veered
off into the direction of the soy bean field.

I had a difficult time finding the drones. Typically a cloud of smoke or
ruined crops would give away their location. Instead I found them just
resting in the beans. It looked as if someone grabbed them out of the
air and just gently placed them down onto the ground. I assumed their
internal batteries just died and the fluttered to a peaceful rest in the
field.

Each drone had a wingspan of three feet making them just big enough to
be a hassle to carry. I flipped the first one belly up and popped open
the hatch to its internal battery. It looked completely fine, no scorch
marks of a burn out and no leakage of fluids. When I made my way around
to the others it was the same thing.

Just as I stood from examining the third drone I heard the hum overhead
of another drone. When I looked up it was going about its routine as
usual. Then it slowly started to dip. I watched as the life slowly left
the machine and it glided slowly to the ground just like the others must
have.

“Miriam.” I called over the radio.

“Yeah, did you find those drones?”

“Yeah and I just watched another one fall out of the sky. You got
anything on your end?”

The radio went quiet and I looked out over er the field. Far in the
distance I saw another drone lazily falling from the sky.

“Actually I have alot going on over on my end. Shit Pete maybe you were
right, maybe there is another space storm coming through.”

“What are you seeing?”

The radio then filled with static, went quiet and then hummed loudly. It
was so loud I pulled it away from my body. The static returned followed
by a warbling sound.

“Miriam?” I tried again.

Nothing just more static and that warble.

“Ah shit…” i said as I hooked the radio on my belt.

If this was a storm coming through then that means we were gonna go
dark. I made my way to the methane storage and grabbed two tanks. We
could use these if we lost electricity. The house would stink but at
least we would be able to eat.

My radio crackled again and I grabbed it waiting for Miriams voice to
come through. The crackling faded into the strange warbling noise again.
It was slightly different this time. I could hear things, like they were
in the background or something. It sounded like knuckles cracking. Small
little pops and clicks. Then silence.

How could a storm like this come through without anyone noticing? How
could several other planets go completely dark and nobody knows why. I
could feel the unease start to expand in my stomach so I made my way
back to the house.

Miriam stood outside watching off in the distance as drones continued to
fall.

“Its the strangest thing Pete.”

I unloaded the tanks and set them on the porch.

” Yeah thats why I grabbed these just incase we lose power.”

She turned to look at me for a moment then back out to the fields.

“Good thinking. What about the livestock?”

“Those should be alright if we lose power, they all have backups that
use the stored methane, and if those fail I can manually reroute the
filters.”

She turned back to me with a smile, and she reached a hand out to my
face.

“You know I love me a man that has things under control.”

I smiled and braced for a kiss that never came. Before she could there
was a sound. Thwip…thwip thwip…thwip. We paused looking into eachothers
eyes confused. It started slow then got faster and more frequent. The
sound of something in the atmosphere.

We both stepped off the porch and looked up. The sky was littered with
light speckles. Streaks tearing through the broad daylight.

“Oh wow, I didnt think that was supposed to be until later tonight.”
Miriam said.

I too found it strange that not only was the forecast wrong but that we
were seeing it during the day. That and we were hearing it.

“When’s the last time you heard a meteor shower Miriam?” I asked.

“Why are they moving like that?” she asked.

At first I wasn’t sure what she meant but as I looked closer they
weren’t just passing through the sky but they appeared to be undulating.
Moving as a whole, like a school of fish.

“Miriam, something’s not right…” I whispered.

She didn’t respond as she stared at the phenomenon. We were both
transfixed on the sight before us. The distant growl of the meteor
shower was something new to me.

Then I noticed something. One individual meteor seemed to vear off from
the rest. The reason it caught my eye was because all the other were so
tightly grouped. It was like watching a group of children move through a
museum, stopping at each exhibit together. Then as the group continued
on one child was left still standing in awe.

I noticed it start to trail downwards more and I nudged Miriam.

“You see that one?” I asked.

“Pete it looks like its heading for the northern domes.”

She was right, it was getting closer amd louder. We both watched it in
amazement as it found its destination exactly where she said. Even from
this distance we felt the ground shake and heard the domes shatter. A
cloud of dark soil exploded into the sky and sparkled with the fragments
of shattered dome panels.

“Jesus how are we gonna fix that.” I said to myself.

“let me go get dressed and I can help you herd any of the animals that
made it. We should be able to get them into the other domes.”

I looked back at her and she was already heading inside. When I turned
back I glanced up at the sky. The swarm of meteors was no longer there.
Just as quickly as they invaded our atmosphere they were gone.

“Alright lets go save those moo moo’s.” Miriam said as she walked past
me and off the porch.

She tried to get on the solar bike but just like the drones it too was
now dead.

“Looks like we are hoofin it.” I said as I reached out for her hand.

We walked to the northern dome field, it was roughly a mile away from
the house. The walk was short, too short. I wouod have liked the extra
time with my wife.

“Oh wow what luck.” Miriam said.

Then she saw the bicycle propped up beside the entrance.

“Thats where I left it! Wow I was wondering where i parked this thing.”

She ran over and wiped the layer of dust away from the seat.

“Remind me to go into town this week so I can buy us another one incase
the power ever goes out again.”

I smiled and nodded to her. We continued on to the second dome and the
front side looked fine. Then as we rounded it the back half was
completely ruined.

“Hmm that’s strange…” I mumbled.

Miriam looked at me confused.

“Where are all of the cattle?” I asked.

The thought seemed to hit her as abruptly as it did me. It was odd that
after the impact and destruction there weren’t any running about. With
no walls to hold them even a few would have wondered out. Yet as we
approached we hadn’t seen a single one.

The smell of burnt earth quickly filled my nose and the smoke burned my
lungs. Miriam coughed and pulled her shirt over her nose.

When we rounded the edge of the shattered dome we had found the cattle
we had been wondering about. They were all coralled into the section of
dome that was still standing. As if someone herded them by hand they all
stood close together away from the impact crater.

The crater itself was empty. No large asteroid, no debris. Just scorched
earth and soil strewn about.

“Must have been awfully brittle to not leave any trace dont you think?”
I asked looking back to Miriam.

Her eyes were thin as she was looking all around at the debris from the
dome. Then her eyes worked their way to the cattle. She stared at them,
studying them.

“They seem scared.”

“Well i would be too if a meteor just came through my house.” I replied.

“No… I dont know how to explain it.”

She was right. There was something off about their behavior.

“Lets go ahead and get them moved, I dont want to walk home in the
dark.” I said.

Over the next few hours we corraled the cattle in groups of five at a
time to the closest domes. Oddly enough with them being afraid of the
crater it made it much easier.

“Pete here comes the last of them!” Miriam shouted past the last group.

She followed them and as they made their way inside I noticed her
looking back at the shattered dome.

“Something on your mind?” I asked.

She stood silent for some time before saying anything back, as if she
hadn’t heard me at all.

“Pete the more I think about this the more it starts to creep me out.”

“Whys that?”

She turned and wiped sweat from her forehead.

“I dont know, I feel freaked out but I dont know why.”

I walked over and put my hand around her waist.

“Lets get home and relax. Ill try and see if I can get through to any
other farms and see if they are having the same problems.”

She smiled at me unsure, then nodded.

Just as we began to head home both of our radios started to whine to
life. Just like before they crackled and hissed, then the same noise as
before. The cracking of knuckles and… moaning.

Miriam looked at me to confirm if what she heard was real and I gave her
a short nod. We brought the radios closer to our ears to try and listen
closer. The noise started faint then suddenly what we thought was a
popping noise became a growl and then an ear piercing scream. Miriam
threw her radio to the ground and stepped back away from it.

“What the fuck what that Pete!?”

“Im not sure,” I said.

“Pete I feel like we should go check on another farm.”

She was right, I was starting to feel even more uneasy. I didnt see any
other meteors fall from the sky but that doesn’t mean it didnt happen.

The Mastersons were the closest farm to ours, they handled sheep and
alpaca.

“Lets head to the Masterson farm. Who knows maybe just we are having
these were electronic problems.”

“Pete thats nearly ten miles away.”

“So we run home and see if one if the old tractors fires up.”

She grabbed my hand and we finally made our way home.

Miriam ran inside as I went around back to see if the tractor would fire
up. To my surprise after just two attempts it turned over and was ready
to go. I pulled it around the front of the house just as Miriam came
back out.

“I made a few sandwiches for the ride, I’d say we earned them.”
