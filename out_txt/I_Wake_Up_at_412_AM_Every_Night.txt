I don’t know exactly when it started.
If you had asked me two weeks ago how I slept, I would have said “fine.”
Now I know the exact time I wake up every single night.
4:12.
Not around four.
Not between four and five.
Exactly 4:12.
I know because the first thing I do every night is open my eyes and look
at the digital clock on the dresser across from my bed.
The numbers are always the same.
4:12.
The apartment is always silent.
For about twenty seconds.
Then my refrigerator starts.
It’s old enough that you don’t hear the motor gradually come alive. It
simply begins.
A low mechanical hum.
Steady.
Predictable.
The first few nights I thought nothing of it.
Sleep is strange. People wake up all the time.
Eventually I started wondering if my brain had learned the
refrigerator’s schedule somehow.
Maybe the sound woke me before it actually happened.
Maybe I was anticipating it.
That explanation made enough sense that I stopped thinking about it.
For exactly one day.
The next night I counted.
One…
Two…
Three…
…
Nineteen.
Twenty.
The refrigerator started.
Every night after that, I counted again.
Sometimes nineteen seconds.
Sometimes twenty-two.
Never immediately.
Never longer than half a minute.
Always after I woke up.
Not before.
I bought a notebook.
Not because I thought something supernatural was happening.
Because writing things down usually makes them less mysterious.
I wrote down the number of seconds it took for the fridge to turn on
everyday.
Nothing unusual.
Just data.
Data doesn’t lie.
People do.
Memories do.
So I trusted the notebook.
After nine nights I noticed something else.
I never remembered dreaming anymore.
Not once.
It felt less like waking up…
…and more like being switched on.
My eyes would already be open before I realized I was awake.
No transition.
No confusion.
Just darkness.
The clock.
4:12.
The counting.
Then the refrigerator.
I decided to test it.
I unplugged it before going to bed.
I checked twice.
The kitchen light didn’t come on.
No power.
No compressor.
No mistake.
That night I woke at 4:12.
I counted.
Twenty-one.
I heard the refrigerator.
Exactly the same sound.
Exactly the same place.
I got out of bed immediately.
The apartment was dark.
The refrigerator was still unplugged.
The kitchen smelled faintly warm.
The compressor wasn’t running.
There wasn’t even electricity.
But I could still hear it.
The sound stopped the moment I reached the kitchen doorway.
I plugged it back in the next morning.
I didn’t tell anyone.
I thought maybe I had imagined it.
Sleep deprivation can do strange things.
At least that’s what every article online said.
So I tried another experiment.
I stayed awake.
Coffee.
Cold shower.
Every light in the apartment on.
At 4:11 I was sitting on the couch staring directly at the clock.
I remember thinking how ridiculous I felt.
4:12.
Nothing happened.
No sound.
No humming.
No strange feeling.
I laughed.
Actually laughed.
I remember saying out loud,
“Okay. I’m officially losing my mind.”
Then I woke up.
Lying in bed.
The room was dark again.
The digital clock read
4:12.
I counted.
Twenty seconds later…
the refrigerator started.
