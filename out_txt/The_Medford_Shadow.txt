I recently had a fascinating conversation with a colleague of mine, Lou,
about an intense haunting he endured. If you were to meet him, Lou is
the last person you’d expect to entertain a ghost story, let alone star
in one. But the experience he relayed to me was so mind-blowing that I
had to ask if I could formally interview him and document it. To my
surprise, he agreed.

As always, my instinct is to approach any supernatural incident through
a logical lens first. My immediate thoughts are: How can we debunk this?
Where are the common threads that suggest this was just a series of
exceptionally strange coincidences masquerading as the paranormal?

However, I have to admit that this incident is the first one I’ve
written about that carries a genuinely demonic aura. If I’m being
completely honest, sitting down to put this into words actually bothered
me a little. I typically make a point to steer clear of anything
demonic, but Lou’s experience skirts right on the borderline of what I’m
willing to delve into.

The events below took place over a six-month period in 2009. To capture
the full, visceral impact of what happened, the following report is told
directly from Lou’s perspective.

Lou’s Story

I was 24 years old and had just relocated from Arizona to Medford,
Oregon, after graduating college. I signed a six-month lease on a modest
apartment with a good friend of mine from Arizona named Jesse. I moved
in a few weeks before she did to get everything set up.

The apartment had a pretty standard layout: you’d walk straight into an
open living room, and to the right was a breakfast nook that flowed into
a U-shaped kitchen. The kitchen had subway tile walls and a cutout
window above the sink so you could look out into the living space. Down
a small hallway were the two bedrooms and a shared bathroom.

When I first arrived, the place felt fine. But over the course of those
six months, the energy shifted into something incredibly dark. I started
to notice something was off whenever I walked through the front door
after work. I’d enter the apartment and be hit with an overwhelming
sense of dread. It would make the hair on the back of my neck stand up,
though the feeling usually dissipated after a minute or two.

What followed was a slow, terrifying build-up of activity that
eventually made myself and my friend Jesse vacate the property once our
lease had ended.

Part 1: Environmental Displacement

The activity started before Jesse even arrived, though I completely
brushed it off at the time. I had moved into the building a few days
before my friend to get a head start with setting up our new home. I
woke up one morning to find a dish completely shattered on the kitchen’s
tile floor. I simply assumed I’d left it too close to the edge of the
counter and it had slipped off in the night.

But when Jesse moved in, the strange occurrences became a shared
experience. We started finding our belongings in completely illogical
places. For instance, I always hung my keys by the front door, but one
day they went missing. We eventually found them shoved underneath a
couch cushion on the opposite side of the room. We’d also find couch
pillows thrown across the living room not long after we had just tidied
up. We didn’t see the objects move, we would find them once they had
been displaced.

At first, we just blamed each other. I thought Jesse was moving my
stuff, and she thought I was messing with hers. We’d wake up in the
morning to find a kitchen cupboard or a drawer wide open. I figured she
was getting midnight snacks and forgetting to shut them; she assumed the
same of me. It wasn’t until things started to lean into the more
unexplainable that we actually sat down and talked about it. Even though
it was almost ridiculous to think at the time, we began to realise
neither of us was responsible for what was happening.

Part 2: Electronic Anomalies

The kitchen and the breakfast nook quickly became the centre point of
whatever was happening in the apartment. The energy in the home had now
ramped up to include cupboard doors banging in the middle of the night,
as well as dishes continuously ending up broken on the floor. Around
this time was when we had a very specific, recurring issue with an
electronic keyboard.

Jesse had brought a Casio keyboard with her when she moved in, and we
kept it in the breakfast nook. We would occasionally go out to the bars,
knowing for a fact that the keyboard was turned off and the apartment
was silent. When we’d return, we’d hear it playing from the hallway.
We’d walk in, and the keyboard would be on, looping a pre-set drum beat.

Things with the keyboard got really weird on one particular occasion in
the middle of the night. I woke up to a loud, solid electronic note
blaring through the apartment, just a continuous beeeeeep. I bolted out
of bed, thinking the fire alarm was going off. When I walked into the
breakfast nook, the keyboard was on, playing a single note, but no keys
were depressed. Jesse was fast asleep. Fed up, I finally pulled the plug
from the wall.

Annoyingly, this was an older Casio model, and the keyboard had a backup
battery built into it. Even when we’d unplugged it from the wall, it
continued to turn itself on until we finally removed the batteries. It’s
important to note that from what Jesse told me, the keyboard never did
this before moving into the apartment.

Part 3: Physical Manifestation

While the physical activity was mostly confined to the kitchen, Jesse
was dealing with her own torment in her bedroom. Almost from the moment
she moved in, she started having severe, recurring nightmares.

She would dream that her bedroom door was wide open, and standing in the
doorway was a shadowy figure. She described it as being blacker than the
shadows around it, roughly human-shaped, but slightly mutated or
distorted. In these dreams, the figure radiated bad intent.

Oddly enough, my bedroom was completely untouched by the activity. When
I was ten years old, a childhood friend who is Navajo made me a
dreamcatcher, and I had hung it above the inside of my bedroom door when
I moved in. My room felt oddly protected when all of this was happening.
The energy in there was completely different from the rest of the
apartment. On the nights when Jesse felt too uneasy to be alone, she
would sleep in my room. Whenever she did, the nightmares didn’t occur.

One morning around 5:30 AM, I was jolted awake by the sound of Jesse
screaming at the top of her lungs.

I rushed out of my room and found her bedroom door wide open (we had
locks on our bedroom doors, and we would frequently lock them when we
went to sleep). Jesse was in bed, absolutely consumed by fear. Every
single sheet and blanket had been stripped off her body and piled up in
the furthest corner of her bedroom. When I managed to calm her down, I
saw three distinct scratch marks running down her neck and chest, and
also a separate set down her back. They were welting up as if dragged by
three really long fingernails.

She told me she had been dreaming about the shadow figure again. Only
this time, it hadn’t stayed in the doorway. It had come into her room,
held her down, and started scratching her. She had been physically
paralyzed, completely unable to move or wake up for what felt like
hours, until she finally managed to scream.

When I stepped out of her room to get my bearings, I looked down the
hallway into the kitchen. Every single cupboard and every single drawer
was standing wide open.

Jesse packed a bag and stayed at her boyfriend’s house for the next few
days. However, his place was in the next town over, and the long commute
for work eventually forced her to come back. While she was away, I
stayed behind in the apartment alone. Unsurprisingly, whatever was
happening didn’t leave with her. The heavy banging in the night, objects
mysteriously shifting places, and the kitchen cupboards and drawers
opening on their own continued the entire time she was gone.

Part 4: Violent Escalation

Jesse coming back didn’t calm things down; and things continued
happening at a steady pace. We had lost a mounting amount of dishes to
the kitchen floor in the middle of the night, and in a desperate attempt
to try and rationalize it, I had taken our dish drying rack and shoved
it into the furthest back corner of the counter. It was now as far away
from the edge as physically possible, leaving zero chance for anything
in it to become the victim of gravity.

Wanting some kind of normalcy, we started using safety in numbers as a
coping mechanism. We began inviting friends over to the apartment,
hoping a crowded room would force whatever was happening to stop. One
evening, we had a group of about five or six friends over. We were all
casually hanging out and chatting across the kitchen cutout. My friend
Lindsay was sitting on the edge of the counter, directly opposite the
sink and the drying rack that I had shoved all the way to the back.

I was standing in the kitchen entrance when I saw a flash of movement
out of the corner of my eye. One of the drinking glasses physically
lifted out of the dish drying rack in the back corner, flew across the
width of the kitchen, and violently smashed into the subway tile right
behind Lindsay’s head. It missed her by inches.

It hit the wall with such force that it literally pulverized into tiny
shards of glass. Lindsay immediately burst into tears. The rest of us
stood there in absolute shock. Survival mode kicked in, and I ordered
everyone out of the apartment. Before I left, I spoke to the empty room,
telling whatever was there that it needed to leave and wasn’t welcome.
This was the first time I had openly spoke to whatever was in the
apartment. We all slept at a friend’s house that night.

Conclusion

For Jesse, this was the final straw. She packed a bag, went to work, and
then went to stay with her boyfriend. She never spent another night in
the apartment.

Being young, stupid, and stubborn, I stayed at the apartment alone for
another week while I packed my things. I refused to let a ghost chase me
out of my own home immediately. During that final week before our lease
came to an end, the activity continued. Another glass broke. The
cupboards would randomly slam shut in the middle of the night, and I
heard knocking coming from the bathroom during the night.

As soon as our six-month lease was up, we permanently moved out. Jesse
moved in with her boyfriend in the next town over, and I moved into a
house about half a mile away. Neither of us ever experienced anything
paranormal again.

Lou’s Thoughts

Looking back, I don’t believe the apartment was haunted before we got
there, and I don’t believe it was haunted after we left. I think
whatever happened was specifically attached to me.

Before I left Arizona, I had a massive falling out with a girl from my
college. She was a self-proclaimed “energy vampire” and medium. She was
heavily involved in things I wanted no part of, and when I called her
out on some of her behavior towards my friends, things got ugly. Before
I moved to Oregon, she explicitly told me she was going to “send a
spirit” after me to ruin my life.

At the time, I laughed it off as dramatic nonsense. But considering the
intense, localized activity we experienced, the shadow figure, and the
physical attack on my roommate and friend in the kitchen, I now have a
strong suspicion that she actually made good on her threat.

I now regularly smudge my house with sage and palo santo. I am a true
believer, and I pray I never encounter anything like that again.

Addendum: The Arizona Anomaly.

Aside from the chaos of the Medford apartment, it’s worth noting that
this wasn’t technically Lou’s first encounter with the unexplained.
However, true to form, he completely rationalized it away at the time.

This earlier event took place about three years prior, in a completely
different state, and under entirely different circumstances. I’ve
included it due to the nature of the interview, but Lou says he believes
this is completely unrelated to what happened in Medford. The below is
told again through Lou’s perspective.

Lou’s Story

At the time, I was in college and living in my childhood home in
Arizona. For context, the house was built in 1970. My family were only
the second residents, and there was absolutely no dark history attached
to the property, no tragic deaths, no creepy folklore. It was just a
normal, safe family home where I had lived my entire life without
incident.

One weekend, a best friend of mine was visiting from California. It was
around 3:00 AM, and we were both fast asleep in my room. Suddenly, we
were both jolted awake by the distinct sound of someone whispering.

The sound wasn’t coming from the hallway or outside the window. It
sounded like it was coming from above our heads in the top corner of the
bedroom. Neither of us could make out any specific words; it was just a
localized, disembodied, whispery noise.

Startled, we both turned our attention to the upper corner of the room
where the sound was coming from. Hovering right there in the darkness
was a distinct, glowing ball of light. It hung there for a moment before
simply dissipating into thin air.

Confused and feeling a little uneasy, my friend and I just looked at
each other, asked, “What the hell was that?”, and then, exhausted, went
right back to sleep.

The next morning, the rationalization kicked into high gear. We tried to
convince ourselves it was just a rogue car headlight shining through the
glass. Deep down, I knew the layout of the house and the angle of the
windows made that physically impossible. But we were young, and neither
of us wanted to entertain the idea of a ghost.

I shoved the memory into the back of my mind and wrote it off as a
shared hallucination.
