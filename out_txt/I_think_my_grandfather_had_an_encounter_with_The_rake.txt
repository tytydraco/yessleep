In 1987, I found my grandfather’s diary among his belongings. He had
died two months prior. There was a note that read: “I allow any family
member to read this diary only after my death.” I knew he didn’t like to
share much about his secrets, of which he had several, so my curiosity
was piqued, and I read it. I discovered several secrets he had never
told me while he was alive. However, the one that shook me the most, and
the one I couldn’t keep to myself forever, was an event he experienced
in 1935. Apparently, he had an encounter with some kind of malevolent
creature one fateful night. Here is what my grandfather wrote:

1935. I had just arrived home from my factory job, or rather, from my
      day of servitude, since the wages were a pittance after the 1929
      crash, but it was better than starving to death or living on the
      streets.

Anyway, none of that mattered as long as I could lie down in bed and
forget all my big problems for a while. I made myself a sandwich and
started watching TV. Since I was only a few years old, there were only
news channels or very basic ones, so I didn’t stay on for long.

Finally, bedtime arrived, and I couldn’t have been more excited. The
simple fact of being able to sleep undisturbed was enough to celebrate
silently. He put on his pajamas, turned off the lights, and closed his
eyes.

Who would have thought that this would be the beginning of a nightmare?
I was in a kind of long-lasting, peaceful calm while I slept, as if my
body were floating in the middle of the sky, a blue sky full of oxygen
without a trace of pollution, but suddenly my eyes opened, forcing me
out of my blissful place.

When I woke up, it was completely dark. That surprised me because I
always, I mean, always woke up when it was already morning or close to
it. It was hard to find my clock in the darkness, and after looking
carefully, I saw that it was 2:17 a.m.

I looked around then; it really did seem as if I were in absolute
emptiness, only able to make out the window and part of the wardrobe.
Part of the wardrobe? Yes, because one of the doors was open, not
completely, but enough to say it wasn’t the wind, because it would have
taken a human movement to leave it half open. Then I heard some noises
upstairs. It didn’t scare me, because I thought it was just a stray cat
wandering around, so I decided to close my eyes again and go back to
sleep. Nothing made a sound this time, it just woke me up suddenly as if
the early morning had passed in the blink of an eye. When I got up I
looked around, and I saw the wardrobe again, now it was closed. Strange?
Perhaps, but I think it would be valid to say that it was the wind.

I decided to get dressed and head to work. I’d eat there; the factory is
very demanding, and I wasn’t about to waste my hour of sleep just to eat
a measly piece of bread before work. I glanced out the window for a
while, just to admire the view before leaving. However, I noticed a
small mark on the window, like a scratch, but I didn’t think much of it
and went on with my life. I didn’t want to waste my time investigating
something so insignificant. After all, the scratch was barely
noticeable, and it wasn’t like it contained a threatening message.

I went to do my boring shift at the factory. Honestly, this part of my
life isn’t important, so I’ll skip it.

When I got home, the routine continued as usual: dinner, watching TV,
getting dressed, and sleeping. Nothing special, and that was fine, until
bedtime arrived.

Once again, I had to wake up at 2:17 a.m. That shouldn’t be normal
anymore, at most a strange coincidence, but when I looked around, there
was nothing unusual. However, those sounds from the ceiling started
again. I simply ignored them, thinking it was just another stray cat,
and went back to sleep, but this time not deeply, because I heard the
window open. My window opens from the bottom up, so it couldn’t have
been the wind. I was curious to see what it was, but terror wouldn’t let
me. I felt that if I did, something terrible would happen to me,
something very bad. But in my mind, I told myself that it would be worse
than doing nothing, because it could be a thief or even a murderer, and
if it was the latter, it would be a terrible idea to stay still, since
they would probably kill me. But fear was precisely what kept me from
moving.

I closed my eyes in fear. Perhaps if they thought I was asleep, the
thief wouldn’t find the need to kill me or hurt me. If it was a
murderer, well, I honestly had no idea, maybe so I wouldn’t have to see
my own blood. Then I heard breathing. I started to get so scared that I
wanted to scream as loud as possible, but I couldn’t. The fear of what
would happen if I did was so great that I could only cry while covering
myself with the blanket, and that’s how I fell asleep.

I woke up at dawn. For a split second, I forgot what had happened until
it all came flooding back. Looking around, I didn’t find anything
unusual—no furniture moved, no footprints, even the window was already
closed. But that changed when I looked at my bed and noticed scratches
on the front of it, the wooden frame that holds my mattress. I couldn’t
ignore it. I was sure it wasn’t a dream and that someone had broken into
my house. Maybe, in the best-case scenario, they left before I woke up,
but even so, I grabbed a hockey stick I kept handy and carefully checked
the whole house. I didn’t want to risk looking ridiculous in front of
the police, because aside from the scratches, there was no other
evidence that anyone had been there.

I checked every corner and every room and found nothing. After seeing
that no one was home, I started to wonder what could have happened, and
I couldn’t let it go. So I decided to use some flour and put it at every
entrance and in front of every door, at least so that whoever entered
the house would leave footprints to notify the police, if that was
indeed the case—something I was sure of at that point, because
otherwise, there was no explanation for how my window opened.

Then I got dressed and went to work. After my shift, I returned home and
saw that everything was as I had left it. I avoided stepping on the
flour I had left and went to my night shift, although deep down I didn’t
want to. I knew that being paranoid wasn’t ideal either, so I went to
bed and went to sleep.

And again at 2 a.m. And at 17 I woke up, but now I heard that noise
again. I started trembling, but I knew I had to stay quiet. I just had
to pretend to be asleep so whoever it was wouldn’t suspect anything.
Besides, the flour would leave fingerprints that could be used as
evidence.

But I didn’t foresee what would happen. This time, the intruder stood on
top of the bed. I was terrified and went into a kind of paralysis. Then
a hand pulled the blanket off me. I jumped and saw who the intruder was.
It was a gray, pale creature with white eyes and shark-like teeth. I
also saw that it had very sharp claws.

I couldn’t even scream; I just stood there trembling with fear.
Suddenly, that abominable creature put its claws on my face, and it
started making noises with its mouth: “Cho-grrgrkrkrk-che.”

I could only cry at what I saw.

“Trtrtrrrrrtrtrtrtrtrtr,” the creature said again.

I closed my eyes while listening to those sounds, until I heard a word.

-“The rake.”

I opened my eyes and saw that the creature was staring at me.

-“What?”.- I asked, scared.

-“I am…the rake,”.- the creature said with its teeth.

-“The…rake? How?”.- I asked again.

The rake just smiled.

-“You wouldn’t understand,” .-it said.

I started to cry while the rake just laughed at me, in a psychopathic
way.

-“Alright, kid, let’s not make a long story short,”.- the monster said
while slapping my face. —“From now on, I am the owner of this house, and
I will be the one to keep order from now on, understand?”

I began to feel a mixture of fear and fury.

-“No, this is my house, and I paid for it with the sweat of my brow…”
.-At that moment, the rake struck me so hard that I fell out of bed.

The rake then stood and placed its foot on my chest. The pain I felt was
so great that only the footstep of an elephant could surpass it.

-“I am the rake, a far superior being. I have lived for millennia, and I
possess absolute knowledge of the history of this universe,” .-the being
said in a voice that sounded like a demon’s.

.- “Hey, do you know what happens to those who disobey me?”

The rake then stopped stepping on me, went to a corner, and crouched
down. I noticed that its back was covered in scars. Finally, the
creature stood up, and in its hand it held a head—yes, it was the head
of a Chinese man. It was already half-rotten, and from the expression,
it was clear he had been screaming before dying.

“I decapitated him,” .-the rake said with a malicious smile. Then it
threw it toward me. I was terrified and backed away, kicking the head.

-“Now, if you don’t want to end up like that, you’ll have to do what I
say, otherwise you’ll have a very painful and horrible death,”.- it said
with a smile. I could only cry, but I knew I couldn’t do anything. I
didn’t want to be another one in that abomination’s collection of heads.

“Yes, I accept,” .-I said reluctantly.

-“Good. Now, as your first order, you’ll go to sleep while I watch you.”

-“What?”.- “I asked.

-“You see, I love watching people sleep. The sounds they make are so
endearing to me, so I beg you to go to sleep immediately. You don’t want
dark circles under your eyes the next day. Oh, and one more thing: you
will feed me every night, without fail. Not tonight, because I already
ate some homeless people before I got here, but in the next few days
I’ll tell you I’ll be starving, so I suggest you don’t forget.” .-said
the being.

-“Good,”.- I said.

-“Good boy, now go to sleep,”.- he said.

I lay down on my bed and closed my eyes. I was just trembling with the
desire to sleep, at least to escape what seemed to be a nightmare. While
I did, I could still hear the sound of the rake.”

-“Cho.”

I just closed my eyes, pretending I didn’t hear a thing.

The next day, when I woke up, the Rake was gone. For a moment, I
harbored the hope that it had all been a lucid dream, until I saw a
message on my mirror, written in the blood of what looked like a
corpse—perhaps the Chinese man’s head. It read:

“I will visit you every night.”

I couldn’t help but burst into tears. I knew I was his slave now. The
only thing I could do was pretend that none of this had happened; after
all, I just had to feed him and go to sleep to fulfill that being’s
strange fantasy. Perhaps, over time, he would get bored and leave my
house.

I went about my usual day and managed to distract myself a little with
work, but upon arriving home, I remembered what the Rake had said. I put
on my cooking attire, prepared some bread with butter, and also made one
of the best teas I had ever brewed. People always said I should have
focused more on cooking, but the crisis had ruined all of that.

When it was time to sleep, I left the Rake’s food on the nightstand and
lay down. Before I could fall asleep, a black smudge appeared in the
corner of my room, and the Rake slowly emerged from it.

“Good evening, Mr. Mike,” .-he said.

I just nodded and pointed to the plate. The Rake approached, sniffed the
food, and violently threw it at me.

“What is this filth?!”.- he screamed.

“It’s the food you asked for,”.- I replied in a pleading tone.

“And you call this food?.-” the monster roared. .-“This is just trash
that your weak species eats!”

-“I’m sorry… just tell me what you want and I’ll bring it to you.”

“Meat!”.- he shouted.

“Meat?”.- I asked.

“Yes, meat.”

“But where am I going to get meat?– I don’t have any…”

Suddenly, a sound was heard outside. The Rake went to check and saw a
homeless man scavenging through the trash before running off. He stood
still for a while, until he turned back toward me.

“Alright, kid, that’s the plan. You’re going to get your ass out on the
streets and get meat from those homeless people,”.- he said.

“But if they are poor, how…?” .-Then I understood what he meant..- “No,
I won’t do it. Don’t even dream of it.”

“You will! You will take, even if it’s just a finger from them and bring
it to me. You have until tomorrow morning; if you don’t bring a shred of
meat when you return, it will be your end!”.- he said.

I covered my face with my hands but nodded. I left the room, grabbed a
knife from the kitchen, covered myself with a blanket, and headed out.
It was freezing cold; I wished I had put on a sweater, but I decided to
abstain and look for a homeless person. I walked for about ten minutes
until I reached an alley known for them.

I saw three men eating around a campfire made of junk. I took a deep
breath, pulled out the knife, and approached. I stopped dead in my
tracks. My blanket fell, revealing my face, full of guilt and despair.
They looked at me with confusion.

“Hey, are you alright?”.- one asked.

I couldn’t take it anymore and started to cry.

“Sir, what’s wrong with you?”.- another asked.

“I’m sorry… I’m sorry to bother you, it’s just… I need meat,” .-I
replied.

“Meat?”

“Yes, I don’t have any meat at home and I really need some… please…”

I kept crying. One of them felt sorry for me and offered me a piece.

“Take it,”.- he said.

I nodded in gratitude and walked straight home. Upon arriving, I was
shaking with fear, not knowing how that monster would react seeing that
I didn’t have the will to kill anyone.

“Well, did you bring me my meat?”.- he asked from the kitchen.

“Yes,”.- I replied.

He approached and looked at the piece.

“This meat is not human,”.- he stated.

“It’s beef,”.- I said, trembling.

He lunged at it and devoured it as if there were no tomorrow. Then he
moved away.

“Hmm, quite generic,” .-he said..- “Now to bed, it’s time to sleep.”

I went to my bed and did as he asked. What was once my greatest hobby
became a routine to satisfy the fetish of that abomination. A whole week
passed: I would wake up, work, go to the market, cook, and sleep. But
after seven days, I realized my money was no longer enough. I was on a
tightrope: if I didn’t get meat tomorrow, I would die. I collapsed to
the floor and cried.

How did this happen? Why me? I realized that I just had the bad luck
that this thing chose me. I couldn’t go on like this. No more. I
couldn’t tell him anything, so I had to act. I had some money left,
enough to buy something lethal.

After work, I prepared the food, and when finished, I poured a bottle of
cyanide into it and the drink. Night came, I left the plate on the
table, but this time I didn’t go to bed. I preferred to stay awake.

The monster arrived.

“Why are you awake, kid?” .-he asked.

“I just wanted to see you,”.- I lied; I know it’s a weak tactic, but I
couldn’t think of anything better.

“I’m the one who should see you, not you,”– he said hypocritically..-
“Anyway, perhaps for tonight we can talk for a while.”

He sat down, started eating, and I stared at him, hoping the poison
would take effect. I wasn’t a hero, but my desperation, added to the
knowledge that after me he would go after others, forced me to commit
that stupidity.

“Well,” .-he said, stopping his meal..- “I’m going to be honest with
you: I truly admire how you accepted being my servant.”

I was surprised.

-“Really?”

-“Yes, no one else but you has had that will; I killed the others
instantly.”

“-Thanks,” I.- said, faking it.

He took another bite.

-“Oh, it feels so good to be almost all-powerful, but I truly detest
that I cannot enter Oceania. You see, I can teleport to any continent in
the world, but for some reason, something prohibits my entry there. It’s
as if I wasn’t a powerful being, just an outcast.”

He started shedding crocodile tears; his ego was so huge that he was
crying over that. He finished the meat.

-“It is a true shame, but the meat always suppresses that pain. Though
I’m tired of animal meat.”

-“Why?”.- I asked.

--“It’s legal, and its taste is so generic that it annoys me. I was
thinking that starting tomorrow we could go out and kill to get human
meat,”.- he said with a terrifying smile. .-“Oh yes, I’m excited to see
which people I could eat: elderly, young, children… and you will help
me.”

He drank the beverage in one gulp. I prayed to heaven that the cyanide
would work, but the being moved with more energy than ever.

-“Don’t worry, you will have your reward with the killing: the pleasure
of exterminating the weak people of this world,”.- he said.

I knew that if I didn’t end him, he would force me to become a murderer.
I grabbed the knife I had hidden as a precaution.

-“And that is how we will get all the me…”.- Before he could finish, I
tried to stab him, but he caught my hand in mid-air.

-“Were you trying to kill me?”.- he asked in a dazed tone, before
throwing me to the floor like trash.

He enlarged his claws while his teeth grew sharper. I scrambled away,
but he threw me down the stairs. We struggled; he tried to decapitate
me, but I managed to kick him and enter the kitchen, shutting the door.
The being started splintering the wood with his nails while I screamed.

He got in. He grabbed my face and sniffed me.

-“Ah, that’s why I wanted human meat… because when they are killed, they
always feel a deep fear that releases toxins that make the meat more
delicious,” .-he smiled maliciously. .-“I’m sorry our relationship has
to end this way, but you decided this. And now, unfortunately, you must
receive your punishment.”

I didn’t cry. I felt pure, blind rage. I gripped the knife tightly, and
when he raised his hand to decapitate me, I screamed and plunged it into
his right shoulder.

He stopped smiling. He stared at the knife, from which black, thick
blood was flowing. I pulled it out slowly while the Rake let out an
agonizing scream. He ran out, climbed to the second floor, and threw
himself out the window. I ran to check: I saw him open a portal in the
air and disappear.

I collapsed to the floor, unable to believe it. I didn’t sleep that
night, fearing his return, but he never came back. I am free, although I
have never been the same person since.

I never heard from that creature again, although I’ve heard news reports
of people being murdered, apparently during the early morning hours. And
now, considering what happened to that Chinese man, I suspect the real
reason I was able to survive. It wasn’t because I stabbed him with a
knife; no, it’s because, of all his victims, at least as far as I know,
I was the only one who had the courage to stand up to him. That’s what
scared him, because I didn’t decide to surrender to him, something that,
unfortunately, the other victims made—that mistake, and they are no
longer in this world.
