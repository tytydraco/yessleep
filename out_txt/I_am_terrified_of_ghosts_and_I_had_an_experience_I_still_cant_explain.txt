I am terrified of anything remotely scary, ghosts haunted places horror
movies I avoid all of it. I once watched a movie called “Lights Out” and
couldn’t sleep for 3 days straight and people told me it’s not even that
scary so yeah that’s the level we’re at. But this is something that
actually happened to me and I still think about it.

One thing life has taught me through multiple lessons, never ever under
any condition ignore your instincts, if it’s saying no just listen to
it. This is one of those lessons.

It was just another evening, I was at badminton practice and was about
to leave for home it must have been around 8:30 PM when my friend
called, said he was nearby and would pick me up. We went to our
favourite hangout spot ate and laughed like friends do, after about an
hour or so around 10 PM we left and decided to go for a ride on our old
circuit, just a route we created over time whenever we go out for a
ride.

My friend was driving everything was fine we were talking and listening
to music then came the last stretch.

It’s a beautiful road surrounded by perfectly identical and naturally
aligned trees with dense forest on both sides, that’s what it looks like
in the day at least. At night it’s completely different. When I first
went through there at night I realised something, there are actual
levels to darkness. Living in a neighbourhood you always have a street
light or two, a lit up driveway, some glow from somewhere, you never
really get the feeling of what real pitch black darkness is. That road
showed me. But it wasn’t our first time going through there it was our
usual route we knew it.

Halfway through I felt it, this constant feeling of unease, everything
in my body was telling me to just turn around and go back first time i
felt something like this. And it wasn’t just me, my friend said he was
feeling a little weird too. We shook it off and kept going.

Then I heard a sudden thud.

I thought maybe a tree branch on the road, pretty common there. I asked
my friend what that was, he paused for a second and said

“I think that was the tyre, it’s flat”

I was suddenly alert, before any creepy thoughts could enter my mind I
just said one thing, get the tools we’re changing this fast. I stepped
out turned on my phone torch and saw the rear left tyre completely
deflated. I knew it would take us hardly 10 minutes with two people but
the darkness all around was already getting to me, our phone torches
were the only source of light.

I grabbed the jack and wrench and started on the bolts, told my friend
to pull the spare out from the trunk while I set up the jack. Got down
and started adjusting it under the car.

That’s when I saw them.

Opposite side of the car, a pair of legs. Just standing there. Facing
forward. Still. Like someone was standing in the dark just watching us.

I thought it was my friend messing around so I snapped at him

“What the f*** are you doing standing there I told you to get the spare
tyre”

He replied from right beside me

“Are you stupid I’m standing right next to you, what are you talking
about”

I jumped up. My friend saw my face and instantly knew, he didn’t need me
to explain anything. But we had an unbolted tyre, half placed jack and
the spare already out, we were in no condition to just run.

Then we started hearing it.

Leaves crackling. Like something heavy moving through them slowly. I was
too scared to look or even turn around, we just scrambled back into the
car and locked every door. I closed my eyes. I was mortally terrified
and I mean that, I have never felt fear like that in my life.

For the next 10 minutes I sat there listening to things I still can’t
explain.

Banging on the roof, not aggressive, slow, like something walking over
it. Soft thuds on the glass. Bushes rustling on both sides. And then the
sound that got me the most, something dragging its feet slowly along the
road beside the car.

I finally opened my eyes.

Far ahead just at the edge of where our headlights stopped reaching,
there was a figure. Just standing there. Watching.

I told my friend just drive, we’ll see what happens. He was about to
when a truck came from the other direction and its lights lit up almost
the entire road. The driver stopped and rolled his window down, I’m sure
he saw how terrified we looked because he didn’t ask much, he just told
us it’s not safe to come here this late. He kept his lights on without
us even asking. We jumped out changed the tyre as fast as we could,
thanked him and got out of there so fast I don’t even remember the drive
home.

I still don’t know what those legs belonged to.

I still don’t know what was on that roof.

And honestly I don’t want to.
