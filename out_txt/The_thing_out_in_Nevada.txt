May 10th, 2026, happy Mother’s Day to everyone out there.

I work for Fish and Wildlife. I’m not saying which office. If this gets
removed, then I guess someone was still watching the keywords.
Everyone thinks Devils Hole is guarded because of the pupfish. That is
the official answer at least , and it is not entirely wrong. The Devils
Hole pupfish is real. It is rare enough that its entire wild population
lives in that one flooded limestone fracture in the Nevada desert. In
1952, President Truman added Devils Hole to Death Valley National
Monument by proclamation. Later, the fences went up. Then the nuclear
underground  tests. Then the Cameras followed. Motion sensors after
that. Every few years, some article comes out about the little blue fish
and everyone laughs about federal agents guarding a puddle.
I laughed too, before I got assigned there. The first thing they teach
you is that the water is deeper than it looks. The second thing they
teach you is not to stand close to it after sunset.
Nobody writes that part down though. The old files say the Shoshone knew
something was wrong with that hole long before we named it. They had
stories about the water babies, and Dzoavits, a thing that stole
children and was trapped underground. Officially, we treat that as
folklore. Unofficially, the oldest ranger notes use the same word three
times in red pencil.
Dzoavits.
I found those notes in a locked cabinet after the Beatty disappearances
started.
At first it was livestock. Goats split open near Amargosa. Dogs dragged
from yards without barking. Then an old man vanished off his porch in
Pahrump about a month ago, leaving one slipper and a wet trail across
dry concrete. The sheriff blamed coyotes, then meth, then family
problems. Anything but the thing all of us were seeing on the trail
cameras.
It came out of the water at 2:13 a.m. 2 weeks ago. Not all the way. Just
enough for a peek.
A hand first, pale and too long, gripping the limestone shelf beneath
the surface. Then another. Then a head, slick and black, with eyes that
reflected infrared like an animal’s but held still like a person’s. It
had hair hanging off it in ropes. It opened its mouth, large like a frog
and lined with teeth, the hydrograph spiked like the water itself had
panicked.
The pupfish scattered before it surfaced and that was the part that made
my supervisor go quiet.
He told me the fish were never the reason we guarded Devils Hole. They
were the alarm system. As long as they stayed alive, as long as they
bred, as long as they kept circling that shallow shelf, the thing below
stayed down.
When you look at the underground tests that happened out in the Nevada
desert back in the day, you notice something. Years of shaking. Years of
pressure waves moving through stone and water. The files never say
contamination. They say “disturbance.” They say “behavioral changes.”
They say “increased nocturnal emergence.”
4 nights ago, we lost radio contact with one of the contractors working
security at the perimeter fence and for 37 seconds our video feed was
cut. We found his flashlight still on beside the south catwalk and there
were wet footprints leading away from the platform into the desert. Bare
human footprints. They went on for almost half a mile before changing.
The toes disappeared first.
Then the heel, into what looked like talons.
By the end, the tracks looked less like prints and more like drag marks
left by something pulling itself forward. through the dirt. 3 days ago,
one of our cameras caught something new and I was able to see enough of
it before the feds got to the footage.

The thing was standing outside the fence.
Behind it, the water was perfectly still
and there were no fish visible anywhere.
