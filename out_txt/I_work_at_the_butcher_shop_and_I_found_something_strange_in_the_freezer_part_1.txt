Well, I’ve been working at a butcher shop for almost three months now.
It’s in Grayfield — a small town you guys probably never heard of. I
don’t blame you. Almost nothing from here makes the news even though a
lot of serious stuff goes down. The place is called Buckley’s Family
Barbecue. So anyway, yesterday morning I had just gotten to work, walked
into the butcher shop — it was morning — my boss, Barry, was already
there, cleaning some of the butcher knives. Barry owns the place and is
the butcher too. I’m basically his gofer. But when I got there, he
greeted me and mentioned I was about twenty minutes late. He asked if
I’d gone to bed early. I said no, and he told me to go to bed earlier
next time, then sent me to grab the task list

When I picked it up, it was the usual stuff

-   clean the bathroom
-   water the plants
-   give bone meal to the plants (he’s one of the few people I know who
    does that)
-   mop the floor

and other stuff like that — the same everyday — but there was still one
thing that was a problem for me

-   kill the roaches out back

the roaches were the problem because I have catsaridaphobia — that’s the
phobia of cockroaches. I got tense just knowing there were roaches on
the premises, so I asked Barry if I really had to deal with them since I
have catsaridaphobia. Right away he took the roaches off the list and
said he’d kill them himself later. I was pretty relieved about that, but
I knew I’d have to wait for him to actually do it to really calm down,
so I’d be on edge for the rest of my shift

I also wondered why he doesn’t just hire someone else — then the other
person could do that job

but I tried to let it go and get on with my work, so I did my tasks
until my break. I was eating the instant noodles I brought. Barry
absolutely hates it when I eat that stuff — he’s always going on about
healthy eating and all that — he always says ‘my employees don’t go
hungry.’ He’s even offered to cook for me several times

so obviously I was eating behind his back so he wouldn’t complain. But
while I was eating, Barry asked me to watch the counter because he had
some emergencies at his farm. Apparently the cattle he uses for the shop
were getting sick, which would hurt the business. Right after that, he
left immediately — just handed me the keys before he went out

I kept watching some stuff and eating for a bit before going back to
work, but then I got back to it, opened up, and started serving
customers. Buckley’s Family Barbecue doesn’t just sell raw meat — we can
also cook, fry, and prepare it for customers — and I’ll admit I burned
some of the meat. I’ve never been great at that cooking part

The problem starts now. A customer came in and ordered a cut of meat
that wasn’t in the floor freezer. Here at Buckley’s we have two freezers
— one is the regular chest freezer, and then we have the freezer room —
like a walk-in cooler. And I’m only allowed to use the chest freezer.
But the meat the customer wanted was only in the walk-in

So I tried to tell him we didn’t have it, but he insisted. He said it
was for a recipe for his daughter — she had some terminal illness, and
her last wish was to eat a dish her mom used to make. Sounded like a
scam to me, but there was a chance it was real. So I called Barry to ask
permission to go into the walk-in, and as a bonus, find out why I wasn’t
allowed in there in the first place

—“Hey boss! Glad you picked up!”

—”spit it out, Caleb, I already know something happened there”

—”sooooo Barry, can I go into the walk-in to grab some meat?”

—“No.”

‘look, Caleb, I’m renovating the place — it’s dangerous in there, you
could end up hurting yourself’

‘plus I still have to regulate it — opening it uses a ton of power! and
last I checked, I’m the one paying the bill’

—“but Barry, the customer’s getting impatient”

—“argh… fine, go ahead, grab it and get out — in and out — zip — you got
it?”

—“yeah, thanks!”

I hung up and headed to the walk-in door. It was gonna be the first time
since I started working there that I’d go inside — I’d never seen the
interior because the door doesn’t even have a window. I went in — I felt
the cold flow almost like a liquid. It was obviously freezing in there,
but like — REALLY freezing. I mean, it wasn’t my first time in a
walk-in, but definitely not my first either — so I knew the temperature
was supposed to be around -18°C or -20°C. But it was so cold that my
phone sent me a notification saying the room was approximately -33°C!
And it definitely wasn’t supposed to be that way — so Barry wasn’t lying
about the regulation. But aside from that, the place looked like any
other walk-in — didn’t seem like it was being renovated to me — but I’ve
never really messed with one so I can’t say for sure. I crouched down to
grab the meat because I hadn’t even been in there two seconds and I was
already freezing

So I grabbed the meat — but I noticed the only thing that didn’t make
sense to me. A square on the wall — maybe a little bigger than a
handspan. I brushed it off — he was doing renovations, could be part of
it. I got out and handed the meat to the customer. I kept working until
Barry got back. He was clearly exhausted — he was slightly dirty — and
he told me it’s because it’s hard trying to convince the cattle to take
care of themselves. He looked at the clock and got startled because it
was already past my shift — so he apologized for that and told me the
week was busy, then sent me home

I always walk home. I’d noticed a man lurking around the butcher shop
for a few days, but yesterday he seemed way closer. I was scared, but I
didn’t look too much

I got home — I live in a cramped shithole I call an apartment — first
thing I did was grab a tub of ice cream, sit on the couch, and watch a
little TV. But I couldn’t stop thinking about that square. But I brushed
it off again, went to sleep — but I just couldn’t stop thinking about
that freezer

This morning I got to the butcher shop like always. Barry mentioned I
showed up right on time — like always — and asked if I’d gone to bed
earlier. I said yes, and he said so I’d taken his advice. He said the
list was in the usual spot. When I picked it up, I noticed he hadn’t put
the roaches on there

So I asked if he’d already dealt with those disgusting creatures. He
said he’d gotten rid of them last night after I left, and on top of that
he’d spread roach traps around the place, and thank God I wouldn’t have
to worry about them. I thanked him, but I remembered the same question
from the day before — so I gathered my courage and asked

—”Barry… uh… why am I the only one working here?” ”I mean, why don’t you
hire at least one other employee — they could’ve handled the roaches
yesterday and I could’ve done the rest”

—”It’s simpler with just one — only one paycheck to worry about” ”plus
it avoids rivalries, office romances, or rivalry over romances”

Barry wasn’t wrong — it really is simpler — but I still had a nagging
feeling. But when I got to work and put my hand in my pocket, I noticed
something was missing — a lucky charm my grandpa gave me before he died.
Well, I don’t really know if it actually works since I didn’t have it
when I got the job — but I was sure I’d dropped it inside the walk-in
when I went in yesterday

I asked Barry if I could go in again real quick just to grab it — it’d
be fast. But he asked what it was so he could go get it himself. So I
explained that only I could touch the charm and all that — and after a
lot of insisting, he let me go in to get it. I went back in — this time
it wasn’t as cold — he’d apparently adjusted the temperature. I
immediately started searching the floor for my charm

but again I came across… the square — it was still there. I tried to
ignore it, kept searching, and found my charm — but I couldn’t let this
slide

Barry was already getting a little impatient, telling me to get out. I
made an excuse that I couldn’t find it but said I didn’t need help. Then
I got closer to the square — it wasn’t a drawing — it was a cutout in
the wall, like a door frame but the line was almost invisible. So I gave
it two little knocks — it was hollow — and besides that, the square
moved slightly, and you could hear a sound like a door on tracks

I tried pushing it since I couldn’t pull — nothing. So I decided to
slide it sideways — and yeah, it was a mini sliding door

I am CERTAIN that isn’t part of the renovation — because inside was a
metal tube — about forearm length — at the end there was something to
hold onto — and on the walls of it there were little pegs. I had to
close it right away because Barry was already threatening to dock my
pay, so I had to get out — but now it was a fact — that thing shouldn’t
be in there!

I didn’t take a photo because with the temperature in there, it could’ve
wrecked my phone — and I don’t need another expense. I got out and
showed him the charm

I didn’t make a scene. For some reason, I wanted to watch his reaction
first.

But the weird thing is — he didn’t seem to notice anything different. If
that really was part of the renovation, why hide it? And if it wasn’t,
why didn’t he react when I came out?

I don’t know what that thing is — I don’t know who made it — maybe that
creepy guy from last night has something to do with it. That’s a lot of
questions. If anyone has any ideas, help me out — I’ll be back with more
updates. part 2
