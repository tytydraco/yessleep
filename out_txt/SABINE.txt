My name is Sabine.

I was born behind towering walls that swallowed the sky.

In my world, fathers broke their daughters before strangers did.
Soldiers laughed as dogs tore men to pieces. Children learned fear
before they learned hope. We called that place Scarpia.

At sixteen, I killed a man to save my mother.

After that, the world called me a monster.

They locked me in darkness. They buried me with snakes, rats, and
ghosts. But the creatures of darkness were kinder than the humans above.

So I escaped.

I crossed the Atlantic in a rickety wooden boat, seventeen days between
life and death. The sea tried to drown me. Hunger tried to empty me from
within. The cold tried to stop my heart.

But I survived.

When I finally reached freedom, I discovered something terrifying.

The monsters weren’t confined to Scarpia.

But she was everywhere.

I stole. I lied. I forged. I killed. I became everything I hated just to
stay alive. One day, I looked in the mirror and saw my father’s shadow
staring back at me.

It scared me more than death itself.

So I ran away again.

To Paris.

To a city preparing for war.

There I met Elias.

A writer searching for a story.

He thought the war was the story.

He was wrong.

I was the one who was wrong.

A girl carrying a sea of ​​scars under a stolen name.

For the first time, someone looked at me and saw more than just a
survivor. They saw a human being.

They taught me that kindness exists.

That love exists.

And that maybe I deserved both.

Then the world went up in flames.

A ship sank.

People burned.

And when death finally stood before me, giving me a choice between love
and survival…

I chose survival.

I left the only person who ever truly loved me behind. I pushed him into
the raging sea.

Some call it selfishness.

Others call it cowardice.

Perhaps they’re right.

But if you spend your whole life fighting for survival, then survival
ceases to be a choice.

It becomes a religion.

My name is Sabine.

And this isn’t a story of heroism.

After a while, I think I lost my mind.

I went back to the ocean, wanting to reclaim Elias.

The police met me and imprisoned me for my rebellion, but I found
Elias’s notebook, the one he wrote about me.

He gave me a lesson in love, taught me how to love.

And I taught him how to betray him.

I’ll never forget the widening of his eyes when I pushed him into the
ocean.

His whispered, those inaudible words:

“Sabine…why?”

After a while in prison, and after Scarpia searched for me for a long
time, I was returned to Scarpia.

To exile me to the place where I was when I killed the soldier.

This time, I wasn’t afraid.

The venomous desert snakes had injected their poison into every part of
my body.

I’m dying.

And you’re reading this story.

Don’t defend me.

Just read.

You’re the monster, not me.
