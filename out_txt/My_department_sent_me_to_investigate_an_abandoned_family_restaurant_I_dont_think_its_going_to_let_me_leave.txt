I don’t know why Reddit is the only thing loading on my phone. My radio
died the second I walked into this building, my phone insists I have no
signal, and every attempt to call dispatch fails. Somehow this site
loaded after I spent several minutes trying. Maybe it’s a glitch. Maybe
text needs less of a connection than everything else. I don’t know, and
at this point I don’t care. I don’t know if this reaches anyone…

My name isn’t important right now. My body camera, badge number and the
case file will identify me if they’re ever recovered. I’m currently
barricaded inside what used to be the security office of an abandoned
family pizza restaurant. Something is moving through the halls outside.
I don’t know exactly what it is, but I know what it looks like, and I
know it should not be capable of moving.

The restaurant closed almost 8 years ago after children disappeared over
the course of a decade. Despite repeated searches, investigators never
found enough evidence to explain what happened. Last week our department
received anonymous blueprints showing an underground maintenance area
that had never appeared on the official plans. Since I work missing
persons, I was assigned to verify whether the hidden section actually
existed.

The building looked abandoned in the ordinary sense. The parking lot was
cracked, weeds pushed through the asphalt, windows were shattered and
the faded mascot on the sign had almost completely lost its paint.
Dispatch joked over the radio not to let the old costumes scare me. We
laughed. I wish that conversation had lasted a little longer.

Before entering I completed a routine radio check. Crystal clear. Then I
stepped through the front doors and the signal dissolved into static. My
phone behaved the same way. Full service outside. Absolutely nothing
inside. I chalked it up to reinforced concrete and old steel.

The first hour was uneventful. I photographed the dining room, stage,
arcade, kitchen and offices. Dust coated everything. Mold had claimed
the wallpaper. Birthday banners still hung from the ceiling, faded
enough that I couldn’t read what they once said. Every room smelled of
mildew, stale grease and damp carpet. Underneath all of it lingered a
metallic smell that grew stronger toward the back of the building.

The first thing wasn’t visual. It was a slow metallic groan, as if an
overloaded chain had shifted somewhere. Following it brought me to the
employee hallway where every room proved empty except for the restroom.

I almost overheard the second sound because it was so faint, just
overlapping whispers. I searched for vents, damaged plumbing and hidden
speakers before realizing the sound was strongest behind the last
restroom stall. I pressed my ear against the cold tile. Dust drifted
from the ceiling as a seam slowly appeared in the tiled wall. An entire
section slid inward, revealing a hallway leading into darkness. The
whispering ceased the exact moment the passage opened, leaving behind a
silence that felt almost oppressive.

The air felt colder and carried the smell of old machine oil. My
flashlight illuminated concrete walls, workbenches, shelves stacked with
replacement parts and a maintenance bay far larger than the blueprints
suggested. Heavy chains hung from rails fixed to the ceiling. At first I
thought they supported machinery waiting to be repaired. Then the beam
settled on a cracked mascot face, followed by another, and another. 7
animatronics each 2 meters in size hung from reinforced hooks fixed to
brackets on the wall.

Years of neglect had stripped away everything cheerful about them. Their
painted shells had cracked, fabric had rotted away in strips and rust
stained almost every exposed joint. Black grease had dried along their
torsos like old blood. Every head hung forward. Every eye socket
appeared empty. I convinced myself I was simply looking at machines that
had been abandoned when the restaurant closed…

I approached the nearest mascot to document its condition. Its face was
split down the middle, exposing tangled wiring inside the head. As I
leaned closer, my flashlight caught two pale circles far back within the
darkness where nothing should have been. The mascot’s fingers flexed
with quiet metallic clicks. Its head rose slowly until the cracked grin
faced me. The chain above groaned as the full weight shifted. The hook
bent but didn’t snap. Instead, the machine carefully lowered itself onto
the floor. The remaining mascots lifted their heads. Every one of them
was staring at me.

I ran… Survival took over. I could hear heavy footsteps, slow,
accompanied by the scrape of metal against concrete. Somehow it always
sounded closer than it should have been. I reached the old security
office, shoved a filing cabinet against the door and locked myself
inside.

That’s where I am now. My radio is still dead, my phone still says
there’s no service but somehow Reddit works well enough to let me type
this. I haven’t heard footsteps for several minutes, which scares me
more than when I could hear them. I’m going to look through the narrow
window in the door once I finish this paragraph…
