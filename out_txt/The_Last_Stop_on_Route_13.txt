Nobody in Briar County liked driving at night.

The roads were too narrow, the forests too thick, and the fog rolled in
fast enough to swallow entire streets. But that was not what people
feared most.

People feared Route 13.

Officially, the road did not exist.

There were no signs for it on modern maps. GPS systems avoided the area
completely. If you tried searching for Route 13 online, you would only
find old forum posts, blurry photographs, and arguments between people
claiming the road was either an urban legend or some kind of prank.

Still, everyone in Briar County knew the stories.

A school bus appearing in the fog.

Missing teenagers.

Headlights outside bedroom windows after midnight.

And the rule everyone whispered about but never explained.

If the bus stops for you…

Do not get on.

Most people laughed at the stories.

Until Caleb Morrow disappeared.

1

Caleb was seventeen years old and impossible to scare.

At least, that was what everyone at Briar Ridge High believed.

He was the kind of person who climbed abandoned buildings for fun,
explored storm drains at night, and uploaded videos online daring his
friends to follow him into places they avoided.

His account had thousands of followers because he always acted fearless.

The problem was that Caleb believed his own reputation.

That was why he agreed to go searching for Route 13.

It started after football practice one rainy Thursday night in October.

The storm had knocked out power across parts of town, and most students
hurried home before the weather got worse. Caleb stayed behind with his
friends Marcus and Eli in the locker room.

Marcus was scrolling through his phone when he suddenly stopped.

“You guys remember the Route 13 thing?” he asked.

Eli groaned immediately.

“Don’t start with that.”

“No, seriously,” Marcus said. “Somebody posted another photo.”

He turned his phone around.

The image showed a yellow school bus sitting in thick fog near a forest
road. The picture quality was terrible, but the bus number was visible.

13. 

Caleb laughed.

“That’s fake.”

“Probably,” Marcus admitted. “But look at the comments.”

Hundreds of people were arguing beneath the photo.

Some claimed they had seen the bus themselves.

Others swore relatives had disappeared after mentioning Route 13.

One comment simply read:

> If you hear the brakes hiss outside your house at night, lock the
windows.

Eli shoved the phone away.

“My cousin used to talk about this stuff,” he muttered. “Said a kid
vanished back in the nineties after seeing the bus.”

Caleb grinned.

“Then let’s go find it.”

Marcus blinked.

“You’re joking.”

“Nope.”

Outside, thunder rattled the school windows.

Eli shook his head immediately.

“Absolutely not.”

But Caleb already had his car keys in his hand.

Twenty minutes later, the three of them were driving through the
outskirts of Briar County while rain hammered against the windshield.

2

The deeper they drove into the woods, the worse the storm became.

Trees bent violently in the wind.

Fog crawled across the road.

Marcus checked his phone again.

“No signal.”

“Mine too,” Eli said.

Caleb only smiled.

“That means we’re close.”

“There’s no ‘close,’” Eli snapped. “The road isn’t real.”

Then the GPS screen went black.

The car fell silent.

Even Caleb looked uneasy for the first time.

Outside the windows, the forest seemed wrong somehow.

The trees were too close together.

The fog too thick.

And the road ahead stretched unnaturally straight through the darkness.

Marcus swallowed.

“Guys…”

A sign emerged from the fog.

ROUTE 13

The lettering looked old and rusted.

Rainwater dripped down the metal.

Eli immediately shouted.

“Turn around.”

But Caleb drove forward.

The road beyond the sign was cracked and narrow, surrounded by endless
trees.

There were no houses.

No lights.

No sounds except rain hitting the roof of the car.

Then Marcus noticed something ahead.

Headlights.

A vehicle sat motionless in the fog.

As they got closer, the shape became clear.

A yellow school bus.

Number 13.

The bus engine was not running.

Its lights were dark.

Yet somehow it looked like it had been waiting for them.

The doors slowly folded open.

Nobody moved.

Inside the bus, the lights flickered weakly.

Rows of empty seats stretched into darkness.

Caleb nervously laughed.

“Okay… that’s creepy.”

Eli grabbed his arm.

“We’re leaving. Right now.”

Then something stepped into the doorway.

It was shaped like a person.

Too tall.

Too thin.

Its arms hung below its knees.

And where its face should have been…

There was only darkness.

Marcus screamed.

Caleb slammed the car into reverse.

The tires spun violently in mud before the car shot backward down the
road.

Behind them, the bus headlights suddenly turned on.

3

The chase lasted less than a minute.

But years later, Marcus would still have nightmares about it.

The bus moved impossibly fast.

Its headlights flooded the road behind them.

The engine roared like metal screaming.

Caleb drove wildly through the storm while Eli shouted directions that
made no sense.

“Left! LEFT!”

“There is no left!” Caleb yelled.

The road stretched endlessly ahead.

No turns.

No intersections.

Only fog.

The bus kept getting closer.

Marcus looked back one final time.

Figures were sitting inside the bus now.

Dozens of them.

Perfectly still.

Watching.

Then the bus horn blared.

The sound was wrong.

Not loud.

Not mechanical.

It sounded almost human.

Caleb lost control of the car.

The vehicle skidded sideways across wet pavement before crashing into a
ditch.

Everything went black.

## 4

Marcus woke first.

Rain poured through the shattered windshield.

His head throbbed painfully.

“Caleb?”

No answer.

Eli groaned from the back seat.

But Caleb was gone.

The driver’s side door hung open.

Footprints led toward the road.

Marcus climbed out shakily.

The fog had become impossibly thick.

The world beyond a few feet barely existed.

Then he heard it.

The hiss of bus brakes.

Somewhere nearby.

Marcus grabbed Eli and dragged him toward the road.

A yellow shape sat silently in the fog.

The bus.

Its doors were open again.

And Caleb stood inside.

He faced forward without moving.

Marcus shouted his name.

No response.

Then Caleb slowly turned his head.

His expression was empty.

Not scared.

Not angry.

Just blank.

Like he did not recognize them anymore.

The doors closed.

The bus disappeared into the fog.

Caleb Morrow was never seen again.

5

Police searched the woods for weeks.

Search dogs lost the trail near the ditch.

No evidence was found.

No footprints.

No body.

Nothing.

The official report claimed Caleb likely wandered into the forest after
the crash and died from exposure.

But people in Briar County knew better.

Marcus and Eli tried telling the truth.

Nobody believed them.

Adults blamed trauma.

Teachers avoided discussing it.

Students whispered anyway.

Then the videos appeared online.

Anonymous accounts began uploading short clips filmed from inside a
school bus.

Fogged windows.

Dark aisles.

Silent passengers.

The uploads lasted only minutes before disappearing.

But people screen-recorded them.

One clip showed the reflection of a teenage boy sitting near the back.

Caleb.

His face looked pale.

His eyes looked hollow.

And scratched into the window beside him were the words:

NEXT STOP.

6

Three months later, another student disappeared.

Her name was Lily Harper.

Unlike Caleb, Lily had never gone searching for Route 13.

She was quiet, smart, and spent most of her time working at the town
library after school.

The only strange thing about her disappearance was the phone call.

At 12:13 AM, Lily called her older brother Nathan.

The call lasted only eighteen seconds.

At first all he could hear was static.

Then Lily whispered:

“They won’t let me off the bus.”

Nathan asked where she was.

Lily began crying.

“There are people on here that aren’t alive.”

Then came the sound of brakes hissing.

The line disconnected.

Police traced the call.

The signal came from an empty stretch of forest thirty miles outside
Briar County.

There were no roads nearby.

7

By winter, Route 13 had become an obsession online.

Forums filled with theories.

Some people believed the bus was haunted.

Others thought it was part of a government experiment.

One theory appeared repeatedly.

The bus only appeared to people who were already “marked.”

Nobody knew what that meant.

Marcus tried ignoring everything.

He stopped posting online.

Stopped hanging out with friends.

Stopped driving at night.

But every few weeks, he noticed something impossible.

A yellow bus parked somewhere nearby.

Across the street.

At the edge of parking lots.

Between trees.

Always watching.

Always gone seconds later.

Then one night he heard brakes outside his house.

Marcus froze in bed.

The sound came again.

HISSSSS.

He slowly walked toward the window.

Fog covered the street outside.

Headlights glowed faintly beyond the trees.

A bus sat motionless near the curb.

Number 13.

Every light inside was dark.

Except one.

Near the back.

Someone was waving at him.

Marcus slammed the curtains shut.

The bus horn blasted.

Then silence.

The next morning, muddy footprints circled his house.

8

Spring came.

Disappearances continued.

Not many.

Just enough.

A teenager walking home after work.

A truck driver on a back road.

A college student whose car was found abandoned near the woods.

The stories always sounded similar.

Fog.

Headlights.

A bus.

Then nothing.

The county finally closed several forest roads after dark.

Still, Route 13 sightings increased.

People claimed the bus appeared farther away now.

Nearby counties.

Highways.

Even cities.

The legend was spreading.

Then a package arrived at Marcus’s house.

No return address.

Inside was an old VHS tape labeled:

LAST ROUTE.

Marcus almost threw it away.

Instead, he watched it.

The footage was grainy and distorted.

A man filmed himself sitting inside a school bus sometime during the
1980s.

The passengers behind him looked terrified.

“We’ve been driving for hours,” the man whispered. “The driver won’t
stop.”

The camera turned toward the front.

The bus driver sat unnaturally straight behind the wheel.

Its hands looked too long.

Its face could not be seen.

One passenger suddenly screamed.

Something moved outside the windows.

Tall figures running beside the bus.

The tape ended with the sound of brakes.

On the final frame, someone had written:

YOU CANNOT LEAVE ONCE YOU ARE SEEN.

9

Marcus stopped sleeping.

Every night he heard distant engines.

Every night he dreamed about endless roads surrounded by fog.

Then came the messages.

Unknown numbers texting him at exactly 12:13 AM.

COME BACK.

WE SAVED YOU A SEAT.

One message included a photograph.

It showed the inside of a bus.

Caleb sat near the window.

Smiling.

But something about the smile was horribly wrong.

Too wide.

Marcus smashed his phone.

The messages kept coming.

10

Everything changed on October 14th.

Exactly one year after Caleb disappeared.

A massive storm hit Briar County.

Power lines collapsed.

Roads flooded.

Schools closed early.

Marcus stayed home alone while rain pounded the roof.

At 11:57 PM, the power went out.

The house fell silent.

Then came the headlights.

They slowly swept across his bedroom wall.

Marcus looked outside.

The bus sat directly in front of his house.

This time the doors were already open.

Figures filled every seat.

Their faces pressed against the windows.

Watching him.

One of them stood near the front.

Caleb.

He slowly pointed toward the open bus door.

Marcus backed away from the window.

Then his phone buzzed.

A final message.

IT IS YOUR TURN.

The front door downstairs creaked open.

Marcus heard wet footsteps enter the house.

He locked his bedroom door.

The footsteps climbed the stairs.

Slow.

Heavy.

Then came the sound of bus brakes directly outside his bedroom window.

HISSSSS.

The doorknob began turning.

Marcus grabbed a baseball bat.

The door slowly opened.

A tall silhouette stood in the darkness beyond.

Too thin.

Too tall.

Its arms dragged against the floor.

And behind it, Marcus could see rows of bus seats stretching endlessly
into blackness.

Caleb stood among the passengers.

Still smiling.

Marcus swung the bat.

The creature did not move.

Instead, it raised one impossibly long arm and pointed toward an empty
seat.

Marcus suddenly realized something horrifying.

The passengers were not trapped.

They were waiting.

Waiting for more people to join them.

The creature stepped closer.

Marcus stumbled backward toward the window.

Outside, the bus engine roared louder.

Then every passenger turned their heads toward him at the exact same
time.

The lights went out.

11

Police found Marcus’s house the next morning.

The front door stood open.

Mud covered the hallway.

His bedroom window had shattered outward.

Marcus was gone.

No signs of struggle were found.

Only one strange detail.

Scratched into the bedroom wall were the words:

LAST STOP.

The story spread across the internet within days.

Most people dismissed it as another fake creepypasta.

Others believed every word.

Videos analyzing the disappearances gained millions of views.

People searched old records and discovered dozens of similar cases
dating back decades.

Every few years.

Different towns.

Different victims.

Same stories.

Fog.

Headlights.

A school bus.

Then disappearance.

Eventually the county demolished the stretch of road where Caleb
crashed.

Workers refused to stay after dark.

Several quit after claiming they heard children whispering in the trees.

One worker vanished during a night shift.

The project was abandoned.

Today, Briar County still denies Route 13 exists.

But locals avoid driving near the forest at night.

And sometimes, during heavy fog, people claim they see headlights moving
slowly between the trees.

A yellow school bus.

Number 13.

Waiting.

Some people say the bus only appears to those who already know about it.

Others believe reading the story itself is enough.

There is one detail almost every witness agrees on.

The passengers never look frightened anymore.

They only stare forward silently.

Like they are waiting for the next stop.

And if you ever hear brakes hissing outside your window after midnight…

Do not look outside.

Because according to the stories, once the driver sees your face…

You eventually become a passenger too.
