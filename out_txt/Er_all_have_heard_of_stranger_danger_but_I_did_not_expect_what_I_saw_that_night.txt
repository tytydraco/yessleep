I haven’t talked about this in years.

Not because I forgot.

Because I tried to.

Some things get stuck in your head in a way that never leaves. Like your
brain keeps replaying the same moment over and over even when you’re
trying to sleep. Sometimes I’ll wake up at 3 in the morning already
sweating because I dreamed about those eyes again.

The worst part is that I still drive that road sometimes.

Not by choice.

There’s just no other way home.

And every single time I pass that stretch of forest, I look at the exact
same spot.

Every time.

Expecting to see him.

This happened three years ago when I was driving home from work.

It was a Monday night. Around 8 PM.

I worked evening shifts at a warehouse outside town, and the road home
cut straight through miles of forest. No houses. No gas stations. No
lights. Just trees packed so tightly together that even during daytime
the woods looked dark.

At night it was worse.

The road always felt wrong after sunset. Too quiet. Like the entire
forest was holding its breath.

That night it had been raining earlier, and fog hung low over the
asphalt. My windshield wipers dragged back and forth with that annoying
squeaking sound old wipers make. I remember turning the radio louder
because the silence outside was getting to me.

Then my headlights caught someone standing beside the road.

At first I thought it was a deer because of how still it was.

Then I realized it was a man.

He stood right at the edge of the trees with one thumb raised.

Hitchhiking.

I slowed down automatically.

Looking back now, I don’t know why I did.

Maybe because it was freezing outside.

Maybe because he looked stranded.

Or maybe because something about him didn’t feel human, and my brain
couldn’t process it yet.

When my headlights fully lit him up, I felt my stomach tighten
immediately.

His clothes looked old. Not dirty-old.

Ancient old.

Like clothes dug out of a grave.

He wore a dark coat with tears all over the sleeves, and parts of the
fabric looked stiff like they’d been soaked and dried hundreds of times.
His pants hung loose around his legs, and his shoes looked uneven like
the soles were falling apart.

But it was his face that made me uncomfortable.

His skin looked pale gray under the headlights.

Not pale like sick.

Pale like wet ash.

And his eyes…

I honestly don’t know how to explain them correctly.

At first I thought the shadows were hiding them.

But no.

His eyes were just… dark.

Completely dark.

No reflection from the headlights.

No whites.

No color.

Just black.

Like somebody carved two holes into his face.

I rolled my window down halfway.

“You need help?” I asked.

The man didn’t answer.

He didn’t blink either.

He just stared directly at me.

Not at my face.

At my eyes.

I remember getting this horrible feeling all over my body.

Like every instinct I had was screaming at me to leave.

Then I noticed something else.

The forest behind him was completely silent.

No insects.

No wind.

Nothing.

Even the trees looked still.

The man slowly tilted his head to one side.

Too far.

I heard a soft cracking sound from his neck.

And that was enough for me.

I started reaching for the window button—

Then another car passed behind me.

Its headlights flashed across my mirrors for maybe half a second.

I looked away from the man for one second.

One second.

When I looked back—

He was gone.

Not walking away.

Not running.

Gone.

The road was empty.

I actually got out of my car because my brain couldn’t understand what
happened. I shined my phone flashlight into the trees, expecting to hear
footsteps or branches snapping.

Nothing.

Just darkness between the trees.

I drove home shaking so badly I almost hit a guardrail.

That night I barely slept.

Every little sound outside my house made me jump awake.

At one point around 2 AM I swear I heard footsteps outside my bedroom
window.

Slow footsteps.

Crunching through gravel.

Back and forth.

Back and forth.

But when I looked outside, nobody was there.

The next day I tried convincing myself I imagined everything.

By the end of my shift I almost believed it.

Then I saw him again.

Same road.

Same time.

Same spot.

Standing there waiting.

This time I didn’t stop right away.

I just slowed down while staring at him through the windshield.

Something had changed.

His clothes looked wetter now.

Like he’d been standing in rain for days.

And his face looked thinner somehow.

I could see the shape of his jaw pressing tightly against his skin.

Then he smiled.

Very slowly.

I immediately slammed the brakes and locked my doors.

The smile didn’t look normal.

It stretched too wide across his face.

The skin near the corners of his mouth cracked slightly as it spread.

And even from inside the car I could see his teeth looked wrong.

Too long.

Too dark.

I grabbed my phone to call someone, but before I could dial, the inside
of my car suddenly fogged up.

Not the windows.

The air itself.

Cold white breath filled the car like someone had opened a freezer.

I could suddenly smell something rotten.

Wet dirt.

Mold.

And something metallic underneath it.

Blood.

I looked back toward the road.

The hitchhiker was gone again.

But this time I didn’t feel relieved.

Because something tapped gently on my driver-side window.

Tap.

Tap.

Tap.

I turned slowly.

He was standing inches from the glass.

I screamed so loudly my throat hurt instantly.

Up close his face looked inhuman.

His skin looked stretched tightly over something that barely resembled a
skull anymore. Veins black as ink crawled beneath his skin, pulsing
slowly around his neck.

And his eyes…

There was movement inside them.

Shapes moving in the darkness where his eyes should’ve been.

Like something alive was crawling around deep inside his skull.

Then he opened his mouth.

Not to speak.

Just opened it.

Far wider than any human being should be able to.

I saw darkness inside.

No tongue.

No throat.

Just blackness going impossibly deep.

I hit the gas so hard my tires spun.

When I looked in the mirror a second later, he was gone.

Again.

The third night was when everything happened.

I should’ve stayed home.

I should’ve quit my job.

I should’ve done literally anything else.

But I drove that road again.

And there he was.

Waiting.

Except this time he stood in the middle of the road.

Completely still.

My headlights lit him up while fog rolled around his legs.

I stopped the car nearly thirty feet away from him.

And for the first time…

He started walking toward me.

Slowly.

Not normal walking either.

His arms hung too low at his sides, and every movement looked stiff and
jerky like a puppet learning how to move.

Step.

Drag.

Step.

Drag.

I called the police immediately.

I couldn’t even speak properly to the dispatcher because I was panicking
so badly.

The hitchhiker kept walking toward my car while I talked.

Then he stopped directly in front of my headlights.

He just stood there staring at me.

Smiling.

The police arrived maybe seven minutes later.

Longest seven minutes of my life.

Two officers stepped out of the cruiser.

I remember feeling so relieved I almost cried.

The younger officer approached my window while the older one looked
toward the hitchhiker.

“You okay, sir?” the younger officer asked.

Before I could answer, the older officer called out:

“Hey! You can’t stand in the middle of the road like that!”

The hitchhiker slowly turned toward him.

The officer walked closer.

And suddenly every sound around us stopped.

The police radio cut into static.

The insects stopped.

Even the wind disappeared.

I remember noticing my own heartbeat because it suddenly sounded so
loud.

The older officer stopped walking.

“Sir?” he asked again quietly.

The hitchhiker moved so fast I barely saw it happen.

One second he stood six feet away.

The next his hand was around the officer’s throat.

The officer immediately started choking.

Not screaming.

Choking.

Like he couldn’t breathe at all.

The younger cop drew his gun and shouted something, but I couldn’t hear
it properly because I was staring at the older officer.

His skin was changing color.

Gray spreading upward from the hitchhiker’s fingers.

The officer clawed desperately at the thing’s arm while his face sank
inward like his body was drying out from the inside.

His eyes shriveled.

His lips cracked open.

And then pieces of him started falling apart.

Dust.

Actual dust.

His fingers crumbled first.

Then his jaw.

I heard bones snapping inside his body.

The younger officer started firing.

The gunshots sounded deafening in the silence.

Every bullet hit the hitchhiker directly in the chest.

Nothing happened.

No blood.

No reaction.

The thing slowly turned its head toward the younger cop.

Still smiling.

Then the older officer collapsed completely.

Not a body.

A pile of gray ash in a police uniform.

The younger cop froze.

Just for one second.

That was enough.

The hitchhiker grabbed him by the throat too.

The younger officer looked directly at me with pure terror in his eyes.

“DRIVE!” he screamed.

I did.

God, I did.

I drove so fast the car shook violently.

I couldn’t stop shaking.

I kept hearing those choking sounds in my head.

I kept seeing the officer’s face collapsing inward.

Then I noticed something strange.

The inside of my car was getting colder again.

My breath became visible.

The smell returned.

Wet dirt.

Rot.

Blood.

I already knew before I looked.

But I looked anyway.

The rearview mirror reflected the backseat.

And he was sitting there.

Right behind me.

Too tall for the car.

His head bent sideways against the roof.

Smiling inches from my ear.

I screamed and swerved across the road.

The thing didn’t move.

Didn’t blink.

It just stared at me while black liquid slowly dripped from its mouth
onto the seat.

Then it whispered something.

Not words.

More like multiple voices talking at once underneath water.

I remember one sentence clearly though.

“Thank you for the ride.”

I lost my mind after hearing that.

I aimed the car straight at an old concrete barrier near an abandoned
gas station and slammed the accelerator.

The crash felt like getting hit by a train.

Glass exploded everywhere.

Pain shot through my chest.

For a second everything went white.

Then silence.

I lifted my head slowly.

The windshield was gone.

And so was he.

I looked outside just in time to see the hitchhiker standing up from the
pavement.

Bones cracked loudly as they snapped back into place.

His head twisted completely around toward me.

Still smiling.

Then the lights at the gas station flickered once.

And he disappeared.

Just gone.

The police never found the officers.

Officially they’re listed as missing.

There were no bodies.

No blood.

Nothing except piles of gray dust found on the road.

I moved away after that.

New town.

New house.

New life.

But sometimes late at night I still wake up because I hear tapping on
glass.

Three slow taps.

Tap.

Tap.

Tap.

And last week…

I saw someone standing beside the road again.

Thumb raised.

Waiting.

But this time when my headlights passed over him…

I realized he wasn’t alone anymore.

Two police officers were standing beside him.

Completely still.

Smiling.
