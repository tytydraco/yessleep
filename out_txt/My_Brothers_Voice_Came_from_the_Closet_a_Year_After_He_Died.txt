You know how some people say they can still hear the voices of the dead?
I used to think that was just grief… playing tricks. Your brain,
desperate for one more moment, one more word, just… makes something up.
Echoes of the past in the halls of your mind.

But I heard my brother’s voice.

Every night.

Coming from the closet.

Not just any closet—the same one we used to hide in during storms when
the lightning scared us more than the thunder ever could. The same
closet that was sealed shut… after Tyler died.

I was sixteen when he vanished.

They said it was an accident. He had wandered into the woods behind our
house one night—no one knew why—and never came back. For a week, search
teams combed every inch of those trees. And when they finally found him…

His body was twisted. Mangled. Like it had fallen from a height. Bones
shattered, neck turned wrong, eyes wide open like they froze in fear.

But here’s the thing:

There’s nothing tall in those woods.

No cliffs. No towers. Nothing he could have fallen from.

Mom couldn’t take it. She sealed his room, packed away every trace of
him. She said she couldn’t breathe with all the memories choking the
air. Dad… he just went quiet. Like a switch flipped. He stopped being
there, even when he was in the room.

We moved on. Or at least… we tried to.

But on the exact anniversary of his death… I heard it.

2:17 AM. I remember the time because I looked at the clock, my heart
already pounding from a nightmare I couldn’t remember.

And then I heard it.

A whisper.

My name.

“Sophie… open the door… please… it’s so dark…”

Tyler’s voice.

Clear. Scared. Small. Just like I remembered it.

I froze. My blood turned to ice. My mouth opened, but nothing came out.

The next night, it happened again.

Same time. Same voice.

And again the next.

Every night. 2:17 AM. My brother. Calling from the closet.

I thought maybe I was cracking up. Maybe this was the grief I’d been
warned about. Some delayed trauma unspooling in my mind like a horror
film.

So I started recording it. Just to prove—to myself, to anyone—that I
wasn’t crazy.

But every morning, the recordings were ruined. Corrupted. White noise.
Static. Sometimes a high-pitched shriek. Once… a faint laugh. Like
someone—something—was mocking me.

I finally broke down and told Mom. She refused to listen. Wouldn’t even
let me finish the sentence. She screamed at me to stop. Said it was
cruel to bring him up. That he was gone. End of story.

But then… I found the drawing.

Tucked into the frame of the closet door.

It was old, crumpled, drawn in Tyler’s scribbly handwriting. He’d always
drawn little monsters—his weird imagination splattered in crayon.

But this wasn’t a monster.

It was me.

Standing in front of the closet.

And behind the closet door… a shadow. With glowing eyes. Watching me.

He drew it before he died.

He knew.

That’s when I stopped sleeping.

I set up a camera. I needed proof. I needed to see if I was imagining it
all.

But the next morning… when I checked the footage…

I wasn’t in bed.

I was standing right in front of the closet.

Just staring at it.

Smiling.

Like something was behind the door… whispering to me.

And I had no memory of it.

Sleepwalking. That’s what they said.

But why always at 2:17?

Why always toward the closet?

One night, I almost opened it. My hand was on the doorknob. I could feel
the cold metal, slick with condensation. My other hand was shaking. And
just before I twisted the knob…

Something grabbed my wrist.

Cold. Ice cold. Like death itself. And it pulled me back.

I swear… it felt like Tyler.

Like he was trying to stop me.

Or maybe… it wasn’t him at all.

Maybe something else was using his voice.

I started digging. Researching. Looking up any story, any legend, that
even remotely sounded like mine.

And I found it.

An old urban myth. Whispers on forums, deep in Reddit threads, and dusty
paranormal blogs.

They called it The Hollow Voice.

A creature, a spirit—no one really knows. But it mimics the dead. Takes
the voice of someone you’ve lost. Uses your grief, your pain, like a key
to get in.

It waits for anniversaries. Places of trauma. Doors that were once left
open in fear or love.

And it calls out to you.

With the voice you miss the most.

If you answer…

If you open the door…

You trade places.

That’s when it clicked.

Tyler didn’t die in the woods.

He opened the closet.

He heard something that sounded like me.

I should’ve burned the closet. I should’ve sealed the whole room. I
should’ve listened.

But that night… something changed.

I felt calm. Still. Like everything was right again. Like Tyler was
there—really there. His voice wasn’t scared this time. Just… cold.

“I’m cold, Sophie… please…”

I opened the door.

If you ever hear your dead loved one calling from a dark place… don’t
answer.

Because whatever’s calling you…

It’s not them.

And if you hear me now…

…it’s already too late.
